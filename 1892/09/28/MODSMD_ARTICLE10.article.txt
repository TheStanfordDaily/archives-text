# History Seminary.
## 
### 
The History Seminary met in Prof.
Howard's private study yesterday af
ternoon at the usual hour of 2 o'clock.
The work presented was a continua
tion of that begun at the previous
meeting, and a supplement to that at
present occupying the class in the
study of the English Constitutional
History.
Mr. E. I). Lewis led in the discus
sion, giving a review of Seebohm's ex
position of the three-field system. In
addition to Mr. Lewis' discussion I'rot.
Howard and the members contributed
to the subject from such authors as
Denman Ross, Vinogradoff and Kem
ble.
