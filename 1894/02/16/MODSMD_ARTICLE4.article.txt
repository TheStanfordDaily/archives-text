# Meeting of the Athletic Board.
## 
### 
The Athletic Hoard met Wednesday
at 7 p.m. in room 96, Enciua. H. M.
Leadbetter, '95, was elected to fill the
vac incy made by Geo. Campbell, who has
left the University. A committee of
three was appointed to draw up an
intercollegiate agreement with the U.
C. concerning college athletics; and a
permanent committee was appointed
whose business will be to arrange for
all benefits which may be given here
for athletics.
The German theatrical troupe which
played in the chapel some time ago
may, through the influence of this com
mittee, be induced to appear here again,
but at present uothing definite is known.
