No more important office, after
the presidency of the Associated |

Students, exists under the new con
stitution than that of the treasurer.
His duties will entail none of the
disagreeable and annoying details
of business management, but will
require only a careful mechanical
record of finances. Traveling round
the country with the teams—if that
be part of his duty —to take charge
of gate receipts, will be an easy and
agreeable occupation, and one with
which the experiences of the foot
ball manager, coupled as they are
with arrangements and financeer
ing, cannot compare.
