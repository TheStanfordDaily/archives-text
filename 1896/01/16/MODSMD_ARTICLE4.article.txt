# Sophomore Nominations.
## 
### 
A well attended meeting of the
Class of '98 was held this afternoon.
The office of president is being
eagerly contested and several can
didates are in the field. For pres
ident, Stewart Cotton was nom
inated by Li Hard, Geo. IT. Francis
by Gillette, and Frederick Raseli by
Oskison. For vice-president, W.
I'. Harrington was nominated by
Johnson. C. S. Pole and Miss j.
V. Fielder were nominated for sec
retary. Miss Mackintosh was
nominated for historian, C. N. Rig
gins for sergeant-at-arms, and T.
M. Storke for baseball manager.
