It is to he hoped that the new
feature to he inaugurated by the
literary societies on Saturday night
will prove the success its promoters
anticipate. The plan, as set forth
in another column, is to make the
proceedings an exact copy of those
of the House of Representatives.
Apart from the valuable training
to he derived in the methods of
conducting a deliberative assembly,
great advantage may be had from
the fact that the speakers will ad
dress a larger and entirely differ
ent audience from those to which
they are accustomed in their weekly
meetings. It will be a good thing
for the societies to come in contact
with each other occasionally.
Otherwise they might fall into ruts.
