# College Notes.
## 
### 
The University of Indiana has
adopted a course in journalism.
Mr. Deland has resigned his po
sition as head coach of the Harv
ard football team. Ex-Captain B.
(i. Waters has been appointed in
his place.
A gift of half- a million dollars
has lately been received by MeCill
University from W. C. McDonald,
the wealthy tobacco manufacturer
of Montreal, making a total of $2,-
000,000 given to that institution by
Mr. McDonald.
An effort, is being made by Harv
ard graduates to arrange the dona
tion of scholarships so that they
will be given solely on the basis of
scholarship and not on the basis
of indigence. Scholarship and in
digence, heretofore, have both been
taken into account.
Mr. William Blaikio, Harvard
'66, Harvard's ideal strong man,
in a smoke talk at Cambridge chose
a crew from the great men in hist
ory, also men of muscle, as follows:
Bow, Oliver Cromwell; 2, Duke of
Wellington; 3, Washington; 4, Lin
coln; 5, Napoleon; 6, Bismarck; 7,
Peter the (ireat; stroke, Julius
Caesar.
The school of law at Cornell
University is again strengthened
by the appointment of a brilliant
young professor. This time it is
Professor Edwin 11. Woodruff of Le
land Stanford Jr. University. He
is an authority on both library and
legal matter. Although a very
young man he lias been assistant
in the Astor Library and Cornell
University library, librarian of
Professor Willard Fiske's library
at Florence, Italy, and of the Stan
ford University library. Besides
this he has been instructor in Eng
lish at Cornell, and for two years
professor of law at Leland Stanford
University. Professor Woodruff
was a member of the lirst class that
ever graduated from the Cornell
Law School, and his graduation
thesis, entitled a "History of
Equity -lurisprudence in Massachu
setts" was published in the London
Law Quarterly Review, the great
est legal periodical in the English
language. Jointly with Professor
Huffcut of Cornell he is author of
"American Cases on Contract," now
used in the law schools of Cornell,
Stanford, the University of Penn
sylvania. University of the City of
New York, Michigan University,
and other schools. Professor Wood
ruff is at present engaged in pre
paring an elementary treatise on
law.—(Cornell.
