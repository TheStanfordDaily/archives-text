The attention of shareholders in
the Co-op. should be given to the

communication in another column
regarding the approaching election
for manager. The disclosures
therein made reveal a tendency all
too prevalent in our student politics.
That any one should resort to such
a desperate practice in order to se
cure the position, it may he argued,
is a purely business proposition;
hut it. is not the sort of business in
which a student should be engaged.
While it may he perfectly legal to
purchase stock in this way, yet it is
done only for one purpose and will
have but one result—to defeat the
wishes of a majority of the persons
holding legitimate stock.
It is a transaction as unique as
it is reprehensible, consequently it
will be interesting to watch the
progress of the affair to see whether
the sentiment of the students will
allovv such.a deal to gain the up
per hand. Proxies can he changed
lip to the time of the election. Let
every self-respecting shareholder see
to it that no share of his is used in
the furtherance of such a scheme as
is outlined in this communication..
