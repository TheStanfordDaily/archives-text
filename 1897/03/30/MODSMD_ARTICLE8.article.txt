# Announcements.
## 
### 
Y. M. C. A. —Meeting in room 31
quad tomorrow evening at 7:15.
Leader, Prof. Stearns.
At Home. —Dr. and Mrs. Ross
will be at home to students Thurs
day evening, April 1.
Literary Societies, —There will
be no regular meeting of the liter
ary societies next Saturday even
ing. There will be inter-society
debates.
Chapel Sermon. —The chapel
sermon April 4 will be delivered by
Rev. Geo. C. Adams of the First
Congregational Church of San
Francisco.
Chess Chjb.—There will be an
extra meeting Saturday at 2 p.m.
al the home of Dr. Richardson. All
members who have not played in
the tournament will meet there to
play. C. J. Dulley, Pres.
Philological Association. —The
next regular meeting will be held on
Thursday, April 1, at 2 o'clock in
room 31. Principal paper by Prof.
A. G. Newcomer, "Translations
from Pindar." Minor communica
tion by Prof. J. C. Branner, "French
place names in the State of Arkan
sas." The principal paper for
April is promised by Prof. K. G.
Rendtorff. H. R. Fairclough,
