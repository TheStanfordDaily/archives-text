# Announcements.
## 
### 
Euphhonia.— There will be a
business meeting of Euphronia to
morrow at 1:30 p.m. in room 31
quad. W. P. Gifkord, Pres.
Co-op. Meeting.—There will be
a meeting of the stockholders of the
Co-operative Association on Tues
day, April 20, at 1:30 p.m. in the
chapel. E. E. Farmer, Pres.
Classical Lectuhe.—The regular
monthly classical lecture will be
given Friday morning at 11:30 in
room 3. Professor Fairclough will
speak on ''Latin popular poetry."
Junior Meeting.—There will be
a meeting of the Class of '98 in the
chapel at 1:30 Friday to hear re
port of the manager of the Annual,
and for other important business.
C. M. Fickert, Pres.
Faculty Ladies' Club.—The
Faculty Ladies' Club will meet
with Mrs Sanford at 3 p.m. Mon
day, April 12. Prof. Hudson will
speak on "William Morris." Mem
bers are requested to bring their
dues for the semester.
»SENioR CoMmittee. —There will
be a meeting of the committee on
Class Day, consisting of Miss Laura
Mann, Miss Lillian Ray, Miss
Edith Jordan, T. M. Williams, and
G. R. Greenleaf, Thursday in room
11 at 1:30 p.m. C. S. Pope.
Philolexia. —The program for
the meeting of Philolexia Saturday
evening, April 10, is as follows:
Speech, Metteer, '00; open debate,
''Resolved, That the Senate should
confirm the Anglo-American arbi
tration treaty;" regular debate,
''Resolved, That the Democratic
party, to succeed, must depart from
the policy as evinced in the Chi
cago platform"; affirmative, E. C.
Page, '00, and C. M. Bradley, '99;
negative, li. E. Filcher, '99, and
C. T. Hayden, '00.
