# The 						Junior Farce.
## 
### 
The scenery for the Junior Farce,
which is to be given in Encina
gymnasium tomorrow night, came
down today. The play is to be
finely staged. Mr. Connick has or
ganized an orchestra of fifteen
pieces, which will play during the
evening.
About three hundred tickets for
the farce have already been sold.
The audience is requested to come
earl)% so as to be seated before
eight. The overture will begin
sharply at eight o'clock.
