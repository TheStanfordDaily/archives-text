# Announcements.
## 
### 
Sophomore Class. —There will be
a meeting in the physics lecture
room Friday at 1.30 p.m. for the
nomination of officers.
J. P. Rusk, Pres.
Varsity Candidates are re
quested to meet on the football iield
tomorrow (Thursday) at 4 p.m.
Candidates should be in suits and
fully equipped for practice.
Steuart W. Cotton, Captain.
Students' Guild. —There will be
a meeting of the board of directors
of the Students' Guild Thursday,
September 9, at 5 p.m. in room
10 quad. All members are re
quested to lie present.
John C. Swenson, Pres.
Elementary Al athematics. —
Those desiring to take work in Ele
mentary Mathematics are requested
to meet me at their earliest conven
ience. 1 shall be in room 15 quad
every day from 1:30 to 2 p.m.
J. M. PREEMAN.
Alpha.—The first meeting will
be held on Saturday, Sept. 11, at
7:30 p.m., room 23 quad. Visitors
are cordially invited to attend.
Members should be prepared to dis
cuss the problem of college rushes.
J. M. Oskison, President.
To Subscribers. —Subscriptions
to this paper may be left at the
Bookstore, Daily Palo Alto office
in the powerhouse, or in the manu
script box at the corner of the Uni
versity postoflice. Those wishing
back copies can have them at the
Daily Palo Alto office any after
noon this week.
University Announcement. —
Beginning with September, 1897,
all work reported "inc." must be
completed within one semester af
ter the '"incomplete" is incurred, if
such work is to count towards grad
uation. Conditions must be re
moved within one year.
O. L. Elliott, Registrar.
