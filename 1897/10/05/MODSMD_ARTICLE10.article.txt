# Announcements.
## 
### 
King's Daughters.—There will
be a meeting of the King's Daugh
ters in room 13 quad tomorrow at
1:30 p.m.
Band Practice.—There will be
a meeting of the Band for practice
tomorrow at 7:00 p.m. in Encina
gymnasium.
Executive Committee. — The
Executive Committee of the stu
dent body will meet Thursday at 2
p.m. in room 11 quad.
A. B. Morgan, Pres.
Tennis Club. —There will be a
meeting of all those interested in
the Tennis Club on Thursday at
1:30 p.m. in room 10 quad.
W. C. McNeil, Pres.
Faculty Ladies' Club. — The
Faculty Ladies' Club will meet at
the home of Mrs. Walter Miller in
College Terrace on Monday. Octo
ber 11, from 3 to 5 o'clock. The
program will consist of piano music
by Mrs. Starbuck.
New Books —The following new
books have been received at the
University Bookstore: Chrystal,
Elementary Algebra; Dryden's
Poems; Dryden's Discourses on
Satire and Epic Poetry; Ploetz,
Epitome of History.
Freshmen Football Men. —All
freshmen who have suits and can
not get out to practice at least three
times a week or who do not intend
to play must hand in these suits at
once for the use of men who are
anxious to play but have no clothes.
C. M. Bradley,
Freshman Manager.
Mandolin Club. —The following
men will hereafter report for prac
tice: First mandolins, Sewall, Lan
agan, Arnold, and Field; second
mandolins, C. Strohn, Knecht,
Fong, and Corbusier; lute, Starr;
guitars, Nichols, Sladen, R. Strohn,
Seward, Pledger, Durrell, McNeil.
Executive Committee.
