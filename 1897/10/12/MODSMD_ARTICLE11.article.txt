# Announcements.
## 
### 
—Tennis Club. —There will be a
meeting of the Tennis Club in room
11 quad at 1:30 p.m. Wednesday.
Y. M. C. A.—The Y. M. C. A.
will meet Thursday evening, Octo
ber 14, at 7:15 p.m. in room 33
quad. Professor Johnston will lead.
University Band. —There will
be a meeting for practice in Encina
gymnasium Wednesday at 7:30
p.m. All members are earnestly
requested to be present.
Freshman Class. There will be
a meeting of the Class of 1901 in
the chapel tomorrow at 1:30 p.m.
to adopt a class j'ell and to elect
yell leaders. Haroi.d Havens,
Pres.
Debating League.—The execu
tive committee of the Inter-society
Debating League will meet with
those who will try for the Carnot
Debate Wednesday at 1:30 in room
10 quad. E. 11. Rothrock,
Chairman.
Student Body Election.— At a
meeting of the Athletic Committee
today Frank V. Keesling and Roy
X. Strohn were candidates for the
baseball managership. The ballot
was a tie, so the committee referred
the matter to a student body elec
tion. This election will take place
Thursday, October 12, between 11
a.m. and 2 p.m. at the main en
trance to the quad.
A. B.Morgan, President.
New Books. —The following new
books are at the University Book
store: Bellamy's Looking Back
ward; Brothers, Statutes and Con
stitutional Documents; Mommsen,
History of Rome; Parker, Elemen
tary Biology; Committee of Fifty,
The Liquor Problem; Sir Thomas
More, Utopia; Carpenter, Exercises
in Rhetoric; Miiller, The German
Classics; Goodyear, Roman and
Medieval Art; Weise, Unsere Mut
terspraehe; Spencer's Education;
Milinan, History of Latin Chris
tianity; Darwin, Origin of Species.
