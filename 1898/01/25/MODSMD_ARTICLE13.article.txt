Much encouragement is to lie de
rived from the showing made on
the diamond yesterday. The new
men not only came out in large
numbers but showed a variety of
ball that speaks well for the pres
ent and future seasons. We have
been beaten butoneeon the diamond
by Berkeley, and that last year.
Every effort should be made this
year to prevent a repetition of this
defeat. With the captain we have,
with the men at his service, and
with good management, a hard
struggle ought to be made for the
championship.
No man should be disconcerted
by the large number of men trying
for the nine places. The second
nine system will doubtless be con
tinued this year, giving plenty of
room for all candidates.
