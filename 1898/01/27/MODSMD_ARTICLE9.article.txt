# Announcements.
## 
### 
Senior Meeting.—The Class of
'98 will meet in the chapel Friday
at 2 p.m. for the election of officers.
W. W. Kemp, President.
King's Daughters.—The King's
Daughters will meet Sunday after
noon, January 30, from 3 to 4 p.m.,
at the home of Miss Berdine on
Channing avenue, Palo Alto.
Junior Farce.--A prize of $10
is hereby offered for the best pro
duction of a junior farce. The
competition is open to all students.
J. E. McDowell, President.
Ladies' Mandolin Club. —There
will he a meeting of the executive
committee of the Ladies' Mando
lin Club, Friday, 1:45 p.m., room
10 quad.
Helen Swett, Chairman.
Inter-Society Debating League.
—The League will meet Saturday
evening at 7:15 in room 21 quad.
The amendments proposed by the
executive committee of the League
will be voted upon and other busi
ness of much importance will be
considered. Every member is
urged to be present.
E. H. Rothrock, President.
New Books. —The following new
books have been received at the
University Bookstore: Farbell, A
History of Greek Art; Lanciani,
The Ruins and Excavations of
Ancient Rome; Serrano, Marie
Bash kirtseff; Ford, The True
George Washington; Stark, The
Primitive Family.
