# The 						Vesper Service.
## 
### 
The vesper service held in the
chapel yesterday ufterno n, lasting
about a hour, was very successful.
The attendance was estimated at
about one hundred, which was
greater than had been expected.
Mrs. Sinith and Mr. Hyde each
gave a solo, and a quartette from
the chapel choir sang a selection.
Dr. Thoburn spoke of the need and
advantages of such a religious'ser
vice as a quiet hour of rent in the
midst of the labors of the week, and
offered prayer. Professor Johnston
will lead the service next Thursday
afternoon.
