# Alumni Notes.
## 
### 
—A. IS. Miller, '95, lias taken his
Ph. D. degree at Heidelberg since
graduation, and now holds the
chair of mining, metallurgy, and
geology at the University of Idaho.
—J. E. Reynolds, '96, who is
now a law student at Columbia,
represented department 2 of the
Hamilton Club as counsel in the
annual inter-class moot-court trial
of 1898.
—H.D.Sheldon, president of the
student body in 1895-96 and an
assistant in the Education de
partment last year, will give a
course of lectures on the history of
American education this July at
the session of the summer school of
Clark University, Muss.
