# Announcements.
## 
### 
Encina Club. —There will be a
meeting of the Encina Club Wed
nesday at 7:30 p.m.
Pacific Slope History.—The
class in History 10a (Pacific Slope)
will meet in room 10 instead of
room 31.
Mandolin Club. —There will be
a try-out for those wishing to make
the club this year in room 33 quad
at 7:45 Thursday evening, Sept. 8.
Ralph Arnold, Leader.
English Department. — 1 wish
to announce that my office hour
hereafter will be on Monday, Wed
nesday, and Friday, 4:30 to 5 p.m.
Melville B. Anderson.
Chess Club.—A meeting for the
purpose of re-organizing the Chess
Club will be held in room 62 quad
at 1:30 next .Monday. All those
interested in chess are invited to be
present.
Library Hours. —The Univer
sity Library will be open daily ex
cept Saturday from 8 a.m. to 5
p.m., remaining closed between the
hours of 5 p.m. and 7 p.m. Un
Saturdays it will be open from V
a.m. to o p.m.
Symphony Club. —An effort to
re-organize the Symphony Club
will be made next week. All stu
dents having fair ability on any
orchestral instrument are invited
to join. Date of meeting will be
announced later.
Ladies' Mandolin Club. —There
will be a meeting of the Ladies'
Mandolin Club on Thursday, 4:30
p.m., room 10 quad. All old mem
bers are expected to be present,
and any young ladies wishing to
join will be made welcome.
Susie L. Dyer, President.
Women's Sitting Room. — There
will be a meeting of all women in
the University interested in main
taining a room in which women
students may lunch, study, or rest,
in room 10 quad, 1 p.m. Wednes
day. All who are willing to do
anything toward maintaining such
a room are urged to attend.
Committee.
Encina Club Assessments. —The
assessment of one dollar levied by
the Board of Directors last evening
is now due from every student in
Encina, and may be paid to any
one of the following: R. E. Filcher,
S. P. Elias, J. R. Hamilton, Alfred
Haslacher, R. A. Leiter, or any
member of the Board of Directors.
J. F. English,
Treasurer Encina Club.
