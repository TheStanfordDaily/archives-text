# ANTI-TAXATION MEETINGS.
## Schedule of the Conferences to be Held Tomorrow Afternoon from 1:30 to 4:30 p.m. 
### 
The executive committee of the
anti-tax club has sent to all stu-J
dents in the University who reside
in California, aside from those in
Santa Clara county, requesting their
presence at one of the nine meet
ings to be held tomorrow afternoon
from 1:30 to 4:30 p.m. The aim of
the meetings is to impress on the
students the need of active work
and the way in which they may be
useful. The schedule of the meet
ings is as follows:
Room 143, Engineering building,!
at 1:30 p.m. —Students from San I
Mateo, Santa Cruz and Monterey'
counties.
Room 143, 2:30 p.m. — Students
from San Benito. Kings, San Luis
Obispo and Kern counties.
Room 143,3:30 p.m.—Students
from Santa Barbara, Ventura, San j
Bernardino, Riverside and San i
Diego counties.
Room 144, Engineering building, I
1:30 p.m. —Students from Sonoma,!
Napa, Solano and Marin counties.
Room 144,2:30 p.m. Students
from Los Angeles and Orange
counties.
Room 144,3:30 p.m. —Students)
from San Francisco county.
Room 23, quad, 1:30 p.m. —Stu-
dents from Alameda county.
Room 23, 2:30 p.m.— Students
from Siskiyou. Shasta, Humboldt,
Butte, Glenn, Mendocino, Sierra,
Modoc, Lassen, Trinity, Del Norte,
Plumas, Lake, Colusa, Sutter,
Yuba, Nevada, Placer, Eldorado,
Alpine, Amador, Sacramento, and
Yolo counties.
Room 23, 3:30 p.m. —Students
from Tulare, Tuolumne, Calaveras,
Contra Costa, San Joaquin, Mono,
Inyo, Fresno, Madera, Mariposa,
Merced, and Stanislaus counties.
All students who live in any of
the.above-named counties and who
have not received postal cards are
requested by the" committee to
meet with the proper section as
shown in the above schedule. In
addition all others who are inter
ested are cordially invited to be
present.
