At Home. —Kappa Kappa Gam
ma will be at home Saturday even
ing, February 11.
The Faculty Ladies' Club will
meet at the home of Mrs. C. L>.
Marx in Palo Alto, Monday at 3
p.m. The meeting will be entirely
social, without a formal program.
Euphronia. — Euphronia will
meet at Franklin's gallery at 2 p.m.
sharp Saturday. The regular meet
ing will be held immediately fol
lowing. J. T. Nourse, Pres.
Graduate CLUB.-The next meet
ing of the Graduate Club will be
held in the Ruble Parlors Satur
day evening, February 11. The
program is strictly social.
David A. Curry, Pres.
Freshman. —Candidates for the
Freshman track team will meet in
the chapel on Friday at 4:30 for
the purpose of electing a captain.
F. W. Bancroft,
Manager Freshman Track Team.
Junior Class Picture. —Every
member is requested to be at the
Museum steps Wednesday, Febru
ary 15, at 2 p.m., for the purpose
of taking the class pictures. The
men will wear their plugs and the
women their caps.
Student Body.—The members of
the Executive Committee and the
president, vice-president, and treas
urer of the Associated Students will
meet at Thors' gallery lor photo
next Saturday at 1:30 p.m.
Chas. E. Schwartz, Pres.
Sophomore Track Men. — All
members of the 11)01 track team
will meet in room 11 quad Friday
at 1:30 p.m. to elect a captain. All
men who took the trip to Reno last
spring, and Hahn, are entitled to
votes. A. B. Stewart, Manager.
Tuesday Evening Lecture. —Dr.
Jordan gives the next Tuesday
evening lecture on " The Question
of the Philippines." The lecture
will be given under the auspices of
the Graduate Club and Mr. David
A. Curry of the club will preside.
New Books. —The Bookstore has
received the following books: Trag
edies of zTCschylus, PI umpire; Life of
Cicero, Forsyth; Briefs tor Debate,
Brookings and Ringwalt; Text-
Book on Physiology, Dr. M. Foster;
Democracy and Other Papers,
James Russell Lowell; Faraday as
a Discoverer, Tyndall; Introduc
tion to American Literature, Pan
coast.
