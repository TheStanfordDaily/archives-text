# Announcements.
## 
### 
Dr. Flugel's Classes. —Dr. Flu
gel will not be able to meet his
classes until further notice on ac
count of illness in his family.
At Home. —Dr. and Mrs. Jordan
will he unable to be "at home" to
the faculty on March 4, but they
will receive on the eleventh instead.
Mandolin Club.-Memhers of the
Mandolin Club will meet in room
33 quad Thursday evening at 7:15
p.m., and Tuesday afternoon at
4:30.
Bookstore. —Owing to the in
creased interest in golf the Book
store will carry from now on a full
line of golf goods, including balls,
clubs, and rule books.
Chess Club. —The Chess Club
will meet this evening at Dr. Rich
ardson's residence. Important mat
ters are to be considered and a
large attendance is desired.
Next Sunday's Sermon. — Pro
fessor F. B. Dresslar of the depart
ment of pedagogy of the Univer
sity of California will preach next
Sunday in the chapel on "Super
stition and Education."
Sophomore Meeting.—There will
be a meeting of the Class of ; 0l
Thursday at 1:15 in the chapel. Im
portant business will be transacted.
A large attendance is urged.
P. 11. Wilson, President.
Masonic Association. —There will
be a meeting of the Stanford Ma
sonic Association in room 21 quad,
Monday, March 0, at 4:30 p.m., for
the purpose of electing officers for
the ensuing year.
J. H. Coverley, Sec.
Class Track Captains. All
class track managers (or captains)
will meet me in room 11 quad
Thursday at 4:30 p.m. Classes
which have no track manager will
please elect one as soon as possible.
K. W. Smith.
San Qukntin Trip.—A trip to
San Quentin prison will be made
on next Saturday. Those wishing
to go, especially the members of
last semester's class in Social Prob
lems, should speak to Professor
Fetter regarding it. The party
will take the 7:58 train.
Basket-Ball.—There will be a
practice game between the basket
ball teams of '00 and '01 next Sat
urday at 9:30 a.m. on the Roble
field. All who by that time shall
have been chosen on the teams of
either class are requested to meet
promptly at the time announced.
