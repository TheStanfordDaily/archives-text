# Prize for Debaters.
## 
### 
The sum of money which Sena
tor George C. Perkins placed in the
hands of the Intercollegiate Debat
ing League to be devoted to prizes
for the winners of the annual In
tercollegiate debate has been en
tirely used up and there would be
no such prize this year were it not
for the generosity of Mr. James
Moffitt of the firm of Blake, Moffitt
& Towne, wholesale paper dealers
in San Francisco. Mr. Moffitt. has
offered a cash prize of two hundred
dollars to be divided as has been
customary with the Perkins prize.
Mr. Moffitt has a deep interest in
the two universities, having two
sons graduates of U. C.
