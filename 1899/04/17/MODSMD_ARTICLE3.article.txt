# STANFORD LOSES THE SERIES.
## Berkeley Captures the Second Baseball Game of the Season by Score of 9 to 7. 
### 
Stanford lost the second game
and the championship to Berkeley
Saturday. The game was won and
lost in the first three innings. Lan
agan was in poor condition and
could not muster any speed or
control his delivery until the
Berkeleyans had batted out a vic
tory. After the third inning he
settled down and pitched a mag
nificent game, striking out ten men
to his opponent's two.
The main feature of the game
was Swindell's catching. He put
up the best article of ball behind
the bat seen this year. His throw
ing to second was excellent, cutting
off several attempted steals.
The fielding of both teams was
at times ragged, Berkeley playing
the looser game. In the fourth in
ning Stanford rallied at the hat and
did good work,scoring several runs.
At the beginning of the fifth, with
the score '.) to 7 in favor of Berkeley,
it looked as though Stanford would
yet win—four innings in which to
make two runs to tie the score.
Every one smashed the ball and
drives were frequent, but they fell
into Berkeley pockets.
