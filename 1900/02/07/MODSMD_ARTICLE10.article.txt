# Announcements.
## 
### 
Vesper Service.— The vesper
service will be held in the chapel
Thursday at 4:30 p.m. Professor
Kip will lead.
New Books. —New books now at
the Bookstore are: Philip and Alex
ander of Macedon, by Hogarth; A
Victorian Anthology, by Stedman.
Sunday Sermon. — The chapel
sermon next Sunday morning will
be delivered by Professor Star buck.
His subject will be announced
later.
Tuesday Lecture. —Lieut. C. G.
Calkins, the navigating officer of
the "Olympia" at the Battle of Ma
nila, will talk on "Cromwell's
Quest for Empire."
"Quad" Benefit Postponed.—On
account of the illness of Ferris
Hartman, the comedian, the 1901
Quad benefit announced for Thurs
day has been indefinitely postponed.
Notice will be given later as to
when the entertainment will be
held.
Junior Farce. — The Class of
1901 offers a prize of $15.00 for the
most acceptable farce portraying
Stanford life. It should be of
three acts and capable of presenta
tion on the gymnasium stage The
Farce Committee meets February
20th, Tuesday, at 1:30 p.m., when
competitors are expected to present
at least two acts.
L. MacF. Bowman,
Chairman.
