# Announcements.
## 
### 
At Home. —Kappa Kappa Gam
ma will be at home Saturday even
ing, Feb. 10th.
Sunday Sermon.—Professor E.
D. Starbuck will speak Sunday
morning in the chapel, on " In The
Stillness."
Tuesday Lecture. —Lieut. C. G.
Calkins, the navigating officer of
the "Olympia" at the Battle of Ma
nila, will talk on "Cromwell's
Quest for Empire."
Faculty Ladies' Club. —There
will be a meeting of the Faculty
Ladies' Club at Mrs. Stillman's, on
Monday, Feb. 12th, at 3 o'clock.
Mrs. Comstock will address the
club, on u The History of the Na
ture-Study Movement in New
York State, as conducted by Cor
nell University."
Electrical Engineers.—Profes
sor Copeland will meet electrical
engineers and others who may wish
to go, in the Palace Hotel office at
9:30 a.m., Saturday, Feb. 10th, to
inspect electrical plants. Those
who intend going will kindly leave
their names at the Engineering
office.
Junior Farce. — The Class of
1901 offers a prize of $15.00 for the
most acceptable farce portraying
Stanford life. It should be of
three acts and capable of presenta
tion on the gymnasium stage The
Farce Committee meets February
20th, Tuesday, at 1:30 p.m., when
competitors are expected to present
at least two acts.
