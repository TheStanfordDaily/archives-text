# Announcements.
## 
### 
Girls' Glee. There will be no
meeting of the Girls' Glee until
Thursday afternoon.
Jessie A. Moore, Pres.
Tuesday Lecture. —Lieut. C. G.
Calkins, the navigating officer of
the "Olympia" at the Battle of Ma
nila, will talk on "Cromwell's
Quest for Empire."
Students' Guild. —The Board of
Directors and the Patronesses of
the Students' Guild will meet in
the History office Tuesday at 4:30
p.m.
Euphronia Picture. —The Eu
phronia society will meet at Frank
lin's studio Wednesday, February
11th, at 4:30 p.m., to have their
picture taken.
Varsity Glee Club. —The Glee
Club will meet for rehearsal in the
chapel Wednesday evening, Feb
ruary 12th, at 7
F. B. Riley, Leader.
Athletic Committee. — There
will be an important meeting of
the Athletic Committee Thursday
evening, February 15th, at 7:30 in
room 11 quad.
J. H. Coyerley, Chairman.
Inter-Society Debating League.
—There will be a meeting of the
executive committee of the Inter-
Society Debating League tomorrow
(Tuesday) at 1:15 p.m. in room 10
quad. Helen Svvett, Chairman.
Y. M. C. A.—Rev. H. E. Milnes
of Santa Clara, who is conducting
revival services in Palo Alto, will
address the Y. M. C. A. at 4:30
p.m. Tuesday, in room 23 quad.
A cordial invitation is extended to
all the men of the University.
Junior Farce. — The Class of
1901 offers a prize of $15.00 for the
most acceptable farce portraying
Stanford life. It should be of
three acts and capable of presenta
tion on the gymnasium stage The
Farce Committee meets February
20th, Tuesday, at 1:30 p.m., when
competitors are expected to present
at least two acts.
"Quad" Benefit Given Up.--On
account of an unexpected circum
stance, the Hartman entertainment
announced for last Thursday will
not be given. Tickets will be re
funded at the University Bookstore
if obtained at the postoffice. Those
obtained at Hall's Drugstore will
be refunded there.
H. A. Friedman.
