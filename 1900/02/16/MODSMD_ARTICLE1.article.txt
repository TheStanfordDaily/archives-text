# SENIORS VS. FRESHMEN.
## Final Game for Inter-Class Championship Monday.— Varsity Defeat of Santa Clara. 
### 
Next Monday the Seniors cross
bats with the Freshmen for the
Interclass baseball championship,
liy winning from the Sophomores
the youngsters qualified to meet the
Seniors, who defeated '01 in the
final game. The classes will oc
cupy different sections of the
bleachers where the class presidents
call them. The game will proba
bly be a close one, with chances
favoring '00, which will make
every endeavor to close its four
years' course by reasserting its title
as champion which it won in its
freshman year. The game will be
called at 3:30 p.m. on the campus
diamond. The price of admission
has been reduced from the usual
amount, twenty-five cents, to ten
cents, at which price the bleachers
will undoubtedly be full.
Captain Murphy of the Seniors
announces the line-up of '00 as fol
lows: Strohn, catcher; Lanagan,
pitcher; Dutton, first; Coffin, sec
ond; Longheed, short; Murphy
(captain ), third; Hawley, right
field; Braden, center-field; Cover
ley, left-field; Davis, substitute.
The Freshmen will line us as
follows: Horn (captain), catcher;
Hampson, pitcher; Traeger, first;
Lower.thai, second; Parker, short;
Brown, third; Moore, left; Copp,
center; Knight, right.
The contest between the Y r arsity
and Santa Clara College team yes
terday was won by Stanford by a
score of 6 to 8. The Santa Clarans
kept the game in their hands till
the seventh inning when Havens,
by a home-run, brought in four
men. While the Varsity played a
winning game, nine errors were
piled up against them as compared
to three made by their opponents.
The fielding was rather uncertain,
but the in-field played in fairly
good form. Lanagan held his po
sition down in good style, striking
out twelve men, while Kelley, who
should, be in better condition than
Lanagan, struck out but thirteen.
The score was as follows:
