# Announcements.
## 
### 
Sunday Sermon. — Rev. E. L.
Parsons of Menlo Park will preach
in the chapel Sunday morning.
Student Body Meeting.—There
will he a meeting in the chapel at
1:15 next Tuesday.
J. H. Coverley, Pres.
Greek Club.—The Greek Club
will meet at 7:15 Saturday night at
the home of Professor Fairclough.
A. T. Murray.
Philolexian. —Regular debate,
" Resolved, That the army canteen
should be abolished" ; affirmative,
White, '00, Roseberry, '02 ; nega
tive, Foster, '01, John, '03.
Saturday Night Club. —Regular
debate, " Resolved, That the em
ployer of the present day does not
sufficiently consider the health of
his employes affirmative, Miss
Cone; negative, Miss Davis.
1901 Class Picture.—The Jun
ior Class will have the picture for
the Annual taken at 4 p. m. Tues
day, March 13th., on the steps
of the museum. All those who
affiliate with the Class of 1901 are
requested to be there promptly.
Faculty Ladies' Club.—There
will be a meeting of the Faculty
Ladies' Club at Mrs. Sanford's on
Monday, March 12th, at 3 p.m.
Professor Stillman will speak in
formally on "The Manufacture of
Pottery." Specimens of Japanese
pottery will be shown as illustra
tions.
