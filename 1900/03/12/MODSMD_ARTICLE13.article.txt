With the showing made on the
track last Thursday we have every
reason for encouragement for good
results in the coming intercolle
giate meet. Although it is the
general opinion, both here and at
Berkeley, that the intercollegiate
field-day is merely a practice event
for the Berkeley team, there is at
present a determination among the
Stanford men to make the contest
interesting at least. With Thurs
day's results to judge from we have
reason for hope in the final out
come, and this, added to the ad
vantage of a meet on our own
grounds, should be sufficient to
bring out every man who has any
ability on the tfack, and to assure
to these men for the rest of the
season the hearty support of the
college.
