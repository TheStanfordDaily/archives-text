# STANFORD WINS.
## Second Intercollegiate Game Pulled Down by the Varsity. 
### 
The second, game of ball in the
intercollegiate series went to Stan
ford by a score of 10-7. The game
was an exciting one and closely
contested, though the first inning
looked like a walk-over for the Var
sity, who went to the bat first.
During this inning twelve men went
up before Kaarsburg and seven
runs were scored. California's
work was ragged and several costly
errors were responsible for the
heavy scoring. Later the Berkeley
men men took a brace and held the
Varsity down to three runs for the
remaining eight innings and ran
up a score of seven for themselves.
In the lastof the ninth inning Cal
ifornia filled the bases and left it
to Hamlin to end the game. This
he did by sending a fly to center,
which Murphy pulled in.
Lanagan pitched great ball,
striking out twelve men, while
Kaarsburg succeeded in striking
out but two. The Berkeley out
field put up a good game, covering
their ground in fine shape.
The concluding game of the series
will be played in San Francisco
April 28th. This game should draw
a large crowd, as both teams will
putin two weeks of hard practice to
bring them up to first-class con
dition.
