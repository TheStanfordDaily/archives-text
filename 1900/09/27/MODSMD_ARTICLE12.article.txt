# Announcements.
## 
### 
Chebs Club. —There will be a
meeting of those interested in chess
Friday at 1:30 in room 2 quad.
F. W. Bennett.
Nestoria. —The society will have
its literary session this week on
Friday instead of Saturday evening.
F. E. Thompson, President.
New Books.—The following have
been received at the Bookstore:
Willoughby's Nature of the State,
Laurie's Historical Survey of Pre-
Christian Education.
Stanford Volunteers. — There
will be a meeting of the Stanford
Volunteers Thursday evening at
7:30 in room 32 Encina.
M. Elliott, Secretary.
Note-books. —All new students
who handed in laboratory note
books for examination can have
them by calling at the Registrar's
office. If not called for, they will
be destroyed.
Junior Plugs.—All Juniors de
siring to order plugs will please
hand their names and size of hat
worn to either one of the following:
M. F. McCormick, VV. R. Hamilton,
or R. S. Fisher.
Faculty Ladies' Club. —A bus
iness meeting of the Faculty Ladies'
Club is called for Friday afternoon,
September 28th, at Mrs. Stillman's
at 3:30. It is urgently requested
that as many of the faculty ladies
attend as possible.
Student Body.— Persons with
bills against the Associated Stu
dents can have them paid tomor
row (Friday), the 28th, at room
128 Encina. from 3:30 to 4:30 and
8:30 to 9:30.
Henry H. Taylor, Treasurer.
Employment Bureau. —Students
who wish to do canvassing during
their vacations, and would like to
look over the propositions of some
Eastern firms, apply at Employ
ment Bureau, at office hours, room
20. Lloyd E. Harter.
Mandolin Club. —There will be
a meeting of the Mandolin Club
next Tuesday at 4:30 in room 33
to elect a leader, and for practice.
The following will report for prac
tice: Copp, Col Iyer, Dole, Powers,
and McCormick; also any others
who play the guitar.
Women's Athletics. —There will
be a meeting of the directors of the
Women's Athletic Association in
the Roble gymnasium Monday,
October Ist, at 4:30 p.m. Nomina
tions for Freshman directors and
appointment of committees on ten
nis and wheeling will be made
Christian Associations. —Miss
Everett will lead the meeting of the
Christian Associations in the
chapel, Sunday, Sept. 29th, at 7:30
p.m. The subject for discussion is
"The Relation of the Christ Life to
our Life Today." All students
who can sing or who enjoy singing
are reminded of the invitation to
meet with the music committee in
the chapel at 7 o'clock to practice
the hymns before the regular ser
vice.
Football Songs and Yell Com
petition.—The Executive Commit
tee has offered a prize of $10 for
this year's best football song, and
$2.50 for the best yell. Popular
airs preferred for the songs. All
copy, signed by a nom de plume,
must be presented to one of the fol
lowing committee by Tuesday
noon, October 9th
W. A. Morris,
J. F. English,
Phil Oilman.
