# Announcements.
## 
### 
At Home.—Dr. and Mrs. Still
man will be at home to the stu
dents and their friends Friday
evening, October sth.
New Books. —The following have
been received at the Bookstore:
Lee's The Microtomist's Vade-Me
curn, Lyon's Deutsche Grammatik.
Sunday Sermon. —Rev. George
G. Eldredge of St. John's Presby
terian Church, San Francisco, will
deliver the regular Sunday sermon.
Freshman Meeting.—There will
be an important meeting of the
1904 class at 4:30 Friday. Every
member is urgently requested to be
present. F. Roehr, Pres.
Junior Plugs.—All Juniors de
siring to order plugs will please
hand their names and size of hat
worn to either one of the following:
M. F. McCormick, W. R. Hamilton,
or R. S. Fisher.
Los Angeles Members.—There
will be a meeting of all Los Ange
les students tomorrow afternoon at
3:30 o'clock, room 1 quad, in the
interest of the constitutional amend
ment. C. M. Wardall,
Chairman.
Amendment Club.—All students
in the University from Monterey
county are requested to meet in
room 1 tomorrow, Friday, at 4
p.m., to arrange for the coming

campaign with regard to the Stan
ford Amendment.
Edward Berwick, Jr.,
Chairman.
Carnot Preliminaries. —Entries
to Carnot Preliminaries close Fri
day noon, Oct. sth. Hand names
to one of the following:
J. F. English,
C. 1). Scott,
M. C. DeCarli.
Glee Club Meeting.—The fol
lowing men will report in the
chapel Friday at 4:30 p.m.: First
tenor, Gay, Brown, Walker, Haw
ley, C. H. Baker; second tenor,
Hardy, Scoville, Dryer, Perrin,
Brown; first bass, Oilman, Cor
busier, Whitaker, Sherword, Rich
ards; second bass, Slemons, Jensen,
Firebaugh, Parkin, Thompson.
Debating Executive Commit
tee. —There will be a meeting of
the Debating Executive Committee
tomorrow, Friday, at 1:30 p.m.,
room 10 quad, for the purpose of
dividing Carnot entries into dif
ferent sections. These sections
must have their questions chosen
by Monday at 1:30 p.m., when
committee will meet to assign po
sitions and sides.
J. F. English, Pres.
