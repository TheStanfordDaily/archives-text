Five hundred and ninety-one
student body assessments have
been paid, which is nearly two
hundred more than were collected
up to this time last year. But this
represents not many over half of
the students. It seems to us that
we ought to be able to do consider
ably better than this. Every stu
dent is, or should be at least, more
than anxious to win the Thanks
giving day game, and he or she
should be willing to contribute the
small amount of one dollar toward
this end. There are several dona
tions and gifts asked from students
every year, and there are many in
our midst who cannot afford to
help them all along, but everyone
should be able to pay the small
student body assessment, when he
considers that this is the only
financial support asked from him
toward paying the expenses of foot
ball, track, baseball, tennis, and
the debates.
