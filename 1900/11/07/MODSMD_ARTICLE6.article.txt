# Announcements.
## 
### 
Students' Guild. —There will be
a meeting of the patronesses of the
Students' Guild Friday, November
9th, at 11 a. m.in the women's sit
ting room.
11303 Canes. —All those who have
not procured Sophomore canes may
do so by seeing \V. H. Shadburne,
room 116 Encina Hall, before
Thursday evening.
Sunday Sermon. —The regular
Sunday sermon on November 11th
will he delivered by the Rev.
George C. Adams of the First Con
gregational Church of San Fran
cisco.
Freshman Clabs Meeting.—
There will be a meeting of the Class
of 1904 Thursday at 1:15 p.m. in
the chapel to take action in regard
to procuring sweaters for the class
football team. F. Roehr.
Tuesday Evening Lecture. —Dr.
Jordan will deliver the regular bi
weekly lecture next Tuesday even
ing, November 13th, in the Assem
bly Hall. His subject will be
"Japan," and the lecture will be
illustrated with lantern slides.
Faculty Ladies' Club. —The
next meeting of the Faculty Ladies'
Club will be held at Mrs. M. A.
Kimball's, Waverly St., I'alo Alto,
on Monday, November 12th, at
three o'clock. Mrs. A. W. Smith
will give her impressions of the
Passion l'lay at Oberammergau.
