Woman's Employment Bureau.-
The Woman's Employment Bureau
has opened its office at room 20,
Roble Hall, and may be seen from
10:15 to 11:15 a.m., or from 1:30
to 2:30 p.m.
Address to University Women.
— Mr. Mott will address the
women of the University Saturday,
January 12th, at 3 p.m. Mrs.
Mary Roberts Smith will have
charge of the meeting.
Tuesday Evening Lecture.—
The regular University lecture will
be delivered by Professor Fair
olough, on the subject, "A Trip
Through Canada." The lecture will
be illustrated with lantern slides.
New Economics Courses. —Prof.
Curran will give the following new
courses for this semester: Eco
nomics 4, "Money," on Mondays
and Wednesdays, and Economics
G, "Legislation," on Tuesdays and
Thursdays.

According to the custom of societies
in other universities upon the oc
casion of Mr. Mott's visits, the
three literary societies, Nestoria,
Euphronia, and Philolexian, have
postponed their meetings one week.
