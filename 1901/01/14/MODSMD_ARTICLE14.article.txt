Club meets in the chapel at 4:30
p.m. Monday for reorganization
and a first rehearsal.
P. K. Gilman, Leader.
Baseball Notice. —All candi
dates for battery positions will re
port to me on the baseball dia
mond Wednesday afternoon.
W. H. Murphy, Coach.
Woman's Employment Bureau.-
The Woman's Employment Bureau
has opened its office at room 20,
Roble Hall, and may be seen from
10:15 to 11:15 a.m., or from 1:30
to 2:30 p.m.
Junior Class Meeting.—There
will be a meeting of the Junior
Class in room 50 Wednesday at
1:30 p.m. to nominate officers for
the present semester.
C. I)eW. Scott, President.
"Quad" Assessments. —All Jun
iors who have not paid their Quad
assessments are urged to pay them
at the class meeting on Wednesday.
M. F. McCormick,
Business Manager.
"Quad" Contest. —The time for
submitting copy in the Quad con
test has been extended to Friday,
January 25th, on which date all
manuscript must be in the hands
of the editor. M. E. McDougald.
Tuesday Evening Lecture. —
The regular University lecture will
be delivered by Professor Fair
clough, on the subject, "A Trip
Through Canada." The lecture will
be illustrated with lantern slides.
Physics 1. —Owing to an unus
ually large registration in this
course, there will be two sections
instead of one as was first an
nounced. The sections will meet
as follows: Section 1, laboratory
work from 1:30 to 4:30 Mondays
and Fridays; lecture, from 1:30 to
2:30 on Wednesdays. Section 2,
laboratory work from 1:30 to 4:30
Thursdays and Fridays; lecture,
from 1:30 to 2:30 on Wednesday.
It is desirable that all who can
register for Section 1.
F. J. Rogers.
