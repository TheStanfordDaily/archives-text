# Announcements.
## 
### 
History Department.—Students
affected by any of the changes in
History courses may further delay
filing study cards until Monday,
Jan. 28th.
Girl's Glee Club. —There will
be a meeting of the Girl's Glee
Club next Tuesday afternoon at
4:30 in the chapel.
Jessie A. Moore, President.
Orchestra Meeting. — There
will be a meeting of those who wish
to play in the Orchestra on Mon
day evening in the chapel at 7:30.
Arrangements will be made for the
semester.
Filing Study Cards Postponed.
—On account of a necessary read
justment of courses the tiling of
study cards may be delayed until
Wednesday, January 23d.
0. L. Elliott, Registrar.
Football Notice. —All men hav
ing football goods in their posses
sion belonging to the student body
will please deliver them at once to
W. M. McLeod. R. S. Fisher,
Acting Manager.
New Books. —The following have
been received at the Bookstore:
Waldan, Thoreau;The Englishman
at Home, Porritt; The American
Commonwealth. Bryce; Botany of
the Region of San Francisco Bay,
Greene.
Freshman Reception.—The reg
ular reception of the Y. M. C. A.
and Y. W. C. A. to Freshmen will
be given in Encina club room Fri
day evening, January 25th. A
short and enjoyable program will
be arranged.
"Quad" Assessments. — Junior
Quod assessments must be paid
at once in order to get photographs
in time. Collections will be made
at the voting place for class officers
on Wednesday.
M. F. McCormick,
Business Manager.
Debating Committee. —The De
bating Executive Committee will
meet tomorrow, room 11 quad, 1:15
p.m., to consider a challenge for de
bate from the University of Ne
braska. Every member should be
present. J. F. English,
President.
Freshman-Sophomore Debate.
— All members of the Freshman
Debating Society who intend to en
ter the preliminaries for the Fresh
man debating team must hand
their names to one of the under
signed before Friday noon, Jan.
25th. W. B. Chandler,
Frank Roehr,
M. A. Thomas,
Committee.
