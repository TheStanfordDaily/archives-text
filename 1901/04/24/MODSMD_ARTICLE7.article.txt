# Announcements.
## 
### 
Vesper Services. —Mr. Baker
will sing at the vesper services to
morrow.
Orchestra. —There will be a
meeting of the University Orchestra
tomorrow at 7:30 p.m. in the chapel.
Hygienk Club. —Thursday even
ing, room 134. Subject for general
discussion, "The value of the phys
ical measurements in relation to
gymnasium work."
There will be an important
meeting of the Athletic Committee
Thursday evening at 7:30 in room
11 quad. Every member should
be present.
Cecil M. Marhack, Pres.
Senior Farce. —There will be a
rehearsal of the Senior Farce to
morrow at 4:30 p.m. in the Assem
bly Hall. All members of the cast
and chorus should be present.
W. M. Erb, Stage Manager.
Student Employment. — Any
student wishing to do canvassing
work during the summer and who
would like to look over terms of
fered by firms may drop a postal
card to L. E. Harter.
The University Philological
Association will hold its next regu
lar meeting on Thursday, April
25th, at 2:30 o'clock in room 31.
Principal paper by Dr. Matzke, on
the oldest French version of Bo
ethius. Karl G. Rendtorff,
Secretary.
