Football Men. —All men expect
ing to play football should get their
suits at once at the training-house,
as only a few outfits are left.
Class of 1902. —There will be a
meeting of the Senior Class in the
chapel at 1:15 Wednesday.
A. E. Cooley, Pres.
English Ballads. —The class in
Ballads will meet Fridays at 8:30
p.m. in room 34 (not in the semi
nary room at 2:30, as announced).
Dr. Ewald Flugel.
Elementary Mathkmatics. —
Students who desire instruction in
Elementary Algebra or Plane Ge
ometry are requested to meet with
Miss Wright Wednesday or Thurs
day at 1:15 p.m. in room 13.
R. L. Green.
Glee Club.—There will be a
Glee Club try-out Thursday even
ing, September 12th. Any one who
has ever sung, or ever thought of
singing, is requested to come around
and "try-out." The meeting will
be held in the chapel at 7:30 p.m.
En Oilman, Leader.
University Orchestra.—All re
turning members of last year's Or
chestra and all others desirous of
joining in the organizatian of the
same are requested to meet in Ihe
chapel on Thursday evening, Sep
tember 12th, at 7:30 p.m. Bring
instruments and music stands.
W. Young.
Students' Employment Bureau.
—The General Secretary of the Y.
M. C. A. will keep permanent hours
in the Economics office, room 20
quad, from 10:15 to 11:15. It is
requested that all applications for
student help, and registration of
students for work, be made at that
time. All communications should
be addressed to
Lloyd E. Hakter,
Gen. Sec. Y. M. C. A.
