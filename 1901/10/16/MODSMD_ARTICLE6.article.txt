# Announcements.
## 
### 
Bookstore. —The following have
been received at the Bookstore: The
Cavalier, by George W. Cable; A
Day With a Tramp and Other Days,

by Walter A. Wyckoff; and Wild
Flowers of California, by M. E.
Parsons and M. W. Buck.
Education. —The recitation room
of Education 12 has been changed
from the chapel to room 1.
Syllabus Fees. — Syllabus fees
should be paid before November Ist
to avoid any additional charge.
At Home. —Mr. and Mrs. Still
man at home to the students and
their friends Friday evening, Oc
tober 18th.
Railroad TiOKETS.-Special rates
of $1 for round trip tickets to the
city Saturday may be had at the
Registrar's office.
Handball. —The Handball Com
mittee will meet at 7:15 Thursday
evening in room 180 Encina.
W. F. Dunn, Chairman.
Chess Club. —There will be an
important meeting of the Chess
Club in room 1 Wednesday, Octo
ber 16th, at 7:30 p.m.
N. C. Powers.
Football. —All football material
held by men not appearing regu
larly on the field must be turned in
at once. Harry J. Edwards,
Treasurer.
Advanced Degrees.—Candidates
who expect to come up for advanced
degrees in May, 1902, should tile
formal application at the Regis
trar's office not later than October
23d.
Debating Teams. — All those de
siring to enter the try-outs for this
semester's debating teams should
hand their names to H. C. Jones,
J. W. Cameron, A. J. Copp, Jr., or
E. W. Kice not later than Saturday,
October 19th.
Bookstore. —The following have
been received at the University
Bookstore: The Poetical Works of
John Milton, in 3 vols., edited by
David Masson; and an unusually
large and fine consignment of sta
tionery..
Special Rates. —A rate of $I.QO
for the round trip ticket to San
Francisco on Saturday, October
19th, the day of the Freshman
football game, has been offered by
the Southern Pacific Company.
Tickets are on' sale at the Palo Alto
office.
Uni v e rsit y Orch est r a. —There
will be an important business meet
ing of the Orchestra in the chapel
at 7 o'clock (not 7:30) on Thurs
day evening. Important matters
relating to the coming concert of
the Royal Italian Band, as well as
the matter of organization, will be
discussed. If a sufficient number
of members desire it there will be a
rehearsal immediately following
the business meeting. The largest
possible attendtnce is desired.
Plug Ugly Performance. — All
men of the Junior Class are ex
pected to participate in the Plug
dedication ceremony Friday even
ning, October 25th. Each one
must provide himself with a hood
made of black cloth, covering his
entire head, with the exception of
his face, and having horns "au
Mephistopheles." This is pre
requisite to a place in the perform
ance. Time of rehearsals will be
announced later.
Plug Ugly Committee.
