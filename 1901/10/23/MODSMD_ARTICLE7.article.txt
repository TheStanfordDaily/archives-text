# QUESTIONS ASSIGNED.
## Subjects for the Try-outs Preliminary to the Carnot Debate. 
### 
Below are published the subjects
for the Carnot preliminary debates,
the positions of the men, and the
schedule for try-outs:
First Debate — "Resolved, That
the policy of excluding Chinese
from all territory within the
boundaries of the United States
should be rigidly maintained."
(The United States is understood
to mean only the continental terri
tory.) Affirmative, Hitter, '04, and
McComish, '08. Negative, Brown,
'02, and Thompson, '03.
Second Debate —"Resolved, That
employers are justified in refusing
recognition to labor unions."
Affirmative, Everett, '03, and Rose
b/srry, '03. Negative, Spencer, '04,
and Williams, '02.
Third Debate —"Resolved, That
the jury system should be abol
ished." Affirmative, Loofbourow,
'02, and Jones, '02. Negative,
Ryan, '03, and Maloy, '03.
Fourth Debate —"Resolved, That
the principle of compulsory arbi
tration should be established in
disputes between capital and
labor." Affirmative, Scott, '02. and
Leistner, '04. Negative, Copp, '02,
and Page, '03.
Fifth Debate —"Resolved, That
Congress should prohibitjhe immi
gration into this country of all per
sons who cannot read and write
some language, dependents on qual
ified immigrants excepted." Affirm
ative, Rice, '02, and Hillings, '03.
Negative, Hill,'o3, and Thiriot, '04.
Sixth Debate —'"Resolved, That
Chinese immigrants should be ex
cluded from the United States
proper." Affirmative, A. S. Will
iams, '03, and Bacon, '03. Neg
ative, Farrar, '04, and Wood, '02.
Seventh Debate —"Resolved, That
the open advocacy of anarchy
should be punishable as a felony."
Affirmative, Dittmar, '03, and Cam
eron, '03. Negative, Winans, '02,
and Caldwell, '04.
Eighth Debate —"Resolved, That
it is economically disadvantageous
for the Uidted States to own pos
sessions in the tropics." Affirm
ative, Wagner, '02, and Roehr, '04.
Negative, Nangle, '03, and Wise
hart, '04.
Squads one, two, and three will
debate on November 14th; squads
four, five, and six on November
loth; and squads seven and eight
on November 16th. All the de
bates will be held on the evening
of the day scheduled, in the Uni
versity chapel.
