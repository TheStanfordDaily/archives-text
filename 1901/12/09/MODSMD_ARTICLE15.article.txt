# Announcements.
## 
### 
"Quad" Prizes. —The manage
ment of the 1908 Quad offers prizes
of ten dollars for the best poem and
ten dollars for the hest story. This
contest closes February 1, 1902.
Y. M. C. A. —The mid-week
meeting of the Y. M. C. A. will he
held tomorrow afternoon at 4:30
in room 21. Mr. E.*G. Lamb will
lead. All men of the Association
are urged to come out.
No English Club Meeting.— No
more meetings of the English Club
will he held this semester. The
one scheduled for tomorrow has
been postponed until after (he va
cation. C. A. Whitmore, Pres.
Notice to Seniors Expecting to
Teach. —Students graduating in
1902 who expect to teach are re
quested to meet representatives of
the Department of Education in the
chapel at 1:30 on Wednesday,
December 11th.
David S. Snedden.
Geological Society.—The regu
lar monthly meeting of the Geolog
ical Society will be held at the
Delta Upsilon house on Wednesday
evening at 8 o'clock. 11. 11. John
son will present a paper on "The
Geology of Shasta County."
E. 11. Nutter, Pres.
Cast-off Clothing Wanted. —
Stanford people remember Mrs.
Biillington Booth so kindly that we
are sure they will co-operate in any
movement to help in her work.
The Volunteers of America (more
commonly known as the Balling
ton Booth branch of the Salvation
Ann}') of San Jose is gathering in
old clothing of every kind to he
given to the poor at Christmas.
The Young Women's Christian As
sociation has agreed to help by
collecting such material on the
campus. It is asked that everyone
will look through their trunks and
closets and send such cast-off cloth
ing to room 54 Roble, within the
next few days. The Association
will pack and send them to San
Jose.
