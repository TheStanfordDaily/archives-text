# Intercollegiate Try-Out.
## 
### 
The date for the preliminary de
bates to Belect men for the Inter
collegiate finals has not as yet been
decided. The subjects of the try
outs and the positions of the men
are:
First debate — "Resolved, That
it is unwise for the United States to
continue to oppose the extension of
foreign control over any territory
of the Western Hemisphere."
Affirmative, McCormick, Riste,and
Firebaugh; negative, Scott, Cam
eron, and Wagner.
Second debate—" Resolved, That
the adoption of the Fifteenth
Amendment was unwise." Affirm
ative, Lockwood, Hull, and Thomp
son; negative, Maloy, Billings, and
Schwartzlander.
Third debate —" Resolved, That
municipalities should own and op
erate their own street railways."
Affirmative, Thiriot, Mori, and
Page; negative, Everett, A. Jones,
and Dittmar.
Fourth debate —" Resolved, That
all executive functions should be
concentrated in the mayor, and
that his appointments should not
require confirmation." Affirma
tive, Bacon, Kimball, and Loof
bourow; negative, Williams, Rice,
and Edwards.
Fifth debate —"Canteen Ques
tion," Wintler, Favre, McComish,
Caldwell, Roehr and Leistner.
Sixth debate — "Resolved, That
the United States should maintain
a system of Bounties and Subsi
dies on the protection of its mer
chant marine." Affirmative, Leib,
H. C. Jones, and Capp; negative,
Hitter, Spencer, and Nangle.
