Senior Assessments. —At a re
cent meeting of the Senior class a
$2.50 assessment was levied upon
each member of the class. It is of
extreme importance to a successful
carrying out of the plans for the
semester that this assessment be
paid at once, and every Senior is
urged to be prompt in payment.
The treasurer will be in room 23
quad on Wednesday and Friday
afternoons of this week from 1 to 2
o'clock. Metnbers will confer a
great favor by paying fit that time,
if possible. Frank H. Boren,
