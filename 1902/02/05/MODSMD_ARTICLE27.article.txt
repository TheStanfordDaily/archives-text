At Home. — Kappa Kappa Gam
ma will be at home Saturday even
ing, February Bth.
Orchestra Rehearsal.—The Or
chestra will meet at 7:30 .p.m.
Thursday in the chapel.
Vesper Service. —Mrs. Bolton
C. Brown will play two violin solos
at Thursday's vesper service. Pro
fessor Murray will lead. Chapel,
4:30 p.m.
University Inn Meal Hours. —
Saturday, February, Ist the follow
ing meal hours go into effect:
Breakfast, 7:30-8:45 a.m.; lunch,
12:15-1:30 p.m.; dinner, 5:45-7:00
p.m., the last the same as before.
Date of Contest Extended. —
The contest for tiie prize story and
poem in the 1903 Quad has been
extended until February 28th.
R. E. Renaud, Editor.
Women's Employment Bureau.—
Miss Kip may be found at Roble
Hall at meal hours, or at Roble
gymnasium Monday, Wednesday,
and Friday afternoon, 2:30 to 5:30.
Faculty Women's Club. — The
next meeting of the Faculty Wo
men's Club will be at Mrs. O. L.
Elliott's, 12 Alvarado row, on Mon
day, February 10th, at 3 o'clock.
Pirates of Penzance. — Rehear
sals for the Pirates of Penzance
will be held on the following even
ings: At the Assembly Hall, cm,in
pus, February (i, 8, 10, 12, 13.
Varsity Football Men. — All
Varsity football men are expected
to have their pictures taken at
Franklin's gallery for the Annual
immediately.
Harry J. Edwards, Treas.
Tennis Manager.—T. E. Steph
enson will hereafter act as tennis
manager. All entries for tourna
ments, etc., must be arranged
through him.
Harry .1. Edwards, Treas.
Senior Committee. —There will
be an important meeting of the
chairmen of the Hall, Promenade,
and Encina Reception committees
Thursday afternoon at 4:30 in
room 21. Frank Hinman.
"Quad" Pictures.—All Juniors
who have not as yet returned the
proof of the picture which they
wish placed in the Quad should
send it immediately to Franklin
Chester Naramore, Bus. Mgr.
Senior Assessments. —At a re
cent meeting of the Senior class a

$2.50 assessment was levied upon
each member of the class. It is of
extreme importance to a successful
carrying out of the plans for the
semester that this assessment be
paid at once, and every Senior is
urged to be prompt in payment.
The treasurer will be in room 23
quad on Wednesday and Friday
afternoons of this week from 1 to 2
o'clock. Members will confer a
great favor by paying at that time,
if possible. Frank H. Borkn,
Treasurer 1902.
