-—A hockey training-table has
been started at Harvard.
—Forty-two men entered the
trials for the swimming and water
polo teams at Harvard.
—Black sweaters with the insig
nia "II 2d,'' were awarded to
second eleven men at Harvard last
fall.
—The University of Nebraska is
planning to send a football team
East next fall to play Harvard and
Princeton, and possibly Yale.—
Yale News.
Dr. William G. Anderson,
physical director of Yale Univer
sity, has just completed a new in
vention for measuring the effect of
different kinds of exercise upon
the human brain. —Daily Califor
nian.
—Brown University is at present
enthusiastic over the prospects of a
new Y. M. C. A. building which,
when completed, will satisfy the
social and religious needs of the in
stitution. Plans have been made
for a $00,000 building.—Chicago
Weekly.
—Yale defeated Brown by a
score of 11 to 1 at hockey. The
Yale News says of the contest: "A
noteworthy feature of the game
was the entire absence of rough
play. The players on the Brown
team were much the heavier, but
were neither as clever on their
skates nor as good at team work."
