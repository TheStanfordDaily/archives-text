Geological Lkctuke Postponed.
—The lecture on the geology of the
western part of the Great liasin, by

Mr. H. W. Turner, announced for
next Wednesday evening, is post
poned until Saturday evening at
7:30. E. H. Nutter.
Antigone Chorus. — Antigone
Chorus meets Wednesday at 4:30
p.m. in the chapel.
Caps and Gowns. —The Senior
girls' caps and gowns have been
received at the Haberdasher.
Foil and Mask. —Foil and Mask
will meet Wednesday evening at
7:30 p.m. on the second floor of the
gymnasium.
Tennis. —Entries for Saturday's
tournament, with the fifty-cent fee,
should he left with me before Fri
day noon. T. E. Stephenson.
Pirates of Penzance. —Rehears-
als for the Pirates of Penzance will
he held on the following evenings
at the Assembly Hall, campus:
February 12, 14, 17, 15), '20.
Tuesday Evening Lecture.—
Tuesday evening lecture for Febru
ary 18th will be delivered by Mr.
Seward of the English department
on "Student Life at Oxford."
Glee Club Meeting.—There will
be a meeting of the Glee Club in
chapel Wediiesday evening at 7:30
sharp. Cleveland H. Baker,
Leader.
Date of Contest Extended. —
The contest for the prize story and
poem in the 1903 Quad has been
extended until February 28th.
11. E. Renaud, Editor.
Creditors ok Encina Club. —All
persons having accounts against
Encina Club are requested to hand
in bills before February 25th.
R. P. McLaughlin, Pres.
Executive Committee Meeting.
—The weekly meeting of the Exec
utive Committee will be held
Wednesday at 7 p.m. in room 13.
M. F. McCormick, Chairman.

matter concerning fraternities to

bly Hall Wednesday evening at
7:30. Full attendance of all ex

perative. Louis W. Weer,
Director.

are requested to meet me in the
