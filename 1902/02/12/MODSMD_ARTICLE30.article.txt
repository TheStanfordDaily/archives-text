# Announcements.

Orchestra. —Orchestra meeting
Thursday night at 7:30 in the
chapel.
Caps and Gowns. —The Senior
girls' caps and gowns have been
received at the Haberdasher.
Journal Club in Education. —
Next regular meeting will be held
at l)r. Starbuck's on Thursday
evening, February 13th.
Tennis. —Entries for Saturday's
tournament, with the fifty-cent fee,
should be left with me before Fri
day noon. T. E. Stephenson.
Antigone Chorus. —The Antig
one chorus will meet at the chapel
Thursday evening at 7:45. They
will be accompanied by the orches
tra.
Vesper Service. — At the vesper
service to be held in the chapel at
4:20 tomorrow afternoon Mrs. Tho
burn and Miss Avery will sing a
duet.
Pirates of Penzance. —Rehears-
als for the Pirates of Penzance will
be held on the following evenings
at the Assembly Hall, campus:
February 14, 17, 19, 20.
Tuesday Evening Lecture. —
Tuesday evening lecture for Febru
ary 18th will be delivered by Mr.
Seward of the English department
on "Student Life at Oxford."
Bookstore. —The following books
have been received: Determinate
Mineralogy and Blowpipe Analysis,
Brush (Penfield revised), and Die
versum kene Glocke —Hauptmann.
Date of Contest Extended. —
The contest for the prize story and
poem in the 1903 Quad has been
extended until February 28th.
R. E. Renaud, Editor.
Creditors of Enuina Club. —All
persons having accounts against
Encina Club are requested to band
in bills before February 25th.
R. P. McLaughlin, Pres.
Notice to Fraternities. — All
matter concerning fraternities to
be inserted in the Quad must be
handed in before the end of this
week. R. E. Renaud.
Baseball Suits.—All baseball
suits taken out for class games
must be turned in at the training
house immediately.
Harry J. Edwards, Treas.
Baptist Social. — The Baptist
Young People of Palo Alto and the
University will give a social to
their friends at Mrs. Loder's, 42(i
Florence street, next Friday even
ing.
Return Mask. —The person who
took the baseball mask the day of
the Sophomore-Freshman game
will please return the same to the
training-house.
Harry .J. Edwards, Treas.
Antigone Chorus. —All members
are requested to meet me in the
Library Seminary room before the
the next Monday rehearsal at one
of the following hours: Thursday,
1:30 p.m. Music-books should be
brought. 11. R. Fairclough.
