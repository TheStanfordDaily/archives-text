Greek Sculpture. — Hereafter
slides will be connection
with this course.
Vesper Service. — Professor
Johnston will lead the Vesper Ser
vice in the chapel tomorrow at 4:30.
Syllabus Fees. —Syllabus and,
laboratory fees must be paid by
March Ist, or an additional charge
will be made.
Library Closed Saturday.—The
Library will be closed Saturday,
February '22d. Books may be
taken out Friday at 4:30 p.m.
Opera Posters. —The posters for
the Pirates of Penzance, printed on
heavy cardboard, may be obtained
at the Bookstore for ten cents.
Date of Contest Extended. —
The contest for the prize story and
poem in the 1903 Quad has been
extended until February 28th.
Sophomore Debaters. — Sopho
more try-out debate is postponed
until February 28th. The debate
will be held in the chapel at 7:30.
Antigone Chorus —There will be
an important meeting of the An
tigone chorus with the orchestra
Thursday at 7:45 p.m. in the
chapel.
Pirates of Penzance. —Rehears-
als for the Pirates of Penzance will
be held on the following evenings
at the Assembly Hall, campus:
February 20.
Zoology Club. — The Zoology
Club will meet at the residence of
Dr. Gilbert next Monday evening.
Mrs. Bell will talk on the Quanti
tative Study of Variation.
Freshman Track Candidates. —
All Freshmen who expect to try
for the Freshman track team
should appear on the track at once.
W. A. Dole, Athletic Mgr.
Rehearsals of Zaragueta.— El
Primer Ensayo de Zaragueta ten
dia lugar sin falta en el ''Assembly
Hall" el jueves 20 a las 4:30. Se
usaran los libros. H. C. Brown.
Senior Finance Committee. —
There will be a meeting of the
Finance Committee of the Class of
1902 Thursday at 1:15, room 11
quad. E. H. Nutter, Chairman.
Senior Girls. —It is desired that
the Senior girls procure their caps
and gowns as soon as possible in
order to begin wearing them Fri
day.
Committee on Caps and Gowns.
Antigone Chorus. —Training in
the Greek will be given at the fol
lowing hours this week: Thursday,
1:30 p.m.; Friday, 11:15 a.m. and
1:30 p.m. Bring music-books.
H. R. Fairclough.
History.—Professor Show is un
able to resume his lectures for the
present. His classes, however, will
meet on Thursday at the regular
hours to receive notices concerning
collateral reading and examina
tions.
Pirates of Penzance Orchestra.
—The members of the Pirates of
Penzance orchestra are requested
to be present at the dress rehearsal
Thursday evening. The atten
dance at the last two rehearsals has
been irregular, and it is
imperative that the entire orches
tra be present in order to perfect
the production.
Louis Wm. Weer, Director.

farces to be submitted for the Jun
ior Farce Competition must be in
before February 25th. A prize of
$25 is offered for the one adjudged
the best. Manuscript may be

handed to any member of the Com
mittee or mailed to the chairman,
Box 32, Stanford University.
