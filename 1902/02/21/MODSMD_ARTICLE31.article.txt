# Announcements.
## 
### 
Tuesday Evening Leotuhe. —No
lecture will be given for Tuesday
evening.
Festival Choir. —The Festival
Choir will meet Sunday at 4:30 in
the chapel.
Library Closed Saturday.—The
Library will be closed Saturday,
February 22d.
At Home Postponed. — Kappa
Alpha Theta will be at home March
Ist instead of February 22d.
Syllabus Fees. —Syllabus and
laboratory fees must be paid by
March Ist, or an additional charge
will be made.
Opera Posters. —The posters for
the Pirates of Penzance, printed on
heavy cardboard, may be obtained
at the Bookstore for ten cents.
Dr. Halsey Preaches. —The ser
mon on Sunday morning will be
given by Prof. John J. Halsey.
Subject, "The Heavenly Vision."
Sophomore Debaters. — Sopho
more try-out debate is postponed
until February 28th. The debate
will be held in the chapel at 7:30.
Zoology Club. — The Zoology
Club will meet at the residence of
Dr. Gilbert next Monday evening.
Mrs. Bell will talk on the Quanti
tative Study of Variation.
Notice. — Bicycle riders and pe
destrians are requested to refrain
from crossing the turf in front of
the Memorial Arch.
J. C. Branner, Acting Pres.
Freshman Track Candidates. —
All Freshmen who expect to try
for the Freshman track team
should appear on the track at once.
Antigone Rehearsals. — The
Acting Chorus will meet in the
Assembly Hall Monday evening
next, at 7 o'clock. The first chorus
should be committed to memory.
Y. W. C. A. Meeting.—"The
Chinese Girls in America," is the
subject of the Mission Study class
of the Y. W. C. A., which will meet
in the women's sitting-room at 10
a.m. Saturday, February 22d. Mrs.
Critchlow will lead.
Junior Farce Competition.—All
farces to be submitted for the Jun
ior Farce Competition must lie in
before February 25th. A prize of
$25 is offered for the one adjudged
the best. Manuscript may be
handed to any member of the Com
mittee or mailed to the chairman,
Box 32, Stanford University.
