# Announcements.

Tuesday Evening Lecture. —No
lecture will be given for Tuesday
evening.
General Choiu. —The General
Choir meets Tuesday at 4:30 p.m.
in the Assembly Hall.
Antigone Ciiokus. —The Acting
Chorus of Antigone meets Wednes
day at 7:30 p.m. in the Assembly
Hall.
Y. W. C. A. Meeting.—Miss Ross
leads the Y. W. C. A. Tuesday at
4:30 in the chapel. Subject,
"Prayer."
Syllabus Fees. —Syllabus and
laboratory fees must be paid by
March Ist, or an additional charge
will be made.
Y. M. C. A. Meeting. — The
Young Men's meeting tomorrow
will be led by Harry F. Henderson.
Subject for discussion, "Prayer."
Club Tertulia. —El comite ejec
tivo del Club Tertulia se reune el
martes a las cuartro y media.
Carlos E. Cortes, Presidente.
Spanish Play.—El Ensayo de
Zaragiieta tendra lugar en el cuar
to 133 el martes 25 a las 4:30.
H. C. Brown.
Sophomore Debaters. — Sopho
more try-out debate is postponed
until February 28th. The debate
will be held in the chapel at 7:30.
Notice. —Bicycle riders and pe
destrians are requested to refrain
from crossing the turf in front of
the Memorial Arch.
J. C. Branner, Acting Pres.
English Club. — The English
Club meets hereafter on Wednes
day instead of Tuesday evenings.
There will be a meeting next
Wednesday, place to be announced
later.
Orchestra Picture. —All mem
bers of the University Orchestra
must be present at Franklin's stu
dio on Wednesday at 1:15 sharp to
have picture taken for Quad. Bring
instruments.
Junior Farce Competition.—All
farces to be submitted for the Jun
ior Farce Competition must be in
before February 25th. A prize of
$25 is offered for the one adjudged
the best. Manuscript may be
handed to any member of the Com
mittee or mailed to the chairman,
Box 32, Stanford University.
Work in Oratory.—An effort is
going to be made to form an ora
torical association at Stanford. The
purpose of the organization is to
drill men to become efficient pub
lic speakers, to furnish a thorough
training for future oratorical con
tests, and to give an adequate prep
aration to those who wish to try
for intercollegiate debating teams
in future years. The idea for the
present is to put the association on
a firm basis, so as to be ready to
receive new incoming students next
year. Those interested are request
ed to come to room 23 next Friday,
February 28th, at 4:30, and discuss
plans for the work.
