Foil and Mask. —The Foil and
Musk Club will meet in the gym
nasium Wednesday at 7:30 p.m.
Freshman Meeting.—There will
he a meeting of the Freshman class
Thursday afternoon in the chapel
at 1:15 o'clock.
Glee Club. —There will he a
meeting of the (ilee Cluh in the
chapel Wednesday at 8 o'clock.
C. 11. Haker, Leader.
Women's League.—The Women's
League will meet Friday afternoon,

March 14th, from 4 to 6, in Roble
Hall. There will be a short mu
sical program.
Y. W. C. A. Entertainment. —
Tickets are on sale at the Bookstore
and at Jordan's Cash Store in Palo
Alto for the Y. W. C. A. entertain
ment next Friday evening.
Pikate Flag.—The person or
persons who, by accident or other
wise, took from the Assembly Mall
the pirate flag and a pair of gloves,
may leave them at the training
house immediately.
Senior Clabs Meeting.—There
will be an important meeting of the
Senior class in the chapel Wednes
day afternoon at 1:15. The ques
tion of men wearing caps and
gowns will be discussed.
F. Hinman, Pres.
1903 Meeting.—There will be a
meeting of the Junior class in the
chapel Friday, March 13th, at 1:15,
to hear the report of the committee
on athletic caps and to take action
thereon. W. 1. Traeger, Pres.
English Club.-—The English
Club will meet at 7:45 tomorrow
evening with iMiss Brown at the
home of Dr. lilliott, 12 Alvarado.
Professor Newcomer will read from
Mr. Dooley, and Mr. Byrne will
give some selections.
English 13. — The meeting of
this course for March 12th will be
omitted, out of respect to the mem
ory of Mr. Cameron, who was ap
pointed to speak on that day. All
appointments will lie set forward
one week. R. M. Alden.
Y. M. C. A. Reception.—The
men of the University, both mem
bers of the Y. M. C. A. and their
friends, are invited to attend a re
ception to be tendered the Advisory
Hoard of the Y. M. C. A. on Fri
day evening, March 14th, from 8 to
10, at. our home, corner of Middle- j
field road and Channing avenue,!
Palo Alto. Hugh A. Moran,
President Y. M. C. A. I
