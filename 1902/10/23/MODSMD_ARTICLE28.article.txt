# Announcements.
## 
### 
Sophomore Caps.—The remain
der of the '05 caps have arrived
and may be procured at the Haber
dasher, Palo Alto.
New Books.—The following have
been received at the Bookstore:
Lord's Livy; Die Versunkene
Glocke, Hauptmann.
Sunday Sermon. — Ilev. E. E.
Baker of the First Presbyterian
Church of Oakland will preach in
the chapel on Sunday morning.
Football Suits. —Those suits not
in use must be turned in at once.
They are needed.
Harry J. Edwards.
Sophomore Minstrels. —Import-
ant meeting and rehearsal of end
men and barkers at the Phi Delta
Theta house Friday at 7:30 p.m.
Tuesday Evening Lecture. —
Mr. Eugene Soule, instructor in
Manual Training, will give the reg
ular Tuesday evening lecture, on
the subject "Leland Stanford in
California.''
\V()MEN'sATHLETIC ASSOCIATION. —
A meeting of the Women's Athletic
Association in the chapel Friday,
Oct. 24th, 1:16. Important busi
ness. All interested in athletics
are cordially invited.
University Fees. —All labora
tory and syllabus fees are due
November Ist. If not paid by that
time an additional fee of $2 will be
charged. 0. L. Elliott,
Registrar.
University Directory. — The
University Directory is now being
put into type. All students who
have changed their place of resi
dence since filing study-card are re
quested to report such change to
the Registrar at once.
Descriptive Geometry.—Recita
tions begin Monday, Oct. 27th, and
Tuesday, Oct. 28th. There will be
two recitation sections: (I) M. W.
9:15; (II) T. Th. 10:15. All who
can, without conflict, will join Sec
tion (I). Notice of rooms will be
posted in room 137 by Saturday.
Text, Watson's "Descriptive Geom
etry." Drafting will begin Nov.
10th, taking the place of Linear
Drawing and Lettering.
