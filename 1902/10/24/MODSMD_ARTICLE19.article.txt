# Announcements.
## 
### 
Kappa Alpha Theta.—At home]
Saturday evening, October 25th.
Sunday Sermon. — Rev. E. E.
Baker of the First Presbyterian
Church of Oakland will preach in
the chapel on Sunday morning.
Football Suits. —Those suits not
in use must be turned in at once.
They are needed.
Harry J. Edwards.
New Books. —The following have
been received at the University
Bookstore: Rossetti's I'oems, W.
Rossetti ed.; El Si De Las Minas,
Moratins.
University Lecture. —Dr. Chas.
R. Brown, of Oakland, will lecture
in Assembly Ilall, Monday at 1:30,
on "The Greatest Man of the Nine
teenth Century."
Ushers. —L. C. Hawley will have
charge of the ushering at the big
game. All applications for jobs
must be made to him.
Harry Edwards, Treas.
"Quad" Bills. —All bills against
the 1903 Quad may be handed to
any member of the committee.
They must be in at once.
T. E. Stephenson, Chairman.
Tuesday Evening Lecture. —
Mr. Eugene Soule, instructor in
Manual Training, will give the reg
ular Tuesday evening lecture, on
tho subject "Leland Stanford in
California.''
University Fees. —All labora
tory and syllabus fees are due
November Ist. If not paid by that
time an additional fee of $2 will be
charged. 0. L. Elliott,
Registrar.
University Directory. — The
University Directory is now being
put into type. All students who
have changed their place of resi
dence since filing study-card are re
quested to report such change to
the Registrar at once.
Rooms for Descriptive Geome
try.—Section I at 9:15 Wednes
days in room 52, and Fridays in
room 10. Section II at 10:15
Tuesdays and Thursdays in room
50. First meeting of Section I
Wednesday, Oct. 29 th; and of
Section II Tuesday, Oct. 28th.
Descriptive drafting will be started
Monday, Nov. 1 Oth, on which day
at 11:15 a.m. the final examination
of work in Civil Engineering la
will close.
