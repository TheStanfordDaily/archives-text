# Announcements.
## 
### 
Foil and Mask.—There will be
no meeting Thursday evening.
University Orchestra.— No re
hearsal this week on account of
rooters' meeting in the chapel.
Sophomore Minstrels. — There
will be a rehearsal of the Minstrel
Show tomorrow night in the As
sembly Hall at 7:30. All partici
pants must be present.
New Books. —The following have
been received at the Bookstore:
Old China and Other Essays of
Elia, Lamb; Prose Tales, Poe; Der
Probekandedat, Dreyer.
Rooters' Hats may be obtained
at the Haberdasher's on and after
Friday, Oct. 31st. The committee
requests all students to obtain hats
before the rally of Thursday, No
vember 6th.
Quad Photos. —Those Juniors
who have had pictures taken please
select as soon as possible the proofs
they want printed.
A. S. Henley, Manager.
Y. W. C. A. At Home.—The
young ladies of the Y. \V. C. A.
Club will be at home to their
friends on the afternoon and even
ing of Saturday, Nov. Ist, instead
of Nov. Bth.
University Fees. —All labora
tory and syllabus fees are due
November Ist. If not paid by that
time an additional fee of $2 will be
charged. O. L. Elliott,
Registrar.
University Philological Asso
ciation. —Next regular meeting on
Thursday, Oct. 30th,at.2:30 o'clock,
in room 31. Principal paper, Dr.
B. O. Foster, "The Latin Supine'';
minor communication, Professor
O. M. Johnston, "The French Ad
verb tote jor." W. A. Cooper,
Secretary.
