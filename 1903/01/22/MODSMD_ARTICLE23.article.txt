# Announcements.
## 
### 
Caps and Gowns —Have arrived
at the Haberdasher.
Press Club —Will meet at Zeta
Psi house on Saturday evening.
Glee Club —Will meet in the
chapel Friday afternoon at 4:30.
C. H. Baker.
Band Meeting.—Members of the
University Band, report at room
31 quad on Thursday at 4:30.
Y. W. C. A. Treasurer. —The
office hours of the treasurer of the
Association are 1 to 1:30 Tuesday
in the women's sitting-room.
Sophomore Candidates. — '05
baseball team will meet at the
training-house at 4:00 o'clock Fri
day afternoon to elect captain.
"Quad" Competition.— Quad lit
erary competition is extended from
January 15th to February sth.
F. H. Fowler, Assoc. Ed.
Freshman Baseball. — The
Freshman baseball squad will
meet in the chapel at 1:15 tomor
row to elect a captain for class
games.
University Women. —The women
of the University are requested to
meet in the chapel Friday, January
23d, at 1:15 to discuss a question
of interest to all.
Elizabeth A. Peckham.
"Quad" Pictures. —All Juniors
who expect to have their pictures
in the Quad must have them taken
before February Ist. This is posi
tively the last date on which they
will be accepted.
Women's League.—The regular
monthly meeting of the Women's
League will be held in Roble Hall
on Friday, Jan. 23d, from 4 to 6
o'clock. New members will be cor
dially welcomed. Dues for the sec
ond semester may be paid to the
treasurer, Mrs. John C. Branner.
Second Senior Election. —At
the first election for president of
1903 no candidate received a
majority of the votes cast. A
second election will take place next
Friday, January 23d, from 11:15 to
1:30. W. G. Morrison and L. G.
Moore will be voted for.
R. O. Hadley, Pres. '03.
President's Bulletin.—Students
in entering the quad are asked to
refrain from crossing the grass of
the oval or the lawns in front of

the buildings. It is further urged
that the rights of the gardener, Mr.
Lynch, and of the custodian of the
grounds, Mr. Atkinson, to insist on
the compliance with this rule be
properly respected. They are
charged to report violations of the
rule to this ofiice.
David Starr Jordan,
President.
