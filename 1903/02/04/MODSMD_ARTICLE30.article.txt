ingham, England, is a guest of R.
G. King, '06.
— \V. 11. Shadbourne, '03, whs
married in San Francisco on Sat
urday evening.
—The bronze doors for the main
entrance of Memorial Church have
arrived and are being putin place.
—Joseph Marx, '01, who is em
ployed in the California Powder
Works at Pinole, has been visiting
oh the campus a few days.
—The bricklayers resumed work
on the University buildings Mon
day. The strike of the San Fran
cisco Bricklayers' Union is not yet
settled, but Contractor McGilvray
is paying the wages asked for.
—At the meeting of the Senior
Week committee yesterday after
noon, an outline for the festivities
of Commencement Week was partly
planned. The most important ac
tion was taken when it was deter
mined to omit the Senior Smoker
to the Alumni, a step taken for
financial reasons. The Class of
1902 was the first to inaugurate the
custom of a smoker to the alumni.
