# Announcements.
## 
### 
English Play Book.—ln re
sponse to inquiries the following
announcement is made: On Febru
ary 4th, acting on the suggestion
of a member of the committee in
charge of the English play, the
Bookstore arranged for a large
supply of copies of the Temple edi
tion of the "Knight of the Burning
Pestle." Not having received or
heard of the books, on Thursday,
the 2Gth, a telegram was sent to

New York regarding the matter.
An answer has been received stat
ing that the books were Bhipped by
book express on February 25th.
They should be received on the
3d or 4th of March, in ample time
for the play.
Syllabus Fees. — All syllabus
and laboratory fees are due March
10, and if not paid by then an addi
tional charge of $2 will be made.
Boating Club Constitution. —
Signatures of those wishing to be
come members of the Boating Club
will be received on the quad tomor
row from 10:15 to 11:15.
"Quad" Illustration. — All
kinds of photographs of the Uni
versity and josh photos suitable for
use in the Quad may be sent either
to Isaac Russell or B. R. Cocks.
Intersociety Debating Commit
tee. —There will be a meeting of
the executive committee of the
Intersociety Debating League Mon
day, March 2d, at 1:15, in room 10,
for the purpose of arranging for
the Intercollegiate finals. Those
who will participate in the finals
are requested to attend.
J. H. Page, Pres.
Senior Assessment.—The $4.00
assessment must be paid by April
Ist. The following are authorized
collectors: Miss Sue Bird, W. G.
Morrison, W. B. Barnhisel, Miss
Elizabeth Peckham, Miss Jane
Evans, Miss Mary Hendrick, C. E.
Waite, H. P. Kuhn and R. O. Had
ley; and the members of the Fi
nance Committee—N. C. Grider,
C. W. Thomas, W. W. Copp.
Geo. A. Scoville, Treas. '03.
