# Announcements.

Faculty Women's Club. —The
final meeting of the season is post
poned until further notice.
Girls' Glee and Mandolin
Cluhs —Will meet at Roble at 3
o'clock Wednesday afternoon.
Club Tertulia. —Members who
wish to see "Zaragueta" and have
not yet made arrangements, notify
Prof. Carlos Bransby of Berkeley
immediately.
Applied Mathematics. — Exam
inations for removing deficiencies
in first semester's work will be giv
en in room 11 at 2:30, April 27,
courses 2 and 5.
Woman's Edition Staff—Will
report to Miss Alice Kimball, '04,
managing editor, between 8:15 and
10:15 Wednesday morning at the
Daily Pat,o Alto oflice.
Boat Club. — April dues may
now be paid. The' charter roll will
be open this week to those paying
March and April dues. Gaither
and Beach carry receipt-books.
Associated Students.— There
will be a meeting of the Associated
Students at 1:15 Wednesday, for
the purpose of nominating oflicers
for the ensuing year.
C. F. Dittmar.
French Play.—The following
will report at Donaghho's Wednes
day afternoon at 2:30: Mrs. Bouil
ler, Misses Mourot, de Luna, and
Abbott, W. J. Stack, W. G..Schulte,
11. Brown, and L. G. Levy.
Women's Lioague.—The last so
cial meeting of the Women's League
this year will be held Friday after
noon from 4 to (> in Roble Ilall.
The Girls' Glee Club will sing.
There should be a large attendance
of members.

sences were excused from the exam
inations in History 15, March

portunity to make up the same
Saturday, April 25th, at 9 a.m., in
the History seminary room in the
Library. Max Farrand.
