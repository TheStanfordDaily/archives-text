# Prohibition Orations.
## 
### 
The intercollegiate Prohibition
oratorical contest takes place to
morrow evening in the Y. M. C. A.
hall, Oakland. Palmer's orchestra
will play. Dr. Barton is to give a
talk while the judges are out.
There will be a business meeting of
the college associations to arrange
for next year's work. In the draw
ing of lots the order of speakers re
sulted as follows: Oakland first,
California second, University of
the Pacific fourth, Stanford fifth.
The orations have already been
sent in to be judged on thought
and composition. A different set
of judges will pass on the delivery.
H. S. Percival, '03, will represent
Stanford.
