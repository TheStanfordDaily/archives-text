Sophomore Comedy Committee
—Meets in English office at 5 p.m.
Wednesday.
Sophomoke Hats. —Leave orders
for fezes with the class treasurer at
room 67 Encina Hall.
Juniou Girls. —Those who wish
mortar-boards leave orders at the
Bookstore before Thursday noon.
French Club.—Will meet at
the Kappa Kappa Gamma house
on Thursday evening at 8 o'clock.
John E. Matzke.
Freshman Debaters. — Squad
2 meets in room 89 Encina
Wednesday at 8 p.m. Squad 5
meets in room 8 Encina at same
time.
Spanish Club —Meeting at the
Sequoia oflice at 7:45 Wednesday
evening, and from there proceed to
the Fonda Mexicana, where supper
is ordered at 9:15.
Sophomore Fezes. —Sophomores
are reminded that orders for fezes
can be given no later than Thurs
day noon. Orders and deposits
should he handed to
S. T. Jordan, Treasurer.
Orchestra Practice. —The Or
chestra will meet in the chapel
Wednesday at 7:30 p.m. All the
extra parts have come, so there
will be copies for everyone.
Arthur Scott Brook.
New Books.—The following have
been received at the Bookstore:
The Gate to the Anabasis, Gleason;
Geological Maps; Vorstadtgeschich
ten, Heinrich Seidel; Lang's Vision
of Piers the Plowman, Kate M.
Warren.
Scholarship Notice. —Students
who have fallen behind seriously
in their work owing to sickness or
other valid excuse should confer
promptly with the chairman of the
committee cn scholarship, room
443. Oflice hours, 1:30 to 2:30
daily.
Girls' Basket-Ball. —Any wo
men students who desire to play
basket-ball with the ltoble gymna
sium squad will hand their names
to Miss Helen Salisbury and ap
pear on the field at 4:45 Thursday
afternoon. Business meeting of
old squad girls at 4:30 of same
afternoon.
University Directory.—All stu
dents who have changed their
roomß in the halls or their street
address since filing study-cards are
requested to report such changes at
the Registrar's oflice immediately,
in order that the new Directory
may be as accurate as possible.
O. L. Elliott, Registrar.
University Philological Asso
ciation. —At the first regular meet
ing of the year, to be held on
Thursday, October Ist, at 2:30, in
room 31, Prof. J. E. Matzke will
present a paper on "A Neglected
Source of Corneille's Horace."
After the reading and discussion of
this paper the association will elect
officers for the new year.
W. A. Cooper, Secretary.
