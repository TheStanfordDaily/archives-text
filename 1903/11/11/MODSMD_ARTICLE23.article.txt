# Announcements.
## 
### 
Rooters' Hats —Will be on sale
Thursday at the Haberdasher's.
Tennis Players.—Leave on the
bulletin-board at the new court
the scores of your matches.
Library Closed —Saturday, No
vember 14th. Books may be
drawn out for home use after 2:30
Friday.
Baseball Practice — Will be
omitted Thursday on account of
football practice. Regular practice
next Tuesday.
Normal Bible Class —Room 472
at 3:30 Thursday. Professor Show
will speak on "The study and
Teaching of Bible History."
Elementary Spanish — Section
111, 11:15. Take for Friday lesson
32 in grammar; write sentences on
page 202. S. Smith.
Football Ushers —Must meet
Saturday at 10:30 sharp at Rich
mond grounds. A considerable
amount of preparatory work must
be done at that time.
H. W. Bell.
Y. M. C. A. Dues. —Subscriptions
may be paid to Hugh A. Mo ran,
'04, F. E. Ulrich, '06, C. L. Duncan,
'04, George Thomas, '05, S. S.
Smith, '07, H. F. Coolidge, '04,
Royce R. Long, '05, H. F. Hender
son, '06, and A. T. Griffin, '06.
Law Association Address —Each
member of the association is enti
tled to a copy of the address de
livered last May by Mr. Wheeler,
and can obtain a copy by calling
on the secretary-treasurer at the
Law library. B. P. Oakford.
Freshman Assessment — Should
be paid at once to one of the fol
lowing: J. Leonard, 78 Encina; J.
F. Biennan, College Terrace; E. P.
Stott, Zeta Psi house; MissM.E.
Turner, 22 Roble Hall. The money
is needed at once to pay for the
football sweaters.
Recommendations for Teachers.
—Several calls for teachers for
next term have already been re
ceived. Students who wish posi
tions are requested to see at once
Miss Bristol, secretary of the com
mittee on recommendation of
teachers.
Examinations Postponed.—The
government examinations for male
teachers for the Philippines have
been postponed from November
18th and 19th to November 27th
and 28th. These examinations
will be held in San Francisco,
Marysville, Fresno, and Los Ange
les.
Collegiate Alumnae. —The As
sociation of Collegiate Alumnae
hopes that the alumnae, and senior
and junior women of Stanford who
attend the football game Saturday
will find time to visit the tea-room
at 131 Post street, room 9 Someone
will be there entire day to welcome
guests. Katherine Chandler,
Elise W. Graupner, President.
• Secretary.
