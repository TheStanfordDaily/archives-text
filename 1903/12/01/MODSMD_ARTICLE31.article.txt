1905 "Quad" Committer. —Meet
in room 71 Encina, Thursday at
7 p.m. Chairman.

Freshman Football Team.
—Sweaters may be had at gymnas
ium any time tomorrow.
Cercle Francais—Reunion du
Club Mardi soir chez Mme. P6ck
Lincoln and Ramona, Palo Alto.
New Book.—The following has
been received at the Bookstore:
Neuenglisches Lesebuch, Ewald
Fliigel.
Football Men.—All suits must
be turned in at once. I shall be in
my oflice every day.
D. V. Cowde'n.
English Club. —Meeting at the
Delta Tau Delta house at 8 o'clock
Wednesday. Members are asked
to be punctual.
Christmas ''Chaparral."—Copy
for Christmas issue will bo received
until December Bth. Drawings and
light verse are especially desired.
Student Body Meeting —On
Wednesday at 1:15 in the chapel.
Nominations will be made for
editor-in-chief of the Daily Palo
Alto for next semester.
"Sequoia" Prizes.—The German
contest will close December 10th.
Competing manuscripts must be in
the hands of the assistant in charge
or left with the editor on or before
that date.
Second Team and Substitutes.
—Measures for sweaters will be
taken at gymnasium at 3 o'clock
Thursday. A full list of men en
titled to wear them will be pub
lished tomorrow.
Donations oe Clothing.—The
Y. W. C. A. has received several
requests for clothing. All persons
in Palo Alto or on the campus who
have any articles of clothing which
they are willing to donate,
are asked to send their names
and addresses to Box 5, campus.
University Philological Assoc
iation. — Meeting room 31, Thurs
day, December 3d, at 2:30 p.m.
Program—Principal paper: Pro
fessor Elmore, "The Subjunctive in
the so-called Restrictive Quod
Claußes." Minor Communica
tions: Professor Rice, "The Ety
mology of French aller, Provencal
anar, Spanish andar, Italian an
dare, etc."; Dr. Foster, "Interpre
tation of Gellius vi, 7."
Medical Notice.—A number of
cases of ton si 1 i tie have developed
among students suffering from
"colds" contracted during the rec
ent unsettled weather. In sev
eral instances these cases are
of marked severity, and as
tonsilitis may at times be con
sidered to a certain degree contag
ious, all cases of nose or throat
"colds" should be reported to me
or to one of the physcians of the
community. W. F. Snow.
