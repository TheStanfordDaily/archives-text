Seniors.—All Seniors who wish
lo rent caps and gowns for
Commencement should leave their
orders at the Bookstore at once.
Seniors. — Assessments will l>e

H. L. Hamilton, Treasurer.
Baskball Tkam —Will meet at

11. B. Ball.
Senior Opera Rehearsals. —
Assembly Hall, Monday, Tuesday,
and Wednesday nights at 7, for
principals; 7:30, for principals and
chorus.
Tickets for Opera— Will be
placed on sale Monday at 9:15 on
the quad and at the Stanford
Pharmacy in Palo Alto. After
Monday the sale will be transferred
from the quad to the Bookstore.
Prices, $1.00, 75c, and 50e.
Women's Athletic Association.
— Election of officers for next
semester will be held Mondav, May
9th, from 9:15 to 12:15, in the wo
men's rest-room on ihe quad.
Committee on Recommendation
ok Teachers. —Students who desire
to do tutoring during the summer
are asked to see the secretary at
once. O. L. Elliott, Chairman.
Combined Musical Clubs —Will
meet for election of officers of both
individual and combined clubs in
the chapel at 4:30, Monday.
W. H. LAN AG AN.
Law Association. — Address by
Professor Ames, Assembly Hall,
Friday, May 13th, 2:30. Reception
in Law building, 4to 6. The Uni
versity community is invited.
J. T. Burcham, President.
Claims Against Student Body.
—Those having claims against, or
owing money to the student body,
will attend to the same promptly.
I will be in my oflice in the gym
nasium all this week.
D. V. Cowden, Treasurer.
Training-Table Dues. —All pay
ments due from track and football
men must be made by Wednesday,
May 11th. D. V. Cowden,
Treasurer.
Applied Mathematics.— Atten
tion is called to the fact that
students deficient in any course in
Applied Mathematics can not be
admitted to courses of the follow
ing year in Applied Mathematics
nor to courses 2 and 3 in Engineer
ing. Examinations for the remov
al of conditions and incompletes
will be given on the first registra
tion day at the opening of the fall
semester.
