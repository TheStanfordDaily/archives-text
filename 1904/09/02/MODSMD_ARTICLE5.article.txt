# Will Do Work on Census.
## 
### 
During the summer Prof. S. J.
McLean of the Economics depart
ment has been engaged in a study
of the railroads of the Pacific Slope,
the results of which will appear in
the volume of "Wealth, Debt, and
Taxation" of the next census. Pro
fessor McLean's work has been to
determine as far as possible the ap
proximate value of all railroad
properties of the Pacific Slope
states. Within six weeks he ex
pects to have the result of his
labors in final form and ready to
forward to the Census Bureau at
Washington. In this work Pro
fessor McLean has been associated
with many of the leading authori
ties of America on economics, not
able among them being Professor
Dixon of Dartmouth College.
