Gymnasium Club —Will meet at
7 Friday in the Gymnasium ofiice.
"Chaparral" Board —Will meet
Saturday morning at 10 in room
179 Encina Hall.
History Syllabus Fees —Must
be paid during week of Sept. 12th.
Office hours posted on History bul
letin-board.
Junior Women's Meeting.—
There will be a meeting of the Jun
ior women in the chapel at 1:15 Fri
day, Sept. 16th.
New Books. —The following books
have been received at the Book

store: Andrews' History of Eng
land; Edser, Heat, for advanced
students; Gide, Principles of Polit
ical Economy; Remsen, Organic
Chemistry; Spaulding's Football
Guide, 1904.
Mechanics of Materials. —
Classes will be held as originally
scheduled, on Monday, Wednesday,
and Friday, in room 50.
Stanford Boat Club —Will meet
Tuesday, September 20th, at 7:30
in room 31.
H. G. Butterfield, Pres.
Tennis Players—May become
members of the Tennis Club by
handing their applications to the
undersigned.
J. F. Cassell, Secretary.
History 21.—Members of my
advanced section will please report
to me not later than Friday, Sep
tember 16th, for assignment.
C. A. Duniway.
History.—Blue-books handed in
by students in History 1 and 4 b
during last semester will be de
stroyed if not called for before Sep
tember 16th. H. L. Cannon.
Women's League—Will meet Fri
day, Sept. 16th, from 4 to 6, at the
home of Mrs. Jordan. All Univer
sity women, especially the new stu
dents, are cordially invited to be
present.
Treasurer's Hours. —Those wish
ing to pay their student body as
sessment, or transact any other bus
iness, will find me in my office in
Encina gymnasium from 1 to 2
every day. R. W. Barrett.
Plug Ugly.—Prize of $15, with
$10 added by individual subscrib
ers, is offered for best product. All
manuscripts must be submitted to
the committee not later than Octo
ber 10th. W. H. B. Fowler.
Prize Football Song.—The com
petition for the prize football song
will close October 12th, songs to be
handed in to A. L. Trowbridge, '05,
M. H. Thorpe, '05, or A. W. Hooper,
'05. The first prize will be $10
and the second prize $5.
Boating Men. —All those inter
ested in intercollegiate rowing are
requested to see any one of the
following men in regard to joining
the Stanford Boat Club: H. G. But
terfield, '05, W. J. Morin, '05, or F.
Zimmerman, '07. W. H. Dole.
Freshman I)ebaters.-All Fresh
men interested in debating meet in
room 11 at 8 Friday, September
16th. It will be especially neces
sary for all who intend entering for
the Freshman-Sophomore debate
to be present. A. Sherriffs,
Pres. Debating League.
Laboratory Fees.—All labor
atory fees are now due and pay
able at the Business Office of
the University. If not paid on
or before October 7th, an additional
charge of two dollars will be made.
If not paid on or before November
7th, registration will be cancelled.
In filling out the blanks for the
payment of fees, the student must,
in every case, give the number of
his registration card.
Romanic Languages: Spanish.—
Provided at least six students sig
nify their intention to take more ad
vanced work in Spanish, I am ready
to organize classes in Classical Span
ish (Course 14) and Advanced
Spanish Composition (Course 15).
Hours can be arranged to suit.
Names should be left at the office,
room 242, or by mail, not later than
Saturday evening, Sept. 17th. P.O.
box 105, Stanford University.
John E. Matzke.

Laboratory Fees.—All labor
atory fees are now due and pay
able at the Business Office of
the University. If not paid on
or before October 7th, an additional
charge of two dollars will be made.
If not paid on or before November
7th, registration will be cancelled.
In filling out the blanks for the
payment of fees, the student must,
in every case, give the number of
his registration card.
