# Announcements.
## 
### 
At Home. —Kappa Alpha Theta
will be at home next Saturday night.
Football Tickets.—No season
tickets will he issued.
R. W. Barrett, Treas.
Combined Musical Clubs —Will
practice Monday, at 7:30 in the
chapel. W. H. Lanagan.
A. W. Hooper.
Women's Tennis.—All women in
terested in tennis are requested to
meet in room 10, Monday, Septem
ber 26th, at 1:15.
Mabel Ray, Capt.
Registration. — Registration of
undergraduates and special stu
dents will not he permitted later
than October Ist. 0. L. Elliott,
Registrar.
Descriptive Geometry Exami
nation. —9 to 12 Saturday, October
Ist, room 62. For removal of con
ditions of December, 1903, and
May, 1904. J. C. L. Fish.
Plug Ugly.—Prize of $15, with
$10 added by individual subscrib
ers, is offered for best product. All
manuscripts must he submitted to
the committee not later than Octo
ber 10th. W. H. B. Fowler.
French Examination. —An ex
amination to remove conditions in
Elementary French will be held in
the Romanic Languages building
Saturday, October Ist, at 9:30.
C. Searles.
University Band. —Will meet
Saturday morning at the Haber
dasher's to have measurements for
uniforms taken. Clarinets meet at
9:00, cornets 9:30, altos and piccolos
10:00, trombones and baritones
10:30, basses and drums 11:00. It

is important that measurements
be in to-morrow,as hat size must be
wired to Cincinnati, Ohio, at once.
Change of Studies. — Friday,
September 30th, will be the last
day on which new subjects may be
taken up, except upon payment of
a special fee of $2. O. L. Elliott,
Registrar.
Student Labor. —The following
positions are listed at the Y. M.
C. A. Employment Bureau: (1)
Work by the hour in Palo Alto;
(2) work for room in Palo Alto;
(3) work for board in College Ter
race; (4) work for cash at campus;
(5) posing at studio.
Prize Football Song.—The com
petition for the prize football song
will close October 12th, songs to be
handed in to A. L. Trowbridge, '05,
M. H. Thorpe, '05, or A. W. Hooper,
'05. The first prize will be $10
and the second prize $5. A prize
of $5 is offered for the best yell.
Laboratory Fees. —All labor
atory fees are now due and pay
able at the Business Office of
the University. If not paid on
or before October 7th, an additional
charge of two dollars will lie made.
If not paid on or before November
7th, registration will be cancelled.
In filling out the blanks for the
payment of fees, the student must,
in every case, give the number of
his registration card.
