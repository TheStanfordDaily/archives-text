# Announcements.
## 
### 
Juniors. —The plugs have arrived
and are now ready for distribution
at the Haberdasher's.
Boat Club Committee. —Meet-
ing of play committee postponed.
M. M. Steakns.
Chess Club —Will meet Satur
day at 7:30 in room 129 Encina
Hall. Meeting open to all inter
ested in chess, and entries for fall
tournament will be made.
Football Men. —Those who do
not intend to remain on the foot
ball squad will return their suits
as soon as possible. I will be in
my office every day from 1 to 2 to
receive them. R. W. Barrett.
New Books. —The following new
books have arrived at the Book
store: Brown, Colloquial German;
Cremona, Second edition, Elements
of Projective Geometry; Jordan
and Kellogg, Animal Life; Fraser
and Squair, Exercises Accompany
ing French Grammar.
Junior Class. —The '06 Quad
assessment is now due. I will be
in room 14, on Tuesdays from
11:15 to 12:15, and on Thursdays
from 1:30 to 2:30, for the purpose
of collecting. Assessments may
also be paid at room 65 Encina
Hall. J. L. Maloy.
Sophomore Hats. — Those who
ordered their hats from L. M. Ed
wards may obtain them at room
201 from 12:15 to 1:30 to-morrow.
Hats ordered from T. L. Everson,
J. C. MacFarland, G. O. Spurrier,
A. B. Packard, or J. M. Ward may
be obtained from the man with
whom the order was placed.
Descriptive Geometry.—Work
begins November Ist, before which
those concerned who are not now
taking Linear Drawing should ar
range for sections and desks with
Associate Professor J. C. L. Fish at
room 136, M. T. 1:30 to 4:30. He
fore beginning this work each stu
dent must have the required text
book, of which the supply now at
the bookstore is sufficient for only
one half the class. When this sup
ply is exhausted, copies can be ob
tained by placing personal orders
at the bookstore, but these will re
quire two weeks for filling. See
notice in room 137.
