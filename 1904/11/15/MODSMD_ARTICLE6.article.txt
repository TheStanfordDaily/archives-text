# ORGANIZE STANFORD CLUB.
## Sixty Alumni in Los Angeles Effect a Permanent Association in the South. 
### 
Sixty Stanford men banquetted
the evening of the intercollegiate
game in Los Angeles. A perma
nent Stanford Club was organized.
A committee consisting of Guy
Cochran, '96, F. B. Braden, '00, W.
I. Traeger, ex-'O3, W. F. Dunn, '04,
A. J. Copp, Jr., '02, J. F. Cooper,
'04, and G. W. Dryer, '02, was ap
pointed to have charge of organiza
tion and direct club affairs for the
ensuing year. There was much
enthusiasm over reports of the
game. A telegram of congratula
tion was sent to the team.
B. F. Bledsoe, '96, acted as toast
master. Among the speakers were
C. K. Field, '95, L. H. Gilmore,'94,
S. B. Osborne, '97, Rea Smith, '99,
Chester Thomas, '98, Fred Braden,
'00, B. E. Page, '99, Chester Magee,
'95, and W. F. Dunn, '04. Field's
talk was characteristic and the hit
of the evening.
