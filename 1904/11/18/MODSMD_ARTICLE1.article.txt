# TRACE MEET TO-MORROW.
## Annual Fall Field-Day Will Be Held on the Oval at 10 O'clock. 
### 
To-morrow morning at 10 o'clock
on the oval will take place the first
track meet of the year. The meet
will be in the form of a try-out
among the men who have been do
ing wotk on the track since the
middle of last September. This
number includes some forty men,
of whom about half are new men.
Two of the track men have been ex
cused on account of the pressure of
college work, but all others who
have been out on the track this sea
son will take part in to-morrow's
meet.
As this meet will be the only
chance this Bemester to see and
judge the new track material, it is
hoped that a large crowd will at
tend. The admission will be free.

events will be the 220-yard dash,
the quarter-mile, and the two-mile.
The men who take part in these
runs are all evenly matched, and
represent some of the best material

now on the track. With the excep
tion of the two-mile, which will be
shortened to five laps, and the mile,
which will be reduced to three laps,
the races will be run in regulation
distances.

Probably the most interesting
events will be the 220-yard dash,
the quarter-mile, and the two-mile.
The men who take part in these
runs are all evenly matched, and
represent some of the best material

now on the track. With the excep
tion of the two-mile, which will be
shortened to five laps, and the mile,
which will be reduced to three laps,
the races will be run in regulation
distances.
To-morrow morning at 10 o'clock
on the oval will take place the first
track meet of the year. The meet
will be in the form of a try-out
among the men who have been do
ing wotk on the track since the
middle of last September. This
number includes some forty men,
of whom about half are new men.
Two of the track men have been ex
cused on account of the pressure of
college work, but all others who
have been out on the track this sea
son will take part in to-morrow's
meet.
As this meet will be the only
chance this Bemester to see and
judge the new track material, it is
hoped that a large crowd will at
tend. The admission will be free.
