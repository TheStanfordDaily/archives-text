# Instructor in Metallurgy.
## 
### 
G. Howard Clevenger has been
appointed assistant in the Metal
lurgy department and will assume
his duties there at once. Clevenger
graduated in 1901 from the South
Dakota School of Mines, and re
ceived the degree of Master of Arts
from Columbia in 1903. Since then
he has had practical experience in
mining and metallurgy, being su
perintendent of the Horse Shoe
mines at Terry, S. D. He has also
written a number of papers on
scientific subjects.
