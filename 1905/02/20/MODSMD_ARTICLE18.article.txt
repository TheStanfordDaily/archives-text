# TO ENTER FINAL TRY-OUT.
## Six Debaters Chosen to Compete with Old Men for Intercollegiate Teams. 
### 
i tedious try-out held in
pel Friday evening in
which twenty-three candidates par
ticipated, Professor A. B. Show, In
structor A. M. Cathcart and H. M.
Lewis, '04, acting as judges, selected
Miss M. F. Balcomb, '06, E. A.
Cunha, '06, B. C. Dey, '05, J. L.
Maloy, '06, C. S. Osgood, '06, and
P. D. Swing, '05, as the six best
speakers of the evening. This con
test was held for the purpose of
thinning out the candidates for the
intercollegiate debating teams. The
six debaters chosen will enter an
other try-out later with A. M. Dib
ble, '05, O. C. Spencer, '04, W. L.
Blair, '05, A. Sherriffs, '05, R. G.
Barnett, '05, D. D. Sales, '06, and
A. R. Fletcher, '05, who have pre
viously served on some Varsity
team, and from this number will
be selected eight who will consti
tute the teams which will meet
California and Nevada.
Most of the speakers chosen have
served on debating teams previous
to this contest. Miss Balcomb iB
the first woman to make an inter
collegiate final for several years.
She is a forceful and logical speaker
and represented Stanford in the
Pacific Coast prohibition contest
last spring.
Following are the speakers and
questions as they were arranged by
squads:
First squad — Question, "Re
solved, That it would be expedient
for the United States to adopt an
income tax." Affirmative, J. \V.
Maloy, '07, G. D. Gibson, '06, E. A.
Pier,'o7; negative, J. F. Brennan,
'07.
Second squad—Question, "Re
solved, That Chinese should be ad
mitted to the United States under
the general immigration laws at
present in force." Affirmative,
Miss M. F. Balcomb, '06, C. S. Os
good, '06, E. Klette, sp.; negative,
P. D. Swing, '05, B. B. Houston,'os.
Third squad — Question, "Re
solved, That the present system of
trial by jury should be abolished."
Affirmative, W. C. Shelton, '08, R.
Fountain, '05, G. C. Halsey, '08,
N. P. Bryan, '07; negative, J. L.
Maloy, '06, M. M. Stearns, '06.
Fourth squad—Question, "Re
solved, That the United States
•hould resist by force, if necessary,
any further attempt at coloniza
tion in South America by Euro
pean powers." Affirmative, E. A.
Cunha, '06, E. E. Miller, '05, C. A.
Beardsley, '06; negative, F. A.
Fisher, '07, W. R. Taft, '06, B. C.
Dey, '05.
