# AMENDMENTS VOTED UPON AND OFFICERS FOR NEXT YEAR NAMED.
## Proposal to Abolish Athletic Committee and Change Form of the Executive Committee Defeated. 
### 
AT the Bemi-annual student body
meeting held in the chapel to
day officers for the ensuing year
were nominated. In addition, the
students voted upon the proposed
amendments drawn up by the sub
committee of the Executive Com
mittee, consisting of A. L. Trow
bridge, '05, W. E. Crawford, '05,
and E. Y. Pomeroy, '05. The
amendments were read by E. Y.
Pomeroy, secretary of the Associ
ated Students, and then taken up
one by one and voted upon.
There was a lively discussion
when Article 111, Section 2, was
brought up, regarding the personnel
of the Executive Committee. An
amendment to the amendment
striking out the Students' Guild
passed, but the whole amendment
to the constitution then failed to
pass. A 8 a consequence the Execu
tive Committee will be composed of
members from the different classes
as before.
By the amendment to Article 11,
Section 5, of the constitution, the
treasurer is empowered to employ
such assistants as he deems neces
sary, excepting ticket collectors,
who are to be appointed by the
Executive Committee.
The Executive Committee will
appoint ticket collectors for all
games, meets, and entertainments
held under the auspices of the
association, with the exception of
the Varsity football game.
Section 1 of Article IV of the by
laws, regarding revenues, was
adopted. By the adoption of this
amendment the annual student
body assessment has been increased
to one dollar each semester. All
the other amendments to the by
laws passed.

AT the Bemi-annual student body
meeting held in the chapel to
day officers for the ensuing year
were nominated. In addition, the
students voted upon the proposed
amendments drawn up by the sub
committee of the Executive Com
mittee, consisting of A. L. Trow
bridge, '05, W. E. Crawford, '05,
and E. Y. Pomeroy, '05. The
amendments were read by E. Y.
Pomeroy, secretary of the Associ
ated Students, and then taken up
one by one and voted upon.
There was a lively discussion
when Article 111, Section 2, was
brought up, regarding the personnel
of the Executive Committee. An
amendment to the amendment
striking out the Students' Guild
passed, but the whole amendment
to the constitution then failed to
pass. A 8 a consequence the Execu
tive Committee will be composed of
members from the different classes
as before.
