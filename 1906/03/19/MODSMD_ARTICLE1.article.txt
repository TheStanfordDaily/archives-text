# PILES UP BIG SCORE
## VARSITY MAKES THIRTY RUNS AGAINST SOLDIERS. Fort McDowell Plays Very Poor Ball, Making Total of Fifteen Errors. 
### 
By the largest Bcore made on
the Varsity diamond this season,
Stanford defeated the Fort McDow
ell team last Saturday afternoon.
Eight innings of play left the Var
sity on the long end of a 30 to 2
score. The soldiers did everything
that a good baseball team should
not do, piling up fifteen errors and
allowing eighteen hits and eleven
stolen bases. Fenton and Dudley
did the best hitting, the former
making five and the latter four hits
out of five times up. Fenton also
stole home from third on the pi tell
er's wind-up. Both Sales and Thei
le pitched good ball, the former
allowing no hits for the first five
innings. In the seventh, a base on
balls and two two-baggers brought
in two runs. The score follows:
