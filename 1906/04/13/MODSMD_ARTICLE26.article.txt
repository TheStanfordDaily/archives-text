# ST. MARY'S WINS GAME
## VARSITY LOSES 9 TO 8 IN TEN INNINGS. Sales, Theile and O'Banion Are Hammered Hard.—Chalmers Badly Hurt. 
### 
Stanford lost to St. Mary's yes
terday afternoon in a close ten
inning game !>y the score of 9 to 8.
Both teamH hit the ball hard, St.
Mary's sending Sales and Theile to
the bench, and Stanford knocking
O'Banion out of the box in the sec
ond, with seven hits and six runs.
Witmer and McGregor finished out
the game. Chalmers made a bril
liant running catch in left field,
but later wrenched the ligaments
of bis ankle, sliding into first base,
so severely that he may be out of
to-morrow's game with California.
In the tenth, with two men,
Haley hit a fast grounder down the
third-base line. Kenton made a
brilliant stop, but threw wild to
first, Haley going to second. Brady
then singled to center field, scoring
the winning run.
