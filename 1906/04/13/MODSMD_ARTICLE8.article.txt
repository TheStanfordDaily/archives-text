To-morrow afternoon the Varsity
baseball team will meet the nine
from the University of California
on the diamond at the State Uni
versity, in the second game of the
intercollegiate series. It is scarce
ly necessary to impress upon the
student body the necessity of a
good attendance to support the ef
forts of the baseball men. Stan
ford has never failed to support her
athletic and debating teams loyal
ly by sending lar e delegations
when the contests have been held
on the Berkeley campus. To-mor
row more than ever is it necessary
that the student body send a liberal
representation to cheer the Cardi
nal team. The best way to insure
victory to-morrow, and with that,
end the baseball series, is to send a
large number of rooters to Berkeley.
