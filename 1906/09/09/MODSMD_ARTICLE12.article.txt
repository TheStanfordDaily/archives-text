# Announcements.
## 
### 
University Orchestra. —All old
men are to report at the gymnasium
at 4:30 Saturday to rehearse for the
Freshman Reception.
E. G. Shiebley, '08.
Entrance I, English Composi
tion, Deficiences. —The examina
tion postponed from last May for
students in entrance English 1, to
have been held last Saturday, iB
placed for Saturday, September Bth,
at 10, room 200.
Student Employment Bureau. —
Students desiring positions in Palo
Alto or on the campus, waiting on
table, doing gardening or house
work for room,board or cash,should
apply at once at the office in the
Sequoia building.
Civil Engineering,^—'Two-hour
men will report for work at 6:30
a.m., Saturday. Bring drafting
tools. M. C. Burr, '07.
Civil Engineering Society.—
Active members will meet in room
80, Encina, Monday evening at 8,
for transaction of business.
M. C. Burr,'o7, Secretary.
English 6. —All sections of Eng
lish 6 will unite for the special ex
amination for conditioned students.
Examination will be held in room
212, on Saturday afternoon, Sep
tember Bth, at 2 o'clock.
University Band —Will meet
Monday, September 10th, at 7:30
o'clock in room 1 on the quad. All
men desirous of trying out for a
position are requested to attend.
Guy Knupp, '07.
Faculty Women's Club —Will
meet at 3 o'clock Monday, Sept.
10th, at home of Mrs. Hopkins, 305
Lincoln Ave. Wives of new mem
bers of faculty are cordially invited.
