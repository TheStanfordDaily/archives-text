# Announcements.
## 
### 
Members of the Graduating
Class —Are charged $2.50 each for
diplomas. This amount must be
paid at the Treasurer's office not
later than 1 o'clock September 15th.
O. L. Elliott,
Registrar.
Senior Women. —All women of
1907 desiring caps and gowns leave
order at the Bookstore on or before
September 19th.
Men's Chorus. —Dr. Blodgett
will meet all men interested in
chorus work in the chapel Thurs
day evening, September 12th, at
7:30.
T. Barkelew, '08.
Senior Reception. — President
and Mrs. Jordan will be at home to
members of the class of 1906, grad
uates taking advanced degrees, the
faculty of the University and their
families, and visiting alumni, on
Saturday afternoon, September
15th, from 4 to 6.
Auditing Committee, '09, —Will
meet at the Delta Kappa Epsilon
house Thursday evening, Septem
ber 13th, at 7:30.
L. li. Minturn,
Chairman.
Geology and Mining, 5. —An ex
amination to remove incompletes
in Geology and Mining 5 (Miner
alogy) will be held in room 360 on
Thursday afternoon, September
27th, 1:30 to 4:30. Those who fail
in this examination will be required
to repeat a part of the course.
A. F. Rogers.
Geology and Mining,6.—An ex
amination to remove incompletes in
Geology and Mining, 6 (Petro
graphy) will be held in room 360
on Friday afternoon, September
28th, 1:30 to 4:30. Those who fail
in this examination will be required
to repeat a part of the course.
A. F. Rogers.
Track Men. —All men intending
to try-out for track will meet in the
new training house Thursday eve
ning at 7:30.
J. C. Macfarland, '07.
German Club —Will not meet on
Saturday evening.

Are now at the Bookstore. Each
member of the class is entitled to
five programs without charge.
