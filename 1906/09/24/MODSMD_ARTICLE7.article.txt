# HENRY WINS TOURNAMENT.
## Try-out for Freshmen Tennis Representative Brings Out Interesting Play. 
### 
T. C. Henry won the try-out for
the Freshman singles representative
on the Encina courts last Saturday.
Henry was closely pressed by C. A.
Green in the semi-final round, Green
winning the first net and a good
lead in the second before Henry fin
ally pulled himself together and won
the match. In the final round R. Lit
tle, who won in good form from the
entries in the upper half of the tour
nament, defaulted to Henry. The
playing of the Freshmen shows that
the entering class has much good ma
terial with Henry, Green, and Little
above the average.
The summary follows:
Preliminary round—J. Runyon de
feated Brewster, 6 -2, 6-3; C. A.
Green defeated R. R. Hind, 6-0, 6-3.
First round —J. Runyon defeated
E. Hyatt, 7-5, 6-8, 6-0; Mackey de
feated A. L. Green, 6-4, 6-2; R. Lit
tle defeated J. G. Boyley, 6-2. 1-6,
6-1; T. C. Henry defeated H. J. llad
ley, 6-3. 6-0; A. Drew defeated E. W.
Cheney, 6 -2, 6-2; F. L. Wilde de
feated A. Otis, by default; E. R.
Sheldon, a bye.
Second round—Mackey defeated
Runyon, 6-3, 6-1; Little defeated
Sheldon, 6-2, 7-5; Henry defeated
Drew, 6-1, 6-1. Green defeated
Wilde, 6-4. 6-3.
Semi-finals—Little defeated Mack
ey- 6-3, 6-r. Henry defeated Green,
4-6, 9-7, 6-2.
Final round—Henry defeated Lit
tle by default.
