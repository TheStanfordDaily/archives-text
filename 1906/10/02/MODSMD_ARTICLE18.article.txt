# ANNOUNCEMENTS.
## 
### 
Orchestra Rehearsai.—All mem
bers of the University orchestra meet
for rehearsal in the chapel, Wednes
day evening at 7 ; 15.
K. G. SHIEBLEY, '08.
Philological Association—Will meet
Thursday, Oct. 4th, at 130 in room
31. Professor U. R. Fairclough will
read a paper 011 "Professor Tyrrell's
Criticism of Horace"; Professor O.
M. Johnston on, "The Survival of
the Imperfect Indicative iieri (Latin)
in old Italian."
C. SEARLES, Secretary.
Gymnasium Club—Will meet on
Wednesday evening, October 3d, at
8 o'clock in Encina Gymnasium.
Glee Club.—Rehearsals will be held
in the chapel on Monday evening at
7:30 and Thursday evenings at 8:15.
S. B. THOMPSON, '07,
Leader.
Senior Class—Will meet on Wed
nesday at 1:15 in room 68.
E. P. STOTT, President.
Junior Women—Desiring class hats
leave their names before Friday noon
with Miss E. Murdoch, room 63,
Roble.
Tennis Men.—All matches in the
try-outs for the interclass doubles are
scheduled and players must arrange
to play them at once.
A. J. GOWAN, Secretary.
Greek Epic.—All students not al
ready enrolled, who wish to take the
course in Greek Epic should report
in the chapel, Thursday, October 4th,
at 10:15. First year students are not
debarred.
A. T. MURRAY.
1907 Class.—"Quad" assessments of
$2.50 may be paid at room 14 on the
quad 011 Monday, Wednesday and
Friday from 9:15 to 10:15, to S. M.
Salisbury, or to S. S. Smith, room 29,
Encina.
English Club—Will meet tomorrow
evening at the Gamma Phi Beta
House.
Freshman Yell Committee—Will
receive yells by mail if addressed to
Box 209.
