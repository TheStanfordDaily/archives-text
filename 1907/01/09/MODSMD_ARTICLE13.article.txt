# Announcements.
## 
### 
Boat Club.—There will be a busi
ness meeting of the Boat Club at
Boat House, Thursday evening at
7:30. F. W. Turner, '08.
Press Club—Will meet usual time
and place, Friday.
Ben Allen, '07.
At Home.—Rev. and Mrs. D.
Charles Gardner will be at home to
students and friends 011 Sunday after
noons from three to six o'clock.
Latin 13 and 16.—Students wishing
to register for these courses will meet
in room 21 011 Thursday, January 10,
to decide 011 the hours.
H. R. Fairclough.
English 6.—My 11:15 section will
meet 011 Friday according to schedule.
No section of English 6 will he given
at 10:15. Students asked to meet pro
visionally at that time will attend the
sections at 11:15.
F. A. Manchester.
German.—Students electing Ger
man courses with me will please meet
in room 3, Thursday afternoon, at the
hours given below, in order to select
permanent hours of meeting. Any
student who is unable to be present
should put a schedule of his work in
my hands. —4a, Phonetics and Phon
ology, 1:15; 14, Teaching Modern
Languages, 1:45; 19, Gothic, 2:15; 20.
Grammar, 2:30.
George Hempl.
Sophomore Comedy.—Try-outs will
he continued this week every after
noon from 3 until 5. Appointments
for these try-outs are to he made with
Prof. Gray at his office in the English
building. Regular rehearsals for
those who make the cast will begin
next week, so it is very important
that those who are still trying out
should make an early appointment
with the coach. Prof. Gray wishes
especially to meet all those who arc
siill trying for parts, on Friday after
noon at 3 o'clock.
