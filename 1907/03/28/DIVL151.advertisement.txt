The Harvard Medical School
Boston, Mass.
With the completion of the now buildings, which were dedicated September 25th, 1006,
this school now has facilities and equipment for teaching and research in the various
branches of medicine probably unsurpassed in this country. Of the live buildings, four
are devoted entirely to laboratory teaching and research. Tbe numerrus hospitals of
Boston afford abundant opportunities for clinical instiuction in medicine and surgery.
COURSE FOR TIIE DEGREE OF M. I).
A four years' course open to bachelors of art, literature, philosophy or science, and to
persons of equivalent standing, leads to the degree of M.D. The studies of the fourth year
are wholly elective; they include labortory subjects, general surgery, general medidine,
and the special clinical branches
The next school year extend* from September 2fi, 1907, to June 29, 1908.
