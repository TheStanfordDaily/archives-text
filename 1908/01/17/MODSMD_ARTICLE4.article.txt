## Moulton and Captain Lanagan Address Meeting--One Hundred and Fifty-Four Men Sign Up.

The number of Freshmen is unu

there are many who have good pre
paratory school records in their var

Training rules will go into effect
next Monday, though the men will
be allowed to attend dances up to
February 22. Captain Lanagan em
phasizes the fact that no half-hearted
training will lie tolerated, but that
every man must be out every day
and report to Trainer Moulton or
some one in charge of his event.
Trainer Moulton spoke first and
made a direct appeal to all men to
come out for track work. He said,
"We want everyone to come out,
but we especially want the new men,
not only Freshmen, but all who have
not been out before. When you
consider how track is handicapped
by having the cream of the athletes
taken by football, baseball and the
scholarship committee, it will be easy
to realize that we need all the ma
terial we can get. I have things
'doped' to a fine point, and 1 figure
that we can win this year if we can
find men to take ten third places.
So let, everybody come out. Every
man out there will help you and
there is no such thing as ridicule.
The work may appear to be a grind
but it. will later prove a pleasure. All
the older men will constitute them
selves trainers and do all in their
power to help everyone who reports
for work."
Captain Lanagan, in a few brief
and concise statements, summed up
the situation and outlined the meth
ods of training. In closing he said:
"We must win the meet this year,
and we can win it, if we go out. The
question is, will you do it?"
