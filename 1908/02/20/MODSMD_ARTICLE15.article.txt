# DEBATING DATES NAMED
## PRELIMINARY TRYOUT TO BE HELD MARCH 25. Plans Announced and Candidates For Intercollegiate Team Should Hand in Names. 
### 
At a meeting of the Intersociety
Debating League held this afternoon
it was decided to hold the prelimin
ary tryout for places on the inter
collegiate debating team on March
2F>. No date for the finals was set.
All men who intend to enter the
tryouts should hand their names to
J. F. Reilly, '08, L. Newland, '08,
R. E. Hodge, '08, R. B. Murphy, '08,
T. T. Bennett, '08, or G. B. Bush,
'09, as soon as possible. On next
Monday afternoon, all candidates for
the team will meet in room 1, Law
building, at 1:10, for the purpose of
being assigned to their squads and
selecting the side of the question
which they wish to uphold.
All the squads will debate the
subject which the University of Cal
ifornia submits for the intercolleg
iate contest. Six men will compose
each squad. The main speeches are
to be of ten minutes duration and
four minutes wil be given the
first speaker on the affirmative side
of each squad in which to refute
the arguments of the negative. Sev
en men will be selected at the pre
liminary tryout to compete in the
finals for the honor of representing
Stanford in the debate with Califor
nia.
Following the usual custom, the
veteran debaters, who have already
been members of an intercolegiate
team, wil not be expected to enter
the preliminary tryouts. W. C.
Shelton, '08, J. E. Shelton, '10, D.
C. Boyd, '08, G. D. Ferrell, '09, and
L. Newland, '08, are the men now
in college who are eligible to enter
the finals without participating in
the preliminary contest.
