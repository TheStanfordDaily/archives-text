# TRYOUTS TOMORROW.
## Debating Team To Be Selected To Meet California Speakers. 
### 
Because only ten men were pre
pared to enter the preliminary try
out for the intercollegiate team
which had been announced for last
night, it was decided not to hold the
elimination debate and the ten men
were declared eligible to compete in
the final tryout which will be held
in the University Chapel tomorrow
evening at 7:30. L. Craven, '09,
G. B. Bush, '09, C. C. Shoemaker,
'10, A. Macmillan, '10, J. W. Gal
loway, *08, R. B. Murphy, '08, W.
E. Hettman, '10, R. J. Miller, '11,
F. B. Wood, '10, and A. M. Hamil
ton, 'OS, are the debaters who have
thus obtained the right of participat
ing in the finals. W. C. Shelton,
'OS, D. C. Boyd, '08, L. Newland,
'08, G. D. Ferrell, '09, and J. E.
Shelton, '10, the varsity veterans,
have a similar privilege, but several
of these have not fully decided to
try out for the team.
Professor C. A. Duniway, of the
History department, Professor H. A.
Millis, of the Economics department,
and Professor C. A. Huston, of the
Law department, are the judges who
will select the Cardinal team and
the alternate. Each speaker will
be given ten minutes for the pre
sentation of his main argument and
four minutes in which to refute the
contentions of bis opponents. The
order of speaking is to be deter
mined by the drawing of lots.
