versity Board of Trustees 011 Friday,
a large number of appointments of
student assistants in the various de

by the departments and approved by
the President of the University. In

gineering to fill the place left vacant

and draughtsman under the Commis
sion of Engineers in the reconstruction
work. The appointments of Dr. John
H. Blair, a graduate of Harvard, to
an instructorship in American History,
and of Dr. S. L. Ware, a graduate of
Johns Hopkins, as instructor in Medi
aeval History, were also ratified.
