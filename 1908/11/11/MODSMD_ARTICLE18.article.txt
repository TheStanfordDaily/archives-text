# TOURNAMENT FINISHED
## HANDICAP TENNIS MATCHES ARE WON BY MORGAN. Defeats Peabody in Hard Fought Match This Afternoonâ€”Victor Large Handicap. 
### 
H. S. Morgan, '12, of San Diego, to
day won the handicap tennis tourna
ment and the Spaulding Gold Medal
racquet donated by the Stanford Book
store, by defeating C. C. Peabody, '12,
of Ontario, by the score of 2-6, 6-1,
6-2.
Morgan gave Peabody a handicap of
30 on each game. Peabody fought
hard throughout.
Peabody started with a rush and
took the first set easily by a score of
6-2. After that Morgan took a brace
and by using his strokes carefully,
pulled out the victory.
Peabody, as runner-up of the tour
nament, receives a pair of tennis
shoes. The winner of the Collins and
Parton match as third man gets some
tennis balls as prizes.
The concluding matches of the up
per half of the tournament though
not as brilliant as those of the upper
class men in the lower half, were still
very close and warmly contesed, giv
ing promise of much good tennis ma
terial. Peabody beat both Batkin and
Collins in three-set matches, winning
both matches after losing the first
set in each.
The scores stand:
Fourth round: Domaschenz beat
Crandall 6-4, 6-4.
Fifth round: Peabody beat Batkin
1-6, 6-2, 6-1; Collins beat Doma
schenz 6-3, 6-4.
Semi-Finals: Peabody beat Collins
2-6, 6-4, 6-4.
Finals: Morgan beat Peabody 2-6,
6-1, 7-5, 6-2.
