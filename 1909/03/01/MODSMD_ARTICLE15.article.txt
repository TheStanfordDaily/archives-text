# MORGAN WINS CHAMPIONSHIP
## Defeats Mann in Filial Match of Interclass Singles Tennis Tournament. 
### 
The interclass singles tennis tour
nament was completed last Saturday,
when H. S. Morgan, '12, defeated H.
Mann, '10, in the finals match by ;i
score of 6-1, 6-1,'6-2. As a result of
this match, Morgan holds the inter
class singles championship. The vic
tory was decisive, Mann evidently be
ing off his game, as he seldom made
more than one stroke to the point,
while Morgan used all his strokes
cleverly, showing the best form dis
played this year. Previous to tbe final
match, Morgan defeated R. Steinberg
er, '11, by a score of 6-0, 6-3, in
which the latter fought hard, but was
outclassed.
In the doubles tournament, Morgan
and E. R. Canterbury, '12, have reach
ed the finals and will play A. J. Gowan
and E. Jordan of the '09 class, some
time during the week. The Freshman
team defeated W. E. Ferguson, '12,
and W. F. Geary, '12, by a score of
8-6, 6-2; J, A. Davis, '12, and S. C.
Kennedy, '12, by a score of 6-0, 6-2;
and W. H. McConnell, '11, and R.
Steinberger, '11, by 6-0, 7-5. With this
showing, the underclassmen are con
ceded to hold an equal chance with
the Seniors.
Entries are now being received for
the first singles tournament of the
varsity tryout. It is planned to close
ihe entries Monday night, March S,
and start play immediately.
