# STUDENT BODY NOMINATIONS HELD
## Candidates for Offices Are Named at Meeting.—Four Amendments to Constitution Proposed. Election Wednesday. 
### 
The business of the annual Student
Body meeting this afternoon consisted
in the nominations for the various
officers of the Student Body and the
offering of four amendments to the
Constitution by a sub-committee of
the Executive Committee. Although
many of the offices are uncontested
so far, four disputes will result in the
choice of as many officers. Two nom
inations were made for the position
of graduate manager and two for edi
tor of the Sequoia. There are two
candidates for the office of business
manager of the Daily Palo Alto while
three freshmen will contest for the
one place on the Executive Committee
open for a first-year man.
As submitted by R. B. Wheeler, '11,
one of the amendments to the Con
stitution of the Associated Students
enables the Student Advisor to have
a voice in the discussions of the Exec
utive Committee although he does not
possess the right to vote. This is an
amendment to Article 3, Section 2, and
reads as follows:
" and Student Advisor, who shall
have the right to attend meeting and
shall have the right to discuss but
not to vote in these meetings.
Another amendment provides for the
reorganization of the Board of Con
trol in the following words:
"The Board of Control shall be
composed of two members of the Fac
ulty Athletic Committee, the chairman
and one member being appointed by
the president of the University, the
Chairman to be ex-officio chairman
of this general committee; two alumni,
one a resident within two miles of the
grounds, these two to be selected by
the Executive Committee of the alum
ni ;three students, one secretary of
the Student Body to be ex-officio a
member, the other two to be chosen by

the Executive Committee of the Stu
dent Body.
The third amendment dealing with
the University Conference reads as fol
lows:
"The composition and duties of the
University Conference shall be as out
lined in the Constitution of the Uni
versity Conference as adopted on
March 10, 1909 by the Associated Stu
dents.
The fourth amendment changes the
third section of Article 3 of the Con
stitution by providing that there shall
be three standing committees of the
Executive Committee.

The business of the annual Student
Body meeting this afternoon consisted
in the nominations for the various
officers of the Student Body and the
offering of four amendments to the
Constitution by a sub-committee of
the Executive Committee. Although
many of the offices are uncontested
so far, four disputes will result in the
choice of as many officers. Two nom
inations were made for the position
of graduate manager and two for edi
tor of the Sequoia. There are two
candidates for the office of business
manager of the Daily Palo Alto while
three freshmen will contest for the
one place on the Executive Committee
open for a first-year man.
As submitted by R. B. Wheeler, '11,
one of the amendments to the Con
stitution of the Associated Students
enables the Student Advisor to have
a voice in the discussions of the Exec
utive Committee although he does not
possess the right to vote. This is an
amendment to Article 3, Section 2, and
reads as follows:
" and Student Advisor, who shall
have the right to attend meeting and
shall have the right to discuss but
not to vote in these meetings.
Another amendment provides for the
reorganization of the Board of Con
trol in the following words:
"The Board of Control shall be
composed of two members of the Fac
ulty Athletic Committee, the chairman
and one member being appointed by
the president of the University, the
Chairman to be ex-officio chairman
of this general committee; two alumni,
one a resident within two miles of the
grounds, these two to be selected by
the Executive Committee of the alum
ni ;three students, one secretary of
the Student Body to be ex-officio a
member, the other two to be chosen by

the Executive Committee of the Stu
dent Body.
The third amendment dealing with
the University Conference reads as fol
lows:
"The composition and duties of the
University Conference shall be as out
lined in the Constitution of the Uni
versity Conference as adopted on
March 10, 1909 by the Associated Stu
dents.
The fourth amendment changes the
third section of Article 3 of the Con
stitution by providing that there shall
be three standing committees of the
Executive Committee.
