# INTERSCHOLASTIC MEET ENTRIES
## Names of Athletes Who Will Compete Saturday Are Received.—Twenty-Seven Schools Will Be Represented. 
### 
The entries of twenty-seven prepar
atory schools have been received by
the Interscholastic Association for the
meet next Saturday. The list, how
ever, will not be closed for several
days, due to the fact that the entries
from several schools have not yet been
received, being delayed in the mails.
The schools so far entered will be
represented by 130 athletes.
Oakland High School has entered a
team in the meet but owing to the
conflict between the pupils and school
authorities over fraternities, the team
may not be allowed to compete. Presi
dent Holman of the Association has
added the names of C. R. Young, 'OS,
and H. F. Allen, '10, to the Reception
Committee. The entries are as fol

The entries of twenty-seven prepar
atory schools have been received by
the Interscholastic Association for the
meet next Saturday. The list, how
ever, will not be closed for several
days, due to the fact that the entries
from several schools have not yet been
received, being delayed in the mails.
The schools so far entered will be
represented by 130 athletes.
Oakland High School has entered a
team in the meet but owing to the
conflict between the pupils and school
authorities over fraternities, the team
may not be allowed to compete. Presi
