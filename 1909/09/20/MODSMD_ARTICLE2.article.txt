The Handicap Singles Tennis Tour
nament will probably be finished early
this week. Play in the upper half \vas
ended last Saturday when H. S. Mor
gan, '12, defeated S. C. Kennedy, '12,
in the semi-finals. In the lower half
E. Kobbe, '11, has reached the semi
finals and will play the winner of
the Murray-Ferguson match for the
right to meet Morgan in the final.
The tournament has been greatly
prolonged, owing to the large number
of long deuce sets which have beeen
played as the handicaps generally
have proven well placed. The long
est match of the tournament was that
played between Professor A. A. Young
and W. F. Geary, '12, Professor Young
finally won out after playing forty
six close games.
