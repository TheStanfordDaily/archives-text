V. \\. \. —Will meet, in the Chapel
Tuesday, October 19 at 4:30.
Oratorio Society—Will meet in room
GS to-morrow evening at 7:30.
Orchestra —Will meet Wednesday
evening at 7:30 in the Chapel.
Mandolin Club—Will meet to-mor
row evening at 7:30 in room 55.
Bookstore — Erchendorff — Ansdein
Lebineines Tangenietres has arrived.
Big Game Stunts—Committee asks
Student Body for hints on new stunts
for this year.
Skull and Snakes—Will meet at the
Kappa Sigma house to-morrow eve
ning at 9 o'clock.
Sophomore Social Club—Will meet
at 327 Emerson St., Palo Alto, next
Wednesday evening, Oct. 20.
Tryout for Minstrel Men—Will be
held Wednesday, Oct. 20, at 7 o'clock
at the Theta Delta Chi house.
Spanish Club.—All interested in
forming a Spanish Conversation Club
meet in room 229 next Tuesday eve
ning at 7:30.
Auglo-Saxon—During the absence
of Mr. Briggs, Mr. Seward will meet
the class in Anglo-Saxon on Mondays
and Wednesdays.
Fortnightly Music Club —Will hold
its first open meeting on Friday. Oc
tober 22, at 8 o'clock, at. Miss Mar
ker's School, Palo Alto.
Cricket Club—Will practice Tues
day and Thursdays, as baseball prac
tice will be held on the diamond dur
ing the other days of the week.
1911 Quad Assessments —Are now
due. All photographs must be taken
this semester at Davey's.
C. A. Christin, Mgr.
Chaparral.—All students wishing to
tryout for the management of the
Chaparral call at 6 Salvatierra, Tues
day, at 5 p. m. Manager.
Nestoria—Will meet October 19.
Society will organize itself in political
"convention"and nominate municipal
officers of San Francisco. All in
vited.
Euphronia—Will meet October 19.
Question for debate: "Resolved, That
the protective tariff should be with
drawn from goods, the manufacture
of which has been established in this
country." Affirmative, Summerfield,
Burr; negative, Bllven, Hamilton.
