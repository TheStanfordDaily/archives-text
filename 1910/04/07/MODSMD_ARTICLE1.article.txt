# RAISE LARGE AMOUNT
## FUND FOR WOMEN'S CLUB HOUSE BEACHES $5530. Spirited Rally Brings Forth Unexpected Number of Charter Members.—Freshmen (Jive Most 
### 
As a result of the rally yesterday
afternoon, $5,530 in cash is assured to
the Committee in charge of the Wo
man's Club-house by October 1, 1911.
Miss T. Morgan, '10, presided over the
meeting. Miss M. Hutchins, the first
speaker, gave a short history of the
Club-house movement to date. She
explained that it is the wish of the
Committee to dedicate a completed
Club-house on October 24, 1912, the
twenty-first anniversary of the found
ing of the University. The finished
structure will cost $25,000 and one
half of that sum of money must be in
the hands of the Committee by Oct
ober 1, 1911. Therefore, until that
time, life memberships (payable in
any number of installments before
that date) will until then be $25 each,
but will probably be increased after
wards. Pledge cards may be obtained
from Mrs. E. W. Allan, Mrs. P. T.
Treat, Miss J. Morgan or any member
of the Committee. The speaker also
said that the Club-house would be sit
uated, if possible, somewhere between
the Quadrangle and Roble Hall.
The next speaker was Miss H. Camp
bell, '11, who told what the Club
house would mean to a student. Miss
F. Williams then spoke of Roble's
pledge of $1340 and urged all to aid in
making the building a reality as soon
as possible.

As a result of the rally yesterday
afternoon, $5,530 in cash is assured to
the Committee in charge of the Wo
man's Club-house by October 1, 1911.
Miss T. Morgan, '10, presided over the
meeting. Miss M. Hutchins, the first
speaker, gave a short history of the
Club-house movement to date. She
explained that it is the wish of the
Committee to dedicate a completed
Club-house on October 24, 1912, the
twenty-first anniversary of the found
ing of the University. The finished
structure will cost $25,000 and one
half of that sum of money must be in
the hands of the Committee by Oct
ober 1, 1911. Therefore, until that
time, life memberships (payable in
any number of installments before
that date) will until then be $25 each,
but will probably be increased after
wards. Pledge cards may be obtained
from Mrs. E. W. Allan, Mrs. P. T.
Treat, Miss J. Morgan or any member
of the Committee. The speaker also
said that the Club-house would be sit
uated, if possible, somewhere between
the Quadrangle and Roble Hall.
The next speaker was Miss H. Camp
bell, '11, who told what the Club
house would mean to a student. Miss
F. Williams then spoke of Roble's
pledge of $1340 and urged all to aid in
making the building a reality as soon
as possible.
