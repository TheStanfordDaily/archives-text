With an outburst of enthusiasm that
presages success for the 1910 football
season, the opening rally of the year
was held on Friday night in the big
Enciiia club-rooms. From Captain
Dole's call to the men to the final
words of Coach George Presley intense
interest prevailed, and each speaker
was greeted by such a round of ap
plause that it is easy to predict that
fault-finding will no longer be the
favorite bleacher pastime.
Probably the feeling of the entire
Student Body was voiced in the earn
est talk made by Coach Presley, who,
in his few remarks struck the domi
nant tone of the whole rally. In his
eloquent plea for harmony, Presley
said: "Broadmimleduess and unity,
to my mind, is the essence of the Stan
ford Spirit. If you are a debater or a
hard student, do not think that any
man who makes the team, and gives
his time to athletics, lias ho greater
ideal than to make one of tlie teams or
sport an "N." What the Stanford Spirit
should he is a spirit of helpfulness.
The thing that nil! give you most
pleasure when out of the University is
the remernberaiice that you helped a
fellow. If we go to Berkeley with a
united spirit, we will go with a team
which the veterans of Berkeley cannot
withstand."
The rally was opened by Captain K.
L. Dole outlining in a few words the
task that confronts Stanford this year.
To replace the men who have left col
lege will require great effort and Dole
urged all available men to sign up.
"As a result of the Australian trip,"
the captain said, "I am certain that
Presley is one of the best coaches in
the game, and all that we have to do is
use the things he teaches."
Student Advisor Roth followed with
a few words of encouragement to the
men and a few stories of the Austra
lian trip.
G. A. Ditz, '11, President of the Stu
dent .Body, was the next speaker. He
particularly sought to impress upon
the men absolute confidence in their
coach and trainer, and stated that a
victory on November 12, would only be
secured by the undivided support of
the Student Body during the long pre
paratory season. G. N. Richardson,
'09, also spoke along the same lines.
Trainer Moulton sent every one
away happy by narrating a few of his
own stories. No rigid set of training
rules will be adhered to for some time.
At the termination of the rally, 185
men signed up for practice.

lian trip.
G. A. Ditz, '11, President of the Stu
dent .Body, was the next speaker. He
particularly sought to impress upon
the men absolute confidence in their
coach and trainer, and stated that a
victory on November 12, would only be
secured by the undivided support of
the Student Body during the long pre
paratory season. G. N. Richardson,
'09, also spoke along the same lines.
Trainer Moulton sent every one
away happy by narrating a few of his
own stories. No rigid set of training
rules will be adhered to for some time.
At the termination of the rally, 185
men signed up for practice.

presages success for the 1910 football
season, the opening rally of the year
was held on Friday night in the big
Enciiia club-rooms. From Captain
Dole's call to the men to the final
words of Coach George Presley intense
interest prevailed, and each speaker
was greeted by such a round of ap
plause that it is easy to predict that
fault-finding will no longer be the
favorite bleacher pastime.

Student Body was voiced in the earn
est talk made by Coach Presley, who,
in his few remarks struck the domi
nant tone of the whole rally. In his

to my mind, is the essence of the Stan

man who makes the team, and gives
his time to athletics, lias ho greater

fellow. If we go to Berkeley with a

which the veterans of Berkeley cannot
withstand."
