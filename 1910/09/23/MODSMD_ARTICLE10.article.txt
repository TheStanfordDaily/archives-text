# Announcements.
## 
### 
Pee Wee Club. —Cluster. B. S., Sec
retary.
Junior Class—Will meet Tuesday at
1:10 in room 214.
Oratorical Society.—Meeting will be
postponed until further notice.
Joshes*—All jokes for 1912 Quad to
be mailed to J. W. Howell, Box 258.
Musical Theory.—Class postponed
one week on account of delay in se
curing text books.
All (lancing classes—Will meet in
Roble gymnasium, on Tuesdays and
Thursdays at 4:45.
Pastime Club—Will meet Friday
evening in customary manner.
B. P., Secretary.
Gymnasium Rugby.—All men drop
ped from the rugby squad may play
with the Gymnasium Team without
registering in the Hygiene Course.
Faculty Fencing.— Instructor Ma
loney will be unable to meet his
Faculty Fencing class until Saturday,
October 1, at 9 a.m. in Encina Gym
nasium.
Applied Mathematics.—Students de
siring to take test examination in ap
plied mathematics I, scheduled for Sat
urday, September 24, will be excused
from shop lectures on the same date.
E. P. Lesley.
Basket Hull. —The Misses Woodhead
will be at home to all basket ball girls
on Friday evening, September 23, at
6 Alvarado. All girls who are in
tending to sign up for the class-series
are invited, as well as those already
registered.
Xestorin —Will meet on Tuesday
night, September 27, in room 214
Question for debate: "Resolved, That
the conservation of forests should rest
in the power of the State Government
rather than in the National Govern
ment." Affirmative, Tincher, Hen
shaw; negative, Guerna, Henderson.
Professor Adams will deliver a lecture
on "Twentieth Century Ideals."
Eiiphronia — Will meet Tuesday
evening, September 27, room 231.
Question for debate: "Resolved, That
international policy requires that U.
S. should establish forts on the Istha
mus of Panama." Affirmative, Tucker,
Fogel; negative, Gregg, Wood. Ad
dress by Professor Bonnell.
