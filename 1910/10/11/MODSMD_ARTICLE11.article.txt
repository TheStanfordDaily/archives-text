# Announcements.

Joshes.—All jokes for 1912 Quad to
be mailed to J. W. Howell, Box 255.
X. T. Association—Will assemble in
convention on the prescribed date.
Sophomore Cotillion Committee —
Will meet Thursday afternoon at 1:30,
in the Palo Alto Armory.
Civil Engineering Association—Will
meet Tuesday evening, October 11, at
the Phi Delta Theta house.
Stanford Trampers.—There will be
a walk Wednesday afternoon, at 4:30.
Miss X. Ferand, '11, will lead.
Woman's Boat Club Committee —
Will meet at Madrona, Wednesday
evening at 7:30 sharp. Important.
Senior Women.—On account of late
orders, Caps and Gowns will not be
worn until Wednesday, October 19.
French Club.—Open meeting at Del
ta Gamma house Wednesday evening,
at 7:30. All interested are cordially
invited.

Girl's Tennis.—All girls who have
not passed the athletic test by Sat
urday, October 15, will be ineligible
for the handicap tournament.
Gymnasium llugby.—All men drop
ped from the rugby squad may play
with the Gymnasium Team without
registering in the Hygiene Course.
Bleacher Stunt Committee—Requests
that suggestions of original stunts be
submitted to H. Rittigstein, '10, R. W.
Woodville, '11, or J. B. Be man, '12.
Twenty-five Doiler Prize—Offered
for best Plug Ugly farce submitted to
Junior committee. Manuscripts must
be turned in by October 18.
A. G. Bower, '12, Chairman.
Philological Association—Will meet
Thursday, October 13, in room 31, in
commemoration of Professor Matzke.
Papers by Professors Johnston,
Searles, Allen, Flugel, Murray, and
Hempl. R. M. Alden, Sec.
Interclass Tennis Tournament—En
tries for both singles and doubles will
close next hursday. Give names to H.
S. Morgan, Phi Delta Theta house, or
H. L. Loud, room 128, Encina. En
trance fee 50 cents for singles and $1
a team for doubles.
Cosmopolitan Club.—Professor Treat
of the History department will lecture
at the next open meeting of the club
on Wednesday, October 12 in the chap
el. His subject will be "The Uplift of
the Philippines." The lecture will
begin at 8 o'clock.
Graduation Candidates.—All candi
dates for graduation in January should
fill out the required blanks and file
with the Registrar's Office not later
than October 20. Blanks may be had
at the Registrar's Office.
O. L. Elliott.

are due and payable at once. Photos
for annual must be taken at Davey\s
this semester. Transfers, affiliating
with 1912, may send assessments to
manager, B. J. O'Donnell, P. O. Box
234, campus.
Freshmen Debaters—Will meet in
room 231 of the English building, Wed
nesday, October 12, at 7:30. Question
for debate, "Resolved, That vivisection
should be prohibited." Affirmative,
Wood and Good; negative, Cowen and
Pierce. Professor I. B. Cross will
speak.
Sophomore Debaters— Members of
the second squad have chosen the fol
lowing question to debate in the try
outs: "Resolved, That all American
cities should adopt the commission
form of government." The squad has
been divided as follows: Affirmative,
Akaboshi and Needham; negative,
Wood, Gilbert and Smith. The men
are requested to begin work at once.

submitted to H. Rittigstein, '10, R. W.
Woodville, '11, or J. B. Be man, '12.
Twenty-five Doiler Prize—Offered
for best Plug Ugly farce submitted to
Junior committee. Manuscripts must
be turned in by October 18.
A. G. Bower, '12, Chairman.
Philological Association—Will meet
Thursday, October 13, in room 31, in
commemoration of Professor Matzke.
Papers by Professors Johnston,
Searles, Allen, Flugel, Murray, and
Hempl. R. M. Alden, Sec.
Interclass Tennis Tournament—En
tries for both singles and doubles will
close next hursday. Give names to H.
S. Morgan, Phi Delta Theta house, or
H. L. Loud, room 128, Encina. En
trance fee 50 cents for singles and $1
a team for doubles.
Cosmopolitan Club.—Professor Treat
of the History department will lecture
at the next open meeting of the club
on Wednesday, October 12 in the chap
el. His subject will be "The Uplift of
the Philippines." The lecture will
begin at 8 o'clock.
Graduation Candidates.—All candi
dates for graduation in January should
fill out the required blanks and file
with the Registrar's Office not later
than October 20. Blanks may be had
at the Registrar's Office.
O. L. Elliott.
Juniors.—l9l2 Quad assessments
are due and payable at once. Photos
for annual must be taken at Davey\s
this semester. Transfers, affiliating
with 1912, may send assessments to
manager, B. J. O'Donnell, P. O. Box
234, campus.
Freshmen Debaters—Will meet in
room 231 of the English building, Wed
nesday, October 12, at 7:30. Question
for debate, "Resolved, That vivisection
should be prohibited." Affirmative,
Wood and Good; negative, Cowen and
Pierce. Professor I. B. Cross will
speak.
Sophomore Debaters— Members of
the second squad have chosen the fol
lowing question to debate in the try
outs: "Resolved, That all American
cities should adopt the commission
form of government." The squad has
been divided as follows: Affirmative,
Akaboshi and Needham; negative,
Wood, Gilbert and Smith. The men
are requested to begin work at once.
Joshes.—All jokes for 1912 Quad to
be mailed to J. W. Howell, Box 255.
X. T. Association—Will assemble in
convention on the prescribed date.
Sophomore Cotillion Committee —
Will meet Thursday afternoon at 1:30,
in the Palo Alto Armory.
Civil Engineering Association—Will
meet Tuesday evening, October 11, at
the Phi Delta Theta house.
Stanford Trampers.—There will be
a walk Wednesday afternoon, at 4:30.
Miss X. Ferand, '11, will lead.
Woman's Boat Club Committee —
Will meet at Madrona, Wednesday
evening at 7:30 sharp. Important.
Senior Women.—On account of late
orders, Caps and Gowns will not be
worn until Wednesday, October 19.
French Club.—Open meeting at Del
ta Gamma house Wednesday evening,
at 7:30. All interested are cordially
invited.

not passed the athletic test by Sat
urday, October 15, will be ineligible
for the handicap tournament.

ped from the rugby squad may play
