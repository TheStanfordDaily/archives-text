# NESTORIA DEBATES PROGRESSIVE MOVEMENT.
## 
### 
"Resolved, That the recent move
ment of the progressive element of the
Republican party should have the ap
proval of the people of the United
States, formed the question for the set
debates in Nestoria last evening. In
an interesting discussion of this timely
topic, C. H. Gilbert, 13, and T. Cronyn,
'13, the affirmative speakers, were
given the decision over A. G. Bradford,
'13, and P. A. Pier, '14. Impromptu
talks on various subjects related to
that of the coining Carnot debate added
to the interest of the meeting. The
regular talk on Parliamentary Practice
was given by B. B. Bush, '12, who
spoke on "Amendments."
