ing climaxed the great football rally
in Encina Clnbroom Friday evening,
as Coach George J. Presley mounted
the table to speak to the rooters who
filled the hall. The signing-up follow
ing Presley's talk set a new record.
For the first time in the football his
tory of Stanford the two hundred mark
was shattered, the official number
being 210.
From the time that the first yell
was called for by Yell Leader Kennedy
until the ovation to the coach was
stopped by a "skyrocket" the evening's
enthusiasm never dulled for an in
stant.
Presley called for everyone with
spirit to come out for the team, but
those who lacked it he advised to stay
off the field. Just so long as a man
would put his heart and soul into the
game, so long would he have patience
with him. "We are going to meet a
strong team," continued the Coach,
"and to beat them we must have three
essentials; plenty of material, a team,
and that team and a Student Body
with spirit behind them."
We have many veterans back of that
team which met defeat last year, but
which fought all the way thru," said
Presley, "and we've got the best
fighting second team that we've had
since I've been around here." "Let's
start in now and get into the game all
the heart and soul that we have got
and not begin at the final rally-up.
Every fellow should realize that it is
up to him to help and get behind the
men, not to criticise, but to help.
Spread it over the season, and not
simply at the final rally.
For the first time in the recollec
tion of the rooters, the Stanford Band
turned out in force and was one of the
features that helped to score the
rally's tremendous success. They
filled in the gaps between the speakers
with the red-blooded music that will
heli> fire the enthusiasm of the Car
dinal bleachers next November.

years disused, custom of the disbur
