Sixteen representative men will be
elected to the University Conference
of men students tomorrow afternoon
at 1:15. The voting will take place
in the department buldings, where
men in each department eligible to
vote and to hold office will cast his
ballot. A list of men in each depart
ment who can be elected is given be
! low, together with the personnel of
i last year's Conference.
The Conference is the nearest ap
proach to student self-government or
ganized so far at Stanford. Its pur
pose is to meet and discuss events as
they occur on the campus, to adjust
any differences, and to act as an agent
of harmony between students and fa
culty. The Advisory Committee of the
Conference is made up of five men who
are judged to be most capable of
dealing with the most vital problems
at close hand.
A number of vacancies are to be
filled tomorrow. Every man with fi
fty hours to his credit is entitled to
vote, according to the department he is
registered in. Lists of eligible voters
have been posted in the different
buildings where balloting takes place,
i In case anyone has any corrections to
make to these lists he may report the
I same to the men in charge of the polls
j tomorrow afternoon, and the matter
will be given prompt attention. Each
eligible man has a right to vote for
.is many representatives as the list
calls for among the names printed.

! men has been called for Thursday eve
; ning in the council room. President

Sixteen representative men will be
elected to the University Conference
of men students tomorrow afternoon
at 1:15. The voting will take place
in the department buldings, where
men in each department eligible to
vote and to hold office will cast his
ballot. A list of men in each depart
ment who can be elected is given be
! low, together with the personnel of
i last year's Conference.
The Conference is the nearest ap
proach to student self-government or
ganized so far at Stanford. Its pur
pose is to meet and discuss events as
they occur on the campus, to adjust
any differences, and to act as an agent
of harmony between students and fa
culty. The Advisory Committee of the
Conference is made up of five men who
are judged to be most capable of
dealing with the most vital problems
at close hand.
A number of vacancies are to be
filled tomorrow. Every man with fi
fty hours to his credit is entitled to
vote, according to the department he is
registered in. Lists of eligible voters

buildings where balloting takes place,
i In case anyone has any corrections to
make to these lists he may report the
I same to the men in charge of the polls
j tomorrow afternoon, and the matter
will be given prompt attention. Each
eligible man has a right to vote for
.is many representatives as the list
calls for among the names printed.

Sixteen representative men will be
elected to the University Conference
of men students tomorrow afternoon
at 1:15. The voting will take place
in the department buldings, where
men in each department eligible to
vote and to hold office will cast his
ballot. A list of men in each depart
ment who can be elected is given be
! low, together with the personnel of
i last year's Conference.
The Conference is the nearest ap
proach to student self-government or
ganized so far at Stanford. Its pur
pose is to meet and discuss events as
they occur on the campus, to adjust
any differences, and to act as an agent
of harmony between students and fa
culty. The Advisory Committee of the
Conference is made up of five men who
are judged to be most capable of
dealing with the most vital problems
at close hand.
A number of vacancies are to be
filled tomorrow. Every man with fi

j tomorrow afternoon, and the matter
will be given prompt attention. Each
eligible man has a right to vote for
.is many representatives as the list
calls for among the names printed.
