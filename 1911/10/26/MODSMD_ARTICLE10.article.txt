The following are the minutes of
the Executive Committee for the
meeting of October 25, 1911:
Meeting called to order. President
K. L. Schaupp in the chair.
Thomas absent.
The minutes of the meeting of Octo
ber 18 were read and approved.
It was moved, seconded, and passed,
that the President appoint a committee
to arrange for an entertainment for
the members of the Vancouver team.
Wilson, Lewis, and Beman, chairman,
appointed.
Moved, seconded, and passed, that
Coleman be empowered to purchase a
new interscholastic debating cup at a
cost not to exceed $40.
Moved, seconded, and passed, that
the President appoint a committee to
place and house the Vancouver men
this coming Sunday. Worthington and
Coleman, chairman, appointed.
It was moved, seconded, and passed,
that Roth and Guerena be empowered
to publish the student body hand book.
Roth appointed to supervise the
choosing of Freshmen to act as es
corts to the Australian boys on their
visit to the University.
Meeting adjourned.
R. S. Worthington, secretary.
Vancouver to he Entertained.
It has been the custom in the past
when the teams from British Columbia
visit the University to give an enter
tainment for them. Such an entertain
ment will be given this year. A com
mittee of three was appointed last
evening to supervise the work of the
affair. J. B. Beman, '12, W. S. Wilson,
'13, and L. S. Lewis, '14, were appoint
ed to take charge of the evening. It
is intended to give an informal per
formance of sketches, acrobatic acts,
and the like. The affair will be held
in Encina Club room as in the past.
The cup offered by the student body
to the High Schools of northern Cali
fornia as a debating trophy to be con
tested for annually, to be given per
manently to the school winning it
three successive times, last year be
came the property of San Jose high
school by virtue of three victories. It
was decided last evening to purchase
a new cup to be contested for in the
same way as the old one. This in
sures the continuance of these debates
according to the Executive Committee.
T. L. Coleman, '12, was appointed to
purchase the cup.
Vancouver Men Housed.
Vancouver's second game with the
California Varsity takes place on next
Saturday. Either on that evening or
Sunday morning the team will come
down to Stanford for the series of two
games to be played the following
week. During their stay on the cam
pus they will be quartered in the vari
ous fraternity houses and at Encina.
With this object in mind a committee,
consisting of R. S. Worthington, '12,
and T. L. Coleman, '12, was appointed
to supervise the placing of the men on
their arrival at the campus.
Handbook to be Continued.
It was decided last evening to issue
a revised edition of the Student Body
hand book. This book contains the
constitution of the Student Body, and
copies of the various agreements in
force at the present time between
Stanford and the University of Cali
fornia relating to all branches of In
tercollegiate athletics, debates, and
other contests. It is issued each year
by the Student Body. A. E. Roth and
F. L, Guerena, '11, were appointed to
supervise the publication.
Will Guide Australian Hoys.
At the last meeting of the Executive
Committee it was decided to entertain
the boys of the Australian League on
October 28. In order that all of the
visitors may be properly shown around
the University, it was decided that one
Freshman would be chosen from each
fraternity house and others from En
cina to act as guides during the day
for the Antipodeans. Each Freshman
will be given charge of two of the
visitors. Student Adviser Roth will
choose today the men from among the (
Freshmen to serve on Saturday.

Handbook to be Continued.
It was decided last evening to issue
a revised edition of the Student Body
hand book. This book contains the
constitution of the Student Body, and
copies of the various agreements in
force at the present time between
Stanford and the University of Cali
fornia relating to all branches of In
tercollegiate athletics, debates, and
other contests. It is issued each year
by the Student Body. A. E. Roth and
F. L, Guerena, '11, were appointed to
supervise the publication.
Will Guide Australian Hoys.
At the last meeting of the Executive
Committee it was decided to entertain
the boys of the Australian League on
October 28. In order that all of the
visitors may be properly shown around
the University, it was decided that one
Freshman would be chosen from each
fraternity house and others from En
cina to act as guides during the day
for the Antipodeans. Each Freshman
will be given charge of two of the
visitors. Student Adviser Roth will
choose today the men from among the (
Freshmen to serve on Saturday.
The following are the minutes of
the Executive Committee for the
meeting of October 25, 1911:
Meeting called to order. President
K. L. Schaupp in the chair.
Thomas absent.
The minutes of the meeting of Octo
ber 18 were read and approved.
It was moved, seconded, and passed,
that the President appoint a committee
to arrange for an entertainment for
the members of the Vancouver team.
Wilson, Lewis, and Beman, chairman,
appointed.
Moved, seconded, and passed, that
Coleman be empowered to purchase a
new interscholastic debating cup at a
cost not to exceed $40.
Moved, seconded, and passed, that
the President appoint a committee to
place and house the Vancouver men
this coming Sunday. Worthington and
Coleman, chairman, appointed.
It was moved, seconded, and passed,
that Roth and Guerena be empowered
to publish the student body hand book.
Roth appointed to supervise the
choosing of Freshmen to act as es
corts to the Australian boys on their
visit to the University.
Meeting adjourned.
R. S. Worthington, secretary.
Vancouver to he Entertained.
It has been the custom in the past
when the teams from British Columbia
visit the University to give an enter
tainment for them. Such an entertain
ment will be given this year. A com
mittee of three was appointed last
evening to supervise the work of the
affair. J. B. Beman, '12, W. S. Wilson,
'13, and L. S. Lewis, '14, were appoint
ed to take charge of the evening. It
is intended to give an informal per
formance of sketches, acrobatic acts,
and the like. The affair will be held
in Encina Club room as in the past.
The cup offered by the student body
to the High Schools of northern Cali
fornia as a debating trophy to be con
tested for annually, to be given per
manently to the school winning it
three successive times, last year be
came the property of San Jose high
school by virtue of three victories. It
was decided last evening to purchase
a new cup to be contested for in the
same way as the old one. This in
sures the continuance of these debates
according to the Executive Committee.
T. L. Coleman, '12, was appointed to
purchase the cup.
Vancouver Men Housed.
Vancouver's second game with the
California Varsity takes place on next
Saturday. Either on that evening or
Sunday morning the team will come
down to Stanford for the series of two
games to be played the following
week. During their stay on the cam
pus they will be quartered in the vari
ous fraternity houses and at Encina.
With this object in mind a committee,
consisting of R. S. Worthington, '12,
and T. L. Coleman, '12, was appointed
