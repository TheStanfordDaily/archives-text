# Y.M.C.A. CONFERENCE TO BE HELD THIS WEEK.
## University Brandies Will Begin Intercollegiate Relations Next Friday. 
### 
Intercollegiate relations will be
taken up by the two university
branches of the Y. M. C. A. in a big
conference to be held Friday evening
of this week between the members of
the cabinets of the two organizations
and their Boards of Directors. Ar
rangements have been made to get
Gale Seaman, International Secretary
of the Association on the Pacific
Coast, to address the meeting, and in
addition men from both of the uni
versities will read papers and discuss
their successes. A special feed is be
ing prepared at which the man can get
acquainted with each other.
This afternoon at 4:30 E. M. Rob
inson, the International Secretary of
Work Among Boys, spoke to 25 men
in the Y. M. C. A. rooms, on, "What
College Men Can Do For Boys of Our
Cities and Country Towns." lie car
ries on most of his work in New York
City, but has been on the Coast for
some time and stopped over between
trains to speak to the local men.
To-morrow evening, R. G. Cole '12,
will load the regular weekly Men's
meeting in the Y. M. C. A. room at
7:15.
