Sophomore Cotillion programs will
be ready for distribution to-morrow
morning at the Bookstore. They con
tain fourteen dances, starting with a
two-step and followed by two waltzes.
The selections alternate in this man
ner throughout the program. The
final dance is the customary waltz.
The two favor dances are not on the
program.
J. L. McDonald, '14, and his nine
piece orchestra, will play for the dan
cers. The music has been carefully
selected and calls for pieces from the
"Uvernian Princess',' "The Follies of
Stanford," and other late and catchy
music.
