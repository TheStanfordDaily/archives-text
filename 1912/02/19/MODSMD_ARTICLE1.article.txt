# SURPRISES SPRUNG IN SATURDAY'S TRACK MEET
## TRACKMEN STARTLE SPECTATORS WITH PERFORMANCES. Juniors and Freshmen Gather Most Points.—Miller's Schedule is Successful. 
### 
Annexing a total of eighty points,
the trackmen'representing the Junior
and Freshmen classes were victors
over their rivals from 1911, 1 IJ. and
1911, in a meet replete with remark
able performances, exciting finishes
a id comedy furnished by the veteran
relay runners of former days, on the
Stanford oval, Saturday morning. The
losing combination scored a total of
sixty-two points. Additional brats in
the horter distances were responsible
for tlv extra points in the grand total.

Coleman Captures Century.
Breasting the tape in the record time
of 10 fiat, Captain T. L. Coleman, '12,
won the century by scant inches from
P. M. McKee, '14. E. P. Campbell, '13,
was third, covering the distance within
the intercollegiate mark for the race.
Campbell took the low hurdles at
which is excellent early season
time. This event was won at a mark
but four-fifths of a second better last
year.
R. L. Murray, '13, opened the morn
ing's sport by finishing the mile in
4:36-3. Freshman Bonnett was the
surprise of the meet by placing first
in the 880 yard run, a scant yard
ahead of W. 13. Fletcher, '11, and F. L.
Gard, '14. The half milers are work
ing close to the two minute mark and
will undoubtedly go under this time in
another week.
G. li. Horine, '13, cleared the bar at
<> feet, 2VL inches in an exhibition leap,
after tying for first place in the high
jump with W. It. Finney, '12. The win
ning mark was (J feet.
J. A. Miller, '13, raised his limit in
the pole vault b> doing 11 feet, 9
inches. This is one inch better than
the winning leap made in the 1911 in
tercollegiate meet by the same athlete.
A. W. Peake, '12, made his entry into
the eleven foot class by taking second
with a vault three inches less than the
first place mark.
Broad jumpers, represented by li
Morrison, '13, C. W. Knight, '14, I).
Dawson, ' 1 , and R. R. Templeton, 'If',
were unable to take the measure of
their older rivals in the relay, and had
to be content with a dead heat.
A veteran team of relay men, com
posed of C. S. Morris, 'OS, .1. O. Miller,
'08, It. S. Worthington, '12, and 1). P.,
Arrell, '10, was defeated by a group of
jumpers turned runners. Morris was
a member of the 1909 intercollegiate
relay team. Miller has been a wearer
of an "S" since 1905. Arrell was one
of the quartet of Cardinal relay men
who participated in this event in the
1908 Provo and Boulder, Colorado,
meets. Worthington represented Stan
ford at the Western Conference meet
at Chicago in 1909.
