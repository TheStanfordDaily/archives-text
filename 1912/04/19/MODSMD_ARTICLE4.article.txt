# NAME ASPIRANTS FOR STUDENT OFFICES
## EDITORSHIP OF "THE SEQUOIA" IS TO BE BONE OF WARM CONTENTION, AS FOUR ARE NAMED TO RUN FOR IT. 
### 
Nominations for Student Body offi
cers to be chosen at the election Wed
nesday, April 24, were made this after
noon at the meeting in the Chapel.
Rut few of the candidates have contests
on their hands, the principal bone of
contention being the office of editor
of the Sequoia, for which honor four
candidates aspire.
Following is a list of nominees:
Student Holy president, A. W. Am
brose, '13; vice-president, C. R. Nunan,
'12: secretary, C. H. Real, '13; gradu
ate-manager and treasurer, D. W. Bur
bank, '09; representative of 191,3 on
Executive Committee, A. H. Frank, and
W. R. Chandler; 1914 representative,

P. P. Clover; 1915 representative, R.
R. Templeton.
I. I. Ingraham, '13, was nominated
for editor-in-chief of the Daily Palo
Alto and J. E. Wolley, '12, was named
for manager. For the editorial toga of
the Sequoia, the following four will
compete: Miss M. Driscoll, '13, C. Paul,
'13, K, Green, '13, and.1. W. Howell,
'12. For business manager of the
publication, E. P. Campbell, *13, is
the only candidate so far mentioned.
A. W. Ambrose is secretary of the
Stanford Inter scholastic Association, a
member of the Executive Committee
and was Campus Secretary of the
Stanford Union. In athletics be won
his numerals during his Freshman
year when he played on the class foot
ball and track teams. Ambrose is
enrolled in the Geology and Mining
department and is a member of Encina
Club.
The candidate for secretary, C. H.
Deal, was captain and stroke of his
Freshman crew and a member of this
year's victorious aggregation. Beal
was president of the 1913 class during
the second semester of his Sophomore
year. He is a Geology and Mining
student and resides in Palo Alto. D.
W. Burbank is making his fourth cam
paign for the post he holds. Burbank
is a graduate from the Law depart
ment, a member of Encina Club, Phi
Delta Phi, and was manager of the
1909 Quad.
I. I. Ingraham, the lone aspirant for
the editor's desk of the Daily Palo
Alto, has been connected with the Uni
versity daily during his entire college
career. He is at present managing edi
tor of the paper. Ingraham refistered
in the History department aud is a
member of the Press Club aim" belta
Kappa Epsilon. J. E. Woolley, the
candidate for managerial duties, has
been a member of the track team for
four years and has won his "S" for
the past three years. He is a member
of Phi Delta Phi. and is also enrolled
in Skull and Snakes, Encina Club, and
the Executive Committee.
Of the candidates for editor of the
Sequoia Miss M. Driscoll is at present
on the Sequoia staff and a member of
the Women's Press Club, Rohle Club
and English Club; C. Paul is on the
Sequoia staff and a contributor to the
Chaparral; K. Green's work has often
been seen on the Sequoia's pages,
while J. W. Howell is at present on the
Sequoia and Chaparral staffs, was edi
tor of last year's Quad, is a member of
the Press Club, Phi Delta Phi, Skull
and Snakes and Beta Theta Phi. He
was eoxswain of his Freshman crew.
Green is a member of the English
Club and Cardinal Club.
E. P. Campbell, candidate for mana
ger of the Sequoia, is a member of En
cina Club and the track team. He is
registered in the Civil Engineering
department.

Nominations for Student Body offi
cers to be chosen at the election Wed
nesday, April 24, were made this after
noon at the meeting in the Chapel.
Rut few of the candidates have contests
on their hands, the principal bone of
contention being the office of editor
of the Sequoia, for which honor four
candidates aspire.
Following is a list of nominees:
Student Holy president, A. W. Am
brose, '13; vice-president, C. R. Nunan,
'12: secretary, C. H. Real, '13; gradu
ate-manager and treasurer, D. W. Bur
bank, '09; representative of 191,3 on
Executive Committee, A. H. Frank, and
W. R. Chandler; 1914 representative,

P. P. Clover; 1915 representative, R.
R. Templeton.
I. I. Ingraham, '13, was nominated
for editor-in-chief of the Daily Palo
Alto and J. E. Wolley, '12, was named
for manager. For the editorial toga of
the Sequoia, the following four will
compete: Miss M. Driscoll, '13, C. Paul,
'13, K, Green, '13, and.1. W. Howell,
'12. For business manager of the
publication, E. P. Campbell, *13, is
the only candidate so far mentioned.
A. W. Ambrose is secretary of the
Stanford Inter scholastic Association, a
member of the Executive Committee
and was Campus Secretary of the
Stanford Union. In athletics be won
his numerals during his Freshman
year when he played on the class foot
ball and track teams. Ambrose is
enrolled in the Geology and Mining
department and is a member of Encina
Club.
The candidate for secretary, C. H.
Deal, was captain and stroke of his
Freshman crew and a member of this
year's victorious aggregation. Beal
was president of the 1913 class during
the second semester of his Sophomore
year. He is a Geology and Mining
student and resides in Palo Alto. D.
W. Burbank is making his fourth cam
paign for the post he holds. Burbank
is a graduate from the Law depart
ment, a member of Encina Club, Phi
Delta Phi, and was manager of the
1909 Quad.
I. I. Ingraham, the lone aspirant for
the editor's desk of the Daily Palo
Alto, has been connected with the Uni

in Skull and Snakes, Encina Club, and
the Executive Committee.
