# MORGAN AND SHELDON WIN CHAMPIONSHIPS
## STANFORD MEN SWEEP MATCHES IN OJAI TOURNEY. Cardinal Players Shut Other Entrants Out in Singles and Win Doubles In Fast Play. 
### 
Winning for Stanford the Intercol
legiate tennis championship of Cali
fornia, H. S. Morgan, '12, and V. A.
Sheldon, '15, today have in their pos
session six trophies won Friday and
Saturday in the nineteenth annual Ojai
Valley tournament at Nordhoff.
Besides winning every intercollegiate
match they entered, Morgan and Shel
don made a substantial showing in the
open singles, participated in by the
best players on the coast.
In the championship singles, Sheldon
and Morgan, entered in,the upper and
lower frames, easily disposed of the
other intercollegiate entrants, and
the Stanford men found themselves
opposed to each other in the finals
for the championship cups. Players
from the University of Southern Cali
fornia, the University of Redlands and
Occidental and Pomona colleges were
outplayed in one-two order by the
Stanford racquet handlers. Sheldon
defeated Morgan, his team-mate, 6-2,
6-3, and won the intercollegiate cham
pionship.
In the doubles, Morgan and Sheldon
won the finals in a match with K. New
ell and F. Chaffee of the University of
Southern California.
Sheldon and Morgan each hold a
doubles championship cup, which will
become the permanent property of the
doubles team that wins three times in
succession. The trophies were annex
ed last year by Winnie Mace and C.
Barker of Occidental. In addition,
Morgan and Sheldon each hold a per
manent trophy mug for this year's
championship. Sheldon holds the sin
gles championship cup, won last year
by Mace, and a companion individual
trophy mug.
Following is the summary of the
matches played by Morgan and Sheldon
Championship Singles—Morgan de
feated Chaffee, U. S. C., 6-3, 6-0; Hun
tington, Redlands, 6-0, 6-2; Dixon, U.
S. C., 5-7, 6-4, 6-3; Sheldon defeated
Huntington, Occidental, 6-4, 6-0; Cage,
Redlands, default; K. Newell, U. S. C.,
6-2, 6-1. Finals, Sheldon defeated Mor
gan, both of Stanford, 6-2, 6-3.
Championship Doubles—Morgan and
Sheldon defeated Dixon and Hall, U. S.
C., 6-4, 7-9, 6-4; Kingman and Cage,
Pomona, 6-2, 6-2; Newell and Chaffe,
U. S. C., 6-2, 6-3.

Winning for Stanford the Intercol
legiate tennis championship of Cali
fornia, H. S. Morgan, '12, and V. A.
Sheldon, '15, today have in their pos
session six trophies won Friday and
Saturday in the nineteenth annual Ojai
Valley tournament at Nordhoff.
Besides winning every intercollegiate
match they entered, Morgan and Shel
don made a substantial showing in the
open singles, participated in by the
best players on the coast.
In the championship singles, Sheldon
and Morgan, entered in,the upper and
lower frames, easily disposed of the
other intercollegiate entrants, and
the Stanford men found themselves
opposed to each other in the finals
for the championship cups. Players
from the University of Southern Cali
fornia, the University of Redlands and
Occidental and Pomona colleges were
outplayed in one-two order by the
Stanford racquet handlers. Sheldon
defeated Morgan, his team-mate, 6-2,
6-3, and won the intercollegiate cham
pionship.
In the doubles, Morgan and Sheldon
won the finals in a match with K. New
ell and F. Chaffee of the University of
Southern California.
Sheldon and Morgan each hold a
doubles championship cup, which will
become the permanent property of the
doubles team that wins three times in
succession. The trophies were annex
ed last year by Winnie Mace and C.
Barker of Occidental. In addition,
Morgan and Sheldon each hold a per
manent trophy mug for this year's
championship. Sheldon holds the sin
gles championship cup, won last year
by Mace, and a companion individual
trophy mug.
