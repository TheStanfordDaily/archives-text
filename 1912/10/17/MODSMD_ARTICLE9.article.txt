# CREW COACHES APPOINTED BY EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE
## FRESHMEN ARE AWARDED BLOCK NUMERALS. Football Coaching Committee Empowered to Grant Eighteen Block Sixteen's to Players. 
### 
The Rowing Coaching Committee of
five, which will have charge of the
principal aquatic activities for the
coming year was appointed by the
Executive Committee last evening.
Crew Captain J. F. Partridge, '12, R. F.
Duryea, '13, C. H. Beal, '13, P. P.
Clover, '14, and R. M. Finch, '14, were
named. These men will serve as
coaches and will pick the Cardinal
oarsmen for the big races.
Eighteen members of the Freshman
squad who played in the recently
closed season will be presented with
their block numerals. The candidates
will be named by the Football Coach
ing Committee.
An auditing committee was also
named to pass monthly upon the re
ports of the managers of the Sequoia
and the Daily Palo Alto, and the
Graduate Manager. .T. E. McDowell,
'00, R. F. Duryea, '13, and A. W. Am
, brose, '13, will act in this capacity.
Stanford is to apply for membership
in the Pacific Coast Lawn Tennis
Association, a motion being made and
passed last night that membership be
secured.
Arrangements were also made last
evening for the publishing of the Stu
dent Body Handbook.
Following are the minutes:
President A. W. Ambrose in the
chair.
Roll call. Schaupp, Summerfield,
Burbank, Cass and Frank absent.
Moved, seconded and passed that
eighteen men of the Freshman team,
picked by the Coaching Committee on
their season's showing, be awarded
block "16's."
J. F. Partridge, '12, R. F. Duryea,
'13, C. H. Beal, '13, P. P. Clover, '14.
R. M. Finch, '14, were appointed on the
Rowing Committee for the coming
season.
It was moved, seconded and passed
that Stanford University become a
member of the Pacific Coast Lawn
Tennis Association.
It was moved, seconded and passed
that an auditing committee of three
be appointed by the chair to audit
monthly the reports of the Sequoia,
Daily Palo Alto, and the Graduate
Manager. .T. E. McDowell, '00, R. F.
Duryea, '13, and A. W. Ambrose, '13.
were appointed.
A. W. Ambrose, '13, K. L. Schaupp,
'12, and T. I. Tngraham, '13, were ap
pointed to put out the Student Body
Handbook.
Adjourned.

The Rowing Coaching Committee of
five, which will have charge of the
principal aquatic activities for the
coming year was appointed by the
Executive Committee last evening.
Crew Captain J. F. Partridge, '12, R. F.
Duryea, '13, C. H. Beal, '13, P. P.
Clover, '14, and R. M. Finch, '14, were
named. These men will serve as
coaches and will pick the Cardinal
oarsmen for the big races.
Eighteen members of the Freshman
squad who played in the recently
closed season will be presented with
their block numerals. The candidates
will be named by the Football Coach
ing Committee.
An auditing committee was also
named to pass monthly upon the re
ports of the managers of the Sequoia
and the Daily Palo Alto, and the
Graduate Manager. .T. E. McDowell,
'00, R. F. Duryea, '13, and A. W. Am
, brose, '13, will act in this capacity.
Stanford is to apply for membership
in the Pacific Coast Lawn Tennis
Association, a motion being made and
passed last night that membership be
secured.
Arrangements were also made last
evening for the publishing of the Stu
dent Body Handbook.
