# PROFESSORS OF HISTORY WILL MEET IN BERKELEY.
## 
### 
Stanford will he represented by four
speakers from the faculty at the tenth
annual meeting of the Pacific Coast
branch of the American Historical
Association at the University of Cali
fornia next Friday and Saturday.
P. A. Martin will speak Friday after
noon on the subject, "Notes on the
Biography of Cardinal Schinner." At
the dinner, Friday evening, Professor
A. B. Show, president of the associa
tion, will deliver an address. Profes
sor E. E. Robinson will tell of "Party
Grouping in the Twenty-Second Con
gress," and Professor M. S. Wildman,
of the Economics department, will dis
cuss the subject "Some Effects of Pub
lic Opinion."
