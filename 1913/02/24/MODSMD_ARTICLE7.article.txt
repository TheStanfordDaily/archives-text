# STANFORD TAKES THIRD GAME FROM ST. MARY'S
## EXCITING CONTEST WON IN THE NINTH INNING. Workman Stars at Bat Scoring Six Runs.—Maple Twirls Extra Good Game. 
### 
Three runs in the ninth inning broke
a 4 to 4 tie and won for Stanford
against St. Mary's on Saturday. A dou
ble from Workman's bat cleared the
bases and put the game on ice. Three
straight wins from this team gives the
Cardinal the series from these players,
as but one more contest is scheduled
with St. Mary's before the first inter
collegiate game.
Workman starred at bat, hitting safe
ly three times and bringing in six of
the Stanford runs. Terry was next in
line, getting on base five times and
scoring four tallies. Maple pitched a
good game allowing but seven hits and
tightening up in the pinches besides
starting the ninth inning rally with a
single.
Captain Terry scored the first run in
the opening inning without the aid of a
hit. In the third he again reached
first, went to second on Argabrite's
single, each advanced on a double steal
and later scored on Workman's single.
With the score three to nothing against
them in the fourth St. Mary's scored
twice on two hits and an error. In the
fifth Terry again reached first and was
i scored by the Stanford first baseman.
In the seventh the losers tied the score
j after two were out on a double and
two errors.
