# VARSITY WINS EIGHTH STRAIGHT BALL GAME
## OLYMPIC CMUB DEFEATED 6 TO 3 ON SATURDAY Varsity Ways Without Error and Hits Hard—Halm and Hayes Work Well. 
### 
Stanford won her eighth straight
game on Saturday when the Olympic
Club team was defeated by a 6-3 score.
This contest completed play with this
nine for the season, the Varsity hav
ing won all three games.
Saturday's game while not a contest
of the nip and tuck variety, was never
theless an interesting contest to watch,
plenty of hitting and fast fielding on
the part of the Varsity being display
ed. The Stanford men hit the ball
safely nine times and fielded without
error.
Halm opened the game and pitched
good ball for five innings, being re
lieved by Hayes. Stanford scored in
the second inning after two were out.
Workman was hit by a pitched ball,
advanced to third on Dent's double,
and both scored on Cass' single. In
the fourth McCloskey doubled,, ad
vanced on Halm's infield hit, and
scored on Terry's long fly to left. Ar
gabrite's single registered Halm a
minute later.
Rippon hit for four bases in the sec
ond inning, and the other Olympic runs
came one at a time, the city men never
leading, the game.
