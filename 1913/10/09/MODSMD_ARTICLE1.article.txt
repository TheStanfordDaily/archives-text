# PHILADELPHIA OVERWHELMS NEW YORK IN THE THIRD GAME OF WORLD'S SERIES
## Tesreau and Crandail Hit to All Corners While Athletics Pile Up Eight Runs 
### 
Special to Daily Palo Alto by United Press.
POLO GROUNDS, New York, Oct. J.
—Mack's first kid pitcher made good
today. Bush, tiie Salt Lake City
youngster, had the Giants at his mer
cy and let them down with a scant
pair of runs and five hits.
The Athletics took their jump in
the first and second innings, bunch
ing hits on Tesreau. Again in the
seventh they hit Tesreau for a triple
and two singles, netting them two
more runs. The Ozart Giant then
went to the bench, and Crandall fol
lowed him on the mound. Crandall
started out nicely, but Schang caught
one to his liking and laced out a
homer.
The Athletics outplayed, outfielded,
and outbatted the Giants.
The batteries were announced as
Tesreau and McLean for the Giants,
Hush and Schang for the Athletics.
First inning—"Athletics: Murphy
went out, Fletcher to Merkle. Then
Oldring singled and was followed by
Collins, who also drove out a single.
Raker came up and sent out the third
single of the inning, scoring Oldring.
Mclnnis fanned. Collins and Harry
both scored on Fletcher's wild throw
over Merkle's head. Harry popped to
Fletcher. Three runs.
Giants: Herzog was out, Barry to
Mclnnis. Doyle singled and Fletche.-
wa3 hit by a pitched ball. Burns
popped to Collins, who tossed to Har
ry, doubling up Doyle. No runs.
Second inning—Athletics: Schang
fanned and Hush Hied to Murray, after
which Murphy and Oldring singled.
Collins then singled to Center, scor
ing Murphy and Oldring. Baker forc
ed Collins, Doyle unassisted. Two
runs.
Giants: Shafer was out, Collins ,o
Mclnnis. Murray flied to Collins and
McLean popped to Schang. No runs.
Third inning—Athletics: Mclnnis
flied to Burns, and Strunk followed
with one to tlie same place. Barry
was out on a pop to McLean. No
runs.
Giants: Merkle Hied to Strunk and
Tesreau fanned. Herzog popped 'o
Collins. No runs.
Fourth inning—Athletics: Schang
fanned, then Bush singled. Murphy
filed to Shafer, and Oldring went out,
Doyle to Merkle. No runs.
Giants: Doyle fouled to Baker and
Fletcher singled. Then Burns fanned
and Fletcher stole second. Shafer
went out, Collins to Mclnnis. No runs.
Fifth Inning—Athletics: Collins
out on a fly to Murray. Baken flied
to Fletcher and Mclnnis Hied to Mur
ray. No runs.
Giants: Murray walked and thon
stole second. McLean singled, scor
ing Murray. Cooper ran for McLean.
Merkle Hied to Murphy. Cooper stole
second. Tesreau went out, Baker to
Mclnnis, Cooper taking third. Her
zog went out, Schang to Mclnnis,
One run.
Sixth inning—Athletics: Strunk up
and Hied to Burns. Barry singled and
Wilson relieved McLean behind the
bat. Schang fouled out to Wilson.
Bush Hied to Doyle. No runs.
Giants: Doyle was out, Mclnnis
unassisted. Fletcher walked and then
Burns hit into a double play, Bush to
Barry, to Mclnnis. No runs.
Seventh inning—Athletics: Murphy
singled. Oldring forced Murphy,
Fletcher to Doyle. Collins tripled,
scoring Oldring, and Baker followed
with a single, scoring Collins. Cran
dall relieved Tesreau. Mclnnis flied
to Doyle, who touched out Baker.
Two runs.
Giants: Shafer doubled and Murray
singled, scoring Shafer. Murray was
out stealing second, Schang to Col
out stealing second, Sschang to Col
lins. Wilson fanned. Merkle walked,
Wiltse running for Merkle. Crandall
out, Collins to Mclnnis. One run.
Eighth inning—Athletics: Strunk
went out, Crandall to Wiltse, who had
relieved Merkle at first. Barry was
out, Crandall to Wiltse. Schang hit
a home run. Bush fanned. One run.
(Jiant3: Herzog lined to Baker and
Doyle went out,Mclnnis unassnrted.
Fletcher fouled to Schang. No runs.
Ninth inning—Athletics: Murphy
Hied to Murray. Oldring fouled to
Herzog. Collins flied to Shafer. No
runs.
Giants: Burns flied to Murphy and
Shafer drew a pass. Murray forced
Shafer, Barry to Collins. Wilson then
fouled out to Baker. No runs.
R. H. E.

today. Bush, tiie Salt Lake City
youngster, had the Giants at his mer
cy and let them down with a scant
pair of runs and five hits.
The Athletics took their jump in
the first and second innings, bunch
ing hits on Tesreau. Again in the
seventh they hit Tesreau for a triple
and two singles, netting them two
more runs. The Ozart Giant then
went to the bench, and Crandall fol
lowed him on the mound. Crandall
started out nicely, but Schang caught
one to his liking and laced out a
homer.

and outbatted the Giants.
