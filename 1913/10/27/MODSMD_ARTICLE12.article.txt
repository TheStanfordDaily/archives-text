# VARSITY ANNEXES SLOW GAME FROM BARBARIANS
## FOREWARDS FOREMOST IN MATCH WITH 21-5 SCORE Second Varsity Accepts Nine Points to Three of Insurrectos in Preliminary. 
### 
The Varsity defeated the Barbar
ians Saturday in a slow game
that was appropriated by the forwards
who smothered most chances for back
Held play. Passing rushes were rare
on both sides, and the ball rarely
appeared back of the scrum. The
clubmen were in the game, and their
defense was effective most of the
time. King, of the Stanford squad,
played full for the Barbs. Sargent
and Braden also helped out the vis
itors. The Second Varsity pinned a
9 to 3 discount tag on the Insurrectos
in a preliminary match that resem
bled the principal exhibition. An
drews' accurate kicking to touch for
long gains from the full back's posi
tion featured the game.
Patterson kicked oft for Stanford,
and the Varsity was successfully
fought back from the goal for a half
hour. At the end of that time Hall
took the ball from a three yard line
out and crossed the line. Darsie fail
ed to convert the only time during
the match.
The scoring for the first half was
ended with a second try from a five
yard scrum by Blase and a conver
sion by Darsie, raising the score to 8.
Second Half.
At the beginning of the second half
the Varsity began a series of fast
rushes which brought the ball to the
thirty yard line, where Darsie was
given a penalty kick, which went true
for three points.
Several chances were given the
Barbs to score, but they took advant
age of only one. In a three yard
scrum, Cashel secured and crossed
Ihe line. Brown converted, bringing
the Barbs' score to highwater mark,
five.
The prettiest passing run of the
game resulted in the Varsity's next
try. Erb took the ball from a forty
yard scrum and passed to Davidson,
who passed to Urban for a try, after
a fast thirty yard run downfield. Dar
sie converted.
Carroll ended the scoring play by
scooping the ball from the ground
neatly on the Barb forty yard line
and running to a try. Darsie con
verted. The final score was 21-5.
Second Varsity.
In the Second Varsity-Insurrectos
game, Clark, Rehm, Halm, Pettingill,
Whitaker and Burns figured prin
cipally in the scoring runs. Sample,
the visitors' rear ranker, scored their
only try.
The line-ups were:
Varsity:—Til ton (Andrews), full;
Urban, Reeves, wings; Carroll
(Wynne), center; Patterson (Lach
mund), second five; Austin (David
son), first five; Erb, Half; Card (Og
den), Blase, Darsie, Clover, Watkins
(Dubendorf), Hall, Peck, Wines, for
wards.
Barbarians:—King, full; Couch,
Smith, wings; Brezoni, center; La
mare second five; Momson, first five;
Schmidt, half; Boulware, Phinnister,
Cohen, Bartel, Cashel, Reid, Brown,
Perkins, forwards.
Second Varsity:—Andrews, full;
Long, Halm, wings; Hollister, center;
Huttman, second five; Dickey, first
five; Burns, half; Harrigan, Vauder
burg, Whitaker, Rehm, Pettingill,
Clark, Franceschi, Fyfe, forwards.
Insurrectos: —Condee, full; Smith,
Bishop, wings; McGuire, center;
Gibbs, second five; Easton, first five;
McMnrtry, half; Monlux, Sample,
Holmes, Christensen, Wrin, Fowler,
Tippen, Snook, forwards.

weight and experience, the Palo Alto

that was appropriated by the forwards
