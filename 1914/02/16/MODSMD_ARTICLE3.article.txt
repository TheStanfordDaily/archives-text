# TENNIS TOURNAMENT IS MARKED BY SURPRISES
## Uhls Wins Place on Team to Meet California by Defeating Hutchinson. 
### 
By defeating J. S. Hutchinson, 'IG,
in the final match of the first try-out
tennis tournament on Saturday after
noon, K. B. Uhls, '16, won a place on
the team that is to meet California
this spring. Five sets were necessary
to decide the winner, Hutchinsin tak
ing the first two and Uhls scoring in
the last three. The scores were 5-7,
.1-0, G-3, 6-4, 6-4.
The playing of both the victor and
the runner-up has been the surprise
of the tournament. Each man won
his way into the final by defeating a
veteran of former intercollegiate
teams. Hutchinson was credited with
a victory over Captain L. Sloss, '15,
during the week, and Uhls vanquished
ex-Captain V. L .Sheldon, '15, in
straight sets, 6-1, 6-3, on Friday.
The second try-out tournament was
also started over the week-end, Sloss
and H. L. Halm, '16, reaching the
semi-final round. Sloss won his
place by virtue of two victories, re
feating P. M. Bennett, '16, 6-3, 6-2, and
S. Chapman. '16, 6-4, 6-3, while Hahn
reached the semi-finals as the result of
of the default of H. M. Jones. '17.
Earlier in the play Chapman defeat
ed O. T. Brun, '16, in a three set
match, 1-6, 6-4, 9-7, and J. M. Ham
mond, '17, fell before A. McAndrew,
'17, 6-4, 6-2.
