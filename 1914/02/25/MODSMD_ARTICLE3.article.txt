# COMMITTEES TO ARRANGE FOR SENIOR WEEK EVENTS APPOINTED BY WATKINS
## 
### 
President F. B. Watkins of the
Senior class lias named the men and
women of the graduating class who
will conduct the various events of
Senior Week. The students who
compose the committees have contrib
uted to the success of Stanford activi
ties during the four years they have
attended this University, and merit
well the honor which has been be
stowed upon them.
The appointment of Miss Betty

Glover Funsten and Frank J. Gard to
the Flower Committee has been con
firmed in this final arrangement.
O. H. Castle will write the chronicle
of 1914's career in college. The class
will has been placed in the keeping of
A. M. Standish, while E. C. Behrens
will deliver the commencement day
oration.
Miss Carol M. Green has been re
warded for her efforts in women's af
fairs by the position of permanent
class secretary.
The Senior Week Committee, which
will have general supervision over the
festivities preceding graduation, con
sists of the Misses Eileen Everett,
Beatrice Bingham, Carol Green, Jean
Garver, Helen T. Billiard, and P. P.
Clover, L. \V. Lewis, S. J. W. Sharp,
C. W. Knight, G. O. Wilson, P. B. Mc-
Kee, and F. B. Watkins, chairman.
The chairmanships of the various
sub-committees have been granted to
the following members of the class:
Miss J. -T. Garver, program; L. W.
Lewis, Senior farce; Miss Carol
(ireen, Senior-Alunmni reception; S.
J. W. Sharp, class plate; C. W.
Knight, carnival; P. B. McKee,
Senior prom; P. P. Clover, Senior
ball; and G. O. Wilson, finance.
The personnel of the committees as
announced today by the Senior Presi
dent are as follows:
