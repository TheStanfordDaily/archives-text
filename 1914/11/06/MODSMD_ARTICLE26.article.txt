# On the Quiet
## 
### 
Stolon Bases.
Russian victories may be pro
nounced successes, but we can't pro
nounce 'em.
Race prejudice has kept many a dol
lar out of the bookmaker's hanrls.
'Tis said the optimist sees the dough
nut—the pessimist the hole. More like
this —the pessimist sees the doughnut,
while the optimist looks right through
the hole and sees the next doughnut.
After children grow up their parents
often prove a disappointment.
War Poem by the Czar.
Shots must fly,
Shells must burst.
At home I stay—
"Safety First!"
—Hearst.
fnrriinnl Virtues.
Attending chapel.
Queening only on week-ends.
Wearing white collars.
Reading the poetry in the Daily.
Refusing to scan the Chaparral.
Never passing roughs' alley.
Concentrating on "work" in the li
brary.
Come to think of it, the rush tomor
row night will give '1"> great, oppor
tunity for head work. Without a
doubt, "Ifi has he.r dodgers ready.
