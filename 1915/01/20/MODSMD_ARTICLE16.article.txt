# Y.W.C.A. ADDRESSED BY EXPOSITION WORKER
## 
### 
Miss T. Brookman, who has charge
of some 2000 women employed by the
Panama-Pacific International Exposi
tion, spoke to the Y. W. C. A. yester
day. She told of the different amuse
ments at the Exposition grounds, and
of the amount of work and time being
spent, on beautifying the place, but
until lately the great lack of efforts
fo.r the protection of women employes
and visitors.
Mr. McClellan, the traveling secre
tary for the Student Volunteeers, gave
an interesting talk on the need of
American missionaries in the Orient.
