# JUNIOR WEEK PROGRAM WILL BE LAUNCHED TOMORROW NIGHT AT LAGUNITA
## OPERA SET FOR THURSDAY Aquatic Sports on Lake, Pomona-Occidental Track Meet, Prom, Marathon and Baseball Complete Week 
### 
Beginning Wednesday night with
the fire-works and music on the lake,
Junior Week participants are offer
ed an alluring program for the whole
of the festival season. The opera, the
Prom and the lake celebrations, and
a half dozen events of first impor
tance on the athletic fields fill the
week-end, up to Saturday evening.
Attractive displays and music are
already arranged by the Fireworks
and Music committees for Wednes
day evening. With a prediction that
the present favorable weather will
continue, Junior Week will start to
morrow night under most favorable
circumstances.
The opera, "At the Dragon's Eye,"
is the Thursday night attraction. In
the afternoon of that day the inter
class soccer series will be played. The
Juniors will meet the seniors and
the Freshman the Sophomores and the
winners of these matches will then
play for the inter-class title.
On Friday morning, Junior Day,
the lake events will start on Lagun
ita at 9:30. The first entry on the
program is the inter-class race and
swimming, diving, and canoe contests
follow in rapid succession with no
delays. A long list of prizes has been
offered by Palo Alto merchants as
awards to the winners. The band will
be on hand all morning to dispense
music to the spectators on the banks
of Lagunita.
The track meet between Stanford
q.nd the combined Pomona-Occidental
team is scheduled for Friday afternoon.
Manager Wilcox has also arranged for
a freshman meet with Santa Clara
which will be run off in conjunction
with the varsity event.
In the evening comes the apex of
all the Junior W*eek functions—the
Junior Prom. Seignor Zampato, Mem
orial Church decorator, has designed
the beautiful decorations which will
adrvn Encina C'lubroom.
To the dreamy music of the Cliff
House Orchestra, the dancers will rev
el in a veritable power of beauty.
Tickets to the Prom are selling
rap'dly. A larse number of dancers
is assured the Junior Class. All those
having dates should make sure of
getting tickets by purchasing imme
diately.
Two big events are scheduled on
Saturday. The annual Irish Mara
thon with all its side stunts will be
held in the morning. This event is
described in another column. In the
afternoon Stanford and California will
clash, in the first game of the inter
collegiate baseball series, and this
will end the events of 1916's Junior
Week.
The order of events on the lake
Friday morning lias been determined
and the list of prizes for the win
ners is announced below. It is essen
tial that the committees in charge
know immediately the entries in the
inter-class swimming race and in the
canoe races. A. J. Oyster, '16, G.
E. Crary, '15, W. H. Henry, '18, and
E. R. Obrikat, '15, comprize the swim
ming committee and one of them will
be at the lake tevery afternoon after
four o'clock to take the names of
those entering.
A scarcity of canoes may prevent
some of the enthusiasts from entering
the canos races. If that is found to
lie true, those signing up first to en
ter will he given preference. Those
wishing to enter canoe races for the
tilting contests may hand in their
names to C. H. Orme, '16. P. N. Worth,
'If!, or H. Goodman. '16.
One new event on the lake this year
will he a float fight. Ten Sophomores
w'll contest with ten Freshmen in
this minature naval battle.
The officials of Friday morning's
contests are: starter. D. it. Carroll,
'16; race judges, E. M. Bonnett, *15,
and P. R. Wilson, '16; timers, Pro
fessor C. B. Wing and Dr. Frank An
gell. E. Smith, a well known diver
from California, will be one of the
judges of the diving contests and the
other will be chosen later.
The events will start at 9:30 sharp.
The order and the prizes for the win
ners follow:
1. Interclass crew race.
2. Interclass swimming meet.
a. 50 yds. Prize —Gold chain. (The
Regent.)
i). 100 yds. Prize —$3.50 hat. (Wide
man & Son.)
c. 220 yds Prize —$3.50 safety razor.
(Ws'ngartner & Co.)
d. Diving contest. Prize —$2.50 to
bacco jar. (Larkin's)
e. Plunging contest. Prize—$2.50
picture. (Robertson)
f. Relay, four men; 25 yd. sprints.
3. Canoe races:
a. Singles. $1.50 Kaiser tie. (The
Regent)
b. Doubles. 1-2 dozen sox and $1.00
tie. (Wideman & Son)
4. Tilting contest—$3.50 fountain
pen. (Un'versity Pharmacy) $1.50
jewelry sot. (Christy Rhodes *" Co.)
5. Float fight—Prize 2 three lb.
boxes of chocolates. (Wilson's)

Beginning Wednesday night with
the fire-works and music on the lake,
Junior Week participants are offer
ed an alluring program for the whole
of the festival season. The opera, the
Prom and the lake celebrations, and
a half dozen events of first impor
tance on the athletic fields fill the
week-end, up to Saturday evening.
Attractive displays and music are
already arranged by the Fireworks
and Music committees for Wednes
day evening. With a prediction that
the present favorable weather will
continue, Junior Week will start to
morrow night under most favorable
circumstances.
The opera, "At the Dragon's Eye,"
is the Thursday night attraction. In
the afternoon of that day the inter
class soccer series will be played. The
Juniors will meet the seniors and
the Freshman the Sophomores and the
winners of these matches will then
play for the inter-class title.
On Friday morning, Junior Day,
the lake events will start on Lagun
ita at 9:30. The first entry on the
program is the inter-class race and
swimming, diving, and canoe contests
follow in rapid succession with no
delays. A long list of prizes has been
offered by Palo Alto merchants as
awards to the winners. The band will
be on hand all morning to dispense
music to the spectators on the banks
of Lagunita.
The track meet between Stanford
q.nd the combined Pomona-Occidental
team is scheduled for Friday afternoon.
Manager Wilcox has also arranged for
a freshman meet with Santa Clara
which will be run off in conjunction
with the varsity event.
In the evening comes the apex of
all the Junior W*eek functions—the
Junior Prom. Seignor Zampato, Mem
orial Church decorator, has designed
the beautiful decorations which will
adrvn Encina C'lubroom.
To the dreamy music of the Cliff
House Orchestra, the dancers will rev
el in a veritable power of beauty.
Tickets to the Prom are selling
rap'dly. A larse number of dancers
is assured the Junior Class. All those
having dates should make sure of
getting tickets by purchasing imme
diately.
Two big events are scheduled on
Saturday. The annual Irish Mara
thon with all its side stunts will be
held in the morning. This event is
described in another column. In the
afternoon Stanford and California will
clash, in the first game of the inter
collegiate baseball series, and this
will end the events of 1916's Junior
Week.
