# IRISH MARATHONERS READY FOR SPRINTS
## Holdovers in Clown Stunts to Add to Merriment Provoked by Obstacle Race. 
### 
Tomorrow morning Seniors, Juniors,
Sophomores and Freshmen will vie
for honors in the Quadrangle Club's
annual Irish Marathon, wliile squads
of holdovers will compete in the at
tempt to create the greatest number
of laughs in:10 flat. Judging from
the air of secrecy which prevails in
holdover ranks, these skits will con
tain many novel departures from tlie
comedy numbers of previous Mara
thons.
Many have signified their inten
tions to participate in the three fea
ture races of the day. but the entry
list is by no means closed, anyone
being eligible to compete.
The big amusement of the day is
the obstacle race, recommended by
Danny Carroll, '16, as exactly similar
to the* latest Australian contests. Ad
venturous mariners who embark on
this 300 yard voyage must combat
such barriers as a tarpaulin, ladder
rungs, suspended bags, suspended ap
ples and barrels. These must be pas
sed under, over or around in the most
difficult manner that the committee
has been able to advise. To further
retard their speed, contestants must
hop fifty yards of the distance on one
and the same foot. All apples used
in this event will be carefully in
spected, and only the largest and jui
ciest pippins will be strung up for
the runners to bite.
Preliminary features planned are a
75 yard three-legged Siamese race
and a sack race over a course of the
same length.
Marathon teams have been announc
ed by the various class captains. Each
of the twenty-four men named is to
run a quarter-mile lap for his class.
Awards in the shape of gold medals
will go to the varsity man and the
novices who make the best time in
their respective divisions. The varsity
and novice entrants who run the
second fastest laps will receive silver
medallions similar in design to those
of the winners.
A. E. Worthy has asked that all
men having stop watches appear with
them at the meet. He asks that the
captains of the various teams have
their men listed in the order in which
they will run.
