# SWIMMING CLUB DANCE WILL INITIATE GYMNASIUM FLOOR
## 
### 
The new gymnasium is to be offi
cially opened next Saturday night
when a dance is to be given under the
auspices of the Swimming Club.
The Swimming Club which existed
last semester as a loose organization
has taken an active stand in student
affairs, and it is due to its activity that
the new swimming pool has been com
pleted. Under its supervision the
necessary funds were guaranteed by
a loan from the Associated Students.
The dance will be given with the
expectation of defraying a part of the
expense incurred in the construction
of the pool.
Tickets are $1.00 and may be ob
tained with the programs at the Stan
ford book sto.re or at Wingartner's in
Palo Alto. A. J. Oyster, '16, is in gen
eral charge.
