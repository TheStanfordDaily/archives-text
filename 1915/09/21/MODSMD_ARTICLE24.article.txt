# HAHN AND HOLT TIED
## 
### 
With J. Holt, '19 and H. L. Hahn.
'16, tied with one set each in their
match in the second round of the Re
gent Cup Tournament, five men re
mained in the race for the prize at 3:30
o'clock this afternoon. Yesterday
these two players also tied after play
ing two sets, but Holt was too tired to
continue play. This afternoon, how
ever, play was continued. The other
three men remaining are C. Wolford,
'IS, V. I). Johns, '18, and C. S. Teits
worth, '19. In the second round of
play Wolford defeated .Tones 6-0, 4-6,
6-2. Teitsworth had no trouble in de
feating Melcon. 6-2, 6-3. Johns bested
ITawkes in straight sets, 3-6, 6-3, 6-4,
while Holt and Hahn had each an
nexed a 6 4 set at 3:30.
