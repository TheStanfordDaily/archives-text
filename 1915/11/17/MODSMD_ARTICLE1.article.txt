# ELEVEN NEW MEMBERS ELECTED TO PHI BETA KAPPA
## SIX TO GRADUATE CHRISTMAS Five Women and Six Men Included In List Chosen for Honor 
### 
Six Stanford men and five women
have been elected to Phi Beta Kappa,
the national scholarship honor society,
according to an announcement just
made public by Assistant Registrar
J. E. McDowell. Of this number six
are members of the January graduat
ing class, while five will receive their
diplomas in May.
Those honored from the January
class are A. J. Hettinger, Miss Hazel
M. Fisher, R. E. Best, Miss Mary Jo
sephine Beebe, Miss Margaret Irving
Forbes, and Miss Ruby M. Kerr.
Hettinger Is Honored
Hettinger is one of the best debat
ers Stanford has ever produced, hav
ing done efficient work in the intercol
legiate debates of this year and last,
and having competed in last year's
Carnot contest. He is a member of
Delta Sigma Rho, debating honor fra
ternity, and of Euphronia Debating
Society. He is also treasurer of the
Stanford Y. M. C. A. Hailing from
Fowler, he registers in the Economics
department and resides at the Faculty
Club bouse.
Miss Fisher Elected
Miss Fisher is president of Women's
Conference and Roble Club. She be
longs to Cap and Gown, and majors in
German, coming from Penryn.
Hest is a Physiology major. River
side is his home town. Miss Beebe is
a member of Roble Club, registering
in the History department from Wood-
land. Miss Forbes holds membership
in Kappa Kappa Gamma Sorority,
lives in Palo Alto, and specializes in
German. Miss Kerr is from San Jose,
and majors in History.
Five From .May Class
For special distinction in their aca
demic work, the following five mem
bers of the May class were elected to
Phi Beta Kappa: *R. N. Wenzel, G. C.
Hodges, Miss Katherine Hutchinson,
N. W. Rakestraw, and G. G. Ferris.
Wenzel is a Chemistry major, and
is best known on the campus for his
art work, having served as art editor
of the 191 r, Quad, and being on the
art staff of Chaparral. He belongs
to Hammer and Coffin and Encina
Club, and is a member of the Y. M. C.
A. cabinet, having edited the last edi
tion of the Stanford Handbook, or
"Freshman Bible." St. Louis, Mis
souri, is Wenzel's home.
Hodges, who lives in Palo Alto, is
an assistant in the History depart
ment, in which he is .registered. He
is a frequent contributor to the Inter
national Magazine and other high
class periodicals, bis special work be
ing the treatment of the relations ex
isting between the United States and
the far east.
Miss Hutchinson is a Kappa Alpha
Theta Sorority member, and makes
German her major subject. He.r home
is in Palo Alto. Rakestraw registers
in Chemistry from Los Angeles, and
belongs to Encina Club and Gym Club.
Ferris also belongs to Encina Club,
bailing from Modesto, and making En
tomology his universitl specialty.
The History and German depart
ments tie for representation, each
claiming three of the novitiates.
Chemistry follows with two, while
each of the following departments
claim one apiece: Physiology, Eco
nomics, and Entomology.
Roble Club claims the largest num
ber of women members to receive the
scholarship honor—with two. Kappa
Kappa Gamma and Kappa Alpha
Theta Sororities each claim one, while
the fifth woman lives in San Jose.
For new men members in Phi Beta
Kappa, Encina Club ranks highest with
three names on the honor roll. Two
others live in Palo Alto, and one stays
at the Faculty Clubhouse. Palo Alto
leads the home-town list, claiming
three native sons and daughters
among the scholars.

Six Stanford men and five women
have been elected to Phi Beta Kappa,
the national scholarship honor society,
according to an announcement just
made public by Assistant Registrar
J. E. McDowell. Of this number six
are members of the January graduat
ing class, while five will receive their
diplomas in May.
Those honored from the January
class are A. J. Hettinger, Miss Hazel
M. Fisher, R. E. Best, Miss Mary Jo
sephine Beebe, Miss Margaret Irving
Forbes, and Miss Ruby M. Kerr.
Hettinger Is Honored
Hettinger is one of the best debat
ers Stanford has ever produced, hav
ing done efficient work in the intercol
legiate debates of this year and last,
and having competed in last year's
Carnot contest. He is a member of
Delta Sigma Rho, debating honor fra
ternity, and of Euphronia Debating
Society. He is also treasurer of the
Stanford Y. M. C. A. Hailing from
Fowler, he registers in the Economics
department and resides at the Faculty
Club bouse.
Miss Fisher Elected
Miss Fisher is president of Women's
Conference and Roble Club. She be
longs to Cap and Gown, and majors in
German, coming from Penryn.
Hest is a Physiology major. River
side is his home town. Miss Beebe is
a member of Roble Club, registering
in the History department from Wood-
land. Miss Forbes holds membership
in Kappa Kappa Gamma Sorority,
lives in Palo Alto, and specializes in
German. Miss Kerr is from San Jose,
and majors in History.
Five From .May Class
For special distinction in their aca
demic work, the following five mem
bers of the May class were elected to
Phi Beta Kappa: *R. N. Wenzel, G. C.
Hodges, Miss Katherine Hutchinson,
N. W. Rakestraw, and G. G. Ferris.
Wenzel is a Chemistry major, and
is best known on the campus for his
art work, having served as art editor
of the 191 r, Quad, and being on the
art staff of Chaparral. He belongs
to Hammer and Coffin and Encina
Club, and is a member of the Y. M. C.
A. cabinet, having edited the last edi
tion of the Stanford Handbook, or
"Freshman Bible." St. Louis, Mis
souri, is Wenzel's home.
Hodges, who lives in Palo Alto, is
an assistant in the History depart
ment, in which he is .registered. He
is a frequent contributor to the Inter
national Magazine and other high
class periodicals, bis special work be
ing the treatment of the relations ex
isting between the United States and
the far east.
Miss Hutchinson is a Kappa Alpha
Theta Sorority member, and makes
German her major subject. He.r home
is in Palo Alto. Rakestraw registers
in Chemistry from Los Angeles, and
belongs to Encina Club and Gym Club.
Ferris also belongs to Encina Club,
bailing from Modesto, and making En
tomology his universitl specialty.
The History and German depart
ments tie for representation, each
claiming three of the novitiates.
Chemistry follows with two, while
each of the following departments
claim one apiece: Physiology, Eco
nomics, and Entomology.
Roble Club claims the largest num
ber of women members to receive the
scholarship honor—with two. Kappa
Kappa Gamma and Kappa Alpha
Theta Sororities each claim one, while
the fifth woman lives in San Jose.
For new men members in Phi Beta
Kappa, Encina Club ranks highest with
three names on the honor roll. Two
others live in Palo Alto, and one stays
at the Faculty Clubhouse. Palo Alto
leads the home-town list, claiming
three native sons and daughters
among the scholars.
