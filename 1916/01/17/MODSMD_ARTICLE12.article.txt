Masquers Society has named the
players who will take the parts in
the triumvirate of modern one-act
plays to lie given in the Assembly Hall
on the evening of February 4. The
east, as named includes actors well
known in Stanford dramatics, as well
as personages whose footlight ex
perience antedates their residence on
the campus.
" 'Op 'o My Thumb," an English
comedy, has been substituted for
Maeterlinck's "The interior," which
was originally chosen for production.

" 'Op 'o My Thumb" tells a story that
takes place in the workroom of a
laundry in the Soho district of Ixin
don.
Hold Dual Holes.
Important parts in "Happiness" and
" 'Op 'o My Thumb" have been assign
ed to Miss Florence Stewart and Miss
Marion Vaughan. Miss Stewart has
been seen in her sophomore play, "The
Fortune Hunter," in "Men and
Women," "Pillars of Society." Miss
Vaughan has appeared in three junior
operas, "Men and Women" and "Stop
Thief." Miss Ellen Galpin will hold
roles in "Rosalind' and " 'Op 'o My
Thumb." Miss Galpin appeared last
semester in Masquer's "Jinks." A. D.
Wilkie, who will play Scowcroft in
"Happiness," made his Stanford debut
in his sophomore play, "Bobbie
Hurnit." Miss Alice MacDougal is to
portray "Rosalind." Miss MacDougal
held a role in "Sherwood," and has
seen considerable professional experi
ence. F. C. McCulloch plays Horace
Greensmith in " 'Op 'o My Thumb."
McCulloch starred in "Pillars of So
ciety," and also appeared in "Ready
Money." Miss Geraldine Cluff and
Goodwin Knight have never acted on
a Stanford stage. Several parts are
yet to be assigned.
Masquers Fleet Manager.
Miss Marion Vaughan, '1(>. has been
elected to the managership of Mas
quers Dramatic Society for the en
suing year. Miss Vaughn has won
prominence in Stanford dramatics,
having appeared in Junior operas and
important plays for the past three
years.

Masquers Society has named the
players who will take the parts in
the triumvirate of modern one-act
plays to lie given in the Assembly Hall
on the evening of February 4. The
east, as named includes actors well
known in Stanford dramatics, as well
as personages whose footlight ex
perience antedates their residence on
the campus.
" 'Op 'o My Thumb," an English
comedy, has been substituted for
Maeterlinck's "The interior," which
was originally chosen for production.

" 'Op 'o My Thumb" tells a story that
takes place in the workroom of a
laundry in the Soho district of Ixin
don.
Hold Dual Holes.
Important parts in "Happiness" and
" 'Op 'o My Thumb" have been assign
ed to Miss Florence Stewart and Miss
Marion Vaughan. Miss Stewart has
been seen in her sophomore play, "The
Fortune Hunter," in "Men and
Women," "Pillars of Society." Miss
Vaughan has appeared in three junior
operas, "Men and Women" and "Stop
Thief." Miss Ellen Galpin will hold
roles in "Rosalind' and " 'Op 'o My
Thumb." Miss Galpin appeared last
semester in Masquer's "Jinks." A. D.
Wilkie, who will play Scowcroft in
"Happiness," made his Stanford debut
in his sophomore play, "Bobbie
Hurnit." Miss Alice MacDougal is to
portray "Rosalind." Miss MacDougal
held a role in "Sherwood," and has
seen considerable professional experi
ence. F. C. McCulloch plays Horace
Greensmith in " 'Op 'o My Thumb."
McCulloch starred in "Pillars of So
ciety," and also appeared in "Ready
Money." Miss Geraldine Cluff and
Goodwin Knight have never acted on
a Stanford stage. Several parts are
yet to be assigned.
Masquers Fleet Manager.
Miss Marion Vaughan, '1(>. has been
elected to the managership of Mas
quers Dramatic Society for the en
suing year. Miss Vaughn has won
prominence in Stanford dramatics,
having appeared in Junior operas and
important plays for the past three
years.
