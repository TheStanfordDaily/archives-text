# WICKERSHAM IS CHOSEN VICE-PRESIDENT OF A.S.S.U.
## TAKES PLACE OF TEMPLETON Basketball Letters Awarded and Freshman Numerals Are Given Sanction 
### 
N. W. Wickersham, '15, was last
night appointed vice-president of the
Associated Students of Stanford Uni
versity by the Executive Committee,
taking the place of R. R. Templeton,
'15, who has found it necessary to
leave college on account of trouble
with his eyes. Wickersham is a mem
ber of the varsity baseball squad, and
last year won his German "S" in base
ball, making the trip to Honolulu
during the summer. His fraternity
is Kappa Sigma.
He will be a member of the Execu
tive Committee by virtue of his office
as vice-president.
Award Basketball "S."
Six block and three German "S's"
were voted by the Executive Commit
tee for the varsity basketball players
and substitutes. Those who will re
ceive the block letter are A. E.
Worthy, '15, W. B. Blodget, *15, W.
R. Wheatly, '17, J. S. Wallace, '17, E.
G. Caughey, '18, and O. D. Dolan, '18.
I. E. Farrar, '15, R. S. Lytle, '17, and
F. X. Schwarzenbek, '18, are winners
of the German letter.
Itlocks for Freshmen.
A limit of thirteen was placed on
the number of block nineteens which
may be given to freshmen baseball
players for participating in games
with California. Formal sanction was
I given for the granting of block nine
teens to the freshman track, baseball,
and basketball teams, the same re
quirements being demanded of the
| first-year men as are stipulated for
! members of varsity teams,
i The minutes follow:
Regular meeting, March 15, 191G.
President Wilson in the chair.
Braden and E. L. Hayes absent.
Minutes read and approved.
MSG that title for Quad for de
bators shall not be "Wearers of De
bating S" or "Wearers of Varsity
S."
MSG that freshmen taking points in
track and making baseball and bas
ketball teams shall receive block
numerals.
MSC that thirteen be limit for fresh
man baseball numerals.
N. W. Wickersham appointed vice
j president of A. S. S. U., to fill va
cancy left by Templeton.
MSC that Worthy, Blodget, Wheatly,
; Wallace, Caughey, and Dolan be
(granted varsity "S"; Schwarzenbek,
Lytle, and Farrar, German "S."
MSC meeting adjourn.

A. S. HAYES, Secretary.
N. W. Wickersham, '15, was last
night appointed vice-president of the
Associated Students of Stanford Uni
versity by the Executive Committee,
taking the place of R. R. Templeton,
'15, who has found it necessary to
leave college on account of trouble
with his eyes. Wickersham is a mem
ber of the varsity baseball squad, and
last year won his German "S" in base
ball, making the trip to Honolulu
during the summer. His fraternity
is Kappa Sigma.
He will be a member of the Execu
tive Committee by virtue of his office
as vice-president.
Award Basketball "S."
Six block and three German "S's"
were voted by the Executive Commit
tee for the varsity basketball players
and substitutes. Those who will re
ceive the block letter are A. E.
Worthy, '15, W. B. Blodget, *15, W.
R. Wheatly, '17, J. S. Wallace, '17, E.
G. Caughey, '18, and O. D. Dolan, '18.
I. E. Farrar, '15, R. S. Lytle, '17, and
F. X. Schwarzenbek, '18, are winners
of the German letter.
