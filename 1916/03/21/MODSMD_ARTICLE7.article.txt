# PUGILISTS SHED BLOOD FREELY IN ANNUAL TOURNAMENT
## REGISTER MANY KNOCKOUTS Carey, Lytle, Sullivan, Watson and Piney Capture Titles 
### 
Sullivan and Wass in the welter
weight class and Lytle and Erb, in
the middle class, furnished the best
bouts in the sixth boxing tourna
ment held last evening at the gymnas
ium. before one of the largest crowds
which has witnessed an indoor event
this year. The fans showed their ap
preciation of the bouts. Prom the
opening featherweight semi-final
match until the end of the extra
round in heavy finals they were un
stinted in their praise.
Watson Feather Chump.
In the opening semi-final struggle
Watson gained a unanimous decision
of the judges over Madden. Both
boys were good boxers but Watson
was more successful in placing his
blows. That there was a punch be
hind the blows was shown when Mad
den several times went to the floor,
only to come gamely back.
Witherbee was given a unanimous
decision after going two extra rounds
against Hritt. Britt fought namely,
keeping • the aggressive for three
rounds, but Witherbee's cleverness
was too much for him. In the finals
Watson was easily the winner and
the unanimous decision was popular
with those at the ringside.
Kinney Wins In Lightweight Class.
The bouts in the lightweight class
wave promise of developing an ex
cellent final contest, but Jones, who
defeated Svenson in five rounds, was
unable to meet Pinney on account of
a broken thumb. In the opening bout
in this class Malcolm and Pinney
gave an excellent exhibition, present
ing the slugger and the boxer re
spetivel.v. As the point system was
used in this tournament Pinney re
ceived a unanimous decision.
Jones had a punch which was too
much for Svenson and the latter
took the mat three times, but came
back strong.
Sullivan Defeats Wass.
Blackington and Wass went an ex
tra round in the opening welter
event, Wass getting a unanimous de
cision. Sullivan, who drew a bye,
was forced to appear in an exhibi
tion, as provided by the amateur box
ing rules, and Flint was chosen as
his opponent. Both boxers took
things easy, clinching frequently, but
nevertheless giving a good exhibi
tion.
It was in the finals in this class
that one of the best bouts of the
evening occurred. Sullivan and Wass
are clever lads in the ring. Sulli
van's speed, footwork and accuracy
in placing punches gave him the de
cision. Sullivan gained the admira
tion of the crowd by the way he fol
lowed up his blows.
Lytle and Erb Have Good Bout.
The shortest bout of the evening
was that between Lytle and Sample.
The first round had scarcely opened
when Lytle caught his opponent with
a right hook to the jaw, which sent
him to the floor. On Sample's aris
ing the operation was repeated, and
the round went to Lytle. In the sec
ond round Sample got in front, of
another blow from Lytle, which sent
him to the floor again, so the decis
ion was given to Lytle. In the bout
between Hurley and Erb both boxers
sparred considerably, but frequent
exchanges of blows sent each to his
corner breathing heavily after every
round.
Erb and Lytle were both cautions
in the finals of this class. The ag
gressive went from one to the other
but Lytle proved too clever and
gained the decision.
The heavyweights were freer in
their exchange of blows than the
lighter men. Dean and McNulty
were at each other constantly, with
but little sparring. They had to be
pulled out of clinches time after
time. McNulty received the right to
go into the finals from this bout.
Carey drew a bye and Kirksey was
chosen to meet him in an exhibition
three rounds.
Tll the finals Carey and McNulty
were forced to go an extra round.
The big fellows sparred consider
ably and fell into a clinch almost
every time blows were exchanged.
Carey proved to be his opponent's
superior and was awarded the decis
ion.

was that between Lytle and Sample.

when Lytle caught his opponent with

round.

in the finals of this class. The ag
