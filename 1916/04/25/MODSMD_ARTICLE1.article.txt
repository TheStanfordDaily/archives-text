# EIGHTEEN TO RECEIVE PHI BETA KAPPA KEY THIS YEAR
## TWELVE DEPARTMENTS ELECT Nine Women and Nine Men Receive Honors For Scholarship 
### 
Eighteen seniors, nine men and nine
women, have been elected to Phi Peta
Kappa, scholarship honor society.
This is the first time in a number of
years that as many men as women
have received this honor. Including
the number elected in September and
January, the total membership
amounts to ten per cent of this year's
graduating class. Twelve depart
ments are represented by one or two
major students each. The 1910 men
and women awarded the coveted Phi
Peta Kappa key are: Misses Dorothy
,T. Simon, Florence D. Stewart, Edith
H. Smith, Jessie E. Smith. Elinor V.
Cogswell, Marion V. Dorn. Pearl
Weeks, Muriel Turner, and Minerva
Anderson; and H. H. Purrows, G. R.
Cowgill, P. Ling, ,T. K. Norton, H. R.
English, W. ,T. Gross, G. F. Ferris, C.
L. Hubbs, E. C. Scott.
Prominent Women.
Miss Simon is a History major, reg
istering from Putte, Mont., and be
longs to Sigma Kappa. Miss Stewart
also majors in History, coming from
Los Angeles. She has been prominent
in college dramatics, having appeared
in her 1910 sophomore play, in the
Sword and Sandals show, "Men and
Women," and in the Masquers' pro
duction, "Pillars of Society." She
holds membership in Kappa Alpha
Theta sorority and Masquers.
Miss Edith Smith has also seen ser
vice behind the Assembly Hall foot
lights, in "Seven Days," last year's
senior farce, and in the English Club's
play, "The Great Divide." She be
longs to Delta Gamma sorority and
French Club. She registers in the
French department from Easton. Miss
Jessie Smith is also a French major,
coming from Redwood City. She is
president of the Stanford Music Clul),
president of French Club, a member
of Cap and Gown, of Sigma Kappa
sorority, and has played on the
women's basketball team three years.
.Miss Cogswell registers in English
from Klamath Falls, Ore. She be
longs to English Club, and is a "Se
quoia" contributor. She lives in Palo
Alto. Miss Dorn specializes in
Graphic Art, hailing from San Fran
cisco. She is a member of Gamma
Phi Reta sorority, and Art Club. Miss
Weeks registers in the Physics de
partment from Pasadena, and lives in
Palo Alto. Miss Turner is a Mathe
matics major from Palo Alto, and be
longs to Alpha Omicron Pi. Miss An
derson conies from Regina, Saskatche
wan, Canada, and majors in Latin,
and holds membership in Roble Club.
Norton and (*ross Honored.
Among the men honored, Norton and
Gross are most prominent in student
affairs. Norton has made bis block
"S" in track for three years, He is
one of the speediest hurdlers ever de
veloped at Stanford. He registers in
Education from Palo Alto, and belongs
to Phi Delta Kappa educational fra
ternity, and Nestoria. Gross served
on the staff of The Daily Palo Alto
three years as reporter, news editor
and managing editor, until the illness
of his father compelled him to drop
university work for a while. He be
longs to Delta Kappa Epsilon, Press
Club and Sword and Sandals, and reg
isters in Economics from San Jose.
Seven Other Men.
English majors in Economics from
Stanford University, and is a member
of Encina Club and Eupronia. He de
bated on the Tri-State team last year.
Burrows comes from Salt Lake City,
Utah, majors in Physiology, and be
longs to Encina Club. Cowgill is also
a Physiology major, bailing from
Fresno. Ping Ling comes from Ho
nan, China, registers in the Education
department, belongs to Cosmopolitan
Club, and lives in Palo Alto. Ferris
is an assistant in the Entomology de
partment, in which he majors. He be
longs to Encina Club and comes from
Modesto. Hubbs majors in Zoology
from Los Angeles, and belongs to En
cina Club. Scott is a Chemistry major
from Tola, Kan. He holds member
ship in Phi Lambda Upsilon chemistry
honor society, and Encina Club.
