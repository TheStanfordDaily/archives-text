# CREW AND TRACK GIVEN FORMAL SANCTION FOR TRIPS
## DECIDE TO SEND FRESHMEN Executive Committee Provides Standard Letters-Four Stars Granted 
### 
Formal permission was granted the
Stanford crew to make the trip to
Poughkeepsie, and the track team to
compete in the I. C. A. A. A. A. meet,
by the Executive Committee, at its
meeting last evening. It was also de
meeting last evening. Authorization
was also given to send east any fresh
men track men who qualify for places
on the Stanford squad. All first-year
men are eligible to compete in the
eastern meet who have not represent
ed the University in any open competi
tion, according to the association
rules.
A standard block "S" was adopted
last evening, with specifications for
two sizes. One is to be nine inches
high and eight inches wide, with a
one and one-half inch line; the other
is four and one-half inches high and
five and one-half inches wide, with a
one-inch line.
The minor activities were divided
into two divisions, soccer and tennis
comprising the first, and swimming,
boxing, wrestling and fencing the
second. For the first division, a block
"S," four and one-half by five and one
half inches, with a seven-inch circle,
will be awarded, while the second
division sports will get a round "S"
of the same dimensions, and with the
same seven-inch circle.
Four Stars Granted.
Four stars were granted L. F. Dent,
'15, and to G. A. Jacomiui, '15. Dent
has played on the varsity nine for
four consecutive years, making the
team when a sophomore, and serving
as catcher over since, and captaining
the team this year. Jacomini is
the only man who has ever won
the four star distinction in crew. He
has rowed since his sophomore year
in the varsity shell, and was captain
of the 1915 eight which took second
place at Poughkeepsie.
Baseball Blocks.
The block "S" was given thirteen
men for taking part in the intercol
legiate series with California. They
are Dent, Hoever, Wickersham, Staf
ford, Stevens, Hensel, Hayes, Noonan,
Lander, Sanborn, Bihlman, Braden
and Campbell. The German letter
was given to substitutes Howe and
Mattei.
Crew Letters.
Orme, Maurer, Lyon, Green, Rogers,
Jacomini, Heron, Worth and Neuner
was awarded block letters for crew.
Steinbeck and Andrews win their
German letter as substitutes. The
freshmen who get the block "19" for
crew are King, Montgomery, Watts,
La Forge, Williamson, Bent, Taylor,
Brown and Atwater.
Constitution Amendment.
A committee composed of C. R.
Wakefield, '16, A. E. McEuen, '17, and
R. R. Coleman, 'IS, was appointed to
draw up an amendment to the con
stitution of the Associated Students,
relative to the election of the Quad
editor and manager. As the constitu
tion now stands, these officers are to
be elected by the entire student body,
there having been a mistake when the
last amendment was passed.

A. S. TTAYES. Secretary.
Formal permission was granted the
Stanford crew to make the trip to
Poughkeepsie, and the track team to
compete in the I. C. A. A. A. A. meet,
by the Executive Committee, at its
meeting last evening. It was also de
meeting last evening. Authorization
was also given to send east any fresh
men track men who qualify for places
on the Stanford squad. All first-year
men are eligible to compete in the
eastern meet who have not represent
ed the University in any open competi
tion, according to the association
rules.
A standard block "S" was adopted
last evening, with specifications for
two sizes. One is to be nine inches
high and eight inches wide, with a
one and one-half inch line; the other
is four and one-half inches high and
five and one-half inches wide, with a
one-inch line.
The minor activities were divided
into two divisions, soccer and tennis
comprising the first, and swimming,
boxing, wrestling and fencing the
second. For the first division, a block
"S," four and one-half by five and one
half inches, with a seven-inch circle,
will be awarded, while the second
division sports will get a round "S"
of the same dimensions, and with the
same seven-inch circle.
Four Stars Granted.
Four stars were granted L. F. Dent,
'15, and to G. A. Jacomiui, '15. Dent
has played on the varsity nine for
four consecutive years, making the
team when a sophomore, and serving
as catcher over since, and captaining
the team this year. Jacomini is
the only man who has ever won
the four star distinction in crew. He
has rowed since his sophomore year
in the varsity shell, and was captain
of the 1915 eight which took second
place at Poughkeepsie.
Baseball Blocks.
The block "S" was given thirteen
men for taking part in the intercol
legiate series with California. They
are Dent, Hoever, Wickersham, Staf
ford, Stevens, Hensel, Hayes, Noonan,
Lander, Sanborn, Bihlman, Braden
and Campbell. The German letter
was given to substitutes Howe and
Mattei.
Crew Letters.
Orme, Maurer, Lyon, Green, Rogers,
Jacomini, Heron, Worth and Neuner
was awarded block letters for crew.
Steinbeck and Andrews win their
German letter as substitutes. The
freshmen who get the block "19" for
crew are King, Montgomery, Watts,
La Forge, Williamson, Bent, Taylor,
Brown and Atwater.
Constitution Amendment.
A committee composed of C. R.
Wakefield, '16, A. E. McEuen, '17, and
R. R. Coleman, 'IS, was appointed to
draw up an amendment to the con
stitution of the Associated Students,
relative to the election of the Quad
editor and manager. As the constitu
tion now stands, these officers are to
be elected by the entire student body,
there having been a mistake when the
last amendment was passed.
