# 1918 Winners in Basketball Series
## 
### 
Sophomores were declared cham
pions of the Inter-class basketball ser
ies Saturday. Having defeated both
the juniors and freshmen in the pre
liminary contests, they were awarded
the senior game by default, because
of a misunderstanding on the part of
the 1916 class, as to the eligibility of
players. This is the second year in
succession that the class of 1918 has
won the university championship.

Formations by Girls' Gym Club.
Promptly at 1:30, the afternoon
events began with an exhibition by the
Women's Gym Club. The pyramid
building was particularly good, and
the women are to be commended upon
their rapid progress since the forma
tion of the club early this semester.
This exhibition was followed by a
short program under the direction of
Miss Etta Paris, by members of her
dancing classes. H. Westerman, '18,
and D. Estcourt, *18, gave a Grecian
dance, the "Moment Musical," and M.
Swingle, '18, and C. Bourdeaux, '19.
rlanced the Spanish Waltz together.
Folk dances of all nations were given
by members of the dancing classes.
"Wooden Shoes" being particularly
clever. H. Greening, '17, danced the
Sailors' Hornpipe and D. Estcourt, '18,
interpreted "Pizzicato."
