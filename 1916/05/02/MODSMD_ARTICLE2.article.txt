# A. S. S. U. to Elect Officers Tomorrow
## Football Question and Right of Quad Election Will Also Be Decided 
### 
In addition to electing student body
officers, Stanford men and women will
ballot on the football question on the
Quad tomorrow, and will also pass
upon the amendment to kill the
"joker" in the amendment passed last
fall which deprived the sophomores
of their heridetary right to elect the
editor and manager for the Quad, pub
lished by their class.
Footlinll Question.
Two questions will he put up to the
student body in regard to the football
situation. The recommendations of
the athletic advisory board, printed in
another column tonight, will be placed
on the ballot for the students to ap
prove nr disapprove. Below this the
voters may express their preferance
for rugby or for American football.
Ml who favor American should »rote
against the recommendations of the
advisory board for a rugby agreement
with Santa Clara, according to a state
u.ent by President P. R. Wilson, '16,
in explanation of the ballot. Wilson
said that an affirmative vote for Amer
ican, if the recommendations of the
board were defeated, would probably
be followed by negotiations for a date
with California. A favorable vote for
the recommendations will probably re
sult in a two-year rugby agreement
with Santa Clara. In any case, ul
timate power rests with President
Wilbur.
Contest for Student Offices.
F. C. McColloch, '17, and T. E. Swi
gart, '17, are the candidates for the
office of president of the Associated
Students. E. C. Behrens, '14, the in
cumbent, and W. H. Bloeser, '15, are
running for graduate manager. P. R.
Wilson, '16, has withdrawn his candi
dacy for this position.
W. F. Noonan, '16, for vice-president,
and R. Maurer, '17, for secretary, have
no opponents. G. K. Howard, '17, and
B. Williams, '16, are the sole candi
dates for the respective offices of edi
tor and manager of The Daily Palo
Alto.
Ex. Committee Competition.
Two 1917 representatives, one 1918
member, and one 1919 man, are to be
elected to Executive Committee. W.
A. Lynn, '17, and G. H. Bihlman, '17,
are the only next year's seniors in
the field. H. P. Hauser, '18, and F. J.
Taylor, 'IS, are running for junior
representative next year. Taylor was
nominated today by C. M. Miller, '14.
M. D. House, '19, and R. F. Pelouze,
'19, are out lor next year's sophomore
berth on the Executive Committee.

the athletic advisory board, printed in
another column tonight, will be placed
on the ballot for the students to ap
prove nr disapprove. Below this the
voters may express their preferance
for rugby or for American football.
Ml who favor American should »rote
against the recommendations of the
advisory board for a rugby agreement
with Santa Clara, according to a state
u.ent by President P. R. Wilson, '16,
in explanation of the ballot. Wilson
said that an affirmative vote for Amer
ican, if the recommendations of the
board were defeated, would probably
be followed by negotiations for a date
with California. A favorable vote for
the recommendations will probably re
sult in a two-year rugby agreement
with Santa Clara. In any case, ul
timate power rests with President
Wilbur.
