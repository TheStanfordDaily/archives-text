# Stag Reception For Union Friday Night
## President Wilbur Will Speak to Freshmen on "Stanford Ideals" 
### 
Freshmen will be given opportunity
to "get on the boat" about Stanford
affairs at the "frosh" stag scheduled
for Friday evening, at 7:30 o'clock, in
the Stanford Union. Under the aus
pices of the Y. M. C. A., the new ar
rivals will be given a real Stanford
welcome, while detailed information
about University affairs will be given
by leaders in various lines of student
activities. A varied program, begin
ning promptly at 7:30 o'clock and con
tinuing until "eats" are gone, contain
ing musical numbers, speeches, and
sleight-of-hand tricks, has been ar
ranged.
President To Speak.
President Wilbur will address the
freshmen on "Stanford Ideals." This
occasion will give the "boys of '20"
their first real opportunity to meet
the Stanford president. Harry O.
(Chunk) Hill, state secretary of the
Y. M. C. A. for the universities, will
be on hand with his usual instructive
and entertaining talk about the col
lege man's opportunity . Other speak
ers will be H. W. Maloney on "Stan
ford Athletics"; A. J. Hettinger, '16,
on Debating at Stanford; G. K. How
ard, '17, editor of The Daily Palo
Alto, on "University Publications";
C. F. Joyce, '16, president of the
Glee Club, on "Musical Opportunities
at Stanford"; and J. B. Watson, gen
eral secretary of the Stanford Asso
ciation.
