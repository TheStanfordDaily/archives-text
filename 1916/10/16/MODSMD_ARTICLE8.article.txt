# Contest for Berwick Prize on Wednesday
## Eight Aspirants to Compete for Fifty Dollar Peace Award 
### 
Professor J. S. P. Tatlock of the
English department, Assistant Pro
fessor M. R. Kirkwood of the Law
school, and Dr. H. R. Stolz, head of
the Physical Training department,
were named today as judges for the
Edward Berwick .junior Peace Prize
contest, which will be held at 7:30
Wednesday evening in the Little The
ater. Debating Coach K. E. Leib, '15,
will preside. It is possible that Ed
ward Berwick of Pacific Grove, the
donor of the prize, may be present.
Eight Contestants Enter.
Eight contestants have signified
their intention to compete, and each
of these will be allowed eighteen
minutes in which to speak. The fifty
dollar award will go to the orator who
delivers the speech most effective in
promoting international peace.
Compete In National Talk.
This year's Berwick winner will
compete in the spring against peace
orators from other universities in
California and in Oregon, Washington,
Utah, and Nevada, for the Pacific
Coast prize of $50 offered under the
auspices of the Intercollegiate Peace
Association of the United States, of
which Stanford is now a member.
Associate Professor L. E. Bassett of
the English department is responsible
for the organization of this western
division of the association.
Prize Is Fifty Dollars.
Winners from the various sectional
contests will compete for the $100 na
tional prize at Mohawk, N. Y., next
fall.
