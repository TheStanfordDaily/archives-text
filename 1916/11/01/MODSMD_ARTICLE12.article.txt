# Contracts Signed for Printing and Engraving Quad
## 
### 
Contracts for the engraving and
printing of the 11)18 Quad were signed
last evening by the I!HS Quad Advis
ory Hoard and by Manager F. W.
Weeks, 'JB. Engraving work in the
year-book will be done by the Com
mercial Art Company of San Fran
cisco, while printing will be in the
hands of the H. S. Crocker Company
of San Francisco.
Insure High Class Work.
Both of these firms represent the
highest in their particular line, and
the Junior advisory committee be
lieves that placing of contracts with
them will insure technical perfection
in this year's annual.
Contracts for paper, with the Zel
lerbach Company of San Francisco,
and for binding, with the John Kit
chen, Jr., Company of the same city,
were approved by the committee.
A $1500 bond lias been put up by
the Commercial Art Company to guar
antee* the quality of the engraving
work lor the *18 Quad, and the time
of delivery. This insures the class
and the University public against in
ferior illustrations 'such as have mar
red some previous annuals. All
proofs must be acceptable to Editor
B. C. Wohlford, '18, according to the
contract. Photos in the 1918 year
book will be re-etched copper half
tones of the best quality.
Export in Charge.
H. S. Crocker Company, the print
ers, have agreed to place the publi
cation of the Quad under the imme
diate personal supervision of J. B.
Swart, foreman for Paul Elder for
many years, a master of the highest
standing in the profession of artistic
printing.
A special monotype will be used for
the Quad, which will be of the same
dimensions as last year. A tint bor
der for the pages will be selected
later. Eight duotone "signature"
pages at the front of the book will
contain two-color views of the cam
pus and its environs. From six to
eight four-color inserts are also plan
nod as features for the 1918 annual.
