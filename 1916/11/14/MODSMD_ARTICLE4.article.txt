# Alumni Players in Drama Here
## Peninsula Society Folk Will Be Seen in Benefit for Stanford Clinics 
### 
Originally produced by David Bel
asco in New York, Loo Ditriclistein's
"The Concert" is to be seen in its
second amateur presentation in the
Stanford Assembly Hall, Monday eve
ning, November 27. This will be two
nights after the premier playing of
this comedy by amateurs, which is to
take place in the San Mateo high
school auditorium on the evening of
the 2nth.
Cause Is Worthy One.
While the San Mateo performance
was for Hie benefit of the needy of
that county, the Stanford production
is to aid the auxiliary of the Stanford
Clinics at Lane Hospital, San Fran
cisco. The worthiness of this cause
is not questioned.
With a cast representing many
Peninsula communities, and including
a full percentage of well known so
ciety people, it is expected that Penin
sula folks will be out en masse for
both productions. Palo Alto and the
j adjoining communities will turn out
lor the Stanford offering.
Two of the players are Stanford
graduates. W. Palmer Fuller, .Jr., who
appeared in local dramatics before he
received bis diploma in 1910, and has
of late years been better known for
his work with the whistle on various
rugby turfs, is to be seen in the lead
ing role of Gabor Arany, the musician.
Mrs. W. P. Fuller was Natalie Wright,
I 'io.
Others in the east are John D. Hart
igan, .1. F. Moroney, Miss Josephine
Parrott, Mrs. Andrew McCarthy, Miss
Amy Brewer, Mrs. Thomas Baker,
Mrs. W. P. Fuller, Jr., Mrs. Thomas
1 Driscoll, Mrs. Carol Wolff, Miss Caro-
J line Coleman, Miss Einelie Parrott,
j Mrs. Christian de Guigne, Miss Helen
Crocker, Mrs. Frederick Hussey, Mrs.
Herbert Payne.
Frank Mathieu, who is dramatic di
rector for Sword and Sandals, is
coaching "The Concert." Tickets at
the customary campus prices, 50
cents, 75 cents and one dollar, are to
be on sale at every fraternity and
sorority house on the campus, at En
cina and Roblc Halls, and from Mar
garet Evans, '17, president of the Cam
pus Auxiliary.
