# LAZY INDIFFERENCE
## 
### 
When numerous attractions and
side-shows crowd the daily calendar,
man is apt to overlook a gem here
and there lost among the various op
portunities. Apparently Stanford is en
meshed in such a dilemma, as evi
denced by its failure to fully appre
ciate the Thursday morning assem
blies —an hour that appears invalu
able to the community, dividends far
overlapping capital. Through its elim
ination of hot-air artists and mechan
ical thinkers in favor of genuinely
talented, progressive speakers, the
public exercises committee proved it
self wide-awake to the manifold pos
sibilities that lie dormant. Associa
tion with characters who mould the
destinies of today is an inseparable
factor which rounds out a university
education and affords a broader and
more tangible grip on life. Here
after best not let slide golden moments
in lazy indifference.
