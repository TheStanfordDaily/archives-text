# Student Council Action
## 
### 
The following disciplinary measures
were taken last semester by the
Men's Student Council:
October 29, 1916.
The Men's Student Council recom
mends that an upper class student be
suspended from the University for the
remainder of the present semester, as
discipline for flagrant cheating in
connection with laboratory reports.
November 16, 1916.
The Men's Student Council recom
mends that seven hours be added to
the graduation requirements of a
freshman student as discipline for
cheating in an examination.
December 7 1916.
The Men's Student Council recom
mends that a student be dismissed
from the University, as discipline for
thefts committed in the University
community.
The Men's Student Council recom
mends that an upper class student be
suspended from the University com
munity until the fall semester of 1917,
as discipline for fraudulent enroll
ment in the University.
(Signed) J. W. TEMPLETON,
President, Men's Student Council.
