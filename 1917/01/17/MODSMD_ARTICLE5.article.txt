# VARSITY FIVE OPEN SEASON
## 
### 
Basketball will begin officially Sat
urday night, when the varsity takes
on the fast College of the Pacific quin
tet in the latter's gymnasium for the
first game in the California-Nevada
league. The contest was originally
scheduled for March 10, but will be
played earlier, in case a third game
is necessary to decide the champion
ship between Stanford and the Uni
versity of California. Only two con
tests are on the present schedule be
tween the two universities, and in case

each team wins a game the tie will
be played off March 10.
College of the Pacific will put up a
strong game for the varsity and should
give an idea of the strength of the
five for the big games coming in a few
weeks. Stanford met the College of
the Pacific twice last year and the
teams each won a game. The south
ern team is stronger this year than
ever before but after the wonderful
showing made by the varsity on its
trip south during the Christmas re
cess a victory is conceded for the
Cardinal.
Coach Wilson will pick the men to
morrow who are to make the trip.
Righter, Dolan, Estes, Roney, Bonney,
Sullivan, Greve, and captain Wheatly
have put up a strong bid for places
and the lineup is hard to pick.
U. C. Defeated Quakers.
Word was received yesterday that
California had defeated the Whittier
team by a score of 40 to 30. On its
southern trip the varsity won from
Whittier 46 to 12. Stanford won from
U. S. C. by a larger score than the
Blue and Gold registered against the
southerners. Although the city papers
concede llie edge to Berkeley com
parative scores point the other way.
Coach Wilson announces that regu
lar practice is being held for the var
sity on Monday and Wednesday after
noons and Thursday evening at 7 p.m.
The freshmen have the floor on Tues
day, Thursday and Friday afternoons
at 5 o'clock, under the coach. The
freshman squad will not be picked for
several days yet and men reporting
now have a chance to make the team.
