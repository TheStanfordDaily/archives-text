# Founders to Be Honored in All Day Exercises
## 
### 
Final preparations for the observ
ance of Founders' Day and the twenty
fifth anniversary of the opening of the
University are rapidly being com
pleted by the various committees in
charge of affairs for next Saturday,
March 10.
Exercises Start at 9530.
Exercises will be opened in 'the
inner quad promptly at 9:30, when
the Stanford battalion, Reserve Of
ficers' Training Corps, will form an
impressive "Escort of the Colors."
They will be assisted by the Stanford
Band. At 10 o'clock there will be a
dedication of the colors in Memorial
Church, where the battalion will be
addressed by Rev. D. Charles Gardner
On the "Meaning of the Flags."
On Thursday final plans will be
made by the seniors for the tree
planting ceremonies which will 'be
held in the Union grounds at 10:45.
Several trees will be planted by the
class in the inauguration of the cus
tom. Following this the annual sen
ior pilgrimage to the mausoleum will
be held.
The program for the concert to be
given by the Stanford Band in the
new band shell has been announced
and will be from 1:15 to 2 o'clock,
just preceding the main exercises of
the day.
The program of five numbers fol
lows:
1. Grand March —"Celebre" (Lachner)
2. Overture —Franz Schubert
(Von Suppe)
3. Song—Walther's Traumlied....
(Wagner)
4. Incidental Music —Henry VIII..
(E. German)
5. Patrol American (Meacham)
"Star Spangled Banner."
At 2:15 the formal observances will
be opened by a song written by Miss
Doris Estcourt, '17, entitled "In
Memoriam." This will be sung by the
Glee Club. President Wilbur is sched
uled as the first speaker. He will be
followed by President-Emeritus John
Casper Branner. Scott Calhoun, Esq.,
'95, of Seattle, will speak next, as the
alumni speaker of the day. Chan
cellor-Emeritus David Starr Jordan
and W. Mayo Newhall, president of
the Board of Trustees, will speak in
order. These exercises will be con
cluded about 3:45 with the singing
of "Hail, Stanford, Hail," led by the
Glee Club. The entire program will
be held in the new band shell except
in the event of rain during the week,
in which case the Assembly Hall will
be used.
At 4:15 music lovers will be pro
vided with a recital in Memorial
Church by Organist Louis H. Eaton.
Arrangements are now under way for
a game between the varsity nine aud
Santa Clara on the varsity lot at the
same time.
As a wind-up to the biggest day of
the year alumni, students and faculty
will meet around the festive board in
the Women's Club House to partake
of the well-rounded spread that has
made previous Founders' Day Dinners
popular. The dinner will start
promptly at 6:15, and will be followed
by speeches from Dr. Jordan, Presi
dent Wilbur, Dr. Branner and Scott
Calhoun. Informal dancing will fol
low.

As a wind-up to the biggest day of
the year alumni, students and faculty
will meet around the festive board in
the Women's Club House to partake
of the well-rounded spread that has
made previous Founders' Day Dinners
popular. The dinner will start
promptly at 6:15, and will be followed
by speeches from Dr. Jordan, Presi
dent Wilbur, Dr. Branner and Scott
Calhoun. Informal dancing will fol
low.
Final preparations for the observ
ance of Founders' Day and the twenty
fifth anniversary of the opening of the
University are rapidly being com
pleted by the various committees in
charge of affairs for next Saturday,
March 10.
Exercises Start at 9530.
Exercises will be opened in 'the
inner quad promptly at 9:30, when
the Stanford battalion, Reserve Of
ficers' Training Corps, will form an
impressive "Escort of the Colors."
They will be assisted by the Stanford
Band. At 10 o'clock there will be a
dedication of the colors in Memorial
Church, where the battalion will be
addressed by Rev. D. Charles Gardner
On the "Meaning of the Flags."
On Thursday final plans will be
made by the seniors for the tree
planting ceremonies which will 'be
held in the Union grounds at 10:45.
Several trees will be planted by the
class in the inauguration of the cus
tom. Following this the annual sen
ior pilgrimage to the mausoleum will
be held.
The program for the concert to be
given by the Stanford Band in the
new band shell has been announced
and will be from 1:15 to 2 o'clock,
just preceding the main exercises of
the day.
The program of five numbers fol
lows:
1. Grand March —"Celebre" (Lachner)
2. Overture —Franz Schubert
(Von Suppe)
3. Song—Walther's Traumlied....
(Wagner)
4. Incidental Music —Henry VIII..
(E. German)
5. Patrol American (Meacham)
"Star Spangled Banner."

be opened by a song written by Miss
