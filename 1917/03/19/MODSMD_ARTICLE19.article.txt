# Women's Field Day Plans Drawn Up by Committee
## 
### 
Women's Field Day committee, com
posed of the captains of the various
sports, met Friday afternoon, and
drew up the schedule of events for the
day. The meet will be held on April
28, and following is the list of events
and the time of each.
8:30 a.m.—Tennis.
9:00 a.m. —Fencing and field
sports.
10:00 a.m.—Basketball.
11:00 a.m. —Baseball and volleyball.
2:00 p.m.—Gym Club exhibition and
dancing.
2:30-3:00 p.m.—Swimming and div
ing contests.
t
3:30-5:00 p.m.—Lake sports.
More than one event will be going
on at the same time but each one will
start at the scheduled time.
The girls who will captain their re
spective teams are as follows: Miss
Anita Marcus, seniors: Miss Mildred
Beaver, juniors; Miss Betty Barroll,
sophomores; Miss Ruth Garland,
freshmen.
After the sports a basket lunch will
be served in the Women's Club House,
following which the trophy will be
awarded to the winning class. The
trophy will probably be in the form of
a banner.
A large number of girls have al
ready signed on the placard in the
Women's Rest Room, but Miss Helen
Greening, captain of Field Bay,, urges
that more come out and support their
classes, and for as many even as they
care to enter.
