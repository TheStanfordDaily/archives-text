# ALUMNI MEN TO BANQUET AT REUNION
## PRECEDES BIG MEET Graduate Manager Explains Seat Sale for Intercollegiate Track Contest 
### 
SAN FRANCISCO will be the scene
of the biggest alumni reunion
ever held the night before the big
track meet, according to an announce
ment by L. W. Cutler, '06, president
of the Alumni Association, made to
day. The reunion will take the form
of a dinner in the rooms of the San
Francisco Commercial Club, on the
top floor of the Merchants' Exchange
building.
Committee Appointed .
A committee consisting of L. W.
Cutler, chairman; R. W. Barrett, '04,
W. H. 13. Fowler, 'OG, John T. Nourse,
00, and George Presley, '07, has been
appointed to take charge of all ar
rangements in all centers in the states
of Oregon, Washington and California,
to see that every alumnus is present
at the reunion.
Wilbur To Be Present.
An added feature of this dinner will
be the presence of President Wilbur
to explain all the features and changes
in Stanford. He will be prepared to
answer all questions put to him.
Cutler has recently returned from
a tour of the northwest and the south-
ern part of California, and declares
that every alumnus he has met is
enthusiastic about the "biggest re
union." Tickets have been placed all
over the coast for the alumni.
The dinner will be informal and
will serve merely as a get-together
for graduates. Tickets can be pur
chased for $1.50 each. The banquet
is for men only.
Local alumni may purchase their
pasteboards from Graduate Manager
Behrens, in the Stanford Union or
from Burton W. C'randall, '07, of Rob
inson & Crandall, in Palo Alto. The
committee in charge announces that
they are anxious to have tickets
spoken for before April 10.
At the same time announcement was
made of the alumni reunion, Graduate
Manager Behrens explained how tick
ets for the California track meet may
be secured by graduates, students
and outsiders.
The seat sale will be conducted
along the same lines as for the annual
football game. All the seats will be
numbered. As a result they will be
reserved. To faculty members and
students with Student Body tickets
the price of the Big Meet pasteboards
will be $1.00. The allotment for each
purchaser is two, however. These
tickets are good for either the root
ers' or general sections.
The Stanford part of the bleachers
will be divided into rooters', alumni
and women.
Full regulations are as follows:
1. Students holding A. S. S. U. cards
or faculty members may purchase two
tickets at $1.00 each, for either the
rooters' or general section. Reser
vation on these pasteboards will begin
March 28 and will end April 12. The
tickets can be secured at the gradu
ate manager's office. A receipt will
be given at this time to the pur
chaser. On April 14 the tickets will
be distributed to the purchaser. The
receipt must be handed in at this time
to secure the pasteboards.
2. Alumni and former students may
make application for tickets between
March 30 and April 18. These reser
vations may be made by letter or at
the graduate manager's office. The
price is $1.50. Applications will be
fulfilled as received.
3. Tickets will open to the general
public after April 18 and will be $1.50.
4. All checks should be made to
Associated Students, Stanford Univer
sity.

SAN FRANCISCO will be the scene
of the biggest alumni reunion
ever held the night before the big
track meet, according to an announce
ment by L. W. Cutler, '06, president
of the Alumni Association, made to
day. The reunion will take the form
of a dinner in the rooms of the San
Francisco Commercial Club, on the
top floor of the Merchants' Exchange
building.
Committee Appointed .
A committee consisting of L. W.
Cutler, chairman; R. W. Barrett, '04,
W. H. 13. Fowler, 'OG, John T. Nourse,
00, and George Presley, '07, has been
appointed to take charge of all ar
rangements in all centers in the states
of Oregon, Washington and California,
to see that every alumnus is present
at the reunion.
Wilbur To Be Present.
An added feature of this dinner will
be the presence of President Wilbur
to explain all the features and changes
in Stanford. He will be prepared to
answer all questions put to him.
Cutler has recently returned from
a tour of the northwest and the south-
ern part of California, and declares
that every alumnus he has met is
enthusiastic about the "biggest re
union." Tickets have been placed all
over the coast for the alumni.
The dinner will be informal and
will serve merely as a get-together
for graduates. Tickets can be pur
chased for $1.50 each. The banquet
is for men only.
Local alumni may purchase their
pasteboards from Graduate Manager
Behrens, in the Stanford Union or
from Burton W. C'randall, '07, of Rob
inson & Crandall, in Palo Alto. The
committee in charge announces that
they are anxious to have tickets
spoken for before April 10.
At the same time announcement was
made of the alumni reunion, Graduate
Manager Behrens explained how tick
ets for the California track meet may
be secured by graduates, students
and outsiders.
The seat sale will be conducted
along the same lines as for the annual
football game. All the seats will be
numbered. As a result they will be
reserved. To faculty members and
students with Student Body tickets
the price of the Big Meet pasteboards
will be $1.00. The allotment for each
purchaser is two, however. These
tickets are good for either the root
ers' or general sections.
The Stanford part of the bleachers
will be divided into rooters', alumni
and women.
Full regulations are as follows:
1. Students holding A. S. S. U. cards
or faculty members may purchase two
tickets at $1.00 each, for either the
rooters' or general section. Reser
vation on these pasteboards will begin
March 28 and will end April 12. The
tickets can be secured at the gradu
ate manager's office. A receipt will
be given at this time to the pur
chaser. On April 14 the tickets will
be distributed to the purchaser. The
receipt must be handed in at this time
to secure the pasteboards.
2. Alumni and former students may
make application for tickets between
March 30 and April 18. These reser
vations may be made by letter or at
the graduate manager's office. The
price is $1.50. Applications will be
fulfilled as received.
3. Tickets will open to the general
public after April 18 and will be $1.50.
4. All checks should be made to
Associated Students, Stanford Univer
sity.
