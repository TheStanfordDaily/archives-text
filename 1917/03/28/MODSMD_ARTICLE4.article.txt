# Illuminations at Lagunita Tonight Open Junior Week
## 
### 
An ideal evening lias been prom
ised by the weather man for the
opening of Junior Week festivities on
Lagunita tonight. With a ceiling
dotted with a million stars and a
yonng moon, and the air soft and
balmy, the already romantic spot will
be enhanced till even the most prosaic
and materialistic non-queener will
have to admit a thrill. Shortly after
7 o'clock, students and friends will
begin to line the shores of the lake
with flashlights and cameras, ready
to record the "dope."
The boathouse is decorated for the
occasion. The band will give its con
cert from there. The Mandolin Club
and Glee Club will also contribute
numbers and the committee has plan
ned a couple of new stunts. Follow
ing the musical program, when dark
ness is complete, the fireworks will
be set off.
Tomorrow afternoon, at 4 p.m., the
interclass soccer teams will compete
for the shield, the freshmen opposing
the sophomores, and the juniors the
seniors. The winners will play the
final immediately after the last half.
The games will be played on the foot
ball field. At the same time the
freshmen baseball teams of California
and Stanford will clash on the base
ball diamond.
There will be a dress rehearsal of
the Junior Qpera tonight, and those
vho will be unable to attend the reg
ilar performance will be admitted at
i charge of fifty cents. Because oC
he short time the cast has had for
n - aetice it has had to work overtime,
jut by tomorrow night expect to be
veil ready to put over a splendid pro
luction. The costumes are undeni
ibly the prettiest and most striking
hat have ever been seen at Stanford.
vV. S. Wilson, '13, has written some
)f the songs, and his "Sapphire
Seas" bids fair to take the audience
by storm.
On Friday, at 10 o'clock, come the
lake sports, canoe racing anl tilting,
and swimming races, and the inter
class crew races. The participation
of the girls in the events promises
to add a touch of novelty to the morn
ing, as this is the first time they
have had a part.
At 2:30 in the afternoon the var
sity track team will meet the com
bined aggregation of University of
Southern California and Los Angeles
Athletic Club.
The Prom in the evening will close
Junior Week activities. Music will
be furnished by Philip Sapiro of San
Francisco on the saxophone, accom
panied by local talent as follows:
.1. D. McLachlan, piano; R. R. Cole
man and W. H. Henry, banjos; M,
Blumenthal, violin; B. W. Gillespie,
horn, and I). F. Maddox, drums.
The grand march will start at 3:15.
