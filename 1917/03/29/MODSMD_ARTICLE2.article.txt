# Varsity Overwhelms St. Ignatius Squad
## Hoever and Draper Pitch and Hit While Stanford Wins by 7-1 Score 
### 
When Guy Draper and Gus Hoever,
whose collective batting average totals
about .02:], both garner safe hits for
themselves in one afternoon off the
same pitcher, it is very apparent that
that pitcher has nothing more than the
proverbial glove and a prayer.
Hurler Morrissey had little more
than those two articles yesterday af
ternoon, and as Guy and Gus pitched
as well as they batted, Stanford had
no difficulty in downing St. Ignatius,
7-1, in seven innings of rather weird
baseball on the varsity diamond.
Show Up Well.
The showing of the varsity should
warm the cockles of its supporters'
hearts. Thirteen hits, including two
safeties by Guy and Gus, the twirling
twins, were straightened out off the
crooked-arm shoots of Morrissey.
This array of hits included a home
run, a triple, a double and ten
singles. During the four innings he
was on the mound, Draper allowed
