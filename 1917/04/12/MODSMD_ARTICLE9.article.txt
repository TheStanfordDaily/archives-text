# Fencing Bouts With Berkeley on Friday
## Skelton, Flint, Bullis, Kahle and Shamberger Make Up Stanford Entries 
### 
Intercollegiate fencing between the!
University of California and Stanford :
is to take place tomorrow evening at
8 o'clock, at Hearst Hall Annex, Berk
eley.
Stanford will he represented by L.
R. Skelton, '17 (captain), J. D. Flint,
gr.. R. O. Bullis, '18, R. F. Kahle, '17,
and J. P. Shamherger, '19, while S. L.
Warren, 'IS (captain), J. M. Howson,
'17, R. M. Walker, '17, W. S. Baller
man, '18, and H. K. Norcross, '19, will
uphold the Blue and Gold.
Each man on the team will fence
one bout with each man of the Cali
fornia squad, making a round robin
tournament of twenty-five bouts, the
side winning the majority being the
final victor of the tournament.
Judges of very high caliber have
been chosen to decide the events. B.
C. Bainbridge of the New York Ath
letic Club, Robert Miller, fencing in
structor at the Olympic Club and at
the University of California, and H.
W. Maloney, who has charge of fenc
ing al Stanford, being those selected.
Owing to the fact that the crew
races, ball game and track meet will
be held on the following day, a large
crowd is expected to attend the Stan
ford-California fencing tournament.
Fencing matches between the women
i)f California and Stanford will prob
ably be hold a week from Saturday.
