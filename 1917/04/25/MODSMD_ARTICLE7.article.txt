# Summer Camp Is Planned for Women
## National Service Training School at Presidio From June 1 to July 1 
### 
Women will be able to enlist in the
National Service Training School,
which is to be held at the Presidio
of San Francisco from June 1 to
July 1. This will be the second en
campment for women which has been
held in San Francisco, the first hav
ing been last summer. The training
school will be under the auspices of
the San Francisco chapter of the
Women's Section of tne Navy.
Courses in Red Cross work of all
j sorts will be given. In addition to
a two weeks' course in Red Cross
Home Dietetics, there will be a two
weeks' course in the making of sur
gical dressings, general supplies, and
Ited Cross Base Hospital garments.
In order to obtain a First Aid cer
tificate it will be required that a two
weeks' course be taken under author-
I ized Red Cross physicians. A two
weeks' course in Elementary Hygiene
and Home Care of the Sick will be
conducted by a staff of registered Red
Cross nurses.
There will be special courses given
in heliography, cipher code study,
plain and wireless telegraphy. These
will be of great importance and it is
urged that students who enroll in the
summer encampment include one of
these forms of signaling in their
courses.
Colonel Guy L. Edie, commandant
of the Lettermann General Hospital,
who was in charge at the last en
campment, will again be in command.
Dr. George H. Richardson, chairman
of the committee on Red Cross in
struction is to be head of the staff of
physicians who will conduct the First
Aid classes. Miss Rutley, who has
conducted the training camp at Ft.
Lawton and at Ft. Vancouver, will
serve as supervising nurse in resi
dence. Miss Charlotte P. Ebbets, a
graduate of Pratt Institute and an in
structor at Teachers' College, Colum
bia University, will be the chief dietic
ian. Beside the general corps of in
structors, there will be a number" of
prominent speakers who will give
special lectures.
The official uniform, consisting of a
Norfolk jacket and skirt, will be re
quired. Information regarding the en
campment may he obtained from Mrs.
F. H. Colburn, 972 Bush street, San
Francisco.

Women will be able to enlist in the
National Service Training School,
which is to be held at the Presidio
of San Francisco from June 1 to
July 1. This will be the second en
campment for women which has been
held in San Francisco, the first hav
ing been last summer. The training
school will be under the auspices of
the San Francisco chapter of the
Women's Section of tne Navy.
