# War Causes Several Changes in Faculty
## Stanford's Staff of Instructors Give Many of Their Number to Service 
### 
Changes are numerous in the ranks
of the Stanford University faculty
with the opening of the new year. The
world war has claimed a large num
ber, although absences, resignations
and new additions have considerably
altered the instructing staff.
Leave of absence has been granted
to Professor E. Whitney Martin of
the Greek department for the first
quarter. He is serving as a field di
rector of the Red Cross at Linda
Vista. Professor C. 13. Wing of the
Engineering department, is major in
an engineering corps at Vancouver.
The Fourth Stanford Ambulance
unit, stationed at Allentown, Pa.,
claims Professor S. S. Seward of the
English department. Dr. 11. It. SLolz,
medical head at the gymnasium, lias
received a first lieutenant's commis
sion in the army. Me will be suc
ceeded by Dr. W. L. Adams. Thomas
L. Dyer, law librarian, is now a cap
tain in the national army, into which
John F. Connell, instructor of Bac
teriology, was also drafted. Professor
E. W. Smith, head of the Journalism
department, is employed in the inter
ests of the food conservation cam
paign under Herbert C. Hoover in
Washington, D. C. The Medical
School has lost Dr. Langley Porter,
Dr. George Rothganger, Dr. J. M.
Wolfsohn, Dr. Leonard W. Ely, and
Dr. Harry L. Langnecker, all of whom
have been called for medical service
in the U. S. army.
Absence on leave has been granted
to Professor Alvin S. Johnson of the
Economics department, Elizabeth Lee
Buckingham of the English depart
ment, Professor G. li. Clevenger,
mteallurgist, Professor A. 13. Show of
the History department, and Pro
fessors A. W. Meyer and H. W. Young
of the Anatomy and Metallurgy depart
ments respectively.
Several new additions have been
made to the Stanford faculty. Dr.
Johann A. Backer of Harvard, Dr.
Ludwig A. Emge, and Dr. John A.
Sperry will fill vacated places in the
Medical School, Lane Hospital, San
Francisco. N. Howell Furman of
Princeton, and Norris F. Hall of Har
vard have been added to the Chem
istry teaching staff. Arthur N. Hol
conibe of Harvard will take the posi
tion of an instructor in Political Sci
ence. Mary Blanchard and Muriel
Vail are new additions in the women's
Physical Training department. Mrs.
D. V. Kingsley, on leave of absence
from the University of Denver, will
substitute for Miss Buckingham in
public speaking instruction. Miss Ger
trude Sherer, formerly a teacher at
Swarthmore College, Pennsylvania,
who registered in the English depart
ment of the graduate school last year,
will assist in English composition.
Dr. Elliot Jones of the University of
Texas, comes to Stanford as a pro
fessor in the Economics department,
this year, and Professor Felipe Mor
ales do Setien has been secured as an
instructor of Romanic Languages.
Harry Petersen, former curator or
the Stanford Museum, is to tak a po
sition in Charleston Museum, the old
est in the United States. He will be
succeeded by Pedro J. Lemos of the
San Francisco Art Institute.
Two resignations complete the list
of changes in the instructing staff.
Those who have resigned are Profes
sor G. S. Boliart of the Chemistry de
partment, who is doing government
work in Washington, D. C., and Flor
ence C. Burrell from the woman's
Physical Training department, who
has entered the University of Wash
ington faculty.
