# BOARD OF TRUSTEES FILLS VACANCIES IN FACULTY
## WAR HAS CALLED MANY Three New Men Named for Faculty and Leave or Resignation Granted to Eight 
### 
Appointment of new members of
the faculty to fill places left vacant
by the call for Stanford professors in
war service was made by the Board
of Trustees at its meeting this after
noon in San Francisco. Leave of ab
sence was formally granted to in
structors who have already left the
campus, and to others who will be
away during the winter quarter.
Edward M. Hulme, head of the
History department and Dean of the
School of Arts at the University of
Idaho, was appointed acting professor
of European History for the winter
quarter to take the place of Professor
A. B. Show, who will be away for the
winter and spring on sabbatical leave.
Professor Hulme is a Stanford grad
uate, class of '97. He took graduate
work at Harvard and received his
A. M. degree at Cornell in 1902. He
has published a text book on the
renaissance.
In the School of Law, Henrie H.
Hill, San Jose attorney, and W. C.
Dalzell of Tulsa, Okla., will take up
the work of Dean C. A. Huston and
Professor J. W. Bingham, who will
be away doing work on the war ex
ports board.
Mr. Hill is at present lecturer in
the Law School, giving the course in
California practice. In the winter
quarter he will offer Professor Bing
ham's curse in wills. Mr. Hill is a
Stanford alumnus, A. B. '09 and J. D.
'11.
Since graduation Mr. Dalzell, A. B.
| '12 and J. D. '17, ha! been practicing
law in Tulsa. He will give in the win
ter quarter a four-hour course in
mortgages as a substitute for Dean
Huston's International law. Mr.
Huston's course in agency will be
given in the spring by Professor C.
|G. Vernier, and Mr. Dalzell will con
duct Mr. Vernier's course in bills and
notes.
Messrs. Hill and Dalzell are both
members of the honor law society,
the Order of the Coif.
The board gave Professor Vernon
L. Kellogg of the Entomology depart
ment leave of absence for the winter
quarter to resume his government
work. He will depart in about two
weeks for Washington to resume
vork with the Commission for Relief
in Belgium and to aid Herbert C.
Hoover in the food administration.
Mrs. Kellogg will accompany him to
do Belgian relief work.
Professor Kellogg is scheduled to
deliver the annual address of the En
tomological Association of America
in Pittsburg, December 28. Next
Wednesday evening he will address
the Stanford Science Association at
the Faculty Club House on the inter
national food problem.
Leave for the first two weeks of the
winter quarter was given Professor
H. R. Fairclough of the Latin depart
ment, who has been asked by the
Archaeological Institute of America
to speak in the east. He will lecture
on "Ancient Art Treasures of Italy."
On his way east Professor Fairclough
will attend a teachers' meeting in Los
Angeles, December 17 to 21. In
Philadelphia he will attend the meet
ing of the Archaeological Institute, of
which he is western secretary.
The "board accepted three resigna
tions. Wilfred Eldred, instructor in
Economics, who was drafted and is
now in the government service, sent
in his resignation, and Dr. A. N. Hol
combe of Harvard, who was to have
been here for the next three quarters
as associate professor of Political
Science, notified the University that
he could not come as he wished to
continue in war work. Dr. Paul
Whelan, assistant in medicine, also
resigned. Two other members of the
medical faculty, Dr. Tenney L. Davis
and Dr. Norris F. Hall, instructors in
Chemistry, received leave of absence
as they are working for the govern
ment.
