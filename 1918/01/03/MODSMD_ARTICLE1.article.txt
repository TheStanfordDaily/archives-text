# DAN CUPID SCORES HEAVILY DURING RECENT VACATION
## ANNOUNCE ENGAGEMENTS Prominent Stanfordites Are Among Those Wedded and Betrothed 
### 
In spite of the shortened vacation
Dan Cnpid got in some good licks and
as a result a considerable number of
marriages and engagements were an
nounced during the vacation.
The marriage of Miss Rachel Smith,
'20, and Karl Leib, '16, came as a
great surprise to many on the campus.
The wedding took place on December
28, at the Unitarian Church in Los
Angeles.
Miss Margaret Shedd, '20, was the
only guest present outside of the im
mediate families.
Mrs. Lieb was a member of Roble
club and was elected secretary for
her of Women's Gym club and was
the coming quarter. She was a mem
prominent in athletics.
Mr. Lieb is well known on the cam
pus, having been president of Encina
club last quarter. He is manager of
the debating clubs and is an instruc
tor in the English department.
Mr. and Mrs. Lieb will live in Palo
Alto. Both will continue work in the
University.
Miss Mary Gard, '15, was married
to Lieutenant Henry Jameson on No
vember 30 at her home in Los An
geles.
Mrs. Jameson is a member of Alpha
Phi sorority and was elected to Cap
and Gown, fencing and basketball
teams and the board of directors of
the Women's Athletic Association.
Lieutenant Jameson is a graduate
of Cornell and is now stationed at the
Presidio in San Francisco.
Miss Albertina Grove, '14, was mar
ried to George F. Straub last Monday
at the Presbyterian church in Palo
Alto.
Mr. and Mrs. Straub will make their
homo in Sutter City, where Mr. Straub
is engaged in business.
Miss Mary Willis TatT, 20, of Palo
Alto announced her engagement dur
ing the holidays to Sergeant Major
William Charles O'Hare of San
Francisco.
Miss Taff first became acquainted
with Sargeant O'Hare when she was
president of the "godmothers' club,"
an organization of twenty-five Palo
Alto girls who corresponded with the
National Guardsmen when at the
Mexican border. She wrote to him un
der the 110m de plume of Peggy
O'Neil, later meeting him at a party
given by some Palo Alto friends in
honor of the returned guardsmen.
Miss Taff is a member of Gamma
Phi Beta sorority. The date of the
wedding has not as yet been fixed.
The engagement of Miss Evelina
Jones of San Francisco to Martin
Detels, '16, was recently announced.
Mr. Detels is a former student of
Santa Clara, Stanford and Columbia
universities. He graduated from the
law department here, entering Colum
bia for postgraduate work. Detels
was co-author of the 1915 Junior
Opera and is a member of Delta Tau
Delta fraternity.
Miss Jones is prominent in the
younger set of San Francisco. No
date has been set for the wedding.
Another engagement of interest is
that of Miss Elizabeth Eaton of San
Francisco to Captain James Lord Hay
den of the Coast Artillery Corps.
Miss Eaton formerly lived in Palo
Alto, and is well known on the cam
pus.
Captain Hayden is a graduate of
West Point and is now stationed at
Fort Winfield Scott. He is the son of
John Hayden in command of the
camp at Macon, Ga.
Owing to the uncertainty of Captain
Hayden's plans no arrangements have
been made for the wedding.
Miss Anna L. Burton, 'IG, and How
ard K. Zimmerman, '16, were married
on Christmas evening at the Methodist
Episcopal church in Palo Alto.
Mrs. Zimmerman, after her gradua
tion, taught school for a year in Utah
and returned last semester to take up
postgraduate work.
Mr. Zimmerman graduated from the
law department with the highest hon
ors of his class. He has been enlisted
in the Coast Artillery stationed on
the Columbia river, and recently re
ceived word of his election to the third
officers' training camp at Camp Lewis.

The engagement of Miss Evelina
Jones of San Francisco to Martin
Detels, '16, was recently announced.
Mr. Detels is a former student of
Santa Clara, Stanford and Columbia
universities. He graduated from the
law department here, entering Colum
bia for postgraduate work. Detels
was co-author of the 1915 Junior
Opera and is a member of Delta Tau
Delta fraternity.
Miss Jones is prominent in the
younger set of San Francisco. No
date has been set for the wedding.
Another engagement of interest is
that of Miss Elizabeth Eaton of San
Francisco to Captain James Lord Hay
den of the Coast Artillery Corps.
Miss Eaton formerly lived in Palo
Alto, and is well known on the cam
pus.
Captain Hayden is a graduate of
West Point and is now stationed at
Fort Winfield Scott. He is the son of
John Hayden in command of the
camp at Macon, Ga.
Owing to the uncertainty of Captain
Hayden's plans no arrangements have
been made for the wedding.
