# BOARD OF TRUSTEES HAS MONTHLY MEETING
## Grants Leave of Absence to Fisher and Woolsey and Makes Appointments 
### 
For the purpose of joining a bio
logical expedition to the British West
Indies, Professor Walter K. Fisher of
Ihe Department of Zoology was this
afternoon granted a leave of absence
by the Hoard of Trustees at its month
ly meeting. Dr. Fisher will be away
from April 10 to August 15. He will
accompany the expedition, whiefo will
be under the auspices of the State
University of lowa, as a representa
tive of Stanford. In charge of the
party will be Professor C. C. Nutting,
head of the Department of Zoology at
lowa. Besides representatives from
other universities, a number of grad
uate students from lowa will go with
the expedition.
Dr. Augustus V. Saph, one of the
leading consulting engineers on the
coast, was appointed by the trustees
as lecturer in civil engineering for
the winter quarter. Dr. Saph is al
ready giving a course of lectures on
reinforced concrete and the elastic
arch. This is part of the work for
merly given by Professor C. B. Wing,
who is now a major in the engineer
ings corps of the United States army.
At Cornell Dr. Saph was fellow in
hydraulic laboratory work with Pro
fessor Shoder and obtained his Ph. D.
there. He is a native of San .lose
and took his bachelor of arts degree
at the University of California. He
has been an instructor at U. C., was
in the United States reclamation ser
vice, in the city engineer's olllee in
San Francidco, and assistant stato
engineer in charge of harbor work in
San Francisco. He is now a con
sulting engineer there. His home is
in Berkeley.
The board granted a leave oi ab
sence to Dr. Chester 11. Woolsey,
clinical instructor in surgery, for the
period during which he is employed
by the government as acting assistant
surgeon in the United States public
health service.
Dr. Florence Holsclaw, assistant
clinical professor of medicine, re
signed.
Appointment of holdA's of a fellow
ship and three scholarships for the
remainder of the the academic year
was made.
The Dickey scholars are Shungo
Furui, '20, of Selma, and George H.
Cloud, '19, of Fresno. The Dickey
scholarships are awarded from a fund
established to "aid young men from
Fresno county, preferably from
Fresno City." Furui is a mechanical
engineering major and Cloud a his
tory major.
Ag the Roble scholar the board
named Miss Rose M. Kelley, '18, of
Redlands, who is a French major.
F. C. Vickery of Palo Alto was ap
pointed University fellow. He is a
graduate of the University of Cali
fornia and is now taking graduate
work here in the Department of Geol
ogy and Mining for his master's de
gree. Mr. Vickery was employed in
the east in chemical work of muni

tions during the summer of 191 fi and
that fall entered Stanford as a special
student to prepare himself as an in
structor in geology. .He has made an
excellent record both in laboratory
and field work.
The trustees appropriated $47,000
for special furniture, linoleum,
shades, and all equipment for the new
building.

For the purpose of joining a bio
logical expedition to the British West
Indies, Professor Walter K. Fisher of
Ihe Department of Zoology was this
afternoon granted a leave of absence
by the Hoard of Trustees at its month
ly meeting. Dr. Fisher will be away
from April 10 to August 15. He will
accompany the expedition, whiefo will
be under the auspices of the State
University of lowa, as a representa
tive of Stanford. In charge of the
party will be Professor C. C. Nutting,
head of the Department of Zoology at
lowa. Besides representatives from
other universities, a number of grad
uate students from lowa will go with
the expedition.
