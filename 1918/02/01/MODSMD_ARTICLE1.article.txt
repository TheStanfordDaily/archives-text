# SOLDIERS ALLOWED ON TRACK BY ATHLETIC COMMITTEE
## BARRETT AND HERTEL ELECTED TO OFFICE Graduate Manager Authorized to Close Boathouse at Meeting 
### 
In addition to voting to meet with
California on the football question
through a committee the Board of
Athletic Control at their regular
meeting yesterday took action on sev
eral minor points. R. W. Barrett,
alumni member on the board, was
elected chairman and H. R. Hertel,
president of the Student Body, was
elected secretary of the board.
Track Free to Soldiers.
Soldiers at Camp Fremont will be
given free use of the track at Stan
ford, according to further action ac
tion taken by the board. Since crew
is to be abolished the graduate man
ager was authorized to repair and
close the boat-house on Lagunita.
The minutes of the meeting are as
follows:

Control at the Union, January 31,
1918, 4:30 p.m.

R. W. Barrett was elected chair
man and H. R. Hertel secretary.
The treasurer reported information
he had obtained concerning insurance
on the bleachers and was instructed
to investigate the matter more fully
and report at a later meeting.
The following actions were then
taken:
The use of the track was granted
to the soldiers at Camp Fremont for
a regimental track meet.
The Graduate Manager was in
structed to repair and close the boat
house on Lake Lagunita.
A committee of three was appointed,
to consist of Barrett, Sullivan, and
either Angell or Murray to attend a
preliminary meeting with a committee
from the University of California on
Saturday, February 2, at the Palace
Hotel, at 12 o'clock. The object of
this meeting is to arrange for a fu
ture meeting between this Hoard and
one of equal authority from the Uni
versity of California for the purpose
of settling definitely the football ques
tion.
The treasurer read a letter from
Dr. Wilbur transmitting a report
from the Faculty Committee 011 Ath
letics. This report was then read
and discussed.
Mr. Sullivan was authorized to de
termine the admission prices for the
track meet and major baseball games.
Meeting adjourned.
H. R. HERTEL,
Secretary.
