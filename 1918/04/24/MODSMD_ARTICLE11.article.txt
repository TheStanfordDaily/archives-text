# FOURTH OFFICERS' CAMP ANNOUNCED BY WAR DEPARTMENT
## DATE STILL INDEFINITE Captain Parker to Select Twenty-five Men As Stanford's Quota 
### 
A fourth officers' training camp for
the purpose of fitting men for com
missions in the army will be held this
summer, according to an announce
ment made at the R. O. T. C. head
quarters this morning. The date for
the camp has not been definitely set,
but it will probably begin some time
during the latter part of May.
Stanford's quota to this camp has
been announced as twenty-five men.
The men are to be selected by the
University and not by the War De
partment. This means that the recom
mendations will come from Captain
Parker, commandant of the R. O. T. C.
Men Must Enlist
The regulations for this camp dif
fer from those of former training
camps. All men entering the camp
with the object of gaining commis
sions must enlist the same as in the
army. They remain privates until
the training period is over and their
commissions are granted. Upon fail
ure to win commissions they will be
sent back into the ranks of the army
as privates or as non-commissioned
officers. They will not be sent back
to civilian life as formerly. Recom
mendations for commissions will be
given at the end of the training pe
riod, but the men may be put into
the reserve and not called into active
service as officers until there is need
for them.
Transportation will be paid to the
training camp and allowances there
will be given as to privates in the
army. Men discharged for any rea
son or placed 011 the reserve will be
given their transportation back to
Stanford. The location of the camp
lias not yet been named and the date
given above is only tentative.

Captain Parker, commandant of the
R. O. T. C., announces that the men
who wish to go to the camp should
putin their applications with him as
soon as possible. He is anxious to be
gin selecting the men at once. Fur
ther information in regard to the
camp can be obtained by writing to
or calling at the commandant's office
at the R. O. T. C. headquarters.
Three Classes of Applicants
Those who apply for admission to
the training camp will be divided into
three classes. The first class includes
those men who ave just finishing four
years' training in the R. O. T. C. un
der the War Department. There are
only a few men in the University who
fall in this class.
The second class includes those
men who have had one full year and
300 hours of drill since January 1,
1917. Men who began with the in
tensive course last year and con
tinued in military throughout this
year fall into this class. Graduates
who have had one year of training
are placed in the third class.
