# STANFORD'S WAR PART TO BE PUBLISHED IN BOOK
## TO BE COMPLETE RECORD Committee On Information Has Charge of Volume Out Soon 
### 
Stanford's part in the great war is
to l)p told in a compiled history of
lior eighteen hundred men and women
now serving in some activity, under
the direction of the Committee on
War Information.
For the last few months the war
information committee has been work
ing on two big phases of the war ac
tivity for the University. The first
was to arrange for the lectures and
discussion of different phases of the
war, the first of which was given yes
terday by Professor P. J. Treat. The
second work of the committee was
to arrange for the compilation of the
history of the part of Stanford men
in the war.
Since August 1914 Stanford has
done much in relief work and in pub
licity and since April 1917 in govern
mental serviee of every kind. From I
the outset an ell'ort has been made to |
keep n record of all this work. It is i
now possible to commence a more j
complete record and to gather ma- j
terials of all kinds.
A tile containing cards for every
Stanford man and woman in the ser
vice will be placed in the History j
department and a careful record kept, j
Everyone is urged to send any news I
that they may know about these per- '
sons. Copies of letters or parts of j
letters, diaries, pictures, records of
work of men in Belgium, the am- j
bulance units, or in the training camps j
are wanted.
Information may be brought or sent !
to the secretary of the war informa- j
tion committee, or dropped into a box
to be placed on Engineering corner
Monday.
Further information may be had ■
daily at 11 o'clock in Room 235 in the
History building of Professor Edgar
E. Robinson.
