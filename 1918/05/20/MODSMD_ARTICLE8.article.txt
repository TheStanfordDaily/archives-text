# 1921 WINS TENNIS TOURNAMENT
## 
### 
Hy winning two out of three
matches in the final rounds of the In
terclass tennis tournament, the fresh
men annexed the class championship
of the University on the local courts
last Satufday.
C. C. Crosby, '19, was the only one
to stop the onrush of the freshmen
racquet men for a clean sweep. He
opposed the star performer of fresh
man combination, Ixuinsbury, Fish,
and after losing the first set came
back strong in the next two, display
ing a stellar game of tennis.

Crosby's serve completely baffled
Fish in the second and third sets,
which, coupled with superior judg
ment and ability to cover all corners
of the court put. Fish down for the
count 6-8, 6-2, 6-2.
The second match of the day proved
a victory for F. S. Turner, '21, over
W. C. Bent,'l9, captain of the Varsity.
Bent took the agressive in the first
set, displaying good form and accur
ate line driving but could not keep
up the pace in the second round. A
week ago the freshmen won the dou
ble match.
The outcome of this match gives
Turner credit for a win in singles and
also a win in doubles with his part
ner P. 13. Kinney, '21. Turner's con
sistent playing has been responsible
for a double victory. His playing has
to a great extent brought the cham
pionship to the freshman class for the
first time since the days of Murray
and Haber.
Summary of the tournament:
Preliminary rounds —F. S. Turner,
'21, defeated A. C. Devlin, *20, 6-0,6-3.
W. C. Bent, *19, defeated H. H. Ploss,
'18, 6-4, 6-2.
G. C. Thomas, *19, defeated 0. S.
Kegley, '18, 8-6, 6-3.
F. S. Turner, '21, anl P. P. Kinney,
'21, defeated C. W. Doolittle, *20, and
D. N. Barry, '20, 6-1, 6-2.
C. C. Crosby, '19, and W. C. Bent,
'19, defeated C. S. Kegley, '18, and
H. W. Hoss, '18, 7-5, 8-6.
L. Fish. '21, and D. N. Barry did not
play.
Finals:
C. C. Crosby, '19, defeated L. Fish,
'21, 6-8, 6-2, 6-2.
F. S. Turner, '21, defeated W. C.
Bent, '19, 6-4, 6-1.
F. S. Turner and P. B. Kinney, '21,
defeated W. C. Bent and G. C. Thomas,
6-1, 6-2.

Hy winning two out of three
matches in the final rounds of the In
terclass tennis tournament, the fresh
men annexed the class championship
of the University on the local courts
last Satufday.
C. C. Crosby, '19, was the only one
to stop the onrush of the freshmen
racquet men for a clean sweep. He
opposed the star performer of fresh
man combination, Ixuinsbury, Fish,
and after losing the first set came
back strong in the next two, display
ing a stellar game of tennis.

Crosby's serve completely baffled
Fish in the second and third sets,
which, coupled with superior judg
ment and ability to cover all corners
of the court put. Fish down for the
count 6-8, 6-2, 6-2.
The second match of the day proved
a victory for F. S. Turner, '21, over
W. C. Bent,'l9, captain of the Varsity.
Bent took the agressive in the first
set, displaying good form and accur
ate line driving but could not keep
up the pace in the second round. A
week ago the freshmen won the dou
ble match.
The outcome of this match gives
Turner credit for a win in singles and
also a win in doubles with his part
ner P. 13. Kinney, '21. Turner's con
sistent playing has been responsible
for a double victory. His playing has
to a great extent brought the cham
pionship to the freshman class for the
first time since the days of Murray
and Haber.
