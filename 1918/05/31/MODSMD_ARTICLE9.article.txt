# WOMEN'S COUNCIL ELECTION WILL BE HELD MONDAY
## 
### 
A new Women's Conference for 1919
met last Wednesday evening and nom
inated the following for members of
the 1 10 council: Dorothy Hanna, '19,
Ruth Chandler, '19. Ruth Searcy, '20.
Catherine Williams, '20, Margaret
Shedd, '20. Irene Bonnett., 'IS, Elinor
Eppich, '19. Ellen Clark, '19, Ellen
Calhoun, '20, and Frances Willis, '20.
Of these nominees the senior or
graduate receiving the highest number
of votes will be elected president of
Organized Women Students, of Wom
en's Conference and Council, taking

precedence over any junior who
might receive more votes. For the
offices of vice-president and secretary,
respectively, the nominees will take
office in order of the number of votes
which they receive.
Election for clubhouse board mem
j hers will also be held on Monday,
l votes being cast on the same election
blanks on which Council members will
be chosen. One junior representative
will be chosen and two from the soph
omore and freshman classes, respec-
tively. Following are the nominees for
the board:
Junior, Georgia Hell and Dorothy
Plummer; sophomore, Katheriue Ott,
Florence Fabling, Alice Faitout, and
Catherine Williams; freshman, Anne
Hardy, Elizabeth Gregory, Dorothy
Winkle, and Alberta Marx.
Women voted last Wednesday at a
mass meeting to amend the constitu
tion of the Organized Women Stu
dents by providing for a finance com
mittee to control the finances of the
women students. The committee is
to consist of three members: one
elected by Conference, not necessarily
a Conference member, and one elected
by the Women's Athletic Association.
The third member is to be elected by
the women at large at a Quad election
from four nominees: two nominated
by Conference and two nominated by
Women's Athletic Association.
The committee is to have charge of
collecting all Associated Student Body
dues and will distribute the percentage
of them allowed to the women's ac
tivities, according to budgets present
ed by the different women's organiza
tions. The committee is to hold office
for one year and any member can at
any time be recalled by joint vote of
W. A. A. and Conference.
An amendment providing for a war
board as standing committee of Or
ganized Women Students was passed.
The chairman of the board to have
full control is to be appointed by the
president of Organized Women Stu
dents and is to be responsible to her.
The chairman of the war board is to
have full control of all war work, to
appoint her own committees and to
make reports to Women's Conference.

An amendment providing for a war
board as standing committee of Or
ganized Women Students was passed.
The chairman of the board to have
full control is to be appointed by the
president of Organized Women Stu
dents and is to be responsible to her.
The chairman of the war board is to
have full control of all war work, to
appoint her own committees and to
make reports to Women's Conference.
A new Women's Conference for 1919
met last Wednesday evening and nom
inated the following for members of
the 1 10 council: Dorothy Hanna, '19,
Ruth Chandler, '19. Ruth Searcy, '20.
Catherine Williams, '20, Margaret
Shedd, '20. Irene Bonnett., 'IS, Elinor
Eppich, '19. Ellen Clark, '19, Ellen
Calhoun, '20, and Frances Willis, '20.
Of these nominees the senior or
graduate receiving the highest number
of votes will be elected president of
Organized Women Students, of Wom
en's Conference and Council, taking

precedence over any junior who
might receive more votes. For the
offices of vice-president and secretary,
respectively, the nominees will take
office in order of the number of votes
which they receive.
Election for clubhouse board mem
j hers will also be held on Monday,
l votes being cast on the same election
blanks on which Council members will
be chosen. One junior representative
will be chosen and two from the soph
omore and freshman classes, respec-
tively. Following are the nominees for
the board:
Junior, Georgia Hell and Dorothy
Plummer; sophomore, Katheriue Ott,
Florence Fabling, Alice Faitout, and
Catherine Williams; freshman, Anne
Hardy, Elizabeth Gregory, Dorothy
Winkle, and Alberta Marx.
Women voted last Wednesday at a
mass meeting to amend the constitu
tion of the Organized Women Stu
dents by providing for a finance com
mittee to control the finances of the
women students. The committee is
to consist of three members: one
elected by Conference, not necessarily
a Conference member, and one elected
by the Women's Athletic Association.
The third member is to be elected by
the women at large at a Quad election
from four nominees: two nominated
by Conference and two nominated by
Women's Athletic Association.
The committee is to have charge of
collecting all Associated Student Body
dues and will distribute the percentage
of them allowed to the women's ac
tivities, according to budgets present
ed by the different women's organiza
tions. The committee is to hold office
for one year and any member can at
any time be recalled by joint vote of
W. A. A. and Conference.
