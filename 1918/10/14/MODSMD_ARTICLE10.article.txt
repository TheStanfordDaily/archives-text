# PLANS FOR DRIVE ARE DISCUSSED BY WAR WORK COUNCIL
## 
### 
A meeting of the War Work Council
was held this afternoon at 4 o'clock, at
the Women's Club House, to discuss
plans for the coming drive for $27,000
to be used in war work during the com
ing year.
Representatives from the Knights of
Columbus, the V. M. C. A., and the Y.
W. C. A., as well as from several other
similar organizations, attended the meet
ing.
Last year the Y. M. C. A. and Y. W.
C. A. in conjunction raised over $12,000,
and it is hoped that with the seven or
ganizations in cooperation the amount
desired will be raised.
A. E. Worthy, in charge of the Y. M.
C. A. at Stanford, today announced that
I lie freshman Bibles had been unavoid
ably delayed. He said that he had had
no word from the company with which
lie had contracted for the printing of the
Bibles, but that he hoped that the books
would arrive soon.
