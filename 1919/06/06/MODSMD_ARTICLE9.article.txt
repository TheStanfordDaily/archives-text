# SENIOR EDITION OF DAILY PALO ALTO OUT ON JUNE 16TH
## 
### 
The traditional Senior Edition of
the Daily Palo Alto will be published
next week and placed on sale on
Commencement Day, Monday, June
16th. This issue will be unusually
attractive, since it will consist of
eight pages of cuts and stories of
general interest. It will be absolute
ly devoid of the undecorative adver
tisement.
Each event of Senior Week will
be given special mention in the Sen
ior Edition, and many of them will
be illustrated. Pictures of prominent
members of the Senior Class and sev
eral good josh snapshots will add to
the general quality of the sheet.
The front page of the last D. P. A.
issue will be a symbolic illustration
drawn by George Kocher, '19. This
picture will characterize the spirit of
the graduating class as it departs
from four years of college life to enter
the business world.
The Commencement Address by Dr.
Paul Shorey and the Baccalaureate
Address by the Rev. Bishop Matt S.
Hughes, will both be reproduced in
full in the Senior Edition. The ad
dress to the members of Phi Beta
Kappa, which is to be delivered by
Professor William F. Qurand, will
also be printed.
The Class Oration, the Class Poem,
the Class History and the Class Will,
as well as the Class Roll, will all be
included in the Senior Edition. An
other item of especial interest will be
an article in which will be all of the
engagements announced at Seniro
breakfasts, at the sorority houses.
A detailed criticism of the Senior
Farce will be written for this issun
of the Daily. The Service Pageant
will also be covered in full.
It is planned that this issue will
be so constituted that it will be of
interest not only to members of the
Senior Class, but also to Alumni and
to undergraduates. Many items on
general topics will be included.
Neil H. Petree, '19, was appointed
by President Pelouze of the Senior
Class, to edit th Snior Edition. He
will assume the management of the
sheet also. Copies of the Senior
Edition will sell at fifteen cents (15c)
apiece if ordered previous to Com
mencement. Day. On that date they
will be sold for twenty-five cents
(25c) per copy. To those who desire
to order copies but who are not go
ing to be here on Monday, they will
be sent free of extra charge. Orders
may be made previously by anyone,
however, and the papers will be sent
or called for, as the purchaser desires.
Order may be made from date
throughout the next week, through
Neil Petree, at the Delta Tan Delta
House, or through Miss Ruth Searcy
at Roble Hall.
