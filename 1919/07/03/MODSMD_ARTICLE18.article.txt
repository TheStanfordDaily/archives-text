# STANFORD UNIVERSITY
## WEEKLY CALENDAR 
### 
Vol. X. No. 3

PUBLISHED EVERY FRIDAY

July 6-12, 1919

SUNDAY, July 6
11 a.m. DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The
Reverend Stephen B. L. Penrose, D.D., President of Whitman
College, Walla Walla, Washington.
4:00 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, in the Memorial Church.
MR. WARREN D. ALLEN, Organist.
The organ recitals in the Memorial Church at Stanford University are
continuing throughout the summer quarter as usual—on Sun
days and Wednesdays at 4:00 o'clock.
Next Sunday, July 6th, Mr. Warren D. Allen, the University organist,
will play a special patriotic program which will be in full as
follows:
Program :
Triumphal March from "Aida" Verdi
Prayer for Peace Paul Held
Carillon Sir Edward Elgar
(On the poem "Chantons, Beiges") arranged for organ by
the performer.
Thanksgiving March E. H. Lemarc
(Written Nov. 11th. 1918)
Hymns of the Allies:—
France, Belgium, Italy, England, United States.
TUESDAY, July 8
8:00 p.m. ADDRESS, by Professor Bailey Willis; Subject: "South
American Peace Problems." In the Little Theatre.
WEDNESDAY, July 9
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, in the Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m. CHOIR REHEARSAL, in the Memorial Church.
THURSDAY, July 10
8:00 p.m. LECTURE on "Religion and Industrial Reconstruction,"
by Mr. Walter McArthur, of the Seamen's Fournal, San
Francisco: in the Little Theatre.

OFFICIAL NOTICES

MEMORIAL CHURCH
University Preacher:
July 6—The Reverend Stephen I_>. L. Penrose, President of Whitman
College.
July 13—President Penrose.
Half Hour of Music: 4:00 p.m., Sunday; 4:15 p.m., Wednesday.
CHRISTIANITY FACING THE NEW AGE.
llie following Weekly Lectures by leading men will be given under
the auspices of the Stanford Young Men's Christian Association during
the Summer Quarter. They will be open to the general public and will
be given on Thursday evenings, at 8 :00 p.m., in the Little Theater.
July 10—"Religion and Industrial Reconstruction," by Mr. Walter Mc-
Arthur, of the Seamen's Journal, San Francisco.
July 17—"Christianity and Internationalism," by Dr. Tully C. Knoles,
President of the College of the Pacific, San Jose.
July 2A—"Religious Education and the Future of Democracy," by Pro
fessor E. C. Rugh, of the Department of Education, Uni
versity of California.
July 31— J he Religious Ideas Behind Democracy," by the Reverend
C. S. S. Dutton, D. D., of the First Unitarian Church, San
Francisco. (Conditional).
August 7—"Christianity the Dynamic of Social Progress," by Professor
K. C. Leebrick, of the Department of History, University of
California.

TUESDAY EVENING MEETINGS
July 15—Mr. Ben S. Allen, of the Sacramento Union, late of the U. S.
Food Administration, an address: "How our Democracy
Accepted Food Conservation."
July 22—The Reverend C. S. S. Dutton, Pastor of the First Unitarian
Church. San Francisco, a lecture.
July 29—Professor Warner Fite, of Princeton University, a lecture;
"Our .Biological Ethics."
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
The Art Gallery is open to the public, without charge, during the
following hours: 10:0Q a.m. to 5:00 p.m., daily, including Sunday.
THE WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, legibly written and
signed, should be in the hands of the President's Secretary, President's
Office, not later than 5 :00 p.m., Thursday.
President's Secretary.
