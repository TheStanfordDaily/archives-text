Program
Prelude in G major Bach

TUESDAY, July 29
:00 p.m LECTt RE on "Our Biological Ethics", by Professor

WEDNESDAY, July 30
4:15 p.m. Organ RECITAL, in the Memorial Church.

8:00 p.m. LECTURE on "Problems of* Educational Reconstruction,
in England and the United States", by Dr. I. L. Kandel,
Lecturer in Foreign School Systems at Teachers' College,
Columbia University, in Room 50.
OFFICIAL NOTICES
MEMORIAL CHURCH
University Preachers:
August 3—The Reverend Caleb S. S. Dutton of the First Unitarian
Church, San Francisco.
August 10—The Reverend J. Wilmer Gresham, Dean of Grace Cathedral.
San Francisco.
August 17—The Reverend J. Wilmer Gresham.
August 24 —The Reverend J. Wilmer Gresham.
Half Hour of Music: 4:00 p.m., Sunday; 4:15 p.m., Wednesday.
SPECIAL EDUCATIONAL LECTURES
Wednesday, July 30—Dr. I. L. Kandel, Lecturer in Foreign School
Systems at Teachers' College, Columbia University, New
York City, will give a lecture on Problems of Educational
Reconstruction, in England and the United States, in
Room 50, at 8:00 p.m.
TUESDAY EVENING MEETINGS
July 29—Professor Warner Fite, of Princeton University, a lecture:
"Our Biological Ethics".
August s—Professor Alfred Coester of New York, a lecture: "Ob
servations in Chile, Argentine, and Brazil, During the
Great W]ar".
August 12—Dr. E. B. Copeland, Chico, California, a lecture: "The
State in the Light of Natural Ethics".
AT HOME
Sunday, July 27—4:00-6:oo—At Home. Prof, and Mrs. Cubberley,
12 Cabrillo Avenue, Campus. (To Summer Quarter Stu
dents majoring in the Department or School of Education.)
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
The Art Gallery is open to the public, without charge, during the
following hours: 10:00 a. m. to 5:00 p.m., daily, including Sunday.

Vol.. X. No. 6
PUBLISHED EVERY FRIDAY
July 27-Aug. 2, 1919
SUNDAY, July 27
11:00 a.m. DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The
Reverend Caleb S. S. Dutton, of the First Unitarian Church,
San Francisco.
4:00 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, in the Memorial Church, Warren D.
Allen, Organist.
Program
Prelude in G major Bach
Adagio and Allegretto (from the Fifth Symphony)....Widor
Cantabile in B major Franck
Marche aux Flambeaux (Torchlight March) Guilmant
TUESDAY, July 29
:00 p.m LECTt RE on "Our Biological Ethics", by Professor
Warner Fite, of Princeton University; in the Little Theatre.
WEDNESDAY, July 30
4:15 p.m. Organ RECITAL, in the Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m. CHOIR REHEARSAL, in the Memorial Church.
8:00 p.m. LECTURE on "Problems of* Educational Reconstruction,
in England and the United States", by Dr. I. L. Kandel,
Lecturer in Foreign School Systems at Teachers' College,
Columbia University, in Room 50.
OFFICIAL NOTICES
MEMORIAL CHURCH
University Preachers:
August 3—The Reverend Caleb S. S. Dutton of the First Unitarian
Church, San Francisco.
August 10—The Reverend J. Wilmer Gresham, Dean of Grace Cathedral.
San Francisco.
August 17—The Reverend J. Wilmer Gresham.
August 24 —The Reverend J. Wilmer Gresham.
Half Hour of Music: 4:00 p.m., Sunday; 4:15 p.m., Wednesday.
SPECIAL EDUCATIONAL LECTURES
Wednesday, July 30—Dr. I. L. Kandel, Lecturer in Foreign School
Systems at Teachers' College, Columbia University, New
York City, will give a lecture on Problems of Educational
Reconstruction, in England and the United States, in
Room 50, at 8:00 p.m.
TUESDAY EVENING MEETINGS
July 29—Professor Warner Fite, of Princeton University, a lecture:
"Our Biological Ethics".
August s—Professor Alfred Coester of New York, a lecture: "Ob
servations in Chile, Argentine, and Brazil, During the
Great W]ar".
August 12—Dr. E. B. Copeland, Chico, California, a lecture: "The
State in the Light of Natural Ethics".
AT HOME
Sunday, July 27—4:00-6:oo—At Home. Prof, and Mrs. Cubberley,
12 Cabrillo Avenue, Campus. (To Summer Quarter Stu
dents majoring in the Department or School of Education.)
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
The Art Gallery is open to the public, without charge, during the
following hours: 10:00 a. m. to 5:00 p.m., daily, including Sunday.
THE WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, legibly written and
signed, should be in the hands of the President's Secretary, President's
Office, not later than 5 :00 p.m., Thursday.
President's Secretary.
