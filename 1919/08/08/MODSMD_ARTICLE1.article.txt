# SUCCESSOR OF STOLZ IS NAMED BY TRUSTEES
## GET WELL KNOWN MAN Other Appointments Made; Two Resignations Are Accepted 
### 
r\R. A D. BROJVNE, former director
of physical training at the State
Agricultural College at Corvallis, Ore
gon, and for the last year secretary
of the Pacific Coast Intercollegiate
Conference, has been appointed associ
ate professor of physical training and
medical adviser of men to succeed Dr.
H. R. Stolz, who recently resigned.
The appointment was announced this
week by the Board of Trustees in San
Francisco.
Dr. Browne is in charge of the de
partment of physical education at the
University of California summer
school at the present time. He was
in the army as director of athletic
courses for the training of athletic
instructors. Later he was director of
a school for training reconstruction
aids for military hospitals. He was
a captain of the Medical Reserve
Corps.
The new head of the physical train
ing department has taken a keen in
terest in athletics always and has
done much for the success of the
Pacific Coast Intercollegiate Confer
ence. He comes to Stanford highly
recommended.
Other appointments made by the
Board of Trustees included Dr. Jean
R. Oliver as associate professor of
pathology. Dr. Oliver graduated from
Stanford Medical School in 1914. He
was assistant instructor in 1913-15 and
was an instructor in 1915-16. In 191(5
he went to the Rockefeller Institute
for Medical Research in New York,
where he served as an assistant, and
later an associate, which position he
now holds.- He is a contributor to
the Journal of Experimental Medicine,
the Journal of Medical Research and
the Journal of Anatomy.
Don Antonio Heras of Madrid, was
appointed instructor in Spanish for
the coming year. He formerly was
instructor in Spanish at the University
of Chicago, and is" now assisting in
the preparation of a dictionary of the
Spanish language.
Miss Yolanda Shaw Allen was ap
pointed instructor in Roble Gymnasi
um. At present she is instructor of
physical education for women at the
University of Colorado. She is a grad
uate of Wellesley College, in the de
partment of hygiene.
Dr. Hans Barkan, assistant clinical
professor of surgery, has resumed his
duties at the medical school after
being discharged from the army.
Dr. Yamato Ichihashi, assistant pro
fessor of Japanese history and govern
ment, was granted a leave of absence
for the academic year 1919-20.
Leave of absence for the first two
quarters of the coming year was
granted professor Vernon L. Kellogg,
to enable him to continue his work
with the National Research Council.
Dr. William Kenny was appointed
clinical instructor in medicine; and
Nathalie Selling was appointed clinical
instructor in medicine (electro
therapy).
Dr. Harry L. Langnecker was pro
moted from the rank of clinical in
structor to that of assistant clinical
professor of surgery (orthopedics) in
the medical school.
The following resignations were ac
cepted :
Dr. F. Wolfsolin as clinic dentist,
to take effect immediately.
Dr. Elmer W. Smith, Instructor in
pathology, to take effect September 1.
