## MANY VETERANS BACK Interfraternity Club Series Will Commence on October 15 
### 
Soccer men held their first practice
this afternoon under the direction of
James A. Quinby, '18, as coach. Quin
by recently was named to direct the
soccer players by H. W. Maloney,
physical training instructor. A large
number of new and old men were on
the field.
Coach Quinby handled the soccer
team that represented the Ninety-first
Division in the northwest and also in
France. This army team made an ex
cellent showing. It was defeated only
once, and that time by a fast team
from Canada. Quinby is confident
that he can develop a fast team to
represent Stanford.
Among the old men who are back
are William and James Adams, who
are fast backfield men. Pillsbury,
who captains the soccer men, is also
back, and with Quinby, is the nucleus
for a strong forward aggregation.
Stanford must win the series with
California this year in order to re
tain the cup permanently.
It is expected tnat a large number
of men will be attracted to succer in
that a Circle "S" is awarded to the
men playing in the series with Cali
fornia.
Quinby announced that a freshman
team will be organized also and the
1923 men are urged to report at the
gymnasium for this sport. Men mak
ing the freshman team win their class
numerals.
According to present arrangements,
the game with California will be play
ed on the Stanford turf, the morning
of November 22d, the day of the
American football contest with the
Blue and Gold. Games in the inter
fraternity and club league will com
mence on October 15th.
Newman Blood, a Stanford soccer
enthusiast, who is at present in Brit
ish Columbia, is attempting to make
arrangements with Canadian teams
for the Cardinal soccer team to make
an Eastern trip during the Christmas
vacation, according to advices received
by Student Manager Ames.
