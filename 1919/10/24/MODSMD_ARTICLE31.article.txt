# OFFICIAL NOTICES
##

MEMORIAL CHURCH
University Preacher:
October 26—The Reverend O. P. Gifford of Los Angeles.
7:50 a. m. Morning Chapel, with address by the Rev
erend Bryant C. Preston of the First Congregational
Church, Palo Alto, Monday to Friday, inclusive.
Half hour of Music: 4:00 p.m., Sunday; 4:15 p.m.,
Tuesday and Thursday.
TUESDAY EVENING MEETINGS
October 28—Mr. A. F. Whyte, M. P., Editor of The New Europe, an
address on "Labor in Great Britain."
November 4—Professor Allan Abbott, of Teachers' College, Columbia
University, an address on "Can the Taste for Poetry Be
Measured ?"
November 11—Professor Lewis M. Terman, of the Department of Edu
cation, an address on "Psychological Tests in the Army,
and their Applications in Peace." (Illustrated with slides.)
November 18—Lieutenant Milutin Krunich, late of the Serbian Army, an
address on "Serbia and Her People." (Illustrated with
slides.)

November 25—Professor Edward K. Rand, Professor of Latin, Harvard
University, and Sather Professor of Classical Literature
at the University of California, an address on "Libraries
in the Middle Ages." (Illustrated with slides.)
BOARD OF TRUSTEES
October 28—Meeting of the University Committee, at 1:30 p. m.
October 29—Meeting of the Finance Committee, at 11 a. m.
October 31—Meeting of the Board of Trustees, at 1:30 p. m.
DEAN OF WOMEN
Miss Bradford will be at home informally, from 4 nntil 6, on Wednes
day afternoon, at her residence, 2 Lasuen street.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
The Art Gallery is open to the public, without charge, during the
following hours: 10: 00 a. m.to 5:00 p. m., daily, including Sunday.
Exhibition of Artistic Textiles and Weavings, ancient and modern;
October 19th to November 17, 1919.
THE WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, legibly written and signed.
should be in the hands of the President's Secretary, President's Office, not
later than 5:00 p.m., Thursday.
President's Secretary.

MEMORIAL CHURCH
University Preacher:
October 26—The Reverend O. P. Gifford of Los Angeles.
7:50 a. m. Morning Chapel, with address by the Rev
erend Bryant C. Preston of the First Congregational
Church, Palo Alto, Monday to Friday, inclusive.
Half hour of Music: 4:00 p.m., Sunday; 4:15 p.m.,
Tuesday and Thursday.
TUESDAY EVENING MEETINGS
October 28—Mr. A. F. Whyte, M. P., Editor of The New Europe, an
address on "Labor in Great Britain."
November 4—Professor Allan Abbott, of Teachers' College, Columbia
University, an address on "Can the Taste for Poetry Be
Measured ?"
November 11—Professor Lewis M. Terman, of the Department of Edu
cation, an address on "Psychological Tests in the Army,
and their Applications in Peace." (Illustrated with slides.)
November 18—Lieutenant Milutin Krunich, late of the Serbian Army, an
address on "Serbia and Her People." (Illustrated with
slides.)

November 25—Professor Edward K. Rand, Professor of Latin, Harvard
University, and Sather Professor of Classical Literature
at the University of California, an address on "Libraries
in the Middle Ages." (Illustrated with slides.)
BOARD OF TRUSTEES
October 28—Meeting of the University Committee, at 1:30 p. m.
October 29—Meeting of the Finance Committee, at 11 a. m.
October 31—Meeting of the Board of Trustees, at 1:30 p. m.
