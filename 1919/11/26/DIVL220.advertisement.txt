November 30-December 6, 1919
MONDAY, December 1
7:15 p.m. LECTURE on "How to Choose a Life Work," by Pro
fessor William M. Proctor, of the Department of Educa
tion, under the auspices of the Y. M. C. A.; in the Little
Theatre.
TUESDAY, December 2
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Warren D. Allen, University
Organist; in the Memorial Church.
7:30 p. m. ADDRESS on "The New Industrial Revolution in Britain,"
by Dr. John A. Hobson, of London; in the Assembly Hall.
WEDNESDAY, December 3
3:00 p.m. RESIDENT ALUMNAE TEA, in the Women's Club
House. Hostesses: Mrs. J. W. Mitchell, Mrs. Joseph
Hutchinson, Mrs. Frederic Wheeler, Mrs. J. G. Sharp,
Mrs. Jerome Thomas, Mrs. G. J. Peirce.
4:15 p.m. INFORMAL READING from the Short Stories of Henry
James, by Mrs. J. S. P. Tatlock; in Room 42.
4:45 p. m. WOMEN OF THE UNIVERSITY STAFF will meet at
the Resident Alumnae Tea, in the Women's Club House.
7:30 p.m. NESTORIA DEBATING SOCIETY, in Room 12, Law
Building.
7:30 p.m. STANFORD CHESS CLUB, in Encina Hall.
THURSDAY, December 4
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Warren D. Allen, University
Organist; in the Memorial Church.
7:30 p. m. EUPHRONIA DEBATING SOCIETY, in Room 12, Law
Building.
7:30 p. m. CHOIR REHEARSAL, in the Memorial Church.
8:15 p.m. ISADORA DUNCAN DANCERS, assisted by George
Copeland, pianist, under the auspices of the Peninsula
Musical Association; in the Assembly Hall.
FRIDAY, December 5
8:15 p.m. "NEVER SAY DIE," a Three-Act Comedy, presented by
the Sophomore Class; in the Assembly Hall. Tickets
may be obtained at Crandall's, Palo Alto, and at the
Stanford Bookstore, Campus.
OFFICIAL NOTICES
MEMORIAL CHURCH
7:50 a. m. Morning Chapel, Monday to Friday, inclusive, with address
by the Reverend David Evans, Rector of All Saints'
Church, Palo Alto.
Half hour of music, 4:15 p. m., Tuesday and Thursday.
LANE MEDICAL LECTURES
The Lane Medical Lectures will be given by Dr. Alonzo Englebert
Taylor, Professor of Physiological Chemistry at the University of Penn
sylvania, at 8:15 p. m., in Lane Hall, Stanford University Medical School.
Sacramento Street near Webster, San Francisco.
Subject: "The Feeding of the Nations at War."
December B—l. "The Problem of Feeding a Nation."
December 9—2. "The Feeding of the United Kingdom."
December 10—3. "The Feeding of France and Italy."
December 11—4. "The Feeding of the Enemy States."
December 12—5. "The Food Problem of Europe after the War."
TUESDAY EVENING MEETINGS
December 2—Dr. John A. Hobson, of London, an address.
December 9—Miss Dorothy Marcuse, pianist, a concert.
AT HOME
December 4—Mrs. Wilbur will be at home on Thursday afternoon. Presi
dent's House.
DEAN OF WOMEN
Miss Bradford will be at home informally, from 4 until 6, on Wednes
day afternoon, at her residence, 2 Lasuen street.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
The Art Gallery is open to the public, without charge, during the
following hours: 10: 00 a. m.to 5: 00 p. m., daily, including Sunday.
