# Y.M.C.A. ARRANGES LECTURE PROGRAM
## 
### 
Arrangement of a program for the
course of lectures that will be given
the latter part of this week and the
first part of next week by speakers of
the Inter-Church World Movement has
been made by the local Y. M. C. A.
which has charge of the coming lec
ture series. The following program
has been arranged:
Friday, February 27, 10:00 a. m.—
Address by Dr. Raymond Robins on
"The Challenge of the Hour," in the
Assembly Hall. 12:00 m., informal
address by Dr. Robins at the Faculty
Luncheon, Women's Clubhouse. 7:30
p.m., address by Dr. Robins in the
Assembly Hall on the topic, "The In
dispensible Place of Christianity in
the New Democracies."
Sunday, February 29, 11:00 a. m. —
Address by Dr. Silas Evans, president
of Occidental College, at the Memorial
Church. 7:00 p.m., Union Young Peo
ple's Meeting at the Congregational
Church, Palo Alto. This meeting will
be a "Life Work" meeting and will
be addressed by five speakers.
Monday, March 1, 7:30 p.m.—
Stereopticon lecture in the Assembly
Hall, by Dr. H. H. Bell, of the Inter-
Church World Movement, New York.
Tuesday, March 2, 7:30 p. m. —Ad-
dress by Bishop E. L. Parsons, of
Oakland, at the Little Theater.
