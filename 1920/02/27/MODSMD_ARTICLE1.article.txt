# STAFF FOR THE SUMMER QUARTER APPOINTED BY TRUSTEES
## Board Selects Well Known Men From Eastern Universities 
### 
Confirmation of several appointments
to the teaching staff of the University
for the summer quarter, was the chief
business of the Board of Trustees at its
meeting today. Two appointments for
the spring quarter were also m&de, and
the names of special preachers for the
summer quarter were announced.
Appointments for the summer quarter
are:
Professor ['"rank A. Golder, of Wash
ington State College, to he acting profes
sor of European History.
Duane Stuart Reed, professor of
Classics at Princeton University, to be
acting professor of Latin. Dr. Stuart
will offer courses that were scheduled
for Professor Fairclough, who will not
return from his work as Red Cross
Commissioner to Montenegro in time to
take up his work at Stanford this sum
mer.
Rudolph Schacffcr to be instructor in
Design in the Division of Graphic Art.
Mr. Schaeffer is an instructor in the Cal
ifornia School of Fine Arts in San Fran
cisco, and has been an instructor at
Stanford for the last two summer quar
ters. He was one of the fifteen men
sent to Europe recently by the govern
ment to study European methods of art
instruction.
James Wilfred Garner, head of the
Political Science Department at the Uni
versity of Illinois, to be acting profes-
sor of Political Science. Dr. Garner
carried on advanced work at the Univer
sity of Chicago, where he received his
Ph. D. He was formerly lecturer in
History at Columbia University, and is
a voluminous writer, having written a
number of books, and contributed arti
cles to magazines and encyclopedias in
his special subjects. He has been edi
tor of the "American Journal of Crimi
nal Law and Criminology."
Dr. Edward Scribner Ames, associate
professor of Philosophy at the Univer
sity of Chicago, to be acting associate
professor of Philosophy. Dr. Ames re
ceived his degree of Bachelor of Divin
ity from Yale, and that of Doctor of
Philosophy from Chicago University.
He is also pastor of the Hyde Park
Church of the Disciples in Chicago. He
was university preacher at Harvard in
1912-13-14, and is author of three reli
gious books.
Professor Tom Peete Cross, profes
sor of English and Celtic Literature at
the University of Chicago, to be acting
professor of English for.the first half of
tlie quarter.
Stuart Walker is to be lecturer on
Dramatic Art in the English Department
for the second half of the quarter. Mr.
Walker is the author of several plays
and the founder of ' the Portmanteau
Theater. He has been stage manager for
David Belasco and has lectured at Stan- j
ford before.
The following appointments of clergy-1
men as special preachers during the sum
mer quarter wece made:
Dean J. W. Gresham, of Grace Cathe
dral, San Francisco, from July 4 to 18;
Rev. Carl S. Patton. First Congrega
tional Church, Los Angeles, July 25 to
August 8; Rev. Sydney Snow, of King's
Chapel, Boston, from August 15 to 29.1
Mr. Snow was pastor of the Unitarian'
Church of Palo Alto for several years.
August J. Affleck was appointed teach
ing assistant in Bacteriology for the
spring quarter; and the appointment of
Miss Eva N. Newnan was continued
through the spring quarter as acting in
structor in Latin.

mer.
Rudolph Schacffcr to be instructor in
Design in the Division of Graphic Art.
Mr. Schaeffer is an instructor in the Cal
ifornia School of Fine Arts in San Fran
cisco, and has been an instructor at
Stanford for the last two summer quar
ters. He was one of the fifteen men
sent to Europe recently by the govern
ment to study European methods of art
instruction.
James Wilfred Garner, head of the
Political Science Department at the Uni
versity of Illinois, to be acting profes-
sor of Political Science. Dr. Garner
carried on advanced work at the Univer
sity of Chicago, where he received his
Ph. D. He was formerly lecturer in
History at Columbia University, and is
a voluminous writer, having written a
number of books, and contributed arti
cles to magazines and encyclopedias in
his special subjects. He has been edi
tor of the "American Journal of Crimi
nal Law and Criminology."
Dr. Edward Scribner Ames, associate
professor of Philosophy at the Univer
sity of Chicago, to be acting associate
professor of Philosophy. Dr. Ames re
ceived his degree of Bachelor of Divin
ity from Yale, and that of Doctor of
Philosophy from Chicago University.
He is also pastor of the Hyde Park
Church of the Disciples in Chicago. He
was university preacher at Harvard in
1912-13-14, and is author of three reli
gious books.
Professor Tom Peete Cross, profes
sor of English and Celtic Literature at
the University of Chicago, to be acting
professor of English for.the first half of
tlie quarter.
Stuart Walker is to be lecturer on
Dramatic Art in the English Department
for the second half of the quarter. Mr.
Walker is the author of several plays
and the founder of ' the Portmanteau
Theater. He has been stage manager for
David Belasco and has lectured at Stan- j
ford before.
