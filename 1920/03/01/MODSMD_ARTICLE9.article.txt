# LARGE AUDIENCE HEARS ROBINS ON FRIDAY EVENING
## Gives Interesting Address On Religions Place In Democracies 
### 
Raymond Robins delivered the last
of a series of three addresses in the
Assembly Hall Friday evening, speaking
on the subject of "The Ultimate Place
of Christianity in the New Democ
racies."
The speaker told of his experiences in
social relief work in Chicago where
75,000 people lived on an area of one
square mile, and in this large popula
tion thirty-one different languages were
spoken. Social and economic conditions
could hardly have been in a lower state.
Pure food, and an adequate supply, had
to be gained. Labor conditions had to
be improved. Child labor had to be
fought in the state legislature. A living
wage had to be earned by the laborers.
In order to gain these tilings it was nec
essary for them to strike.. In discussing
the results gained in this particular ward
in Chicago, Dr. Robins said:'
"Moral and spiritual forces are great
factors in solving social problems but
religion is the greatest force there is in
this connection.
J>r. Robins then outlined the religious
condition of Russia and Germany before
the war and showed how the decided
4ack of spiritual influence had a great
elfect in bringing the two countries to
their present deplorable conditions. He
also paid a high tribute to the allied
soldiers and to the religious influences
of their respective countries when he
said:
"The allied soldiers, most of them in
the flower of life, laid down their lives
that we might live and enjoy life, and
in so doing they showed a very high and
unselfish sense of religion."
In closing, the speaker said that the
rate of advancement of a country, and
the liberty and freedom of its inhabi
tants would depend largely upon the
place which religion occupied in the
minds of the people. Dr. David Starr
Jordan, chancellor emeritus, introduced
the speaker, and acted as chairman of
the meeting.
