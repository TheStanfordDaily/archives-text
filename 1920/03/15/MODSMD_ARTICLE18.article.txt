While Gray and Schwartz were hold
ing Sail Jose High School's baseball
team to one hit and one run, the fresh
man nine piled up a total of seven hits
and five runs and won the game, which
was played on the local diamond Satur
day afternoon.
The Babes scored first in the second
inning, when George Green walked,
stole second, advanced to third on ;m
infield out, and scored 011 Carver's single
|o left center. Two more runs wen.
.•1 elded to the score in the third, when
Peavy and C. Green were brought in
with a long triple to right bv Hecken
dorf.
San Jose made its lone run in the
seventh, and its only hit in the ninth.
The freshman pitchers seemed to be in
rare form, and were well supported by
the infield. Two double plays were ex
ecuted by the C. Green to Peavy to Pat
terson route.
Bruno replaced Dowle for San Jose
in the fourth and held the freshmen to 1
tour liits and no runs for the remainder
of the game.
