ATHLETIC courtesy is still one subject which has not been thoroughly ab
sorbed even yet by a few members of the student body. The athletic
events last Saturday afternoon proved this. Constant jeering at times made by
opponents or even Stanford men at the track meet was embarrassing to those
men and women who felt some responsibility as hosts. The same thing "can
be said for the baseball game. It is to be hoped that these students will
realize that such things aren't being done at Stanford, never have been, and
never will be. Thoughtlessness of action brings criticism upon the Univer
sity as a whole, upon the student body in general, and upon certain indi
viduals in particular. Let's watch our step.
