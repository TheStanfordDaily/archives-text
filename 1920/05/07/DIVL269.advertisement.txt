VOL. XIII. No. 6
PUBLISHED EVERY FRIDAY
May 9-15, 1920
SUNDAY, May 9
10:00 a.m.—UNIVERSITY BIBLE CLASSES, in the Memorial
Church.
11:00 a.m.—DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The
Reverend James L. Gordon, D. D., Pastor of the First
Congregational Church, San Francisco. Soloist: Miss
Marion Brown.
4:00 p.m.—ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Warren D. Allen, University
Organist; in the Memorial Church.
Program
Adagio from the Clarinet Quintet Mozart
Symphonie, No. 5, opus 13 Widor
Andantino, quasi Allegretto
Adagio
Toccata
TUESDAY, May 11
4:15 p.m.—ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Warren D. Allen; in the
Memorial Church.
7:30 p. m.—ILLUSTRATED LECTURE on "The Eruption of Saku
rajima, January 10, 1914," by Professor William Leonard
Schwartz; in the Little Theatre.
8:30 p. m.—PHILOSOPHY CLUB, in the Women's Clubhouse. Sub
ject: "Centralization vs. Decentralization of Government."
Open to members and their friends.
WEDNESDAY, May 12
9:00 a. m.—UNIVERSITY ASSEMBLY, in the Assembly Hall. Ad
dress by President Ray Lyman Wilbur on "Ihe Uni
versity."
3:00 p. m.—RESIDENT ALUMNAE TEA, in the Women's Club
house. Hostesses: Members of the Palo Alto and Stan
ford Chapter of the Daughters of the American Revolu
tion, Mrs. E. Whitney Martin, Regent.
7:30 p. in.—STANFORD CHESS CLUB, in Encina Hall.
7:30 p. m.—EUPHRONIA DEBATING SOCIETY, in Room 12, Law
Building.
8:00 p. m.—STANFORD CLASSICAL CLUB, in the Women's Club
house. Address by the Reverend Joseph M. Gleason, of
Palo Alto.
8:00 p.m.—JOINT MEETING of the University Science Association
and the Society of the Sigma Xi; in the Faculty Club
house. Address by Professor H. F. Blichfeldt on "Ein
stein's Theory of Relativity." The members of the Uni
versity are invited.
THURSDAY, May 13
4:15 p.m.—ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Warren D. Allen; in the
Memorial Church.
7:15 p. m.—WRANGLERS, in the Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 p. m.—CHOIR REHEARSAL, in the Memorial Church.
8:00 p.m.—DEBATE, in the Little Theatre. Nestoria Debating So
ciety vs. St. Ignatius University. Question: "Resolved:
That the Government Should Own and Operate the Rail
roads of the United States." Nestoria: C. Minard,
Gerson, R. Lewis. St. Ignatius : V. Clarke, W. Fitzpat
rick, L. Swiegert.
SATURDAY, May 15
8:00 p. m.—"RUMPELSTILTZKIN," a Fairy Play, presented by the
Skylark Corps of the Girl Reserves; in the Little Theatre.
Tickets may be obtained at the Community House, Palo
Alto.
OFFICIAL NOTICES
MEMORIAL CHURCH
University Preachers:
May 9—The Reverend James L. Gordon, D.D., Pastor of the First
Congregational Church, San Francisco.
May 16—The Reverend Richard Roberts, Pastor of the Church of the
Pilgrims, Brooklyn, N. Y.
May 23—The Reverend Richard Roberts.
May 30—The Reverend Richard Roberts.
Morning Chapel: 7:50 a.m., Monday to Friday, inclusive.
Half Hour of Music: 4:00 p.m., Sunday; 4:15 p.m., Tuesday and
Thursday.
TUESDAY EVENING MEETINGS
May 11—Professor William Leonard Schwartz, an illustrated lecture
on "The Eruption of Sakurajima, January 10, 1914."
May 25—The Reverend Richard Roberts, a lecture on "The Background
of Labor Unrest."
DEAN OF WOMEN
Miss Bradford will be at home informally, from 4 until 6, on Wednes
day afternoon, at her residence, 2 Lasuen Street.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
The Art Gallery is open to the public, without charge, during the fol
lowing hours: 10:00 a. m. to 5 :00 p. m., daily, including Sunday.
THE WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, legibly written and signed,
should be in the hands of the President's Secretary, President's Office, not
later than 5 :00 p. m., Thursday.
President's Secretary.
