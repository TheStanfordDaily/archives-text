# Many Betrothals Revealed by Fair Sorority Members
## 
### 
Senior breakfasts and senior ban
quets in the sorority houses have not
been without their "spice" this year.
The usual number of engagement an
nouncements have come forth with the
usual surprises and"I told you so's."
The following are a few, but some
have preferred to keep their secret
still concealed and reporter "no pub
lic announcements."
Chi Omega comes first with three.
Loraine Noble. '21, house president,
announced her engagement to Leslie
Hills, '19, Theta Delta Chi. Miss Noble
took part in the Junior Opera last
year and this year, was a member of
Women's Conference, and acted on
Women's Council in the vacancy left
by Ruth Garland. Hills is now in
San Francisco but may attend Cornell
next year while Miss Noble finishes
her course here. She will attend the
Chi Omega National Convention at
Knoxville, Tenn., with Imogene Aten,
on the 26th of this month.
The engagement of Kathryn Ott, '2ft,
to "Duke" Curtice, '19, was also made
public. Curtice is a member of Delta
Upsilon, has been prominent in foot
ball, and track, and is an all-round
athlete.
Crystal Sayre, '17, announced her
betrothal to Leland Yost of Imperial
Valley. Miss Sayre is to be married
in March and will live in the valley.
She met Yost when he was a Lieuten
ant in the army.
Gamma Phi Beta had both an en
gagement and a marriage to announce.
Ruth Prather, '20, gave out the in
formation that she would be married
to Churchill Duncan of Beldon, Texas,
on August 11, at her home in Salt Lake
City. Duncan is in the stocks and
bonds business and met Miss Prather
when he was in training for aviation
at the ground school at Berkeley dur
ing the war. Helen Dominguez will
return to Salt Lake City with Miss
Prather to act as brides maid.
Carmen Seeman, '20, became the
bride of Ray B. Lyon, '17, on Tuesday
afternoon, in the Memorial Church.
The wedding was announced at the
Gamma Phi Beta senior banquet. The
bride and groom left for a honeymoon
trip to the Humboldt mountains and
will enter their home in Paso Robles
when they return about July 1. Lyon
took his J. D. degree here in 1919 and
is now a practicing lawyer.
Sigma Kappa announced the engage
ment of Edith Patterson, '19, to D. C.
Wise, '17. Miss Patterson is now
studying at Lane hospital while Wise
is pursuing his medical studies at the
Washington Medical School. While
attending Stanford he acted as wrest
ling coach, and assistant instructor in
physical education.
Kappa Alpha Theta's banquet
brough forth two announcements. Ele
anor Marx, '18, is to marry Ashley
Brown, '18. Brown is a member of
Chi Psi fraternity.
Geraldine Cluff, '18, will become the
bride of Weston Wilson, '15, Zeta Psi.
Wilson was prominent while on the
campus in musical circles, having com
posed the music for several Junior
Operas. He has since followed up his
music and has published songs in New
York houses for the last few years.
Alice Moore, '17, announced also to
the Alpha Omicron Phi sisters that she
was to marry James Patton, 'IG, prob
ably in September. Patton is a Phi
Gamma Delta. The' couple plan to
live in Los Angeles, where Patton's
home is located.
