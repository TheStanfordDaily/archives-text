George F. Morrell of Palo Alto, who
secured the beginning .of Fremont Post
last August with a charter roll of sixteen
members, was given a demonstration of
appreciation by the meeting, at the time
of relinquishing his office as commander.
Under Morrell's guidance Fremont Post
has gained national prominence as a
champion of service men in the nearby
government hospital. Direct action on
officials at Washington has resulted in
nlimerous adjustments of delayed com
pensation cases among the patients.
William Ruinbold, who superintended
erection of the big flag pole in front of
the Community House, was tendered a
vote of thanks for his service, as were
other Legionaires and citizens of the
community. The Legion post raised the
pole and provided a flag for the City of
Palo Alto, with funds secured from the
Elsie Janis benefit movie some weeks
ago.
Delegates to the convention will be
given instructions by the Post at the
August meeting, which will precede the
San Diego conclave. Fremont Post del
egates will be specially active in urging
decentralized organization of the War
Risk Insurance Bureau at Washington;
in opposing combination of the Voca
