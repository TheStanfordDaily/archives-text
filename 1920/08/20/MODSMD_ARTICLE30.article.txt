# STANFORD UNIVERSITY
## WEEKLY CALENDAR 
### 
VOL. XIV, No. 9

PUBLISHED EVERY FRIDAY

August 22-28

SUNDAY, AUGUST 22
10:00 a.m. CHOIR REHEARSAL, in the Memorial Church.
11:00 a.m. DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The Reverend
C. S. S. Dutton, of the First Unitarian Church, San Francisco.
Soprano Soloist: Mrs. Charles Corbett.
Anthem: "My God and Father, While I Stray," Marston
4:00 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Warren D. Allen, University Organ
ist; in the Memorial Church.
PROGRAM
Sketch in F minor Schumann
Summer sketches E. H. Lemare
Fantasia Emil Sjogren
TUESDAY, AUGUST 24
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Warren D. Allen; in the Memorial
Church. The Sunday program will be repeated.
8:00 p.m. ADDRESS, "Our Foreign Policy and America's Duty," by Dr.
James Wilford Garner, in the Little Theater.
THURSDAY, AUGUST 26
3:15 p.m. PHILOLOGICAL ASSOCIATION, in Room 95, (Pfiilosophy
Building). Papers will be read by Dr. H. S. Gray and Dr. A. G.
Kennedy.
8:00 p.m. MASTER PIERRE PATELIN, a XVth Century farce, will be
presented in the Little Theatre by the Renaissance Club under the
direction of Mr. Irving Pichel. The public is cordially invited.
OFFICIAL NOTICES
MEMORIAL CHURCH
University Preachers:
August 22—The Reverend C. S. S. Dutton, of the First Unitarian Church, San
Francisco.
August 29—The Reverend W. R. H. Hodgkin, of St. Mark's Church, Berkeley.
Organ Recitals:
The last Recitals of the season will be given on Thursday, August 26th, and
Sunday, August 29th. At each Recital Mr. Allen will play the great 94th Psalm
by Julius Reubke and the Grail March from Wagner's "Parsifal."
END-QUARTER EXAMINATIONS
8:00 a.m. Wednesday, September 1st, to 6:00 p.m., Friday, September 3d.
BOARD OF TRUSTEES
August 24 —Meeting of the University Committee, at 1 :30 p. m.
August 25—Meeting of the Finance Committee, at 11:00 a. m.
August 27—Meeting of the Board of Trustees, at 1 :30 p. m.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
The Art Gallery is open to the public, without charge, during the following
hours: 10:00 a. m. to 5 :00 p. m., daily, including Sunday.
THE WEEKLY CALENDAR
The next issue of the Weekly Calendar will appear on Friday, October 1, 1920.
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, legibly written and signed, should
be in the hands of the President's Secretary, President's Office, not later than
12:00 m., Thursday.
President's Secretary.

OFFICIAL NOTICES
MEMORIAL CHURCH
University Preachers:
August 22—The Reverend C. S. S. Dutton, of the First Unitarian Church, San
Francisco.
August 29—The Reverend W. R. H. Hodgkin, of St. Mark's Church, Berkeley.
Organ Recitals:
The last Recitals of the season will be given on Thursday, August 26th, and
Sunday, August 29th. At each Recital Mr. Allen will play the great 94th Psalm
by Julius Reubke and the Grail March from Wagner's "Parsifal."
END-QUARTER EXAMINATIONS
8:00 a.m. Wednesday, September 1st, to 6:00 p.m., Friday, September 3d.
BOARD OF TRUSTEES
August 24 —Meeting of the University Committee, at 1 :30 p. m.
August 25—Meeting of the Finance Committee, at 11:00 a. m.
August 27—Meeting of the Board of Trustees, at 1 :30 p. m.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
The Art Gallery is open to the public, without charge, during the following
hours: 10:00 a. m. to 5 :00 p. m., daily, including Sunday.
THE WEEKLY CALENDAR
The next issue of the Weekly Calendar will appear on Friday, October 1, 1920.
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, legibly written and signed, should
be in the hands of the President's Secretary, President's Office, not later than
12:00 m., Thursday.
President's Secretary.
