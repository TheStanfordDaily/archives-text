# TRUSTEES CONFIRM NEW APPOINTMENTS
## 
### 
Miss Gwendolen Harriet Garsed's ap
pointment to the position of Assistant
Registrar was confirmed at the last
regular monthly meeting of the Board
of Trustees. Following her graduation
from Stanford in 1918, Miss Garsed has
been an assistant to Registrar O. L.
Elliott. The new appointment will fill
the vacancy left by the resignation of J.
E. McDowell who recently retired from
the position after holding it many years.
To assist Professor Lewis M. Terman
of the Department of Education, Miss
Jessica M. Chase was appointed special
earch assistant in education. A re
search fund was recently established by
Professor Terman in cooperation with
the Board of Trustees for the promo
tion of the study of gifted children. A
part of Miss Chase's work will be
in connection with the administration of
the fund. Miss Chase graduated with
the 1918 class from Stanford and is now
completMM! work for her master's de
gree.
Walter L. Bachrodt was appointed a
teaching fellow in the Department of
Education on a foundation recently
established by Professor Ellwood P.
Cubberly, head of the department. The
new fellowship is designed for special
work in school administration. Mr.
Bachrodt has had practical work in the
School of Education.
The new appointee to the Buckel Fel-
lowship for the coming year in the De-
partment of Education is Miss Maude
A. Merrill. Established in 1914 through
the joint contributions of the estate of
Dr. C. Annette Buckel, the Buckel Fel-
lows promote the study of feeble-minded
children. For a number of years Miss
Merrill was assistant psychologist at the
State Home for the Feeble-Minded in
Minnesota. She received her master's
degree from Stanford at the close of the
last summer session.
Three of the University Graduate
Scholarships, paying $200 apiece, were
granted to Herbert B. Rielly, D. McLeod
Boyle, and Newton R. Jackson.
The leave of absence of Assistant
Professor Yamato Ichihashi of the His-
tory Department, now carrying on re
search work in Europe, was extended
through the year 1921-22.
