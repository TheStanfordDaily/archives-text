# RUSHING RULES FOR FRESHMEN MEN REPRINTED
## 
### 
For the benefit of the freshmen stu
dents the following rules of the inter
fraternity conference are republished:
1. The term "freshmen" shall be con
sidered to mean one not having com
pleted three-quarters or its equivalent of
university work here at this University
or any university or college granting a
degree.
No freshman shall be pledged prior to
the fifth Sunday after registration day of
the quarter in which he matricualtes.
2. No freshman shall be initiated un
til he lias completed at least two quar
ters of the university work, excepting
that freshmen matriculating for the
winter quarter may be pledged and in
itiated in the spring quarter.
3. No man shall be initiated into a
fraternity until he shall have completed
at this University the quarter's work
preceding his initiation without having
been put on probation.
4. No freshman, with the exception of
brothers' brothers, the former necessar
ily being members of the active chaptcr,
shall be allowed to live in a fraternity
house for more than one night a week
or board there steadily, except during
registration week... (Note: Registration
week for this quarter ends at midnight
Saturday, October 2.)
6. Brothers' brothers may be given
permission to live in a fraternity house
upon application to the conference by
the chapter's representative.
7. At the discretion of the Inter fra
ternity Conference a freshman may be
allowed to work at a fraternity house
for his board and lodging. No fresh
man is eligible to be pledged by the fra
ternity for which he has been working
for his board or lodging until one quar
ter after he shall have ceased such work.
8. Bids shall be extended to freshine.i
by a lawyer to be selected by the Inter
fraternity Conference, each freshman to

appear before him and state to him his
preference of fraternities. He shall then
receive from the lawyer only that bid
from the fraternity for which he has
first indicated a preference and which
has extended a bid to him.
9. The lawyer shall give no informa
tion to any freshman nor answer any
question for freshmen except such as
may be pertinent to the method of ac
cepting a bid.
10. The fraternities shall turn in their
lists of men bid three days prior to the
day of extending bids, notice to be sent
by the lawyer the day following to ail
freshmen who have bids, notifying them
of the time and place to appear before
the lawyer.
11. No fraternity shall have any date
with a freshman that will make it im
possible for the freshman to be on the
campus by 9 a. m. on the days of bid
ding-.
12. There shall be a second bidding
date, within a week after the first one.
to care for those freshmen who did not
appear on the first bidding date. After
this second bidding, and not before, any
freshman then eligible 10 be pledged
may be pledged at any time by any fra
ternity.
15. All rushing shall cease at mid
night of Wednesday, October 27. (It is
to be noted here that rushing may be
continued after the first bidding date
except in the cases of men who did not
appear at the first bidding date. These
men shall not be rushed after the Wed
nesday preceding the formal bidding
date until after the second bidding date.
16. All rushing shall be restricted, to
the Campus and Palo Alto.
