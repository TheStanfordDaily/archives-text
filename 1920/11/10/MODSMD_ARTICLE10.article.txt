# EX COMMITTEE VOTES APPROVAL OF NEW PAVILION
## Plans of Paying for the New Building Receive Support; Yearly Budget Considered 
### 
Tlie plan of the Board of Athletic
Control to build a permanent Basket
ball-Dance Hall Pavilion was accepted
by the Executive Committee at a meet
ing held last night So far a loan of
$75,000 has been offered by the Uni
versity authorities provided the Student
Body will pay the interest on this loan,
which will be $4,500 a year.
Following are the minutes of the
Executive Committee:
President McEleroy in the chair.
Absent: Roy Hill.
M. S. C. That the A. S. S. U. pur
chase 150 volumes of the 1921 Quad
at $3.00. for distribution in the high
schools.
M. S. C. That Tlieta Sigma Phi be
given the sole privilege of selling "hoc
dogs" on the night of the football rally,
November 28, 1920.
M. S. C. That the rally committee be
loaned $22.50 for transportation ex
penses for bleacher workers to Berke
ley.
M. S. C. That the Executive Com
mittee approves of the plan of Sequoia
Club for a parade on the night preced
ing the Big Game rally.
M. S. C. That the Executive Com
mittee approve of the plan of the Board
of Athletic Control to build a perma
nent Basketball-Dance Hall Pavilion.
M. S. C. That a student body assem
bly be held November 17 at 9 a. m. to
explain this plan.
M. S. C. That a student body election
be held November 17, between 11 a. m.
and 2 p. in., to pass on this plan.
M. S. C. That the Student Manager
be authorized to pay poster bills of
$5.50 and election bills of $2.10.
v M. S. C. That the resignation of Stu
dent Manager E. R. Martin be accepted.
M. S. C. That K. L. (Dink) Temple
ton be appointed Student Manager.
M. S. C. To adjourn.
J. EDWIN SIMPSON, Secretary.
