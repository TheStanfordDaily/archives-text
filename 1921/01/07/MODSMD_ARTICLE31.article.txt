FRIDAY
ASSOCIATED FEDERA.L STUDENTS
—Union reading room, at 7 p.m.
MONDAY
RENAISSANCE CLUB — Women's
Clubhouse, at 7:30. Important busi
ness meeting. All members re
quested to attend.
AT HOME
Dr. and Mrs. Wilbur, at the Presi
dent's house, Saturday ecening. There
will be dancing for those who care to
dance. All students in the University
are invited.
MORNING CHAPEL exercises are
held every morning this week, at
7:50, sharp, in the organ loft of the
Memorial Church. Rev. Willard
Thorpe is the speaker for this week
NOTICE!
The assignment in Economics 2 for
Monday, is Chapter 8.
All candidates for teaching or school
administrative positions should begin
registration in the Appointment Officc at
their earliest convenience, preferably not
later than January 10th, inasmuch as the
high school principals' convention,
which has formerly been held in April,
will this year he held early in Febru
ary. The necessary registration forms
can he secured from one of Mrs. Snell's
assistants.
(Mrs.) Elizabeth Snell,
Appointment Sccrctary.
All students who registered with the
Appointment Office during the fall quar
ter and who still want to he considered
for positions during the winter quarter
should call immediately at the Appoint
ment Officc to renew their registration.
Only those who do renew registration
will lie considered for positions.
(Mrs.) Elizabeth Sntll,
Appointment Sccrctary.
