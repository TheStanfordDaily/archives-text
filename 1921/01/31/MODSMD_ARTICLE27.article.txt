MONDAY
ENCINA BULL PEN CLUB—Meets at
9:30 p.m. in Room 430.
COSMOPOLITAN CLUB CABINET—
Meets in the Women's Clubhouse,
at 7:15 p.m.
RENAISSANCE CLUB—The meeting
scheduled for this evening is post
poned until a week from tonight.
The regular meeting of Panhellenie
Association which was to be held at
5 o'clock today will not be held until
next Monday, at that time.
TUESDAY
SWORD AND SANDALS—WiII meet
on steps of tho English Building, at
12:45, for the Quad picture, instead
of 12:15 as previously announced.
WEDNESDAY
HONORARY MILITARY SOCIETY—
Special meeting, at the Stanford
Union, 7:00 p.m., sharp.
MASONIC CLUB of STANFORD UNI
VERSITY —Meets at the Women's
Clubhouse. All members request
ed to be present. Business meeting.
FRIDAY
MASQUERS —Meet at Library for the
Quad picture, at 12:00 m.
NOTICE!
All freshmen women, all other wo
men students newly registered for
re quired work in Physical Education,
and all students who failed to pass
the hygiene qui«tf last year are here
by notified to attend the first of the
series of Freshman Hygiene Lectures,
to be held in the Little Theater on
Wednesday, February 2, at 4:15 p.m.
(Signed) HELEN M. BUNTING,
Director Roble Gymnasium.
ANNOUNCEMENT!
Graduate students who are begin
ning advanced work with the expec
tation of securing the degrees of
Master of Arts or Engineer at the end
of the summer quarter, should file pre
liminary statements of candidacy at
the Registrar's Office before February
12th. Students expecting to complete
their undergraduate work in March
should file applications for the degree
of Bachelor of Arts before February
15th at the Registrar's Office.
