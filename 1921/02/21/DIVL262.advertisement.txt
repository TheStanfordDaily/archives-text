When May Students
Buy Jewelry?
For the best answer to the above question this firm
will award a cash prize of $25.00. Second and
third prizes of $10.00 and $10.00 respectively will
be awarded. ||
Contest is open to any student, regularly enrolled at Stanford.
Essays should not be longer than 200 words.
Awards will be made by three members of the faculty, Profes
sors Everett Smith, Miller McClintock, and Paul Clyde.
Contest closes February 28, 1921, and only one paper may be
submitted by an individual. There are no fees or restrictions
of any kind.
Winners of prizes must consent to the publication of their es
says in the Daily Palo Alto.
Th'e object of this contest is first, of course, to gain the atten
tion of the students to this firm—for advertising this business.
We do not, however, desire that the essays be written with a
view to furthering the jewelry business or the business of this
firpi. We want a straightforward answer to the question, re
gardless of our interests.
We want this information as a guide to our efforts in advertising
jewelry to Stanford students. If there are limitations, set by ✓
good taste, on the purchase of jewelry by students we want to
know it. If jewelry may be purchased by students, with prop
riety, to a greater extent than is generally recognized we want
to know that. What articles of jewelry may they properly
buy and when is the occasion appropriate?
Write your paper with these thoughts in mind and mail it to the
undersigned.
