# Contest Winners To Be Announced Tomorrow
## 
### 
Awards in the "When May College
Students Buy Jowelry" contest, in
augurated by Sumuel's Jewelers of
San Francisco, last quarter, will be
announced in tomorrow's paper. The
committee of the faculty acting as
judges consists of Professor Everett
W. Smith, Miller MeClintock, and Paul
Clyde.
The first-place winner will receive
twenty-five dollars in cash, the sec
ond-place winner will be given fifteen
dollars, and the third, ten dollurs. By
the rules of the contest, papers were
limited to 200 words, but due to the
number of entries, the judges have
had a great deal of difficulty in de
termining the three best papers.
