TUESDAY
PHI LAMBDA UPSILON—Meets at
7:15 at El Toro Club.
PHI ALPHA DELTA — Regular
monthly meeting and smoker at
Theta Chi House, 16 Alvarado.
MONTANA CLUB —Meets in the read
ing room of the Women's Clubhouse,
at 7:30. All Montana students are
asked to attend.
WEDNESDAY
CHRISTIAN SCIENCE SOCIETY—In
the Women's Club House, at 7:30
p.m. Testimonial meeting.
WOMEN'S CONFERENCE—WiII meet
at the Women's Clubhouse, at 7:15.
LA LlANA—Meets in the rest room of
the English Building, Wednesday
noon. Bring dues.
TEXAS CLUB—Meets tonight in the
Little Theater, at 7:30, All Lone
Star students should be present.
THURSDAY
WRANGLERS—Meet at 7:15 o'clock,
in the Women's Clubhouse.
SENIOR FARCE COMMITTEE—Meets
at 4 p.m. in room 207-B in the Eng
lish Building.
A SPECIAL EXAMINATION for stu
dents who are unable through sick
ness to take the final examination
in Citizenship 1, will be held Thurs
day, April 14, at 2 p.m., in Room 225.
NOTICE!
' Dr. Knowles, president of the Col
lege of the Pacific, who was to speak
here tomorrow for the Worlds Prob
lems Forum, has been taken suddenly
ill and will be unable to came. The
subject was to be "The Challenge of
the Third Internationale to Labor."
The lecture will probably be delivered
sometime in the future.
VACATION WORK
1 want to get in touch with a
couple of students who have had suc
cessful canvassing experience. There
will be an exceptional opportunity
this summer for a high-grade man.
Write me immediately. L. H. WHITE
MAN, Gen. Del. Palo Alto. tf
