# LABOR DAY SET FOR MAY 3; EXTENSIVE PROGRAM SCHEDULED
## 
### 
Stanford's second annual Labor Day
will be held on Tuesday, May 3. After
noon classes will be suspended on this
occasion to allow the students to clean
up the grounds about the old Stanford
residence, now the Children's Convales
cent Home.
In addition to an elaborate dinner, at
which a barbecue will be the main fea
ture, a varied program has been ar
ranged by those in charge. Softie num
bers on the entertainment list include
a skit by D. Evans and Art Harzfeld,
songs by the Carnival Quartette, a dance
by the women students, speeches by
Dean Culver and Dr. Gardner, and se
lections by the Stanford Band.
