# A 						Little Love Does a Lot
## 
### 
Lacking in any particular charm or
originality, "Mary" survived on Broad
way because it was a veritable speed
demon among musical comedies. If the
chorus couldn't think of anythin gelse to
do, they would simply jump up and
down. Anything to keep moving.
That speed kept the show alive—that
AND the "Love Nest." Like "Poor
Butterfly," the "Love Nest" has a beauty
in its melody that would make it popular
regardless of what words were put wit!)
it. That can be said of few modern
songs—but why heap praises on a num
ber already so sweetly popular that at
one time it threatened to be America's
unofficial national hymn?
