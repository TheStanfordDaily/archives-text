# SOPHOMORES WIN FIELD DAY
## Women Take Part in Contests in All Branches of Sport 
### 
The sophomores came first in the Wom
en's Field Day Saturday, scoring 335
points out of a possible 400, the juniors
second with 250, the seniors third with
225, and the freshmen last, having scored
160. The score was based on the places
made by the classes in various sports
during the day, 100 points being given
for first place, 75 for second, 50 for
third and 35 for fourth. The sopho
mores came first in tennis, archery, and
baseball, and fourth in swimming, the
juniors second in swimming and base
ball, and third in archery and tennis.
The seniors took first in swimming, sec
ond in tennis, and third in baseball, but
did not compete in archery. The fresh
men were second in archery, third in
swimming, fourth in tennis, and were not
represented in baseball.
The Field Day exercises began with
the final matches in the interclass tennis
tournament. Elsie Leicester, '21, won
from Marion Echols, '22, and Frances
Fish, '23, from Katherine Wheeler, '24,
in the semi-finals. Frances Fish, '23, was
then matched against Elsie Leicester for
the championship, and won after two
sets, characterized by hard, swift drives
and brilliant headwork on the part of
both contestants. The final score was
6-4, 6-4. This makes the second con
secutive year that she has won the cham
pionship for the class of '23.

easily the star of the meet, taking three
first palces, and making a new record of
36 1-5 in the 50-yard free style race.

The records made in this meet will be
sent to the national Women's Athletic
Association for comparison with those
of other colleges. This system of com
parison of records makes it possible to
have the equivalent of nation-wide com
petition between the women's athletic
teams.
After the swimming meet, luncheon
was served by the members of W .A. A.
at the Women's Clubhouse. Miss Helen
Masters Bunting, head of the Women's
Physical Education department, spoke on
the success of athletics and increase in
the number of women competing in the
different sports since the introduction of
the interclass system. Carey Bailard,
'22, newly elected president of the Wom
en's Athletic Association, was next to
speak. Individual silver cups were then
awarded to Margaret Younglove, '22.
Elizabeth Spillman, '22. Myra Water
man, '21, Marion Echols, '22, and
Frances Fish, '23, winners in the inter
class intercollegiate tennis tournament
with California last quarter, and block
letters and numerals were awarded to
the members of the all-star and class
teams in hockey, swimming, basketball,
and baseball, tennis and archery.
In the afternoon the archery contests
were played off, Helen Harris, '23, win
ning the tournament with 123 points,
Jeane Elmendorf, '24, coming second
with 95, Sarah McCann. '23, third with
92, and Julia Spears fourth with 88.
Each contestant shot ten rounds of four
arrows each, the winners hitting the tar
get 32 times out of a possible 40.
The new fields were dedicated after
the archery contest. Dr. Ray Lyman
Wilbur, who presented the fields to the
students on behalf of the University,
spoke of the way in which women's ath
letics have progressed, and outlined
plans that are being formulated which
will extend the scope of the women's
territory from the present gymnasium
to Lagunita, the main object of the plans
being the centralization of women's ac
tivities.
Hester Proctor, retiring president of
W. A. A., thanked the president on be
half of the association and students.
Miss Harriet Bradford then spoke on
the future possibilities in women's ath
letics at Stanford, and the things that
had been achieved thus far.
After the dedication, cups were
awarded to the representatives of the
championship teams in the different
sports by Marion Dwight, Field Day cap
tain. Helen Harris received the Archary
Cup for the sophomores, Carey Bailard
the Hockey Cup for the juniors, Frances
Fish the Tennis Cup for the sophomores,
Doris Stevenson the Basketball Cup for
the juniors, Esther Culver, representing
the sophomores, the Baseball Cup, and
Alvera West received the Swimming Cup
for the senior team.
During the awards the advanced
dancing class, in costume, presented a
classical dance, "Greek Maidens Playing
Ball."
In the final event the sophomores de
feated the juniors in the first game on
the new baseball diamond by a score of
16-2. The juniors, who were first in
the field, held the sophomores down to
four runs in the first inning, but on com
ing into the field a second time allowed
them to bring in twelve more men. The
sophomores struck out the first three
juniors that came to bat,*and only al
lowed two women to come home, and
four to make bases when the juniors
came up a second time.
