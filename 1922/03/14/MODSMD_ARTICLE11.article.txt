# Y.W.C.A. TO NAME OFFICERS THURSDAY
## 
### 
Election' of officers for the coming
year's Y. W. C. A. cabinct will lie made
at the Y. W. C. A. meeting Thursday
afternoon at 5 o'clock in the Women's
Clubhouse. Kathryn Donald. '22, chair
man of the Nominating Committee, an
nounced the following nominations:
President, Marion Potter, '22; vice
president, Helen Whitney, '24; secre
tary, Ethel Mohr, '24; treasurer, Lysle
Blythe, '23; undergraduate representa
tive. Pearl Bundiek, '23. Grace Stroebel,
'24, has been nominated for secretary.
Further nominations may* he made in
writing to Marjorie Woolscv, general
secretary of the Y. W. C. A., at the
Women's Clubhouse, before the meet
ing Thursday afternoon.
Miss Elizabeth Buckingham of the
English Department will give some
readings at the social part of the meet
ing which will follow the election of
officers. All members of Y. W. C. A.
are requested to be present.
