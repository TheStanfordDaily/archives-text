# Y.W.C.A. CABINET MEMBERS MEET FOR ANNUAL CONFERENCE
## 
### 
After two days of intensive work
and play, the Y. W. C. A. conference
of cabinet and advisory board repre
sentatives from six campuses closed
its* meeting Sunday morning at 10:45
in the Little Theatre. The conference
was held for the discussion of prob
lems to be brought up at the national
convention of the Y. W. C. A. at Hot
Springs, Arkansas, the later part of
April.
Eighty-five delegates, representing
the University of California, Mills,
Nevada, College of Pacific, San Jose
Normal, and the Stanford, California,
and Letterman hospitals iu San Fran
cisco, registered for the conference.
Margaret Brown, from Mills Col
lege, presided at the Friday evening
rieeting in the Women's Clubhouse.
The Reverend F. W. Morrison of the
San Jose Congregational Church lead
the devotional service, and Dean Mary
Yost welcomed the conference to
Stanford. Owing to the illness of Miss
Mary I. Bentley, Miss Clara Taylor
gave the main address of the even
ing. Miss Taylor outlined the work
rf the Y. W. C. A. in its fight for better
industrial conditions for women.
Saturday morning, Miss Ellen Smith
led a dTscussion on the financial poli
cy to be proposed at the national
Y. W. C. A.convention to be held at
Hos Springs, Ark., this month.
Pre-convention jforums were held
under Mrs. William Palmer Lucal, on
"The Basis for Membership in the As
sociation," Miss Ellen Smith led a dis
sociation," Miss Ellen Smith, "Fi
nance,' and Miss Clara Taylor, "In
dustrial Co-operation." Marion Pot
ter, '22, presided at the general dis
cussion following the forum hour. ,
Gertrude Mathews of the University
of California was toastmistress at the
field, two double plays being credited
to them.
Jim Lawson was the heaviest hitter of
the game, getting a double and two sin
gles in four trips to the plate. Johnston,
with a double and a single out of three
times at bat, and Aydelotte with two
singles in three trips to the plate, were
not far behind.
The two teams will meet in the second
game of the series at Berkeley next Sat
urday morning at 10 o'clock. Score of
Saturday's game:
