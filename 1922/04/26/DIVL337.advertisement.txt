invited to attend the meeting tomor
row night.

Cercle Francais to Hear
Professor Regis Michaud
Professor Regis Michaud of the de
partment of Romanic Languages at
the University of California, will ad
dress the members of the Cercle Fran
cais at their next meeting in the Wom
en's Clubhouse, Thursday, April 27,
at 8. His subject will be "La France
et Les Difficultes Actuelles."
Miss F. Badger, who is a vocal teach
er in San Francisco, will give several
vocal selections for the musical part
of the program. She is a pupil of
Mr. Chamberlain, a well-known teacher
of the bay district.
All those interested are cordially
invited to attend the meeting tomor
row night.
