# FULL PROGRAM FOR SENIOR WEEK WILL BEGIN WEDNESDAY
## DR. ALONZO TAYLOR WILL GIVE ADDRESS AT COMMENCEMENT 
### 
Dr. Alonzo E. Taylor will deliver
the Commencement Address to the
class of 1922, on the thirty-first Com
mencement Day, Monday, June 19, in
the Memorial Church. Dr. Taylor has
been at the head of the Stanford Food
Institute since its establishment here
almost a year ago.
Dr. Hugh Black, of the Union Theo
logical Seminary, New York, will de
liver the sermon on Baccalaureate
Sunday, June 18. Baccalaureate Ser
vice will be held in the Memorial
Church, at 11 o'clock.
Senior Week has its inception on
Wednesday evening with the Univer
sity and Club dinners, followed at
S: 15 o'clock by the Senior Farce in
the Assembly Hall. On Thursday the
picnic to Congress Springs will be
the last informal reunion of the class.
President Wilbur's reception to the
graduating class will be held at his
home at 4 o'clock Friday afternoon.
This will be the last opportunity for
the Seniors to meet the faculty as a
group.
Friday night will see the Senior
Ball holding forth in the sunken gar
dens of the New Union. Small tables
will be placed at intervals around the
gardens for the serving of supper.
The Women's Clubhouse will be lavish
ly decorated and used for dancing.
Saturday, June 17, has been set
aside as class day. The class day
exercises will begin at 10 o'clock in
the Memorial Church, with the reading
of the class will, history, and poem.
Molly Windish is the class poet,
Thomas Carskadon drew up the class
will, and Ross Fisher is the recorder
of the class history. Dan Goodwin will
deliver the class oration. The class
plate will be placed in the Quad at
this time. The exercises in the Church
finished, the class will make its pil
grimage to the Stanford tomb,
where the Memorial duty will be trans
ferred to the class of 1923, to hold
for one year.
At 4:30 the Phi Beta Kappa frater
nity will hold its open meeting in the
Little Theater, followed by a recep
tion, dinner, and initiation in the even
ing.
At 8 p.m., the second social event
of the week comes—the Senior Prome
nade. This will also be held in the
court of the Unio'n, with the same
background of Mission style as for the
ball. A Hawaiian orchestra has been
secured for the occasion, and the old
Union, and Women's Clubhouse will
be thrown open to the guests.
Sunday will be set aside as Bacca
laureate Sunday, with the sermon in
the Church at 11 o'clock, and a sacred
concert at 4 o'clock.
On Monday the class will complete
its final duties to the University, and
the University to the class of 1922, at
the commencement exercises in the
Memorial Church.
T. E. Green, chairman of the pro
gram committee, announces that invi
tations may be secured at the Alumni
secretary's office and urges that all
seniors secure theirs as soon as pos
sible. Souvenir programs, bound in
leather, have been ordered but will
not arrive until Saturday. They will
also be available at the Alumni Secre
tary's office. Class dues may be paid
at the time the invitations are se
cured.
