DRAMATIC CRITIC TO
ADDRESS JOURNALISTS
George C. Warren, dramatic critic
of the San Francisco Chronicle, will
address the Stanford chapter of the
American Journalists' Association Fri
day evening at the home of Professor
E. W. Smith, 11 Alvarado.
Warren, who has been connected
with the stage through journalism for
rainy years, will speak of his expeii
ences with plays and actors, as well
as of dramatic criticism.
The meeting will be open to the
campus as a whole, and will last from
7:15 to 8 o'clock.
Redwood Preservation
Will Be Lecture Topic
Joseph D. Grant, trustee of the Uni
versity, will deliver an illustrated lec
ture on "Saving the Redwoods" next
Wednesday afternoon at 4:15 at the
Little Theater.
Mr. Grant is chairman of the board
of directors of the "Save the Red
woods League." and has spoken on
this subject before the National Con
ference of State Parks. Dr. Wilbur
and Professor Charles B. Wing are
members of the council.
Motion pictures and colored slides
will be used to illustrate the talk.

DRAMATIC CRITIC TO
ADDRESS JOURNALISTS
George C. Warren, dramatic critic
of the San Francisco Chronicle, will
address the Stanford chapter of the
American Journalists' Association Fri
day evening at the home of Professor
E. W. Smith, 11 Alvarado.
Warren, who has been connected
with the stage through journalism for
rainy years, will speak of his expeii
ences with plays and actors, as well
as of dramatic criticism.
The meeting will be open to the
campus as a whole, and will last from
7:15 to 8 o'clock.
Redwood Preservation
Will Be Lecture Topic
Joseph D. Grant, trustee of the Uni
versity, will deliver an illustrated lec
ture on "Saving the Redwoods" next
Wednesday afternoon at 4:15 at the
Little Theater.
Mr. Grant is chairman of the board
of directors of the "Save the Red
woods League." and has spoken on
this subject before the National Con
ference of State Parks. Dr. Wilbur
and Professor Charles B. Wing are
members of the council.
Motion pictures and colored slides
will be used to illustrate the talk.
