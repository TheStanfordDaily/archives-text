VOL. XXIII. NO. 7 PUBLISHED EVERY FRIDAY November 12-18
SUNDAY, NOVEMBER 12
9:15 a.m. STUDENT VOLUNTEER CONFERENCE, in the Little Theatre.
10:00 a.m. BIBLE CLASSES AND DISCUSSION GROUPS, in the Memorial Church.
11:00 a.m. DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The Reverend D. Charles Gardner, D. D., Chaplain of the
University.
Anthem: "God So Loved the World" Stainer
Solo: "How Lovely Are Thy Dwellings" Liddle
Miss Ruth M. Garten
Choral: "Blessed Is He That Cometh" Gounod
(Immediately after this service there will be a celebration of the Lord's Supper.)
2:30 p.m. STUDENT VOLUNTEER CONFERENCE, in the Little Theatre.
4:00 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Dr. Latham True, acting University Organist, assisted by Martin D'Atidrea, tenor,
and Miss Elizabeth Peirce, violinist; in the Memorial Church.
Solemn Prelude, Op. 24 Barnes
Tenor Solo: Panis Angelicus Franck
(with violin obligato)
Three Folk-Melodies:
Farewell to Cucullain Irish
Netherland Folksong Dutch
Deep River Negro
Violin Solo: Morgen Strauss
Finale (Suite in D, Op. 25) Barnes
MONDAY, NOVEMBER 13
8:00 p.m. FACULTY WOMEN'S CLUB, in the Women's Club House. Program by Mrs. Lawrence B. Wallis.
7:45 p.m. COSMOPOLITAN CLUB, in the Women's Club House. All interested are invited to attend.
TUESDAY, NOVEMBER 14
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Dr. True; in the Memorial Church. Sunday's program will be repeated.
7:30 p.m. DICKENS PROGRAM, by Mr. Frank C. Thompson, Impersonator; in the Little Theatre.
WEDNESDAY, NOVEMBER 15
3:00 p.m. RESIDENT ALUMNAE TEA, in the Women's Club House. Hostesses: Mrs. M. R. Kirkwood, Mrs. J. W.
Bingham, Mrs. A. M. Cathcart, Mrs. C. B. Whittier, Mrs. C. G. Vernier, Mrs. W. B. Owens, Mrs. C. A.
Huston.
7:30 p.m. EUPHRONIA DEBATING SOCIETY, in Room 12, Law Building.
THURSDAY, NOVEMBER 16
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Dr. True; in the Memorial Church.
Prelude to Lohengrin Wagner
Walter's Prize Song Wagner
Symphonic Poem: Les Preludes Liszt
7:30 p.m. NESTORIA DEBATING SOCIETY, in Room 12, Law Building.
SATURDAY, NOVEMBER 18
8:00 p.m. ENTERTAINMENT, by Members of the Chinese Club, for the benefit of the Second Million, Stanford
Endowment Fund, in the Assembly Hall.
OFFICIAL NOTICES

MEMORIAL CHURCH
University Preacher:
November 12 —The Reverend D. Charles Gardner, D. D.,
Chaplain of the University.
Morning Chapel—7:so a.m., Monday to Friday, inclusive.
Half Hour of Music: Sunday, 4:00 p.m.; Tuesday and
Thursday, 4:15 p. m.
TUESDAY EVENING MEETINGS
November 14 —Mr. Frank C. Thompson, Impersonator,
"Dickens Program."
November 21 —Dr. H. D'Arcy Power, of San Francisco, a
lecture on "Six Months in Germany."
November 2S —Professor Franck L. Schoell, a lecture on
"The French Academy in the 20th
Century."

THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Special Exhibitions, October 15 to November 13. Cali
fornia Landscapes, painted in oils by J. Edward Walker.
English Landscapes, in water-colors and etchings, by
William Henry Borrow. Oil paintings by Theodore
Wores, November 5 to 26.
The Art Gallery is open to the public, without charge,
during the following hours: 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.,
daily, including Sunday.
DEAN OF WOMEN
Miss Yost will be at home, informally, from 4 until 6,
Thursday afternoon, at her residence, 2 Lasuen Street,
Campus.
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the WEEKLY CALENDAR,
