# DANCE WILL FOLLOW FOOTBALL FROTHIES
## 
### 
After the tables are cleared from the
floor to admit a larger crowd, the "Big
Game" dinner dance will be opened
to the general public at 10:30 in the
Pavilion, November 25. Thus it will
accommodate those who wish to come
after the Ram's Head show.
Whitmer's eight-piece orchestra has
been secured for the entire evening,
and admission to the later dance will
be $1.
Special entertainment is arranged
for the intermissions at the dinner
dance, which commences at 7:30. Res
ervations can still be made by apply
ing to J. E. MacDowell, alumni secre
tary. The charge is $2.25 per plate.
