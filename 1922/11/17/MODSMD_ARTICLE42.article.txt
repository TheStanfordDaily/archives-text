7:30 p.m. LECTURE on "Six Months in Germany," by Dr. H. D'Arcy Power, of San Francisco; in the Little Theatre.
8:00 p.m. CLASSICAL CLUB, in the Women's Clubhouse. Address on "Modern Greece and the Near Eastern
Question," by Professor R. H. Lutz, of the Department of History.
WEDNESDAY, NOVEMBER 22
3:00 p.m. RESIDENT ALUMNAE TEA, in the Women's Clubhouse. Hostesses: Mrs. John Lee, Mrs. E. C. Hughes,
Mrs. M. J. Kennedy, Mrs. H. B. Fisher, Mrs. J. A. Dunker, Mrs. A. T. De Forest, Mrs. D. H. Arnold.

Theatre. Tickets may be obtained at Cran dall's in Palo Alto and Alumni Secretary's office. Adminis
tration building.

California," by Professor E. C. Starks.
8:00 p.m. CIRCLE FRANCAIS, in the Delta Delta Delta House.
THURSDAY, NOVEMBER 23

7:30 p.m. FOOTBALL RALLY, in the Assembly Hall.
FRIDAY, NOVEMBER 24
1:45 p.m. BOARD OF TRUSTEES. Regular Monthly Meeting, at the Offices of the Board, Balfour Building, San
Francisco.
8:00 p.m. INTERSOCIETY-INTERCOLLEGIATE DEBATE, in the Little Theatre. Euphronia Debating Society of
Stanford vs. Senate Debating Society of the University of California. Subject: Resolved, That the
public schools should be placed under the jurisdiction of the national government. The public is
welcome.

2:30 p.m. AMERICAN INTERCOLLEGIATE FOOTBALL, in the Stanford Stadium. Stanford vs. California.
8:15 p.m. FOOTBALL SHOW, under the direction of Ram's Head; in the Assembly Hall.

7:30 p.m. LECTURE on "Six Months in Germany," by Dr. H. D'Arcy Power, of San Francisco; in the Little Theatre.
8:00 p.m. CLASSICAL CLUB, in the Women's Clubhouse. Address on "Modern Greece and the Near Eastern
Question," by Professor R. H. Lutz, of the Department of History.
WEDNESDAY, NOVEMBER 22
3:00 p.m. RESIDENT ALUMNAE TEA, in the Women's Clubhouse. Hostesses: Mrs. John Lee, Mrs. E. C. Hughes,
Mrs. M. J. Kennedy, Mrs. H. B. Fisher, Mrs. J. A. Dunker, Mrs. A. T. De Forest, Mrs. D. H. Arnold.
7:30 p.m. EUPHRONIA DEBATING SOCIETY, in Room 12, Law Building.
7:30 p.m. LECTURE on "Lloyd George, His Present Position in England," Cowper Powys; in the Little
Theatre. Tickets may be obtained at Cran dall's in Palo Alto and Alumni Secretary's office. Adminis
tration building.
8:00 p.m. SCIENCE ASSOCIATION, in the Faculty Club House. Illustrated address on"The Marine Mammals of
California," by Professor E. C. Starks.
8:00 p.m. CIRCLE FRANCAIS, in the Delta Delta Delta House.
THURSDAY, NOVEMBER 23
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Dr. True, assisted by Miss Elizabeth Peirce, violinist; in the Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m. FOOTBALL RALLY, in the Assembly Hall.
FRIDAY, NOVEMBER 24
1:45 p.m. BOARD OF TRUSTEES. Regular Monthly Meeting, at the Offices of the Board, Balfour Building, San
Francisco.
8:00 p.m. INTERSOCIETY-INTERCOLLEGIATE DEBATE, in the Little Theatre. Euphronia Debating Society of
Stanford vs. Senate Debating Society of the University of California. Subject: Resolved, That the
public schools should be placed under the jurisdiction of the national government. The public is
welcome.
SATURDAY, NOVEMBER 25
2:30 p.m. AMERICAN INTERCOLLEGIATE FOOTBALL, in the Stanford Stadium. Stanford vs. California.
8:15 p.m. FOOTBALL SHOW, under the direction of Ram's Head; in the Assembly Hall.
