# BARBARIANS WIN 1-0 IN UNIVERSITY AND CLUB SOCCER LEAGUE
## 
### 
The seventh game of the University
and Club Soccer League proved to be
unlucky for the San Francisco Vikings
as they lost to the Barbarians last
Saturday, 1-0.
Both the Barbs and the Vikings, up
until this game, were tied for second
honors in the league, each team losing
a game to the varsity. The fight for
the championship during the second
half of the season will be between
Stanford and the Barbarians.
According to those who saw the
game, the Vikings outplayed the Barbs
in every department. The winning
score for the Barbarians came just a
few minutes before the final whistle.
It was the result of a quick succession
of short passes which momentarily
caught the Viking defense off its
guard.
Palo Alto's Legion soccer team near
ly hung up a league record when they
defeated the Italia Virtus Club, 7-0,
on the field back of the Community
House last Sunday afternoon.
The Olympic Club had practically
everything its owu way against the
Sons of St. George and won 2-0.
Stanford and California are now the
only teams that have not completed
the first half of the schedule for the
season.
The standing of the teams at pres
ent is as follows:
Team — P W L T Pts.
Stanford 6 6 0 0 12
Barbarians 7 6 1 0 12
Vikings 7 5 2 0 10
Olympic Club 7 4 2 1 9
Legion 7 4 3 0 8
California 6 2 3 1 4
Italia Virtus 7 0 6 1 1
Sons of St. George 7 0 7 0 0
