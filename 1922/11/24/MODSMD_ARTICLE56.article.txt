AMERICAN HISTORICAL ASSOCIATION:
PACIFIC COAST BRANCH
Eighteenth Annual Meeting, December 1 and 2, 1922,
Stanford University, Room 231, History Building.
Program
General Session, Friday, December Ist, 2:30 p. m.
President Payson J. Treat, Presiding.
1. The Newer View of Erasmus, by Henry Stephen
Lucas, University of Washington.
2. The Causes of the War of 1812, by Edward L.
Beach. Stanford University.
3. Mahomet et Charlemagne, by Henri Pirenne, Uni
versity of Ghent.
Annual Dinner, Friday. December Ist, 7:00 p. m., at the
Stanford Union. Professor E. D. Adams, Presiding.
The President's Annual Address.
General Session. Saturday, December-2d. 9:30 a. m.
1. The Beginnings of the Impressment Issue, by Sam
uel F. Bemis, Whitman College.
2. The American Fur Trade in the Far Southwest, by
J. J. Hill, Bancroft Library, University of Cali
fornia.
3. The Need of a State Historical Society in Cali
fornia, by Louis J. Paetow, University of Cali
fornia.
4. The Colonization of Utah in 1847, by Levi E. Young,
University of Utah.
5. Business Session.
Teachers' Session, Saturday, December 2d, 2:00 p. m.
The Field and Function of History.
1. Franklin C. Palm, University of California.
2. Edward M. Hulme, Stanford University.
3. Margaret Bennett. Berkeley High School.
4. General Discussion.
