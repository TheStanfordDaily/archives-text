# More Than 500 Students At Sophomore Cotillion
## 
### 
More than five'hundred students at
tended the 1925 sophomore cotillion,
which was held in the basketball
pavilion Friday night. Every one of the
250 tickets was sold, and according to
John Hall, chairman of the committee,
the cotillion was a financial success.
The general scheme of decorations
represented a garden. White-washed
lattice fences surrounded the dancing
space, and the tables were placed be
hind this fencing. Lights with shades
to represent stars were strung from
side to side, and branches along the
balconies and fence carried out the
plan. The dome lights under the bal
conies were covered with lavender
crepe paper with the numerals '25
cut in.
