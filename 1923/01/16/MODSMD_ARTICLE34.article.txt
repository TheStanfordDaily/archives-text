# CAPPER PRESIDENTIAL BOOM IS PUNCTURED
## 
### (By United Press 
WASHINGTON, Jan. 16.—The boom
to make Senator Capper of Kansas,
farmer and progressive a presidential
candidate in 1924 was punctured today
by Capper himself.
"I'd like to see a farmer from the
middle west President of the United
States," he told the United Press, in an
interview; "I think it would be a good
thing for the country—but not for me.
I've got a man-sized job here in the sen
ate for the next few years. I am going
to devote my political life to working out
a permanent solution of the farmers' ills.
