# EDUCATION CLUB WILL DISCUSS JOHN DEWEY
## 
### 
Professor Harold Chapman Brown of
the Philosophy department will discuss
"The Educational Influence of John
Dewey" at a regular meeting of the
Education Club this evening at 7 :30 in
Room 50 of the Education building.
Professor Brown is a student of John
Dewey, and an authority on educational
philosophy. He is one of the thirty con
temporary writers who contributed to
the work entitled "American Ciliviza
tion."
John Dewey is professor of philosophy
at Columbia University, and a member
of the National Academy of Sciences.
He is best known as the author of sev
eral books, including "How to Think,"
"German Philosophy and Ethics," and
"Democracy and Education."
All students who are interested are
invited to attend the meeting.
