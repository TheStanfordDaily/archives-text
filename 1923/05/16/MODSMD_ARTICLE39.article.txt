# Recreational Chemistry Will Be the Subject of Education Club Lecture
## 
### 
"Recreational Chemistry" will be the
subject of a speech by William L. Bur
dick, a graduate student in chemistry,
at a meeting of the Education Club to
night in Room 50 of the Education build
ing at 7 :30.
Burdick, who will receive the degree
of Doctor of Philosophy in June, will
translate some of the technicalities of
chemistry into a popular style, so that
the person with an average education
can understand them.
Burdick came to Stanford to take his
Ph. D. in chemistry. He formerly taught
high school in Massachusetts, Kansas,
and California.
Anyone who is interested in the sub
ject is invited to attend the meeting.
