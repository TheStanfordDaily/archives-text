The following oral examinations for the Ph.D. degree
have been scheduled, to take place in the Trophy Room
of the Old Union. Persons not members of the faculty
who wish to attend will ask permission of the acting
chairman, Dr. J. s. P. Tattbck.
August. 21, 3 p. m. —Miss M. A. Merrill: Major, Psy
chology; Minor, Education. Examining committee, all
professors in the Departments of Anatomy, Bacteriology,
Botany, Education, Food Research, Philosophy, Physi
ology, Psychology, Zoology.
August 23, 3 p. m. —Miss E. B. Sullivan: Major, Psy
chology; Minor. Education. Examining committee, all
professors in the Departments of Anatomy, Bacteriology,

' Botany. Education. Food Research, Philosophy, Physi
ology, Psychology, Zoology.
August 28, 3 p. m.— Mr. Mervyn Crobaugh: Major,
Economics; Minor, Philosophy. Examining committee,
all professors in the Departments of Economics, Educa
tion, History, Philosophy, Political Science.
August 30. 3 p. m. —Mr. John T). Cooke: Major, Eng
lish; Minor, Classical Literature. Examining committee,
all professors in all ancient and modern language depart
ments and in Education and Philosophy.
August 31, 0 a. m. —Mr. E. H. Brunquist: Major. Physi
ology. Examining committee, all professors in the De
partments of Anatomy, Bacteriology, Botany, Education,
Food Research, Philosophy, Psychology, Zoology.
August 31, 3 p. m.—Mr. L. I). Elliott: Major, Chem
istry; Minor, Biological Chemistry. Examining com
mittee, all professors in the Departments of Anatomy,
Bacteriology, Botany, Chemistry, Food Research,
Physics, Physiology, Psychology, Zoology.
END QUARTER EXAMINATIONS
Thursday, August 30, 8:00 a.m., to Saturday. Septem
ber 1, 6:00 p.m.
