# GIANTS RALLY IN NINTH AND TAKE FIRST GAME, 5-4
## 
### 
(Special Dispatch)
NEW YORK, Oct. 10. —Coming into
the last inning with a tie score, the
New York Giants today won the first
game of the World Series from the
Yanks, 5-4. Hoyt, Bush and Schang
performed for the Yankees, while Wat
son and Ryan pitched and Gowdy
caught for the Giants.
At the end of the eighth inning the
score was four-all. It appeared at this
stage of the game that many extra in
nings were yet to come, as both pitch
ers were in great shape. However, Mc-
Graw did not leave the outcome of
the game in doubt very long, as he
sent his men back to the contests
with the instructions to hit. The net
result was one run, and victory.
The box score:
R. H. E.
Giants 0 0 4 0 0 0 0 0 I—s 8 0
Yanks 1 2 0 0 0 0 1 0 12 1
Batteries —Nationals: Watson, Ryan,
and Gowdy. Americans: Hoyt, Bush,
and Schang.
