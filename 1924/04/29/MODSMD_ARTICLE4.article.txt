# SCHOOL JOURNALISTS IN CONVENTION HERE
## 
### 
The first state-wide convention of
high school journalists will be held
next Friday and Saturday under the
auspices of the Stanford chapter of
Sigma Delta Chi. Over 175 delegates
will be present, representing fifty-five
high schools in all parts of California.
Carl Shoup, President of Sigma
Delta Chi, has arranged the following
program for the two days of the con
vention:
8:30 to 11 a.m. Arrival and registra
tion of delegates, Stanford Uni
versity Administration
A. R. Boone, *23, chairman Reg
istration Committee.
11:00 a.m. Student Assembly in the
Stanford University Assembly
Hall. Al Masters in charge.
Speakers: Governor Friend W.
Richardson, President Ray Ly
man Wilbur, James Swinnerton,
cartoonist and journalist (crea
tor of "Little Jimmy").
12:00 m. Luncheon at fraternity
houses.
1:00 to 1:30 p.m. Opening session of
Convention in the Stanford Lit
tle Theatre. Appointment of
committees.
1:30 to 4 p.m. Round table discussion
groups meet in seminar rooms,
Stanford University Library.
Talks by professional journal
ists and newspaper publishers.
A tour of the Stanford Press will
also be made.
4:00 p.m. Convention Picture.
4:00 to 5:30 p.m. Committee meetings
7:30 p.m. Convention banquet, held
in the Stanford Union. Speak
ers, Chancellor Emeritus David
Starr Jordan, President Ray Ly
man Wilbur, Charles K. Field,
editor Sunset Magazine, Profes
sor E. W. Smith, Professor of
Journalism, Stanford Univer
sity.
Saturday, May 3
9:00 to 9:30 a.m. General session of
the Convention, Stanford Little
Theatre. Committee reports,
9:30 to 12 m. Round table discussion
etc.
groups continued. seminar
rooms, Stanford University Li
brary.
12:00 to 2 p.m. Convention barbecue
under auspices of Stanford Ath
letic Board of Control and the
Associated Students.
2:00 to 2:30 p.m. Organ Recital in
Memorial Church. Warren D.
Allen, University Organist.
2:30 to 3:30 p.m. Last general session
in Little Theatre. Awarding of
prizes.
