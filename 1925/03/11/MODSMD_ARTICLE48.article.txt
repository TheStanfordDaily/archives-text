WEDNESDAY
i'HE BAHAI SOCIETY will hold its
regular meeting this evening at 7:15
in the Women's Clubhouse. The sub
ject for the discussion will be
"Mashrak-el-Azkar," the Bahai tem
ple now being erected in Chicago.
THE FRENCH CLUB will hold an im
portant meeting in the Women's
Clubhouse at 8 o'clock. All members
urged to be present.
WRANGLERS will meet at 7:15 in
Women's Clubhouse for short busi
ness meeting.
FRENCH CLUB meets at 7:30 in the
Women's Clubhouse.
COSMOPOLITAN CLUB meets tonight
• at 7:30 at the Chinese Club. Pro
gram of Chinese music and lantern
slides. Cabinet meets Monday,
March 11, at 4:30 in Women's Club
house.
OPEN FORUM in lobby of Old Union
at 7:30 o'clock on question, "Should
fraternities and sororities be abol
lished from the Stanford campus?"
Dean Yost will speak.
THURSDAY
EUPHRONIA BANQUET in Union at
6 o'clock. Election of officers. Tick
ets can be obtained from W. L. Goss
lin, G. N. Crocker, H. W. Peterson,
and J. A. Leiter.
SPANISH CLUB will hold the last
meeting of the quarter at 8 o'clock
on the third floor of the Women's
Clubhouse.
USHERS report Thursday evening for
Peninsular Concert.
SUNDAY
STANFORD EPISCOPAL CLUB will
meet at 6 p.m. in the parish house.
Fifty tons of soot a year fall on each
square mile of Atlanta, Ga.
