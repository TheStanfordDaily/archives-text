# Japanese Cherry Trees Presented to Arboretum
## 
### 
A collection of twenty-two varieties
of Japanese flowering cherry trees
have been given to the arboretum by
W. F. Wight,who is in charge of the
plant breeding station of the United
States Department of Agriculture on
the campus.
Duplicates of this collection, which
is the finest in America, are growing
in the Arnold arboretum of Harvard
University, and in Hyland Park,
Rochester, New York.
The cherry trees are young plants
and will be kept in the University
nursery for one or (%o years before
being planted in the arboretum.
