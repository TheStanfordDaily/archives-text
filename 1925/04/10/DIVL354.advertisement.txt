SUNDAY, APRIL 12
(EASTER DAY)
S:00 a.m. HOLY COMMUNION, in the Memorial Church.

(Organ recitals will not be given this week, since the organ is undergoing reconstruction).
MONDAY, APRIL 13
3:00 p.m. FACULTY WOMEN'S CLUB, in the Women's Clubhouse. Social meeting.
7:30 p.m. LEAGUE OF NATIONS NON-PARTISAN ASSOCIATION, STANFORD BRANCH, in the Little Theatre.
Outline of work to be done this quarter. The public is invited to attend.

4:30 p.m. GRADUATE WOMEN'S CLUB, in the Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m. ILLUSTRATED LECTURE on "Modern Turkey," by Mr. Eliot G. Mears, Lecturer in the Department
of Economics.
WEDNESDAY, APRIL 15
1:00 p.m. ANNUAL LABOR DAY, at the Stanford Home for Convalescent Children.

Committee in charge: Mrs. R. B. Kellogg, Mrs. Gertrude Huff, Mrs. George Whisler, Mrs. J. C. Silliman,

Blair.

bly Hall. General question: "French Policy in Asia and Africa" (specific question to be announced
at the debate). Speakers: California, Harold F. Cherniss, Raymond G. Stanbury, and Bernard E.
Witkin; Stanford, John C. Duniway, Victor Hunt Harding, and Robert E. Lewis. Mr. Chester H.
Rowell will preside.

ford Glee Club; in the Assembly Hall. Admission, 75 cents and $1.

S:00 a.m. HOLY COMMUNION, in the Memorial Church.
11:00 a.m. EASTER SERVICE. Preacher: The Reverend D. Charles Gardner, D.D., Chaplain of the University.
Solo: "Hosanna!" Granier
Mrs. Esther Houk Allen, Contralto
Choruses from "The Messiah" Handel
1. "Since by Man Came Death"
2. "By Man Came also the Resurrection of the Dead"
3. "For as in Adam All Die"
4. "Even in Christ so Shall All Be Made Alive"
12:15 p.m. HOLY COMMUNION.
(Organ recitals will not be given this week, since the organ is undergoing reconstruction).
MONDAY, APRIL 13
3:00 p.m. FACULTY WOMEN'S CLUB, in the Women's Clubhouse. Social meeting.
7:30 p.m. LEAGUE OF NATIONS NON-PARTISAN ASSOCIATION, STANFORD BRANCH, in the Little Theatre.
Outline of work to be done this quarter. The public is invited to attend.
TUESDAY, APRIL 14
4:30 p.m. GRADUATE WOMEN'S CLUB, in the Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m. ILLUSTRATED LECTURE on "Modern Turkey," by Mr. Eliot G. Mears, Lecturer in the Department
of Economics.
WEDNESDAY, APRIL 15
1:00 p.m. ANNUAL LABOR DAY, at the Stanford Home for Convalescent Children.
3:00 p.m. RESIDENT ALUMNAE TEA, in the Women's Clubhouse. Hostesses: Chapters T, DC, and DD of P. E. O.
Committee in charge: Mrs. R. B. Kellogg, Mrs. Gertrude Huff, Mrs. George Whisler, Mrs. J. C. Silliman,
Mrs. Robert Templeton, Mrs. R. F. Meredith, Mrs. George Mercer, Mrs. Jessie Simpson, Miss Emma
Blair.
8:00 p.m. ANNUAL JOFFRE MEDAL DEBATE between the University of California and Stanford; in the Assem
bly Hall. General question: "French Policy in Asia and Africa" (specific question to be announced
at the debate). Speakers: California, Harold F. Cherniss, Raymond G. Stanbury, and Bernard E.
Witkin; Stanford, John C. Duniway, Victor Hunt Harding, and Robert E. Lewis. Mr. Chester H.
Rowell will preside.
THURSDAY, APRIL 16
7:00 p.m. RALLY, at the Lake.

Lectures and Others."
7:30 p.m. GERMAN CLUB, in the Women' 3 Clubhouse.

of the University, Room 165, Administration Building.
8:15 p.m. EXCHANGE CONCERT by the Glee Club of the University of California, under the auspices of the Stan
ford Glee Club; in the Assembly Hall. Admission, 75 cents and $1.
