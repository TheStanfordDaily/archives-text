# "Dirigibles" Subject of Colonel Blee Tomorrow
## 
### 
"The Development of Balloons and
Dirigibles and Their Place in Commer
cial Aeronautics" will be discussed by
H. H. Plee, '10, tomorrow afternoon at
4 o'clock in the Little Theatre. Blee,
who is lieutenant-colonel in the Air
Service Reserve, holds an internation
al license as spherical balloon pilot and
dirigible pilot. He has also senior mili
tary ratings of balloon and airship
pilot.
A resume of aerial photography and
its application to engineering work
will be included in the lecture, which
will be of general rather than techni
cal interest. Lantern slides will be
used to illustrate the talk, to which
the general public Is invited.
