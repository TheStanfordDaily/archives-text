# COMMENCEMENT WEEK WILL OPEN WITH SENIOR FARCE
## BALL TO BE FINAL EVENT President Wilbur Addresses Graduates in Memorial Church, June 22 
### Hulda Hoover 
Commencement Week starts this
year on Wednesday, June 17, with the
Senior Farce, and ends with the Senior
Ball 011 Monday night, June 22. Final
arrangements for the week have been
completed by James Dickey, president
of the 1925 Class.
The Memorial Church will again be
the scene of the graduating exercises,
which will be held on June 22. Presi
dent Ray Lyman Wilbur will address
the seniors on Commencement Day.
Friday is Class Day, and at this
time the Class Will is •to be read,
along with the Class History and Poem
and other graduating activities. The
Class Plate will be laid at 11 o'clock
by R. J. White.
The Promenade this year will be
held in the Inner Quadrangle of the
Union, which will be laid out with a
Spanish setting. Singing and other
musical numbers will make up the
entertainment for the first part of the
evening. A dance has been planned for
the later hours.
The complete program Is as follows:
Wednesday, June 17
8:15 p.m.—SENIOR FARCE, "Polly
With a Past," by George Mid
dleton and Guy Bolton; in the
Assembly hall.
Thursday, June 18
7:00 p.m.—DINNER TO SENIORS by j
the Executive' Committee of i
the Alumni Association; in
the Union.
9:00 p.m.—SENIOR PROMENADE in
the Inner Quadrangle of the
Union.
Friday, June 19
9:00 a.m.—MEETING OF THE ACA
DEMIC COUNCIL, in Room
12.
10 a.m.—CLASS DAY EXERCISES, in
the Memorial Church.
11:00 a.m.—DEDICATION OF THE
1925 CLASS PLATE, in the
Inner Quad.
4:00 p.m.—PRESIDENT AND MRS.
WILBUR'S RECEPTION, at
the President's House.
Saturday, June 20
4:30 p.m.—ANNUAL OPEN MEET-!
ING OF PHI BETA KAPPA,
in the Little Theatre.
6:30 p.m.—PHI BETA KAPPA LUN-j
CHEON, DINNER, AND INI
TIATION, in the Union.

Jones, Professor of Philosophy

Commencement Week starts this
year on Wednesday, June 17, with the
Senior Farce, and ends with the Senior
Ball 011 Monday night, June 22. Final
arrangements for the week have been
completed by James Dickey, president
of the 1925 Class.
The Memorial Church will again be
the scene of the graduating exercises,
which will be held on June 22. Presi
dent Ray Lyman Wilbur will address
the seniors on Commencement Day.
Friday is Class Day, and at this
time the Class Will is •to be read,
along with the Class History and Poem
and other graduating activities. The
Class Plate will be laid at 11 o'clock
by R. J. White.
The Promenade this year will be
held in the Inner Quadrangle of the
Union, which will be laid out with a
Spanish setting. Singing and other
musical numbers will make up the
entertainment for the first part of the
evening. A dance has been planned for
the later hours.
