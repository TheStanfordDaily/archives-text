Stanford University Weekly Calendar
VOLUME XXXIII. No. 9 PUBLISHED EVERY FRIDAY May 31-June 5
SUNDAY, MAY 31
11:00 a.m. MEMORIAL DAY SERVICE. Preacher: The Reverend Robert E. Brown, of Oakland.
Solo: "Battle Hymn of the Republic"
Soloist, Mrs. Esther H. Allen
Anthem: "Recessional" Tertius Noble
4:00 p.m. SACRED CONCERT, by the University Choir, Mrs. Violet Cowger and Mrs. Esther H. Allen, Soloists,
and Mr. Warren D. Allen, Organist; in the Memorial Church.
TUESDAY, JUNE 2
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Allen; in the Memorial Church.
4:30 p.m. GRADUATE WOMEN'S CLUB, in the Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m. LECTURE on "The Economics of Morality," by Dr. Sartell Prentice, founder of The Nyack Movement;
in the Assembly Hall.
WEDNESDAY, JUNE 3
3:00 p.m. RESIDENT ALUMNAE TEA, in the Women's Clubhouse. Hostesses: Mrs. A. E. Roth, Mrs. J. T.
Nourse, Mrs. George B. Culver, Mrs. Clifford Allen, Mrs. Andrew Kerr, Mrs. E. F. Roth, Mrs. W. B.
Owens.
4:15 p.m. LECTURE on "Building the Good Will Necessary to World Peace," by Mr. Frederick J. Libby, Executive
Secretary, National Council for Prevention of War; in the Little Theatre.
7:30 p.m. OPEN FORUM, sponsored by Euphronia Debating Society, in the Lobby of the Old Union. Question:
"What Part Should the Y. M. and Y. W. C. A. Play in Stanford Life?" Announcement of plans for
next year. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. RESEARCH CLUB, in the Faculty Clubhouse. Papers: "Eye Movements from Photographic Records,"
by Professor W. R. Miles; "The Nature of Speech," by Professor Frederick Anderson.
8:00 p.m. FRENCH CLUB, at the home of Professor W. L. Schwartz, 450 Harvard St., College Terrace. Pro
fessor Schwartz will speak on literary reminiscences of a trip to France.
THURSDAY, JUNE 4
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Allen; in the Memorial Church.
4:15 p.m. LECTURE on "Sheridan; His Plays and Place in the Theatre," by Mr. Clayton Hamilton, author and
editor; in the Little Theatre.
6:00 p.m. EUPHRONIA BANQUET, in the Stanford Union. Tickets may be secured from W. L. Gosslin, G. N.
Crocker, H. W. Peterson, and J. A. Leiter.
7:30 p.m. THURSDAY EVENING CONFERENCE, at the home of Dr. David Starr Jordan. Subject: "My Experi
ence as an M.D."
8:15 p.m. OPEN MEETING OF DRAMATIC INTERPRETATION, English 19B; in the Little Theatre. Presentation
of one-act plays, "He Said and She Said," directed by Aileen Burks; "The Flattering Word," directed
by Lida Rittler. The public is invited to attend.
FRIDAY, JUNE 5
8:00 p.m. FACULTY CLUB, in the Faculty Clubhouse.
