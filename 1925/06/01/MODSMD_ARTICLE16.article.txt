# STANFORD Y. M. C. A. WORK SUBJECT OF FORUM WEDNESDAY
## 
### 
"What is the place of the Y. M. C. A.
and Y. W. C. A. on the Stanford cam
pus?" is the subject of the open forum to
be sponsored Wednesday at 7:30 in the
Ivobbv of the Old Union by the Eu
phronia Society.
As a basis for discussion, representa
tives of the two organizations will give
five-minute talks on the present work of
the associations on the campus and their
plans for the future. H. S. Laughlin,
Ann Fitzhugh, R. W. Taylor, and Esther
Greenacre will speak in the order named.
Following these talks, the meeting will
be opened for discussion of the question.
Questions are invited from the floor and
it is hoped that opposition and support
of the organizations will be brought out'
in the discussion.
All students interested in the work of
the Y. M. C. A. and all those who want
to know what the Y. M. C. A. is doing on
the Stanford campus are invited to take
part in the forum Wednesday.
This will be the last of the series of five
forums being conducted by Euphronia
this quarter.
