VOLUME XXXIV. No. 8 PUBLISHED EVERY THURSDAY August 16-21 :
SUNDAY, AUGUST 16
11:00 a.m. DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The Reverend Arthur Hillier Beaty, of Cody, Wyoming.
(Reverend Beaty will preach August 23, as well.)
Baritone Solo: "The King of Love" Gounod
Mr. Albert Gregersen
Anthem: "Sing to the Lord" Tye
4:00 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Warren D. Allen, University Organist; in the Memorial Church.
Allegro from the Fifth Symphony C. M. Widor
Fantasie in D flat, op. 101 Camille Saint-Saens
Funeral March and Seraphic Chant Alexander Guilmant
Adagio from the Suite in Ancient Style Georges Enesco
Finale from the Suite in D major.... Edward Shippen Barnes
. TUESDAY, AUGUST 18
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Allen, in the Church.
8:00 p.m. LECTURE on "The Gullah Negro Dialect: 'The Worst English in the World'," by Professor Reed Smith,
Visiting Professor in English from the University of South Carolina; in the Little Theatre. (The lec
ture deals with the whimsicalities of race, language, and tradition of a little-known people of the
Carolina coast.)
THURSDAY, AUGUST 20
4:16 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Allen, in the Church.
4:15 p.m. LECTURE on "The Evolution of Nations," by Dr. David Starr Jordan; in the Assembly Hall. (Last
of a series of lectures on "Evolution.")
7:30 p.m. THURSDAY EVENING CONFERENCE, at the home of Dr. David Starr Jordan. Subject: "Is War j
Eternal?"
8:15 p.m. OPEN MEETING OF ENGLISH (Public Speaking) 18a. "The Goal," by Henry Arthur Jones (directed by
Edmund Spellacy) and "Smarty's Party" (directed by Hildegarde Tolman and Mabel Marsh) will be
presented; in the Little Theatre. Open to the public.
FRIDAY, AUGUST 21
7:30 p.m. OPEN MEETING OF PUBLIC SPEAKING 152, in the Little Theatre, Question for debate: "Should the
government legalize the sale of light winos and beer?" Open to the public.
OFFICIAL NOTICES
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY during the following hours: 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.,
Exhibition of Wood Blocks and Etchings, August 1- daily, including Sunday.
September 1. WEEKLY CALENDAR
Exhibition of Water Color Paintings of California Announcements for the WEEKLY CALENDAR, legibly
Wildflowers by Ethel Wickes, May 24-September 1. written and SIGNED, should be in the hands of the
Exhibition of Prints and Etchings by Helen Hyde, Secretary to the Committee on Public Exercises, Room
June 13-September 1. 172, Administration Building, not later than noon,
The Art Gallery is open to the public, without charge, Tuesday.
