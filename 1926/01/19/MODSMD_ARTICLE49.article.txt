# SEQUOIA CLUB PLANS VALENTINE DANSANT
## 
### 
A "Valentine Dance" will be given
February 13 by Sequoia Club, accord
ing to plans made by the Sequoia so
cial committee and approved at the
meeting of the club last week. Other
social affairs scheduled by the club
members for the winter quarter are a
musicale, to be given next month
under the direction of Elwin Bugge,
and a "6 to 8 o'clock dinner dance.
George Mauser and Clifford Schinck
were elected to positions on the execu
tive committee of the Eating Club.
The Sequoia Club also decided to aid
in repairing the old Roble tennis
courts next Saturday afternoon.
