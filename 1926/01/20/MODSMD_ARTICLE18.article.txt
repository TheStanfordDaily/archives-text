# NEW ATHLETIC AWARDS PLAN ADOPTED BY EX COMMITTEE
## Effort Made to Standardize Method, Enhance Value Of Emblems 
### 
A new plan of athletic awards has
been drawn up by the Awards Com
mittee and was accepted with a few
changes by the Executive Committee
at its regular meeting last night. The
endeavor was made In the plan to
standardize the method of award, and
to enhance the value of the award in
the case of minor sports as a whole,
and of some major sports in particu
lar. The new system will go into the
by-laws of the constitution of the As
sociated Students in the following
manner:
There shall be five major sports: *
Football, Track, Basketball, Baseball,
and Tennis.
There shall be four first-class minor
sports: Swimming, Soccer, Boxing, and
Polo.
There shall be four second-class
minor sports: Fencing, Golf, Gym, and
Wrestling.
This plan is to go into effect at the
beginning of the 1926 track season,
and is not retroactive.
AWARDS
Major sport letters to be awarded
as follows:
1. Football —Any man who has
played ten minutes of the Stanford-
California game, or who has played
not less than ten minutes in at least
two Conference games other than the
California game, and whose total play
ing time in Conference games and
post-season games representing the
Pacific Coast. Conference, other than
the California game, amounts to sixty
minutes, shall be awarded a block "S."
2. Track —Any man who wins a
point or fraction thereof in the dual
meet with California, or in the
1.C.A.A.A.A., or in the N.C.A.A.; or
who wins a point or fraction thereof
in the Pacific Coast Conference, or a
first place in the U.S.C. meet, shall be
awarded a Block "S."
3. Basketball —Any man who has
played a total time equal to ten min
utes per game of the total games in
the California series, or who shall
have played one-half the time of the
remaining Conference games, or who
shall have played forty minutes in a
post-seaosn series, representing the
Pacific Coast Conference, shall be
awarded a Block "S."
4. Baseball —Any man who has
played four and one-half innings of
the California series, or who has
played one-half of the innings of the
remaining Conference games, shall be
awarded a Block "S."
5. Tennis —Any man who competes
in the Stanford-California Tourna
ment, or who competes in the Pacific
Coast Conference tournament, or any
man winning the Intercollegiate Divi
sion Championship, shall be awarded
a Block "S."
One man in each major sport whose
exceptional work during the season
gives him the unanimous vote of the
captain and coach of that sport, and
of the Graduate Manager and Execu
tive Committee, shall be awarded a
Block "S."
First-Class Minor Sport letters to
be awarded as follows:
1. Swimming— Any man who wins a
point or fraction thereof, in the Cali-
fornia Meet, or who competes in water
polo against California, shall be
awarded a Circle Block "S."
2. Soccer —Any man who competes
against California shall be awarded a
Block Circle "S."
3. Boxing—Any man who competes
against California, or if California is
not met, any man who competes in all
intercollegiate matches, shall be
awarded a Block Circle "S."
4. Polo —Any man who competes in
one-half the time of all intercollegiate
matches shall be awarded a Block Cir
cle "S."
Second-Class Minor Sport letters to
be awarded as follows:
1. Fencing—Any man who competes
against California (not to exceed six
men) shall be awarded a German "S."
2. Golf —Any man who plays on the
team competing against California
(not to exceed six men) shall be
awarded a German "S."
3. Gym—The five highest men plao
ing against California shall be awarded
a German "S."
4. Wrestling—Any man who com
petes against California, or if Califor
nia is not met, any man who competes
in all intercollegiate matches, shall be
awarded a German "S."
Freshman Sports and Awards —
1. The same rules as to the number
of sports shall apply to freshmen as
well as to the varsity.
2. Freshman awards shall be made
only in reference to competition with
California, and the requirements for
these awards shall be the same as for
the varsity in this respect, except in
football. Any man who plays in one
half the U.S.C. game, or in ten min
utes of the California game, shall be
awarded block numerals.
TYPE OF AWARDS
1. Major—
a. First award: a V-neck cardinal
sweater.
b. Second award in the same
sport: choice between any
type of standard sweater, card
inal or white, or a gold block
"S" medal.
c. Third award in the same
sport: choice of any type of
standard sweater, cardinal or
white, with three stars; or a
gold block "S" medal; or a
block "S" blanket with three
stars.
2. First-Class Minor —
a. First award: circle block "S"
and a cardinal jersey.
b. Second and third award in the
same sport: circle block "S"
and a choice between cardinal
or white jersey; third award
in same sport: star may be
placed beside the circle block
"S." •
c. If winner of awards desires he
may add to the cost of the jer
seys enough to buy a sweater.
3. Second-Class Minor —
a. First, second and third award:
German "S" without sweater
or jersey.
b. The German "S" may be sewn
on only a cardinal jersey the
first year; it may be sewn
on only a cardinal or white
jersey the second and third
years of the same sport.
4. Freshmen—
a. In major sports freshmen
shall be awarded a standard
cardinal V-neck sweater with
block numerals.
b. Any freshman who wins his
numerals in four major sports
shall receive a white coat
sweater with block numerals
in cardinal.
c. In minor sports the same rules

that govern varsity minor
sports shall apply, except that
in all cases the emblem shall
be block circle numerals.
CHAMPIONSHIP AWARDS
All letter men of the varsity football
team which wins an annual game with
California, all letter men of the var-
Bity track team which wins the dual
meet with California, all letter men
of the varsity basketball team which
wins the series with California, all
letter men of the varsity baseball team
which wins the series with California,
and all letter men of the varsity tennis
team which wins the annual tourna
ment with California, shall be awarded
gold emblems of the sport in which
they participate.
NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIP OR
COMPETITION IN OLYMPIC
GAMES
1. In swimming, tennis, track, box
ing, or any individual sport, any man
who wins a National Championship, or
who places in the Olympic Games,
shall receive a block "S."
2. In soccer, water polo, or any team
sport, any man competing on a win
ning team in the National Champion
ships, or that places in the Olympic
Games, shall be awarded a block "S."
ELIGIBILITY
1. The Pacific Coast Conference
eligibility rulings shall be given all
sports. Any sport not living up to
these rulings shall not be granted its
awards for that season.

AWARDS
Major sport letters to be awarded
as follows:
1. Football —Any man who has
played ten minutes of the Stanford-
California game, or who has played
not less than ten minutes in at least
two Conference games other than the
California game, and whose total play
ing time in Conference games and
post-season games representing the
Pacific Coast. Conference, other than
the California game, amounts to sixty
minutes, shall be awarded a block "S."
2. Track —Any man who wins a
point or fraction thereof in the dual
meet with California, or in the
1.C.A.A.A.A., or in the N.C.A.A.; or
who wins a point or fraction thereof
in the Pacific Coast Conference, or a
first place in the U.S.C. meet, shall be
awarded a Block "S."
3. Basketball —Any man who has
played a total time equal to ten min
utes per game of the total games in
the California series, or who shall
have played one-half the time of the
remaining Conference games, or who
shall have played forty minutes in a
post-seaosn series, representing the
Pacific Coast Conference, shall be
awarded a Block "S."
4. Baseball —Any man who has
played four and one-half innings of
the California series, or who has
played one-half of the innings of the
remaining Conference games, shall be
awarded a Block "S."
5. Tennis —Any man who competes
in the Stanford-California Tourna
ment, or who competes in the Pacific
Coast Conference tournament, or any
man winning the Intercollegiate Divi
sion Championship, shall be awarded
a Block "S."
One man in each major sport whose
exceptional work during the season
gives him the unanimous vote of the
captain and coach of that sport, and
of the Graduate Manager and Execu
tive Committee, shall be awarded a
Block "S."
First-Class Minor Sport letters to
be awarded as follows:
1. Swimming— Any man who wins a
point or fraction thereof, in the Cali-
fornia Meet, or who competes in water
polo against California, shall be
awarded a Circle Block "S."
2. Soccer —Any man who competes
against California shall be awarded a
Block Circle "S."
3. Boxing—Any man who competes
against California, or if California is
not met, any man who competes in all
intercollegiate matches, shall be
awarded a Block Circle "S."
4. Polo —Any man who competes in
one-half the time of all intercollegiate
matches shall be awarded a Block Cir
cle "S."
Second-Class Minor Sport letters to
be awarded as follows:
1. Fencing—Any man who competes
against California (not to exceed six
men) shall be awarded a German "S."
2. Golf —Any man who plays on the
team competing against California
(not to exceed six men) shall be
awarded a German "S."
3. Gym—The five highest men plao
ing against California shall be awarded
a German "S."
4. Wrestling—Any man who com
petes against California, or if Califor
nia is not met, any man who competes
in all intercollegiate matches, shall be
awarded a German "S."
Freshman Sports and Awards —
1. The same rules as to the number
of sports shall apply to freshmen as
well as to the varsity.
2. Freshman awards shall be made
only in reference to competition with
California, and the requirements for
these awards shall be the same as for
the varsity in this respect, except in
football. Any man who plays in one
half the U.S.C. game, or in ten min
utes of the California game, shall be
awarded block numerals.
TYPE OF AWARDS
1. Major—
a. First award: a V-neck cardinal
sweater.
b. Second award in the same
sport: choice between any
type of standard sweater, card
inal or white, or a gold block
"S" medal.
c. Third award in the same
sport: choice of any type of
standard sweater, cardinal or
white, with three stars; or a
gold block "S" medal; or a
block "S" blanket with three
stars.
2. First-Class Minor —
a. First award: circle block "S"
and a cardinal jersey.
b. Second and third award in the
same sport: circle block "S"
and a choice between cardinal
or white jersey; third award
in same sport: star may be
placed beside the circle block
"S." •
c. If winner of awards desires he
may add to the cost of the jer
seys enough to buy a sweater.
3. Second-Class Minor —
a. First, second and third award:
German "S" without sweater
or jersey.
b. The German "S" may be sewn
on only a cardinal jersey the
first year; it may be sewn
on only a cardinal or white
jersey the second and third
years of the same sport.
4. Freshmen—
a. In major sports freshmen
shall be awarded a standard
cardinal V-neck sweater with
block numerals.
b. Any freshman who wins his
numerals in four major sports
shall receive a white coat
sweater with block numerals
in cardinal.
c. In minor sports the same rules

that govern varsity minor
sports shall apply, except that
in all cases the emblem shall
be block circle numerals.
CHAMPIONSHIP AWARDS
All letter men of the varsity football
team which wins an annual game with
California, all letter men of the var-
Bity track team which wins the dual
meet with California, all letter men
of the varsity basketball team which
wins the series with California, all
letter men of the varsity baseball team
which wins the series with California,
and all letter men of the varsity tennis
team which wins the annual tourna
ment with California, shall be awarded
gold emblems of the sport in which
they participate.
NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIP OR
COMPETITION IN OLYMPIC
GAMES
1. In swimming, tennis, track, box
ing, or any individual sport, any man
who wins a National Championship, or
who places in the Olympic Games,
shall receive a block "S."
2. In soccer, water polo, or any team
sport, any man competing on a win
ning team in the National Champion
ships, or that places in the Olympic
Games, shall be awarded a block "S."
ELIGIBILITY
1. The Pacific Coast Conference
eligibility rulings shall be given all
sports. Any sport not living up to
these rulings shall not be granted its
awards for that season.

beginning of the 1926 track season,
and is not retroactive.

"S" medal.

or white jersey; third award

"S." •

shall receive a white coat
sweater with block numerals
in cardinal.
c. In minor sports the same rules

that govern varsity minor

beginning of the 1926 track season,
and is not retroactive.

Freshman Sports and Awards —
1. The same rules as to the number
of sports shall apply to freshmen as
well as to the varsity.
2. Freshman awards shall be made
only in reference to competition with
California, and the requirements for
these awards shall be the same as for
the varsity in this respect, except in
football. Any man who plays in one
half the U.S.C. game, or in ten min
utes of the California game, shall be
awarded block numerals.
TYPE OF AWARDS
1. Major—
a. First award: a V-neck cardinal
sweater.
b. Second award in the same
sport: choice between any
type of standard sweater, card
inal or white, or a gold block
"S" medal.
c. Third award in the same
sport: choice of any type of
standard sweater, cardinal or
white, with three stars; or a
gold block "S" medal; or a
block "S" blanket with three
stars.
2. First-Class Minor —
a. First award: circle block "S"
and a cardinal jersey.
b. Second and third award in the
same sport: circle block "S"
and a choice between cardinal
or white jersey; third award
in same sport: star may be
placed beside the circle block
"S." •
c. If winner of awards desires he
may add to the cost of the jer
seys enough to buy a sweater.
3. Second-Class Minor —
a. First, second and third award:
German "S" without sweater
or jersey.
b. The German "S" may be sewn
on only a cardinal jersey the
first year; it may be sewn
on only a cardinal or white
jersey the second and third
years of the same sport.
4. Freshmen—
a. In major sports freshmen
shall be awarded a standard
cardinal V-neck sweater with
block numerals.
b. Any freshman who wins his
numerals in four major sports
shall receive a white coat
sweater with block numerals
in cardinal.
c. In minor sports the same rules

that govern varsity minor
sports shall apply, except that
in all cases the emblem shall
be block circle numerals.
CHAMPIONSHIP AWARDS
All letter men of the varsity football
team which wins an annual game with
California, all letter men of the var-
Bity track team which wins the dual
meet with California, all letter men
of the varsity basketball team which
wins the series with California, all
letter men of the varsity baseball team
which wins the series with California,
and all letter men of the varsity tennis
team which wins the annual tourna
ment with California, shall be awarded
gold emblems of the sport in which
they participate.
NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIP OR
COMPETITION IN OLYMPIC
GAMES
1. In swimming, tennis, track, box
ing, or any individual sport, any man
who wins a National Championship, or
who places in the Olympic Games,
shall receive a block "S."
2. In soccer, water polo, or any team
sport, any man competing on a win
ning team in the National Champion
ships, or that places in the Olympic
Games, shall be awarded a block "S."
ELIGIBILITY
1. The Pacific Coast Conference
eligibility rulings shall be given all
sports. Any sport not living up to
these rulings shall not be granted its
awards for that season.
A new plan of athletic awards has
been drawn up by the Awards Com
mittee and was accepted with a few
changes by the Executive Committee
at its regular meeting last night. The
endeavor was made In the plan to
standardize the method of award, and
to enhance the value of the award in
the case of minor sports as a whole,
and of some major sports in particu
lar. The new system will go into the
by-laws of the constitution of the As
sociated Students in the following
manner:
There shall be five major sports: *
Football, Track, Basketball, Baseball,
and Tennis.
There shall be four first-class minor
sports: Swimming, Soccer, Boxing, and
Polo.
There shall be four second-class
minor sports: Fencing, Golf, Gym, and
Wrestling.
This plan is to go into effect at the
beginning of the 1926 track season,
and is not retroactive.
