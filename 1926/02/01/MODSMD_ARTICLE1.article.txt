# FIRST DAY BRINGS SIX NOMINATIONS TOR A.S.S.U. POSTS
## BOXES TO CLOSE FRIDAY Positions for Two Juniors Open on Union Board Of Governors 
### 
Six nominations for A.S.S.U. and
class offices were turned in today,
opening the semi-annual nomination
week. The boxes will be open until 11
o'clock Friday morning.
Two junior members of the Union
Board of Governors must be nomi
nated in addition to the list previously
announced, according to a statement
this morning by Jack Sutro, chairman
of the election board.
Will Elect Two
Two junior men will be elected to
represent the class of 1927 on the
Executive Committee. One will serve
for a full-year term. The other will
serve for six months, filling out the
term of office left vacant by the death
of Leslie Herth last quarter. It is
necessary in nominating to descrimi
nate between the two offices.
Every member of the A.S.S.U. is
eligible to nominate, and candidates'
names should be put in the ballot box
provided for them at the Law steps.
Resignations can be made until 12
o'clock Saturday noon. Men and wo
men may nominate each other this
year.
Elect Editor Tomorrow
Election of the new editor for The
Daily Palo Alto will take place at the
regular weekly meeting of the staff
tomorrow at 1 o'clock. The student
body election for those nominated this
week will be Tuesday, February 9..
The revised list of offices to be
filled now includes the following:
1. Secretary of the A.S.S.U., to serve
for the remainder of this school year.
2. One man Junior member of the
Executive Committee, to serve for the
remainder of the school year.
3. One man Junior member of the
Executive Committee, to serve for one
year, until this election next year.
4. One man Sophomore member of
the Executive Committee, to serve for
one year, until this election next year.
5. One member of the Board of Ath
letic Control, to serve for one year,
until this election next year.
6. Two Junior members of the
Union Board of Governors to serve
one year.
7. Two members of Men's Council,
to serve for one year, until this elec
tion next year.
Four officers for each class, a presi
dent, a vice-president, a secretary, and
a treasurer, are to be elected.
Nominations
Following are the nominations re
ceived this morning:
I hereby nominate William J. Trav
el's for 1928 representative on the
Executive Committee. —Harold Woolf.
I hereby nominate Howard Mitchell
for Board of Athletic Control. —Hugo
Leistner.
I hereby nominate Paul Wayte for
Men's Council. —J. I). VanAmringe.
I hereby nominate Murray Draper
for president of the class of 1928. —J.
C. Canfield.
I hereby nominate John C. Canfield
for treasurer of the class of 1928. —
Claude Leach.
I hereby nominate Jack Leiter for
Junior member of the Executive Com
mittee to serve one year—John C.
Duniway.

Elect Editor Tomorrow
Election of the new editor for The
Daily Palo Alto will take place at the
regular weekly meeting of the staff
tomorrow at 1 o'clock. The student
body election for those nominated this
week will be Tuesday, February 9..
The revised list of offices to be
filled now includes the following:
1. Secretary of the A.S.S.U., to serve
for the remainder of this school year.
2. One man Junior member of the
Executive Committee, to serve for the
remainder of the school year.
3. One man Junior member of the
Executive Committee, to serve for one
year, until this election next year.
4. One man Sophomore member of
the Executive Committee, to serve for
one year, until this election next year.
5. One member of the Board of Ath
letic Control, to serve for one year,
until this election next year.
6. Two Junior members of the
Union Board of Governors to serve
one year.
7. Two members of Men's Council,
to serve for one year, until this elec
tion next year.
Four officers for each class, a presi
dent, a vice-president, a secretary, and
a treasurer, are to be elected.
Nominations
Following are the nominations re
ceived this morning:
I hereby nominate William J. Trav
el's for 1928 representative on the
Executive Committee. —Harold Woolf.
I hereby nominate Howard Mitchell
for Board of Athletic Control. —Hugo
Leistner.
I hereby nominate Paul Wayte for
Men's Council. —J. I). VanAmringe.
I hereby nominate Murray Draper
for president of the class of 1928. —J.
C. Canfield.
I hereby nominate John C. Canfield
for treasurer of the class of 1928. —
Claude Leach.
I hereby nominate Jack Leiter for
Junior member of the Executive Com
mittee to serve one year—John C.
Duniway.
Six nominations for A.S.S.U. and
class offices were turned in today,
opening the semi-annual nomination
week. The boxes will be open until 11
o'clock Friday morning.
Two junior members of the Union
Board of Governors must be nomi
nated in addition to the list previously
announced, according to a statement
this morning by Jack Sutro, chairman
of the election board.
Will Elect Two
Two junior men will be elected to
represent the class of 1927 on the
Executive Committee. One will serve
for a full-year term. The other will
serve for six months, filling out the
term of office left vacant by the death
of Leslie Herth last quarter. It is
necessary in nominating to descrimi
nate between the two offices.
Every member of the A.S.S.U. is
eligible to nominate, and candidates'
names should be put in the ballot box
provided for them at the Law steps.
Resignations can be made until 12
o'clock Saturday noon. Men and wo
men may nominate each other this
year.
