# SEVEN NEW NAMES PLACED ON BALLOT FOR QUAD ELECTION
## Twenty-Three Candidates in Race; Nominations Close Friday 
### 
Seven more nominations for A.S.S.U.
and class officers were added today,
bringing the total up to twenty-three.
The latest date for nominations is 12
o'clock Friday noon. Candidates may
decline not later thin 11 o'clock Sat
urday morning.
Every member of the A.S.S.U. is
eligible to nominate, and candidates'
names should be put in the ballot dox
provided for them at the Law steps.
Following is the list of nominations
received today:
I hereby nominate Robert Raymond
lor Sophomore representative on the
Executive Committee. —Chris Freeman.
I hereby nominate A. Perry Banta
for Vice-President of the class of
1926.—A. E. Heston.
I hereby nominate Frederick N.
Tyroler for Men's Council. —Kenneth
W. Thompson.
I hereby nominate Anne Bancroft
for Vice-President of the class of
1928. —Calvin Behle.
I hereby nominate John Roberts for
Secretary of the A.S.S.U.—Robert
Titus.
I hereby nominate A. Grove Day for
Men's Council.—Merrill Armour.
I hereby nominate John C. Truman
for Junior Representative on th*J
Executive Committer to hold oiTice
for the remainder of this year.—Bob
Baillard.
Offices Open
Following are the offices to be filled:
1. Secretary of the A.S.S.U., to serve
for the remainder of this school year.
2. One man Junior member of the
Executive Committee, to serve for the
j remainder of the school year.
3. One man Junior member of the
Executive Committee, to serve for one
year, until this election next year.
4. One man Sophomore member of
the Executive Committee, to serve for
one year, until this election next year.
5. One member of the Board of Ath
letic Control, to serve for one year,
until this election next year.
6. Two Junior members of the
Union Board of Governors to serve
one year.
7. Two members of Men's Council,
to serve for one year, until this elec
tion next year.
Four officers for each class, a presi
dent, a vice-president, a secretary, and
a treasurer, are to be elected.
