THURSDAY, FEBRUARY 18
3:15 p.m.—Stanford Dames, in the
Women's Clubhouse,
4:15 p.m.—Organ Recital, by Mr. Al
len, in the Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m.—Thursday Evening Confer
ence, at the home of Dr. David
Starr Jordan. Subject: "The
Trend of Universities."
7:30 p.m.—Open House Night, at the
Y.M.C.A. building. All students
are invited.
7:30 p.m.—German Club in the Wo
men's Clubhouse. "An evening of
music."
8:15 p.m.—Concert by Cecilia Han
sen, Violinist, under the auspices
of the Peninsula Musical Associ
ation, in the Assembly hall.
FRIDAY, FEBRUARY 19
4:15 p.m.—Fifth Lecture on Patent
Law, by Mr. Leonard S. Lyon, of
the Los Angeles Bar. Topic:
"How an Invention Must Be De
scribed and Claimed for Letters
Patent." Room 12.
8:00 p.m.—Popular Medical Lecture,
Lane Hall, San Francisco. Sub
ject: "Gastro-intestinal Disturb
ances and Nutrition," by Dr. P.
K. Brown.
8:30 p.m.—Benefit Dance for the Wo
men's Clubhouse, under the au
spices of the Clubhouse Board, in
the Women's Clubhouse.
BATURDAY, FEBRUARY 20
10:00 a.m.—Sixth Lecture on Patent
Law, by Mr. Lyon. Topic: "Pro
ceedings in the U. S. Patent Of
fice Upon an Application for a
Patent." Room 12.

Coasting and snowball fights have
been the pre-examination activities of
the students at the University of
Idaho.
