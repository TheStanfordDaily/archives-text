# COUNCIL ENDORSES RUSHING RULES IN MEET LAST NIGHT
## "GROUP SYSTEM" IS NEW Pledging Day Set for May 6; First Period Opens Sunday, April 11 
### 
Final rushing system rules, adopted
by the Interfraternity Board of Con
trol, were passed last night by the
Interfraternity Council for spring
quarter rushing, which commences
Sunday, April 11, at 12:30 p.m. and
ends Tuesday, May 4, at 8 p.m. Pledg
ing will take place on Wednesday,
May 5.
The houses have been divided into
two groups, which will rush on alter
nate days during the first period. This
is the first time that such a plan of
alternate day rushing has been tried
here at Stanford. Following are the
groups and the houses under them,
and the dates on which the two groups
will rush, as well as a general outline
of the system as it was adopted:
Group 1. —Alpha Kappa Lambda,
Alpha Tau Omega, Beta Tlieta Pi,
Delta Chi, Delta Upsilon, Kappa Alpha,
Kappa Sigma, Phi Gamma Delta, Phi
Kappa Psi, Sigma Alpha Epsilon,
Sigma Chi, Zeta Psi.
Group 2. —Alpha Delta Plii, Alpha
Sigma Phi, Chi Psi, Delta Kappa Epsi
lon, Delta Tau Delta, Phi Delta Theta,
Phi Kappa Sigma, Phi Sigma Kappa,
Sigma Nu, Theta Chi, Theta Delta Chi,
Theta Xi.
Group 1 shall rush on the following
days: Sunday, April 11, 12:30 to 3
o'clock; Tuesday, April 13; Thursday,
April 15; Monday, April 19; Wednes
day, April 21.
Group 2 shall rush on the following
days: Monday, April 12; Wednesday,
April 14; Friday, April 16; Sunday,
April 18, 12:30 to 3 p.m.; Tuesday,
April 20.
There shall be no rushing whatso
ever on Saturday, April 17, the day of
j the Stanford-California track meet.
During this first period a fraternit;
j may have only one date with eaci
freshman, and throughout this perioi
I the dates shall begin at 11:30 a.m
and end at 2 p.m. for luncheon, an<
shall begin at 5:30 p.m. and end at :
I p.m. for dinners.
j From Wednesday evening till Sun
J day noon, April 25, there shall be n<
j rushing.
The second period begins at nooi
on Sunday, April 25, and ends Friday
night, April 30. The time allotted fo'i
rushing during this second period is a;
follows: Sunday, 8 a.m. to 8 p.m.
Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, am
Thursday, 11:30 a.m. to 2 p.m., am
5:30 p.m. to 8 p.m.; Friday, 11:30 a.m
to 1 a.m. Saturday.
The final period begins on Saturday
May 1, and extends till Tuesday eve
ning, May 4. The time allotted foi
rushing during this third period is at
follows: Saturday, 8 a.m. to 1 a.m.
Sunday; Sunday, 8 a.m. to 8 p.m.;
Monday and Tuesday, 11:30 a.m. to i
p.m., and 5:30 p.m. to 8 p.m.
Form Cards
Dates with freshmen will be made
only by means of form cards, and the
first set of these will be given out
tomorrow evening from 8 to S
o'clock at the Old Union. Dates for
giving out the second and third sets
will be announced at Interfraternity
j Council meetings.
I These cards, which will be self-ex
planatory, are to be sent to each
freshman whom the house desires to
invite, with a list of dates from which
he may accept one (either a luncheon
or dinner) during the first period. On
cards covering the second and third
periods a freshman may accept as
many of the dates offered by the house
as he desires.
His signature in the proper place
signifies his acceptance and is bind
ing. If a house permits a visit by a
freshman who has not signed for that
date, it has violated this rule, and it
will be denied pledging privileges as
a penalty. However, on the last eve
ning of rushing, Tuesday, May 4, fresh
men may visit any house or houses
regardless of signatures on the date
cards.
On Monday, May 3, all bids must be
delivered to a clerk appointed by the
Board of Control, which clerk will be
at the Old Union between the hours
of 2 p.m. and 4 p.m. Bids must be type
written and in alphabetical order.
Pledging will take place on Wednes
day, May 5, at the Old Union, where
a lawyer selected by the Board of Con
trol will extend the bids.
A man who is eligible for pledging
who is not pledged at formal bidding
may not be rushed or pledged before
11:30 a.m. on the following day, Mav
6, 1926.
General Rules
There shall be no off-campus rush
ing.
If a pledge is broken by either the
fraternity or by a man himself, he
shall not be eligible to be re-pledged
until the corresponding quarter of the
ensuing year, dating from the time of
the original pledging.
All complaints for rushing rules vio
lations shall henceforth be mailed in
writing to Professor Owens. The signa
ture of the complaining witness shall
be hereon affixed but it shall not be
used or mentioned during or after the
prosecution proceedings.

Form Cards
Dates with freshmen will be made
only by means of form cards, and the
first set of these will be given out
tomorrow evening from 8 to S
o'clock at the Old Union. Dates for
giving out the second and third sets
will be announced at Interfraternity
j Council meetings.
I These cards, which will be self-ex
planatory, are to be sent to each
freshman whom the house desires to
invite, with a list of dates from which
he may accept one (either a luncheon
or dinner) during the first period. On
cards covering the second and third
periods a freshman may accept as
many of the dates offered by the house
as he desires.
His signature in the proper place
signifies his acceptance and is bind
ing. If a house permits a visit by a
freshman who has not signed for that
date, it has violated this rule, and it
will be denied pledging privileges as
a penalty. However, on the last eve
ning of rushing, Tuesday, May 4, fresh
men may visit any house or houses
regardless of signatures on the date
cards.
On Monday, May 3, all bids must be
delivered to a clerk appointed by the
Board of Control, which clerk will be
at the Old Union between the hours
of 2 p.m. and 4 p.m. Bids must be type
written and in alphabetical order.
Pledging will take place on Wednes
day, May 5, at the Old Union, where
a lawyer selected by the Board of Con
trol will extend the bids.
A man who is eligible for pledging
who is not pledged at formal bidding
may not be rushed or pledged before
11:30 a.m. on the following day, Mav
6, 1926.
General Rules
There shall be no off-campus rush
ing.
If a pledge is broken by either the
fraternity or by a man himself, he
shall not be eligible to be re-pledged
until the corresponding quarter of the
ensuing year, dating from the time of
the original pledging.
All complaints for rushing rules vio
lations shall henceforth be mailed in
writing to Professor Owens. The signa
ture of the complaining witness shall
be hereon affixed but it shall not be
used or mentioned during or after the
prosecution proceedings.
