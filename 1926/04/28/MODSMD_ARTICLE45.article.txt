# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
WEDNESDAY
AMERICAN SOCIETY OF CIVIL EN
GINEERS will meet tonight at 8
o'clock in the south reading room,
Toyon hall. Philip Schuyler, editor
of Western Construction News, will
speak on "Observations from a Di
versified Engineering Experience."
UNIVERSITY ASSEMBLY in the As
sembly hall at 11 o'clock. Address
by President Mary E. Woolley of
Mt. Holyoke College.
RESIDENT ALUMNAE TEA will be
held in the Women's Clubhouse at 3
o'clock. Hostesses: Mrs. James Gra
ham Sharp, Mrs. J. Lorber, Mrs. R.
Agnew, Mrs. F. Dodd, Mrs. F. Wick
ett, Mrs. Frank Hall, Mrs. Edwin S.
. Tucker, Mrs. Edgar Alexander, Mrs.
W. Chappie, Mrs. John C. Spencer,
Mrs. Johnson Field.
SCIENCE ASSOCIATION will meet in
the Faculty Clubhouse at 8 o'clock.
Professor David L. Webster, of the
Department of Physics, will speak
on "The Meteor Crater of Arizona."
THURSDAY
ORGAN RECITAL in the Memorial
Church at 4:15 o'clock, by Warren
D. Allen, University Organist.
THURSDAY EVENING CONFER
ENCE will be held at the home of
Dr. David Starr Jordan at 7:30
o'clock. Subject: "The Innumerable
Company."
FRIDAY
CONCERT, by the Stanford Band, in
the Assembly hall, at 8 o'clock, un
der the auspices of the Committee
on Public Exercices and open to the
public without charge.
