FRIDAY
JUNIPERO SERRA CLUB will be the
gu<*sts of the Catholic Church at a
dance tonight in the Palo Alto Wo
men's Clubhouse. All members who
wish transportation should be at the
Stanford Women's Clubhouse at 8:20
o'clock this evening.
CONCERT, by the Stanford Band, in
the Assembly hall, at 8 o'clock, un
der the auspices of the Committee
on Public Exercices and open to the
public without charge.
SUNDAY
DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON at
11 o'clock in Memorial Church. Mon
signor Joseph M. Gleason, St. Thom
as Aquinas Church, Palo Alto, will
preach.
ORGAN RECITAL at 4 o'clock in the
Memorial Church, by Warren D. Al
len, University Organist.
THE STANFORD EPISCOPAL CLUB
will meet in the parish house at tJ
o'clock. Father Viall, S.S.J.E., of
the Church of the Advent, San Fran
cisco, will speak.
MASONIC DEGREE TEAM will re
hearse at 9:30 o'clock this morning
in the Masonic Temple, Palo Alto.
MONDAY
FIESTA PUBLICITY COMMITTEE
will meet at 5 o'clock upstairs in
the Press building.
THETA SIGMA PHI will meet at 5:30
upstairs in Press building. Import
ant.
EDUCATION CLUB will meet at 8
o'clock this evening in the Women's
Clubhouse. Address on "Higher Edu
cational and Professional Standards
for Senior High School Teachers,"
by Joseph Marr Gwinn, Superintend
ent of San Francisco Schools. The
public is invited.
