# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
MONDAY
ANNUAL MEETING of Faculty Wo
men's Club will be held in the Wo
men's Clubhouse at 15 o'clock.
ENGLISH REVIEW CLUB will meet
at the home of Professor Arthur G.
Kennedy, 483 Coleridge Ave., Palo
Alto, at 7:30 o'clock.
ALUMNI AND FORMER STUDENTS
of the University of Indiana hold an
niversary banquet in Stanford Union
at 6:15 o'clock.
TUESDAY
ORGAN RECITAL by Warren D. Al
len, University Organist, in the Me
morial Church, at 4:15 o'clock.
CHRISTIAN SCIENCE SOCIETY will
hold is regular bi-monthly testimo
nial meeting at 7:30 o'clock tonight
on the second floor of the Women's
Clubhouse. Members of the faculty,
students, and others associated with
the University are invited.
GRADUATE WOMEN'S CLUB infor
mal tea will be held in the Women's
Clubhouse at 4:30 o'clock.
THREE ONE-ACT PLAYS, "The Pot
Boiler," "The Thrice Promised
Bride," and "The Maker of Dreams,"
will be presented by the class in
English 19b, in the Assembly hall,
at 7:30 o'clock. Open to the public
without admission charge.
WEDNESDAY
ANNUAL LABOR DAY will com
mence at 1 o'clock at the Stanford
Home for Convalescent Children.

RESIDENT ALUMNAE TEA in the
Women's Clubhouse at 3 o'clock. The
hostesses are Mrs. Dan Evans, Mrs.
Andrew Boone, Mrs. Dudley De-
Groot, Mrs. O. B. Berry, Mrs. Ben
F. Hake, Mrs. Herbert Ormsby, and
Mrs. Dana Burks.

RESEARCH CLUB will meet in the
Faculty Clubhouse at 8 o'clock. The
following papers will be read: "Al
lergic Responses of Smooth Muscles
Produced by Physical and Chemical
Changes in the Environment," by
Professor P. J. Hanzlik, and "A Sim
plified Logical Calculus," by Profes
sor Frederick Anderson.
THURSDAY
BOARD OF TRUSTEES will meet in
regular monthly session in the of
fices of the Board, Balfour Building,
San Francisco, at 1:45 o'clock.
ORGAN RECITAL will be given by
Warren 1). Allen, University Organ
ist, in the Memorial Church, at 4:15
o'clock.

JORDAN CLUB will meet at 4:15
o'clock, Room 4GO, Botany building.
Papers: "The developmental effects
of the gene for yellow color in mice,"
by Mrs. Sophie B. de Aberle; "The
effect on fat metabolism of the gene
for yellow color in mice," by David
A. Wood and H. P. Cope.
UNIVERSITY PHILOLOGICAL ASSO
CIATION will meet in the Women's
Clubhouse at 4:15. Paper on "Three
Notes- on Hamlet," by Dr. Henry
David Gray. Informal discussion on
"Conditions in Russia as they apply
to Scholars in the Arts and Letters,"
by Dr. Henry Lanz.
REGULAR THURSDAY EVENING
CONFERENCE will be held at the
home of Dr. David Starr Jordan at
7:30 o'clock. Subject: "Stevenson."
