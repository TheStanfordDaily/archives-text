# FOUR NOMINEES ADDED TO LIST OF CANDIDATES
## Ballot Box at Law Steps Will Close Tomorrow At 11 O'clock NOMINATIONS TOTAL 33 Only One Nomination Made For Offices in Three Classes 
### 
With tomorrow the last day of nomi
nations for student body and class
officers to be elected next Tuesday a
heavy list is expected by 11 o'clock
when the ballot box at the Law Steps
will be removed. Only one person has
been nominated for the Union Board
of Governors, whereas three are to
be elected. The only class officer who
has been nominated so far is one man
for the president of the Class of 1927.
Following is the list of offices open:
A.S.S.U. president, vice-president,
secretary; all class officers; three
places on the Men's Council, to be
filled by men who have ninety units
or over; one junior man member of
the Executive Comittee, and one wo
man member; one sophomore man
member of the committee, and one
woman; one member of the Board of
Athletic Control to hold office for one
year, and another member to hold
office for a half-year, both to be upper
classmen; three members of the Union
Board of Governors, to be seniors or
holdovers.

Following is the list of new nomina
tions:

for Men's Council —C. W. Carlsmith.

for Junior Man Representative on the

Harter.

With tomorrow the last day of nomi
nations for student body and class
officers to be elected next Tuesday a
heavy list is expected by 11 o'clock
when the ballot box at the Law Steps
will be removed. Only one person has
been nominated for the Union Board
of Governors, whereas three are to
be elected. The only class officer who
has been nominated so far is one man
for the president of the Class of 1927.
Following is the list of offices open:
A.S.S.U. president, vice-president,
secretary; all class officers; three
places on the Men's Council, to be
filled by men who have ninety units
or over; one junior man member of
the Executive Comittee, and one wo
man member; one sophomore man
member of the committee, and one
woman; one member of the Board of
Athletic Control to hold office for one
year, and another member to hold
office for a half-year, both to be upper
classmen; three members of the Union
Board of Governors, to be seniors or
holdovers.
