# HERRINGTON LOSES IN FINALS OF TENNIS AT DEL MONTE TOURNEY
## 
### 
Alan Herrington, who has been a
prominent figure in western tennis
this summer, lost to Mervyn Gricin
in the semi-final round in the Del
Monte annual championship singles
tournament as a result of Sunday's
play. In the doubles, Herrington lost
again when paired with Gerald Strat
ford of the University of California.
They were defeated in the final round
by Neil Brown and William Parker,
4-6, 6-3, 7-5.
In his first match Herrington de
feated Ray Kline, 6-3, 6-1. His win
over Bowie Dietrick at 6-3, 10-8, put
him in the round of eight. He lost to
Griffin after winning the first set, 1-6,
9-7, 6-2. In the doubles Herrington and
Stratford defeated O. A. Elmo and
H. A. Swartz, 6-3, 6-4, in the first
round, and Walter and Waring by de
fault in the second round. In the
round of eight they were not forced
to extend themselves to win from Her
bert Suhr and Wallace Bates at 6-1,
6-4. In the semi-finals they defeated
Kinsey and Dietrick, 6-4, 8-10, 6-3.
Other Stanford tennis players to
play in the tournament were, Law
rence Hall of the 1929 freshmen, Al
man Manspeaker, and William Irvine.
Both Manspeaker and Irvine lost in
the first round, but Hall, after taking
a default from Maurice McLaughlin,
the former world's champion, defeated
Roy Wright at 7-5, 6-4 in the second
round, but lost in the third to Roland
Roberts, a noted coast player, after
taking the first set, 5-7, 6-3, 6-2.
