# HOLMAN, OGDEN LOSE WHEN EAST DEFEATS WEST IN TOURNEMENT
## 
### 
The East scored a decisive victory
over the West, and Holman and Ogden
of Stanford and Chandler of the Uni
versity of California went down to
defeat in the annual East-West tennis
matches held on August 13 and 14 at
Forest Hills, New York.
On the first day of play Chandler
fell before the superior strategy and
volleying of Vincent Richards, 6-3, 6-3.
At the same time, Alfred Chapin, Jr.,
won over Lionel Ogden, 6-2, 6-2, but it
took William Tilden and Chapin three
hard-fought sets to defeat Ogden and
Cranston Holman at 6-3, 7-9, 6-4. The
hopes of the West had been pinned on
the three collegiate players and they
put up a gallant fight. The doubles
match produced some brilliant tennis.
In the second set Ogden's sensational
net work kept Tilden and Chapin on
the defensive, and Holman's effective
service kept the experienced Eastern
stars at bay. Lapses at critical mo
ments brought their downfall.
On the second day, Richards and R.
Norris Williams downed William John
son and Chandler 6-3, 6-4, and Phil
Neer bowed to Francis T. Funter in
straight sets, 6-4, 6-3. The battle be
tween Holman and Manuel Alanzo, the
Spanish Davis Cup star, was bitterly
contested, the latter finally emerging
the winner in two sets, 6-3, 8-6.
The feature match of the tourna
ment was when William Tilden and
William Johnson crossed rackets for
the first time since September of last
year. Tilden won in four sets, 6-3, 4-6,
6-1, 6-1, and thereby proved his super
ior condition. Johnson went to pieces
when he was affected by the heat in
the final two sets; his service became
faulty, and his other strokes lacked
punch and accuracy.
