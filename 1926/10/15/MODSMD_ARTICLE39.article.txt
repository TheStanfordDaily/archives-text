# Y.W.C.A. FLANS RALLY FOR DISCUSSION OF CLUB'S AIMS, IDEALS
## 
### 
Women who are interested in the
Y.W.C.A. will have an opportunity to
become acquainted with its activities
at a rally to be held Tuesday evening
at 7:15 o'clock, downstairs in the
Women's Clubhouse.
Officers of the organization will ex
plain the details and aims of the
Y.W.C.A. Short talks on the kind
of work to be done are to be given
by speakers from San Francisco and
Berkeley.
It is planned to try out a system
here that has proved successful at
the University of California. Groups
of women will be arranged according
to the commissions in which they are
interested. Each group will be led by
an outside person in order that new
Ideas can be brought up for discus
sion.
Not only new women, but also all
others who are interested in getting
into this type of work are asked to be
present.
At the Senior Cabinet meeting, Anne
Hartwell was chosen publicity man
ager. Margaret Owen was elected to
the Cabinet as a representative of the
Asilomar division of the Y.W.C.A. She
■will have charge of the religious life
of the women students on the campus
in connection with the Y.
