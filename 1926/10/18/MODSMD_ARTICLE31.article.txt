# ANNOUNCEMENTS

MONDAY
4:00 P.M. —Managerial tryoutees for
the Stanford Literary magazine will
meet in the Lit office in the Press
building.
7:00 P.M. —Senior Class Meeting will
be held at the Women's Clubhouse.
Senior endowment plans will be
made and a final decision made by a
class vote.
TUESDAY
SOPHOMORE CLASS meeting will be
held in Room 214, instead of Little
Theatre, as previously announced.
1:00 P.M.—Seat sale for "Hay Fever"
in the Old Union and Crandall's.
7:00 P.M. —Quad editorial tryoutees
will meet in the Quad office upstairs
in the Press building. All who are
interested are asked to come to this
meeting.
7:30 P.M.—Members of the Stanford
Daily business staff, together with
all tryoutees, will meet in the busi
ness office.
4:15 P.M. —Organ recital by Warren
D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
4:30 P.M.—Graduate Women's Tea in
the Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 P.M. —Lecture on "Recent Devel
opments at Geneva" by Professor
Stuart in the Little Theatre.
WEDNESDAY
3 P.M. —Football Show committee
meets in Assembly Hall. Those in
terested in any phase of the show
are requested to be present.
3:00 P.M. —Resident Alumnae Tea in
the Women's Clubhouse.
8:00 P.M. —Chi Nu Kappa in the Wo
men's Clubhouse. Professor H. R.
Fairelough will speak.
THURSDAY
12:15 P.M. —Alumni of the University
of Michigan are invited to attend a
luncheon at the Palace Hotel, San
Francisco, in honor of President
Little.
3:00 P.M. —Stanford Dames in the Wo
men's Clubhouse.

D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
7:30 P.M. —German Club in the Wo
men's Clubhouse. Election of offi
cers.
7:30 P.M. —Thursday Evening Confer
ence at the home of Dr. David Starr
Jordan. Subject: "The Menace to
Science."
7:45 P.M. —History Club in the Wo
men's Clubhouse. Professor Golder
will speak. All graduate History
students invited to attend.

8:20 P.M. —"Hay Fever" by Noel Cow
ard in the Assembly Hall.
