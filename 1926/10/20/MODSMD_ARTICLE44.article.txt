# SPEAKERS AT RALLY EXPLAIN OBJECTS OF Y.W.C.A. FELLOWSHIP
## 
### 
Over 150 women turned out for the
Y.W.C.A. rally last night in the Wo
men's Clubhouse. The object and aims
of the organization were explained by
the various speakers.
Ethelind Thomson, president of the
organization, opened the meeting. She
briefly explained the work of the
Y.W.C.A., discussed the possibilities
of the various committees, and out
lined the work on each.
Miss Isabel Capps, secretary of the
Y.W.C.A., in her talk particularly
stressed fellowship and friendliness
among the women on the campus. She
said, "Fellowship is an atmosphere of
friendliness, open-heartedness, and tol
erance. We can only have fellowship
on the campus if we will be friendly,
open-hearted, and tolerant in our
turn."
After the general meeting the wo
men adjourned to the various groups
where they were addressed by the
chairmen and the visiting speakers.
The Y.W.C.A. plans to make this rally
a precedent, and have such gatherings
once each quarter.
