# STUDENTS WILL CONDUCT POLL ON PROHIBITION
## Scheme in Accordance With Students' Federation College Canvass VOTED FOR UNANIMOUSLY Possible Event of Adverse Voting Upon Publicity Is Discussed 
### 
Stanford will have an opportunity
to voice its opinion on the prohibition
question as the result of action taken
last night by the Executive Commit
tee authorizing an early poll on the
subject. The motion was made in ac
cord with a national canvass being
made under the direction of the Na
tional Students' Federation.
Although the proposal passed unani
mously, the question was raised as to
the possible effect on the University's
name in event of an adverse vote. The
date and other details of the polling
will be announced tomorrow.
The minutes of last night's meeting
are as follows:
M.S.C. That the January 22 date
allowed the Gleo Club be made a
closed date.
M.S.C. That the Executive Commit
tee authorize a poll to determine stu
dent opinion on Prohibition, in ac
cord with the request of the National
Students' Federation.
M.S.C. That John Duniway be ap
pointed to represent the A.S.S.U. at
the Northern California Student In
stitute on Pacific Relations.
M.S.C. That the budget be adopted
for the year 1926-27, to replace the
budget adopted last spring.
M.S.C. That the returns of the fresh
man class election of November 4,
1026, approved by George Ellsworth,
be accepted.
M.S.C. That the secretary write let
ters to the Dramatic Manager con
cerning removal of advertising signs
from campus sidewalks.
M.S.C. That this committee modify
action of last week relative to paying
for mailing fifty Stanford Dailies per
day to high schools of the state, so as
to make the Executive Committee lia
ble for the cost of the papers rather
than for a flat rate of $150 per year.
M.S.C. That the following dates be
granted: December 3, Stanford Dames
Club, Informal; January 21, Sigma
Alpha Epsilon, Masquerade.
