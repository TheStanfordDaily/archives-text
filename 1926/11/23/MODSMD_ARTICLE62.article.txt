# UNUSUAL NUMBER OF MEN EARN EXPENSES
## 
### 
Statistics'issued by the Appointment
Office yesterday show that sixty-two
per cent of the men registered in the
University this quarter arc entirely or
partially self-supporting, and that six
teen per cent are earning over half
their income.
The number of self-supporting stu
dents enrolled at the present time ex
ceeds the usual total, and shows a
marked increase over last year when
only forty-nine per cent were earning
their own way. A particularly large
jump is noticeable in the number earn
ing over half their income.
Of the women, nineteen per cent are
earning part of their expenses and
four per cent are entirely self-support
ing, bringing the total number of self
supporting students to something over
fifty-two per cent of the entire enroll
ment.
