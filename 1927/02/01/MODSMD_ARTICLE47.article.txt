# UNIVERSITY HAS 85 STUDENTS FOR EACH LOCAL LIVING GROUP
## 
### 
For every sixty-eight students in
the University there is a living group
on the campus. These figures were ob
tained by the division of the 3549 stu
dents enrolled this quarter by the fifty
two campus living groups.
There are thirty-nine men's living
groups, or one for every seventy-six
men. Included in this are seven eating
clubs, twenty-four fraternities, two
house clubs, and six dormitories.
The women have a slight edge over
the men, with one living group for
every thirty-eight women. There are
ten sororities and three halls for the
women.
