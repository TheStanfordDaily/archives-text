FRIDAY
7:30 P.M. —Ushers report in Assembly
Hall for "The Swan."
8:00 P.M. —Popular Medical Lecture,
Lane Hall, San Francisco. Subject:
"The Relation of Dental Infection to
Disease," by Dr. John A. Marshall.
8:15 P.M. —The Swan, drama by Franz
Molnar, presented by Masquers' So
ciety. Seat sale Tuesday, February
8, at 1:00 p.m. in the Old Union,
Campus, and at Crandall's in Palo
Alto.
SATURDAY
9:00 A.M. —Ceneral rehearsal to be
held of "Princess Ida" in the Assem
bly Hall.
10:00 A.M. —Bruce Curry, leading a
forum on student religious problems.
Sponsored by the Stanford Fellow
ship. Open to both men and women.
Women's Clubhouse.
SUNDAY
9:30* A.M. —Bruce Curry, forum, Wo
men's Clubhouse.
2:00 P.M. —Bruce Curry, forum, Wo
men's Clubhouse.

Club will meet in the Parish House.
Charles J. Simon will speak.
6:00 P.M.—Unitarian Young People's
Society will be guests of the Chan
ning Club of the University of Cali
fornia, Bancroft and Dana Streets,
Berkeley. Cars from the campus will
accommodate those wishing to go
from Stanford.
9:30 P.M. —Bruce Curry, leading a
forum on student religious problems.
Sponsored by the Stanford Fellow
ship. Open to both men and women.
Women's Clubhouse.

sity in the Women's Clubhouse on

come.
