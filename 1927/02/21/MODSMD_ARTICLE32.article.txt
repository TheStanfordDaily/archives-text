MONDAY
12:45P.M. —There will be a meeting
of all freshmen on the editorial staff
in the Daily office today.
5:00 P.M. —Pi Lambda Theta in the
Women's Clubhouse. Dr. Maud A.
Merrill will speak on "Psychological
Work in Connection with the Juve
nile Court."
TUESDAY
3:00 P.M. —Colonial Party and exhibit
of hand loom articles from the
southern mountain states, in the
Women's Clubhouse. Hostesses:
Palo Alto Chapter of the Daughters
of the American Revolution. Small
admission charge.
WEDNESDAY
7:15 P.M. —Alpha Chi Sigma meets in
Conference Room of Chemistry
building. Very important.
3:00 P.M. —Resident Alumni Tea, in
the Women's Clubhouse. Hostesses:
Chapter D.D. of P.E.O. Sisterhood
of Palo Alto.
THURSDAY
4:15 P.M. —Organ Recital by Mr. Al
len. in the Memorial Church.
4:15 P.M. —University Philological As
sociation, in the Women's Club
house. Paper on "Classics and Our
Twentieth Century Poets," by Dr.
Henry Rushton Fairclough.
7:30 P.M. —Thursday evening confer
ence, at the home of Dr. Jordan.
Subject: "Why Jefferson Trembled."
8:00 P.M. —History Club, in the Wom
en's Clubhouse. Professor Ichihashi
will speak on "Art as an Aid to His
torical Research." Faculty members,
graduates, and seniors in the De
partments of History and Political
Science are invited to attend.
8:00 P.M. —Popular medical lecture in
Lane Hall, San Francisco. Subject:
"The Psychology of Disease Symp
toms," by Professor W. R. Miles.
8:15 P.M. —"Princess Ida," comic op
era by Gilbert and Sullivan, pre
sented by Warren I). Allen and
Gordon Davis under the direction of
the Dramatic Council. Tickets $1.00
and $1.25, at Crandall's, Palo Alto;
Old L T nion, Campus; and Sherman
& Clay, San Jose.
