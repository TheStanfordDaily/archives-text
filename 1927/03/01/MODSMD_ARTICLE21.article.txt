# U.S. POLICY TOWARD LATIN AMERICA TOPIC OF WOMEN'S DEBATE
## 
### 
Stanford women debaters will meet
a team from the University of Cali
fornia, Southern Branch, tomorrow
night at 7:30 o'clock, in the Little
Theatre, on the question, "Resolved:
That the policy of the United States
toward Latin America should be con
demned." Stanford will have the af
firmative.
Fifteen-minute speeches will be
given and a twelve-minute rebuttal
will be allowed each side. An audience
decision will be taken, and ballots will
be distributed before and after the
debate.
Rhoda Lewis, manager of Women's
Debate, with Lorraine Reeder, will
take the affirmative against the South
ern Branch team of Griselda Kuhlmau
and Genevieve Gemple. A negative
team composed of Carolyn Converse
and Katherine Drew will debate on
the subject at Mills College that night.
