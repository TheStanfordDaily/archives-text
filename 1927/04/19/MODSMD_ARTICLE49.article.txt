# SOPHOMORE TENNIS SOUAD WINS CLASS MATCHES FROM 1930
## 
### 
Members of the sophomore racket
team yesterday decisively took the
toll of the freshmen in the interclass
tennis tournament in the first matches
of the week's play. Two of the three
singles matches and all the doubles
were taken by the 1929 class players.
The freshmen were unable to meet j
the serves of the sophomores and rag- j
ged playing in general featured all !
the games. The last doubles match
was not finished.
The summary of the day's results
follow:
Singles—
Wheatley ('29) defeated Lee ('3O), j
8-10, 6-2, and 6-3.
Renzel ('29) defeated Richardson
(*3O), 6-2 and 6-1.
Nyquist ('3O) defeated West ('29),'
5-7, 6-4, and 6-3.
Doubles—
Renzel and ('29) defeated
Nyquist and Lee ('3O), 6-0 and 6-0.
Hall and Farwell ('29) defeated
Richardson and Smith ('3O), 6-4, 4-6, I
and 6-2.
Howard and West ('29) defeated
Sorenson and Perin C3O), 6-2 and 6-2. i
Godsol and Olsen ('29) playing Weyl
and Gilmore ('3O), failed to finish after \
each had one set to their credit.
