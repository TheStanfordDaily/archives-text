MONDAY
5:00 p.m.—Pi Lambda Theta will meet
in the Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m.—Education Club in the Wo
men's Clubhouse. President Wilbur
will speak on "The Junior College."
7:30 p.m.—Dr. Wilbur will speak on
"Junior Colleges" before a meeting
of Phi Delta Kappa in the Women's
Clubhouse.
TUESDAY
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Eugenics and
Civilization," by Professor S. J.
Holmes, of the Department of Zool
ogy, University of California, in the
Little Theatre. The public is invited
to attend.
8:00]). m. —Junipero Serra Club will
meet in the Women's Clubhouse.
WEDNESDAY
3:00 p.m.—Resident Alumnae Tea, in
the Women's Clubhouse. Hostesses:
The Young Women's Christian As
sociation.
4:15 p.m.—Conferences sur la Literra
ture Francaise. "Alfred de Vigny,"
by Professor Stanley A. Smitlj, in
Room 242, Romanic Languages
Building. The public, is invited.
5:30 p.m.—All members of the Wo
men's Glee Club will meet upstairs
in the Women's Clubhouse before
the Conference Party concert.
7:00 p.m.—Masonic Club degree team
will confer third degree at Old
Folks Home, Decoto. All Masons in
vited.
7:15 p.m.—University Orchestra will
meet in Room 84.
7:30 p.m.—Oratorio section rehearsal
for women's voices, with orchestra.
Will meet in Room 84.
7:45 p.m.—Tryouts for membership in
Euphronia Debating Society, in the
Lobby of the Old Union. Tryoutees
should be prepared to give five-min
ute talks on the proposal to abolish
the Lower Division at Stanford.
S:oop.m.—Chi Nu Kappa (Honorary
Classical), in the Women's Club
house. Professor B. O. Foster will
speak on "Reading and Writing
Aloud." Open meeting.

in the Memorial Church.
