# GRADUATE STUDENTS SOCIETY WILL HOLD SOCIAL CONFERENCE
## 
### 
The newly-organized Graduate Stu
dents' Club will hold its second meet
ing Thursday night at 7 o'clock in the
Women's Clubhouse, announces S.
Clark Cypher, member of the commit
tee In charge of the club's activities.
All graduate students are eligible
for membership in the organization.
The meeting Thursday will be chiefly
social. The Graduate Students' Club
lias been formed by a group of gradu
ates primarily to make up for the lack
of an adequate social organization
among the advanced students.
No officers have been chosen as yet
for the club, a loose committee struc
ture being planned until it becomes
well started.
