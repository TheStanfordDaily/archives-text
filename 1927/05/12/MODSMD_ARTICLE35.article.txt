# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
THURSDAY
THE BUSINESS MEETING of the
Sophomore Class has be,en indefi
nitely postponed.
10:00 A.M. —University Assembly, in
the Assembly Hall. Address on
"New Opportunities in Industry," by
Mrs. Lillian Gilbreth, consulting
management engineer.
1:45 P.M. —Board of Trustees, regular
monthly meeting, in the offices of
the Board, Balfour Building, San
Francisco.
4:15 P.M. —Organ Recital, by Mr. Al
len, in the Memorial Church.
6:45P.M. —First freshman class jolly
up of quarter to be held at Roble
Hall. All yearlings urged to be on
hand by class officers.
7:00 P.M. —The Graduate Students'
Club will hold a social meeting in
the Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 P.M. —Thursday evening confer
ence, at the home of Dr. David Starr
Jordan. Forum: "How It Looks to
Me."
7:30 P.M. —Oratorical Contest of the
Peninsula Debating League, under
the auspices of Euphronia Debating
Society, in the Little Theater.
7:30 P.M. — Geological and Mining
Smoker in the Old Union lobby. Ad
mission 50 cents.
8:00 P.M. —History Club, in the Wom
en's Clubhouse. Mr. David Harris
will speak. Open to faculty mem
bers, graduates, and seniors in the
Department of History.
