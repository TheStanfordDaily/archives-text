# Stanford University Weekly Calendar
## 
### 
Volume XLII, No. 5 PUBLISHED EVERY FRIDAY July 17-23
Sunday, July 17
11:00 a.m. DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The Reverend Charles R. Brown, Yale Divinity School.
Music:
Soprano solo, by Mrs. Orrin K. McMurray.
Anthem:
Lovely Appear (from"The Redemption") Gounod
7:30 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, "Contemporary Organ Music in Europe—Bonnet, Karg-Elert, Vierne, Widor," by
Mr. Warren D. Allen, University Organist, in the Memorial Church.
Monday, July 18
8:00 p.m. ILLUSTRATED LECTURE on "La Espana de Hoy," by Professor Tomas Navarro Tomas, Visiting
Professor of Romanic Languages, from Centro de Estudios Historicos, Madrid; in the Little Theatre.
Open to the public.
Tuesday, July 19
4:00 p.m. ROBLE INFORMAL TEA, for all students and faculty.
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, "Slavic and Scandinavian Music—Tschaikowsky, Borodin, Grieg, Sibelius," by Mr.
Allen, in the Memorial Church.
8:00 p.m. LECTURE on "Lincoln," by Professor Carl Russell Fish, of the Department of History, University of
Wisconsin; in the Little Theatre. The public is invited.
Wednesday, July 20
4:15 p.m. LECTURE on "Body and Mind," by Professor Kurt Koffka, Professor of Psychology, University of Gies
sen, Germany; in the Little Theatre. Open to the public.
8:00 p.m. LECTURE on "Problems of Learning," by Professor Koffka; in Room 414, Psychology Building. Open
to students interested in Psychology.
Thursday, July 21
4:15 p.m. LECTURE-RECITAL on"The Modern Organ, Its Structure and Its Possibilities," with illustrations from
current programs, by Mr. Allen; in the Organ Gallery, Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m. THURSDAY EVENING CONFERENCE, at the home of Dr. David Starr Jordan. Subject: "Theobald
Smith."
Saturday, July 23
8:30 a.m. AUTOMOBILE EXCURSION, to Big Basin. Leave from the Stanford Union, Main Entrance, return
ing about 5 p.m. Fare $3.00. Reservations must be made and fare deposited two days in advance, in
the office of the Director of the Summer Quarter, Room 173 Administration Building.
