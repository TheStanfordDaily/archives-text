Volume XLII, No. 6 PUBLISHED EVERY FRIDAY July 24-30
Sunday, July 24
11:00 a.m. DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The Reverend Charles R. Brown, Yale Divinity School.
Music:
Anthem, "In Heavenly Love Abiding" Parker
Soprano Solo, by Miss Catherine Urner
7:30 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Warren D. Allen, University Organist; in the Memorial Church.
Monday, July 25
8:00 p.m. ILLUSTRATED LECTURE on "La Espana de Hoy," by Professor Tomas Navarro Tomas, Visiting
Professor of Romanic Languages, from Centro de Estudios Historicos, Madrid; in the Little Theatre.
Open to the public.
Tuesday, July 26
4:00 p.m. ROBLE INFORMAL TEA, for all students and faculty.
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, "American Composers," by Mr. Allen, in the Memorial Church.
8:00 p.m. LECTURE, with readings, on"The Carolina Playmakers," by Professor .Frederick H. Koch, Professor of
Dramatic Literature, University of North Carolina; in the Little Theatre. The public is invited.
Wednesday, July 27
11:00 a.m. UNIVERSITY ASSEMBLY, in the Assembly Hall. Address on"The International Economic Confer
ence," by Dr. Alonzo E. Taylor, of the Food Research Institute, delegate from the United States to the
Conference. The public is invited.
4:15 p.m. LECTURE on"The Clowns of Shakespeare's Stage," by Professor Charles R. Baskervill, Professor of Eng
lish Literature at the University of Chicago; in the Little Theatre. Open to the public.
Thursday, July 28
4:00 p.m. PHILOLOGICAL ASSOCIATION, at the Women's Clubhouse. Papers by Mr. Hardin Craig and
Mr. John McClelland. ,
4:15 p.m. LECTURE-RECITAL on "Contemporary English and American Composers," by Mr. Warren D. Allen,
assisted by Mrs. Esther Houk Allen, Contralto, and Mr. Orley See, Violinist; in the Assembly Hall.
The public is invited.
7:30 p.m. THURSDAY EVENING CONFERENCE, at the home of Dr. David Starr Jordan. Subject: "World
Peace through Education."
Friday, July 29
8:15 p.m. "LOYALTIES," drama by John Galsworthy, presented by the Dramatic Council under the direction of
Gordon Davis, in the Assembly Hall. Tickets, 75c and $1 at the Old Union, Campus, and at Crandall's
in Palo Alto.
Saturday, July 30
8:30 a.m. AUTOMOBILE EXCURSION, to San Francisco, including Presidio, Golden Gate Park, Legion of Honor
Palace, and many other places of interest. Leave from the Stanford Union, Main Entrance, returning
about 4 p.m. Fare $2.50. Reservations must be made and fare deposited two days in advance, in the
office of the Director of the Summer Quarter, Room 173, Administration Building.

4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, "American Composers," by Mr. Allen, in the Memorial Church.
8:00 p.m. LECTURE, with readings, on"The Carolina Playmakers," by Professor .Frederick H. Koch, Professor of
Dramatic Literature, University of North Carolina; in the Little Theatre. The public is invited.
Wednesday, July 27
11:00 a.m. UNIVERSITY ASSEMBLY, in the Assembly Hall. Address on"The International Economic Confer
ence," by Dr. Alonzo E. Taylor, of the Food Research Institute, delegate from the United States to the
Conference. The public is invited.
4:15 p.m. LECTURE on"The Clowns of Shakespeare's Stage," by Professor Charles R. Baskervill, Professor of Eng
lish Literature at the University of Chicago; in the Little Theatre. Open to the public.
Thursday, July 28
4:00 p.m. PHILOLOGICAL ASSOCIATION, at the Women's Clubhouse. Papers by Mr. Hardin Craig and
Mr. John McClelland. ,
4:15 p.m. LECTURE-RECITAL on "Contemporary English and American Composers," by Mr. Warren D. Allen,
assisted by Mrs. Esther Houk Allen, Contralto, and Mr. Orley See, Violinist; in the Assembly Hall.
The public is invited.
7:30 p.m. THURSDAY EVENING CONFERENCE, at the home of Dr. David Starr Jordan. Subject: "World
Peace through Education."
Friday, July 29
8:15 p.m. "LOYALTIES," drama by John Galsworthy, presented by the Dramatic Council under the direction of
Gordon Davis, in the Assembly Hall. Tickets, 75c and $1 at the Old Union, Campus, and at Crandall's
in Palo Alto.
Saturday, July 30
8:30 a.m. AUTOMOBILE EXCURSION, to San Francisco, including Presidio, Golden Gate Park, Legion of Honor
Palace, and many other places of interest. Leave from the Stanford Union, Main Entrance, returning
about 4 p.m. Fare $2.50. Reservations must be made and fare deposited two days in advance, in the
office of the Director of the Summer Quarter, Room 173, Administration Building.
