# Stanford University Weekly Calendar
## 
### 
Volume XLII, No. 7 PUBLISHED EVERY FRIDAY July 31-August 6
Sunday, July 31
11:00 a.m. DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The Reverend W. W. Willard, Claremont, California.
Duet:
"I Waited for the Lord" (from the "Hymn of Praise") Mendelssohn
Miss Verda Hogan
Mrs. Gladys Kofoid
Anthem:
"The God of Abraham Praise" Noble
7:30 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, "American Composers," by Mr. Warren D. Allen, University Organist; in the Me
morial Church.
Tuesday, August 2
4:00 p.m. ROBLE INFORMAL TEA, for all students and faculty.
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, Request Program, by Mr. Allen, in the Memorial Church.
8:00 p.m. LECTURE on "Aesthetic Experience in Art and in Nature," by Professor Louis A. Reid, of the University
of Liverpool, England, Acting Professor of Philosophy; in the Little Theatre. The public is invited.
Wednesday, August 3
4:15 p.m. LECTURE on "Tendencies in Contemporary Literature," by Professor J. Duncan Spaeth, Professor of
English Literature at Princeton University; in the Little Theatre. Open to the public.
Thursday, August 4
7:30 p.m. THURSDAY EVENING CONFERENCE, at the home of Dr. David Starr Jordan. Subject: "Fevers and
the Mosquito-fish."
8:00 p.m. THREE ONE-ACT PLAYS, "When the Moon Falls," by Webster Street; "Finders Keepers," by George
Kelly; "Rivals," by Evelyn Brownell. Presented by Public Speaking 118a, Reading and Staging Plays;
in the Little Theatre. Open to the public.
Friday, August 5
8:30 p.m. RECITAL, by Arthur Loesser, Pianist; in the Basketball Pavilion. Open to the public without admission
charge.
Saturday, August 6
4:00 p.m. AUTOMOBILE EXCURSION, to Lick Observatory, Mt. Hamilton. Leave from the Stanford Union,
Main Entrance, returning about midnight. F are $3. Reservations must be made and fare deposited two
days in advance, in the office of the Director of the Summer Quarter, Room 173, Administration Building.
