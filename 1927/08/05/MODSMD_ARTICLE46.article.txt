# Stanford University Weekly Calendar
## 
### 
Volume XLII, No. 8 PUBLISHED EVERY FRIDAY August 7-13
Sunday, August 7
11:00 a.m. DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The Reverend W. W. Williard, Claremont, California.
Anthems:
"Consider and Hear Me" Pflueger
Miss Carol Lasater
Mr. Walter Kaulfers, Soloists
"O Bone Jesu" Palestrina
7:30 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Warren D. Allen, University Organist; in the Memorial Church
Monday, August 8
1:00 p.m. SEAT SALE for "The Angel In the House," at the Old Union, Campus, and New Stanford Theatre,
Palo Alto.
7:30 p.m. ENGLISH REVIEW CLUB, at the home of Professor Arthur G. Kennedy, 435 Coleridge Avenue, Palo
Alto. All graduate students in English are welcome.
8:00 p.m. THREE ONE-ACT PLAYS, "The Marriage Cake," "Four Women," "The Wares Never Did So," by
Elene Wilbur, presented under the direction of Miss Dorothea Wilson; in the Little Theatre. The
public is invited.
Tuesday, August 9
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Wallace Rolls, Acting Organist; in the Memorial Church.
8:00 p.m. LECTURE on "Contemporary Treatment of Ancient Themes," by Professor Hardin Craig, of the Uni
versity of lowa, Acting Professor of English; in the Little Theatre. The public is invited.
Wednesday, August 10
4:15 p.m. LECTURE on "Les Lettres frangaises en Belgique," by Professor Gustave Charlier, of the University of
Brussels, Belgium, Acting Professor of Romanic Languages; in the Little Theatre. Open to the public.
Thursday, August 11
1:45 p.m. BOARD OF TRUSTEES, regular meeting in the offices of the Board, Balfour Building, San Francisco.
7:30 p.m. THURSDAY EVENING CONFERENCE, at the home of Dr. David Starr Jordan. Subject: "Henry
Ford and THE OSCAR II."
8:00 p.m. CERCLE FRANCAIS, at the Women's Clubhouse. Professor Albert L. Guerard will speak on "L'Avenir
de Paris: Problemes d'urbanisme." Persons interested in French are cordially invited.
Friday, August 12
8:15 p.m. "THE ANGEL IN THE HOUSE," Comedy by Eden Phillpotts and Basil Mac Donald Hastings, presented
by Dramatic Council under the direction of Gordon Davis, at the New Stanford Theatre, Palo Alto.
Saturday, August 13
8:00 a.m. AUTOMOBILE EXCURSION, to Santa Cruz. Leave from the Stanford Union, Main Entrance, return
ing about 4 p.m. Fare $2.50. Reservations must be made and fare deposited two days in advance, in the
office of the Director of the summer quarter, Room 173 Administration Building.
