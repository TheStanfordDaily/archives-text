Volume XLII, No. 9 • PUBLISHED EVERY FRIDAY August 14-21
Sunday, August 14
11:00 a.m. DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The Reverend W. W. Willard, Claremont, California.
Anthems:
"I Sought the Lord" * Stevenson
Miss Carol Lasater, Contralto Soloist
"The God of Abraham Praise" Noble
7:30 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Wallace Rolls, Acting Organist; in the Memorial Church.
Tuesday, August 16
4:00 p.m. ROBLE INFORMAL TEA, for all students and faculty.
4:15 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Rolls, in the Memorial Church.
8:00 p.m.. LECTURE on "Democracy and Education," by Dr. Alexander Meiklejohn, Professor of Philosophy, Uni
versity of Wisconsin; in the Basketball Pavilion. The public is invited.
Wednesday, August 17
4:15 p.m. LECTURE on"A Vindication of Anatole France," by Professor Albert L. Guerard, Professor of General
Literature; in the Little Theatre. Open to the public.
Thursday, August 18
7:30 p.m. THURSDAY EVENING CONFERENCE, at the home of Dr. David Starr Jordan. Subject: "The
Peace Conference at Toronto."
8:00 p.m. PHILOLOGICAL ASSOCIATION, at the Women's Clubhouse. Addresses by Professor Navarro Tomas
and Professor Gustave Charlier; short reports by Professor W. L. Schwartz and Miss Leah Dennis.
Friday, August 19
8:15 p.m. "THE CRADLE SONG," by G. Martinez Sierra, presented by the class in Reading and Staging Plays,
under the direction of Mrs. Margaret Shedd Kisich; in the Union Court. Open to the public.
Sunday, August 21
11:00 a.m. DIVINE WORSHIP AND SERMON. Preacher: The Reverend Leslie E. Learned, D.D., All Saints
Church, Pasadena.
Chorale:
"Now Let Thy Gracious Spirit Shine" Bach
Anthem
"O, All Ye Nations" Schiitz
7:30 p.m. ORGAN RECITAL, by Mr. Rolls, in the Memorial Church.
OFFICIAL NOTICES
