WEDNESDAY
12:45 p.m.—Senior Class meeting in
Room 214. First meeting of the year
and all members are urged to be
present by Bob King, president of
the Class of 1928.
2:00 p.m. — Debate Tryouts for the
Cambridge and California debates,
in the Little Theater. Subject: "Re
solved, That the freshman and
sophomore years at Stanford and the
University of California be abol
ished."
7:00 p.m.—Meeting of the Stanford
Women's Glee Club upstairs in the
Women's Clubhouse. Social lialf
liour will follow the song practice.
7:15 p.m.—U.S C. football rally.
7:30 p.m.—Zoology Club, at the home
of Professor Snyder, 8 Alvarado
Row. Dr. Weymouth will speak.
8:00 p.m.—English Club, in Women's
Clubhouse. Dr. Albert Guerard, Pro
fessor of General Literature, will
speak on"The Art of James Joyce,
or Chaos As a Literary Technique."
8:00 p.m.—The Stanford Radio Club
will meet in the club shack, which
is located in the corporation yard
just back of the fire house. Every
one is welcome.
8:00 p.m.—Research Club, at the Fac
ulty Clubhouse. Papers: "The Role
of the Anterior Pituitary in the De
velopment and Regulation of the
Genital System," by Professor E. T.
Engle; and"The Significant Aspects
of the Agricultural Problem," by
Professor J. S. Davis.

o'clock tomorrow night on account
of the U.S.C. Rally.
