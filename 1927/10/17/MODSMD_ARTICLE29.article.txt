# Getting It Straight
## 
### 
Questions may be mailed to the "In
formation Editor," left at the Daily
office, or phoned to the paper at PA
1074. They need not be signed, but
must concern University or student
affairs.

Question: What is the mail sched
ule on the campus?
Answer by W. C. Werry, post
master:
The heaviest incoming mails are at
7:30 in the morning and 12:30 in the
afternoon. Mail also comes in from
the southern states and Southern Cali
fornia at 10:30 a.m.
About an hour and a half is required
in sorting each mail to the boxes and
general delivery. The best times
therefore to call for mail are after 9
a.m., noon, and 2 p.m.
On Sundays and holidays there is
only one incoming mail, at 7:30 a.m.,
and one dispatch from the campus, at
G a.m. However, at the downtown Palo
office there is a dispatch which closes
at 5:50 p.m.
The mails close at the campus post
office as follows:
6:00 a.m. —North, south, east, and
Palo Alto.
1:00 a.m. —Peninsula and Bay re
gion and Palo Alto.
1:00' p.m.—North, south, east, and
Palo Alto.
4:45 p.m.—North, south, east, and
Palo Alto.
Sundays and holidays:
Mail arrives at 7:30 a.m. from all
points.
Closes at 6:00 a.m. for all points.
Mail arrives on the campus as fol
lows:
7:30 a.m. —North, south, east, and
Palo Alto.
10:30 a.m. —South, and Southern
California.
12:30 p.m.—North, east, and Palo
Alto.
4:00 p.m.—Palo Alto.
5:00 p.m.—Bay cities and Palo Alto.
Air mail leaves San Francisco at
7 a.m. It closes at the campus office
with the regular 4:45 p.m. dispatch.
The 6:08 p.m. to San Francisco car
ries a Railway Post-Office car and in
emergency, letters may be mailed on
this car at the Southern Pacific depot
for the next morning air mail and for
other first-class mail to points north
and east.
Special delivery mail will be deliv
ered to halls, houses, and residences,
and should be so addressed.
Questions may be mailed to the "In
formation Editor," left at the Daily
office, or phoned to the paper at PA
1074. They need not be signed, but
must concern University or student
affairs.

Mail arrives at 7:30 a.m. from all
points.
Closes at 6:00 a.m. for all points.
Mail arrives on the campus as fol
lows:
7:30 a.m. —North, south, east, and
Palo Alto.
10:30 a.m. —South, and Southern
California.
12:30 p.m.—North, east, and Palo
Alto.
4:00 p.m.—Palo Alto.
5:00 p.m.—Bay cities and Palo Alto.
Air mail leaves San Francisco at
7 a.m. It closes at the campus office
with the regular 4:45 p.m. dispatch.
The 6:08 p.m. to San Francisco car
ries a Railway Post-Office car and in
emergency, letters may be mailed on
this car at the Southern Pacific depot
for the next morning air mail and for
other first-class mail to points north
and east.
Special delivery mail will be deliv
ered to halls, houses, and residences,
and should be so addressed.
Questions may be mailed to the "In
formation Editor," left at the Daily
office, or phoned to the paper at PA
1074. They need not be signed, but
must concern University or student
affairs.

Question: What is the mail sched
ule on the campus?
Answer by W. C. Werry, post
master:
The heaviest incoming mails are at
7:30 in the morning and 12:30 in the
afternoon. Mail also comes in from
the southern states and Southern Cali
fornia at 10:30 a.m.
About an hour and a half is required
in sorting each mail to the boxes and
general delivery. The best times
therefore to call for mail are after 9
a.m., noon, and 2 p.m.
On Sundays and holidays there is
only one incoming mail, at 7:30 a.m.,
and one dispatch from the campus, at
G a.m. However, at the downtown Palo
office there is a dispatch which closes
at 5:50 p.m.
The mails close at the campus post
office as follows:
6:00 a.m. —North, south, east, and
Palo Alto.
1:00 a.m. —Peninsula and Bay re
gion and Palo Alto.
1:00' p.m.—North, south, east, and
Palo Alto.
4:45 p.m.—North, south, east, and
Palo Alto.
