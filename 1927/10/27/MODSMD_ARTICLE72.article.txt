# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
Notices for this column should be handed in
at the Stanfokb Daily office by 9 o'clock of
the evening preceding publication.
THURSDAY
12:15 p.m.—Sophomore Cabinet will
meot in the Women's Clubhouse for
lunoh.
,1:00 p.m.—Theta Sigma Phi will meet
' upstairs in the Press Building to
discuss plans for their tea for the
delegates of the C.S.P.A. Very im
portant.
3:30 p.m.—Specialty tryout for Foot
ball Gaieties in Little Theatre.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Mr. Allen
in the Memorial Church.
4:15 p.m. —University Philological As
sociation meeting in the Women's
Clubhouse.
7:3op.m.—Thursday Evening Confer
ence at the home of Dr. David Starr
Jordan. Subject: "The Matterhorn."
7:45 p.m.—Euphronia will meet in the
lobby of the Old Union. Subject for
debate: "Resolved, That This
Houso Favors The Principle of the
Intermarriage of Races." Tryouts
for new members will be held after
the meeting.
FRIDAY
8:20 p.m.—"The Lady of the Rose" by
Martin Flavin, presented by Sword
and Sandals, direction of Anna May
Price. Tickets on sale at Crandall's,
Palo Alto, and at the Old Union.
Price $1.00 and 75 cents.
SATURDAY
2:30 p.m. — Intercollegiate Football,
University of Oregon vs. Stanford
in the stadium.
MONDAY
5:15 p.m.—All staff members and try
outeea for the Stanford Literary
Magazine will meet at the office of
the publication.

VARSITY BASKETBALL MANAGER

desiring to try out for junior man
agerships may do so b/ reporting to
him Thursday or Monday afternoon
at 4 o'clock.
