### 
The first informal party to further
cooperation between the freshman
groups of the Y.M.C.A. and the
Y.W.C.A. will be Riven by the Y.M.C.A.
on Armistice Day, November 11, at
the homo of Trofessor and Mrs. Rob
ert E. Swain, according to Fred Bur
lew, chairman of the Frosh "Y" So
cial Committee. A representative
group of thirty freshman men and
women will be invited.
The event is the forerunner of sev
eral Joint meetings to be held later
in the quarter when the men and
women will hold round-table discus
sions upon the various problems aris
ing in the work.
