TUESDAY
12:00 noon—Meeting of the Fellow
ship Board in the lobby of the New
Union.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
4:30 p.m.—Graduate Women's Club
tea at the Women's Clubhouse. All
graduate women welcome.
6:00 p.m.—Social Committee of the
Y.W.C.A. will meet in the Women's
Clubhouse.
6:00 p.m.—Senior Y.W.C.A. cabinet
will meet for supper upstairs in the
Women's Clubhouse.
6:3op.m.—Members of the De Molay,
active and past, will meet in Room
208 with the Masonic Club.
7:15 p.m.—W.A.A. will meet in the
Council Room of the Women's Club
house.
7:15 p.m.—Alpha Chi Sigma will meet
in the Conference Room.
7:30 a.m. to 5 p.m.—Payment due on
syllabus and incidental fees at the
cashier's office in the Administra
tion building. Fine of $2 will be at
tached to late payments.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture recital by Bruce C.
Buttles in the Assembly Hall. The
second of a series of three lectures
on "Figures and Tendencies in Con
temporary Music"; with piano and
electrola.
WEDNESDAY
12:00 noon — International Luncheon
Club will meet in New Union. All
interested are invited.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
I). Allen in the Memorial Church.
7:00 p.m.—Women's Glee Club will
meet upstairs in the Women's Club
house.
7:30 p.m.—Research Club in the Fac
ulty Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m.—German Club in the Wom
en's Clubhouse. Surprise program.
7:30 p.m.—American Society of Mech
anical Engineers will hold an im
portant business meeting in the
elubrooms. All members should be
present.
7:3op.m.—Radio Club will meet in
Room 268 of the Engineering build
ing. Pierce E. Warrington will
speak on radio manufacturing by
the Westinghouse Company. The
meeting is open to everyone.
7:30 p.m.—Rifle Club meeting in Room
231.
7:30 p.m.—lllustrated lecture by Mme.
Aline Caro Delvaille in the Little
Theater. Subject: "The French Ca
thedrals."
THURSDAY
i1:00 p.m.—Scat sale for "Football
Gaieties" to be presented Friday,
November 18, under the auspices of
Ram's Head Society; in the lobby
of the Old Union.
6:45 p.m.—Freshman Y.W.C.A. discus
sion groups will meet to discuss
"Friendship."
7:00 p.m.—Sophomore Y.W.C.A. cabi
net will meet upstairs in the Wom
en's Clubhouse.

the Assembly Hall. Subject: "Inter
national Problems Confronting the

invited.

Christian, organist of the University
of Michigan, in the Memorial
Church.
