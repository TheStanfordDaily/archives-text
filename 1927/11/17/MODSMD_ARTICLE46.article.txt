Notices for this column should be handed in
it the Stanford Daily office by 9 o'clock of
he evening preceding publication.
THURSDAY
2:00 to 4:00 p.m.—Continuation of
Tuesday's debate tryouts.
3:15 p.m.—Stanford Dames meet in
the Women's Clubhouse. Dr. Almack
will speak on "World Peace."
4:15 p.m. — Lecture by Professor
Hight in the Little Theatre. Sub
ject: "The Native Problem and Its
Solution in New Zealand." The phb
lic is invited.
6:45 p.m. — Freshman Discussion
groups of the Y.W.C.A. will meet.
7:00 p.m.—Sophomore Y.W.C.A. Cabi
net will meet.
7:00 p.m.— Stanford Women's Glee
Club will meet upstairs in the Wo
men's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m.—Ushers report in Assembly
Hall for Peninsula Concert.
8:15 p.m.—Concert by Sophie Bras
lau, Contralto, in Assembly Hall.
FRIDAY
8:15 p.m.—"Big Game Gaieties" in As
sembly Hall. „
7:30 p.m.—Those ushers who have
registered with Tom Drake report
to Assembly Hall for Big Game
Gaieties.
SATURDAY
2:30 p.m. — Intercollegiate Football.
University of California vs. Stan
ford, in the Stadium.
MONDAY
Ito 5 p.m.—Cotillion bid sale for
sophomores only in Old Union.
MISCELLANEOUS
TWENTY MEN WANTED to sell
Chaparrals at the Big Game on
commission. See Dick Guggenhime,
business manager, or Joe Carroii,
circulation manager, at the Chapar
ral office.
