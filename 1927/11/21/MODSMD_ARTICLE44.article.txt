# ANNOUNCEMENTS
##

Notices for this column should be handed in
it the Stanford Daily office by 9 o'clock of
he evening preceding publication.
MONDAY
12:15 pm.—Campus DeMolays and
past DeMolays invited to lunch with
Masonic Club in the Union.
1 to 5 p.m.—Cotillion bid sale for
sophomores only in Old Union.
3:30 p.m.—All men interested in
weight basketball meet in Encina
gym.
3:oU p.m.—Frederick Murray will talk
in Graphic Art department, Room
311, on the making of his sport il
lustrations. The public is invited.
7:00 p.m.—Freshmen interested in de
bating will meet in Room 201 to se
lect a question for tryouts for Cali
fornia, College of Pacitic, and Ne
vada freshman debates.
8:00 p.m.—Women's Debate, Univer
sity of California vs. Stanford, in
the Little Theatre. Subject: "Re
solved, That this house disapproves
of nationalism."
8:00 p.m.—The Education Club will
meet in the Women's Clubhouse.
Dean Elwood P. Cubberley will
speak on "The Professional Study
of Education." All interested are
invited to attend.
TUESDAY
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital, by Warren
D. Allen, in the Memorial Church.
7:00 p-m.—Sophomore Class meets in
the basement of the Women's Club
house.
7:00 p.m.—Women's choir rehearsal
will be held in the Memorial Church.
7:15 p.m.—W.A.A. Board will meet up
stairs in the Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m.—Concert, by Miss Marion
Nicholson, violinist, and William
l'ilcher, tenor, in the Little Theatre.
The public is invited.
8:00p.m.—Full choir rehearsal in the
Memorial Church.
8:30 p.m. — Euphronia Debating So
ciety will hold an open meeting in
the lobby of the Old Union. Bert W.
Levit will speak on "Issues and
Candidates of the 1928 Presidential
Election."
WEDNESDAY
12:15 to 12:45 p.m. — Cosmopolitan
Club will hold its International
Luncheon at the Union. All inter
ested are invited to attend.
8:00 p.m. — Science Association will
meet in the Faculty Clubhouse.

past DeMolays invited to lunch with
Masonic Club in the Union.
1 to 5 p.m.—Cotillion bid sale for
sophomores only in Old Union.
3:30 p.m.—All men interested in
weight basketball meet in Encina
gym.
3:oU p.m.—Frederick Murray will talk
in Graphic Art department, Room
311, on the making of his sport il
lustrations. The public is invited.
7:00 p.m.—Freshmen interested in de
bating will meet in Room 201 to se
lect a question for tryouts for Cali
fornia, College of Pacitic, and Ne
vada freshman debates.
8:00 p.m.—Women's Debate, Univer
sity of California vs. Stanford, in
the Little Theatre. Subject: "Re
solved, That this house disapproves
of nationalism."
8:00 p.m.—The Education Club will
meet in the Women's Clubhouse.
Dean Elwood P. Cubberley will
speak on "The Professional Study
of Education." All interested are
invited to attend.
TUESDAY
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital, by Warren
D. Allen, in the Memorial Church.
7:00 p-m.—Sophomore Class meets in
the basement of the Women's Club
house.
7:00 p.m.—Women's choir rehearsal
will be held in the Memorial Church.
7:15 p.m.—W.A.A. Board will meet up
stairs in the Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m.—Concert, by Miss Marion
Nicholson, violinist, and William
l'ilcher, tenor, in the Little Theatre.
The public is invited.
8:00p.m.—Full choir rehearsal in the
Memorial Church.
8:30 p.m. — Euphronia Debating So
ciety will hold an open meeting in
the lobby of the Old Union. Bert W.
Levit will speak on "Issues and
Candidates of the 1928 Presidential
Election."
WEDNESDAY
12:15 to 12:45 p.m. — Cosmopolitan
Club will hold its International
Luncheon at the Union. All inter
ested are invited to attend.
8:00 p.m. — Science Association will
meet in the Faculty Clubhouse.
