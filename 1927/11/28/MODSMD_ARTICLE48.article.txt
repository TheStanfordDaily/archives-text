MONDAY
12:15 p.m.—Stanford Masonic Club in
vites all Master Masons to luncheon
in the Union.

Squad debates, in the Little Thea
tre. Subject: "Resolved, That the
results of the Great War have
tended toward the peace of the
world."

Lew, Dean of the School of Religion,
Yenclieng University, Peking, in the
Little Theatre. First of three lec
tures on "Old and New China."

committee chairmen will meet in
the Press Building to discuss class
plans.
TUESDAY
12 noon —Theta Sigma Phi will meet
for lunch at the Union. Business i
meeting immediately afterwards.

Ruth L. Savage, in the Memorial
Church.

Little Theatre.
WEDNESDAY
4 to 6 p.m.—Dean Mary Yost will be
at home informally to the women
of the University.

fessor Joseph Bedier, in the Little
Theatre; under the auspices of the
French Club. Subject: "The French
Academy."
7:30 p.m.—Lecture by Ruhi Effendi,
in the Little Theatre, under the
auspices of the Stanford Fellow
ship. Subje.ct, "The Bahai Move
ment."
7:30 p.m.—Zoology Club will meet in
Room 341, Geology building. Mrs.
Oldroyd will speak.
7:45 p.m.—Debate in the lobby of the
Old Union. University of California
Congress vs. Euphronia. Subject:
"Resolved, That life is worth liv
ing."
THURSDAY
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital, by Warren
D. Allen, in the Memorial Church.

Little Theatre.

-7:30 p.m. — Intercollegiate Debate,

solved, That this house regrets the

the Faculty Clubhouse.
