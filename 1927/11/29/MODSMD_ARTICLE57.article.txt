TUESDAY
12 noon—Theta Sigma Phi will meet
for lunch at the Union. Business
meeting immediately afterwards.
12:45 p.m.—Stanford Daily staff meets
in the Daily office. All members of
the editorial staff must be present. ,
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital, by Miss
Ruth L. Savage, in the Memorial j
Church.

Little Theatre.

mittee meets in the Trophy lloom
of the Old Union.

meet in the Chemistry Building.
WEDNESDAY
12:15 p.m.—Cosmopolitan Club will
hold international luncheon in din
| ing room of Stanford Union. All
i interested in club invited.

at home informally to the women
of the University.

fessor Joseph Bedier, in the Little
Theatre; under the auspices of the
French Club. Subject: "The French
Academy."
4:15 p.m.—Joseph Bedier will speak
on the French Academy in the Lit
tle Theatre.
4:15 p.m.—Varsity and freshman soc
cer squads will be photographed on
the soccer field for the Quad.
7:00 p.m.—Sophomore Jolly-Up in the
Women's Clubhouse.
7:15 p.m.—The Business Staff of the
Stanford Daily will meet at the
Daily office. All members must at
tend.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture by Ruhi Effendi,
in the Little Theatre, under the
auspices of the Stanford Fellow
ship. Subject, "The Bahai Move
ment."
7:30 p.m.—Zoology Club will meet in
Room 341, Geology building. Mrs.
Oldroyd will speak.
7:45 p.m.—Debate in the lobby of the
Old Union. University of California
Congress vs. Euplironia. Subject:
"Resolved, That life is worth liv
ing."
THURSDAY

more play, "Expressing Willie," will

Little Theatre.

Class, followed by a meeting of
class officers and committee mem
bers in the Little Theatre.
FRIDAY

Cambridge University vs. Stanford,
in the Assembly Hall. Subject: "Re
solved, That this house regrets the
