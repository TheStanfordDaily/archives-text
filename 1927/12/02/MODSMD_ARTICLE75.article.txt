Notices for this column should be handed in
t the Stanford Daily office by 9 o'clock of
he evening preceding publication.
FRIDAY
9:00 a.m.—University assembly. J.
Alfred Spender, British journalist,
will speak.
3:15 p.m.—The Literary Board of the
Stanford Literary Magazine will
meet in the A.S.S.U. office in the
Old Union.
7:30 p.m. — Intercollegiate Debate,
Cambridge University vs. Stanford,
in the Assembly Hall. Subject: "Re
solved, That this house regrets the
tendency toward easier divorce."
8 p. m.—Faculty Club will meet at
the Faculty Clubhouse.
SATURDAY
3:00 p.m.—lnternational Student Ser
vice bridge tea in the Women's Club
house under the auspices of the
Y.VV.C.A. Tickets 50c.
SUNDAY
7:30 p.m.—Humanist's Club of the
Unitarian Church meets in the
Church hall at Cowper and Chan
ning streets. Ruhi Effendi to speak
on "What is the Bahai Movement?"
MISCELLANEOUS
NEW YEAR'S DAY game general
ticket applications may be obtained
Tuesday and Wednesday, upon pre
sentation of student body cards, at
the Old Union from 1 to 5:30 o'clock
in the afternoons. These applica
tions, when filled out, are to be
mailed to the Pasadena address
stated in the blank. Rooters' tickets
will be sold here probably on De
cember 7 and 8.
THOSE NOT RECEIVING the first
issue of the Stanford Literary Maga
zine are asked to notify the circula
tion manager, Box 2.
ANYONE WISHING TO SELL pro
grams at the Pasadena game, Janu
ary 2, see Murray Draper, 319 En
cina. A commission of two cents per
program will be paid, with a $1
bonus for 300 sales and an addition
al bonus for each 100 over that
figure.

as policemen or traffic directors at
the Rose Bowl on New Year's Day
in Pasadena apply to Tom Joaquin,
Delta Chi house. Phone Palo Alto
1043 or address Box 1304, Campus.

MISCELLANEOUS
NEW YEAR'S DAY game general
ticket applications may be obtained
Tuesday and Wednesday, upon pre
sentation of student body cards, at
the Old Union from 1 to 5:30 o'clock
in the afternoons. These applica
tions, when filled out, are to be
mailed to the Pasadena address
stated in the blank. Rooters' tickets
will be sold here probably on De
cember 7 and 8.
THOSE NOT RECEIVING the first
issue of the Stanford Literary Maga
zine are asked to notify the circula
tion manager, Box 2.
ANYONE WISHING TO SELL pro
grams at the Pasadena game, Janu
ary 2, see Murray Draper, 319 En
cina. A commission of two cents per
program will be paid, with a $1
bonus for 300 sales and an addition
al bonus for each 100 over that
figure.
ALL THOSE DESIRING TO WORK
as policemen or traffic directors at
the Rose Bowl on New Year's Day
in Pasadena apply to Tom Joaquin,
Delta Chi house. Phone Palo Alto
1043 or address Box 1304, Campus.
MEN NOW LIVING IN ENCINA
HALL who wish to reserve their

rooms for winter quarter should
call at the Dean of Men's office
Monday, Tuesday, or Wednesday,
December 5, 6, or 7, and pay de
posits.
Notices for this column should be handed in
t the Stanford Daily office by 9 o'clock of
he evening preceding publication.
FRIDAY
9:00 a.m.—University assembly. J.
Alfred Spender, British journalist,
will speak.
3:15 p.m.—The Literary Board of the
Stanford Literary Magazine will
meet in the A.S.S.U. office in the
Old Union.
7:30 p.m. — Intercollegiate Debate,
Cambridge University vs. Stanford,
in the Assembly Hall. Subject: "Re
solved, That this house regrets the
tendency toward easier divorce."
8 p. m.—Faculty Club will meet at
the Faculty Clubhouse.
