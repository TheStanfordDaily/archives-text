FRIDAY
12:00 m. —Luncheon for Henry Pitt
Van Dusen at the Madrona Dining
Hall, being given under the auspices
of the Stanford Y.W.C.A. and
Y.M.C.A.
12:15 to 2:00 p.m.—Quad photographs
of all groups today except eating
clubs and halls are to be taken in
the inner court of the Union, unless
special arrangements have been
made. Following is the schedule:
Chinese Club 12:15
Kappa Sigma 12:30
Branner Club 12:45
Freshman Golf Squad 1:30
Varsity Golf Squad 1:45
Skull and Snakes 2:00
9:00 to 12:00 a.m.; 1:30 to 4:30 p.m.
—Distribution of tickets for ora
torio to students and faculty mem
bers.
SUNDAY
6:15 p.m.—Regular meeting of the
Stanford Episcopal Club in the
Parish House. Professor Ralph H.
Lutz of the History department will
be the speaker. Everyone invited.
6:45 p.m. — Professor Yamato Ichi
hashi will speak to members of the
♦ Encina Club in the Encina Lobby.
MONDAY
12:00 —Stanford Masonic Club lunch
eon. All Masons and DeMolays are
invited to attend.

meet in the Women's Clubhouse to
make plans for the quarter's activi
ties.
TUESDAY
12:45 p.m.—Senior Class meeting in
Room 214.
2:00 o m.—Stanford Journalists CJub
will meet at the Daily Office "for

a tea in the Women's Clubhouse.

WEDNESDAY

pha Chi Sigma and Phi Lambda

ford Masonic Club at Alpha Kappa
Lambda house.
MISCELLANEOUS
4:00 p.m.—Tuesdays and Thursdays
of the winter quarter a swimming
class for faculty and outside women
will be offered at Roble Gym.
SOPHOMORES wishing to try out for
TRACK MANAGER are asked to
report on the track any day after 4
o'clock. Tryoutees may get physical
education credit by working out in
the gym for twenty minutes, three
times a week.

a.m. to 12 noon. Registration of new
members for choir and oratorio un
der Mr. Allen in Room 84, Inner
Quad.

M&M 105, Pyrometallurgy of Iron
and Steel, will be held Thursday,
January 26, at 4 o'clock, in Rojom
550, Mining Building.
