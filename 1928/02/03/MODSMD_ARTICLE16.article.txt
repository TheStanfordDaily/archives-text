# ADDITIONAL TICKETS ARE NOW AVAILABLE FOR GIGLI'S CONCERT
## 
### 
Because of the infantile paralysis
scare an additional number of tickets
to the concert to be given by Benia
mino Gigli on February 9 is now
available to campus music patrons.
Although the danger from exposure
to the disease is very slight at a con
cert, it is expected that many off
campus patrons will be kept away.
Beniamino Gigli, who will sing in
the Stanford Pavilion under the aus
pices of the Associated Students, is
the leading tenor of the Metropolitan
Opera Company, and is considered to
be the greatest tenor since Caruso.
He is the highest priced artist on the
A.S.S.U. Concert Series this season.
Tickets may be obtained in the
lobby of the Old Union at $1.00 and
$1.50 for balcony, and $2.00 and $2.50
for orchestra, seats. A few season
tickets for the remaining three con
certs are still obtainable at the spe
cial rate of $2.25.
