# M'ELVENNY DEFENDS VARSITY RANK; BABE GAMES PLAYED OFF
## 
### 
Ralph McElvenny successfully de-'
fended his third place on the varsity
tennis squad yesterday, winning two
straight sets from Goraer Thomas,
6-1, 6-3.
McElvenny placed aggressively
from the start, putting Thomas for
the most part on the defensive.
Beginning next Monday two |
matches will be scheduled each week.
During the first half of the week Her- ,
rington and McElvenny will play for
second place, and Hall and Thomas
will play for fourth.
Results of freshman matches are:
Ranney took ninth place from Dodd,
1-6, 6-0, 11-9; Harrington retained
twelfth place against Power, 6-3, 5-7,
8-6; Bailey beat Baumgarten out of
eighteenth place in two straight sets; j
and Best kept twentieth place in a '
match with Cook, 5-7, 7-5, 8-6. In i
doubles, Gantner and Forbes downed J
Wilson and Dodd, 7-5, 6-3, to retain j
third place.
Today's schedule is: varsity—Ren-1
zel vs. Driscoll, and Castellanos vs. j
Sperry; freshman — Cameron vs. I
Thomas at 3:30 o'clock and Baumgar- '
ten vs. Best at 2 o'clock.
