# VARSITY, FRESHMEN TENNIS TEAMS DOWN ST. IGNATIUS SQUADS
## 
### 
Winning all ten matches in straight
sets, tlie varsity and freshman tennis
teams made a clean sweep of the
tournament with St. Ignatius College
racketers here Saturday in the first
contest, of the 1928 season. The Cardi
nal varsity allowed the visitors but
eleven games in ten sets, while the
1931 team lost but fifteen games in an
equal number of sets.
John Wheatley, whose victory over
Alan Herrington during the past week
made him ranking varsity player, had
little trouble in winning from McGoe
of St. Ignatius, 6-0, 6-1. Herrington
had to work somewhat harder to de
feat Joseph of St. Ignatius, 6-3, 6-1.
Ralph McElvenny, third ranking var
sity player, won his match with ease
from O'Brien, 6-0, 6-1.
The varsity doubles were almost a
repetition of the singles. Wheatley
and Thomas defeated McGee and
O'Brien, 6-2, 6-3; wMTe Hall and Far
well defeated Joseph and Joseph,
6-0, 6-0.
Although John T)oeg, first man on
the frosh squad, did not compete, the
first-year men trounced the visiting
freslrtnen. Edward Easton defeated
Gallagher, 6-1, 6-0; Trow Stephens de
feated Smith, 6-0, 6-0; Joseph Dodd
defeated Brady, 6-4, 6-2; Charles
Ranney defeated Reynolds, 6-3, 6-0.
In the doubles Easton and Stephens
defeated Gallagher and Smith, 6-0.
6-1; and Dodd and Ranney defeated
Brady and Reynolds, 6-2, 6-2.
