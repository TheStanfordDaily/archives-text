# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
Notices for this column should be handed in
at the Stanford Daily office by 9 o'clock of
the evening preceding publication.
THURSDAY
12:45 p.m.—Yell leaders will meet at
the Phi Gamma Delta house for
Quad pictures.
1:00 p.m.—All sophomore class of
ficers and heads of committees for
fall and winter quarters will meet
in the court of the Union for Quad
pictures.
3:00 p.m.—The Stanford Dames will
meet at the home of Mrs, Marion
Kirkwood, 249 Lowell, Palo Alto,
for a social time. The children are
also invited.
4:15 p.m.—Organ Recital by Warren
D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
6:45 p.m.—Freshman Y.W.C.A. Cabi
net will meet at Roble.
7:00 p.m.—Senior jolly-up and class
meeting in the Women's Clubhouse.
Important discussion of class busi
ness.
7:00 p.m.—The Stanford Studios Mo
tion Picture Club will meet in the
Press Building. Students interested
in producing amateur movies may
attend.
7:00 p.m.—Rally Committee will meet
in Old Union,
7:30 p.m.— Radio Club meets in the
Radio shack.
7:30 p.m.—Thursday Evening Confer
ence at the home of Dr. Jordan.
8:00 p.m.—Women's Debate in the
Little Theatre. Mills College vs.
Stanford.
FRIDAY
7:30 p.m.—Ushers will report at the
Assembly Hall for the Marquers
Play.
8:15 p.m.—"The Romantic Young
Lady," in the Assembly Hall under
the auspices of Masquers Society.
MISCELLANEOUS
Seats will continue to be sold for
"The Romantic Young Lady" to be.
given March 2, at the office of the
Dramatic Council, in the Old Union.
The management of the Stanford
Literary Magazine announces that
there will be no March issue. Those
desiring refunds on their subscrip
tions for one month may call at the
Lit office between 1 and 2 o'clock
Wednesday afternoon.
Men, now living in Encina, who wish
to reserve their rooms for Spring
Quarter, should do so at the Dean
of Men's Office today, Thursday,
and Friday.
Lists for the Triangular Sports Day
with California and Mills must be
signed immediately by those wish
ing to participate in the events.
Lists are in the women's gymnasium.
