# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
Notices for this column should be handed in
at the Stanford Daii.y office by 9 o'clock of
the evening preceding publication.
THURSDAY
10:00 a.m. — University Assembly.
President Ray Lyman Wilbur will
•speak.
1:00 p.m.—Women's Glee Club will
have Quad pictures taken in Union
Court.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren |
D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
6:15 p.m.—Eagle Scout Club dinner in
the Union. All Eagle Scouts are in
vited.
7:00 p.m.—Freshman jolly-up in Ro
ble Hall.
7:15 p.m.—Women's Choir and ora
torio rehearsal in Memorial Church,
7:30 p.m.—Ushers for Peninsula Con
cert report at the Assembly Hall.
7:30 p.m.—Thursday evening confer
ence at the home of Dr. Jordan.
Subject: "Leland Stanford."
3:15 p.m.—Concert by Harold Bauer,
pianist, in the Assembly Hall under
the auspices of the Peninsula Mu
sical Association.
FRIDAY
Founders' Day
8:00 p.m.—Popular medical lecture by
Dr. Frederick Eberson in Lane Hall,
San Francisco. Subject: "Protec
tion against Tuberculosis."
8:15 p.m.—Concert by the San Fran
cisco Symphony Orchestra, in the
Memorial Church. Admission by
ticket.
SATURDAY
9:00 a.m.—Limnology class will take
a field trip to Calaveras dam.
SUNDAY
12:00 m.—After sermon in Memorial
Church, Lynn White will hold open
forum in the church to answer all
questions on the sermon or matters
pertaining to it.
MONDAY
3:00 p.m.— Katharine Newell Adams,
president of the Constantinople
Woman's College, will address the
meeting of the Faculty Women's
Club in the Women's Clubhouse on
"Women in Turkey."
MISCELLANEOUS
Men now living in the Union or Se
quoia Hall who wish to reserve
their rooms for the spring quarter
should call at the Dean of Men's
office today or tomorrow, March 8
and 9, and pay deposits.
Men who wish to take out or renew
tuition notes for Spring Quarter
should call at the Dean of Men's
office (be week of March 12 to 16.
Notices for this column should be handed in
at the Stanford Daii.y office by 9 o'clock of
the evening preceding publication.

quoia Hall who wish to reserve
their rooms for the spring quarter
should call at the Dean of Men's
office today or tomorrow, March 8
and 9, and pay deposits.
Men who wish to take out or renew
tuition notes for Spring Quarter
should call at the Dean of Men's
office (be week of March 12 to 16.
Notices for this column should be handed in
at the Stanford Daii.y office by 9 o'clock of
the evening preceding publication.
THURSDAY
10:00 a.m. — University Assembly.
President Ray Lyman Wilbur will
•speak.
1:00 p.m.—Women's Glee Club will
have Quad pictures taken in Union
Court.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren |
D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
6:15 p.m.—Eagle Scout Club dinner in
the Union. All Eagle Scouts are in
vited.
7:00 p.m.—Freshman jolly-up in Ro
ble Hall.
7:15 p.m.—Women's Choir and ora
torio rehearsal in Memorial Church,
7:30 p.m.—Ushers for Peninsula Con
cert report at the Assembly Hall.
7:30 p.m.—Thursday evening confer
ence at the home of Dr. Jordan.
Subject: "Leland Stanford."
3:15 p.m.—Concert by Harold Bauer,
pianist, in the Assembly Hall under
the auspices of the Peninsula Mu
sical Association.
FRIDAY
Founders' Day
8:00 p.m.—Popular medical lecture by
Dr. Frederick Eberson in Lane Hall,
San Francisco. Subject: "Protec
tion against Tuberculosis."
8:15 p.m.—Concert by the San Fran
cisco Symphony Orchestra, in the
Memorial Church. Admission by
ticket.
SATURDAY
9:00 a.m.—Limnology class will take
a field trip to Calaveras dam.
SUNDAY
12:00 m.—After sermon in Memorial
Church, Lynn White will hold open
forum in the church to answer all
questions on the sermon or matters
pertaining to it.
MONDAY
3:00 p.m.— Katharine Newell Adams,
president of the Constantinople
Woman's College, will address the
meeting of the Faculty Women's
Club in the Women's Clubhouse on
"Women in Turkey."
