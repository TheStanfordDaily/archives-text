WEDNESDAY
12:15 p.m.—Cosmopolitan Club. Inter
national luncheon, in the lobby of
the Stanford Union dining room.
1:00 p.m.—There will be a meeting of
the Senior Week Committee in the
Trophy Room of the Old Union.
4:00 to 6:00 p.m.—Dean Boezinger
will be at home to women of the
University.
6:00 p.m.—Young Men's Christian As
sociation. Dinner and election of
officers, in the alcove of the Stan
ford Union dining room. J. Stitt
Wilson will speak.
6:15 p.m.—Stanford Masonic Club will
meet in the New Union. Attorney
John McNab will be the speaker.
All members are asked to be pres
ent. Master Masons and De Molays
are invited.
7:00 p.m.—Senior Class will meet in
the Little Theatre.
7:30 p.m.—Engineers' smoker in the
Old Union. Paul M. Downing will be
the speaker.
7:30 p.m.—All ushers, ticket-takers
and doormen for the A.S.S.U. con
cert meet in the Pavilion.
8:00 p.m.—Concert, by the San Fran
cisco Symphony Orchestra, in the
Pavilion, under the auspices of the
Associated Students.
THURSDAY
Dr. Jordan will not hold his regular
conference at his home.
1:45 p.m.—Board of Trustees. Regu
lar meeting, in the offices of the
Board, Balfour Building, San Fran
cisco.
3:00 p.m.—Young People's Concert,
by th 3 San Francisco Symphony
Orchestra, in the Pavilion, under
the auspices of the Palo Alto Par
ent-Teachers' Association.
7:00 p.m.—Women's Glee Club will
meet in the Women's Clubhouse."
7:30 p.m.—German Club will hold
election in Women's Clubhouse.
7:45 p.m.—Euphronia Debating Soci
ety. Debate with Centuriata Debat
ing Society of the University of
California. Subject: "Resolved,
That fraternities should be abol
ished at the University of Califor
nia and at Stanford."
FRIDAY
7:30 p.m.—Ushers report at the As
sembly Hall for "Caesar and Cleo
patra."
8:15 p.m.—"Caesar and Cleopatra,"
by George Bernard Shaw, in the
Assembly Hall, under the auspices
of the English Club. Direction of
Anna May Price.

the University of California to be
held at the Palace Hotel April 21
after the Big Meet will be on sale
at the Old Union until Monday,
April 16.
All men wishing to police for the
B.A.C. during the spring quarter
get in touch with "Chuck" Briggs,
P.O. Box 1832. Men policing this
spring will be given preference next
fall.
A member of the Academic Council
subscribed to the dinner tendered
to President Wilbur on March 14,
but later sent in word that he was
unable to attend and requested the
return of his subscription. Through
« inadvertence, the name of the pro
fessor to whom J:he return was to
be made has been mislaid. The

chairman of the dinner committee,
Dr. A. E. Taylor, will feel grateful
if the professor in question will
notify him so that his subscription
may be returned.

order them at once, as they are
going to press immediately, and
orders must be in before this time.

sign up with L. R. Vincenti, 419

12:15 p.m.—Cosmopolitan Club. Inter
national luncheon, in the lobby of
the Stanford Union dining room.
1:00 p.m.—There will be a meeting of
the Senior Week Committee in the
Trophy Room of the Old Union.
4:00 to 6:00 p.m.—Dean Boezinger
will be at home to women of the
University.
6:00 p.m.—Young Men's Christian As
sociation. Dinner and election of
officers, in the alcove of the Stan
ford Union dining room. J. Stitt
Wilson will speak.
6:15 p.m.—Stanford Masonic Club will
meet in the New Union. Attorney
John McNab will be the speaker.
All members are asked to be pres
ent. Master Masons and De Molays
are invited.
7:00 p.m.—Senior Class will meet in
the Little Theatre.
7:30 p.m.—Engineers' smoker in the
Old Union. Paul M. Downing will be
the speaker.
7:30 p.m.—All ushers, ticket-takers
and doormen for the A.S.S.U. con
cert meet in the Pavilion.
8:00 p.m.—Concert, by the San Fran
cisco Symphony Orchestra, in the
Pavilion, under the auspices of the
Associated Students.

conference at his home.

lar meeting, in the offices of the
Board, Balfour Building, San Fran
cisco.

by th 3 San Francisco Symphony

ent-Teachers' Association.

ing Society of the University of

ished at the University of Califor
