# FROSH RACQUET MEN DROP FOUR MATCHES TO SOPHOMORE TEAM
## 
### 
By taking four matches out of seven I
from the freshmen, the sophomores made
a good start in the interclass tennis tour
nament yesterday. A strong wind both
ered the players in making lobs and side
line shots, but in spite of this handicap,
many sparkling rallies were made.
Playing No. 1 doubles, Wilson and
La Chape lie of the freshmen had little
difficulty in downing James and Lee of
the sophomores, 6-4, 6-4. Though there
were numerous double faults served by
the sophomore team, several good plays
were made.
In the No. 2 doubles Miller and Erlin,
sophomores, downed Gardenier and Ran
ney, freshmen, in a hard-fought match,
4-6, 6-1, 7-5. The other two doubles
matches resulted in easy wins, Craig and
Ricketts of the sophomores defeating j
Dodd and Cameron, freshmen, 6-4, 6-1, j
while the freshmen turned the tables on ;
the sophomores when Baumgarten and j
Best upset Smith and Gilmour, 6-2, 6-4.
In the singles play, the best match of i
the clay was played when Wilson of the j
freshmen took the measure of Lee in i
three sets, 6-1, 4-6, 7-5, at No. 3 position. \
Wilson had the advantage in his heavy j
driving and placements, but Lee put up
a great fight to take the second set. The
third set went to deuce with many games
also going to deuce. Wilson was able to
keep up a winning pace longer than his i
rival and took the set and the match.
McKellip outsteadied La Chapelle in
No. 2 singles to win as he pleased at 6-3, j
6-2. LaChapelle netted his returns to;
lose numerous points in both sets, though !
his play was much improved in the doub- !
les.
In the deciding match of the day, Craig 1
kept Gardinier on the defensive most of
the time to win, 6-4, 4-6, 6-0. This match i
was also featured by fast volleys and
hard drives. Craig served many double
faults in losing the second set, but came
back strongly to take the next set at love.
Gardinier was tiring fast, having played
a three-set doubles match just before his
singles test, and Craig kept him on the
run with well directed placements. Wil
son of the freshmen was the outstanding
player of the whole tournament.
Summary:
Doubes—
1. Wilson and LaChapelle, '31, d.
James and Lee, '30—6-4, 6-4.
2. Miller and Erlin, '30, d. Gardinier
and Ranney, '31—4-6, 6-1, 7-5.
3. Craig and Ricketts, '30, d. Dodd and
Cameron, '31-6-4, 6-1.
4. Baumgarten and Best, '31, d. Smith
and Gilmour, '30 —6-2, 6-4.
Singles—
1. Craig, '30, d. Gardinier, '31—6-4, 4-6,
6-0.
2. McKellip, '30, d. LaChapelle, '31—
6-3, 6-2.
3. Wilson, '31, d. Lee, '30—6-1, 4-6, 6-0.

By taking four matches out of seven I
from the freshmen, the sophomores made
a good start in the interclass tennis tour
nament yesterday. A strong wind both
ered the players in making lobs and side
line shots, but in spite of this handicap,
many sparkling rallies were made.
Playing No. 1 doubles, Wilson and
La Chape lie of the freshmen had little
difficulty in downing James and Lee of
the sophomores, 6-4, 6-4. Though there
were numerous double faults served by
the sophomore team, several good plays
were made.
In the No. 2 doubles Miller and Erlin,
sophomores, downed Gardenier and Ran
ney, freshmen, in a hard-fought match,
4-6, 6-1, 7-5. The other two doubles
matches resulted in easy wins, Craig and
Ricketts of the sophomores defeating j
Dodd and Cameron, freshmen, 6-4, 6-1, j
while the freshmen turned the tables on ;
the sophomores when Baumgarten and j
Best upset Smith and Gilmour, 6-2, 6-4.
In the singles play, the best match of i
the clay was played when Wilson of the j
freshmen took the measure of Lee in i
three sets, 6-1, 4-6, 7-5, at No. 3 position. \
Wilson had the advantage in his heavy j
driving and placements, but Lee put up
a great fight to take the second set. The
third set went to deuce with many games
also going to deuce. Wilson was able to
keep up a winning pace longer than his i
rival and took the set and the match.
McKellip outsteadied La Chapelle in
No. 2 singles to win as he pleased at 6-3, j
6-2. LaChapelle netted his returns to;
lose numerous points in both sets, though !
his play was much improved in the doub- !
les.
In the deciding match of the day, Craig 1
kept Gardinier on the defensive most of
the time to win, 6-4, 4-6, 6-0. This match i
was also featured by fast volleys and
hard drives. Craig served many double
faults in losing the second set, but came
back strongly to take the next set at love.
Gardinier was tiring fast, having played
a three-set doubles match just before his
singles test, and Craig kept him on the
run with well directed placements. Wil
son of the freshmen was the outstanding
player of the whole tournament.
