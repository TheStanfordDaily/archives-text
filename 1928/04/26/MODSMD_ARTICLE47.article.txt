THURSDAY
12:00 Noon—All members of Theta
Sigma Phi will meet for luncheon
at Roble. A short business meeting
will be held afterwards.
4:15 p.m.—Organ Recital by Warren
D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
4:15 p.m. —University Philological As
sociation in the Women's Club
house.
7:00 p.m. — The Cosmopolitan Club
will meet at I)r. Jordan's home. All
members are urged to be there.
7:15 p.m.—Final choir rehearsal will
be hold in the Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m.—Assembly Hall ushers re
port for the Peninsula Concert.
7:30 p.m.—Thursday Evening Confer
ence at the home of Dr. Jordan,
Subject: "Japan."
8:15 p.m.—Concert by Reinald Wer
renrath, baritone, in the Assembly
Hall under auspices of the Penin
sula Musical Association.
FRIDAY
7:30 p.m.—Assembly Hall ushers re
port for the Glee Club Concert.
8:15 p.m.—Concert by the Stanford
Glee Club in the Assembly Hall.
SUNDAY
6:lsp.m.—Dean Marion R. Kirkwood
of the Law School will be the speak
er at the regular meeting of the
Stanford Episcopal Club in the
Parish House, on Hamilton avenue,
just off Waverley. Everyone wel
come.
MISCELLANEOUS
Memorial Church — Morning chapel,
Monday to Friday, inclusive, at 7:50
sharp, in the Organ Gallery.
AM men wishing to police for the
B.A.C. during the spring quarter
get in touch with "Chuck" Briggs,
P.O. Box 1832. Men policing this
spring will be given preference next
fall.
All those who want Quads should
order them at once, as they are
going to press immediately, and
orders must be in before this time.
Candidates for the degrees of Master
of Arts and Engineer, who are now
registered for their second quarter
of advanced work, and who expect
to complete their requirements by
the end of the Summer Quarter,
should file application at the Regis
trar's Office not later than Monday,
April 30.
