# ANNOUNCEMENTS
##

WEDNESDAY
12:15 p.m.—Cosmopolitan Club inter
national luncheon in the alcove of
the Stanford Union dining room.
4:00 to 6:00 p.m. — Dean Boezinger
will be at home informally to wom
en of the University.
6:30 p.m.—Stanford Masonic Club will
hold its regular business meeting in
the alcove of the New Union dining
room. Those unable to attend din
ner are asked to attend the meeting
after the dinner, which will last
about half an hour. All members
are asked to be present.
7:00 p.m.—Women's Glee Club will
meet in the Women's Clubhouse.
8:00 p.m.—Dr. Morris Hertzstein Lec
ture on Diseases of the Pacific Ba
sin at Lane Hall, San Francisco.
Dr. Richard P. Strong, Professor of
Tropical Medicine of the Harvard
Medical School, will speak on "Re
cent Advances in Tropical Medi
cine."
THURSDAY
1:00 p.m.—Seat Sale for the Junior
Opera, "South Seas Preferred," in
the lobby of the Old Union and at
Crandall's, Palo Alto.
3:00 p.m.—Stanford Dames will be
hostesses to the Faculty Advisory
Board in the Women's Clubhouse. A
program and social afternoon has
been planned. All members are
urged to be present.
3:00 p.m.—Stanford Dames in the
Women's Clubhouse. Program and
social afternoon.
4:15 p.m.—Organ Recital by Warren
I). Allen in the Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m.—Thursday Evening Confer
ence at the home of Dr. Jordan.
Subject: "Herbert Hoover in the
Savoy."
7:30 p.m.—Dr. Jordan will hold his
regular conference tomorrow night
at his home. The subject of the lec
ture will be "Herbert Hoover in
the Savoy."
8:00 p.m.—Hertzstein Lecture, Lane
Hall. "Recent Advances in Tropi
cal Medicine. II," by Dr. Richard
P. Strong.
8:00 p.m.—Peninsula Debating League
Annual oratorical contest in the
Little Theatre.
MISCELLANEOUS
Memorial Church — Morning chapel,
Monday to Friday, inclusive, at 7:50
sharp, in the Organ Gallery.
All men wishing to police for the
B.A.C. during the spring quarter
get in touch with "Chuck" Briggs,
P.O. Box 1832. Men policing this
spring will bo given preference next
fall.
Men now living in Toyon and Bran
ner Halls who wish to reserve their
rooms for the Fall Quarter should
call at the office of the Dean of
Men either Monday or Tuesday,
May 7 or 8, and pay their deposits.
Drawings will be held in the offices
of the two halls on next Wednes
night.
Ushers wanted for Sunday's baseball
game between the Stanford Daily
and the University Press. Appli
cants may apply at the Daily Busi
ness Office any time previous to
the game, which will take place
Sunday morning at 10 o'clock on
the varsity diamond.

hold its regular business meeting in

ner are asked to attend the meeting

are asked to be present.

Tropical Medicine of the Harvard
Medical School, will speak on "Re
cent Advances in Tropical Medi
cine."
