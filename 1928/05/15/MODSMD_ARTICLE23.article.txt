# GARDEN PARTY TODAY TO BENEFIT PROPOSED STUDENT REST HOMES
## 
### 
A garden party and benefit musicale
under the auspices of the Mothers
Club will be held at the home of Mrs.
Paul Shoup in Los Altos today at 3
o'clock. The proceeds will go toward
the establishment of rest homes on
the campus next year for students
who are not sick enough to go to the
hospital, but are in a rundown, over
tired condition.
The program will consist of selec
tions by the Stanford Women's Glee
Club and a trio composed of Elizabeth
Peirce, violinist; Mary Moynihan,
pianist, and Evelyn Hahn, flutist. Paul
Brainard, tenor, accompanied by Mrs.
Brainard, and Marjorie Blackwelder
will be the soloists. A second trio
composed of Leland Long, cellist.
Elizabeth Moser, violinist, and Henry
Moser, pianist, will also entertain.
Refreshmens will be served during
the afternoon.
Tickets may be obtained for 50
cents through Mrs. E. C. Baker, King
scote Apartments, or at Mrs. Shoup's
this afternoon. More than five hun
dred tickets have already been sold.
