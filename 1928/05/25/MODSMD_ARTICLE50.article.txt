# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
FRIDAY
2:00 p.m—Chi Nu Kappa picnic at
Searsville for all members of the
Classical Literature department
and their friends.
6:00 p.m.—Cosmopolitan Club annual
banquet at Searsville Lake. Tickets
may be obtained from Dixie Stall or
Leopold Schumacher.
8:00 p.m.—The Stanford Dames will
hold their quarterly party in the
Women's Clubhouse. An evening of
bridge games and music has been
planned. All Dames and their hus
bands are invited.
SATURDAY
7:15p.m.—A1l men ushers report at
the basketball pavilion to usher for
the Sciot Benefit Minstrel Show.
7:30 p.m.—Chemistry Seminar will
meet in Chemistry building. Dr.
Ludwig Rosenstein will discuss re
moval of sulphur from gases. Pub
lic invited.
MONDAY
8:00 p.m.—Concert manager tryoutees
will meet in A.S.S.U. office, Old
Union.
7:15 p.m.—New members of the Eng
lish Club are requested to meet in
the Secretary's office, journalism
department.
8:00 p.m.—Joint meeting of Phi Delta
Kappa, Pi Lambda Theta, and the
Education Club in the Women's
Clubhouse. Professor Cubberley
will speak on "The Doctor of Edu
cation Degree."
MISCELLANEOUS
Anyone having any November 1927
issues of the Stanford Literary
Magazine which they do not want
are requested to bring them to the
Lit office or get in touch with
George F. Mott, Jr.
All Seniors who ordered graduation
announcements may get the same
liy calling at the Stanford Book
store Monday, May 28, between 8
and 6 o'clock; and presenting their
receipt of sale.
Senior Ball bids are on sale at the
Old Union to those seniors who
have paid their class dues every
day between 1 and 5 o'clock. Bids
are $10 each; class dues $2.
Men now living in Toyon or Union
who wish to reserve their rooms
for the summer quarter should call
at the Dean of Men's office Wednes
day or Thursday, May 23 and 24,
and pay a deposit.

liy calling at the Stanford Book
store Monday, May 28, between 8
and 6 o'clock; and presenting their
receipt of sale.
Senior Ball bids are on sale at the
Old Union to those seniors who
have paid their class dues every
day between 1 and 5 o'clock. Bids
are $10 each; class dues $2.
Men now living in Toyon or Union
who wish to reserve their rooms
for the summer quarter should call
at the Dean of Men's office Wednes
day or Thursday, May 23 and 24,
and pay a deposit.
FRIDAY
2:00 p.m—Chi Nu Kappa picnic at
Searsville for all members of the
Classical Literature department
and their friends.
6:00 p.m.—Cosmopolitan Club annual
banquet at Searsville Lake. Tickets
may be obtained from Dixie Stall or
Leopold Schumacher.
8:00 p.m.—The Stanford Dames will
hold their quarterly party in the
Women's Clubhouse. An evening of
bridge games and music has been
planned. All Dames and their hus
bands are invited.
SATURDAY
7:15p.m.—A1l men ushers report at
the basketball pavilion to usher for
the Sciot Benefit Minstrel Show.
7:30 p.m.—Chemistry Seminar will
meet in Chemistry building. Dr.
Ludwig Rosenstein will discuss re
moval of sulphur from gases. Pub
lic invited.
MONDAY
8:00 p.m.—Concert manager tryoutees
will meet in A.S.S.U. office, Old
Union.
7:15 p.m.—New members of the Eng
lish Club are requested to meet in
the Secretary's office, journalism
department.
8:00 p.m.—Joint meeting of Phi Delta
Kappa, Pi Lambda Theta, and the
Education Club in the Women's
Clubhouse. Professor Cubberley
will speak on "The Doctor of Edu
cation Degree."
MISCELLANEOUS

issues of the Stanford Literary
Magazine which they do not want
