# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
TUESDAY
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
7:00 p.m.—Ail those wishing to try
out for business and circulation
managers for next year's Chaparral
are asked to report at the Chaparral
Office in the Press building.
7:30 p.m.—Recital by Marie de For
est Emery, soprano, and Helena
Redewill, pianist, in the Little
Theatre.
7:30 p.m.—Zoology Club will meet at
the Women's Clubhouse. J. C.
Chamberlin will speak and the elec
tion of officers for the coming year
will be held.
WEDNESDAY
12:15 p.m.—Cosmopolitan Club. In
ternational luncheon in the alcove
of the Stanford Union dining room.
4 to 6 p.m.—Miss Olivia Boezinger
will he at home to women of the
University.
8:00 p.m.—Research Club will meet
in the Faculty Clubhouse.
8:15 p.m.—Three one-act plays, pre
sented by members of the class in
vocal expression, in the Little The
atre.
THURSDAY
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m.—Thursday evening confer
ence at the home of Dr. Jordan.
Subject: "The Human Harvest."
7:30 p.m.—Public discussion group in
the Little Theatre (Public Speaking
133).
8:00 p.m.—The University Orchestra
will meet at Mr. Allen's home.
FRIDAY
1:30 p.m.—Horse show under the aus
pices of the Reserve Officers'
Training Corps at the Encina Polo
Field.
2:00 p.m.—Chi Nu Kappa, classical
literature society, will hold its pic
nic at Searsville Lake. Picnic was
announced for last Friday incor
rectly. All friends of the depart
ment invited.
8:00 p.m.—Faculty Club at Faculty
Clubhouse.

afternoon, May 28.

and presenting their receipt of sale.

Old Union to those seniors who
have paid their class dues every
day between 1 and 5 o'clock. Bids
are $10 each; class dues $2.
