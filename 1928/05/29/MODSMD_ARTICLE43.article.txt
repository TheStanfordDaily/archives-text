# OREGON U. WILL RAISE ACADEMIC STANDARDS
## 
### 
EUGENE, Ore., May 27.—(P.1.P.)—
Scholarship standards at the Univer
sity of Oregon will be raised material
ly beginning next term as the result
of action taken at a faculty meeting
Wednesday. The new ruling practi
cally abolishes the probation period
for juniors and seniors. Instead of
being placed on probation such stu
dents as fail to meet the requirements
will be automatically dropped from
the university.

seniors were obliged to pass seven
hours to remain in school and nine
to keep off of probation. Under the
new order they are compelled to pass
