# ANNOUNCEMENTS

THURSDAY
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m.—Thursday evening confer
ence at the home of Dr. Jordan.
Subject: "The Human Harvest."
7:30 p.m.—The Chemistry Seminar
will meet in Room 20 of the Chem
istry Building. Professor Bergstrom,
who has charge of the program, will
speak on "A New Synthetic Reagent
in Organic Chemistry.
7:30 p.m.—Public discussion group in
the Little Theatre (Public Speaking
133).
8:00 p.m.—The University Orchestra
will meet at Mr. Allen's home.
FRIDAY
1:30 p.m.—Horse show under the aus
pices of the Reserve Officers'
Training Corps at the Encina Polo
Field.
2:00 p.m.—Chi Nu Kappa, classical
literature society, will hold its pic
nic at Searsville Lake. Picnic was
announced for last Friday incor
rectly. All friends of the depart
ment invited.
8:00 p.m.—Faculty Club at Faculty
Clubhouse.
MISCELLANEOUS
Men who wish to take out or renew
tuition notes for the summer or fall
quarters should call at the Dean of
Men's office Wednesday, Thursday,
or Friday, June 6, 7, or 8.
Anyone having any November 1927
issues of the Stanford Literary
Magazine which they do not want
are requested to bring them to the
Lit office or get in touch with
George F. Mott, Jr.
Senior Ball bids are on sale at the
Old Union to those seniors who
have paid their class dues every
day between 1 and 5 o'clock. Bids
are $10 each; class dues $2.
