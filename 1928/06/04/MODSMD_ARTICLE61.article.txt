MONDAY «
5:00 p.m.—All women's tennis, swim
ming, and track teams to meet at
the Women's Clubhouse to elect
next year's managers.

ing of Chaparral Circulation try
outees in the Chaparral office.

(Public Speaking 133) in the Little
Theatre. Speaker: Richard A. Fish
er, Leonard D. James, Vernon E.
Perren, Wilbur D. Russell, Edward
H. Sibbett.

new members, in the Faculty Club
house.

Staff of 1929 Quad will meet tonight
In the Quad office of the Old Union.
TUESDAY
Christian Science Society will hold a
business meeting for the election
of officers in the Women's Club
house. All members please be pres
ent.
4:15 p.m.—Organ Recital by Warren
D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
7:00 p.m.—Class of 1928. Meeting in
the Assembly Hall.
7:30 p.m.—Dramatic Reading by Mrs.
Hugh Brown in the Little Theatre.
Mrs. Brown will present "Lazarus
Laughed," by O'Neil.

12:1* nm— Club inter- j

tuition notes for the summer or fall
quarters should call at the Dean of

or Friday, June 6, 7, or 8.

Senior Farce report to Tom Drake,
218 Sequoia.

issues of the Stanford Literary
Magazine which they do not want
are requested to bring them to the

George F. Mott, Jr.

have paid their class dues every

are $10 each; class dues $2.
