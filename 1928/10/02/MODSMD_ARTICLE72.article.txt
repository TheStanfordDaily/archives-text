# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
TUESDAY

4 to G p.m.—Registration tea for all
Stanford women, especially those
registering for the first time, down
stairs in the Women's Clubhouse,
given by the Y.W.C.A.
7:15 p.m.—Executive Committee will
meet in the Student Body office in
the Old Union.
7:15 p.m.—Tryouts for Chaparral busi
ness and managerial positions will
be held in the Chaparral office in
the Press Building.
7:30 p.m.—Concert in the Memorial
Church by Warren D. Allen, Uni
versity organist, assisted by Mrs.
Annie David, harpist, and Mrs.
Esther Houk Allen, contralto.
WEDNESDAY
Stanford Lit tryoutees are to leave
their names at the Lit office in the
Press Building.
Freshmen who wish to try out for
interclass football must report to
Charles Davis, director of intra
mural sports, at Encina Gymna
sium.
7:50 a.m. —Morning chapel in the or
gan gallery.
THURSDAY
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
D. Allen in the Memorial Church.
7:00 p.m.—Varsity debaters of former
years and all those who would like
to try out for the varsity debate
squad this year will hold a meeting
in Room 201.
FRIDAY
11:00 a.m. —University Assembly in
the Assembly Hall. Address by
President Ray Lyman Wilbur.
8:00 p.m.—President and Mrs. Wilbur
will be at home informally to all
students registering for the first
time this quarter. There will be
music for those who care to dance.

7:30 p.m.—Organ recital in the Me
