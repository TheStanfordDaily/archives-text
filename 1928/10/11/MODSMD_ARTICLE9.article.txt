# MURAL GOLFERS TO COMPETE IN ANNUAL TOURNEY
## 
### 
More than twenty teams of two men
each have signed up for the annual
intramural golf tournament, which
will be held at Capuchino Country
Club, Monday, Tuesday, and Wednes
day of next week. Entries close at 5
o'clock Friday evening, and Charles
Davis, director of intramural sports,
expects nearly 70 teams to have
signed up by that time.

Varsity and freshman squad mem
bers will not be allowed to compete
in the tournament, and the usual scor
ing method will be used, five points
being awarded to each organization
entering a team, and additional
awards of 15, 10, 5, 3, and 1 points
being made to the winners of the
first five places. Each organization
will be allowed to enter as many
teams as it desires.

Capuchino Country Club is located
on the San Francisco Highway be
tween Burlingame and San Bruno,
and arrangements are being made to
secure practice privileges for the com
peting teams.
The tournament last year was won
by R. G. Stevenson and R. W. Allen
of Encina, 45 teams entering.
