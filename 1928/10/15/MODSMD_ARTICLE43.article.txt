MONDAY
7:30 p.m.—The varsity debate squad
will hold its regular meeting in
Room 201.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of the English
Review Club at the home of Pro
fessor A. G. Kennedy, 435 Coleridge.
9:00 p.m.—Rally Committee meeting
in Old Union.
TUESDAY
3:00 p.m.—Meeting of the Stanford
Mothers' Club in the Women's
Clubhouse, Becond floor. (Mothers
6f new students invited.)
4:15 p.m.—Organ Recital.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Life Among
the Arabs in French North Africa,"
by Profossor Edward M. Hulme in
the Assembly Hall.
7:30 p.m.—An important meeting of
the Geological and Mining Society
will bo held in Room 550, Mining
Building.
WEDNESDAY
7:00 p.m.—Men's Glee Club tryouts in
Room 260C.
7:30 p.m.—Euphronia debate team
tryouts will be held in the lobby
of the Old Union. Tryoutees will
speak on "Should the Electoral Col
lege be abolished." A musical pro
gram will be offered. ,
THURSDAY
3:00 p.m. — Meeting of Stanford
Dames, in the Women's Clubhouse.
Wives of students invited to attend.
4:15 p.m.—Organ Recital.
7:30 p.m.—Thursday evening confer
ence at the home of Dr. David Starr
Jordan. Subject: "The Adventures
of a Pacifist."

ketball Pavilion write to Mary

al in church 7:30 Thursday evening.
