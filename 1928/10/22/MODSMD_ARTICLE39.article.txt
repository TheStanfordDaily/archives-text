# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
MONDAY
12:00 m. —Last time to submit rooters'
stunt ideas for use at U.S.C.-Stan
ford football game. Mail suggestions
to George Ellsworth, Box 082, Cam
pus.
1:00 p.m.—Sigma Delta Chi will meet
at the Phi Psi House. All members
must be present.
8:45 p.m.—Tryouts for pianist of
Men's Glee Club will be held in
Room 2GO.
TUESDAY
7:00 p.m.—All members of the class
of 1930 are invited to attend the
first Junior jolly-up of the year at
Roble Hall.
7:30 p.in.—All ushers for A.S.S.U.
Concert Series report to basketball
pavilion for short meeting.
7:30 p.m. — The Christian Science
Club will meet on the second floor
of the Women's Clubhouse.
WEDNESDAY
12:15p.m.—International luncheon in
the alcove of the Stanford Union
dining room.
THURSDAY
4: If) p.m.—The University Philological
Association will meet in the Wo
men's Clubhouse. Professor Leon
ard Schwartz will give a paper en
"Autobiographies and Biography of
Anatole France."
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of the German
Club at the Women's Clubhouse. All
interested are invited to attend.
8:30 p.m.—Le Cercle Francais will
meet at the Delta Delta Delta house
Dr. Stauf will show slides of France.
All those interested in French are
invited to attend.
FRIDAY
7:20 p.m.—All ushers for the A.S.S.U.
Concert Series report to Pavilion to
usher at the Tibbett concert.
MISCELLANEOUS
Women desiring to usher at the
A.S.S.U. Concert Series in the Bas
ketball Pavilion write to Mary
Edith Jones, P.O. Box 724, or call
Chi Omega House, P.A. 1057.
ALL COPY for the Stanford Literary
Magazine must be in the mails by
midnight, announces James Tucker,
editor.

Roble Hall.

Concert Series report to basketball
pavilion for short meeting.
7:30 p.m. — The Christian Science
Club will meet on the second floor
of the Women's Clubhouse.
WEDNESDAY
12:15p.m.—International luncheon in
the alcove of the Stanford Union
dining room.
THURSDAY
4: If) p.m.—The University Philological
Association will meet in the Wo
men's Clubhouse. Professor Leon
ard Schwartz will give a paper en
"Autobiographies and Biography of
Anatole France."
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of the German
Club at the Women's Clubhouse. All
interested are invited to attend.
8:30 p.m.—Le Cercle Francais will
meet at the Delta Delta Delta house
Dr. Stauf will show slides of France.
All those interested in French are
invited to attend.
FRIDAY
7:20 p.m.—All ushers for the A.S.S.U.
Concert Series report to Pavilion to
usher at the Tibbett concert.

Women desiring to usher at the
A.S.S.U. Concert Series in the Bas
ketball Pavilion write to Mary
Edith Jones, P.O. Box 724, or call
Chi Omega House, P.A. 1057.

Magazine must be in the mails by
midnight, announces James Tucker,
editor.
