# PILOT, OWNER KILLED AS "YANKEE DOODLE" HITS MOUNTAIN
## 
### 

Captain C. B. Collyer, pilot, and Harry Tucker, passenger-owner of the monoplane Yankee Doodle, who were
killed when the plane jammed against the side of a wall in Crook Canyon, twenty-three miles south of Prescott,
Arizona. The Yankee Doodle is holder of two transcontinental records, and at the time of the accident the plane
was en route to New York from Los Angeles in an attempt to better Goebel's nineteen-hour record. Inset, Captain
Collyer left, and Tucker right. Allan Loughead, center, is designer and builder of the plane. He was not a pas
senger on this trip.
