MONDAY
1:00 to 2:30 p.m.—All classes dis
missed for Hoover reception.
7.30 p.m.—Lecture on "Abraham Lin
coln," by Dr. William E. Barton; in
the Little Theatre. The public is in
vited.
TUESDAY
All freshman or sophomore men wish
ing to try out for Business Depart
ment of Chaparral report to Chapar
ral office in Press Building.
2:15 p.m.—All men ushers report to
the Pavilion for Sousa afternoon
concert.
4:00 to 8:30 p.m.—Election returns,
received by radio, in the Assembly
Hall. Open to the public.
7:20 p.m.—All men and women ushers
report to the Pavilion for Sousa
concert.
8:15 p.m.—Concert, by Sousa and His
Band, under the auspices of the As
sociated Students; in the Basketball
Pavilion.
WEDNESDAY
7:30 p.m.—Dr. J. W. Mcßain will ad
dress a smoker held by Alpha Chi
Sigma and Phi Lambda Upsilon in
the lobby of the Old Union. All
chemistry students invited.
THURSDAY
8:00 p.m.—Research Club meets in
the Faculty Clubhouse. "Forecast
ing Crop Yields," by Professor Carl
L. Alsberg; "The Senate and the
International Court of Justice at
The Hague," by Professor H. Bar
rett Learned.
B:lsp.m.—Concert, by Toscha Seidel,
violinist, under the auspices of the
Peninsula Musical Association, in
the Assembly Hall.
FRIDAY
8:20 p.m.—Play, "The Devil in the
Cheese," by Thomas Cashing, pre
sented under the auspices of Sword
and Sandals Society, in the Assem
bly Hall.
MISCELLANEOUS
Because of the Hoover Homecoming,
Monday afternoon's Military Prac
tice Drill will not be held. Polo will
be at 3 o'clock, however, for those
who wish to play.
Any student desiring to usher at the
Washington Game, November 17,
please sign up at the Board of Ath
letic Control office before Wednes
day night, November 7.
All people taking subscriptions for
Chappie must turn in their books
at once to Dick Holt, Manager.
All freshmen are asked to turn in
their names for work on the bonfire
immediately. They may sign lists
placed in the lobby of Encina Hall
or may see Austin Clapp and Dick
Brodirick, freshman members of the
Labor Committee. Cars are wanted
and any freshmen having automo
biles should notify one of the com
mitteemen.
ANYONE interested In working for a
free ticket to the Sousa and His
Band Concert, Nov. 6, see Sid Mad
den in the Associated Students of
fice, Old Union, beiween 7:30 and
8:30 tonight.
Faculty swimming class is open to
Stanford Dames for this week at
special prices.
All those who will not be at the Uni
versity during the winter and spring
quarters and who wish to have their
pictures in the Quad, must make
their appointments immediately in
the Women's Clubhouse. All others,
too, are requested to make their ap
pointments as soon as possible.
Many have not yet called for their
proofs; they should do so at once.
All students who have their proofs
must return the ones they desire
to put in the Quad without delay.
Any Junior or Sophomore man inter
ested in trying out for Debate Man
ager get in touch with Steve Hal
sted immediately. For particulars,
Box 82 or 237 Toyon Hall.
