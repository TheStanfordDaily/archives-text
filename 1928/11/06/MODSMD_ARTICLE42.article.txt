# CONFERENCE PARTY IS HELD TOMORROW NIGHT
## 
### 
The Women's Conference Party for
merly set for last week will be held to
morrow night from 7:30 to 9:30 o'clock
in the Women's Clubhouse. Alexia Mc-
Carty, chairman of the social committee,
reminds the women that it is a dress-up
affair.
The entertainment of the evening will
consist of a tap dance, two skits, one by
the freshmen and the other by Masquers,
and specialty song numbers. A short
business meeting will precede the social
program. Mary Yost, Dean of Women,
Janet Harris, president of Associated
Women Students, and Chesterlyn Thorn- i
as, president of Women's Athletic Assor i
ciation, will speak.
There will be a charge of ten cents for
the party.
