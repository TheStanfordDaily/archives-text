THURSDAY
5:fl0 p.m.—Pi Lambda Theta, women's
educational society, will give a
Founder's Day program. Dr. J. C.
Almack will speak.
7:20 p.m.—Assembly Hall ushers re
port for the Peninsula Musical As
sociation concert.
7: SO p.m.—Zoology Club will meet at
home of Professor John O. Snyder
at 542 Alvarado. L. A. Walford will
speak.
8:00 p.m.—Research Club meets in
the Faculty Clubhouse. "Forecast
ing Crop Yields," by Professor Carl
L. Alsberg; "The Senate and the
International Court of Justice at
The Hague," by Professor H. Bar
rett liearned.
8:15 p.m.—Concert, by Toscha Seidel,
violinist, under the auspices of the
Peninsula Musical Association, in
the Assembly Hall.
FRIDAY
4:00 p.m.—Masonic degree team of the
Stanford Masonic Club to meet in
Palo Alto at Masonic Temple to
prepare for degree work in San
Francisco and Palo Alto. All mem
bers are urged to be present.
7:20 pjn.—Assembly Hall ushers re
port for the play, "The Devil in the
Cheese."
8:30 p.m.—Play, "The Devil in the
Cheese," by Thomas Cushing, pre
sented under the auspices of Sword
and Sandals Society, in the Assem
bly Hall.

al forests of Oregon and Washington
have decreased almost 100 per cent
