# JORDAN SUFFERS INJURY IN FALL; TALK POSTPONED
## 
### 
Dr. David Starr Jordan, chanccllor
emeritus of the University, suffered a
painful but not very serious injury yes
terday morning when he slipped on the
pavement in front of Jordan Hall. He
received a cut over one eye, bruised his
cheek, and, in trying to break his fall,
strained his left wrist.
He was returning home after spending
the morning at his office, and was ac
companied by his secretary. At present
Dr. Jordan is confined to his bed, but is
resting comfortably. The doctor who is
attending him stated that he will be up
in a couple of days.
Because of the mishap, Dr. Jordan's
regular Thursday evening conference
was postponed.
