# EUPHRONIA MEETS VARSITY TEAM IN PRACTICE DEBATE
## 
### 
Varsity debaters and two members
of Euphronia Debating Society will
participate in a split team debate in
the lobby of the Old Union tomorrow
evening at 7:30 o'clock. The topic to
be debated is "Resolved, That mod
ern advertising is more detrimental
than beneficial in its results."
The debate is in the nature of a
practice contest for the varsity de
baters who are to meet U.S.C. on this
question during the winter quarter.
Leon Carley will speak affirmatively
for Euphronia and Herbert Wenig
will take the negative. Tomorrow's
contest will close the quarter's foren
sic activities of Euphronia according
to John Cranston, president of the
society.
Speakers for the varsity squad will
be selected at a meeting to be held
at 4 o'clock this afternoon, according
to Wheeler Barger, assistant debate
coach.
