# DOEG REMAINS IN RANKING VARSITY TENNIS POSITION
## 
### 
Fighting an uphill battle after los
ing the first set, Johnnie Doeg de
feated Captain John Wheatley, 3-6,
6-4, 6-1 yesterday afternoon in an ex
hibition ranking match. Doeg thus re
tains his position at the head of the
list. Both men showed a good brand
of tennis, considering the earliness
of the season, and it was Doeg's fine
serve and skillful net volleying which
finally won for him.
In the other matches, played at the
same time, Easton was defeated by
Drlscoll, 6-4, 6-3, and Weesner beat
Renzel, 1-6, 6-0, 6-4. In doubles the
Doeg-Wheatley pair downed Weesner-
Forbes, 6-2, 7-9, 6-1. Farwell-Hall lost
to Easton-Renzell, 6-1, 6-4.
Tomorrow the exhibition match will
be between McKellip and Hall for fifth
place, and between Stephens and
Castellanos for ninth position. Wood
ruff and Erlin are paired off, and
Unnney will meet Farwell.
McKellip and Oantner will play
Stephens and Castellanos in doubles.
