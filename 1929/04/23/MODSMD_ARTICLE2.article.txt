# NATIONAL-MINDEDNESS IS LECTURE SUBJECT OF CHICAGO SPEAKER
## 
### 
"National-mindedness and Interna
tional-mindedness" will be the subject
of a lecture by Professor George Her
bert Mead of the University of Chi
cago, to be given at 4:15 p.m. in the
Little Theatre Thursday. Professor
Mead has been a member of the Chi
cago faculty since the early years of
the institution's development and a
generation of students there recognize
in him an intellectual and moral influ
ence of great power.
Professor Mead has been a leader
in movements for civic betterment in
•Chicago and closely associated with
the activities of the University of
Chicago Social Settlement for many
.years, several terms President of the
Chicago City Club and member of im
portant investigating commissions
concerned with municipal and social
affairs in the city. He is also one of
the foremost American students of the
philosophical problems of the natural
sciences. In recognition of his work
in this field, he has been appointed
Paul Carus Lecturer for the American
Philosophical Association for 1931.
During the present semester Pro
fessor Mead is Mills Lecturer in
Philosophy at the University of Cali
fornia.
