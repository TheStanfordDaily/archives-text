# KREY, WESTERGAARD, DUNIWAY TO BE HERE ON HISTORY FACULTY
## 
### 
Outstanding among the visiting pro
fessors who will teach history at Stan
ford during the coming summer ses
sion is Dr. August Charles Krey of
the University of Minnesota. Krey is
an authority in the held of European
history.
Krey is a graduate of the University
of Wisconsin, where he later received
his M.A. and Ph.D. degrees. He has
taught in the Universities of Wiscon
sin, Texas, Michigan, and Minnesota.
Another expert in European history
who will be at Stanford this summer
is Dr. Waldemar Westergaard of the
University of California at Los An
geles. Graduating from the University
of North Dakota, Westergaard took
his Ph.D. work at the University of
California, where he later taught.
Before becoming a member of the fac
ulty of the University of California at
Los Angeles, Westergaard was head
of the department of history at Po
mona College from 1916-25.
Dr. Clyde A. Duniway, former Stan
ford professor, will give a course in
American History, 1789-1848. Duniway,
who has been at Carleton College
since 1924, was a member of the Stan
ford faculty from 1897 to 1908. He
was later president of the University
of Montana.
Professor Yamato Ichihashi and
Professor Edgar Eugene Robinson of
the regular faculty are to remain at
Stanford during the summer quarter.
