# ALL-UNIVERSITY MIXED DOUBLES TENNIS PLAY IS IN SECOND ROUND
## 
### 
Entrants in the fourth annual all-
University mixed doubles tennis tour
nament have advanced to the second
round, which is scheduled to be played
this afternoon at 4 o'clock on the En
cina courts.
The eight matches in the first round
were completed yesterday. Barbara
Beach Thompson and Sam Leib de
feated Nini Jago and Finley Gibbs in
a three-set match, 1-6, 6-3, 7-5. Betts
Hawkins and Ted Easton, varsity ten
nis man, won from the Bowen-Adams
team, 6-0, 6-1.
Wheatley, who left later in the af
ternoon for the Pacific Coast Intercol
legiate Tennis tournament in Los An
geles, coupled with Helen Bell and
defeated Catherine Cartlidge and John
Cassel, 6-3, 6-2.
Frick and Smith scored a victory
over the Sobey-Leiter combination in
the second three-set match of the day,
4-6, 6-3, 6-4. Clark and Wallace lost to
Lozano and Mansfield, 6-3, 6-1. Two
matches of this round were played
Wednesday with the result that Dun
lap and Hall, and Curtis and McCoy,
(he two winning teams, will meet to
day in the second round.
Other contests scheduled to take
place today are; Thompson-Leib vh.
Hawkins-Easton; Bell- Wheatley vs.
Lozano-Mansfield, Frick-Smith vs. the
winner of the Young-Ricketts and
Patton-Hickman contest.
