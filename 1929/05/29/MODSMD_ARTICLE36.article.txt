# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
WEDNESDAY
12:15 p.m.—Cosmopolitan Club lunch
eon in the alcove of the Union.
Tickets will be sold for the annual
International Banquet at Searsville
Lake, Saturday, June 1.
4:00 p.m.—Play-off of the ep6e contest
at Encina gym.
7:15 p.m.—Chi Nu Kappa meets in
Room 255 in the Classical Lit build
ing to elect officers for next year.
7:30 p.m.—Tryoutees for manager
ships of the A.S.S.U. Concert Series
meet in the A.S.S.U. office in the
Old Union.
7:30-8:00 p.m.—New members of Glee
Club will be able to get music for
next year in Glee Club rooms.
THURSDAY
4:15 p.m.—University Philological As
sociation, in the Women's Club
house. Symposium on the subject,
"A Literary Utopia," conducted by
Professor Albert Gu^rard.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
1). Allen, in the Memorial Church.
4:45 p.m.—Memorial Day Service, in
the Memorial Church.
5:00 p.m.—English Club members and
initiates meet at the Women's
Clubhouse to leave for picnic. Bring
7:30 p.m.—Breakfast will be served
in the Union courtyard until 10:30.
The price is twenty-five cents per
person.
7:15 p.m.—The 1929-30 Stanford Daily
business staff will hold a short but
very important meeting in the busi
ness office.
SATURDAY
3:30 p.m.—Cosmopolitan Club Annual
International Banquet at Searsville
Lake. All foreign students welcome.
Tickets and information available
from John Way, 210 Sequoia, or
Alice Behner, 202 Roble.
cars.
MISCELLANEOUS
Men who wish to take out or renew
tuition notes should call at the Dean
of Men's office between May 30 and
June 7.
Men who have had experience in
ushering on the campus and who
would like to usher for the Com
mencement Exercises, should see
Nat Morgan, 107 Sequoia, or ad
dress Box 1742, for information.
