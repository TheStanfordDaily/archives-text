# DR. PAUL CLYDE SAILS FOR ORIENT TO STUDY SOCIAL CONDITIONS
## 
### 
Dr. Paul H. Clyde, acting assistant
professor of history at Stanford dur
ing the past year and an alumnus of
the University, sailed yesterday on
the Tenyo Maru for the Orient, where
he goes as a member of a group
traveling under the auspices of the
Carnegie Endowment for International
Peace. He will study social and in
dustrial conditions in Japan, Man
churia, and Korea.
Following his return to this coun
try in October, Dr. Clyde will go to
Columbus, where he is a member of
the history faculty at Ohio State
University. Mrs. Clyde and their little
daughter will leave later this month
for Ohio.
