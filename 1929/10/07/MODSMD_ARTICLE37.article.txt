# Phi Beta Kappa Regains Traditional First Place In Scholarship Rating
## General Improvement Shown in Stanford Grades; Men Improve Slightly While Women Decline 
### 
Phi Beta Kappa, national scholarship society, won back its traditional place this
year as ranking organization for scholastic attainment according to the annual
scholarship ratings just released by the University administration. Phi Lambda
Upsilon, chemistry fraternity, which last year led the University, placed second,
with the women's archery team and Theta Sigma Phi, women's journalistic society,
third and fourth respectively.
Scholarship at Stanford improved generally during the past year over the
rating of former years. The average of men students improved slightly in both
the fraternity and non-fraternity groups. Although the records of women declined
a little, the sorority groups improved their scholarship showing over the previous
year. In spite of the improvement of the sorority women, non-sorority groups
dropped sufficiently to lower the general average for women. However, these slight
changes did not displace Stanford women from their traditional place as scholastic
leaders on the campus. •»
The average for the University as a whole was a rather high C —, as is normal.
The rank of the women as a group is a high B —, while that of the men as a whole
is a pretty good C+.
Both the varsity and freshman football teams showed improvement over the
previous year, as did the freshman track team, while other teams exhibited slight
declines. However, the freshman basketball team led all others in scholarship, with
the varsity track men second. The varsity football team finished third in the
scholarship race last year with the freshman track team fourth.
