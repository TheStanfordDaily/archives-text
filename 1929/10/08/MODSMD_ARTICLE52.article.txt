TUESDAY
3 ,10 p.m.—Cap and Gown tea for all
*iew women at the home of Dean
Yost.
4 15 p.m.—Organ recital in the Me
morial Church.
7:00 p.m.—Tryouts for the University
brchestra in Room 84.
7:3op.m.—Lecture on "Modern Ten
dencies in Italian Literature" by
Professor Guido Ferrando of the
University of Florence; in the As
sembly Hall.
THURSDAY
4:15 p. m/ —Organ recital in the Me
morial Church.
4:15 p.m.—Lecture, "Concerning Mus
cular Fatigue," by Dr. L. A. Orbeli,
Professor of Physiology of the
Medical Institute of Leningrad,
Russia; in the Little Theatre.
4:45 p.m.—Vesper Service, in the Me
morial Church. Speaker, Reverend
Reed B. Cherington.
MISCELLANEOUS
All those wishing to try out for sopho
more track manager report this af
ternoon at the Old Oval. Tryoutees
will receive gym credit for their
work three times a week.
All men wishing to work at football
games this fall should sign up at the
Board of Athletic Control office be
fore Friday, October 4.
Men wishing to sell programs at the
U.S.C., California, and Army games
see Marshall Taylor, 110 Toyon, or
phone P.A. 3297.
