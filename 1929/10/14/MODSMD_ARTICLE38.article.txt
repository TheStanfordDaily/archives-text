# ANNOUNCEMENTS

MONDAY
1:30 p.m.—Tryouts for membership on
the varsity debating squad will be
held in the Little Theatre. Open to
all men students having no varsity
experience except freshmen and
graduates.
3:00 p.m.—Meeting of the Faculty
Women's Club in the Women's Club
house. Musical program by Dr.
Henry Lanz.
7:00 p.m.—Rehearsal of Act I for "The
Ivory Door" will be held in the As
sembly Hall.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of all Masons in
terested in forming a Stanford Ma
sonic Club to be held in Room 425,
Encina.
8:30 p.m.—Rehearsal of Act 11, Scene
1, for "The Ivory Door" to be held
in the Assembly Hall.
9:00 p.m.—Rehearsal of Act 11, scene
2, for "The Ivory Door" to be held in
the Assembly Hall.
TUESDAY
3:00 p.m.—Stanford Mother's Club, in
the Stanford Women's Clubhouse.
Dean Yost will speak. Mothers and
friends of students are invited to
attend.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Mr. War
ren D. Allen, University Organist,
in the Memorial Church.
7:15 p.m.—A meeting of the Sopho
more class will be held in Room 214
in the English Building. All mem
bers be present.
7:30 p.m.—All men wishing to apply
for positions as Assembly Hall ush
ers meet in Little Theatre.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Labour Party
Personalities and Policies," by Mr.
George Young, British diplomatist,
journalist, and scholar; in the As
sembly Hall. The public is invited
to attend the series of which this
is the first lecture.
WEDNESDAY
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Post-War Con
stitutional Changes in Europe," by
Mr. George Young; in the Assembly
Hall.
7:30 p.m.—The English Club will hold
an open meeting in the basement
of the Women's Clubhouse. There
will be a program. All members are
urged to come.
8:00 p.m. — Faculty Research Club
meeting in the Faculty Clubhouse.
Papers: "The Atom —Model 1929,"
by Professor G. R. Harrison; "The
Medieval Literature of Mexico and
Central America," by Mr. Nathan
Van Patten.
8:30 p.m.—Chi Nu Kappa will meet in
the basement of the Women's Club
house. Dr. B. O. Foster will speak.
THURSDAY
3:00 p.m.—Stanford Dames meeting in
the Women's Clubhouse. Wives of
students are invited to attend.
4:15 p.m.—Organ Recital in the Me
morial Church.
4:45 p.m.—Vesper Service in the Me
morial Church.| Speaker: Rev. Reed
B. Cherington.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Command and
Freedom of the Seas," by Mr.
George Young; in the Assembly
Hall.

see Marshall Taylor, 110 Toyon, or
