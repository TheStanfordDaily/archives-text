TUESDAY
3:00 p.m.—Stanford Mother's Club, in
the Stanford Women's Clubhouse.
Dean Yost will speak. Mothers and
friends of students are invited to
attend.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Mr. War
ren D. Allen, University Organist,
in the Memorial Church.
7:00 p.m.—Rifle Club to meet in room
231 for election of officers. Open
meeting. Anyone interested invited
to attend.
7:00 p.m.—Additional tryouts for the
University Orchestra will be held
in room 84.
7:15 p.m.—A meeting of the Sopho
more class will be held in Room 214
in the English Building. All mem
bers be present.
7:30 p.m.—All men wishing to apply
for positions as Assembly Hall ush
ers meet in Little Theatre.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Labour Party
Personalities and Policies," by Mr.
George Young, British diplomatist,
journalist, and scholar; in the As
sembly Hall. The public is invited
to attend the series of which this
is the first lecture.
WEDNESDAY
7:15 p.m.—Women's Assembly in Wo
men's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m.—Talk by Dr. A. W. Herre,
ichthyologist with the Crane South

Prof. J. O. Snyder, 542 Alvarado
Row, under auspices of the Zoology
Club. Open to students and faculty
in the biological sciences and oth
ers interested.

stitutional Changes in Europe," by
Mr. George Young; in the Assembly
Hall.

an open meeting in the basement
of the Women's Clubhouse. There
will be a program. All members are
urged to come.

meeting in the Faculty Clubhouse.
Papers: "The Atom —Model 1929,"
by Professor G. R. Harrison; "The
Medieval Literature of Mexico and

Van Patten.

the basement of the Women's Club
