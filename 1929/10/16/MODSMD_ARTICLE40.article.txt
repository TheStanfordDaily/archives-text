# ANNOUNCEMENTS

WEDNESDAY
12:00 noon—A meeting of the Cosmo
politan Club will be held in the
Stanford Union. All students are
invited to attend.
7:15 p.m.—Women's Assembly in Wo
men's Clubhouse.
7:150 p.m.—The Stanford Radio Club
will meet in Room 2G3 of the Civil
Engineering Building.
7:30 p.m.—Talk by Dr. A. W. Herre,
ichthyologist with the Crane South
Pacific Expedition, at the home of
Prof. J. O. Snyder, 542 Alvarado
Row, under auspices of the Zoology
Club. Open to students and faculty
in the biological sciences and oth
ers interested.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Post-War Con
stitutional Changes in Europe," by
Mr. George Young; in the Assembly
Hall.
8:00 p.m. — Faculty Research Club
meeting in the Faculty Clubhouse.
Papers: "The Atom—Model 1929,"
by Professor G. It. Harrison; "The
Medieval Literature of Mexico and
Central America," by Mr. Nathan
Van Patten.
8:30 p.m.—Chi Nu Kappa will meet in
the basement of the Women's Club
house. Dr. B. O. Foster will speak.
THURSDAY
3:00 p.m.—Stanford Dames meeting in
the Women's Clubhouse. Wives of
students are invited to attend.
3:00 p.m.—There will be a meeting of
the Stanford Dames in the Women's
Clubhouse. Wives of all are cor
dially invited.
4:15 p.m.—Organ Recital in the Me
morial Church.
4:30 p.m.—Pi Lambda Theta business
meeting in the Women's Clubhouse.
4:4sp.m.—Vesper Service in the Me
morial Church.| Speaker: Rev. Reed
B. Cherington.
7:30 p.m.—The English Club will hold
an open meeting in the basement of
the Women's Clubhouse. There will
be a program. All members are
urged to attend.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Command and
Freedom of the Seas," by Mr.
George Young; in the Assembly
Hall.
FRIDAY
6:15 p.m.—Pi Sigma Alpha will meet
for dinner in the Union. George
Young will be guest of honor.
