THURSDAY
3:00 p.m.—Stanford Dames meeting in
the Women's Clubhouse. Wives of
students are invited to attend.
3:00 p.m.—There will be a meeting of
the Stanford Dames in the Women's
Clubhouse. Wives of all are cor
dially invited.
Sophomore men wishing to try out
for the position of tennis manager
are instructed to report at the ten
nis courts between two and four
o'clock this afternoon.
4:15 p.m.—Organ Recital in the Me
morial Church.
4:30 p.m.—Fellowship Tea on the
third floor of the Women's Club
house. Everyone is invited.
4:30 p.m.—Pi Lambda Theta business
meeting in the Women's Clubhouse.
4:45 p.m.—Vesper Service in the Me
morial Church.| Speaker: Rev. Reed
B. Cherington.
7:30 p.m.—The English Club will hold
an open meeting in the basement of
the Women's Clubhouse. There will
be a program. All members are
urged to attend.
7:30 p.m.—All students who have
sung in the choir are asked to at
tend a meeting at the Memorial
Church.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Command and
Freedom of the Seas," by Mr.
George Young; in the Assembly
Hall.
FRIDAY
Sophomore men wishing to try out
for the position of tennis manager
are instructed to report at the ten
nis courts between two and four

o'clock this afternoon.
3 to 5 p.m.—Dean Yost will he at home
to all foreign students and mem
bers of the Cosmopolitan Club.
C :15 p.m.—Pi Sigma Alpha will meet
for dinner in the Union. George
Young will be guest of honor.
