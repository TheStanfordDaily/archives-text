# STANFORD ATHLETES NOT GIVEN SPECIAL ADVANTAGES--SWAIN
## 
### 
When interviewed late last
night in regard to the Carnegie
Foundation charges of Stanford's
subsidation of athletes Acting-
President Robert E. Swain made
the following statement:
"The policy of this University
in regard to athletes is clearly
defined. They are accorded no
special privileges in this Univer
sity. They are dealt with pre
cisely on the same basis as other
students.
"In meeting requirements for
admission to the University and
for remaining here after they
are admitted they do not repre
sent a preferred class in any of
their relations to the University
nor are they denied any privi
leges which are extended to non
athletes."
