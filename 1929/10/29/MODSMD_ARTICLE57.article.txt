TUESDAY
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Mr. Allen.
6:30 p.m.—Wranglers supper meeting
in Women's Clubhouse.
T-8 p.m.—Senior Jolly-up In Women's
Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Modern French
Pootry: Madame de Noailles," by
Abb 6 Dimnet; in the Assembly Hall.
The public is invited.

Debating Society in Itoom 214 of
the English Building. There will be
a discussion on plans for reorganiza
tion.
WEDNESDAY
7:15 p.m.—Scabbard and Blade holds
first meeting in Room 201, English
Building. Important business will
be taken up including discussion of
prospective members. All members
are expected to be present.

Poetry: Paul Valery," by Abb 6 Dim
net; Assembly Hall.
7:30 p.m.—Special meeting of the
English Club to reorganize constitu
tion and act upon important busi
ness, Women's Clubhouse, first
floor. All members who do not at
tend or send a written explanation
to the president, Box 2461, will auto
matically sever their affiliations.
7:30 p.m.—Chi Nu Kappa, honorary
classical literature society, will
hold the first business meeting of
the quarter in the library of the
classical literature building.
12:00 noon —Fellowship Board meets
for luncheon in the Union.

Rev. R. B. Clierington.
7:15 p.m. — International Relations
Club meeting in the West Memorial
Library. Discussion of current
events.
FRIDAY
7:30 p.m.—Assembly Hall ushers for
"The Ivory Door" are asked to appear.
8:20 p.m.—"The Ivory Door," by A. A.
Milne, presented by Dramatic Coun
cil under the direction of Harold
Helvenston; in the Assembly Hall.
The box office will open at 7 p.m.
MISCELLANEOUS
ALL VARSITY and freshman golfers
who expect to be on the teams, get
in touch with Doug Aiken at the
A.T.O. house.
All kinds of pictures showing wo
men smoking are prohibited in Lynn,
Massachusetts.
TUESDAY
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Mr. Allen.
6:30 p.m.—Wranglers supper meeting
in Women's Clubhouse.
T-8 p.m.—Senior Jolly-up In Women's
Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Modern French
Pootry: Madame de Noailles," by
Abb 6 Dimnet; in the Assembly Hall.
The public is invited.
8:15 p.m.—Meeting of the Euphronia
Debating Society in Itoom 214 of
the English Building. There will be
a discussion on plans for reorganiza
tion.
WEDNESDAY
7:15 p.m.—Scabbard and Blade holds
first meeting in Room 201, English
Building. Important business will
be taken up including discussion of
prospective members. All members
are expected to be present.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Modern French
Poetry: Paul Valery," by Abb 6 Dim
net; Assembly Hall.
7:30 p.m.—Special meeting of the
English Club to reorganize constitu
tion and act upon important busi
ness, Women's Clubhouse, first
floor. All members who do not at
tend or send a written explanation
to the president, Box 2461, will auto
matically sever their affiliations.
7:30 p.m.—Chi Nu Kappa, honorary
classical literature society, will
hold the first business meeting of
the quarter in the library of the
classical literature building.
12:00 noon —Fellowship Board meets
for luncheon in the Union.
