# Henry Brunier To Address Society Of Civil Engineers
## 
### 
Speaking on "The practical aspects
of engineering, as faced by the young
engineer when he graduates from uni
versity," Henry J. Brunier, structural
engineer of San Francisco, will talk
to members of the Stanford Branch,
American Society of Civil Engineers,
at its first social meeting of the year
tonight at 8 o'clock in the home of
Professor Charles Moser, on Foot
hill Road.
Invitations to the meeting have
been sent to all civil engineering ma
jors in the University, and all those
interested in civil engineering are
urged to attend.
Donald Carlson, a member of the
Stanford chapter, will give a short
talk about the new bridge that the
Southern Pacific is building across
Suisuin Bay, north of San Francisco.
