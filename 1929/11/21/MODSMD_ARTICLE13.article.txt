# HELVENSTON ASKS FOR TRYOUTEES FOR SOPH CLASS PLAY SATURDAY
## 
### 
First tryouts for the sophomore play
will take place this Saturday morning
from 10 to 12 o'clock, in the Assem
bly Hall, announces Harold Helven
ston, acting director of dramatics.
The play, which will be given on
December 6, is the work of two of the
best-known and most-popular play
wrights in New York today. Other
dramas by each of these men have
been included in the Burns-Mantle
collection of "Best Plays of 1920."
As there is so little time between
the Gaieties and the sophomore play,
final tryouts will be held Monday
night from 7 to 9 o'clock, and re
hearsals will begin Tuesday night.
The play will necessitate a good-sized
cast, and all sophomores are requested
to appear at tryouts.
