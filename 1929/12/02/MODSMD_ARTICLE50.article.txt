MONDAY
1:15 p.m.—Sigma Delta Chi meeting
in the editorial offices of the Daily.
4:15 p.m. — Chemistry Seminar, in
Room 20, Chemistry Building. Pro
fessor J. M. Luck will speak on
"Chemistry of the Vitamines and
Internal Secretions."
EXAMINATION for the Doctorate,
Chien P'ei (Major, Botany; Minor,
Paleontology). Written examination.

didate should make arrangements
for so doing through the Depart
ment of Botany.

men's debate in the Little Theatre.
Question, "Resolved, That the
breakdown of conventions is detri
mental to the youth of today."
TUESDAY
3:00 p.m.—Oral Examination for the
Doctorate, Sibyl Terman (Major,
Psychology; Minor, Education),
Room 308, Library.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital in the Me
morial Church.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "The Institute
of Pacific Relations: Manchuria and
Extraterritoriality," by Charles K.
Webster, Professor of International
Politics, University of Wales; in
the Assembly Hall. The public is
invited.
8:00 p.m.—Ushers for the Concert
Series will meet in the Pavilion. All
not present will be cut.
WEDNESDAY
8:15 p.m.—The A.S.S.U. Concert with
the San Francisco Symphony and
Ignaz Friedman, pianist, will be held
in the Pavilion.
THURSDAY
3:00 p.m. —Oral Examination for the
Doctorate, John M. Willits (Maj<, \
Psychology; Minor, Education),
Room 308, Library.

at home at 634 Mirada.

morial Church. Rev. Oscar Green
will speak.

morial Church.

the Sigma Kappa house, 550 Salva
tierra. Mr. F. A. Davidson of the

at Stanford will give an illustrated
talk on "Biological Evidences of the

dents the biological sciences.
8:30 p.m.—Le Cercle Frangais, in the
Women's Clubhouse.
FRIDAY

the Faculty Clubhouse.

Alto.
