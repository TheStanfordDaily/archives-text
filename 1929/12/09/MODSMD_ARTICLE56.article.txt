MONDAY
4:15 p.m.—Chemistry Seminar in
Room 20, Chemistry Building. Dr.
Robert Dußois will speak on "Ac
tive Nitrogen."

home of Dr. Kennedy, 435 Coleridge,
Palo Alto.

3:oop.m.—Stanford Mothers' Club in
the Women's Clubhouse. Mr. War
ren D. Allen, assisted by Mrs. Allen,

sity organist, will present the regu
lar Thursday organ recital.

sia" by Mr. Henry Cowell in the
Assembly Hall. The public is in
vited.

4:00 p.m.—Miss Yost will be at home
informally, from four to six, to all
University students at her home,
532 Lasuen.

ulty Clubhouse. Papers: "Some

Phenomena," by Professor Fernando
Sanford; "Some Phases of Land
Colonization in California," by Pro
fessor H. S. Anderson.

tle Theatre by the class in Play
Directing under the supervision of
Mr. Harold Helvenston; "Fancy
Free," by Stanley Houghton; "Min
nie Field," by Ellsworth P. Conkle;
"The Mayor and the Manicure," by
George Ade Dregs. Open to the pub
lic.
