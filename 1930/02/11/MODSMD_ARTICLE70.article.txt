# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
TUESDAY
2:30 p.m.—The reading group of
the Stanford Dames will meet at
the home of Mrs. J. K. Arkush,
522 Webster street.
4:00 p.m.—Dr. Clelia I). Mosher will
receive all University students
until 0 o'clock at her home, 7ti4
Santa Ynez.
4:15 p.m.—The regular Tuesday af
ternoon organ recital in the Me
morial Church by Warren 1). Al
len.
7:3op.m.—Assembly Hall lecture:
"Vergil's Prophecy of a Messiah
—The Fourth Eclogue," by Pro
fessor Marbury 13. Ogle of Ohio
State University.
WEDNESDAY
7:oop.m.—Women's Glee Club will
meet for practice on the third
floor of the Women's Clubhouse.
8:00 p.m.—Research Club meeting
in the Faculty Clubhouse. Papers
will be read by Professors A. F.
Rogers and A. C. Whitaker.
8:15 p.m.—Hawaii-Stanford debate
in the Assembly Hall, "Resolved,
That English Criticism of Ameri
can Education Is Justified."
THURSDAY
4:15 p.m.—Vesper service in the
Memorial Church. Speaker, Dr.
Ross. Followed by Warren I). Al
len's regular Thursday afternoon
organ recital at 4:30.
MISCELLANEOUS
TICKETS for the A.S.S.I', concert,
presenting Lawrence Tibbett,
baritone, are on sale al. $1, $1.50,
and $2 at the A.S.S.U. office ol'
the Old Union and at Dodson's
in Palo Alto.
This is the last week for entries
in the 1930 Junior Opera-writing
contest. All those who intend to
try for the $50 prize to be given
to the author of this year's Jun
ior Opera must turn in a draft
of the plot they intend to use to
Katherine Keho at the Kappa
house before noon, Monday, Feb
ruary 17.
HEIRS TO VISIT
TOKYO, Feb. 10. (/P) —Heirs to
two European thrones are expect
ed to visit Japan this year and
next, and the imperial household is
making preparations accordingly.
