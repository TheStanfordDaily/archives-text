# ENGINEERS RECEIVE BENEFIT FROM NEW PLACEMENT AGENCY
## 
### 
The recently formed committee
on Employment Relations for En
gineers is already achieving grati
fying results, according to Profes
sor Guiclo H. Marx, chairman of
the committee. Professor Marx
stated that about one-third of the
men of the Engineering School
have filled out employment blanks
and that "recruiting representa
tives of about 12 of the larger in
dustrial organizations and corpora
tions have interviewed students
and received applications for em
ployment."
Summer work of a professionally
profitable type for all engineers is
the goal toward which the commit
tee is now working, Marx ex
plained.
