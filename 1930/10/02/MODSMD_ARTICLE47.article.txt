THURSDAY
3:00 p.m.—Members of the "B" Polo
Squad to report for practice.
8:00 p.m.—There will be an open
meeting of the Socialist Party at
Union Hall. 255 University Ave
nue. There will be a review of
"Now It Must Be Done" by St.
John Tucker.
8:30 p.m.—Acting - President and
Mrs. Robert Eckles Swain will
be at home to the faculty and
their wives from 8:30 until 11
o'clock at the President's House.
FRIDAY
12 noon—There will be a meeting
of tiie Roger Williams Club in
the Alcove of the Stanford Union
on Friday at 12:10. All Baptist
students are welcome.
1:30 p.m. —Members of .the "A" Polo
Squad to report for practice.
8:00 p.m. — Acting-President and
Mrs. Robert Eckles Swain will be
at home informally to all new
students at 8 o'clock Friday eve
ning at the President's House.
TUESDAY
7:15 p.m.—Stanford Symphony Or
chestra will hold joint business
and practice meeting in Roble
Gymnasium. Tryoutees, particu
larly string instrumentalists, are
requested to report at that time.
WEDNESDAY. OCT. s
7:30 p.m.—Meeting American Society
of Civil Engineers, engineering
library.
MISCELLANEOUS
WOMEN INTERESTED in usher
ing at the A.S.S.U. Concert Series
should apply at once to Jane
Snyder, Roble Hall, P.O. Box:
2434. No experience is necessary.
ALL MEN wishing to usher at the
A.S.S.U. concerts this year make
application to Chas. Hamman,
Box 1151, Campus.
ALL MEN wishing to sell programs
at the U.S.C., Washington, and
Dartmouth football games leave
word for Marshall Taylor at the
B.A.C.
SOPHOMORES wishing to try out
for swimming managerships should
see Ted Smith at Eneina pool,
10-12 a.m. and 2-4 p.m.
