# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
WEDNESDAY
1-4 p.m.—All freshman women try
ing out for women's manager
should call at the Chaparral of
fice, in the Press Building.
3:30 p.m.—Meeting of Sigma Delta
Chi in room 9 Journalism Build
ing.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of American So
ciety of Mechanical Engineers,
clubroom.
7:30 p.m.—All those interested in
shooting or learning to shoot
should attend the meeting of the
Rifle Club in room 231.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting American Society
of Civil Engineers, engineering
library.
7:30 p.m.—Stanford DeMolay Club
will hold open meeting in the
Y.M.C.A. lobby of Old Union.
7:30 p.m.—Tryouts for the debate
squad and for the English debate
will be held in the Little Theatre.
Candidates must sign up at once
on lists posted on the bulletin
board outside Professor Emerson's
office, Room 200 C, English Build
ing.
THURSDAY
7:30 p.m.—Chaparral tryoutee meet
ing, Chaparral office.
7:30 p.m.—Christian Science' Society
will meet on top floor of Women's
Clubhouse.
FRIDAY
7:00 p.m.—The Stanford Flying Club
will meet at the Palo Alto Airport.
MISCELLANEOUS
ALL SOPHOMORES desiring to
try out for tennis manager may
sign up with Herb Hazeltine, sen
ior manager, at the varsity courts
any afternoon this week between
2 and 4 o'clock.
ALL MEN wishing to sell programs
at the U.S.C., Washington, and
Dartmouth football games leave
word for Marshall Taylor at the
B.A.C.

SOPHOMORES wishing u» tiy oul
for swimming managerships should
see Ted Smith at Encina pool,
19-12 a.m. and 2-4 p.m.
CONCERT TICKETS may be called
for at the Concert office in the
Old Union.
TRYOUTS for sophomore swimming
managerships will be held this
week. All sophomores interested
may see Ted Smith, assistant
swimming coach, at the Encina
pools, any afternoon this week.
The positions are open to any
members of the class, and Lower
Division physical education credit
will be given for the work.
SOPHOMORES who are interested
in trying out for boxing manager
are requested to report Monday,
Wednesday, and Friday at 5 p.m.
at the boxing rooms of the En
cina Gym.

managerships will be held this
week. All sophomores interested
may see Ted Smith, assistant
swimming coach, at the Encina
pools, any afternoon this week.
The positions are open to any
members of the class, and Lower
Division physical education credit
will be given for the work.
SOPHOMORES who are interested
in trying out for boxing manager
are requested to report Monday,
Wednesday, and Friday at 5 p.m.
at the boxing rooms of the En
cina Gym.
WEDNESDAY
1-4 p.m.—All freshman women try
ing out for women's manager
should call at the Chaparral of
fice, in the Press Building.
3:30 p.m.—Meeting of Sigma Delta
Chi in room 9 Journalism Build
ing.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of American So
ciety of Mechanical Engineers,
clubroom.
7:30 p.m.—All those interested in
shooting or learning to shoot
should attend the meeting of the
Rifle Club in room 231.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting American Society
of Civil Engineers, engineering
library.
7:30 p.m.—Stanford DeMolay Club
will hold open meeting in the
Y.M.C.A. lobby of Old Union.
7:30 p.m.—Tryouts for the debate
squad and for the English debate
will be held in the Little Theatre.
Candidates must sign up at once
on lists posted on the bulletin
board outside Professor Emerson's
office, Room 200 C, English Build
ing.
THURSDAY
7:30 p.m.—Chaparral tryoutee meet
ing, Chaparral office.
7:30 p.m.—Christian Science' Society
will meet on top floor of Women's
Clubhouse.
FRIDAY
7:00 p.m.—The Stanford Flying Club
will meet at the Palo Alto Airport.
