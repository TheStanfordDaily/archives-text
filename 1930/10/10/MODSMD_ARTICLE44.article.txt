FRIDAY
9 to 11 a.m. and 12:45 to 4 p.m.—
Senior photos for Quad to be
taken in Quad office in Old Union.
12 noon—The Board of Directors of
the Stanford Y.M.C.A. will meet
for lunch in the Alcove of the
Union at noon today.
12:10 p.m.—Weekly luncheon of the
Roger Williams Club in Stanford
Union alcove, room 10. Students
and friends welcome.
will meet at the Palo Alto Airport.
7:30 p.m.—An "All Wesleyan"' party
to be given at home of Professor
and Mrs. J. Hugh Jackson, 445 El
Escarpado Avenue. Methodist stu
dents, friends, and faculty mem
bers invited.
MONDAY
12 noon—Luncheon meeting on the
third floor of the Women's Club
house for all women students who
do not live at Roble or in sorority
houses. Reservations from Lois
Ruth Bailey, P.A. 39.04.
6:00 p.m.—A "weenie" roast will be
held near Lagur.ita for all junior
women living in Roble or off the
campus. Roble women are to sign
on the bulletin board; commuters
are to phone Lois Ruth Bailey,
P.A. 3934.
MISCELLANEOUS
COSMOPOLITAN CLUB picnic Sat
urday at Sam McDowell's cabin,
near La Honda. Members and vte
itors, American and foreign, are
invited to meet at 4 o'clock in the
Union Court. Transportation pro
vided. Notify Alice Behner, Roble,
or Denny Woodworth, Sequoia
Hall.

ALL MEN wishing to sell programs
at the U.S.C., Washington, and
Dartmouth football games leave
word for Marshall Taylor at the
B.A.C.
CONCERT TICKETS may be called
for at the Concert office in the
Old Union.
TRYOUTS for sophomore swimming
managerships will be held this
week. All sophomores interested
may see Ted Smith, assistant
swimming coach, at the Encina
pools, any afternoon this week.
The positions are open to any
members of the class, and Lower ,
