TUESDAY'
12:30—All officers holding reserve
commissions in Field Artillery will
meet at Captain Williams' office in
the Military Department building.
1:00 p.m.—Tickets for "Mrs. Bump
stead-Leigh," comedy to be pre
sented Friday night, will go on
sale in front of the Women's
Clubhouse and at Keebles in Palo
Alto.
3:00 p.m.—Meeting of the Mothers
Club in the Women's Clubhouse.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital to be given
by Warren D. Allen in the Me
morial Church.
7:00 p.m.—Meeting of ushers for As
sembly Hall productions in room
214, History Building.
7:15 p.m.—Rehearsal of the Stanford
Symphony Orchestra in the Roble
Gymnasium.
7:30 p.m.—The American Institute of
Electrical Engineers will meet in
room 280.
8 00 p.m. —Rehearsal of all sopranos
and basses of the oratorio chorus
in room 34.
WEDNESDAY
8:30 p.m.—The Cercle Francais will
meet in the Women's Clubhouse.
THURSDAY
1:00 p.m.—Sigma Delta Chi meeting
in room 9, Journalism Building.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital to be given
by Warren D. Allen in the Me
morial Church.
7:30 p.m.—Joint meeting of the San
Francisco sections of the A.S.M.E.
and A.I.E.E. at the P.G.&E. audi
torium, 245 Market St. Robert
Sibley will speak on the World
Power Conference. Dinner at the
Engineer's Club in S.F. at 6 p.m.,
$1.50 per plate.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of the Christian
Science Society on the top floor
of the Women's Clubhouse.
8:00 p.m.—Rehearsal of all altos and
tenors of the oratorio chorus in
room 84. No students who have
not attended all of the previous
lehearsals can enroll now.
FRIDAY
1:00 p.m.—Anyone wanting to try
out for sophomore or junior polo
manager report to Edwin Clarke
at the Encina polo field.
7:20 p.m. — Ushers for "Mrs. Bump
stead-Leigh" will report at the
Assembly Hall.

out for sophomore or junior polo
manager report to Edwin Clarke
at the Encina polo field.
7:20 p.m. — Ushers for "Mrs. Bump
stead-Leigh" will report at the
Assembly Hall.
MISCELLANEOUS
CLASS PRESIDENTS should ap
point. managers for interclass
touch football and basketball at
once. The football schedule will
begin October 20.
TUESDAY'
12:30—All officers holding reserve
commissions in Field Artillery will
meet at Captain Williams' office in
the Military Department building.
1:00 p.m.—Tickets for "Mrs. Bump
stead-Leigh," comedy to be pre
sented Friday night, will go on
sale in front of the Women's
Clubhouse and at Keebles in Palo
Alto.
3:00 p.m.—Meeting of the Mothers
Club in the Women's Clubhouse.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital to be given
by Warren D. Allen in the Me
morial Church.
7:00 p.m.—Meeting of ushers for As
sembly Hall productions in room
214, History Building.
7:15 p.m.—Rehearsal of the Stanford
Symphony Orchestra in the Roble
Gymnasium.
7:30 p.m.—The American Institute of
Electrical Engineers will meet in
room 280.
8 00 p.m. —Rehearsal of all sopranos
and basses of the oratorio chorus
in room 34.
WEDNESDAY
8:30 p.m.—The Cercle Francais will
meet in the Women's Clubhouse.
THURSDAY
1:00 p.m.—Sigma Delta Chi meeting
in room 9, Journalism Building.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital to be given
by Warren D. Allen in the Me
morial Church.
7:30 p.m.—Joint meeting of the San
Francisco sections of the A.S.M.E.
and A.I.E.E. at the P.G.&E. audi
torium, 245 Market St. Robert
Sibley will speak on the World
Power Conference. Dinner at the
Engineer's Club in S.F. at 6 p.m.,
$1.50 per plate.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of the Christian
Science Society on the top floor
of the Women's Clubhouse.
8:00 p.m.—Rehearsal of all altos and
tenors of the oratorio chorus in
room 84. No students who have
not attended all of the previous
lehearsals can enroll now.
