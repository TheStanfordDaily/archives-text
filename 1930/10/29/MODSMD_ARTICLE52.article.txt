# NEW SCHEDULE FOR MAIL ANNOUNCED BY LOCAL POSTMASTER
## 
### 
According to an announcement by
W. C. Werry, Stanford postmaster,
the following mail schedule is now
in effect.
Mail is distributed to the boxes
within one hour after it arrives.
Mail from the eastern states arrives
at 7:30 a.m. and 12:30 a.m., from
southern California and the south
ern states at 7:30 a.m. and 9:30 a.m.,
and from the northwestern states,
San Francisco, and the Bay Region,
at 7:30 a.m., 12:30 a.m., 4 p.m., and
5 p.m. The first collection of out
going mail is made at 6 a.m., and
the last at 7:45 p.m.
Air mail to the East leaves San
Francisco every morning and eve
ning except Monday morning. Mail
deposited in the Stanford postoffice
between 3:40 and 6 p.m. connects
with the plane leaving Oakland at
8 a.m. Mail deposited at all other
times connects with the plane leav
ing Oakland at 8 p.m. For the most
advantageous dispatch to eastern
points, air mail should be deposited
at the postoffice between 3 and
3:40 p.m.
Air mail for points between Seat
tle and San Diego must be put in
the drops before 6 p.m. to connect
with the plane leaving Oakland each
morning.
