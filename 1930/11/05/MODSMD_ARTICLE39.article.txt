WEDNESDAY
3 to 5 p.m.—Chrysanthemum Tea at
Hoover home given by Mothers'
Club. Admission by ticket only.
4-6 p.m.—Practice of the degree
team of the Stanford Masonic
Club at the Masonic
Alto.
7:15 p.m.—There will be a rehearsal
of the Stanford Symphony Or
chestra in the Roble Gymnasium.
Tryouts will be held for flute and
'cello players.
7:30 p.m. — The English Club will
meet tonight at the home of Web
ster Street in Palo Alto.
7:30 p.m.—Stanford Flying Club will
meet at Palo Alto School of Avia
tion. Meeting open to all students
interested.
8:00 p.m.—Sigma Xi will meet in
the Faculty Clubhouse. Professor
Perry Byerly of the University of
California will speak on "The In
terior of the Earth."
THURSDAY
7:15 p.m.—Tryoutees for the editorial
staff of the Quad will meet in the
Press Building.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of all members
of the Stanford Masonic Club in
the Old Union.
7:30 p.m.—The Cardinal Chess Club
will meet in the lobby of the Old
Union. All members should at
tend.
8:00 p.m.—Maeting of the Socialist
Party in Union Hall for a discus
sion of future plans. All inter

ested are invited.
FRIDAY
9 n.m.-4 p.m.—Tickets for Brahm's
"Requiem" will be distributed to
faculty members and students in
the lobby of the Administration
Building. Tickets may be obtained
on presentation of student body
cards.
8:00 p.m.—Faculty Club meeting in
the Faculty Clubhouse.
MONDAY
12 noon — Woman commuters will
meet for luncheon on the third
floor of the Women's Clubhouse.
Miss Yost will be the guest of
honor. Reservations must be made
with Lois Ruth Bailey, P.A. 3934,
before Saturday.

STUDENTS in abnormal psychology

WEDNESDAY
3 to 5 p.m.—Chrysanthemum Tea at
Hoover home given by Mothers'
Club. Admission by ticket only.
4-6 p.m.—Practice of the degree
team of the Stanford Masonic
Club at the Masonic
Alto.
7:15 p.m.—There will be a rehearsal
of the Stanford Symphony Or
chestra in the Roble Gymnasium.
Tryouts will be held for flute and
'cello players.
7:30 p.m. — The English Club will
meet tonight at the home of Web
ster Street in Palo Alto.
7:30 p.m.—Stanford Flying Club will
meet at Palo Alto School of Avia
tion. Meeting open to all students
interested.
8:00 p.m.—Sigma Xi will meet in
the Faculty Clubhouse. Professor
Perry Byerly of the University of
California will speak on "The In
terior of the Earth."
THURSDAY
7:15 p.m.—Tryoutees for the editorial
staff of the Quad will meet in the
Press Building.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of all members
of the Stanford Masonic Club in
the Old Union.
7:30 p.m.—The Cardinal Chess Club
will meet in the lobby of the Old
Union. All members should at
tend.
8:00 p.m.—Maeting of the Socialist
Party in Union Hall for a discus
sion of future plans. All inter

ested are invited.
FRIDAY
9 n.m.-4 p.m.—Tickets for Brahm's
"Requiem" will be distributed to
faculty members and students in
the lobby of the Administration
Building. Tickets may be obtained
on presentation of student body
cards.
8:00 p.m.—Faculty Club meeting in
the Faculty Clubhouse.
MONDAY
12 noon — Woman commuters will
meet for luncheon on the third
floor of the Women's Clubhouse.
Miss Yost will be the guest of
honor. Reservations must be made
with Lois Ruth Bailey, P.A. 3934,
before Saturday.
