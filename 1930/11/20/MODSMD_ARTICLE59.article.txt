# ANNOUNCEMENTS

THURSDAY
3 p. m.—Professor Henry Lanz of
the Slavic Languages department
will address the Stanford Dames
at their meeting in the basement
of the Women's Clubhouse.
6:15 p. m. —Pi Sigma Alpha dinner
and meeting in Union. Ernest B.
Price will speak on China.
7:30 p.m.—Stanford Flying Club
meeting at Palo Alto School of
Aviation followed by a supper
party at Mrs. Gorman's Atherton
residence. Those who will attend
please notify the Airport Secre
tary, P.A. 8313, prior to 5 p.m.,
Thursday. Special invitation ex
tended to prospective members.
8:15 p. m.—There will be a very im
portant meeting of the Stanford
Fellowship Board in the Y.M.C.A.
rooms of the Old Union.
8:15 p.m.—There will be an import
ant meeting of the Stanford Fel
lowship Board in the Y.M.C.A.
rooms in the Old Union.
FRIDAY
7:30 p.m.—Ushers for Big Game
Gaieties report at Assembly Hall.
8:15 p.m.—"Big Game Gaieties of
1930," annual Ram's Head Musical
Show, presented under the direc
tion of Harold Helvenston. As
sembly Hall.
MONDAY
12:00 noon —Woman commuters will
meet for luncheon on the third
floor of the Women's Clubhouse.
Reservations may be made with
Lois Ruth Bailey, P.A. 3934, to
morrow.
MISCELLANEOUS
THE STANFORD GLEE CLUB will
not sing at the Fox Theatre's mid
nite show, according to William
Dienstein, manager.
Men now living in Encina Hall who
wish to reserve their rooms for
winter quarter should call at the
Dean of Men's Offlce Monday,
Tuesday or Wednesday, November
24, 25, or 26, and pay deposits.
ALL MEN desiring to sell Chaparral
at the Big Game are requested

to call at the Chaparral office be
tween 1 and 2 o'clock any after
noon or call Ray Winther at
P.A. 8137.
ALL MEN wishing to play in the
interclass basketball champion
ship must sign their names on a
list to be posted on the bulletin
board in the gymnasium at the
beginning of this week.
STUDENTS DESIRING tickets for
the football dinner at the Fair
mont Hotel on Big Game night
should make reservations imme
diately at the office of the Alumni
Secretary in room 174 of the Ad
ministration Building.

THURSDAY
3 p. m.—Professor Henry Lanz of
the Slavic Languages department
will address the Stanford Dames
at their meeting in the basement
of the Women's Clubhouse.
6:15 p. m. —Pi Sigma Alpha dinner
and meeting in Union. Ernest B.
Price will speak on China.
7:30 p.m.—Stanford Flying Club
meeting at Palo Alto School of
Aviation followed by a supper
party at Mrs. Gorman's Atherton
residence. Those who will attend
please notify the Airport Secre
tary, P.A. 8313, prior to 5 p.m.,
Thursday. Special invitation ex
tended to prospective members.
8:15 p. m.—There will be a very im
portant meeting of the Stanford
Fellowship Board in the Y.M.C.A.
rooms of the Old Union.
8:15 p.m.—There will be an import
ant meeting of the Stanford Fel
lowship Board in the Y.M.C.A.
rooms in the Old Union.
FRIDAY
7:30 p.m.—Ushers for Big Game
Gaieties report at Assembly Hall.
8:15 p.m.—"Big Game Gaieties of
1930," annual Ram's Head Musical
Show, presented under the direc
tion of Harold Helvenston. As
sembly Hall.
MONDAY
12:00 noon —Woman commuters will
meet for luncheon on the third
floor of the Women's Clubhouse.
Reservations may be made with
Lois Ruth Bailey, P.A. 3934, to
morrow.
