# PLYMIRE DEFEATS COUGHLIN, RETAINS VARSITY STANDING
## 
### 
Ilary Plymire yesterday held onto
his position as third man on the
varsity tennis team by defeating Joe
Coughlin 7-5, 7-5 in a match that
was no walkaway, even though the
score was decisive enough.
Another successful defender of
his rung on the ladder, Trow Steph
ens, disposed of his challenger some
what more easily than Plymire,
winning from Bob Forbes 6-1, 7-5,
to stay in seventh place. Continuing
his upward spurt, John Gordon
downed Hubert Butler by the con
clusive score of 6-0, 6-1, to pull
into twelfth position.
Among Ihe freshmen, A1 Bogan,
promising netman from the Webb
School, finally defeated Norman
Wright after a three-set struggle,
4-(i, 11-9, 6-4, to keep his tem
porary position of fifth man. Kelley
Thorpe, one rung below Wright, held
ofT Joe Melczer, 6-3, 8-6, and Paul
Fries won from Richard Hodge 6-4,
6-4.
