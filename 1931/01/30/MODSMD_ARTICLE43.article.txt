# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
FRIDAY
1 p.m.—A. J. Music, prominent labor
leader, will speak to the members
of the Walrus Club on the . sub
ject of unemployment in. the lobby
of the Old Union.
7:30 p.m.—Ushers for "Wings Over
Europe" report at Assembly Hall.
8:30 p.m.—"Wings Over Europe,"
presented under the direction of
Harold Helvenston, by the Dra
i matic Council; in the Assembly
Hall. Box office open at 7 p.m.
Tickets at 75c and $1.25.
SUNDAY
5:15-5:30 p.m.—Y.W.C.A. will hold
its regular vesper services on the
third floor of the Women's Club
house. Everyone is invited.
! 8 to 10 p.m.—Cosmopolitan Club
will meet in the Y.M.C.A. room
| in the Old Union to make plans
I for the future*and to exchange
Yosemite pictures.
MISCELLANEOUS
ALL THOSE COMPETING in the
University boxing tournament may
obtain complimentary tickets at
the B.A.C. office this afternoon.
Those who competed in the pre
liminaries may also apply.
| CANDIDATES for degrees of Mas
ter of Arts and Engineer who ex
, pect to complete requirements in
June should file applications for
admission to candidacy not later
than February 2.
