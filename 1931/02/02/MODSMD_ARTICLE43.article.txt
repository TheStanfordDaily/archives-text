# CAP AND GOWN TO SPONSOR TEA FOR SCHOLARSHIP FUND
## 
### 
Plans are being made for the an
nual bridge tea that Cap and Gown
will give on February 7 from 2 to 5
o'clock in the Women's Clubhouse.
Three hundred dollars has been
set as the minimum amount that Cap
and Gown hopes to realize from the
affair.. The funds raised are to be
used for the yearly scholarship which
is awarded to a woman student reg
istered in the University.
Tables may be secured for $2 each
from Mrs. Elmer Ewell in the Club
house; Mrs. D. C. Gardner, PA 352!);
Mrs. J. C. Branner, PA 4217; arid
Mrs. M. O. Johnson, PA 7470.
