# STUDENTS SELECTED TO RUN BRIDGE TEA
## 
### 
Student committees have been ap
pointed and now have their arrange
ments well under way for the Cap
and Gown bridge tea, which is to
be held February 7 from 2 to 5
o'clock in the Women's Clubhouse.
The committee members arc
Marian Strong, chairman; Katherine
Keho, Row tickets; Sarah Grant,
Hall tickets; Betty Alden, candy;
Lee Larsh and Betty Clarke, music.
The tea, which is an annual af
fair, is for the purpose of raising
money for the scholarship given
each year to a woman student regis
tered in the University. Tables are
$2 each.
