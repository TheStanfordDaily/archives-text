# FOREIGN COMMERCE TO BE SUBJECT OF VOCATIONAL TALKS
## 
### 
Second of the Vocational Guid
ance Conferences will be held here
Wednesday, according to C. G.
Wrenn, assistant. It will take the
form of a council on foreign trade.
General interest among the stu
dents in this phase of work was
shown recently when freshman and
sophomore questionnaires rated for
eign trade as third preference
among numerous vocations.
Two men, one from the United
States Bureau of Foreign-Domestic
Commerce, and one, a foreign trade
manager for an Industrial company,
will speak on foreign trade in both
private concerns and in tin* govern
ment foreign service.
