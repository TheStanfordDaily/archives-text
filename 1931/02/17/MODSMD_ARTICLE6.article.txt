# FORBES CLIMBS UP TENNIS LADDER TO HOLD EIGHTH PLACE
## 
### 
Taking advantage of a break in the
rainy weather, tennis ladder matches
are well under way once more, but
with few surprising results.
Bob Forbes clinched eighth place
by a win over Wilbur Cox 8-6, 6-3,
to further change the complexion of
the first ten ranking list. Hubert
Butler wrested twelfth position from
Russ Robinson by a score of 6-2,
6-4, but Wentworth held onto his
nineteenth place by defeating Johns
ton 1-6, 7-5, 8-6. Edwards won over
Wallace 10-8, 6-1, Pike defaulted to
Baker, and Eichelberger lost to
Moore, 6-4, 0-6, 6-4.
Freshman matches were of more
consequence, five positions among
the first ten being disputed. Monte
Shwayder staved off Dan Ellis in
three fierce sets, 6-3, 4-6, 6-3, to
hold his second position, and A1
Bogan downed Kelley Thorpe 6-2,
7-5, to stay in fourth. The latter,
however, easily subdued Aldcn Sad
ler by a score of 6-1, 6-2, to lose no
more ground.
George Torrence continued his
climb in a victory over Norman
Wright, 6-1, 7-5, and a still easier
win over Jim Shcahan, 6-2, 6-1, to
leap into seventh place.
Further down the line, Phil Kneis
ley moved up another rung, into
eleventh place, by defeating Frank
Barnes 6-4, 6-0. Barnes was later
dropped still another step when he
lost to Fred Hills 6-3, 8-6. Leighton
Johnson overwhelmed John Lohman
6-1, 6-1, A1 Woodward turned in an
equally easy win over Paul Fries,
and Kendall Ellingwood held his po
sition nnainst Richard Hodge l>v 6-2,
