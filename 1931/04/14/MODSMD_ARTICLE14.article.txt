# AMERICAN LEGION TO ASK HOOVER PARDON FOR ALBERT B. FALL
## 
### 
ALAMOCxORDO, N. M„ Apr. 13
(/I').—Charles F. Wilhelm, command
er of the Alamogordo post of the
American Legion, announced today
he has called a meeting of thG or
ganization to take united action in
urging President. Hoover to pardon
Albert B. Fall, former Secretary of
the Interior.
"Albert Fall never quit the Amer
ican Legion when he was a man of
power in Washington," said Ed
Gardner, past commander of the
local post, "and the Legion will not
quit him in his adversity."
