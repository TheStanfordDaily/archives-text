# A.S.S.U. OFFICERS TO BE NOMINATED
## Wednesday, Thursday Set For Nominations 
### 
Nominations for A.S.S.U. and class
offices may be filed Wednesday and
Thursday at the regular polling
place in front of the Law Steps be
tween the hours of 8 and 6 o'clock,
according to Stewart Stadler, chair
man of the A.S.S.U. election board.
Nominating blanks which require
the signatures of 15 registered stu
dents may be obtained at the nom
inating ballot box Wednesday or
Thursday.
Offices Open
Candidates for student body presi
dent, vice-president, and secretary
will be named as well as one sopho
more man, one sophomore woman,
one junior woman, and one gradu
ate man to fill impending vacancies
on the Executive Committee.
Three positions on Men's Council
will be open, requiring men who
have 120 units of University credit;
and one seat on the Board of Ath
letic Control, to be filled by a man
student.
Yell Leaders
President, vice - president, secre
tary, and treasurer of the classes of
'32, '33, and '34 will be open for
nominations. The A.S.S.U. Consti
tution requires that the class treas
urer serve for a full year while the
remaining class offices run for one
half year.
Head yell leader and two assistants
will be on the ballots as well as
three constitutional amendments and
three questions to ascertain student
attitude concerning the Honor Code.
