# ANNOUNCEMENTS
##

WEDNESDAY
12 noon—lnternational Table in the
Alcove of the Union.
THURSDAY
10 a.m.—University assembly in the
Assembly Hall. Address on "The
Bridge Builders," by Canon Shat
ford of Christ Cathedral, Montreal,
Canada.
4 p.m. — Chemophysical Biology
Seminar in Room 427.
4:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
D. Allen, University organist, in
the Memorial Church.
3 p.m.— Stanford Dames reading
group meets with Mrs. O. R.
Meacham on Columbia street, Palo
Alto.
4:30-5:30 p.m.—Fellowship tea on
the third floor of the Women's
Clubhouse. Faculty and students
are cordially invited.
FRIDAY
3 p.m.—Stanford Dames Sewing
group meets with Mrs. Duncan
Macruer, (510 California, Mayfield.
4:30 p.m.—Members of the Cosmo
politan Club will meet in the
Union court for a trip to La
Honda. An election will be held
for new officers.
MISCELLANEOUS
Men now living in Toyon and Brar.-
ner halls who wish to reserve
rooms for fall quarter should call
at the Dean of Men's office Mon
day or Tuesday, May 11 or 12, and
pay their deposits. Room draw
ings will be held at 7:30 p.m., on
Wednesday, May 13, in the of
fices of the two halls.
There will be no meeting of the
Y.W.C.A. cabinet this evening on
account of the A.W.S. banquet.
Union, Sequoia, and Encina men
who wish rooms for fall quarter
should call at the Dean of Men's
office Thursday or Friday to make
reservations.
