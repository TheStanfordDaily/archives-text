WEDNESDAY
12 noon—lnternational Table in the
alcove of the Union dining room.
4:30 p.m.—Drawings take place for
horses for the militai'y Horse Show
at the R.O.T.C. stables.
4:30-5:30 p.m.—Fellowship tea on the
third floor of the Women's Club
house. Faculty and students are
cordially invited.
5:30 p.m.—Alpha Phi Omega picnic
will meet in front of the Union.
6:15 p.m.—There will be a meeting
of all sophomore women tonight
downstairs in the Women's Club
house. Supper will be served buf
fet style, and will cost 50c.
7:30 p.m.—There will be an import
ant meeting of the Foil and
Masque Club in the Women's
Gym for the purpose of electing
next year's officers.
7:30 p.m.—lnternational Relations
Club will meet. Professor Aurelio
Espinosa will speak on the dic
tatorship and revolution in Spain.
Election of officers.
8 p.m.—Chi Nu Kappa will meet in
the basement of the Women's
Clubhouse. Professor R. D. Har
riman will speak on "A Medieval
Latin Student."
8 p.m.—Research Club to meet in
the Faculty Clubhouse. Professor
H. D. Gray will speak on "Some
Methods of Shakespearian Criti
cism," and Professor H. A. Kra
mers, on "Light and Matter."

of the German department.

Seminar in Room 427.

organist, in the Memorial Church.
G: 15 p.m.—Pi Sigma Alpha will meet
for the last time this quarter.
Franklin Hickborn, legislative re
porter, will speak. Election of of
ficers.
