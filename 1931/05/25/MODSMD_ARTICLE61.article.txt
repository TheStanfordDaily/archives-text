MONDAY
4 p.m.—Chemistry Seminar in Room
20, Chemistry Building. Speaker:
Dr. Victor K. LaMer. Topic:
"Electrometric Titrations in Ben
zene."
8:30 p.m.—Lecture on "The Silver
Fox" by George Jensen in Little
Theatre.
TUESDAY
4-6 p.m.—Miss Yost will be at home
to graduate women at her home,
532 Lasuen.
5:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
D. Allen, University organist, in
the Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Venezuela"
by Judge Jackson H. Ralston in
the Little Theatre.
WEDNESDAY
4:30-6 p.m.—Miss Yost will be at
home to senior women at her
home, 532 Lasuen.
5:15 p.m.—Meeting of Women's Cir
cle "S" Society in the Women's
Clubhouse.
8 p.m.—Science Association in the
Faculty Clubhouse. Speaker: Pro
fessor J. C. Hinsey.
THURSDAY
4:15 p.m.—Philological Association
in the Women's Clubhouse. Miss
Beatrice White will read a paper
on "Early Tudor Grammarians.
5:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
I). Allen, University organist, in
the Memorial Church.
FRIDAY
4:30 p.m. Those desiring to attend
the annual Cosmopolitan Club
banquet at Searsville assemble in
front of Union.
MISCELLANEOUS
Men who wish to t;ike out or renew
tuition notes' for summer or fall
quarters should call at the Dean
of Men's office during week of
June 1 to 5.
Men living in Eticina desiring to re
serve rooms for fall quarter should
aply at the Dean of Men's office
Wednesday, Thursday, or Friday
to make their $5 deposit.
Men now living in Toyon and the
Union who wish to reserve rooms
for the summer quarter should
call at the Dean of Men's office
Monday or Tuesday, May 25 or
26, and pay the deposit.
Students expecting to enter the
School of Law during either the
summer or autumn quarters should
file applications at once with the
secretary of the school, in Room 1.
Orders are now being taken at the
Bookstore for the Yearbook of
Stanford Writings for 1930-31.
Price $1.

quarters should call at the Dean
of Men's office during week of
June 1 to 5.
Men living in Eticina desiring to re
serve rooms for fall quarter should
aply at the Dean of Men's office
Wednesday, Thursday, or Friday
to make their $5 deposit.
Men now living in Toyon and the
Union who wish to reserve rooms
for the summer quarter should
call at the Dean of Men's office
Monday or Tuesday, May 25 or
26, and pay the deposit.
Students expecting to enter the
School of Law during either the
summer or autumn quarters should
file applications at once with the
secretary of the school, in Room 1.
Orders are now being taken at the
Bookstore for the Yearbook of
Stanford Writings for 1930-31.
Price $1.
MONDAY
4 p.m.—Chemistry Seminar in Room
20, Chemistry Building. Speaker:
Dr. Victor K. LaMer. Topic:
"Electrometric Titrations in Ben
zene."
8:30 p.m.—Lecture on "The Silver
Fox" by George Jensen in Little
Theatre.
TUESDAY
4-6 p.m.—Miss Yost will be at home
to graduate women at her home,
532 Lasuen.
5:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
D. Allen, University organist, in
the Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Venezuela"
by Judge Jackson H. Ralston in
the Little Theatre.
WEDNESDAY
4:30-6 p.m.—Miss Yost will be at
home to senior women at her
home, 532 Lasuen.
5:15 p.m.—Meeting of Women's Cir
cle "S" Society in the Women's
Clubhouse.
8 p.m.—Science Association in the
Faculty Clubhouse. Speaker: Pro
fessor J. C. Hinsey.
THURSDAY
4:15 p.m.—Philological Association
in the Women's Clubhouse. Miss
Beatrice White will read a paper
on "Early Tudor Grammarians.
5:15 p.m.—Organ recital by Warren
I). Allen, University organist, in
the Memorial Church.
FRIDAY
4:30 p.m. Those desiring to attend
the annual Cosmopolitan Club
banquet at Searsville assemble in
front of Union.
