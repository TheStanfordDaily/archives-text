# TOYON DANCE BIDS ANNOUNCED ON SALE
## 
### 
Bids are being sold for the Toyon
Moonlight Dance to be held on Friday
evening in Toyon Hall, according to
Leonard Bartlett, chairman of the com
mittee in charge of arrangements.
The tickets may be purchased at the
A.S.S.U. offices in the Old Union or in
rooms 201, 202, or 203 in Toyon Hall.
Bids are $2.50.
Craig's Cardinals, popular campus
band, has been selected to supply music
for the occasion. A novelty program in
keeping with the "moonlight" idea is
being arranged.
Chaperones are Mr. and Mrs. Jacob
C. Irwin, Mr. and Mrs. James Brad
shaw, and Mr. and Mrs. Brandon Wat
son.
Dick Philippi, in charge of decora
tions, will use golden crescents as a
part of his scheme in decorating the
Toyon ballroom and lobby.
Programs will be distributed from
room 203 in Toyon Hall on Thursday
evening and all day Friday.
