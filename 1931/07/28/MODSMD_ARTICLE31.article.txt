# CONTESTANTS ENTER QUARTER FINALS OF TENNIS TOURNAMENT
## 
### 
Hard-fought tennis prevailed in the
men's tennis tourney during the last
week. Many close battles were playod
with one upset resulting. The contest
ants are now entering the quarter
finals and these matches will be com
pleted by the end of the week.
The surprise came when Lambert
defeated Onishi, 8-(j, 3-6, 6-3. Onishi
is an experienced player, having com
peted in previous Stanford summer
tourneys, and was considered by many
a possible .finalist. Lambert displayed
a steady, consistent brand of tennis
that carried him through to victory
over his experienced opponent.
Dick Webb, another favorite, came
through his third-round match with
comparative ease, dereating Pawl Yost,
6.-4, 6-3. Webb's serve seems to be his
greatest asset.
Fred Glover, better known for his
boxing ability, remained in the tour
ney as the result of his victory over
Osborne, 8-6, 6-4. The result was in
doubt until Glover broke through his
opponent's serve to take the lead. An
other match that drew attention was
Jack Pike's defeat of Arnie West, of
track fame, 6-8, 6-3, 6-1. Arnie started
strong but weakened before Pike's fast
play and what promised to be a great
match resulted in an easy victory for
Pike.

Other matches completed over tho
week were: Hardy d. Patterson, 6-2,
6-2; Burn d. Wright, 6-3, 6-3; Johnston
d. Lohman, 6-1, 6-0; Robbins d. Mc-
Dowell, 6-0, 6-1; Burnett d. Hunter,
6-2, 6-2; and Fields d. Broadbent
through default.
