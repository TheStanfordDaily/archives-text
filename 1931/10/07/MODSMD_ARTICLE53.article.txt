WEDNESDAY
1 p.m.—First tryout meeting for
will deliver an address in the As
sembly Hall at an all-University
assembly.
1 p.m.—Firt tryout meeting for
Women's Editor of Chaparral will
be held in the Chappie business
office, upstairs in the Press build
ing. For freshman women only.
5:45 p.m.—Meeting of the Y.W.C.A.
third floor of the Women's Club-
Cabinet in the Y rooms in the
he use.
7:15 p.m.—Concert group tryoutees
will meet in Concert Offices, Old
Union. Anyone now trying out or
wishing to, urged to attend.
8 p.m.—Members of the faculty will
attend a meeting of the Research
Club in the Faculty Club House.
Speakers will be Dr. Horace Gray
of the Stanford University Hos
pital on "Anthropological and
Mental Measurement in Private
School Children," and Professor
Payson J. Treat, "Some New
Light on Japanese-American Re
lations."
THURSDAY
5 p.m.—A.A.U.W. lecture upstairs
in the Women's Clubhouse. Dr.
Benjamin Lehman will speak on
"Aspects of Current Literature."
Student tickets 50 cents.
5:10 p.m.—Warren D Allen, Uni
versity organist, will present an
organ recital in the Memorial
Church.
7:15 p.m.—All old members and
tryoutees of the Stanford Sym
phony Orchestra meet in the old
Roble gymnasium for practice.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of Christian
Science Organization on third
floor of Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m.—Phi Sigma Alpha, Politi
cal Science Society, will hold an.
important business meeting in the
West Memorial Library. All mem
bers are urgently requested to be
present.
7:30 p.m.—A meeting of Pi Lambda
Theta upstairs in the Women's
Clubhouse.
FRIDAV
8 p.m.—Faculty members will at
tend a meetitng of the Faculty
Club in the Faculty Club House.
