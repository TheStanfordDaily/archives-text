# BROWN REPRIMANDS CLASS OF 1935 FOR PAINTING LAW STEPS
## 
### 
At the first student aclivity as
sembly of the year, which was held
in the Assembly Hall yesterday
morning at 9 o'clock, Bob Brown,
A.S.S.U. president, took occasion to
publicly reprimand the Class of '35
for the painting of the law steps,
calling such conduct vandalism.
He requested that such activities
in the future be .removed from the
vicinity of Quad, and also outlined
the organization of the A.S.S.U.
Executive Committee, the Men's
Council, and the A.S.S.U. Concert
Series.
Barbara Beach Thompson, presi
dent of the A.W.S., enumerated the
special activities for women, and
discussed the honor code, bringing
out the fact that it is a larger issue
than personal inclination since il
involves the honor of the University.
Catherine Crary spoke for dra
matics, while John Hunekc dis
cussed debating and its advantages.
Richard Friendlich, editor of the
Daily, spoke on publications, in
cluding the Chaparral, the Quad,
the Yearbook of Stanford Writing,
and the Daily.
