###

president of the International Rela
tions Club at the first meeting of the
quarter, held Thursday nighl.
Gange was elected to fill the posi
tion left vacant by the resignation
of Joseph Nutt who was recently
elected president of Pi Sigma
political science fraternity.
Professor Ichihashi of the history
department led the discussion of
the meeting by a review of "Modern
Trends in Japan; Social, Economic,
and Political." A spirited discussion
followed the informal spcech of the
professor.
Meetings of the organization will
