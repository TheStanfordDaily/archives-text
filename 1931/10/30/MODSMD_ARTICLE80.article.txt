FRIDAY I
3 p.m.—The Newcomers' Club of
the Women's Faculty Club will
have tea at the home of Mrs. L. L.
Burlingame, from 3 to 5 o'clock.
Hostesses in addition to Mrs. Bur
lingame are the sponsors, Mrs. A.
S. Miles, Mrs. P. A. Ross, and
Mrs. J. B. Baumbergcr.
3 p.m.—Polo manager tryouts. En
cina polo field.
8:30 p.m.—"Berkeley Square" pre
sented by Dramatic Council. As
sembly Hall. Tickets at 75c-$1.25.
7:20 p.m.—All ushers for "Berkeley
Square" will report to J. P.
Franklin at the Assembly Hall.
MONDAY
12:15 p.m.—lnternational Table will
meet for lunch in the alcove of
the Union dining room. All in
terested students invited.
