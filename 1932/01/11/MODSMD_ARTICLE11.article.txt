# SHAKESPEARE PLAYS TO BE GIVEN IN S.F.
## 
### 
A season of Shakespeare will open
tonight in San Francisco, when the
Stratford-upon-Avon Festival Com
pany begins its three-week engage
ment at the Columbia Theatre with
a performance of "A Winter's Talc."
The Stratford company is from
Stratford-upon-Avon, the town of
the master dramatist's birth. The
organization operates under a royal
charter and is under the patronage
of the king. The performances arc
directed by W. Bridges Adams.
Following "A Winter's Tale" to
night, "King Lear" will be played
tomorrow night, "As You Like It"
on the Wednesday matinee, "The
Taming of the Shrew" on Wednes
day evening, "King Henry the
Fourth" (Part One), on Thursday
evening, "The Merry Wives of
Windsor" on Friday evening, "A
Midsummer Night's Dream" for the
Saturday matinee, and "Twelfth
Night" Saturday evening.
