# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
MONDAY
Today marks the final day for un
dergraduate students to register
for admission to the University
for the winter quarter.
TUESDAY
12:15 p.m. —German Table in the
Union dining room.
7:30 p.m.—Tuesday evening lecture.
''Tlk' Size of the Universe," by
Dr. Willem de Sitter, in the As
sembly Hall. The public is invited.
WEDNESDAY
4:15 p.m.—A lecture on "A New
Technique in Language Study,"
by Harold E. Palmer, linguistic
adviser to Imperial Japanese
Government, in the Little Theatre.
Open to the public.
THURSDAY
12:15 p.m.—The French Table meets
in the alcove of the Union cafe
teria. All those interested may
attend.
7:30 p.m.—lllustrated lecture, "The
Charm of the French Cathedrals,"
by Ciarenee Ward, under the
auspices of the classical literature
department. The public is invited.
5:10 p.m.—Organ recital in the Me
morial Church by Warren D.
Allen.
FRIDAY
8:30 p.m—"Bird in Hand," by John
Drinkwater, in the Assembly Hall.
Tickets $1.25, $1, and 75c.
