# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
THURSDAY
12 noon — Skull and Snakes meet at
Alpha Delt house. Pictures to be
taken.
12:15 p.m.— The French Table meets
in the alcove of the Union cafe-
teria. All those interested may
attend.
1 p.m. — Sigma Delta Chi meets in
Room 9, Journalism Building. All
members are requested to be
present.
2:15 p.m. — Meeting of the reading
group of the Stanford Dames at
the home of Mrs. A. M. Cathcart
at 1215 Emerson St.
5:10 p.m. — Organ recital in the Me-
morial Church by Warren D.
Allen.
7-8 p.m. — All-University jolly-up,
sponsored by the Junior Class in
the Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of Christian Sci
ence Organization on third floor
of Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m. — lllustrated lecture, "The
Charm of the French Cathedrals,"
by Clarence Ward, under the
auspices of the classical literature
department. The public is invited.
FRIDAY
7:30 p.m—All ushers will please re
port to the Assembly Hall for
duty at "Bird In Hand."
8:30. p.m. — "Bird in Hand," by John
Drinkwater, in the Assembly Hall.
Tickets $1.25, $1, and 75c.
SATURDAY
8 p.m. — Mrs. Hugh Brown will read
"Once in a Lifetime" by George
Kaufmann in the Community
House in Palo Alto for the bene
fit of the unemployed.
MISCELLANEOUS
All organizations having teams
entered in the intramural swimming
tournaments next week must see
that each entrant passes the medi
cal examination required by intra
mural rules by Tuesday, according
to announcement by Gene Mires.
Examinations may be taken any
time in the B.A.C. Building.
