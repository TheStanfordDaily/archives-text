12 noon—Spanish Table will meet

Modern Socialism," by Harry
Laidler, president of the National
Bureau of Economic Research and
Executive Director League for
Industrial Democracy. Open meet

Clubhouse.

Women's Clubhouse. Tea for fac
ulty and students. All invited.

cabinet for regular weekly supper
in the "Y" rooms on the third
floor of the Women's Clubhouse.
7:30 p.m.—Cercle Francais will meet
at Kappa Kappa Gamma house.
Members and prospective mem
bers are invited.
8 p.m.—Open meeting of Cbi Nu
Kappa, honorary literary society,
in basement of Women's Club
house. Professor Fairclough will
*be the speaker.
8 p.m.—Research Club in the Fac
ulty Clubhouse. Papers: Professor
P. A. Martin on "Slavery and
Abolition in Brazil"; Professor P.
A. Ross on "New X-Ray Limes."
8 p.m.—Stanford vs. College of the
Pacific in debate on question,
"Resolved, That Congress Should
Enact Legislation Providing for
Centralized Control of Industry."
