MONDAY
Last day foi second payments on
Quads. Payments after today will
draw a 50 cent penalty.
1 p.m.—A.W.S. Conference meets in
the Women's Clubhouse.
2 p.m.—Oral examination for the
Doctorate. Clarence G. Osborn
(Major, history; minor, political
science) in Room 311, Library.
2 p.m.—Oral examination for the
Doctorate. YVillial Alford Hething
ton (Major, zoology; minor,
physiology) in Room 308, Library.
3:15 p.m.—Dramatic Council pre
sents Ellsworth Martin's Theatre
Mechanique in Little Theatre.
General admission 50 cents.
4 p.m. —Chemistry department Sem
inar, Room 20, Chemistry Build
ing. Speaker: Dr. F. Simon, Bres
lau, Germany. Subject: "On the
Possibilities of Approaching the
Absolute Zero."
TUESDAY
12:15 p.m.—German Table in the
Union dining room.
3:15 p.m.—Dramatic Council pre
sents Ellsworth Martin's Theatre
Mechanique in Little Theatre.
General admission 50 cents.
4 p.m.—Jordan Seminar, Jordan
Hall, Room 427. Speaker: Dr.
Charles C. Danforth. Subject:
"Experiments in Skin Grafting."
5:10 p.m.—Organ recital, Memorial
Church. Charles L. Safford, act
ing organist.
7:30 p.m.—Lecture on "Leland Stan
ford as a Public Man," by George
T. Clark. Assembly Hall.
8 p.m.—One-act plays by Dramatic
Council in Little Theatre. Admis
sion 50 cents.
THURSDAY
3 p.m.—Professor John Almack will
speak on "The Nature of Humor"
at a meeting of the Stanford
Dames in Women's Clubhouse.
4-6 p.m.—Mrs. Swain will be at
home at 634 Mirada.
5:10 p.m.—Organ recital in Memorial
Church. Charles L. Safford, acting
organist.
8:20 p.m.—Stanford Symphony Or
chestra, conducted by Mrs. Mar
guerite MacManus. Assembly
Hall. No charge.
MISCELLANEOUS
Freshmen interested in trying out
for the Quad business staff are
asked to see Maurits Van Loben

Sels in Room 6 in the Press Build
ing any afternoon this week.
Men now living at Encina Hall
who wish to reserve their rooms for
spring quarter should call at the
Dean of Men's office Monday, Tues
day, or Wednesday, February 29,
March 1, or March 2.
All women expecting to receive
W.A.A. awards this quarter or in
the future are asked to hand in a
full report of their points to Mar
garet McDougall. The point system
is set forth in the W.A.A. Constitu
tion in the back of the A.S.S.U.
Handbook.
