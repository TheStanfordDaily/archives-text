WEDNESDAY
12:10 p.m.—French Table will meet
in alcove of Union today instead
of Thursday, as formerly.
1:15 p.m.—Sigma Delta Chi will
meet in Room 9 of the Press
Building.
3:30 p.m.—The Stanford Life Saving
Corps will resume its monthly
meetings at the Encina pools. My
ron Sprague will give instruction.
4 p.m.—Miss Yost will be at home
informally, 532 Lasuen.
4:30 p.m.—Fellowship tea. "Leisure
Hour" in the Women's Clubhouse.
Faculty and students are invited.
7:30 p.m.—Meeting of the Stanford
DeMolay Club in the Y.M.C.A.
lobby, Old Union.
7:30 p.m.—"Private Lives," by Noel
Coward. Group reading, presented
under the auspices of Sword and
Sandals, in the Little Theatre.
Open to the public.
iS p.m.—Meeting of the Science As
sociation in the Faculty Club
House. Dr. F. Deeds will speak on
"The Glass Electrode in Theory
and Practice."

12 noon—Spanish Table meeting in
back part of Cafeteria.

(>34 Mirada.

ing Europe" by Dr. Paul L. Deng

to the public.

of Thursday, as formerly.

sociation in the Faculty Club

and Practice."
