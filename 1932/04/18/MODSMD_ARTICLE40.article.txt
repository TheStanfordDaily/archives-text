# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
TUESDAY
(5:15 p.m.—Open meeting of Alpha
Phi Omega in the Union alcove.
All Eagle Scouts who are inter
ested are invited to attend. Those
interested see Jack Tarr, Coe
Swift, or Jack Lowson.
WEDNESDAY
12:10 p.m.—French Table will meet
in Union alcove.
5:45 p.m.—Weekly supper and meet
ing of the Y.W.C.A. cabinet in the
"Y" rooms, third floor of the
Women's Clubhouse.
THURSDAY
3 p.m.—Stanford Dames will meet
in the Women's Clubhouse.
MISCELLANEOUS
Members of the Stanford Branch of
the American Institute of Elec
trical Engineering planning to at
tend the joint meeting with the
U.S. branch and San Francisco
section at Santa Clara Friday eve
ning should sign on the bulletin
board at once.
Applications for the Civil En
gineering scholarship should be
turned in by April 25 1o the office,
Room 264, C.E. building.
Exhibit of late acquisitions of the
Stanford Library: specimens of
fine printing, limited editions,
Japanese work.
Senior Class dues payable this
week. Dues, which are $1, will be
i called for at living groups by mem
j bers of the Senior Class Finance
j Committee.
