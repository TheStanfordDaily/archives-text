WEDNESDAY
12:10 p.m.—French Table will meet
in Union alcove.

Chi in the Press Building.

their Founders' Day meeting at
the home of Mrs. Hugh Jackson,
747 Dolores St.
3:30-5 p.m.—The Stanford Life
Saving Corps will meet for prac
tice at the Encina pools, with in
struction by Myron Sprague.
Senior members of the Red Cross
are invited to attend.
4 p.m.—Physics Department semi
nar, Room 373. Professor Leonard
B. Loeb of the University of Cali
fornia will speak on '"Mobilities
of lons in Gases."
4:30 p.m.—Fellowship tea. "Leisure
Hour" in the Women's Clubhouse.
Faculty and students invited.
7:30 p.m.—DeMolay meets, Room 231
of the English Building. Degree
pract ice.
7:30 p.m.—Le Cercle Francais will
meet at the Delta Gamma house.
All those interested in French are
cordially invited.
