# ANNOUNCEMENTS

FRIDAY
4:15 p.m.—Academic Council to meet
in Room 12. Conferring of de
grees.
8 p.m.—Acting President and Mrs.
Swain will be at home informally
to all students registering for the
first time this quarter. There will
be music for thosa who care to
dance. President's House, Lagunita
Knoll.
MONDAY
12 o'clock noon—lnternational Table
will hold its weekiy meeting in
Union Dining Room 12. Anyone
interested is welcome to attend.
MISCELLANEOUS
The deadline for women's physical
examinations is set for next Friday.
All women who have not already
been examined must make appoint
ments for examinations immediately.
Contrary to the notice in Monday
morning's edition of the Daily, the
first meeting of the Cardinal Chess
Club will be held Thursday evening
at 7:30 o'clock in the lobby of the
new Union instead of Wednesday,
as announced before.
All sophomore men interested in
trying out for tennis manager are
requested to sign the list posted in
the gym. The tryout will be held
very shortly.
Students interested in trying out
for choir should see Warren D. Al
len, director, during his office hours
tomorrow from 9 to 12 o'clock or at
some other time by previous ar
rangement. First rehearsals will
take place tomorrow night at 7:30
o'clock.
Classical Literature, Alo4a, Hom
er's Iliad, will meet at 10 a.m. in
stead of at 9 a.m. as scheduled.

Open hours for swimming when
the women's pool is not used by
classes may be used for recreation
by any woman, announces the phys
ical education department. Hours
are Monday, Wednesday, and Fri
day at 4 o'clock and Thursday at 3.
All sophomores and juniors wish
ing to try out for dramatic manager
may report to Manager Girvin
Whitney at the Dramatic Council
office in the Old Union this after
noon between 1 and 4 o'clock.
Four vacancies still remain to be
filled by sophomore managerial try
outs in swimming. Aspirants may
report to Assistant Coach Thornton,
Manager Irvine, or any junior man
ager at the Encina pools this after
noon after 4 o'clock.
Exhibits of water colors by T. M.
Criley, October 2-25. and drawings
and designs for the theatre by
Harold Holvenston and Frederick
Stover, October 2-30. Thomas Wel
ton Stanford Art Gallery.
Exhibit showing the system of
classifying and cataloguing books in
the Stanford University Library.
Arranged by the Catalogue division.
University Library.

may report to Manager Girvin
Whitney at the Dramatic Council
office in the Old Union this after
noon between 1 and 4 o'clock.
Four vacancies still remain to be
filled by sophomore managerial try
outs in swimming. Aspirants may
report to Assistant Coach Thornton,
Manager Irvine, or any junior man
ager at the Encina pools this after
noon after 4 o'clock.
Exhibits of water colors by T. M.
Criley, October 2-25. and drawings
and designs for the theatre by
Harold Holvenston and Frederick
Stover, October 2-30. Thomas Wel
ton Stanford Art Gallery.
Exhibit showing the system of
classifying and cataloguing books in
the Stanford University Library.
Arranged by the Catalogue division.
University Library.

grees.

Swain will be at home informally

will hold its weekiy meeting in

MISCELLANEOUS
The deadline for women's physical
examinations is set for next Friday.
All women who have not already
been examined must make appoint
ments for examinations immediately.
Contrary to the notice in Monday
morning's edition of the Daily, the
first meeting of the Cardinal Chess
Club will be held Thursday evening
at 7:30 o'clock in the lobby of the
new Union instead of Wednesday,
as announced before.
All sophomore men interested in
trying out for tennis manager are
requested to sign the list posted in
the gym. The tryout will be held
very shortly.
Students interested in trying out
for choir should see Warren D. Al
len, director, during his office hours
tomorrow from 9 to 12 o'clock or at
some other time by previous ar
rangement. First rehearsals will
take place tomorrow night at 7:30
o'clock.
Classical Literature, Alo4a, Hom
er's Iliad, will meet at 10 a.m. in
stead of at 9 a.m. as scheduled.

Open hours for swimming when
the women's pool is not used by
classes may be used for recreation
by any woman, announces the phys
ical education department. Hours
are Monday, Wednesday, and Fri
day at 4 o'clock and Thursday at 3.
