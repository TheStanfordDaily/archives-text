# Keeler, '31, Awarded Chicago Honor Medal
## 
### 
Leonard Keeler, '31, has been
awarded the medal for the most
outstanding civic contribution to
Chicago for 1932. Keeler, inventor
of the "Lie Detector" at Stanford in
1927, helped to solve 87 criminal
cases in Chicago during the past
year.
The award, which was given to
Samuel Instill, Jr., in .1931, was pre
sented <o Keeler last Friday night
at a banquet in Hotel La Salle, Chi
cago. The medal is presented an
nually by the Junior Association of
Commerce to an outstanding indi
vidual between the ages of 21 and 35.
