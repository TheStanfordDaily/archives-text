# Hegland Resigns From Work On "Daily" Staff
## 
### 
Finding that graduate work in
economics requires too much time,
Sheridan Hegland, '32, last night
announced his resignation from the
Daily staff, to take effect at the end
of the present term early next
month.
Hegland joined the staff in the
fall of 1930, became a copy editor
on the Associated Press desk the
next quarter, and won a night-edi
torship last year.
He was considered the probable
choice for Managing Editor of Vol
ume 83 of the Daily, beginning Feb
ruary 20.
