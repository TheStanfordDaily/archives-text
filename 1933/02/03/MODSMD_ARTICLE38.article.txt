# Campus Opinion
## "HOLIDAY" 
### 
The Editor, Sir:
The coming "Holiday" makes us
wonder why it is that the best seats
for the offerings of the Dramatic
Council are never available? For
weeks we hear of "finds" in the cast,
new sets, etc., and that there will
be plenty of the best seats. We rush
madly over to the Union on the'day
the sale opens and find that we are
asked to pay top prices for the leav
ings of the favored few. The best
seats are taken before the public
has a chance to bid. Either th so
seats should command a higher
price or everyone should have an
opportunity to get them.
Just ofThand, it seems to be an
undemocratic and annoying custom.
Sincerely, J. R. Bunting, "M
