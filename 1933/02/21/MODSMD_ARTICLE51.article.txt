# ANNOUNCEMENTS
## 
### 
TUESDAY
12:10 p.m. —German Table in the
main dining room of the Union.
All those, interested in German
conversation cordially invited to
attend.
2:30 p.m.—Mothers' Club in the
Women's Clubhouse. Student con
cert arranged by Mrs. Charles
Moser.
2:30 p.m.—The sewing group of the
Stanford Dames will meet at the
home of Mrs. Frank Eastman, 354
Portola.
7:30 p.m.—A program of folk songs
and dances from picturesque lands,
by Ellenor Cook. Assembly Hall.
The public is invited.
WEDNESDAY
8 p.m.—Science Association in the
Faculty Clubhouse. Speaker: Pro
fessor G. D. Shafer. Subject: "The
Effects of Extra Calcium on the
Mammalian Heart."
THURSDAY
2 p.m.—The bridge group of the
Stanford Dames will meet in the
Women's Clubhouse.
4:15 p.m.—The University Philologi
cal Association in the Women's
Clubhouse. Paper: Professor Al
bert Guerard on "Seven Concep
tions of Genius."
FRIDAY
2:30 p.m.—The reading group of the
Stanford Dames will meet at the
home of Mrs. Marion R. Kirkwood,
249 Lowell.
MISCELLANEOUS
Men who wish to take out or re
new tuition notes for spring quarter
should call at the Dean of Men's
office the week of February 27.
Exhibit: The University Library.
English Club exhibit of the pub
lished work of its members, past
and present.
Thomas Welton Stanford Art Gal
lery. Exhibit: Photographs by Bur
ton Crandall, until February 28.
Paintings by the Women Painters
of the West, until March 5.
