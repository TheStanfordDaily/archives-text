# STANFORD NINE DEFEATS GAELS BY 6-1 MARGIN
## Reorganized Varsity Takes Third Game Of Series With Early Lead 
### 
Going into an early lead, Stan
ford's shaken-up varsity ball team
won its series from St. Mary's when
ii took the third game from the
Gaels, 6 to 1, Saturday afternoon on
the Sunken Diamond.
Two new faces appeared in the
Card line-up for the first time this
season, Bill Ladue in centerfield and
Art Hearn on first base, and both
made auspicious debuts. Coach
Harry Wolter also shuffled his bat
ting order with considerable suc
cess, the Indians turning eight hits
into six runs.
St. Mary's opened the scoring in
the first inning to make their only
run.
Card Threat in First
Stanford threatened to score in
its half of the first with two men
on base, but McKay pulled out of
the hole without a run crossing the
plate, only to yield three in the
third.
Ladue singled to center after one
was out in the third on Barreilles'
passed ball and counted when Miles
doubled to right. Livermore's single
scored Miles a moment later.
After the first, Harry Stark kept
the Moragans' eight hits well scat
tered with the exception of the sev
enth inning when St. Mary's filled
the bases, with one out.
The Indians played errorless ball
for the first time this year. The box

ford's shaken-up varsity ball team
won its series from St. Mary's when
ii took the third game from the
Gaels, 6 to 1, Saturday afternoon on
the Sunken Diamond.
Two new faces appeared in the
Card line-up for the first time this
season, Bill Ladue in centerfield and
Art Hearn on first base, and both
made auspicious debuts. Coach
Harry Wolter also shuffled his bat
ting order with considerable suc
cess, the Indians turning eight hits
into six runs.

the first inning to make their only
run.
