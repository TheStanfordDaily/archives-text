TUESDAY
12:10 p.m.—German Table will meet
in the main dining-room of the
Union. Those interested in Ger
man conversation are invited.
4 p.m.—A Jordan Seminar, Jordan
Hall. Room 430. Dr. J. Clausen of
the Carnegie Institution of Wash
ington, Stanford University, will
speak on "Problems and Methods
of Experimental Taxonomy."
5:10 p.m.—Organ recital in the Me
morial Church. Warren D. Allen
will be the organist. '
7:30 p.m.—John A. Lomax will lec
ture on "Some Types of the Amer
ican Ballad" in the Assembly Halt.
The public is cordially invited.
WEDNESDAY
9:10 a.m.—There will be a lecture
on "Polarographic Study of Com
plexes: Amphoters, Cyanides, etc."
by Professor J. Heyrovsky in the
Chemistry Building, Room 20.
12:10 p.m.—There will be a meeting
of the French Table in the alcove
of the Union. All members of the
University are coidially invited.
7 p.m.—The newly formed Pre-
Medical Club will meet in Room
430, Jordan Hall. All students in
terested are invited to attend.
8 p.m.—The Research Club will meet
in the Faculty Clubhouse. Papers
will be: Professor A. M. Espinosa
on "The Oriental Origins of Euro
pean Folk-Tales," and Professor
W. F. Durand on "The Present
Status of the Hoover Dam."
8:15 p.m.—The classes in Creative
Dancing and Orchesis will present
a program in the Dance Studio of
the Women's Gymnasium. Admis
sion will be by ticket only. Tickets
will be available Monday, March
6, at 1 p.m. in the Women's Club
house.
THURSDAY
5:10 p.m.—Warren D. Allen, Univer
sity organist, will give a recital in
the Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m.—Regular meeting of the
Christian Science Organization on
the third floor of the Women's
Clubhouse.
8 p.m.—ln commemoration of the
Founders of the University a pro
gram of choral and orchestral
music, and a brief address by Le
land W. Cutler, President of the
Board of Trustees, will be given
in the Memorial Church. Friends
of the University are cordially in
vited to attend and admission will
be without ticket.
FRIDAY
2 p.m.—Oral examination for the
Doctorate: Harley A. Notter (Ma
jor, history; Minor, education). The
examination will be in Room 308
of the Library.
9:10 p.m.—There will be a lecture
on "Adsorption Maxima on Cur
rent-Voltage Curves" by Professor
J. Heyrovsky, Room 20 of the
Chemistry Building.
MISCELLANEOUS
Any women students wishing to
take out tuition notes for spring
quarter should put in application at
Dean Yost's office immediately.
Until March 31, the art work of
the United States schools will be
exhibited in the art gallery.
The English Club is exhibiting the
published work of its members, past
and present.
