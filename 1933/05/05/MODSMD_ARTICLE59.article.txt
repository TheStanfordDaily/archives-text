# Flowers, Plants Adorn Toyon's Dance Tonight
## 
### 
Toyon Club presents its Spring
Informal with dancing from 9 until
1 o'clock this evening in the spa
cious Toyon Lobby. In keeping with
the motif of Spring, the decorations
will be in the form of flowers and
potted plants, attractively set with
decorative lighting effects, accord
ing to Chairman Don Hardy.
A1 Rushton's Royal Cardinals
will offer a 10-piece orchestra and
vocal trio for the dancers, who wiil
be attired in informal garb of sport
nature.
A few bids, at $2 each, are still
available in the A.S.S.U. offices in
tho Old Union. Programs for the
affair will be given out there also,
on presentation of an invitation.
