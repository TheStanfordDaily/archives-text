# ANNOUNCEMENTS

TUESDAY
11 a.m. —Meeting of the University
Committee of the Board of Trus
tees.
1:45 p.m.—Meeting of the Finance
Committee of the Board of Trus
tees.
7 p.m.—All ushers for the Menuhin
concert are requested to be at the
Pavilion at 7 o'clock. Any regular
ushers not able to work please
send a substitute, or get in touch
with Prentice Messimer at the
Concert Series office.
8:30 p.m.—Yehudi Menuhin, IG
year-old violinist, will appear at
the Pavilion in a concert at 8:30
o'clock. Stanford students $1. Gen
eral public $1.50, $2, $2.50, and $3
(tax exempt). Tickets at the con
cert series office, Old Union, and
Keeble's in Palo Alto.
WEDNESDAY
12:10 p.m.—French Table in the al
cove of the Union. All members
of the University cordially in
vited to attend.
1:30 p.m.—Oral examination for the
Doctorate: Eric Edward Todd (In
Minor, Physics, only). Room 307
Physics Building.
8 p.m.—Research Club in the Fac
ulty Clubhouse. Speakers: Pro
fessors Edward M. Hulme and
Frederick E. Terman.
THURSDAY
1:45 p.m.—Meeting of the Board of
Trustees.
2 p.m.—Oral examination for the
Doctorate: Walter M. Rudolph
(Major, Economics; Minor, Soci
ology). Room 408 Library.
MISCELLANEOUS
Applications for summer and fall
quarter tuition notes will be re
ceived in the Dean of Men's office
during the week of May 15, Miss
Vivienne Church, secretary, an
nounced yesterday.
