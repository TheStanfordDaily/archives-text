Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. LXVI, No. 1 June 22-28, 1933
AT HOME
Friday, June 23
8:00 p.m. President and Mrs. Wilbur will be at home informally to summer
quarter students. President's House, Lagunita Knoll.
CHURCH SERVICE
Sunday, June 25
11:00 a.m. Preacher: The Reverend Dr. Y. Y. Tsu, Peiping, China.
TUESDAY EVENING SERIES
Tuesday, June 27
8:00 p.m. "Mexican Scenes," by "La Tapatias": Mrs. Emilia Lindeman Rathbun,
the Misses Elena, Bertha, and Clotilde Lindeman, assisted by Pablo
Fedelin, mandolin, and Mariano Flores, guitar. Stanford Union Court.
The public is invited.
SPECIAL LECTURES
Thursday, June 22
8:00 p.m. Illustrated lecture on "Three Chief Motives in Art Appreciation: the
Art of Giotto," by Professor A. B. Clark, first of a series of six
lectures to be given on the Appreciation of Paintings and Sculptures.
Little Theatre. The public is invited.
Wednesday, June 28
4:15 p.m. Lecture by Professor Guy Stanton Ford, Acting Professor of History.
Little Theatre. The public is invited.
ORGAN RECITALS
Sunday at 7:30 p.m. (June 25) and Thursday at 4:15 p.m. (June 29).
Warren D. Allen, University Organist, Memorial Church.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Exhibit: Oil paintings by William Ritschel until June 30. Collection of water colors
by J. E. Fitzgerald, until July 1. Photographs by the Stanford Univer
sity Camera Club, until July 2. Drawings of scenes at Stanford Uni
versity by Richard Stephens, until July 2. (The Art Gallery is open to
the public without charge from 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. daily, including
Sunday.)
UNIVERSITY LIBRARY
Exhibit: Recent gifts to the Fclton Library and Rare Book Collections.
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, in writing, should be in the hands of
the Secretary to the Director of the Summer Quarter, Room 173, Administration
Building, not later than 9:00 a.m., Tuesday. Calendar subscriptions at $1 for the
year or 50 cents for the quarter may be sent to the Secretary.
