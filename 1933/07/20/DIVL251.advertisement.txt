Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. LXVI, No. 5 July 20-26, 1933
CHURCH SERVICE
Sunday, July 23
11:00 a.m. Preacher: Dr. Charles R. Brown, Dean Emeritus of tlie Divinity
School, Yale University.
M usic
Andante from the Third Sonata Mendelssohn
Introit: "Lift Thine Eyes" (Elijah) Mendelssohn
Solo: "Be Thou Faithful unto Death" Mendelssohn
(St. Paul)
W. Madison Taylor, Tenor
Anthem: "Consider and Hear Me" Pflueger
Alto Solo: Esther H. Allen
TUESDAY EVENING SERIES
Tuesday, July 25
8:00 p.m. Lecture on "Race and Language Problems in South Africa," by
Dr. A. J. Barnouw, Queen Wilhelmina Professor of Dutch Language
and Literature, Columbia University. Assembly Hall. The public is
invited.
SPECIAL LECTURES
Thursday, July 20
8:00 p.m. "Nineteenth Century Classicists, Realists, and Impressionists: David,
Ingres, Daumier, Delacroix, Millet, Courbet, Manet, Monet, Degas, and
Renoir," by Professor A. B. Clark. Little Theatre. The public is invited.
Wednesday, July 26
4:15 p.m. "Virgil and His Italy," by Professor George D. Hadzsits, Acting Pro
fessor of Latin. Little Theatre. The public is invited.
ORGAN RECITALS
Sunday at 7:30 p.m. and Thursday at 4:15 p.m. Warren D. Allen,
University Organist, Memorial Church.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Exhibit: Oil paintings by William Ritschel, until August 1. Collection of water
colors by J. E. Fitzgerald, until August 1. Photographs by the Stanford
University Camera Club, until August 1. Drawings of scenes at Stan
ford University by Richard Stephens, until August 1. (The Art Gallery
is open to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m. to 5 :00 p.m. daily,
including Sunday.)
UNIVERSITY LIBRARY
Exhibit: Recent additions to the Rare Book Collection.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
12:10 p.m. Spanish Table in the Cafeteria, daily except Saturday and Sunday. All
those interested in Spanish conversation cordially invited to attend.
Saturday, July 22
8:00 a.m. Automobile Excursion to Salinas and the Rodeo. Leave from the Stan
ford Union, Main Entrance, returning 8 :00 p.m. Reservations must be
made two days in advance. Fare ($4.00) may be deposited in the oflice
of the Director of the Summer Quarter, Room 173, Administration
Building, or at the Peninsula Yellow Cab Co.
COMING EVENTS
Thursday, July 27
8:00 p.m. Special Lecture: "The Present Decade; and Its Foundations—Camera
Influence, Broad Visualism, Geometric and Color Plastic Abstractions;
Van Gogh, Cezanne, Matisse, Picasso, Demuth, Dickenson, Manship,
Arcliipenko, Sterne, and Rivera."
Tuesday, August 1
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series: Recital of Period Poetry by Miss Marjorie
1. M. Gullan, Director of The Speech Institute, London, and Lecturer
in Speech Training and Voice Production, Institute of Education, Uni
versity of London.
Wednesday, August 2
4:15 p.m. Special Lecture: "America's Recent Policy in the Far East," by Pro
fessor Charles E. Martin, Acting Professor of Political Science.
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, in writing, should be in the hands of
the Secretary to the Director of the Summer Quarter, Room 173, Administration
Building, not later than 9:00 a.m., Tuesday. Calendar subscriptions at $1 for the
year or 50 cents for the quarter may be sent to the Secretary.
