Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. LXVI, No. 6 July 27-August 2, 1933
CONCERT
Friday, July 28
3:30 p.m. The Pro Arte Quartet, in the Dance Studio of the Women's Gym
nasium. Students, members of the faculty and University staff may
obtain tickets upon application at the office of the Director of the Sum
mer Quarter.
CHURCH SERVICE
Sunday, July 30
11:00 a.m. Preacher: The Reverend Leslie E. Learned, D.D., All Saints Church,
Pasadena.
Music
Duet: "Crucifix" J. Faure
Winifred Elliott Estabkook, Soprano
Waldo Furgason, Baritone
Anthem: "Blest Are They Who Feel Compassion" Bach
Ruth L. Savage, Orijanisl
TUESDAY EVENING SERIES
Tuesday, August 1
8:00 p.m. Recital of Period Poetry by Miss Marjorie Gullan, of the University
of London. Little Theatre. The public is invited.
SPECIAL LECTURES
Thursday, July 27
8:00 p.m. "The Present Decade; and Its Foundations —Camera Influence, Broad
Visualism, Geometric and Color Plastic Abstractions; Van Gogh,
Cezanne, Matisse, Picasso, Demuth, Dickenson. Manship, Archipenko,
Sterne, and Rivera." Little Theatre. The public is invited.
Monday, July 31
8:00 p.m. "The Ability to Concentrate," by Dr. C. Gilbert Wrenn, Assistant
Registrar for Student Personnel. Little Theatre. The public is invited.
Wednesday, August 2
4:15 p.m. "America's Recent Policy in the Far East," by Professor Charles E.
Martin, Acting Professor of Political Science. Little Theatre. The
public is invited.
ORGAN RECITALS
Sunday at 7:30 p.m. and Thursday at 4:15 p.m. Warren D. Allen,
University Organist, Memorial Church.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Exhibit: Oil paintings by William Ritschel, until August 1. Collection of water
colors by J. E. Fitzgerald, until August 1. Photographs by the Stanford
University Camera Club, until August 1. Drawings of scenes at Stan
ford University by Richard Stephens, until August 1. (The Art Gallery
is open to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. daily,
including Sunday.)
UNIVERSITY LIBRARY
Exhibit: Recent additions to the Rare Book Collection.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
12:10 p.m. Spanish Table in the Cafeteria, daily except Saturday and Sunday. All
those interested in Spanish conversation cordially invited to attend.
Wednesday, August 2
2:00 p.m. Oral Examination for the Doctorate: Charles A. Sisson. (Minor ex
amination on thesis and following specified subjects in Romanic Lan
guages and History: Romanic Languages, A2Ol, 205. History, 101,
139.) Room 308, Library.
COMING EVENTS
Thursday, August 3
8:00 p.m. Special Lecture: "Music as an Emotional Expression and as Pure
Design," by Mr. Albert I. Elkus, lecturer in music at the University of
California and Mills College.
Monday, August 7
8:00 p.m. Special Lecture: "Teachers' Salaries and the Costs of Living," witli
illustrations, by Professor Walter C. Eells of thv Department of
Education.
Tuesday, August 8
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series: Lecture on "Where Is America?" by Pro
fessor Edgar E. Robinson, Margaret Byrne Professor of American
History.
Wednesday, August 9
4:15 p.m. Special Lecture: "Unemployment Reserves and Business Stability," by
Professor Alvin H. Hansen, Acting Professor of Economics.
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, in writing, should be in the hands of
the Secretary to the Director of the Summer Quarter, Room 173, Administration
Building, not later than 9:00 a.m., Tuesday. Calendar subscriptions at $1 for the
year or 50 cents for the quarter may be sent to the Secretary.
