Vol. LXVI, No. 10 August 24-September 2, 1933

SPECIAL LECTURES
Thursday, August 24
4:15 p.m. "The Essentials of Screen Acting," by Josephine Dillon Gable. Little
Theatre. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. "The Fugue of J. S. Bach" with illustrations, by Mr. Albert I. Elkus.
Little Theatre. The public is invited.
ORGAN RECITALS
Sunday at 7:30 p.m. and Thursday at 4:15 p.m. Warren D. Allen,
University Organist, Memorial Church.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Exhibit: Oil paintings by William Ritschel. until September 1. Photographs by the
Stanford University Camera Club, until September 1. (The Art Gallery
is open to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m. to 5 :00 p.m. daily,
including Sunday.)
UNIVERSITY LIBRARY
Exhibit: Recent additions to the Rare Book Collection.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
12:10 p.m. Spanish Table in the Cafeteria, daily except Saturday and Sunday. All
those interested in Spanish conversation cordially invited to attend.
Thursday, August 24
2:00 p.m. Oral Examination for the Doctorate: William W. Tinsley. (Major,
History; Minor, Political Science.) Room 310, Library.
4:15 p.m. The University Philological Association in the Women's Clubhouse.
Papers: Professor Nicolai O. Lossky on "The Philosophers G. and E.
Trubetskoy as Representatives of Russian Culture" ; Professor Julius
Petersen on "Die Autizipation in Goethe's Dichtung."
Saturday, August 26
8:00 a.m. Automobile Excursion to San Francisco, by Skyline Boulevard. Leave
from the Stanford Union. Main Entrance, returning about 5:00 p.m.
Reservations must be made two days in advance. Fare ($2.50) may be
deposited in the office of the Director of the Summer Quarter, Room
173, Administration Building, or at the Peninsula Yellow Cab Co.
Tuesday, August 29
2:00 p.m. Oral Examination for the Doctorate: Louis Paul Woerner. (Major,
Germanic Languages; Minor, French.) Room 310, Library.
END-QUARTER EXAMINATIONS
Thursday, August 31, to Saturday, Septembebr 2, inclusive.
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, in writing, should be in the hands of
the Secretary to the Director of the Summer Quarter, Room 173, Administration
Building, not later than 9:00 a.m., Tuesday. Calendar subscriptions at $1 for the
year or 50 cents for the quarter may be sent to the Secretary.
