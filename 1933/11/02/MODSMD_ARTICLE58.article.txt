# CARD, MILLS, U. C., WOMEN 10 HOLD TRIANGULAR MEET
## 
### 
Stanford women will participate
in the annual Triangle Sports Day
to be held in conjunction with the
University of California and Mill!)
College this Saturday from 8:30 un
til 3:30 o'clock. This meet, taking
place at the University of Califor
nia, will feature interclass compe
tion in golf, hockey, archery, tennis,
and swimming.
A bus to carry the participants is
to be sent at the expense of the
W.A.A. board and will leave Roble
G.#n at 7 o'clock Saturday morning. I
Luncheon will be served in the i
Women's Clubrooms of Steven's 1
Union at 12:30 o'clock.
Tryouts for the swimming events
will be held at 4:30 o'clock this af
ternoon at the pool in the Women's
Gym.
The day's schedule is as follows:
Registration at 8:30 o'clock in the
Arcade of Hearst Gymnasium, golf
from 8:30 to 12 o'ciock at the Berk
eley Country Club, tennis from 9 to
1? on Ihe California tennis courts,
archery from 8:45 to 10:30 o'clock, |
hockey from 9:15 to 12:15 o'clock, a '
novelty event at 2 o'clock on the
West Field, and swimming from
2:30 to 3:30 o'clock.
