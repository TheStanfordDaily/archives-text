# U.C. SCENE OF TRI-SPORT DAY
## 
### 
Women athletes of three colleges
will be represented tit Berkeley to
morrow morning when women from
Stanford, Mills, and the University
of California participate in the an
nual Triangle Sports Day. Featured
, will be interclass competition in golf,
hockey, archery, tennis, and swim
ming, which will be the order of the
day from 8:30 until 3:30 o'clock on
the California campus.
Stanford participants will be con
veyed to Berkeley at the expense of
the W.A.A. board, a bus leaving
lioble Gym at 7 o'clock Saturday
morning. At 12 o'clock luncheon
will be served in the Women's Club
rcoms of Stevens Union.
This afternoon at 4:30 o'clock in
the pool in the Women's Gym will
be held tryouts for swimming
1 events.
Following is a program of the
day's events: Registration at 8:30
■ o'clock in the Arcade of Hearst
Gymnasium, golf from 8:30 to 12
o'clock at the Berkeley Country
, Club, tennis from 9 to 12 on the
| California tennis courts, archery
from 8:45 to 10:30 o'clock, hockey
from 9:15 to 12:15 o'clock, a novelty
event at 2 o'clock on the West Field,
and swimming from 2:30 to 3:30
o'clock.
