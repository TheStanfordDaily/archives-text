# Card Netmen Defeat J. C.
## 
### 
Netmen from the 1934 edition of
the Card freshmen team defeated
Modesto Junior College Saturday
morning, five matches to four. Sur
prisingly, the Babe netmen won the
majority of their first singles
matches, but could hardly muster
strength in the last positions.
Ben Dey won the first singles
match handily, defeating Hogin. 6-1,
6-2. In the second singles match,
Captain Bill Seward likewise won in
straight sets, giving his opponent,
Kech, no breaks, and easily outplay
ing him. The scores were 6-1, 6-1.
Teaming together in the first dou
bles match, Dey and Seward played
smoothly together, defeating Van
Bushkirk and Ayres 6-1, 6-1. How
ever, in the remainder of the match
Stanford did not fare so well. Com
plete summaries are:
Ayres defeated Dibblee (S) 6-3,
6-3; Taylor (S) defeated Yates, 8-6,
4-6, 6-3; Woten (M) defeated Cox.
2-6, 6-2, 6-4; Welles (S) defeated
Bright 7-5, 6-2. In the doubles,
Hogin-Keck defeated Dibblee-Tay
lor (S) 10-8, 5-7, 6-4. Wcten-Gates
defeated Cox-Welles, 6-4, 2-6, 6-3.
