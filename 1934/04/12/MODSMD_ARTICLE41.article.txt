# Clubs Will Offer Special Entertainment at Dance
## 
### 
Special entertainment by mem
bers of a 12-piece orchestra is to
be a feature of the Toyon-Branner
dance to be held Saturday night. It
promises to be one of the "better
informals." Admission to the affair
is free for those with Toyon and
Branner cards, and one dollar for
others.
Patrons and patronesses who will
attend are: Mr. and Mrs. George
Wilson, Mr. and Mrs. Stanley Sharp,
Dean Stallings, and Miss Harriett
Pillsbury.
The committee of Branner men
handling the arrangements are Bob
Calkins, chairman, Roy Anderson,
Sam Anderson, Brower Dellinger.
and Art Lundin.
