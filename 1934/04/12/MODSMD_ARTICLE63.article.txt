## CONSTITUTION OF THE ASSOCIATED STUDENTS OF STANFORD UNIVERSITY 
### 
We, the students of Stanford University, in order to provide an organi
zation for the settlement and regulation of all matters delegated by the
University to student government, do ordain and establish the following
constitution:
ARTICLE I. NAME AND MEMBERSHIP
Section 1. —Name. —The name of the Association under this constitution
shall be "The Associated, Students of Stanford University."
Section 2. —Membership.—All regularly enrolled students of Stanford
University at Palo Alto, graduates and undergraduates, are members of this
Association. Only members of this Association shall be entitled to vote in
any Association election, to hold Association or class office, or to represent
Stanford in any intercollegiate activity.

Section I.—President, Vice-President, and Women's Executive.
a. Basis of Election. One man shall be chosen at the regular May elec
tion by the Association at large to be the President of this Association. One
graduate student shall be chosen at the regular May election by the Asso
ciaion at large to be the Vice-President of this Association. One woman
.shall be chosen at the regular May election by the women members of the
Association to be the Women's Executive of this Association.
b. Powers and Duties of the President.—The president shall be the
Executive head of the Association; he shall be chairman of the Executive
Committee, with a casting vote in case of a tie; he shall have a regular vote
on the Executive Committee, and he shall call and preside at meetings of
the Association. He shall appoint, subject to ratification by the Executive
Committee, all officers, committees, or representatives of this Association
not otherwise provided for by this Constitution; he shall have the pov/er to
fill all vacancies in the offices of this Association, except those of President
and Women's Executive and those not othenvisp provided for in this con
stitution subject to ratification by the Executive Committee. ,

of this Association, except those handling matters peculiar to women; and
he shall be one of the representatives of this Association on the Board of
Athletic Control and on the Union Boax*d of Governors.
c. Powers and Duties of the Vice-President.—The Vice-President shall
be a member of the Executive Committee; he or she shall preside over
meetings of the Executive Committee in the absence of the President; he
or she shall exercise the powers and duties of the President in his absence.
In case of the resignation or disqualification of the President, the Vice-
President shall exercise his powers and duties until his successor is elected.
d. Powers and Duties of the Women's Executive.—The Women's Execu
tive shall be a member of the Executive Committee; she shall have the
power to appoint committees to handle matters peculiar to women, subject
to ratification by the Executive Committee. She shall be the President of
Women's Conference; she shall be an ex-officio member (with right to
vote) of all committees appointed by her.
e. Vacancies.—ln case of resignation or disqualification of the President
or Women's Executive, the Executive Committee shall call a special election
immediately thereafter to fill the vacancy in either of these offices.
Section 2. Executive Committee. »
a. Membership.—ln addition to the President, Vice-President, and the
Women's Executive, the Executive Committee shall be composed of five
members as follows: one man and one woman, both senior students in the
University; one man and one woman, both junior students in the University;

be chosen at the regular May election by the members of this Association at
large. The class standing of these members shall be determined as of the
time when they assume office. The definition of the terms Senior, Junior,
and Sophomore shall be such as may be provided by the Executive Commit
tee in the by-laws.
The following shall be ex-officio members without vote; President of
Ihe Women's Athletic Association, Editor of the Stanford Daily and the
Student Manager.
b. Powers and Duties—All legislative and executive powers of this
Association shall be vested in the Executive Committee subject to the pro
visions herein enumerated.

pointments of the President and Women s Executive of this Association not

4. The Executive Committee shall have power to levy all fees and as

and shall have power to accept or reject all authorizations for expenditures
from the funds of this Association.
5. The Executive Committee shall have control over the creation and
dissolution of all organizations on the campus; with the exception of living
groups and eating clubs.
(>. The Executive Committee shall have the power to create, designate,
or dissolve any subsidiary organizations of this Association. It shall also
have the power to grant or revoke charters to the same, subject to the pro

7. The Executive Committee shall have power to designate major and
minor sports, and shall have power of granting athletic letters and other
emblems awarded by this Association.
8. The Executive Committee shall publish a handbook each year con
taining a copy of the constitution of this Association, the by-laws ol this
Association, and any other information it may deem desirable.
9. The Executive Committee shall appoint and provide for the reason
able compensation of a technically qualified secretary to carry out such
duties as the Executive Committee may specify.
10. The Executive Committee shall have the power to appoint such
class committees and officers, not otherwise provided for in this constitution,
as may be necessary.
11. The Executive Committee shall have the power of calling electipns
in accordance with the provisions of Article VII of this Constitution.
Section 3. Roard of Athletic Control.—There shall be three representa

ARTICLE VII. ELECTIONS
Section 1. Time of Elections.
There shall be one regular election held during the University year,
on the second Tuesday in May. This date may be changed by the Exec
utive Committee provided that notice of such change be published in
The Stanford Daily at least two weeks in advance of said date.
Section 2. Special Elections.
Special elections shall be called by the Executive Committee within
two weeks after the receipt of a petition signed by three hundred members
of this Association.
Section 3. Nominations.
Nominations for each election shall be open on the Tuesday preced
ing the elections, and closed on the evening of the following day. II the
election cannot be held on a Tuesday, the Executive Committee may desig
nate an appropriate day.
Section 4. Term of Office.
All officers elected at the regular Association election shall take office
at the beginning of the Fall Quarter and shall hold office for one year.
Sectipn 5. Qualification for Holding Office,—To be eligible for an elec
tive or appointive office under this Constitution, its Charters, or its by-laws,
members of this Association must have a C average in his or her work.
Failure to maintain a C average will automatically vacate such office. The
Executive Committee shall specify in the by-laws the offices to which this
provision shall apply.
Section G. Election Roard.—All elections of this Association and The
Stanford Daily shall be conducted by an election board to be appointed
by the President of this Association, subject to ratification by the Executive
Committee. This board shall consist of a chairman and eleven other mem
bers. Assistants shall be appointed by the President of this Association
upon the recommendation of the Chairman of the Election Board.
The board shall be appointed in October of each year to serve a full
year. The election board shall have complete charge of all elections held
under the authority of this Association. The election board shall also
, determine the eligibility of all candidates for elective or appointive office
as of the current Directory of Officers and Students.
Section 7. Election by Preferential Ballot.—The preferential voting sys
-1 tem shall be used whenever more than two candidates are running for
office. To elect any officer a majority of all Ihe ballots counted is neces
sary. The ballots are distributed according to first choice. If no one candi
date receives the requisite majority of first choices, the ballots of that
candidate who has received the lowest number are redistributed according
to the second choice indicated thereon, and another count for the majority
| is made. The redistribution and recounting are continued until one candi
' date is elected. Third choices are counted only when first and second on
i the ballot have been exhausted.

The by-laws and charters under this constitution may be made,

, ARTICLE X. INTERPRETATION
All matters of interpretation of this constitution, the by-laws of this
Association, or any charters or by-laws of organizations under this Con

shall be "The Associated, Students of Stanford University."
Section 2. —Membership.—All regularly enrolled students of Stanford
University at Palo Alto, graduates and undergraduates, are members of this
Association. Only members of this Association shall be entitled to vote in
any Association election, to hold Association or class office, or to represent
Stanford in any intercollegiate activity.

ARTICLE VII. ELECTIONS
Section 1. Time of Elections.
There shall be one regular election held during the University year,
on the second Tuesday in May. This date may be changed by the Exec
utive Committee provided that notice of such change be published in
The Stanford Daily at least two weeks in advance of said date.
Section 2. Special Elections.
Special elections shall be called by the Executive Committee within
two weeks after the receipt of a petition signed by three hundred members
of this Association.
Section 3. Nominations.
Nominations for each election shall be open on the Tuesday preced
ing the elections, and closed on the evening of the following day. II the
election cannot be held on a Tuesday, the Executive Committee may desig
nate an appropriate day.
Section 4. Term of Office.
All officers elected at the regular Association election shall take office
at the beginning of the Fall Quarter and shall hold office for one year.
Sectipn 5. Qualification for Holding Office,—To be eligible for an elec
tive or appointive office under this Constitution, its Charters, or its by-laws,
members of this Association must have a C average in his or her work.
Failure to maintain a C average will automatically vacate such office. The
Executive Committee shall specify in the by-laws the offices to which this
provision shall apply.
Section G. Election Roard.—All elections of this Association and The
Stanford Daily shall be conducted by an election board to be appointed
by the President of this Association, subject to ratification by the Executive
Committee. This board shall consist of a chairman and eleven other mem
bers. Assistants shall be appointed by the President of this Association
upon the recommendation of the Chairman of the Election Board.
The board shall be appointed in October of each year to serve a full
year. The election board shall have complete charge of all elections held
under the authority of this Association. The election board shall also
, determine the eligibility of all candidates for elective or appointive office
as of the current Directory of Officers and Students.
Section 7. Election by Preferential Ballot.—The preferential voting sys
-1 tem shall be used whenever more than two candidates are running for
office. To elect any officer a majority of all Ihe ballots counted is neces
sary. The ballots are distributed according to first choice. If no one candi

candidate who has received the lowest number are redistributed according

ARTICLE IV. CLASS OFFICERS
Section 1. There shall be a President, Vice-President, and Secretary-
Treasurer of the Senior Class.
Section 2. Senior Class officers shall be elected by members of the
class at the regular Association election in May to serve the following year.
Section 3. There shall be no other elective class officers in any class
save the Senior Class.
ARTICLE V. FINANCE
" Section 1. Management.—The General Manager of the Board of Ath
letic Control shall be the financial officer of this Association. He shall have
supervision over all Association funds, subject to the control and approval
of the Executive Committee. He shall appoint a student manager as his
assistant.
a. The Student Manager.—The Student Manager shall perform such
duties and observe such rules as may be prescribed by this Association, or
by its financial officer. He shall be one of the representatives of this Asso
ciation on the Union Board of Governors ahd on the Dramatic Council; he
shall be an ex-officio member without vote on the Publications Council.
Section 2. Students' Organizations Fund.
a. Requirements.—Funds of this Association, its subsidiary organiza
tions. and of all campus organizations which receive money from student
members and whose finances are in any way controlled by student members
with the exception of living groups and eating clubs, must be deposited with
the Students' Organizations Fund. The financial officer of each organiza
tion shall be required to register with the student manager at the time he
assumes office. The Executive Committee may prscribe additional rules
for the Students' Organizations Fund.
b. Enforcement of Rules.—The Executive Committee and/or the stu
dent manager through the proper councils for student control, shall have
the power to enforce the rules of the Students' Organizations Fund.
ARTICLE. VI. SUBSIDIARY ORGANIZATIONS
Section 1. Enumeration.
a. The following are subsidiary organizations of this Association: Pub
lications Council; Dramatic Council; Debate Council; Rally Committee;
Concert Series; Women's Athletic Association; Women's Conference.
Section 2. Relationship to Association.
a. Each of the above enumerated organizations, together with each as
may be created by the Executive Committee of this Association, or des
ignated by it, shall operate under a Charier and by-laws granted by the
Executive Committee of this Association. The Executive Committee by a
three-fourths vote of its voting membership may amend, abrogate, or
otherwise revise any charter or by-laws granted by it or made by any of
the above enumerated organizations, subject however, to the provisions ol
Article IX of this Constitution.
ARTICLE VII. ELECTIONS
Section 1. Time of Elections.
There shall be one regular election held during the University year,
on the second Tuesday in May. This date may be changed by the Exec
utive Committee provided that notice of such change be published in
The Stanford Daily at least two weeks in advance of said date.
Section 2. Special Elections.
Special elections shall be called by the Executive Committee within
two weeks after the receipt of a petition signed by three hundred members
of this Association.
Section 3. Nominations.
Nominations for each election shall be open on the Tuesday preced
ing the elections, and closed on the evening of the following day. II the
election cannot be held on a Tuesday, the Executive Committee may desig
nate an appropriate day.
Section 4. Term of Office.
All officers elected at the regular Association election shall take office
at the beginning of the Fall Quarter and shall hold office for one year.
Sectipn 5. Qualification for Holding Office,—To be eligible for an elec
tive or appointive office under this Constitution, its Charters, or its by-laws,
members of this Association must have a C average in his or her work.
Failure to maintain a C average will automatically vacate such office. The
Executive Committee shall specify in the by-laws the offices to which this
provision shall apply.
Section G. Election Roard.—All elections of this Association and The
Stanford Daily shall be conducted by an election board to be appointed
by the President of this Association, subject to ratification by the Executive
Committee. This board shall consist of a chairman and eleven other mem
bers. Assistants shall be appointed by the President of this Association
upon the recommendation of the Chairman of the Election Board.
The board shall be appointed in October of each year to serve a full
year. The election board shall have complete charge of all elections held
under the authority of this Association. The election board shall also
, determine the eligibility of all candidates for elective or appointive office
as of the current Directory of Officers and Students.

ARTICLE IX. BY-LAWS AND CHARTERS
The by-laws and charters under this constitution may be made,
amended or revoked by the vote of three-quarters of the voting member
ship of the Executive Committee at a regular meeting; but shall not take
effect until the next regular meeting; and not then it a petition containing
300 signatures of members of this Association asking that the proposed
change be submitted to a vote of the Association has been presented to the
chairman of the Exeptitive Committee. The vote may then be taken at
either a regular or special election of the Association, a majority being
necessary for approval of the proposed changes.
, ARTICLE X. INTERPRETATION
All matters of interpretation of this constitution, the by-laws of this
Association, or any charters or by-laws of organizations under this Con
stitution shall be decided by the Dean of the Law School of the University,
or in the event of his absence, by the acting Dean of the Law School. Re
quests for interpretation may be made only by the President of this Asso
ciation or the person or party aggrieved, and only in writing.
ARTICLE XI. ENACTMENT
This Constitution shall go into effect on May 1, 1934. After its adoption
and before May 1, 1934, the Executive Committee shall have the power to
draw up and install a set of by-laws and Charters in conformity with this
Constitution. No provision of this Constitution .'■hall interfere with or im
pair the authority of any elective officers of this Association already
chosen whose terms of office have not yet expired. All rules or laws of
this Association, the Associated Women Students, the Women's Athletic
Association, contrary to this Constitution, its Charters and by-laws are
hereby repealed. This Constitution supplants the previous Constitutions of
the Associated Students of Stanford University, the Associated Women
Students, the Women's Athletic Association in their entirety.
