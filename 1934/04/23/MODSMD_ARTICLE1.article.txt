# CONSTITUTION'S CHANGES WILL BE CONSIDERED
## Complaints To Be Heard Tomorrow Night By Ex Committee MEETING OPEN TO ALL Forum and All-University Assembly Planned 
### 
Complaints or varying views on
phases of the proposed A.S.S.U. con
stitution will be heard tomorrow
night in the Ex Committee room
when the new Constitution Revision
Committee sponsors a discussion
meeting.
Set for 8:30 o'clock, the committee
will hear speakers from the floor on
four scheduled topics and any others
on which there may be complaints.
Before the meeting, the committee
will meet to consider any written
complaints which may have been
turned in to the A.S.S.U. office.
Eight major changes have already
been determined, and will be in
cluded in the proposed constitution
when it comes up for a second spe
cial election.l A Forum meeting and
an All-University assembly are ten
tatively scheduled for this week be
fore the revised document is brought
before the Ex Committee next Mon
day night.
Items for Discussion
1. Whether the classification of
major and minor sports shall appear
in the constitution proper instead of
the by-laws; whether or not the Ex
Committee shall determine which
are major sports.
2. Membership of the Ex Commit
tee and rotation of its members (ro
tation is not provided for in the
constitution which was defeated).
3. Whether or not prospective
men's sponsors shall be recom
mended by a student committee.
4. Whether Men's and Women's
Councils shall be appointed or
elected.
Points Decided
Points already decided for change
are:
1. The "fraternity clause" is al
tered to give Men's and Women's
Councils jurisdiction over rulings of
the Intrafraternity Board of Control
and Panhellenic "only when re
quested by either of them."
2. Method of removal is specified
for officers and Ex Committee mem
bers.
3. The chairman of the Men's and
Women's Council will be ex officio
members of the Ex Committee
(without vote), and the W.A.A. pres
ident is dropped from the list of ex
officio members.
4. The number of signatures re
quired on a petition for removal or
for a special election is 150 and not
300.
5. Musical activities are to be in
cluded under a charter similar to
the ones now given dramatics and
debating.
Provide Class OlTicers
(i. There are class officers pro
vided for in all classes, with the
Senior class being given a president,
vice-president, secretary-treasurer,
and the others a president and sec
retary.
7. The C average clause is changed
to read that a C avarage is required
at the time of eloction or appoint
ment, but if an officer falls below
that level while in office his position
shall not be vacated for that reason.
8. The springs election." will be
on the third Tuesday in May instead
of the second.
