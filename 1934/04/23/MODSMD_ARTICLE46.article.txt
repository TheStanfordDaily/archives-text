; duel turned out to be a slugfest Sat
urday afternoon on the Sunken Dia
mond as California won by a 12-7
score over Stanford's baseball var
: sity in the final game of the 1934
| series.

I the hands of the Bears was a repeti
| tion in many ways of the previous
; contests. Stanford batters were on
a par with the Bear sluggers, both
I teams accounting for 11 hits. Er
ratic fielding and men left on bases

dians. Three poor innings on
part of the pitchers, during which all
12 of California's runs were scored,
j spoiled defeat for the Cards, although
j the pitchers were responsible for
j only eight of the 12.
Stan Anderson opened on the
mound for the Indians and held the
| Bears to two runs and three hits
; until the fatal fifth when Clint
Evans' men went on a rampage. An
derson lasted only a third of that
inning, when Fred Heringer was sent
in as the relief hurler. Heringer,
too, pitched masterful ball until his
one bad inning, the ninth, when Cal
took advantage of a brace of errors
and four hits to tally five runs.
For Stanford, Captain Dick Miles
and Second Baseman Ed Galbraith
had big days at the plate. Miles hit
three for four, driving in three runs,
while Galbraith sent the other In
dian runs across the plate with his
three hits, one a double, in five at
tempts.
Captain Sparling of California led
the hitters of both teams with four
hits in five trips to the plate.
