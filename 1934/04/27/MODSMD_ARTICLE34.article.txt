AIRLINES —Radio fans will have
to be on their toes to keep track of
their favorite programs when day
light-baying time. goes into effect
Sunday. With a few exceptions all
transcontinental programs will ad
vance one hour in time.
All NBC's programs advance one
hour on Sunday, but CBS programs
are not quite so accommodating.
Waring's Pennsylvanians keep the
same hour, 5:30 o'clock Sunday, and
do not advance in time, while the
New York Philharmonic, Little
Theatre of the Air, Wayne King, and
the mid-evening dance broadcasts
will be heard earlier.
New programs scheduled for
KFRC Sunday include the "Chicago
Knights" at 1 o'clock, "Hampton In
stitute Choir" at 3 o'clock, "Freddie
Rich Entertains" at 4:15 o'clock, and
"California Melodies" at 4:30 o'clock.
NBC will present a new program
entitled "The Court of Human Re
lations" over KGO at 8:15 o'clock
Sunday evening. The airing will be
a dramatic presentation with a
courtroom background and will con
sist of a series of dramatic re-enact
ments of real life stories.
