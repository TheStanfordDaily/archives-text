# Senior Week
## 
### 
With the last vistage of academic strain removed by the end of
linals on Wednesday, June 13, Senior Week begins. Culminating
with graduation ceremonies, the week will be filled with class activi
ties in which relatives and friends of the seniors arc invited to par
take. Following is the list of events;
WEDNESDAY, JUNE 13
6:30 p.m.—Fraternity and club dinners.
THURSDAY, JUNE 14
6:30 p.m.—Complimentary dinner to the Seniors, by the Alumni
Council, in the Women's Gymnasium.
8:00 p.m.—Senior Prom, in the Women's Gymnasium.
FRIDAY, JUNE 15
9:00 a.m.—Meeting of the Academic Council, in Room 12.
9:45 a.m.—Class Day exercises, in the Memorial Church. The class
plate will be laid, the class history read, the class will
read, the class oration given, and the cLass poem read.
Final instructions for Commencement will be made.
11:30 a.m.—Pilgrimage of Commemoration to the tomb of the Found
ers.
12:00 M—Class Day luncheon for the Seniors and their parents
and friends, in the Union Court.
4-fl p.m.—Reception by President and Mrs, Ray Lyman Wilbur, at
the President's Home, to the recipients of degrees and
their families, members of the faculty, and alumni.
10 p.m.-5 a.m.—Senior Ball, in Toyon Hall.
4:30 p.m.—Annual open meeting of Phi Beta Kappa, in the Little
Theatre. Professor Camillo von Klenze will speak on
"The Present Intellectual Crisis and the University.''
0:00 p.m.—Phi Beta Kappa Initiation, Women's Clubhouse.
(i:3O p.m.—Phi Beta Kappa Dinner, Stanford Union.
SUNDAY, JUNE 17
11:00 a.m.—Baccalaureate Services, in the Memorial Church: Ser
mon by the Rev. J. R. P. Sclatcr, D.D., Toronto, Canada.
4:00 p.m.—Faculty at homes.
7:30 p.m.—Organ Recital, by Warren D. Allen, in the Memorial
Church.
