Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. LXX, No. 1 June 21-27, 1934
CONCERT
Friday, June 22
4:15 p.m. Pro Arte Quartet of Brussels. Memorial Church. The public is invited.
AT HOME
Saturday, June 23
8:00 p.m. President and Mrs. Wilbur will be at home informally to summer
quarter students and members of the summer quarter faculty. Presi
dent's House, Lagunita Knoll.
CHURCH SERVICE
Sunday, June 24
11:00 a.m. Preacher: The Reverend Howard Melisli, Church of the Holy Trinity,
Brooklyn.
TUESDAY EVENING SERIES
Tuesday, June 26
8:00 p.m. Lecture on "The Approach to Poetry," by Mr. Edward Davison. Dance
Studio, Women's Gymnasium. The public is invited.
SPECIAL LECTURE
Wednesday, June 27
4:15 p.m. "Poetry in Our Time," by Mn Edward Davison. Little Theatre. The
public is invited.
ORGAN RECITALS
Sunday at 7:30 p.m., and Thursday at 4:15 p.m.
Warren D. Allen, University Organist, Memorial Church.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Exhibits: Oil paintings by Evelyn McCormick, until June 30. Oil paintings by
Charlton Fortune, until June 30. (The Art Gallery is open to the public
without charge from 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. daily, including Sunday.)
UNIVERSITY LIBRARY
Exhibits: Some of the manuscripts and letters of English and American writers
from the Library collection.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
Monday, June 25
7:45 p.m. Stanford University Hour over KPO. Speaker: Mr. Warren D. Allen,
Organist, and Lecturer in Music. Subject: "Music as a Subject in a
Liberal Education."
Wednesday, June 27
12:10 p.m. French Table in the Union Alcove. All members of the University cor
dially invited to attend.
COMING EVENTS
Thursday, June 28
8:00 p.m. Special Lecture : "Recent Currents in the Intellectual Life of Germany,"
by Professor Camillo von Klenze, Acting Professor of German.
Friday, June 29
4:15 p.m. Pro Arte Quartet of Brussels. Students, members of the faculty, and
University staff may obtain tickets upon application at the office of the
Director of the Summer Quarter.
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, in writing, should be in the hands of
the Secretary to the Director of the Summer Quarter, Room 173, Administration
Building, not later than 9:00 a.m. Tuesday. Calendar subscriptions at $1 plus tax,
2 cents, for the year or 25 cents plus tax, 1 cent, for the quarter may be sent to the
Secretary.
