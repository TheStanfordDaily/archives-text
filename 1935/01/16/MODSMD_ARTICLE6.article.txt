# Few Formal Bids Remain
## 
### 
With few bids left to be sold, the
Encina Club dance committee is
completing its preparations for tha
freshmen's number one social event
of the quarter, the Formal. The re
maining bids can be purchased from
either the A.S.S.U. office or George
Ditz, committee chairman. Sale of
bids is open to all persons on the
campus.
Programs for the evening have ar
rived and may be obtained at the
A.S.S.U. office upon presentation of
a bid. Ed Fitzpatrick and his or
chestra will play from 9 until 1
o'clock.
Patron and patronesses who have
consented to serve Friday evening
are: Dean Mary Yost, Mr. and Mrs.
A. E. Roth, Mr. and Mrs. H. J. Rath
bun, Mr. and Mrs. G. M. Sprague,
and Mr. and Mrs. W. S. Hopkins.
