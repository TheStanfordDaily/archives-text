# Women's Annual Field Day Scheduled June 4
## 
### 
Tennis, swimming, baseball, golf,
and archery will provide recrea
tion for athletically minded Farm
women on the annual Field Day,
June 4. Competition in these sports
will begin at 1:30 o'clock and last
until the banquet at 6 o'clock.
One hundred tickets to the ban
quet are now available at 35 cents,
according to Lee Stearns, in charge
of the dinner. Any additional tick
ets will sell at the regular price of
75 cents. New W.A.A. officers will
be installed at the banquet and new
managers announced.
One of the features of the day will
be th'i baseball game between the
faculty and the all-stars.
