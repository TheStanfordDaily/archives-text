# Union Dining Room, Cellar Remain Open
## 
### 
Both the large dining room in the
Union and the cellar will be open to
students and visitors during the sum
mer quarter.
The Union dining room will be open
during week days between 12 and 1 p.m.
and 6 and 7 p.m., and will serve lunch
eons from 45 cents up and dinners 50
cents and up. The Sunday hours will
be 12:15 to 1:45 p.m. and 6 p.m. to
7 p.m. Meal tickets for use in the din
ing room only are available at a saving
of 10 per cent.
The Education Department luncheons
will be held e&ch Thursday, beginning
June 27. Tickets for these must be ob
tained at the Education office.
Special parties and menus may be
arranged through the Union office by
phoning Palo Alto 3136.
The Cellar, which offers fountain and
cofTcc shop service, as well as candy,
tobacco, and household remedies, is
open ihe following hours: Monday to
Thursday inclusive, 7:30 a.m. to 10:30
p.m.; Friday, Saturday, and Sunday
evenings it will be closed at 8:00 p.m.
A circulating library is also main
tained, which is open from 12 to 2 p.m.
Mrs. Helen Miller will be in charge
of dining facilities on the campus dur
ing the first part of the summer. Miss
Ricker will also be on duty.
