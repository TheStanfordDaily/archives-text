# Greeting Given By Dr. Wilbur
## Opportunities for Closer Contact Stressed 
### 
Dr. J3ay Lyman Wilbur today issued
the following message of welcome to
summer quarter students:
Stanford's summer has its own pe
culiar charms. In many ways the
campus is at its best in its quiet and
restful beauty, and such diversions as
there arc have in them a dash of ar
tistic, musical or intellectual interest.
The students come closer to their
professors than is possible during the
rest of the year.
In welcoming the students for the
summer quarter of 1935 may I em
phasize the hope that you will
promptly join in the spirit of com
radeship that has been developed in
past summers, and that you will feel
yourselves full-fledged Stanford men
and women—even if you cannot be
come rooters at athletic games.
There are so many advantages be
fore you that I wish they could be
enjoyed at least once by every Stan
ford student.
