# Refreshments To Be Served at Jolly-Up
## 
### 
Refreshments will be served at a
jolly-up for the fii'st time in Stan
ford history when apple cider is
offered to those attending the jun
iors' no-date affair Thursday eve
ning in the Women's Gym. This
function of the Class of '37 will be
held from 7 until 8 o'clock with a
Hallowe'en background.
With Frank Brown's 12-piece or
chestra providing the music, this
jolly-up should attract a large
crowd. Arrangements are being
made by a committee composed of
Esther PeJton, Ruth Neupert, Jim
Reynolds, Roger Kimball, and Bill
Quade.
