# Indian Netmen Trim Troy By 5-4 Margin
## Doubles Decide Tilt as Seward, Underwood Win Cards Even Tennis Series With U.S.C.; Will Meet Bruins in Next Contest 
### Tim Sullivan 
Second place was reserved for the
Trojan tennis team by Messrs. Bob
Underwood and Bill Seward of
Stanford when they staged a gal
lant up-hill battle to trim Jack
Knemeyer and "Waxy" Wetherell of
S.C. 3-6, 12-10, 11-9, in the decisive
match of the afternoon Saturday. All
other contests were evenly divided,
the final standings favoring the In
dians 5 to 4.
Bill Seward (S) had a fairly easy
time disposing of Knemeyer (SC), as
did his team-mate, Bob Braly, in
trouncing Jack Creamer of the
southerners 6-2, 7-5. However, Ben
Dey found Chuck Carr of Troy a bit
too tough to handle and lost to him
in three sets 1-6, 6-0, 3-6. Likewise,
Vern John (SC) proved a bitter pill
for John Law (S), and beat him 6-2,
6-0. Bob Underwood squared the
singles account with the visitors
when he out-volleyed their man
Hanson, 7-5, 7-5, after Wetherell
(SC) had won from Pommer (S),
6-3, 6-1.
