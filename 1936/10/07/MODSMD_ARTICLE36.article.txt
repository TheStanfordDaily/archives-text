# Y.W.C.A. To Hold Candlelight Rite
## 
### 
An impressive candle-light cere
mony will take place in the Memo
rial Chapel tomorrow at 5:30 o'clock
when the Y.W.C.A. presents its an
nual recognition service for new and
old members.
Organ selections by Ray Kendall,
a vocal solo by Delphine Joy, ai'e
among the musical numbers on the
program. Miss Elizabeth Lam, Y
secretary, and Pat Hamilton, presi
dent, will talk on the meaning and
purpose of Y.W.C.A. membership.
The benediction will be pronounced
by Rev. Elton Trueblood, new Stan
ford chaplain.
From the Chapel the entire group
will proceed by candlelight to the
faculty clubhouse, where the recog
nition banquet will be held. Mrs.
Ray Kendall, president of the
Y.W.C.A. last year, will give the
main speech of the evening. Dinner
tickets, which are 50 cents, may be
obtained in the Y rooms in the
Women's Clubhouse.
