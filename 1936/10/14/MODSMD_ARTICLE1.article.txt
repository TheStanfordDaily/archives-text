# Row Urged To Observe New Rules
## Rushing Dates Planned For Week-end; Periods Lengthened by Board 
### GEORGE VENESS 
With the initial rushing period of
the 1936-37 season only two days
away, Bob Hoover, president of the
Interfraternity Council, asks that all
Ffr>sh and Row men pay particular
attention to the revised rushing rules
and the penalties for their infraction.
Friday and Saturday rushing is
being tried for the first time in hopes
that a much more informal relation
ship will develop between the Row
and rushees, enabling them to be
come better acquainted during the
short period and also perhaps elimi
nating the necessity of rule violation.

Honor Pledge Signed

Penalty for a violation of the rules
is at the discretion of the Interfra
ternity Board. But by means of the
Honor Code signed by each house
president wherein he agrees to be
responsible for the actions of hiS
house during rushing and pledges
himself to utmost obedience of the
rules, it is hoped that no infractions
will result.
The new rushing plan is as fol
lows:

RULES

1. There shall be no off-campus
rushing, except during vacations.
2. No fraternity man shall enter
Encina Hall except with the permis
sion of a sponsor, not of his own
house.
3. Contact of an informal nature
between new lower division men and
fraternity men shall be permitted.
4. No house may pledge more than
14 freshmen.
5. The above rules shall be in ef
fect during the period of fall football
practice prior to autumn quarter.
6. Violations must be reported to
the Interfraternity Board within 15
days of their occurrence.
7. No pledge shall be binding until
formal pledge day.

Rushing Periods

Fraternity houses will be open
from 11:30 a.m. to midnight during
Friday rushing, with the exception
of the Friday before the U.S.C. game
when the houses close at 8 p.m.
Houses will be open from 11:30 a.m.
to 6 p.m. during Saturday rushing.
Rushing will take place every
week-end during the quarter except
those of the U.C.L.A. game, the
Big Game and over Thanksgiving.
There will be no rushing the Satur
day of the U.S.C. game.
In January, houses shall be per
mitted to entertain new lower divi
sion men on the first week-end after
registration, viz., Friday and Satur
day. The final rushing period shall
be on the following Tuesday, Wed
nesday, and Thursday, on which
days the houses $hall be open from
11:30 a.m. to 2 p.m. and 5:30 to 8 p.m.
Formal pledging shall be Friday of
the above week and shall be con
ducted by the lawyer system.

Rushing Periods

Fraternity houses will be open
from 11:30 a.m. to midnight during
Friday rushing, with the exception
of the Friday before the U.S.C. game
when the houses close at 8 p.m.
Houses will be open from 11:30 a.m.
to 6 p.m. during Saturday rushing.
Rushing will take place every
week-end during the quarter except
those of the U.C.L.A. game, the
Big Game and over Thanksgiving.
There will be no rushing the Satur
day of the U.S.C. game.
In January, houses shall be per
mitted to entertain new lower divi
sion men on the first week-end after
registration, viz., Friday and Satur
day. The final rushing period shall
be on the following Tuesday, Wed
nesday, and Thursday, on which
days the houses $hall be open from
11:30 a.m. to 2 p.m. and 5:30 to 8 p.m.
Formal pledging shall be Friday of
the above week and shall be con
ducted by the lawyer system.
With the initial rushing period of
the 1936-37 season only two days
away, Bob Hoover, president of the
Interfraternity Council, asks that all
Ffr>sh and Row men pay particular
attention to the revised rushing rules
and the penalties for their infraction.
Friday and Saturday rushing is
being tried for the first time in hopes
that a much more informal relation
ship will develop between the Row
and rushees, enabling them to be
come better acquainted during the
short period and also perhaps elimi
nating the necessity of rule violation.

Honor Pledge Signed

Penalty for a violation of the rules
is at the discretion of the Interfra
ternity Board. But by means of the
Honor Code signed by each house
president wherein he agrees to be
responsible for the actions of hiS
house during rushing and pledges
himself to utmost obedience of the
rules, it is hoped that no infractions
will result.
