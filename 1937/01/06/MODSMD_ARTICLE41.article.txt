# Alexander Wins Football Post
## Named Senior Manager; Six Sophomores Picked 
### 
Jay Alexander was appointed sen
ior manager of the 1937 football
team by A1 Masters, general manager
of the Board of Athletic Control, at
the close of the 193G grid season. He
competed with four other students as
junior manager last season.
From the 1936 sophomore man
agers, the following were chosen for
the six junior managerships for
1937: Henry Kaiser, James Hart,
Hugh Jackson, Charles Jenney, Ar
vin Brown, and A1 Bradbury.
Plans for the enrollment of new
sophomores and freshmen for man
agers have not yet been announced.
