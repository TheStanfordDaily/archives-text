# 'Y' Workers Plan Meeting
## Reports on Progress Of Drive Scheduled 
### 
A luncheon for all women work
ing on the Y.W.C.A. finance drive
will be held in the basement of the
Women's Clubhouse promptly at 12
this noon.
Problems of soliciting money for
the drive will be discussed and each
team of jvorkers will give a complete
report of funds they have collected.
It is absolutely necessary that every
worker be present, according to Miss
Elizabeth Lam, Y.W.C.A. secretary.
Guest speaker at the luncheon will
be Dr. D. Elton Trueblood, university
chaplain.
The drive is for the purpose of
raising funds which assist in making
possible the continuance of the
Y.W.C.A. program.
The value of the groups is seen by
the statement made by Dr. Helen
Pryor, Medical Advisor for Women,
in regard to the group on modern
youth and marriage. She remarked
that this is a very good field for the
Association to sponsor as it is of in
terest to the women on campus. She
said she liked the attitude taken by
the members in relation to the gen
eral field.
Dr. Ernest R. Hilgard stated that
the yearly Asilomar .intercollegiate
conference ,is a useful supplement to
the college education; the problems
discussed being interesting and real
to the student.
