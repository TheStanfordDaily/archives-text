NYA RELEASE CARDS of under
graduate students must be signed
before 5 o'clock today in the Stu
dent Employment Office, accord
ing to Mrs. Irene Coverley, Di
rector of Student Employment.
TOYON AND BRANNER RESER
VATIONS for spring quarter must
be made today and tomorrow in
the Dean of Men's Office, with p.
deposit of $5.
DR. CHARLES HARTSHORNE, act
ing-professor of philosophy from
the University of Chicago, will lec
ture Wednesday evening in the
Little Theater on "The Relation of
Esthetics to Social Living."
ENGLISH CLUB MEETING will be
held tomorrow night at 8 o'clock
at the home of Dr. B. Q. Morgan,
with Dr. Herbert Merritt scheduled
to speak.
CONVALESCENT HOME DRIVE
COMMITTEE will meet today in
the A.S.S.U. Office at 5 o'clock.
PHILIP W. BUCK will speak on the
Stanford Radio Hour tonight at
8:30 on the significance of the
plebiscite.
WOMEN'S RIFLE TEAM meets in
rifle range below Ad Building to
night at 8 o'clock.
ORGAN RECITAL will be given at
4:15 tomorrow in the Memorial
Church by Alexander Schreiner,
organist of U.C.L.A. and of the Salt
Lake Tabernacle.
ORGAN AND CHORAL CONCERT
will be given for the American
Guild of Organists tomorrow at
8 o'clock in the Memorial Church.
ROOTERS' TICKETS for the Cal
game Friday go on Sale today at
the B.A.C. from 8:30 to 5.
DAILY STAFF MEETING, at 1:30
today in the Daily Shack; edit
board meets at 1 o'clock.
