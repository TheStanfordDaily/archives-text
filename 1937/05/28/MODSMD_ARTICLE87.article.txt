# Process Behind Imprinting of Those Sheepskins
## 
### 

UNIVERSITY PRESS EMPLOYEES check carefully
each step as they prepare diplomas for Stanford's 800-odd
graduates. At left P. A. Ware is taking a proof of a form.

lie passes the prof to Gertrude Owler, who checks care
fully for errors. Assured that there are no errors, Louis
Poulain takes the form, puts it on the press, and makes

one impression. For each diploma the form must he un
locked, changes made. Price, delivered to the student, is
$10 per copy.
