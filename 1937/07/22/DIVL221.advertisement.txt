EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, July 22
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital, by Mr. Warren D. Allen, University Or
ganist.
Friday, July 23
8:00 p.m. Dramatic Reading of Etherege's Sir Foplincj Flutter. Pre
sented under the auspices of the Summer Drama Festival.

Sunday, July 25
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Sermon by Hugh Elmer Brown, D.D.,
Minister of the First Congregational Church, Evanston,
Illinois.
7:30 p.ni. Organ Recital, by Mr. Warren D. Allen, University Or
ganist.
Monday, July 26
7:45 p.m. Early Motion Pictures. Series on "American Film Tech
nique." Sponsored by the Education Club. Room 100, Inner
Quad. Tickets, $1.00 for any three programs, on sale at the
A.S.S.U. Office, Old Union.
8:30 p.m. Stanford University Hour over KGO. Speaker: Dr. Al
fred Coester, Professor of Spanish-American Literature.
Subject: "Some South American Novels."
Tuesday, July 27
4:30 p.m. Music Hour. Speaker: Mr. Hugh C. Baker. Subject:
"Twelfth Night Music." Presented under the auspices of
the Summer Drama Festival. Women's Clubhouse. The
public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Illustrated Lecture on "Sun and Silence in Death Valley,"
by Mr. John Claire Monteith, of Los Angeles. Memorial Hall.
The public is invited.
Wednesday, July 28
4:15 p.m. Program of Recorded Music. Choir Room, Memorial
Church. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Tudor Lecture. "Elizabethan Craftsmanship" (Illustrated
with slides), by Dr. Grace McCann Morley, of tkc San Fran
cisco Museum of Art. Under the auspices of the Summer
Drama Festival. Women's Clubhouse. The public is invited.
COMING EVENTS
Thursday, July 29
8:00 p.m. Lecture on "The Bach Brandenburg Concertos," by Mr.
Alfred Frankenstein, Music Editor of The San Francisco
Chronicle. Illustrated with phonograph records.
Monday, August 2
7:45 p.m. Early Motion Pictures. Series on "Early Spectaclc."
Tuesday, August 3
8:00 p.m. First Concert of Bach Brandenburg Concertos. Under
the musical direction of Mr. Fcri Roth. Gift of the Coolidge
Foundation of the Library of Congress, Washington, D.C.
UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES
Exhibits: Stanford University Library. Exhibit on Tudor life and times.
Hoover War Library. Child Health and Protection. Materials
drawn from the Ray Lyman Wilbur Collection on Social Problems.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Exhibits: Travel posters from Europe; Art exhibit of Javanese batiks.
Art work by Viennese School Children. (The Art Gallery is open
to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. daily,
including Sunday.)
