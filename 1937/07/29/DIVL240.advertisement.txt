Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. LXXXII, No. 7 July 29-August 4, 1937
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, July 29
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital, by Mr. Warren U. Allen, University Or
ganist.
8:00 p.m. Lecture on "The Bach Brandenburg Concertos," by Mr.
Alfred Frankenstein, Music Editor of The San Francisco
Chronicle. Illustrated by phonograph records. Little Theatre,
Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
Saturday, July 31
8 :00 p.m. Dramatic Reading of George Etherege's The Man of Mode
or Sir Foiling Flutter and Lope de Rueda's The Olives. Pre
sented under the auspices of the Summer Drama Festival,
Room 100, Inner Quad. Admission, 10c.
Sunday, August 1
11 :00 a.m. Church Service. Sermon by Dr. D. Elton Trueblood, Chap
lain of the University.
7 :30 p.m. Organ Recital, by Mr. Raymond Kendall, Assistant Univer
sity Organist.
Monday, August 2
7:45 p.m. Early Motion Pictures. Series on "Early Spectacle." Spon
sored by the Education Club. Room 100, Inner Quad. Tickets,
$1.00 for any three programs, on sale at the A.S.S.U. Office,
Old Union.
8:30 p.m. Stanford University Hour over KGO. Speaker: Dr.
Walter Edwards Beach. Acting Assistant Professor of Eco
nomics. Subject: "Regulatory Activities of the Securities
Exchange Commission."
Tuesday, August 3
7:50 p.m. First Concert of Bach Brandenburg Concertos. Under
the musical direction of Mr. Feri Roth. Gift of the Elizabeth
Sprague Coolidge Foundation of the Library of Congress,
Washington, D.C. Memorial Hall. Open to the public.
Wednesday, August 4
4:15 p.m. Lecture on "The Problem of Population and Emigration in
Italy," by Commendatore Luigi Villari. Little Theatre, Me
morial Hall. The public is invited.
4:15 p.m. Program of Recorded Music. Choir Room, Memorial
Church. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Tudor Lecture. "Illyria and the Law," by Dr. Max Radin,
of the University of California. Under the auspices of the
Summer Drama Festival. Women's Clubhouse. The public is
invited.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
Sunday, August 1, to Tuesday, August 10, inclusive
Japanese-American Student Conference.
COMING EVENTS
Thursday, August 5
8:00 p.m. Lecture on "The Making of Law," by Dr. Morris R. Cohen,
Acting Professor of Philosophy.
Friday, August 6
3:20 p.m. Second Concert of Bach Brandenburg Concertos. Under
the musical direction of Mr. Feri Roth. Gift of the Elizabeth
Sprague Coolidge Foundation of the Library of Congress,
Washington, D.C.
UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES
Exhibits: Stanford University Library. Exhibit on Tudor life and times.
Hoover War Library. Child Health and Protection. Materials
drawn from the Ray Lyman Wilbur Collection on Social Problems.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Exhibits: Travel posters from Europe; Art exhibit of Javanese batiks.
Art work by Viennese School Children. (The Art Gallery is open
to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. daily,
including Sunday.)
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, in writing, should be in the
hands of the Secretary to the Committee on Public Exercises, Room 173, Ad
ministration Building, not later than 9:00 a.m.. Tuesday. Calendar subscrip
tions at $1 plus tax, 3 cents, for the year or 25c pjus tax, lc, for the quarter
may be sent to the Secretary.
