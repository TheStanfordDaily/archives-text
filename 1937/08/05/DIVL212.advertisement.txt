Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. LXXXII, No. 8 August 5-11, 1937
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, August 5
4:15 p.m. Lecture on "The Classical String Quartet," by Mr. Warren
D. Allen, Associate Professor of Music and Education. Illus
trated with records of the works by Haydn, Mozart, and
Beethoven which will be played later by the Kolisch Quartet.
Choir Room, Memorial Church. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Lecture on "The Making of Law," by Dr. Morris R. Cohen,
Acting Professor of Philosophy. Little Theatre, Memorial
Hall. The public is invited.
Friday, August 6
3:20 p.m. Second Concert of Bach Brandenburg Concertos. Under
the musical direction of Mr. Feri Roth. Gift of the Elizabeth
Sprague Coolidge Foundation of the Library of Congress,
Washington, D.C. Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
Saturday, August 7
8:30 p.m. Dramatic Reading of the Maxwell Anderson Prize Play of
1937, Souvenir de la Malmaison, by Dorothy Dow. Under the
auspices of the Summer Drama Festival. Memorial Hall.
Admission, 50c.
Sunday, August 8
11 :00 a.m. Church Service. Sermon by Rabbi Jacob Kohn, of the Jewish
Chautauqua Society.
7:30 p.m. Organ Recital, by Mr. Raymond Kendall, Assistant Univer
sity Organist.
Monday, August 9
7:45 p.m. Early Motion Pictures. Series on "Sound Films." Spon
sored by the Education Club. Memorial Hall. Tickets, $1.00
for any three programs, on sale at the A.S.S.U. Office, Old
Union.
8:30 p.m. Stanford University Hour over KGO. Speaker: Dr.
Donald Peery Cottrell, Acting Associate Professor of Edu
cation. Subject: "Redirecting General Education in High
School and College."
Tuesday, August 10
8:00 p.m. Lecture on "Enthusiasms of 1898," by Dr. J. Fred Rippy,
Professor of History, University of Chicago. Little Theatre,
Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
Wednesday, August 11
4:15 p.m. Program of Recorded Music. Choir Room, Memorial
Church. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. French Play. La Dame de Bronze et le Monsieur de Cristal,
by Henri Duvernois. Presented under the auspices of Pi Delta
Phi. Little Theatre, Memorial Hall. Admission, 10c.
8:00 p.m. Tudor Lecture. "The Queen's English," by Dr. William
Wells, of the University of North Carolina. Under the aus
pices of the Summer Drama Festival. Women's Clubhouse.
The public is invited.
COMING EVENTS
Thursday, August 12
8:00 p.m. Illustrated Lecture on "Salzburg and Its Festivals," by Dr.
Robert Franz Arnold, Acting Professor of German.
Friday, August 13
3:30 p.m. Concert by the Kolisch Quartet. Gift of Mrs. Elizabeth
Sprague Coolidge. Memorial Hall.
Saturday, August 14
8:00 p.m. Dramatic Reading of Maxwell Anderson's The Buccaneer.
Presented under the auspices of Sword and Sandals.
UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES
Exhibits: Stanford University Library. Exhibit on Tudor life and times.
Hoover War Library. Child Health and Protection. Materials
drawn from the Ray Lyman Wilbur Collection on Social Problems.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Exhibits: Travel posters from Europe. Art work by Viennese School Chil
dren. (The Art Gallery is open to the public without charge from
10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. daily, including Sunday.)
