Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. LXXXII, No. 9 August 12-18, 1937
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, August 12
4:15 p.m. Lecture on "The Classical String Quartet," by Mr. Warren
D. Allen, Associate Professor of Music and Education. Illus
trated with records from the works which will be played later
by the Kolisch Quartet. (Postponed from last week.) Choir
Room, Memorial Church. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Illustrated Lecture on "Salzburg and Its Festivals," by Dr.
Robert Franz Arnold, Acting Professor of German. Little
Theatre, Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
Friday, August 13
3:30 p.m. Concert by the Kolisch Quartet, of Vienna. Gift of Mrs.
Elizabeth Sprague Coolidge. Memorial Hall. The public is
invited.
Saturday, August 14
8:00 p.m. Dramatic Reading of Maxwell Anderson's The Buccaneer.
Presented under the auspices of Sword and Sandals. Little
Theatre, Memorial Hall.
Sunday, August 15
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Sermon by Dr. D. Elton Trueblood, Chap
lain of the University.
7:30 p.m. Organ Recital, by Mr. Warren D. Allen, University Organist.
Monday, August 16
8:00 p.m. Lecture on "The Untried Domain," by Mr. Lawson P. Cooper,
Los Angeles art critic. Lecture Room, Art Gallery. The pub
lic is invited.
8:30 p.m. Stanford University Hour over KGO. Speaker: Mr.
Leland Taylor Chapin, Assistant Professor of English (Public
Speaking). Subject: "Debating at the University of Edin
burgh."
Tuesday, August 17
3:30 p.m. Concert by the Kolisch Quartet, of Vienna. Gift of Mrs.
Elizabeth Sprague Coolidge. Memorial Hall. The public is
invited.
Wednesday, August 18
4:15 p.m. Program of Recorded Music. Choir Room, Memorial
Church. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Tudor Lecture. "Master Viola," by Mr. James Sandoe, of
the University of Colorado. Under the auspices of the Sum
mer Drama Festival. Women's Clubhouse. The public is in
vited.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
Friday, August 13
2:00 p.m. Oral examination for the Doctorate. Carroll M. Haeske (Ma
jor: English; Minor: German). Room 308, Library.
Monday, August 16
2:00 p.m. Oral examination for the Doctorate. Wendell Magee Keck
(Major: English; Minor: Distributed). Room 308, Library.
Tuesday, August 17
2:00 p.m. Oral examination for the Doctorate. Barlel E. Ebel (Major:
German; Minor: Distributed). Room 308, Library.
Wednesday, August 18
2:00 p.m. Oral examination for the Doctorate. Frank L. Fenton (Ma
jor: English; Minor: History). Room 308, Library.
COMING EVENTS
Thursday, August 19
8:15 p.m. Dramatic Reading of Robert Sherwood's Reunion in Vienna.
Under the auspices of the Summer Drama Festival.
Friday, August 20
1:30 p.m. Three Experimental Plays. Mr. Gordon Lange, Super
vising Director.
3:30 p.m. Concert by the Kolisch Quartet, of Vienna. Gift of Mrs.
Elizabeth Sprague Coolidge.
8:15 p.m. Shakespeare's Twelfth Night. Presented by the Summer
Drama Festival, under the direction of Mr. Hubert Heffner.
Saturday, August 21
2:00 p.m. Three Experimental Plays. (Second performance.) Mr.
Gordon Lange, Supervising Director.
4:00 p.m. Sword and Morris Dances. Presented by the class in
folk dance, directed by Miss Marie Manchee.
8:15 p.m. Shakespeare's Twelfth Night. (Second performance.)
Presented by the Summer Drama Festival, under the direction
of Mr. Hubert Heffner.
UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES
Exhibits: Stanford University Library. Exhibit on Tudor life and times.
Hoover War Library. The theatre in the war. Programs from
prison camp and army theatres in the World War.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
I Exhibits: Travel posters from Europe. Art work by Viennese School Chil
dren. (The Art Gallery is open to the public without charge from
10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. daily, including Sunday.)
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Announcements for the Weekly Calendar, in writing, should be in the
hands of the Secretary to the Committee on Public Exercises, Room 173, Ad
ministration Building," not later than 9:00 a.m., Tuesday. Calendar subscrip
tions at $1 plus tax, 3 cents, for the year or 25c plus tax, lc, for the quarter
may be sent to the Secretary.
