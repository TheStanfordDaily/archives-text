# Martin To Open Luncheon Club
## Foreign Tour Is Basis Of Lecture Today 
### 
Luncheon Club activities will start
tomomjw at noon when Percy A.
Martin, Professor of History, delivers
an "after dinner" address on "Amer
ca Revisited" in Women's Clubhouse.
Professor Martin, just returned
from an extensive trip throughout
South and Central America, will
speak on some of the conclusions
reached as a result of his tour.
Luncheon Club, which has no set
membership and is open to students
and faculty alike, will hold a regu
lar meeting each Wednesday noon
in Women's Clubhouse, where a 25-
cent luncheon will be served. Speak
ers are to be featured. Luncheon
Club ends promptly at 12:55.
