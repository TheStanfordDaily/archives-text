# Labor Conference Makes Progress
## 
### 
WASHINGTON, Nov. 8 TUP). —
The conference of committees from
the American Federation of Labor
and the Committee for Industrial
Organization late today adjourned
until 10 a.m. tomorrow after dis
cussing addition of subcommittees
to facilitate study of the problems
facing them.
It was indicated—despite a report
by George M. Harrison, chairman of
the A. F. of L. committee that prog
ress had been made—that no con
crete advance toward final settle
ment as yet is in sight.
