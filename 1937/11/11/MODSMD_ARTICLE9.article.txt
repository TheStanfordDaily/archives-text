# Miller Speaks To Journalism Groups Today
## Rhodes Scholar, Editor Of Monitor Will Talk At Union Luncheon 
### 
Lewis Rex Miller, Rhodes scholar
and present member of the Editorial
Board of the Christian Science Mon
itor, will speak today at the Sigma
Delta Chi-Theta Sigma Phi lunch
eon in the Union alcove.
The luncheon, second in the bi
weekly series sponsored by the two
journalistic fraternities, is open to
the public. Tickets may be obtained
from Mrs. Ruth Douglass in Room 1
of the Press Building for 50 cents.
Managing Editor
Lewis Miller, A.B. and MA., from
Oxford, and Assistant Professor of
History at Harvard for six years,
is now managing editor of the Pa
cific Coast News Bureau of the
Christian Science Monitor.
Miller was sent to Paris and Ge
neva as correspondent for the Moni
tor in 1932. Previously he was a
member of the trade-treaty commis
sion in Australia under Secretary
of Commerce Herbert Hoover.
Founded Magazine Section
He also founded the Weekly Mag
azine Section of the Monitor and
edited it before coming to the Pa
cific Coast a year ago. His lectures
on "The World from the Press Box"
have been heard at the Century
Club in San Francisco.
The Monitor has its biggest circu
lation outside New England in Cali
fornia. Centered in San Francisc,
the Pacific Coast Monitor covers
the region from Canada to Mexico.
