# Farm Mothers To Meet Today
## Killefer To Preside; Will Present Leaders 
### 
Tom Killefer, A.S.S.U. president,
will preside at the annual student
meeting of the Mothers' Club, to be
held" today at 2 o'clock in the Wo
men's Clubhouse.
Killefer will tell of the activities
of the A.SS.U. and will introduce
other student leaders who will talk
on their fields. Jean Hemphill, out
going chairman of Women's Coun
cil, Gordy Flost, former Daily edi
tor, and Grant Stone, captain of the
football team, will speak.
The combined Men's and Women's
Glee Clubs will present a musical
program, consisting of "Hospidi
Palmilui," "The Echo Song," "Lo,
How a Rose," and "Stanford in
Spring."
Chi Omega mothers will be host
esses at a tea for members immedi
ately following the meeting, which
is also open to students.
A no-host luncheon in the Union
Alcove at 12:15 o'clock will precede
the meeting.
