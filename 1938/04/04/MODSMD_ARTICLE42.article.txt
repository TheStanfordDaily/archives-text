# U.S.C. Team Takes Coast Net Laurels
## Trojan Tennists Defeat Stanford, 9-2; Cal, 7-4 
### 
Pacific Coast Conference net
laurels went to the University of
Southern California for the second
time in as many years over the
past week-end and when a strong
Trojan team swept through Stan
lord, 9-2, on the Farm courts and
then remained unbeaten by taking
Cal, 7-4.
The Card netmen will play an in
tersectional match against the tour
ing University of Miami aggrega
tion on the band house courts to
morrow afternoon in preparation
for their match with Cal here on
Saturday.
While the varsity went down to
its third straight defeat, the year
lings came out with another victory
on Friday,- beating Salinas, 8-1.
Singles: Hunt (USC) d. Braly,
(5-2, 6-1; Wetherell (USC) d. Fol
som, 6-1, 6-1; Leavens (USC) d. Un
derwood, 6-3, 6-4; Brock (S) d.
Buese, 6-1, 6-1; Everfctt (USC) d.
Seaver, 4-6, 6-3, 6-4; Bartell (USC)
d. Gates, 6-3, 8-6.
Doubles: Hunt-Wetherell (USC)
d. Underwood-Brock, 6-2, 2-6, 7-5;
Buese-Leavens (USC) d. Folsom-
Seaver, 6-4, 8-6; Braly-Stoefen (S)
d. Nelson-Bartell, 6-4, 6-2.
