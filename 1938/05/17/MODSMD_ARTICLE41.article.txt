# Gerinans Sweep Libyan Auto Races
## 
### 
TRIPOLI, Libya, May 16 (UP)—
German drivers swept the first three
places Sunday in the sixth Tripoli
grand prix automobile race—as one
driver was killed and two others in
jured. While a crowd of 20.000
watched, Herman Lung drove the
325.404-mile course in two hours 33
minutes 17 seconds to win top prize
of $13,125. Manfred Braushitsch was
second and Rudy Caracciola third.
Eugenio Siena of Italy was killed
when his Alfa Romeo turned over
on a curve.
