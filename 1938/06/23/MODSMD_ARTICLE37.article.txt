# Education Club To Hold Luncheon
## 
### 
The first of a series of six lunch
eons will be held by the Education
Club on Thursday, June 30, in the
Union. The Education Club, a social
organization open to students in the
School of Education, sponsors par
ties, ball games, hikes, and other
activities during the summer quar
ter.
The charge for the luncheons is
sixty cents, but tickets for the entire
series may be purchased for three
dollars. Talks by prominent Ameri
can educators will be featured at
these noon-time meetings.

Socrates was a student of the gui
