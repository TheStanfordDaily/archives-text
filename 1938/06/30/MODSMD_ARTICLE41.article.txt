# Novelist Takes Princeton U. Post
## 
### 
Thomas Mann, distinguished Ger
man novelist and winner of the
Nobel Prize in literature in 1929, has
been appointed lecturer in the hu.-
manities at Princeton for the coming
year, according to an announcement
in the Daily Princetonian.
The German voluntary exile will
take up residence at Princeton next
fall and will deliver occasional public
lectures in the general field of the
humanities. In the second term, in
addition to his lecture work he will
conduct two of the two-hour semi
monthly preceptorial conferences in
the newly-established course deal
ing with the masterpieces of all for
eign literatures.
