Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. LXXXVI, No. 6 July 28-August 3, 1938
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, July 28
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital. Raymond Kendall, Assistant University
Organist. Memorial Church. The public is invited.
4:30 p.m. Lecture. "Deux grands ports: Bruges et Anvers," Suzanne
Charlier-Tassier, Agrege a l'Universitc de Bruxelles, chargee
d'un cours libre a la Faculte dc Philosophic et Lettres. French
House, 549 Lasuen. Open to students and members of the
faculty.
8:00 p.m. Dramatists Program IV. Lecture, "Poetic Drama When
Maxwell Anderson Was Born," Henry David Gray, Profes
sor of English. Women's Clubhouse. The public is invited.
Friday, July 29
8:00 p.m. Dramatic Reading. The Animal Kingdom, by Philip Barry.
Presented under the auspices of Stagers. Little Theater, Me
morial Hall. Admission, 15 cents.
Sunday, July 31
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: D. Elton Trueblood, Chaplain
of the University.
7:30 p.m. Organ Recital. Raymond Kendall, Assistant University
Organist. Memorial Church. The public is invited.
Monday, August 1
8:15 p.m. Roth Quartet. Assisted by Ernst Toch, composer-pianist;
Frank Fragale, clarinetist; Romaine Verney, violist; Stephen
Deak, 'cellist. Final concert in Series A. Presented under the
auspices of The Friends of Music. Auditorium, Memorial
Hall. Complimentary tickets for students and faculty may be
obtained at the Summer Quarter Office, Room 173, Adminis
tration Building. Single admissions, $1.25, may be obtained
at the Vestry, Memorial Church, at Keeble's, Palo Alto, and
at the door.
9:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: Florence
Laura Goodenough, Acting Professor of Psychology. Subject:
"What Do You Want?"
Tuesday, August 2
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Mozart Program by the Roth
Quartet and Warren D. Allen, University Organist. Memorial
Church. The public is invited.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
Thursday, July 28
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Charles H. Horning for the degree of
Doctor of Philosophy. Major, Chemistry; Minor, Mathe
matics. Room 308, Library.
4:15 p.m. The University Philological Association in the Women's
Clubhouse. Speakers: George R. Coffman, Acting Professor
of English. Subject: "Some Recent Trends in English Lit
erary Scholarships with Special Reference to the Middle
Ages"; Henry W. Prescott, Acting Professor of Classics.
Subject: "Artifice in Literary Art."
Monday, August 1 to Friday, August 5
Annual Western Conference for Commercial and Trade
Executives. Under the auspices of the United States Chamber
of Commerce. (For details see Conference Program.)
UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES
Exhibits: Stanford University Library and Hoover War Library. Gifts
and insignia presented to Mr. Hoover in recognition of his leader
ship in American relief and reconstruction in Europe.
Charlotte Ashley Felton Memorial Library. Recent accessions.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Exhibits. Drawings and pottery made by Indians of the Southwest. (The
Art Gallery is open to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m.
to 5:00 p.m. daily, including Sunday.)
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Printed for the University each Wednesday by the Stanford University
Press. Notices to be included should be given in writing to Miss Mildred Wors
wick, Secretary to the Committee on Public Exercises, 173 Administration
Building, by 9:00 a.m. on Tuesday preceding the week in which the event will
occur. Mailing charge: $1.00 for the academic year or 25 cents for the quar
ter. Subscriptions may be sent to the Secretary.
