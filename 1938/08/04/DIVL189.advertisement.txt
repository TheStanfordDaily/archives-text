EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, August 4
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist.
Memorial Church. The public is invited.
8:15 p.m. Uncle Tom's Cabin, by Harriet Beecher Stowe (original
George Aiken version). Presented by the Division of Speech
and Drama under the direction of H. Miles Heberer. Audi
torium, Memorial Hall. Reserved seats, $1.00, 75 cents, and
50 cents on sale, Room 202, Memorial Hall (P.A. 22041
and P.A. 9411, Local 591), or at the door.
Friday, August 5
8:15 p.m. Uncle Tom':} Cabin, by Harriet Beecher Stowe. (Second
performance.)
Saturday, August 6
8:15 p.m. Uncle Tom's Cabin, by Harriet Beecher Stowe. (Third and
final performance.)
Sunday, August 7
11 :00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher, Clarence Reidenbach, Ph.D., of
the First Congregational Church, Oakland.
7:30 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist.
Memorial Church. The public is invited.
Monday, August 8
8:00 p.m. Lecture. "The Bible as Living Literature," Rabbi Joshua L.
Liebman of the Atishe Maariv Synagogue, Chicago, Illinois.
Little Theater. The public is invited.
9:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: A. James
t Casner, Acting Associate Professor of Law. Subject: "Should
I Make a Will ?"
Tuesday, August 9
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Mozart Serenades under the di
rection of Feri Roth with Philip Martin, horn soloist. Stan
ford Union Court. The public is invited.
Wednesday, August 10
4:15 p.m. Roth Quartet. Assisted by Marcel Grandjany, harpist;
Frank Fragale, clarinetist; Carleton Sprague Smith, flutist.
Presented through the courtesy of Elizabeth Sprague Coolidge
and The Friends of Music. Auditorium, Memorial Hall. The
public is invited.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
Thursday, August 4
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Edwin R. Coulson for the degree of
Doctor of Philosophy. Major, English; Minor, History.
Room 308, Library.
Friday, August 5
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Roy DeVerl Willey for the degree of
Doctor of Philosophy. Major, Education; Minor, Psychology.
Room 308, Library.
Monday, August 8 to Thursday, August 11
Research Conference in Photochemistry under the auspices of
the Department of Chemistry. (For details see Conference
Program.)
j Monday, August 8
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Leo Cain for the degree of Doctor of
Philosophy. Major, Education; Minor, Psychology. Room
308, Library.
i Tuesday, August 9
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of William Herbert Swift for the de
gree of Doctor of Philosophy. Major, Education; Minor,
Psychology. Room 308, Library.
UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES
Exhibits: Stanford University Library and Hoover War Library. Gifts
and insignia presented to Mr. Hoover in recognition of his leader
ship in American relief and reconstruction in Europe.
Charlotte Ashley Felton Memorial Library. Recent accessions.
|
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
; Exhibits. Drawings and pottery made by Indians of the Southwest. (The
Art Gallery is open "to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m.
to 5:00 p.m. daily, including Sunday.)
