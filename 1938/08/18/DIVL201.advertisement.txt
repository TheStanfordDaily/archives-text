EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, August 18
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist.
Memorial Church. The public is invited.

Tassier, Agrege a l'Universite de Bruxelles, chargee d'un

faculty.

sion of Speech and Drama under the direction of Gordon C.
Lange. Auditorium, Memorial Hall. Reserved seats, $1.00,
75 cents, and 50 cents on sale, Room 202, Memorial Hall
(PA 22041 and PA 9411, Local 591), or at the door.
Friday, August 19
8:15 p.m. Gringo's Gamble, by Felton Elkins. (Second performance)
Saturday, August 20
8:15 p.m. Gringo's Gamble, by Felton Elkins. (Third performance)
Sunday, August 21
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: Augustus Taber Murray, Emeri
tus Professor of Classical Literature.
7:30 p.m. Organ Recital. Raymond Kendall, Assistant University Or
ganist. Memorial Church. The public is invited.
Monday, August 22
9:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: Elmore
Petersen, Acting Professor of Marketing. Subject: "Progress
in Public Planning."
Tuesday, August 23
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Motion picture, "La Kermesse
Heroique" (Carnival in Flanders), titles in English. Audi
torium, Memorial Hall. The public is invited.

Thursday, August 18
10:30 a.m. Meeting of the University Committee of the Board of
Trustees.
11 :30 a.m. Meeting of the Finance Committee of the Board of Trustees.
1:45 p.m. Meeting of the Board of Trustees.
UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES
Exhibits: Stanford University Library and Hoover War Library. Gifts
and insignia presented to Mr. Hoover in recognition of his leader
ship in American relief and reconstruction in Europe.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Exhibits. Drawings and pottery made by Indians of the Southwest. (The
Art Gallery is open to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m.

ter. Subscriptions may be sent to the Secretary.
