Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. LXXXVI, No. 10 August 25-September 3, 1938
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, August 25
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist. Me
morial Church. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Play Reading. Last Illusion, by Arthur McCullough Samp
ley. Presented by the Dramatists' Assembly and read by
members of the Community Theaters and the Assembly.
Little Theater, Memorial Hall. Admission, 25 cents.
Friday, August 26
8:00 p.m. Lectures. "A Characteristic of American Tragedy," Sophus
Keith Winther, Associate Professor of English, University of
Washington; "The Ten Best Plays in the Maxwell Anderson
Award Contest of 1938," Margery Bailey, Associate Professor
of English. Under the auspices of the Dramatists' Assembly.
Little Theater, Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
Saturday, August 27
2:30 p.m. Lectures. "The Dangers of Libretto," Adolph Frederick
Hansen; "Radio Plays," Samuel Dickson of the National
Broadcasting Company; "A Theme Taking Shape"—Eugene
O'Neill's Notes on the Composition of Mourning Bccotnts
Electro, Raymond Havens Rhodes. Under the auspices of the
Dramatists' Assembly. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. The
public is invited.
8:30 p.m. Play Reading. John Brown, by Kirke Mechem, winner of
the Verse Drama Contest award established in honor of Max
well Anderson. Presented by the Dramatists' Assembly and
read by members of the Community Theaters and the As
sembly. Auditorium, Memorial Hall. Admission, 35 cents.
Sunday, August 28
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: D. Elton Trueblood, Chaplain
of the University.
7 :30 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist. Me
morial Church. The public is invited.
Monday, August 29
9:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: Jeannette
M. Hitchcock, Senior Bibliographer and Keeper of Rare
Books. Subject: "Rare Books in the Stanford Library."
END-QUARTER EXAMINATIONS
Thursday, September 1, to Saturday, September 3
ANNOUNCEMENTS
Thursday, August 25
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Stanley F. Cahen for the degree of
Doctor of Philosophy. Major, School of Biological Sciences.
Room 308, Library.
Friday, August 26
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of John C. McCloskey for the degree of
Doctor of Philosophy. Major, English; Minor, French.
Room 308, Library.
UNIVERSITY LIBRARIES
Exhibits: Stanford University Library and Hoover War Library. Gifts
and insignia presented to Mr. Hoover in recognition of his leader
ship in American relief and reconstruction in Europe.
THOMAS WELTON STANFORD ART GALLERY
Exhibits. Drawings and pottery made by Indians of the Southwest. (The
Art Gallery is open to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m.
to 5:00 p.m. daily, including Sunday.)
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Printed for the University each Wednesday by the Stanford University
Press. Notices to be included should be given in writing to Miss Mildred Wors
wick, Secretary to the Committee on Public Exercises, 173 Administration
Building, by 9:00 a.m. on Tuesday preceding the week in which the event will
occur. Mailing charge: $1.00 for the academic year or 25 cents for the quar
ter. Subscriptions may be sent to the Secretary.
