# Farm Netmen Wield Rackets On East Coast
##

Bob Underwood and Jim Seaver
of the Stanford varsity tennis team
competed with varied success in
three tournaments this summer.
Representing Stanford at Haver
ford, Pa., at the National Intercol
legiate championships held at Mer
ion Cricket Club, Underwood was
eliminated in the fourth round
after leaving in his wake Aston
Beadles of the University of North
Carolina and Bradley Kendis of
U.C.L.A. His conqueror was Uni
versity of California's Robert Har
mon, 6-3, 6-3.
Seaver defeated only his first op
ponent, Dick Henry of University
of Virginia, 6-1, 6-1, and lost to
William Ralbert of the University of
Cincinnati, 6-0, 6-0.
TAKE CHAMPS
The pair did much better at Mont
clair, N.J. In the semi-finals of the
New Jersey Men's Doubles cham
pionship they walloped the newly
crowned eastern intercollegiate
doubles champs, Gordon Giles and
Richard Bennion of the University
of Utah, 6-3, 6-3. They then won
the title by defeating the Univer
sity of California duo of Bob Pea
cock and Doug Imhoff, 6-4, 11-9,
7-5.
Bob Low. frosh ace of last year,
played in the most prominent east
ern tournaments. At Southampton,
N.Y., competing in the 48th Annual
Meadow Club invitational tourna
ment, he defeated William Robert
son of Pasadena, 6-1, 6-1. He went
down in the third round to Bitsy
Grant of Atlanta, 6-4, 6-2.
Low then went to Rye for the
eastern grass court championships
at Westchester Country Club and

there again met his nemesis in
America's fourth ranking star, By
ron Grant, losing by 0-3, 0-3.
He teamed in doubles with Harris
Everett of Jacksonville, Fla., and
met defeat at the hands of Davis
Cupper, Bobbie Riggs and Bitsy
Grant, 6-3, 3-6. 6-2.
Don Budge was Low's next op
ponent. He lost to the world's ama
teur champion in the Newport Ca
sino invitational at Newport, R.1.,
and lost, 6-2, 6-2.
