# Nominations Set Tomorrow
## Special Election To Fill Four Posts 
### 
Nominations for a special elec
tion, to be held Oct. 18, will be taken
tomorrow and Wednesday at the Law
Steps, announces Election Chairman
Dave Stephenson. Hours are from
8 to 6 o'clock.
Positions to be filled include three
seats on Men's Council and the
office of secrctary-treasurer of the
junior class.
Two short - term Men's Council
positions were left vacant when Ed
Kerr and Bob Underwood failed to
register for the current quarter. The
term of those elected lasts until the
February elections, according to Ste
phenson.
The other Men's Council post, to
be filled for the entire year, was left
vacant by George Farrell. Nomi
nees for any of these council posts
must have 120 units according to the
A.S.S.U. constitution.
The position of secretary - treas
urer of the junior class is vacant
because Barbara Rogers, elected last
year, did not have sufficient units of
credit. The constitution pi-ovides
that nominees for this post must
have between 75 and 119 units.
Declinations of nomination will be
received all day Thursday and Fri
day forenoon at the A.S.S.U. office.
Besides Stephenson, members of
election board are Nancy Caswell,
Nancy Hurst, Betty Nichols, Martha
Benson, Diana Hunter, Betty Board
man. Bill Lockie, Temple Wanna
maker, Pete Whittlesey, Don Soltero,
and Bill Kimball.
