# Elections To Be Held Tomorrow
## Student Body Cards Needed for Voting In Special Contest 
### 
The special election for the posi
tions of the secretary-treasurer of
the junior class, for the two vacant
seats on the short-term Men's Coun
cil, and for the one seat on the long
term will be held tomorrow.
The polls will be found both at
the Law Steps and the Engineering
Corner, and will be open from 8:30
until 2 o'clock. All students must
present their student body cards to
be eligible to vote.
All registered men students may
vote for the Men's Council posts,
while all students of junior standing
may vote for the secretary-treasurer
of the junior class. Candidates for
this position are as follows: Virginia
Valentine, Jean Tillotson, Betty
Kline, Olive Freeman, and Sally
Holt. This .position was left vacant
by Barbara Rogers, who did not ful
fill the crcdit requirement of the
office.

SHORT-TERM NOMINEES

Nominees for the short-term Men's
Council office are Walt Fitch, Dar
eld Vestal, William Wilson, John
Porter, George Egan, James Stone,
John Brahtz, and Bill Wohle. This
election was made necessary when
two former councilmen failed to re
turn to school this quarter. Ed
Kerr and Bob Underwood previously
served a half a year. There remain
five months of their terms to be
filled.
Bill Kirsch, James Barnum, Sher
wood Armstrong, Andy Shirey, and
Dick Lyon were named to run for
the full-term position. The full-term
vacancy was left open by George
Farrell when he also failed to re
turn to school.

UNIT REQUIREMENTS

The A.S.S.U. constitution requires
that all school office holders have a
specified number of units. The
secretary-treasurer position requires
between 75 and 119 units, while
Council members must have at least
120 units.
The election will be handled by
Dave Stephenson, head of the Elec
tion Board. Other members are
Nancy Caswell, Betty Nichols, Mar
tha Benson, Nancy Hurst, Diana
Hunter, Betty Boardman, Bill
Leckie, Temple Wanamaker, Pete
Whittlesey, Don Soltcro, and Bill
Kimball.

The special election for the posi
tions of the secretary-treasurer of
the junior class, for the two vacant
seats on the short-term Men's Coun
cil, and for the one seat on the long
term will be held tomorrow.
The polls will be found both at
the Law Steps and the Engineering
Corner, and will be open from 8:30
until 2 o'clock. All students must
present their student body cards to
be eligible to vote.
All registered men students may
vote for the Men's Council posts,
while all students of junior standing
may vote for the secretary-treasurer
of the junior class. Candidates for
this position are as follows: Virginia
Valentine, Jean Tillotson, Betty
Kline, Olive Freeman, and Sally
Holt. This .position was left vacant
by Barbara Rogers, who did not ful
fill the crcdit requirement of the
office.

SHORT-TERM NOMINEES

Nominees for the short-term Men's
Council office are Walt Fitch, Dar
eld Vestal, William Wilson, John
Porter, George Egan, James Stone,
John Brahtz, and Bill Wohle. This
election was made necessary when
two former councilmen failed to re
turn to school this quarter. Ed
Kerr and Bob Underwood previously
served a half a year. There remain
five months of their terms to be
filled.
Bill Kirsch, James Barnum, Sher
wood Armstrong, Andy Shirey, and
Dick Lyon were named to run for
the full-term position. The full-term
vacancy was left open by George
Farrell when he also failed to re
turn to school.

UNIT REQUIREMENTS

The A.S.S.U. constitution requires
that all school office holders have a
specified number of units. The
secretary-treasurer position requires
between 75 and 119 units, while
Council members must have at least
120 units.
The election will be handled by
Dave Stephenson, head of the Elec
tion Board. Other members are
Nancy Caswell, Betty Nichols, Mar
tha Benson, Nancy Hurst, Diana
Hunter, Betty Boardman, Bill
Leckie, Temple Wanamaker, Pete
Whittlesey, Don Soltcro, and Bill
Kimball.
