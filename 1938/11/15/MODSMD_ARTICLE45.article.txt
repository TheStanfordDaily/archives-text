# Fall Benefit Will Feature Drama Hits
## 
### 
Beatrice Edmonds noted play re
viewer, will recreate scenes from
current New York dramatic hits
Wednesday when she appears at the
annual fall benefit sponsored by the
Stanford Mothers' Club.
Music by the Stanford Men's and
Women's Glee Clubs will form a
background for Miss Edmond's per
formance.
Special highlight, of the program,
which begins at 2:30 o'clock in Me
morial Hall, will be the reading of
Thornton Wilder's dramatic success,
"Our Town." The current New York
season offers Robert Sherwood's
"Abe Lincoln of Illinois," which Miss
Edmonds will include in her pro
gram.
An interesting feature of the pro
gram will be Miss Edmonds' inter
pretations of Helen Hayes' famous
characterizations of Queen Victoria
and Shakespeare's Portia, and of
Eva LeGalliene's portrayal of Mad
ame Capet.
"Knickerbocker Holiday," a mu
sical show starring Walter Huston
and two Guthrie McClintic produc
tions, "Missouri Legend" and "Herod
and Marianne," will also be read by
Beatrice Edmonds, who has been
compared to Ruth Draper and Cor
nelia Otis Skinner in her interpre
tation of dramatic roles.
The program follows a luncheon,
which will be held from 12:15 to 2
o'clock. Proceeds from the benefit
will go toward the fulfillment of the
Mothers' Club budget, which main
tains the rest homes, sponsors 20
scholarships, and provides for stu
dent emergency loans.
