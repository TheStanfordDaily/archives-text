# Farm to Hear Peasant Music
## Mills Duo to Play On Tuesday Lecture 
### 
A lecture-recital of Rumanian and
Hungarian folk music will be pre
sented by Elizabeth and Harlow
Mills, violin and piano duo artists,
tomorrow night in Memorial Hall
auditorium at 7:30 o'clock in a pro
gram of the Tuesday Evening Series,
according to the Committee on Pub
lic Exercises, in charge of the
weekly series.
Mr. and Mrs. Mills have made an
extensive study of the customs and
music of Rumania and Hungary, be
ing aided in their research work by
Mr. Mills' brother, a member of the
American diplomatic corps in Bu
charest. The artists will present the
recital attired in authentic native
costumes of the Balkan countries.
Professor of composition, counter
point, and orchestration at Whittier
College, Mr. Mills is composer of
"American Fantasy" for orchestra.
Piano compositions to be played at
the recital include Brahms' "Hun
garian Dance No. 7." "Rumanian
Christmas Songs." by Bart ok, and
"Hungarian Rhapsody No. fi," by
Liszt.
Violin selections which Mr. and
Mrs. Mills will play are: "Rumanian
Air," by Corder; "Hora Staccato,"
by Dinieu-Heifetz; "Rumanian
Dances," by Bart ok; "Gypsy Airs,"
by Sarasate; Ravel's "Tzigane"; and
"Ruralia Hungarica," by Dohnanyi.
