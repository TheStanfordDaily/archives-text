# Amendment, Candidates Up for A.S.S.U. Approval At Elections Tomorrow
## Polls Open from 8:30 to 2 o'clock At Law Steps, Engineering Corner: 41 Office-seekers on Ballot 
### 
Willi 11 names and a constitutional amendment on the ballot, the regular
winter quarter A.S.S.U. elections will be held tomorrow to fill eight stu
dent body offices. Polls will be open from 8:30 to 2 o'clock, according to

Election Chairman Dave Stephen-#
son. Student body cards must be
presented by voters, he said.
Persons with surnames beginning
with A to L will vote at the Law
Steps. Those from M to Z will vote
at the Engineering Comer.
Candidates for office follow:
Ex Committee—Graduate member:
Ralph Stotsky, Roberta Tuthill, Wal
ter Vincenti; junior man: Lyle Cook,
John Driscoll, Barney Ferguson,
Bill Janss, Bob Livingston, Robert
Richardson, Francis Dußois; sopho
more woman: Leila Arthur, Mary
Cowles, Anne Townsend, Lois Whit
more.
Men's Council—Burroughs Ander
son, Bill Ballhaus, Ed Collom, Har
lan Copsey, Jack Laney, Chester
Patton, John Porter, Bob Putnam,
George Rockwell, Gordy Weber,
Lloyd Wilson.
Women's Council — Barbara Gil
bert, Jane Greenwood, Betty Jane
Hart, Virginia McCloskey, Jeanne
' Sichel, Virginia Valentine.
B.A.C. mcmber-at-largc — Hump
ton Pool, Bill Rapp.
Yell leader—Bill McCurdy, John
Seamans. Assistant yell leaders—Bob
Beckham, Ed Cook, Tommy Dec,
John Moore, Gordy Nicoll, Teller
Weinmann.
Although Bob Livingston declined
as a candidate for junior man on
Ex Committee, his name will appear
on the ballot because the blank did
not reach Dave Stephenson, chair
man of Election Board, before the
deadline.
Stephenson said, "Technically Liv
ingston has not declined"; while Liv
ingston asserted, "Though my name
will appear on the ballot, I do not
wish to run for office."
Also on the ballot in the elections
will be the following proposed
amendment to the A.S.S.U. Constitu
tion:
Original article—"Art. 111, Student
Control. Section 3, Women's Coun
cil. a. Membership — Women's
Council shall consist of five women,
who have completed 90 hours of
university work at the time of taking
office."
Proposed amendment — "Art. 111,
Student Control. Section 3, Women's
Council, a. Membership—Women's
Council shall consist of five women
who shall, at the time of taking office,
have completed 120 units if elected
in spring quarter or 105 units if
elected in winter quarter."
Counting will be carried on in
Room 231, English Corner, by the
Election Board, which includes
Nancy Caswell, Nancy Hurst, Betty
Nichols, Martha Benson, Diana Hun
ter, Betty Boardman, Bill Leckie,
Temple Wanamaker, Pete Whittle
sey, Don Soltcro, Bill Kimball, Dave
Stephenson, chairman. Election as
sistants arc Dudley Smith,.Bill Dar
sie. Bob Prince, Helen Schroeder,
Elizabeth Tanner.
