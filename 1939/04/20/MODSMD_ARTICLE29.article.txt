# klahoyas to Give Mothers' Tea
## 
### 
Members of the Klahoya Club,
off-campus women's club, will give
a mother's tea tomorrow afternoon
from 3 to 6 o'clock in the Women's
Clubhouse. This tea is expected to
give the mothers of the club mem
bers an opportunity to become ac
quainted with one another. Miss
Mary Yost, dean of women, has
been invited.
Alice James will entertain the
mothers with violin selections, and
Miss Clare Wagstaffe will play the
piano. The chairmen of the com
mittees are Audrey Corrigan, invi
tations; Betty May Blois, decora
tions; Louise Fish, and Evelyn Dona
hue, refreshments; and Alice James,
entertainment.
