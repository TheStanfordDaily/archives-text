# Mothers Honor Farm's Old Guard
## 
### 
The "Old Guard," faculty and
those connected with the early years
oi the University, have been invited
to be honor guests of Stanford's
Mothers' Club at its annual spring
pageant, "Campus Cavalcade," to be
given Thursday in the Laurence
Frost Amphitheater at 2:30 o'clock.
Some of these honored guests,
who will gather on the stage fol
lowing the last scene for an informal
reception, are; former President and
Mrs. Herbert C. Hoover, Dr. and
Mrs. Ray Lyman Wilbur, Mrs. David
Starr Jordan, Mrs. Timothy Hop
kins, Dr. and Mrs. D. Charles Gard
ner, Professor and Mrs. William A.
Cooper, and Professor and Mrs.
R. E. Swain.
Tickets are now on sale at the
A.S.S.U. office and Keeble's at a
student rate of 50 cents. All other
tickets are $1. It is from this annual
pageant that the Mothers' Club ob
tains the funds to maintain the stu
dent rest homes and other student
benefits.
