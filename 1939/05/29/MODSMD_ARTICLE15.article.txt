# Dr. Lanz Leaves To Tour Europe
## 
### 
Dr. Henry Lanz, associate profes
sor of Slavic languages and phi
losophy, left the campus Saturday
for a five-month study of the music,
rituals, attitudes, customs, and gov
ernmental status of the monasteries
of Europe.
Boarding an Inter-Ocean Line
freighter in San Francisco, the pro
fessor will reach Le Havre, France,
via the Panama Canal; plans to live
with Greek and Russian Orthodox
monks, share their privations, and
study in their libraries.
In September Dr. Lanz will at
tend the Third International Con
gress of Slavic Philologists in Bel
grade. Mrs. Lanz will join him in
England in the early fall.
