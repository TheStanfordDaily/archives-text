Stanford University Weekly Calendar

EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, July 6
4:15 p.m. Lecture. "The Social Function of Science," J. D. Bernal,
F.R.S. Professor of Physics, Birkbeck College, University of
London. Auditorium, Education Building. The public is in
vited.
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist. Me
morial Church. The public is invited.
Friday, July 7
4:15 p.m. Concert. The Roth Quartet. Presented by The Friends of
Music of Stanford University. Auditorium, Memorial Hall.
General admission, $1.00; students, 50 cents.
8:00 p.m. Dramatic Reading. The Ascent of F-6, by W. H. Auden
and Christopher Isherwood. Presented by the Dramatists'
Alliance of Stanford University. Little Theater, Memorial
Hall. Admission, 25 cents.
Sunday, July 9
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: D. Elton Trueblood, Chaplain
of the University. Memorial Church.
5:00 p.m. Vesper Service. Laurence Frost Amphitheater. The public
is invited.
7 :30 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist. Me
morial Church. The public is invited.
Monday, July 10
8:00 p.m. Lecture. "Racine," Albert Guerard, Professor of General
Literature. Presented by the Dramatists' Alliance of Stanford
University. Lounge, Women's Clubhouse. The public is in
vited.
Tuesday, July 11
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. The American Way, by George
Kaufman and Moss Hart, dramatic reading by Mrs. Guy
Stevens Farrington. Auditorium, Memorial Hall. The public
is invited.
Wednesday, July 12
7:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: Ira L.
Wiggins, Associate Professor of Biology (Botany). Subject:
"Bizarre Plants of Baja California."
COMING EVENTS
Thursday, July 13
4:15 p.m. Lecture. "The Contemporary American Theater," Hubert C.
Heffner, Professor of Dramatic Literature. Auditorium, Edu
cation Building. The public is invited.
Tuesday, July 18
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Lecture, "Problems That Chal
lenge Us Today," Lillian M. Gilbreth, Professor of Manage
ment, Purdue University, and President, Gilbreth, Inc. Audi
torium, Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
CONFERENCES
June 30-July 5
Symposium on the Cell and Protoplasm.
July 6—B
Sixteenth National Colloid Symposium.
July 7-9
Conference on Educational Frontiers.
July 11-15
National Association of Directors of Physical Education for
College Women.
EXHIBITS
Stanford University Library. Recent additions to some of the special
collections in the University Library.
Hoover Library. A general exhibit of items selected from the Library's
collections.
Charlotte Ashley Felton Memorial Library. Californiana.
School of Education Library. Exhibition honoring 125 th anniversary
of Albany Female Academy, New York, which Mrs. Leland Stanford attended.
Graduate School of Business Library. Advertising Past and Present.
Thomas Welton Stanford Art Gallery. Paintings by contemporary
artists collected by the Gardena High School of Los Angeles, until July 30;
Paintings for illustrations by Pruet Carter, until July 30. (The Art Gallery is
open to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. daily, including
Sunday.)
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Printed for the University each Friday during the academic year by the
Stanford University Press. Notices to be included should be given in writing
to Miss Mildred Worswick, Secretary to the Committee on Public Exercises,
173 Administration Building, by 9:00 a.m. on Tuesday preceding the week in
which the event will occur. Mailing charge: $1.00 for the academic year or
25 cents for the quarter. Subscriptions may be sent to the Secretary.
