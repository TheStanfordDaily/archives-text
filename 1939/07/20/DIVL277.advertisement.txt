Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. XC, No. 5 July 20-26
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, July 20
4:15 p.m. Lecture. "Some Aspects of American Foreign Policy,"
Robert R. Wilson, Acting Professor of Political Science.
Auditorium, Education Building. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Peribanez yEI Comendador de Ocafia, by Lope de Vega.
Classic Spanish drama presented by The Dramatists' Alliance
of Stanford University and the Department of Romanic Lan
guages under the direction of Margarita de Mayo. Stanford
Union Court. The public is invited.
Friday, July 21
8:15 p.m. Concert. The Roth Quartet. Presented by The Friends of
Music of Stanford University. Auditorium, Memorial Hall.
General admission, $1.00; students, 50 cents.
Saturday, July 22
8:15 p.m. The Tempest, by William Shakespeare. Presented by the
Division of Speech and Drama under the direction of Thomas
Wood Stevens. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. General ad
mission, 50 cents. For reservations, call P.A. 22041. (First
of six performances.)
Sunday, July 23
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: Henry Wilder Foote, Unitarian
Church, Boston, Massachusetts.
7:30 p.m. Concert. Stanford String Ensemble. Feri Roth, conductor;
Jeno Antal, violinist; Warren D. Allen, organist. Memorial
Church. The public is invited.
Monday, July 24 9
8:00 p.m. Lecture. "From Rebellion to Classicism in Goethe and
Schiller," Bayard Quincy Morgan, Professor of German. Pre
sented by The Dramatists' Alliance of Stanford University.
Lounge, Women's Clubhouse. The public is invited.
Tuesday, July 25
8.00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Lecture, "The Penthouse of the
Gods," Theos Bernard. Illustrated with motion pictures.
Auditorium, Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
4:15 p.m. The Tempest, by William Shakespeare.
Wednesday, July 26
7:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: Ernest R.
Hilgard, Professor of Psychology and Education. Subject:
"Psychology and Public Policy."
8:15 p.m. The Tempest, by William Shakespeare. (Evening perform
ances will also be given on July 27, 28, and 29.)
ANNOUNCEMENTS
Thursday, July 20
1:45 p.m. Meeting of the Board of Trustees.
Monday, July 24
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Michael Reid Hanger for the degree of
Doctor of Philosophy. Major, Sociology; Minor, Economics.
Room 308, Library.
Tuesday, July 25
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Max Lund for the degree of Doctor of
Philosophy. Major, Psychology; Minor, Music. Room 17,
School of Education Building.
CONFERENCES
July 16-21
The Third Annual Stanford Business Conference.
July 23-28
Western Conference of Commercial and Trade Executives.
EXHIBITS
Stanford University Library. History of Science from the collections
of the Lane Library.
Hoover Library. An exhibit of items selected from the Library's Belgian
collections.
Charlotte Ashley Felton Memorial Library. Californiana.
School of Education Library. Publications of the National Education
Association.
School of Education. Annual textbook exhibit, July 17-22. Room 300,
Engineering Building.
Graduate School of Business Library. Advertising Past and Present.
Thomas Welton Stanford Art Gallery. Paintings by contemporary
artists collected by the Gardena High School of Los Angeles, until July 30;
Paintings for illustrations by Pruet Carter, until July 30. (The Art Gallery is
open to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m. to 5 :00 p.m. daily, including
Sunday.)

Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. XC, No. 5 July 20-26
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, July 20
4:15 p.m. Lecture. "Some Aspects of American Foreign Policy,"
Robert R. Wilson, Acting Professor of Political Science.
Auditorium, Education Building. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Peribanez yEI Comendador de Ocafia, by Lope de Vega.
Classic Spanish drama presented by The Dramatists' Alliance
of Stanford University and the Department of Romanic Lan
guages under the direction of Margarita de Mayo. Stanford
Union Court. The public is invited.
Friday, July 21
8:15 p.m. Concert. The Roth Quartet. Presented by The Friends of
Music of Stanford University. Auditorium, Memorial Hall.
General admission, $1.00; students, 50 cents.
Saturday, July 22
8:15 p.m. The Tempest, by William Shakespeare. Presented by the
Division of Speech and Drama under the direction of Thomas
Wood Stevens. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. General ad
mission, 50 cents. For reservations, call P.A. 22041. (First
of six performances.)
Sunday, July 23
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: Henry Wilder Foote, Unitarian
Church, Boston, Massachusetts.
7:30 p.m. Concert. Stanford String Ensemble. Feri Roth, conductor;
Jeno Antal, violinist; Warren D. Allen, organist. Memorial
Church. The public is invited.
Monday, July 24 9
8:00 p.m. Lecture. "From Rebellion to Classicism in Goethe and
Schiller," Bayard Quincy Morgan, Professor of German. Pre
sented by The Dramatists' Alliance of Stanford University.
Lounge, Women's Clubhouse. The public is invited.
Tuesday, July 25
8.00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Lecture, "The Penthouse of the
Gods," Theos Bernard. Illustrated with motion pictures.
Auditorium, Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
4:15 p.m. The Tempest, by William Shakespeare.
Wednesday, July 26
7:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: Ernest R.
Hilgard, Professor of Psychology and Education. Subject:
"Psychology and Public Policy."
8:15 p.m. The Tempest, by William Shakespeare. (Evening perform
ances will also be given on July 27, 28, and 29.)
