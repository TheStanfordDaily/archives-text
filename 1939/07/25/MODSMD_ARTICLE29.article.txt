# Woolley Leads In Tennis Tourney
## 
### 
Stanford's Summer Quarter net
tourney passed through the first
round last week and into the sec
ond-round matches. The first player
to reach quarter-final play was
Herbert Woolley, who won by a de
fault over Borgfeld in second-round
matching. Woolley had previously
beaten Baldwin, 6-1, 6-2.
In first-round games Post d.
Wright, 1-6, 6-2, 6-1; Lee d. Whit
tell, 6-1, 6-2; Callyer d. Butterworth,
default; Lindermeyer d. Learned,
6-0, 2-0 (default, second set; Hines
d. Butler, 6-3, 10-8; Haber d. Bunn,
4-6, 6-3, 10-8; Hyams d. Wolpert, de
fault; Gorter d. Trueblood, 6-4, 6-2;
Woolley d. Baldwin, 6-1, 6-2; Borg
feld d. Mersereau, 6-3, 9-7, and
Parker d. O'Neil, 6-1, 6-2.
Boiled snakes are used as a cure
for tuberculosis in Korea.

The consumption of all kinds of
motor fuel increased 1 per cent for
the year 1938 over 1937.

Less than 30 self-propelled ve
hicles were operating in America in
1898.
