Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. XC, No. 6 July 27-August 2
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, July 27
4:15 p.m. Illustrated Lecture. "American Architecture Viewed from
Europe," Siegfried Giedion of the University of Zurich.
Auditorium, Education Building. The public is invited.
8:15 p.m. The Tempest, by William Shakespeare. Presented by the
Division of Speech and Drama under the direction of Thomas
Wood Stevens. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. General ad
mission, 50 cents. For reservations, call P.A. 22041.
Friday, July 28
8:15 p.m. The Tempest, by William Shakespeare.
Saturday, July 29
8:15 p.m. The Tempest, by William Shakespeare. (Final performance.)
Sunday, July 30
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: D. Elton Trueblood, Chaplain
of the University. Memorial Church.
5:00 p.m. Vesper Service. Laurence Frost Amphitheater. The public
is invited.
7:30 p.m. Concert. The Roth Quartet; Warren D. Allen, University
Organist. Memorial Church. The public is invited.
Monday, July 31
8:00 p.m. Dramatic Reading. Distant Harvest, by Arthur McCullough
Sampley, joint winner of the Verse Drama Contest award
established in honor of Maxwell Anderson. Presented by The
Dramatists' Assembly and read by members of the Assembly
and the Community Theaters. Little Theater, Memorial Hall.
General admission, 50 cents.
Tuesday, August 1
4:30 p.m. Lecture. "Lope de Vega," Margarita de Mayo, Acting As
sociate Professor of Romanic Languages. Presented by The
Dramatists' Assembly. Lounge, Women's Clubhouse. The
public is invited.
8 :00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Concert, The Roth Quartet. Pro
vided by the Elizabeth Sprague Coolidge Foundation. Audi
torium, Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
Wednesday, August 2
4:15 p.m. Lecture. "Legislative Origin, Work and Training of School
Trustees," G. L. Aynesworth, President of the California
School Trustees Association. Presented under the auspices of
the School of Education. Auditorium, Education Building.
The public is invited.
7:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: D. Elton
Trueblood, Chaplain, and Professor of Philosophy of Religion.
Subject: "Common Problems of England and America."
8:00 p.m. Under the Gaslight, by Augustin Daly. Presented by Perry
Dilly and his Puppet Theater under the auspices of The
Dramatists' Assembly. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. Gen
eral admission, 50 cents.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
Wednesday, August 2

Doctor of Philosophy. Major, Physics; Minor, Chemistry.
Room 308, Library.
