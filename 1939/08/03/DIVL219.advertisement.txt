EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, August 3
4.15 p.m. Concert. Ihe Roth Quartet Chamber Music Qass. Audi
torium, Education Building. The public is invited.
4:30 p.m. Lectures, "Arts for a National Theater," Gordon Lange, di
rector, and Jester liairston, musical director, Federal Theater,
San Francisco. Presented by The Dramatists' Assembly.
Little Theater, Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Lectures, "Drama and the Related Arts," Robert Balzer,
Helen Morse, and Dorothy Smith. Presented by The Dram
atists' Assembly. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. The public
is invited.
Friday, August 4
8:00 p.m. Lectures, "Experience and Research in the Theater," George
Poultney, former president of the Northern California Drama
Association, and Lawrence Estavan, editor for the Theater
Research Project of San Francisco. Presented by The Dram
atists' Assembly. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. The public
is invited.
Saturday, August 5
2.30 p.m. Lectures, 1 heme and Torm, Thomas Wood Stevens, acting
director of dramatics, Shau Wing Chan, and Helen Cross, of
the San Francisco Theater Union. Presented by The Dram
atists' Assembly. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. The public
is invited.
8:30 p.m. Play Reading. Molehills, by Muriel Roy Bolton. Presented
by the Peninsula Little Theater of San Mateo under the
auspices of The Dramatists' Assembly. Little Theater, Me
morial Hall. General admission, 50 cents.
Sunday, August 6
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: D. Elton Trueblood, Chaplain
of the University. Memorial Church.
7.30 p.m. Organ Recital. Raymond Kendall, Assistant University Or
ganist. Memorial Church. The public is invited.
Monday, August 7
8:00 p.m. Lecture. "Pithecanthropus and Sinanthropus and Their Re
lationship to Later Stages of Human Evolution," Franz
Weidenreich, Professor of Anatomy, Peiping Union Medical
College, China. Presented under the auspices of the Sixth
Pacific Science Congress. Auditorium, Education Building.
The public is invited.
Tuesday, August 8
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Lecture, "A Rendezvous with
Destiny," William Leonard Cmm, Acting Professor of Busi
ness Economics (Graduate School of Business). Auditorium,
Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
Wednesday, August 9
7:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: Margery
Bailey, Associate Professor of English. Subject: "A New
Race of Dramatists."
8:00 p.m. Lecture. "The Biology of the Great Barrier Reef," C. M.
Yonge, of the University of Bristol, England. Presented
under the auspices of the Sixth Pacific Science Congress.
Auditorium, Education Building. The public is invited.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
. Monday, August 7
2 .00 p.m. Oral examination of Jerome Rubin Vinograd for the degree
of Doctor of Philosophy. Major, Chemistry; Minor, Mathe

173 Administration Building, by 9:00 a.m. on Tuesday preceding the week in
which the event will occur. Mailing charge: $1 .00 for the academic year or
25 cents for the quarter. Subscriptions may be sent to the Secretary.

Thursday, August 3
4.15 p.m. Concert. Ihe Roth Quartet Chamber Music Qass. Audi

atists' Assembly. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. The public

Pacific Science Congress. Auditorium, Education Building.
The public is invited.

8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Lecture, "A Rendezvous with
Destiny," William Leonard Cmm, Acting Professor of Busi
ness Economics (Graduate School of Business). Auditorium,
