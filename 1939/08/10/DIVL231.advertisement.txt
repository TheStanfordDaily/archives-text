Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. XC, No. 8 August 10-16
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, August 10
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital. Raymond Kendall, Assistant University Or
ganist. Memorial Church. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Illustrated Lecture. "The Flora of the Hawaiian Islands
and the History of the Pacific Basin," Carl Skottsberg, of the
University of Goteberg, Sweden. Presented under the aus
pices of the Sixth Pacific Science Congress. Auditorium,
Education Building. The public is invited.
Friday, August 11
8:00 p.m. Concert. Stanford String Orchestra, Feri Roth, Director.
Stanford Union Court. The public is invited.
Sunday, August 13
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: D. Elton Trueblood, Chaplain
of the University. Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m. Concert. Raymond Kendall, Assistant University Organist,
assisted by Jeno Antal, violinist. Memorial Church. The pub
lic is invited.
Tuesday, August 15
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Illustrated lecture-recital, "Art
into Music" (based on Moussorgsky's "Pictures from an Ex
hibition" and the Originals by Victor Hartmann), Alfred V.
Frankenstein, Music Critic, San Francisco Chronicle, and Edna
McEachern, Lecturer in Music and Education. Auditorium,
Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
Wednesday, August 16
7:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: Andrew W.
Lind, Acting Professor of Sociology. Subject: "The Ha
waiian Racial Experiment."
8:15 p.m. The Two Shepherds, by G. Martinez Sierra. Presented by
the Division of Speech and Drama under the direction of
Thomas Wood Stevens. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. Gen
eral admission, 50 cents. For reservations, call P.A. 22041.
Evening performances will also be given on August 17, 18,
and 19.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
Thursday, August 10
4:00 p.m. Biology Seminar, Room 430. Speaker: B. H. Willier, Profes
sor of Zoology, University of Rochester. Subject: "Control
of Feather Color Pattern as Revealed by Interbreed Grafting
in the Chick Embryo." Visitors are welcome.
Friday, August 11
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Besse A. Clement for the degree of Doc
tor of Philosophy. Major, French; Minor, Spanish. Room
407, Chemistry Building.
Monday, August 14
2 :00 p.m. Oral examination of William Dempsey Kennedy for the de
gree of Doctor of Philosophy. Major, Chemistry; Minor,
Mathematics. Room 18, Chemistry Building.
Tuesday, August 15
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Robert Linton French for the degree of
Doctor of Philosophy. Major, Psychology; Minor, Distrib
uted. Room 167, Administration Building.
Wednesday, August 16
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Howard Tatel for the degree of Doctor
of Philosophy. Major, Physics; Minor, Mathematics. Room
308, Library.
CONFERENCES
August 6-12
Sixth Pacific Science Congress of the Pacific Science Asso

August 14-25
American Institute of Public Relations.

Stanford University Weekly Calendar
Vol. XC, No. 8 August 10-16
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, August 10
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital. Raymond Kendall, Assistant University Or
ganist. Memorial Church. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Illustrated Lecture. "The Flora of the Hawaiian Islands
and the History of the Pacific Basin," Carl Skottsberg, of the
University of Goteberg, Sweden. Presented under the aus
pices of the Sixth Pacific Science Congress. Auditorium,
Education Building. The public is invited.
Friday, August 11
8:00 p.m. Concert. Stanford String Orchestra, Feri Roth, Director.
Stanford Union Court. The public is invited.
Sunday, August 13
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: D. Elton Trueblood, Chaplain
of the University. Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m. Concert. Raymond Kendall, Assistant University Organist,
assisted by Jeno Antal, violinist. Memorial Church. The pub
lic is invited.
Tuesday, August 15
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Illustrated lecture-recital, "Art
into Music" (based on Moussorgsky's "Pictures from an Ex
hibition" and the Originals by Victor Hartmann), Alfred V.
Frankenstein, Music Critic, San Francisco Chronicle, and Edna
McEachern, Lecturer in Music and Education. Auditorium,
Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
Wednesday, August 16
7:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: Andrew W.
Lind, Acting Professor of Sociology. Subject: "The Ha
waiian Racial Experiment."
8:15 p.m. The Two Shepherds, by G. Martinez Sierra. Presented by
the Division of Speech and Drama under the direction of
Thomas Wood Stevens. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. Gen
eral admission, 50 cents. For reservations, call P.A. 22041.
Evening performances will also be given on August 17, 18,
and 19.
