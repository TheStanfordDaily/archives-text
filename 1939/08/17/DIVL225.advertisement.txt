EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, August 17
8:15 p.m. The Two Shepherds, by G. Martinez Sierra. Presented by
the Division of Speech and Drama under the direction of
Thomas Wood Stevens. Little Theater, Memorial Hall. Gen
eral admission, SO cents. For reservations, call P.A. 22041.
Friday, August 18
8:15 p.m. The Two Shepherds, by G. Martinez Sierra.
Saturday, August 19
8:15 p.m. The Two Shepherds, by G. Martinez Sierra. (Final per
formance.)
Sunday, August 20
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: D. Elton Trueblood, Chaplain
of the University. Memorial Church.
7:30 p.m. Organ Recital. Raymond Kendall, Assistant University
Organist. Memorial Church. The public is invited.
Tuesday, August 22
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Motion picture, "Champs-filysees,"
titles in English. Auditorium, Memorial Hall. The public is
invited.
Wednesday, August 23
7:15 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KGO). Speaker: Theodore
Nicol, Instructor in Romanic languages. Subject: "French
Poets in Prison."
8:15 p.m. Night Before the Border, by Jean Clark (1939 Maxwell
Anderson Prize Play). Presented by the Division of Speech
and Drama under the direction of Thomas Wood Stevens.
Little Theater, Memorial Hall. General admission, 50 cents.
For reservations, call P.A. 22041. (A second performance will
be given August 24.)
ANNOUNCEMENTS
Tuesday, August 22
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Jeannette Snyder Noack for the degree
of Doctor of Philosophy. Maj'or, English; Minor, Education.
Room 407, Library.
EXHIBITS
Stanford University Library. Old plays and new: Rarities from the
collection of George Poultney of San Francisco.
Hoover Library. An exhibit of items selected from the Library's Belgian
collections.
Charlotte Ashley Felton Memorial Library. Californiana.
School of Education Library. Exhibit on Negro achievements.
Graduate School of Business Library. Biographies of Business Firms.
Thomas Welton Stanford Art Gallery. Stage and Drama, by The
Dramatists' Alliance of Stanford University, until August 30. (The Art Gal
lery is open to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., daily,
including Sunday.)
