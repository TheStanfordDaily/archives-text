# Cellar Cook Tells Part in Accident
## 
### 
Confessing responsibility for an
automobile accident that resulted in
a fatality, Earl Ramsey, 32, a cook
in the Union Cellar, surrendered
Saturday to Oakland police on
charge of manslaughter.
The accident took place Wednes
day night as Ramsey side-swiped
another car on the bay bridge, fa
tally injuring Mrs. Anne Snelling,
60, of Oakland. Ramsey, confused,
drove back toward Oakland to get
aid and report the accident, but be
came lost and turned back. Upon
arriving at the scene of the accident
he found all traces of the mishap
gone.
Ramsey confessed to Gordon
Burns, Stanford police officer, who
notified Oakland police.
