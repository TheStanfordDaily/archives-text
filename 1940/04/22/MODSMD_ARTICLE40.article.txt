# Bear Netmen Drop Cards, 5-4
## 
### 
The University of California net
men took four singles matches and
one doubles to defeat the Stanford
Varsity for the second time this
season by a 5-4 score.
Leading the Bear tennis men was
senior Bob Peacock who played al-
I most perfect tennis to outlast Stan
| ford's sophomore number one man
Larry Dee 7-5, 3-6, 6-3. Both play
ers thrilled the crowd with their
flashy game and Peacock drew gasps
time after time with his phenomenal
lobbing.
808 LOW STARS
The Stanford hero proved to be
dark-haired, lanky Bob Low. Low
J handed Cal's Robin Hippensteil a
I 4-6. 6-3, 6-3 beating and then teamed
j with Stan Owen to win his doubles
i match.
Captain Dave Brock gave the Cards
| the only other singles win by down
: ing Cal's Bill Swan in easy fashion
j 6-2, 6-3.
YEARLINGS TAKE CUBS
The Stanford frosh, however, were
of some consolation by whipping the
| Bear Cub netmen 6-3. Tom Kruger
led the frosh with a sensational 6-1,
| 6-4 win over George Spencer. Other
j winners for the frosh were Charles
Hutcheson, Harper Ink and Hal
i Lamb.
Varsity singles; Peacock (C) def.
Dee (S) 7-4, 3-6, 6-3; Harmon (C)
1 def. Wade (S) 6-3, 6-1; Low (S) def.
Hippensteil (C) 4-6, 6-3, 6-3; Can
ning (C) def. Gates (S), 6-3, 2-6, 8-6;
Brock (S) def. Swan (C), 6-2, 6-3;
j Ammonette (C) def. Seaver (S), 9-7,
6-2.

! def. Dee and Wade (S); Low and
Owen (S) def. Horbart and Meyer
I (C), 6-2, 1-6, 6-2; Folsom and Rose
(S) def. Gerwick and Hippensteil
