# Card Netmen Win in National
## Dee and Wade to Try For Third Net Title 
### 
The Stanford combination of
Larry Dee and Jim Wade walked off
with the National Intercollegiate
tennis crown at Haverford, Penn
sylvania, last week, by defeating Bob
Peacock and Bob Harmon of Cali
fornia, 6-3, 6-4, 3-6, 2-6, 6-2.
This week, Dee and Wade again
stroked their way to victory and
won the Eastern Intercollegiate ten
nis doubles title at Montclair Ath
letic Club, New Jersey.
The Stanford team made their
way into the final varsity round by
displaying rugged determination in
coming back to conquer Gordon
Giles and Frank Mehner of Utah,
2-6, 4-6, 9-7, 6-1, 6-1.
WIN SECOND TITLE
In the final round, Dee and Wade
defeated the Trojan pair, Ken Bart
let and Bill Reedy, top seeded play
ers, winning a second title.
From Montclair, the Stanford pair
headed for the Eastern Slope Tennis
Club at North Conway, New Hamp
shire, where they will try for their
third doubles crown of the season.
Emery Neale, ace netster of Stan
ford's freshman team, reached the
finals of the Oregon state tennis
championship.
Neale defeated Walt Davis of
Portland, 8-6, 6-4, to reach the semi
finals, and then entered the finals
after trouncing Ramis 8-6, 6-1, 6-0.
Playing the New York State clay
court championships, Bobby Low,
top-ranking Stanford player, was
eliminated in the second round by
Vincent Paul, of New York, 6-4, 5-7,
6-1.
