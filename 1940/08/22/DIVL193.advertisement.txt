ANNOUNCEMENTS

EVENTS OF THE WEEK
Thursday, August 22
8 :(X) p.m. Recital. George Antheil, pianist. Composer-in-rcsidcnce and
Lecturer in Music. Presented under the auspices of the Di
vision of Music. Auditorium, Memorial Hall. The public is
invited.
Sunday, August 25
11 :00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: D. Elton Trueblood, Chaplain of
the University. The public is invited.
7:30 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist. Me
morial Church. The public is invited.
Wednesday, August 28
9:30 p.m. Stanford University Hour (KPO). Speaker: Arthur Mur
phy, Acting Professor of Philosophy. Subject: "Words,
Things, and Ideas."
END-QUARTER EXAMINATIONS
Thursday, August 29, to Saturday, August 31.
ANNOUNCEMENTS
Monday, August 26
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Guy Hendrickson Harris for the degree
of Doctor of Philosophy. Major, Chemistry; Minor, Physics.
Room 18, Chemistry Building.
Tuesday, August 27
7:00 p.m. Oral examination of Mark M. Horton for the degree of Doc
tor of Philosophy. Major. English; Minor. Germanic Lan
guages. Room 42, English Building.
Thursday, August 29
2:00 p.m. Oral examination of Friench Simpson for the degree of Doctor
of Philosophy. Major, English; Minor, Philosophy. Room 42,
English Building.
EXHIBITS
Stanford University Library. Costumes and Conventions of the Com
media dell'Arte. Exhibit arranged by the Dramatists' Alliance in connection
with the summer lectures on Comedy.
Hoover Library. Contemporary War Propaganda Distributed in the
Republic of Mexico.
Charlotte Ashley Felton Memorial Library. Recent accessions.
Graduate School of Business Library. Business Papers dealing with
the Comstock period, 1860-1875.
Thomas Welton Stanford Art Gallery. Closed for alterations until
October 1.
WEEKLY CALENDAR
Printed for the University each Wednesday by the Stanford University
Press. Notices to be included should be given in writing to Miss Mildred
Worswick, Secretary to the Committee on Public Exercises, 172 Administra
tion Building, by 9:00 a.m. on Tuesday preceding the week in which the event
will occur. Mailing charge: $1.00 for the academic year or 25 cents for the
quarter. Subscriptions may be sent to the Secretary.
