# Farm to Join U.C.L.A. Fete
## Special Train Leaves Paly Tonight at 8:45 
### 
Stanfordites invading the U. C.
L. A. campus this week-end will be
part of a gigantic three-day home
coming program planned by the
Bruins. An activity-packed three
days has been planned by Pierce
Gannon, director of the U.C.L.A.
celebrations.
A special Stanford section has
been assigned and will leave Paly
at 8:45 tonight. Train tickets are
still available at the A.S.S.U. office.
Those who skipped today's
classes will be able to attend the
Coronation of the Homecoming
Queen on the Ucla quad at 11
o'clock this morning.
HOUSES PLAN DINNERS
All fraternities and sororities will
hold open house and will have date
dinners tonight. Some of the south
ern houses have planned floats for
the occasion.
Following a parade tonight, the
rally will be held around the Bruin
bonfire. The evening's festivities
will close with a tremendous dance
featuring the Orrin Tucker band
and Bonnie Baker. The dance will
begin at 10 o'clock with lots of stai's
and guest artists promised.
U.C.L.A. REGULATIONS
Women students staying on cam
pus must abide by the U.C.L.A. reg
ulations. All violations will be re
ported back to Stanford and will be
dealt with here. Those staying in
hotels must be in by 2:30 o'clock.
Announced rates in preferred Los
Angeles hotels include $2 (single) at
the Arcady and $3 (single) at the
Chapman Park. A price of $2 will
be charged at either hotel for people
in doubles or triples. The Stanford
special will return at 6:45 o'clock
Monday morning.
