# G.E. Fellowship Applications Open
## 
### 
Applications for fellowships for
research in the fields of electricity,
physics and chemistry are now be
ing received for the 1941-42 school
year, according to an announcement
of the Charles A. Coffin Foundation,
established by the General Electric
Company.
Carrying a minimum allowance of
$500 each, the fellowships are
granted annually to university, col
lege, or technical school graduates
who have shown that they could un
dertake or continue research work
in educational institutions.
Application forms may be ob
tained by writing the Secretary of
the Foundation at Schenectady, N.Y.,
and they must be filed by Jan. 15,
1941.
