# Loss of British Ships on Decline
## 
### 
LONDON, Jan. 7 (UP).—Britain
claimed today to have reduced
sharply her shipping losses in the
last three weeks of 1940 but admit
ted a severe meat shortage had
forced her to dip into reserves.

Merchant marine losses for the
week ending Dec. 30 were the low
est in 10 weeks—three British ships
aggregating 18,208 tons and four Al
lied ships totaling 19,348 tons, the
Admiralty reported.

A resume of shipping losses, cov
ering the entire war period, indi
cated progress in the drive to ease a
situation said recently to be as
"grave as that in 1917," darkest pe
riod of the World War.
