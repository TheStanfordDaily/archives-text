# Row to Host Men Rushees Again Today
## 
### 
Encina's 1940-41 crop of rushees
will visit the Row again today and
tomorrow for the fifth and next to
last rush period of the season.
The men will lunch at local chap
ter houses this noon from 11:30 to 1
o'clock, followed by another period
from 2 to 4:30, and dinner from 5
to 8.
Tomorrow Stanford's 24 fraterni
ties will play hosts from 8 to 11:30
a.m., and from 12 to 2 for lunch.
The sixth and concluding rush pe
riod of the season will be held next
Tuesday and Wednesday with lunch
from 11:30 to 1 o'clock and dinner
from 5:30 to 7 on both days.
The final period will be followed
by preference dinners on Thursday,
Jan. 16, and pledging and the pledge
prom the next day.
Bob Prince, prom chairman, has
announced that, as is customary,
two orchestras will provide music
for the semiformal prom. Bids will
sell at the A.S.S.U. office at $1.25
each. The affair will be held in the
Women's Gym, with both sides open
for the dancers. Approximately 1200
bids are being printed.
