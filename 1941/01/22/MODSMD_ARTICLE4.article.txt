# A.S.S.U. Seeks Committeemen
## 
### 
Final interviews to fill vacancies
on the A.S.S.U. International Com
mittee will be held today from 2 to
4 o'clock in Room 13 of the Old
Union.
Both foreign and American stu
dents are eligible for positions on
the committee, although the vacan
cies at present are mostly for the
former. Freshmen and sophomores
are especially invited to try out.
The work of International Com
mittee consists mainly of planning
orientation and social activities for
the foreign students attending the
University. Students trying out are
invited to bring suggestions for addi
tional activities, according to Chair
man Janet Snelling.
Professor Emeritus Bailey Willis
will show movies on Japan at the
regular meeting of the Interna
tional Students Club this evening.
Additional entertainment "will be
provided by a professional magician.
The meeting will be held as usual
at the home of Mrs. E. J. Thomas,
365 Lincoln Avenue, Palo Alto.
Cars will leave the Nitery at 7:30
o'clock. Refreshments will be
served.
