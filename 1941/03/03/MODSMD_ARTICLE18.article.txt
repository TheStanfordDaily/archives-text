# Dr. Wilbur Talk Will Highlight Founder's Day
## Evening's Concert With S.F. Symphony Free to All Students 
### 
Dr. Robert E. Swain, general
chairman of the Commemoration
Committee for the Fiftieth Anni
versary Celebration, stated today
that all students are invited to at
tend the events of Founders' Day
on Sunday, March 9.
Events of Foundars' Day include
tower music from the belfry of the
Hoover Library by the Stanford
Brass Choir whioh will take place
at 10:30 o'clock; a special servicc of
commemoration in the Memorial
Church with an address by President
Ray Lyman Wilbur on "The Vision
of Our Founders," at 11 o'clock; and
a sacred concert in the Memorial
Church by the San Francisco Sym
phony Orchestra at 8 o'clock in the
evening.
A feature of the concert will be
the playing for the first time of "Ode
to Truth," written by the well
known Composer Roy Harris espe
cially for Stanford and dedicated to
its fiftieth anniversary.
The morning service is open to the
public, and no tickets are required
for admission; however tickets are
required for admission to the sym
phony concert.
Because of the limited seating ca
pacity of the Church, a distribution
of tickets for the symphony con
cert has been arranged for the stu
dents on Wednesday, March 5, from
8:30 o'clock to 5 o'clock in the lobby
of the Administration Building.
Tickets will be issued to students,
undergraduate or graduate, upon
the presentation of the student body
card.
