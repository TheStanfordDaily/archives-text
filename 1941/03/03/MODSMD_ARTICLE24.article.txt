# 'Y' Tells Plans For Busy Week
## 
### 
The Y.W.C.A. will open this week's
activities with its Music Apprecia
tion Group which will meet today
from 4 to 5 o'clock in the "Y" rooms.
Everyone is invited to hear an hour
of recorded music and have tea.
Tuesday, March 4, the "Y" will
hold two meetings. The "Marriage
and the Home" Group will meet at
G:45 o'clock. This group will be the
last meeting in this series. Dr. Shep
ard, medical adviser to men, will be
the guest speaker, and will talk on
"Sex Relationships in Marriage."
The Cabinet will give a tea in the
"Y" rooms at 4 o'clock Tuesday in
honor of the Advisory Board. Miss
Mary Morrissett, field worker of the
American Friends Service Commit
tee, will also be a guest.
The Social Service Group of the
"Y" will meet jointly with the Stan
ford Co-op Bookstore on Wednes
day, March 5, from 4 to 5 o'clock.
Motion pictures will be shown of
Summer Co-operative Work Proj
ects. Miss Mary Morrissett, repre
senting the Friends Service Com
mittee, will speak on the opportuni
ties for work this summer in the
social service centers.
