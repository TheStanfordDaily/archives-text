# Eighty-nine Candidates Nominated for 31 Positions On Spring Election Ballots
## Nominees to Appear at University Presentation Assembly in Memorial Hall Monday Morning, 9:45 o'Clock 
### 
Eighty-nine candidates yesterday filed nomination blanks in the next
to the last round of the '41 political Iwittle which will bring thirty-one stu
dent positions to the block next Tuesday at the regular spring quarter
A.S.S.U. elections.
Fourteen A.S.S.U. positions, seven class oflices, three Women's Con-

ference positions, and seven W.A.A.<
cilices will be at stake via the ballot
box next Tuesday.
Frank Albert and George Jackson
remained as sole candidates for
A.S.S.U. president, with Pat Adams,
Sylvia Berry and Mary Jean Haynes
opposed for the A.S.S.U. vice-presi
dency.
Last minute political jockeying,
which saw no nomination for senior
class secretary-treasurer and eight

nominees for the post of junior
woman on Excom, saw a flood of
independent candidates swarming
for positions on the ballot.
The Daily received notice late
last night that Libby Eastman will
seek the position of senior class sec
retary-treasurer as a write-in can
didate.
Nominations closed last night at
5 o'clock.
