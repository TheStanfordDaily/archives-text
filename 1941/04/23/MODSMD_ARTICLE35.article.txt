# Tennis Team Whips Miami Netmen, 5-4
## 
### CHES SAROYAN 
Led by another surprising per
formance by Emery Nettle, Stanford's
varsity netmen pulled out a 5-4 win
over the barnstorming University
of Miami team yesterday. The In
dians won three of the singles and
two of the doubles matches.
Neale, lanky sophomore from
Portland, stormed the net to blast
the highly touted Billy Gillespie of
Miami ofl' the court, 6-3, 6-3, in the
number one singles match.
Playing the second spot for the
Cards, Larry Dee bowed down to
Louis Bronstein, 5-7, (5-2, 6-1. Dee
had his sharp serve and blasting
forehand working in the first set.
but the dogged retrieving of Bron
stein ruined his game.
WADE LOSES
Dick McKee, National Public
Parks champion for Miami, swept
through Jimmy Wade of the Cards
in straight sets, 6-3, 6-0. In the
fourth singles match, Captain Bob
Low of the Cards lost another three
set struggle, bowing to Bobbie Deck
er, 6-0, 2-6, 6-3.
Stanford's other two singles win
ners were A1 Rose and sophomore
Tom Kruger. Rose harnessed his
lightning forehand to chase the
chopping, slicing George Parks off
the court, 6-3, 6-2, while Kruger
turned in his best performance of
the season in stopping veteran
George Pero of Miami, 6-1, 6-4.
Kruger was down 4-1 in the second
set, but fought back to win.
DEE. WADE IN FORM
Stanford's undefeated doubles
champions Dee and Wade again
were in top shape, and completely
outclassed Gillespie and Decker of
Miami, 6-2, 8-6. Dee's fiery return
services and Wade's sharp volleys
baffled the visitors.
The number two doubles match
was the most crucial of the after
noon. With the team score at 4-all,
the Indian team of Low and Stan
Owen outlasted Bronstein and Parks,
6-2, 4-6, 6-4.
The Miami team of Pero and Mc-
Kee won the other doubles match,
edging out Jack Gurley and Neale,
8-6, 10-8.
