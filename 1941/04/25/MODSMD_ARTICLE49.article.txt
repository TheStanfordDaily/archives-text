# RACQUETEERS.
## Our Athletes Are Overcome. Berkeley Enemy Victorious. Cards Struggle Valiantly 
### 
Playing under a blazing afternoon
sun, Stanford's varsity netmen
bowed 5-4 before the powerful Uni
versity of California team yester
day. The Bear squad split the sin
gles matches, but came back strong
to take two of the three doubles for
their, second win over the Indians.
In the number one singles match,
veteran Robin Hippensteil of the
Bears proved too strong for sopho
more Emery Neale, and came home
with a sensational 6-2, 3-6, 6-2 win.
Hippensteil moved behind a blind
ing serve, followed it to the net for
sharp, angle volleys to turn in an
impressive win.
SEARING VOLLEYS.
Hippensteil came back to team
with George Spencer to upset the
Indians' prize doubles team of Larry
Dee and Jim Wade, 7-5, 6-4. in the
hottest match of the afternoon. Both
teams served, volleyed, smashed in
excellent form to thrill the large
turnout of fans.
In the second singles Dee showed
a complete return to form,, hit the
ball like the old Dee, to move
through Bill Canning 6-3. 6-2.
WADE TRIUMPHS.
Jimmy Wade came through in the
third singles match, whipping soph
omore Bob McCaughlin 6-2, 6-0.
This is the second time this year that
Wade has cut McCaughlin down.
Playing his last conference match
for Stanford, Captain Bob Clarence
Low turned in a decisive 6-2, 6-4
win over Spencer. Low coupled an
accurate chop forehand with his
deadly backhand to keep Spencer
helpless throughout the match. Low
also had his lobs working, and con
tinually planted the ball just inside
the baseline to the dismay of the
net-rushing Spencer.
ALSO PLAYED.
Also playing his last match for
Stanford, Allan Rose dropped a
hard-fought 7-5, 6-4 decision to
Tom Brown of the Bears.
The number six singles was an
other one of those crucials. A vic
tory would have given the Cards a
four to two lead. But Tom Kruger
threw away a large lead to bow to
the accurate lobbing, passing shots
of Jimmy Livingston. The score,
4-6, 6-4, 6-4.
Captain Low and Stan Owen lost a
three-set struggle to Gunny Amo
nette and Canning, 6-2, 3-6, 7-5,
! while the number three doubles
match went to the Indian team «of
Neale and Jack Gurley, who finished
strong to whip Livingstone and
Brown 5-7, 6-2, 6-1.
