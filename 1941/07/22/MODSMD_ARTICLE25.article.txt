# Indian Tennismen Win Two Titles
## 
### 
Only Larry Dee held up the
name of Stanford in the summer
tennis circuit last week as Ted
Schroeder, Emery Neale, Jim Wade,
and Jack Gurley were conquered by
their various opponents in the tour
naments in which they competed.
Dee defeated George Ball of
Texas in the finals of the Northern
Clay Court tourney at St. Paul, 6-4,
7-5, 2-6, 7-5. However, in a major
upset Dee and Wade were defeated
by two comparative unknowns in
the doubles final.
Although Neale succeeded in at
taining the finals of the Pacific
Northwest championships, he was
outsteadied by Nick Carter in the
deciding match, 3-6, 6-1, 6-3, 3-6,
8-6, while he and Jack Gurley were
eliminated in the semi-finals of the
doubles.
Schroeder, while competing in the
New York Clay Courts tournament,
a major event, was put out of the
singles division in the quarter-final
round by Ted Olewine, Trojan ace,
in a minor upset, 2-6, 6-3, 6-3. He
went on, however, to take his sixth
successive doubles title while team
ing with Jack Kramer by defeating
Don McNeil and Frank Parker.
