STANFORD CO UNIVERSITY
CALENDAR
Volume XCVIII July 24-30, 1941 Number 6
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
THURSDAY, JULY 24
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital. Herbert B. Nanney, Assistant University
Organist. Memorial Church. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Concert. Antonio Brosa, violin; Germain Prevost, viola;
C. Warwick Evans, znolonccllo; and Gunnar Johansen, piano.
Sixth of a series of eight concerts of the chamber music of
Johannes Brahms. Auditorium, Memorial Hall. The public is
invited.
FRIDAY, JULY 25
4:15 p.m. Symposium. "Educational and Social Developments in Cer
tain South American Countries." Speakers: Nicanor Rivera
Caceres, Director, Institute of Educational Experimentation,
Lima, Peru; Agustin Nieto Cabellero, Rector, University of
Colombia, Bogota, Colombia; Rafael Escobar Lara, Director,
Pedagogical Institute, Caracas, Venezuela. Presented under
the auspices of the School of Education. Auditorium, Educa
tion Building. The public is invited.
SATURDAY, JULY 26
8:15 p.m. Ghosts, by Henrik Ibsen. Presented by the Dramatists' Alli
ance under the direction of Ralph Schram. Auditorium, Edu
cation Building. General admission, 40 cents.
SUNDAY, JULY 27
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Speaker: Frederick Anderson, Professor
of Romanic Languages. Memorial Church. All are welcome.
7:30 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist. Me
morial Church. The public is invited.
MONDAY, JULY 28
8:00 p.m. Lecture. "Heroic Sentiment," Margery Bailey, Associate Pro
fessor of English. Fifth of a series of seven lectures on
Tragedy presented under the auspices of the Dramatists' Alli
ance. Lower Lounge, Women's Clubhouse. The public is in
vited.
TUESDAY, JULY 29
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Illustrated lecture, "The Gala
pagos Islands—lsles of Enchantment," Victor Wolfgang von
Hagen. Auditorium, Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
WEDNESDAY, JULY 30
4:15 p.m. Lecture. "Washington and the States: Dominance or Co
operation." Leonard D. White. Acting Professor of Political
Science. Lower Lounge, Women's Clubhouse. The public is
invited.
EXHIBITS
University Library
Main corridors —"Fifty Years of Gifts and Endowment in the University
Libraries."
Charlotte Ashley Felton Memorial Library—"Alice in Wonderland."
Hopkins Transportation Library—"The Central Pacific Railroad."
Elmer Edzvin Robinson Collection—"The Constitution of the United
States."
Hoover Library on War, Revolution, and Peace
East Exhibit Room —"The Development of the Hoover Library on War,
Revolution, and Peace."
C.R.B. Room—"Relief Organizations Directed by Mr. Hoover during the
World War and Its Aftermath."
Lane Medical Library
Sacramento and Webster Streets, San Francisco—"The Development of
Anatomical Knowledge."
Graduate School of Business Library
"Pacific Coast Business from the 1850's to 1940."
School of Education Library
"The Development of Textbooks during Fifty Years."
Thomas Welton Stanford Art Gallery
Mortimer C. Leventritt Collection of Far Eastern and European Art.
(The Art Gallery is open to the public without charge from 10 :00 a.m.
to 5 :00 p.m., daily, including Sunday.)
Stanford Museum
The Stanford Family. (The Museum is open from 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.,
daily, including Sunday.)

TUESDAY, JULY 29

CONFERENCES
JULY 20-25
The Fifth Annual Stanford Business Conference sponsored by the Grad
uate School of Business. Inquiries may be addressed to the Graduate
School of Business, Stanford University.

to be included should be given in writing to Miss Mildred Worswick, Secretary to the Com
mittee on Public Exercises, 172 Administration Building, by 9:00 a.m. on Tuesday pre
ceding the week in which the event will occur. Mailing charge: $1.00 for the academic
year or 25 cents for the quarter. Subscriptions may be sent to the Secretary.
