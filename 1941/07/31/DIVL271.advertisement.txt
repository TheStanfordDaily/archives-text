EVENTS OF THE WEEK
THURSDAY, JULY 31
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist. Me
morial Church. The public is invited.
8:15 p.m. Thunder Rock, by Robert Ardrey. Presented by the Stanford
Players under the direction of F. Cowles Strickland. Audi
torium, Memorial Hall. Tickets, 50 cents. For reservations,
call P.A. 22041.
FRIDAY, AUGUST 1
8:15 p.m. Thunder Rock.
SATURDAY, AUGUST 2
8:15 p.m. Thunder Rock. (Final performance.)
SUNDAY, AUGUST 3
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Speaker: Edgar Eugene Robinson, Mar
garet Byrne Professor of American History. Memorial
Church. All are welcome.
7:30 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist. Me
morial Church. The public is invited.
MONDAY, AUGUST 4
4:15 p.m. Concert. Antonio Brosa, violin; C. Warwick Evans, zriolon
ccllo; Gunnar Johansen, piano. Seventh of a series of eight
concerts of the chamber music of Johannes Brahms. Audi
torium, Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Lecture. "Melodrama in America," Hubert C. Heffner, Pro
fessor of Dramatic Literature. Sixth of a series of seven
lectures on Tragedy presented under the auspices of the Dra
matists' Alliance. Lower Lounge, Women's Clubhouse. The
public in invited.
TUESDAY, AUGUST 5
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Balinese motion picture, Wajan;
titles in English. Auditorium, Memorial Hall. The public is
invited.
WEDNESDAY, AUGUST 6
4:15 p.m. Lecture. "Life and Fiction: a Demonstration," Vladimir
Nabokov, Lecturer in Slavic Languages. Lower Lounge,
Women's Clubhouse. The public is invited.
COMING EVENTS
THURSDAY, AUGUST 7
8:00 p.m. Concert. The Pro Arte Quartet of the University of Wis
consin: Antonio Brosa and Laurent Halleux, rnolins; Ger
main Prevost, znola; C. Warwick Evans, violoncello; with
Gunnar Johansen, piano and Alfred Brain, horn. Final con
cert of the chamber music of Johannes Brahms. Auditorium,
Memorial Hall. The public is invited.
