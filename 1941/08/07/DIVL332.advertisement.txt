Volume XCVIII August 7-13, 1941 Number 8
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
THURSDAY, AUGUST 7
4:15 p.m. Organ Recital. Herbert B. Nanney, Assistant University
Organist. Memorial Church. The public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Concert. The Pro Arte Quartet of the University of Wis
consin : Antonio Brosa and Laurent Halleux, violins; Ger
main Prevost, znola; C. Warwick Evans, violoncello; with
Maxim Schapiro, piano and Alfred Brain, horn. Final concert
of the chamber music of Johannes Brahms. Auditorium, Me
morial Hall. The public is invited.
SATURDAY, AUGUST 9
8:30 p.m. Cardinal Virtue, by Carter Kissell (1941 Etherege Award
Comedy). Presented in formal group reading by the Drama
tists' Alliance under the direction of Myna Brunton Hughes.
Auditorium, Education Building. General admission, 25 cents.
SUNDAY, AUGUST 7
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Speaker: Obert C. Tanner, Instructor in
Religion. Memorial Church. All are welcome.
7:30 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist. Me
morial Church. The public is invited.
MONDAY, AUGUST 11
8:00 p.m. Lecture. "The Technique of Modern Tragedy," Vladimir
Nabokov, Lecturer in Slavic languages. Last of a series of
seven lectures presented under the auspices of the Dramatists'
Alliance, Lower Lounge, Women's Clubhouse. The public is
invited.
TUESDAY, AUGUST 12
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Dramatic lecture-recital, "Shake
speare, Our Contemporary," Irene Tedrow, Auditorium, Me
morial Hall. The public is invited.
WEDNESDAY, AUGUST 13
4:15 p.m. Lecture. "Goethe and His Legacy to the Modern World,"
Karl Vietor, Acting Professor of German. Lower Lounge,
Women's Clubhouse. The public is invited.
COMING EVENTS
THURSDAY, AUGUST 14
4:15 p.m. Concert. Presented by the graduate students in the Division
of Music. Auditorium, Education Building. The public is in
vited.
SATURDAY, AUGUST 16
8:30 p.m. The Levite, by Agnes Irene Smith (1941 Maxwell Anderson
Award Verse Drama). Presented by the Dramatists' Alli
ance under the direction of Ralph Schram. Little Theater,
Memorial Hall. Tickets, 50 cents. For reservations, call P.A.
22041.
