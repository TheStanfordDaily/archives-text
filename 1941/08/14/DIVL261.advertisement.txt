STANFORD |®| UNIVERSITY
CALENDAR
Volume XCVIII August 14-20, 1941 Number 9
EVENTS OF THE WEEK
THURSDAY, AUGUST 14
4:15 p.m. Concert. Graduate students in the Division of Music pre
sented by the Division. Auditorium, Education Building. The
public is invited.
8:00 p.m. Illustrated Lecture. "The Form and Content of Baroque
Art," Parker Lesley, Assistant Professor of Fine Arts, Uni
versity of Minnesota. Presented under the auspices of The
Fine Arts departments (Graphic Arts, Music, Speech and
Drama). Auditorium, Education Building. The public is in
vited.
SATURDAY, AUGUST 16
8:30 p.m. The Levite, by Agnes Irene Smith (1941 Maxwell Anderson
Award Verse Drama). Presented by the Dramatists' Alliance
under the direction of Ralph Schram. Little Theater, Memo
rial Hall. Tickets, 50 cents. For reservations, call P.A. 22041.
SUNDAY, AUGUST 17
11:00 a.m. Church Service. Preacher: D. Elton Trueblood, Chaplain
of the University. Memorial Church. All are welcome.
7 :30 p.m. Organ Recital. Warren D. Allen, University Organist. Me
morial Church. The public is invited.
TUESDAY, AUGUST 19
8:00 p.m. Tuesday Evening Series. Lecture, "Defrosting Alaska,"
Karl Robinson. Illustrated with color motion pictures. Audi
torium, Memorial Hall. The public is invited. (Final pro
gram for the quarter. The series will be resumed on Octo
ber 7.)
WEDNESDAY, AUGUST 20
4:15 p.m. Lecture. "The Cretan Campaign of 1184 8.C.," George M.
Calhoun, Acting Professor of Classics. Lower Lounge,
Women's Clubhouse. The public is invited.
EXHIBITS
University Library
Main corridors—Stanford Dramatists and Novelists of the First Fifty
Years, arranged by the Dramatists' Alliance to illustrate its anniver
sary publication, Stanford Writers.
Charlotte Ashley Felton Memorial Library—"Alice in Wonderland."
Hopkins Transportation Library—"The Central Pacific Railroad."
Elmer Edwin Robinson Collection—"The Constitution of the United
States."
Hoover Library on War, Revolution, and Peace
East Exhibit Room—"The Development of the Hoover Library on War,
Revolution, and Peace."
C.R.B. Room —"Relief Organizations Directed by Mr. Hoover during the
World War and Its Aftermath."
Lane Medical Library
Sacramento and Webster Streets, San Francisco—"The Development of
Anatomical Knowledge."
Graduate School of Business Library
"Pacific Coast Business from the 1850's to 1940."
School of Education
Left Corridor, First Floor—Costumes and Designs of Hillbarn and Drama
tists' Alliance Costumes and Plates.
Thomas Welton Stanford Art Gallery
Mortimer C. Leventritt Collection of Far Eastern and European Art.
(The Art Gallery is open to the public without charge from 10:00 a.m.
to 5 :00 p.m., daily, including Sunday.)
Stanford Museum
The Stanford Family. (The Museum is open from 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.,
daily, including Sunday.)
ANNOUNCEMENTS'
THURSDAY, AUGUST 14
2:00 p.m. Dramatists' Alliance, Ram's Head Room, Memorial Hall.
Final session on the Children's Theater, directed by Mrs. Hazel
Robertson, Lecturer in Dramatic Art. Admission, 50 cents.
FRIDAY, AUGUST 15
2:00 p.m. Dramatists' Alliance, Upper Lounge, Women's Clubhouse.
Last of a series of three lectures on Playwriting by Ransome
Rideout, Professor of Playwriting, Los Angeles City College.
Admission, 50 cents.
8.00 p.m. Dramatists' Alliance, Auditorium, Women's Clubhouse. Dis
cussion of the Problems of the Community Theater. Speakers:
Arthur Hicks, Professor of English, Western Washington
College of Education; Margery Bailey, Associate Professor
of English, Stanford University; Gail Plummer, Manager,
University Auditorium, University of Utah. Open to the
public.
MONDAY, AUGUST 18
2 :00 p.m. Oral examination of Stanley Eugene Rauch for the degree of
Doctor of Philosophy, Room 308, Library. Major, Mathe
matics ; Minor, Physics.
CONFERENCES
AUGUST 9-16 .
Dramatists' Assembly, a week of performances, sessions on playwriting,
children's theater, and problems of the community theater held undor the
auspices of the Dramatists' Alliance. Inquiries may be addressed to Drama
tists' Alliance, Stanford University.
AUGUST 18-22
Third Annual Series of Lectures and Discussions on Public Relations
sponsored by the American Council on Public Relations. Inquiries may
be addressed to Rex F. Harlow, Director, American Council on Public
Relations, 365 Guinda Street, Palo Alto.
