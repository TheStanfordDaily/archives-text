# Italians Say F.D.R. Speech 'War Talk'
## 
### 
ROME, Oct. H (UP). • — Usually
well-informed Italian quarters to
night interpreted President Roose
velt's message to Congress as the
next-to-the-last, step before an ac
tual declaration of war and an effort
to convince Americans that United
States entry was inevitable.
Referring to the President's men
tion of American ships going wher
ever they please, they said the Ital
ian and German navies would he
prepared and would "act accord
ingly."
