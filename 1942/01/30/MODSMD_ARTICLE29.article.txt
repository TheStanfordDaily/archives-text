# Nash, Noted Theologian Talks Sunday
## 
### 
Arnold Nash of the Church School
of Divinity, Berkeley, will deliver the
sermon at the morning service Sun
day in Memorial Church at 11 a.m.
Mr. Nash is a British scholar from
the University of London, where he
held the job of secretary of the Stu
dent Christian Movement, in Great
Britain. He is soon to publish a
hook on "The Modern University in
the Modern World," and will base
his talk Sunday on this subject. At
present he is teaching at the Divin
ity School in Berkeley.
Dr. D. Elton Trueblood, Univer
sity chaplain, will conduct the serv
ice, and the University Choir di
rected by Warren D. Allen, Univer
sity organist, will sing. The prelude
will be Adagio from the Concerto in
A minor by Bach to be given by
Mrs. Helen Atkinson, violinist.
"Blest Are They" by Bach will be
the introit, and the anthem, sung
by the choir, will be "A Hymn of
Freedom" by Thiman.
At 4 p.m. Sunday Dr. Allen will
present an organ recital in the
Church.
