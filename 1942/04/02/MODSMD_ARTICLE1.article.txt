# Easter Rites Will Be Held This Sunday
## Interfaith Council To Sponsor Program In Outdoor Theater 
### 
Stanford University will observe
Easter tbis Sunday with a sunrise
service to be presented by the In
terfaith Council at H a.m. in the
Frost Amphitheater, and a special
church service at 11 a.m. in the
Memorial Church.
At the sunrise service, music will
be offered under the direction of
Lucille Fox Green, director of the
Palo Alto High School A Cappclla
choir, and will include the Bach
Chorale, a Dett anthem, and the
"Hymn of Peace," a chorale bene
diction, by Congdon.
Ray Aldon, president of the Inter
faith Council, will preside; those
taking part include Bill Cowdcn,
president of the senior class who is
substituting for A.S.S.U. President
George Jackson, and Pat Adams,
vice-president of the student body.
PARSONS TO PREACH
The call to worship will be given
by Dr. D. Elton Trueblood, chaplain
of the University. The Rt. Rev
erend Edward L. Parsons, Episcopal
bishop, will deliver the sermon. He
is well known here, having offiicated
at many University services. All are
urged to attend the sunrise service,
and blankets are advised as the
grass will be damp.
The church service music will be
presented by the Stanford Univer
sity Band under the direction of
Frederick Preston Search. The band
will play for the Introit, "God's Son
in Triumph Rose Today," and for
the anthems. The sermon will be
given by Dr. D. Elton Trueblood.
The regular portions of the service
will be given as usual.
CARILLON CONCERT
The Carillon will also give special
music for Easter. There will be a
concert at 7 a.m. before the sunrise
service and there will also be a
concert at 10:30 before the regular
church service.
Various living groups are plan
ning to observe the day. Lagunita
will hold its annual Eastern break
fast after the sunrise service.
