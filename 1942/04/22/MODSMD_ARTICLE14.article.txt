# Students Hear Election Talks
## 
### 
The 111 candidates who will com
prise today's student body election
ballot were introduced to the stu
dents by A.S.S.U. President George
Jackson in yesterday's all-campus
election assembly.
After introducing the candidates,
Jackson invited the candidates for
A.S.S.U. President to make brief
speeches.
A1 Haas, El Campo, a candidate
for A.S.S.U. President, said:
"I believe that everything we do
in the coming year should be guided
by the war effort. We will have to
make sacrifices, but. it is important
that wc preserve the Stanford per
sonality during the war."
SHELLEY PARKER
Shelley Parker, Delt, a candidate
for A.S.S.U. President, said:
"Our most important goal is to
help our country in every way pos
sible. We probably won't have to
change our student government or
ganization, but we must preserve its
flexibility."
Norm Ross, Fiji, a candidate for
A.S.S.U. president, said:
"In choosing your candidates, it.
doesn't matter about row or hall
affiliations. What does matter is
which candidate has the time, the
interest, and the capabilities to do
the best job."
ED ST AMM
Ed Stamm, Zete, a candidate for
A.S.S.U. president, said:
"You should select an A.S.S.U.
president with a practical outlook on
activities. He should have a common
sense approach to the problems which
will arise under a student govern
ment on a wartime basis."
The candidates for the office of
A.S.S.U. vice-president also spoke
briefly, stating their campaign
policies.
