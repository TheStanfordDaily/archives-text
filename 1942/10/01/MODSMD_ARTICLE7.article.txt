# Student Co-op Will Liquidate
## Sellout May Be Due To Money Thefts 
### 
Bill Moran, A.S.S.U. Student Man
ager, last night announced the times
at which the Co-op Bookstore will
be open next week. In addition to
being open from 1 to 5 p.m. today
and tomorrow, the bookstore will be
open from 1 to 5 p.m. every day from
Monday through Friday of next
week.
The week after next, in accord
ance with Excom instructions, the
bookstore will be open from 4 to
5 p.m. from Monday through Friday
in order that all textbooks which the
Co-op has not yet sold may be
claimed by their owners.
"After 5 p.m., Oct. 16," Moran said,
"all books remaining at the Exchange
will become the property of the
A.S.S.U." Moran, as Student Man
ager, will then dispose of these books
and all the property of the Co-op
which remains at the best price he
can obtain, and the money received
will be kept on deposit with the
A.S.S.U. to take care of any claims
brought against the bookstore which
the Student 'Manager, thinks justi
fied.
Excom gave its official stamp of
approval to this plan last night after
Moran gave his report on the state
of the Co-op's finances and organi
zation. In his report Moran pointed
out that several large thefts from the
Co-op's funds had put the book
store in a precarious position. For
this reason the A.S.S.U. is liquidat
ing the Co-op's present large stock
of University textbooks and will ac
cept no more books to sell at the
bookstore.
