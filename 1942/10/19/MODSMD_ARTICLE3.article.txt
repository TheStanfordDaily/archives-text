# G.E. Jobs Open For Farm Men
## 
### 
A representative of General Elec
tric will interview men trained in
engineering and physics who are in
terested in the company's work today
and tomorrow according, to Miss
Adelaide Palmer of the Appointment
Service.
Interviews are still being sched
uled through the appointment serv
ice in the Administration Building
for those men interested in following
some field in the G. E. Co.
Future interviews will be avail
able with representatives of Various
other companies. Senior and grad
uate students who are trained in all
branches of mechanical engineering,
or in physics and chemistry should
register for future interviews, par
ticularly if they are available for
employment after graduation.
Miss Palmer recommended that
men entering the armed forces should
also register for purposes of record
so that the appointment service may
be of assistance to them in finding
private employment after the war.
