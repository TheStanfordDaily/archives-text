# Space Available On L.A. Trains
## New Daylight Is Run For Civilian Travel 
### 
Reservations are still available on
Daylight Los Angeles trains for
students this vacation. Southern Pa
cific announced yesterday. Begin
ning Sunday, a newly scheduled
train to Los Angeles will leave Palo
Alto at 2:20 p.m. daily, and will ar
rive in Los Angeles at 6:30 a.m. the
following day.
This means that persons holding
reservations on trains Nos. 72 and 71
that formerly left Palo Alto at 8:32
a.m. will be transferred to the new
schedule. This train has been added
to provide transportation for civil
ians unable to make reservations on
the regular Daylight, which has been
largely appropriated by the armed
services.
Due to scarcity of space, advance
reservations are required for chair
and pullman cars until Jan. 5.
Students retaining seats on the
regular morning Daylight from San
Jose have been advised of a six
minute schedule change on the con
necting train which will leave Palo
Alto at 8:26 a.m.
The new Daylight train leaving
Los Angeles at 1:20 p.m. daily will
arrive at Palo Alto at 5:15 a.m. the
following day.
These schedule changes have been
made to accommodate soldiers, sail
ors, and marines on Christmas leave.
Although the armed forces come
first, the Southern Pacific is stretch
ing its facilities to meet civilian
needs.
