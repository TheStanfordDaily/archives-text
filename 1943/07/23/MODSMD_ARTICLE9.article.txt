# WEEKEND SCHEDULE
## 
### 
TONIGHT—London String Quar
tet. Concert begins at 8 p.m. in
the Memorial Theater. (See page
one.)
WAA co-recreational affair—From
7:30 to 11:30 p.m. at the Women's
Gym.
TOMORROW NIGHT—"HaIf and
Half" Dance. At Tyler Hall from
9 to 1 o'clock.
Informal dance—At the Com
munity Center from 3 to 12 p.m.
Price, 28 cents.
SUNDAY—RobIe open house—
Soldiers invited from 3 to 5 p.m.
Guide Tour—From 3 to 5 p.m.
Meet in front of Memorial Church.
Church Service—Thomas Oliver.
Memorial Church. 11 a.m.
Student-Faculty Forum — Lagu
nita, 4:45 p.m.

The second program by the Lon
don String Quartet will be presented
tonight at 8 p.m. 'in the Memorial
Theater free of charge.
'Half and Half'
Tomorrow night the unique '"Half
and Half" theme of Tyler Hall's cos
tume dance from 9 to 1 o'clock will
be carried out by costumes which
will be combinations of conflicting
styles.
"For an idea of the proper cos
tume." house representatives said.
"Imagine a person wearing a white
tie. tails, and ballet skirt. Your cos
tumes may join down the middle,
around the waist, or be just .i con
glomerate mixture."
Record music will be provided for
dancing, while refreshments will also
be served. Everyone is invited to
attend this date affair. Company B
of the STAR unit and Company H
of the ASTU have been invited spe
cially.
Costume Dance
From 9 to 12 tomorrow night at
the Community Center there will be
a no-date dance with a Hawaiian
motive. Price is 28 cents. To reach
the Community Center take the
Stanford bus to the railroad station,
then transfer to the Bayshore bus,
which stops at the Center.
Sunday Service
Sunday morning a service of spe
cial interest will be given at the Me
morial Church at 11 a.m. Mr. Thomas
Oliver of Ojai. Cal., will deliver the
sermon.
Starting from the front of the Me
morial Church at 3 p.m. on Sunday,
a guide tour for all soldiers stationed
on campus is planned. The group
will go through the Memorial
Church, the Museum, and to the
Hoover War Library. There small
groups will be taken to the top to
see the view.
At the same time on Sunday from
3 to 5 p.m. Roble Hall has invited
soldiers from three companies to an
open house.
Plans for entertainment include
volleyball, badminton, ping-pong,
and dancing. Refreshments will be
served. Bev Andreucetti is in charge
of the affair.

'Half and Half'
Tomorrow night the unique '"Half
and Half" theme of Tyler Hall's cos
tume dance from 9 to 1 o'clock will
be carried out by costumes which
will be combinations of conflicting
styles.
"For an idea of the proper cos
tume." house representatives said.
"Imagine a person wearing a white
tie. tails, and ballet skirt. Your cos
tumes may join down the middle,
around the waist, or be just .i con
glomerate mixture."
Record music will be provided for
dancing, while refreshments will also
be served. Everyone is invited to
attend this date affair. Company B
of the STAR unit and Company H
of the ASTU have been invited spe
cially.
