# Congress Group Charges Waste of U.S. Personnel
## 
### 
WASHINGTON, Nov. 21. — The
joint congressional economy com
mittee, charging wasteful and ex
travagant use of federal personnel,
tonight demanded immediate dis
charge of at least 300,000 government
workers and advocated a survey of
all departments and agencies to pro
mote fuller utilization of manpower.
Its second report on government
personnel, submitted by Sen. Harry
F. Byrd (D., Va.), said September
figures, the latest available, show
2.964,405 paid civilian employees on
the payroll—"more than necessary
for the successful and effective
proscution of the war." It said 52.2
j per cent "are not engaged in direct,
j mechanical war production."
