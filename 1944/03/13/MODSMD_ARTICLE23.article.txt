# War Board and Y Plan Conference Saturday
## 
### 
will be discussed at a conference sponsored by the Stanford War Board
and the Y.W.C.A. next Saturday, March 18, from 1:30 to 4:30 p.m. in the
Women's Clubhouse, according to Carol Hurwitz, War Board chairman.
The conference will include a discussion of "Y" and War Board activities,

and will be open to all women stu
dents.
Invitations have been sent to rep
resentatives of the "Y" and War
Boards at the University of Califor
nia, Mills College, U.S.F.. San Jose
State, and the College of Holy
Names. Members of the faculty of
some of these schools will speak.
The temporary program for the
afternoon has been announced as
follows: from 1:30 to 2:30 an intro
duction will be given on the subject
of "The Effect of the War on
Women."
Open to All
The rest of the discussions will be
open to all attending and have been
divided into two interest groups,
campus war activities and postwar
responsibilities.
At some time during the confer
ence the War Board chairmen from
the different schools will meet to
discuss the over-all problems of the
organization of student cooperation,
general attitudes, and plans for a
future War Board conference.
USO Activities
From 2:30 to 3:30 the following
war activities will be discussed:
salvage and entertainment of serv
icemen, including ASTP. Navy, Ma
rines, and USO.
Postwar problems to be discussed
will be: political responsibility of
women and financial problems of the
modern woman.
Ked Cross Work
From 3:30 to 4:30 discussions under
the heading of war activities will in
clude: stamps and bonds and Red
Cross work, including bandage roll
ing and blood banks. The effect of
the war on family life and problems
of young career girls will be the
subjects of the concluding postwar
discussions.
Refreshments will be served at the
close of the conference.
