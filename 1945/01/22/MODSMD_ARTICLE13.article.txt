# Nominations End Today at 5 P.M.
## 
### 
Petitions will be available today at the English and Engineering Corners
to enable students to make nominations for the positions ol president of
Women's Conference; vice-president of Women's Conference; members of
Women's Council; members of Men's Council; president of the Freshmen
Class, and secretary of the Freshmen Class.

Petitions must be signed by at
least 15 people and be filed between
8 a.m. and 5 p.m. today. All declina
tions must be in the box on the Law
School steps between 8 a.m. and 2
p.m. tomorrow.
Students must have a bawlout av
erage of at least C to be eligible for
office. Moreover, women running
for positions on Women's Council
must have at least 102 units, while
men must have at least 90 units to
be eligible to serve on Men's Coun
cil. President of Women's Confer
ence must be of senior standing, and
nominees for freshmen offices may
not have more than 29 units.
Wednesday, election booths will be
placed on English Corner, Engineer
ing Corner, and on the west side of
the Chapel on Inner Quad. Voting
will be between 8 a.m. and 2 p.m.
Election rules, as revised by the
Election Board and passed by Excom,
include: No use of written campaign
material outside of that published in
the Daily; no expenditure of cam
paign money, and no campaigning
within 50 feet of the polls. Ballots
will not be accepted if there is more
than one "X" after each name.
Only women may vote for the posi
tions on their Council and Confer
ence and only men for those on
Men's Council. Freshmen will vote
for their own candidates.
