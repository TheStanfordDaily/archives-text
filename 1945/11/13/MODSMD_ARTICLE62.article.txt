# ‘Ike’ Home For Holiday
## 
### 
BOSTON, Nov. 12 (UP). —Gen.
Dwight D. Eisenhower returned to
the United States today for the sec
ond time since Allied victory in Eu
rope and received a great ovation
from more than 1,000,000 persons
who turned out in the rain to see
him lead an Armistice Day parade.
Though pea-soup fog and a chill
drizzle necessitated cancellation of
all commercial airlines flights, the
Supreme Allied Commander's huge
C-54 transport plane—with seven
high-point G.l.'s aboard — landed
without difficulty at the Squantum
naval airbase. It had taken off yes
terday from Frankfurt, Germany,
and arrived here via Bermuda.
