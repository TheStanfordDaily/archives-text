# Ram's Head Seeks Plays
## 
### 
Deadline for entries in the Ram's
I Head one-act play contest is noon
! on Jan. 1G when all original manu
| scripts must bo turned in at the
I office of the Dramatic Council in
j the Old Union building or to any
! of the directors, announced Murray
j Stirton, assistant director.
Tryouts for Parts
Try-outs for parts in the winning
plays as well as for positions on the
technical crew will be held on Jan.
10 and 17 from 7 to 10 p.m. Janice
Swan, director, and Lorna Robert
son, Pat Neilson, and Murray Stir
ton, her assistants, will officiate at
ihe try-outs.
Positions open on the technical
crew will include such jobs as set
construction, make-up and cos
tumes. Marie Jo McGervey, tech
nical director, will supervise this
phase of the winter show.
Traditional Production
This traditional winter produc
tion has been held since 1938, suc
ceeding the earlier three-acts given
by the Masquers, fore-runner of the
j Ram's Head Society. In 1939 a
record group of 32 plays were sub-
I mittcd in the contest.
A highlight in previous produc
tions was a play in 1942 which fea
tured Ted Marcuse. who will soon
! be seen as Petruchio in "The Tam
ing of the Shrew." In 1944 the two
i prize plays were "Coast to Coast"
; by Bill Rose, now an instructor in
i the Stanford English Department,
and "Death in the Limelight" by
i Peggy Fitz, one of the Gaieties stars.
