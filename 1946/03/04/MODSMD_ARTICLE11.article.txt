# Room Change Requests Due
## 
### 
Wom<n planning to change resi
dence next quarter will fill out res
idence choice cards in Miss Doyle's
office tomorrow from 8:30 to 12 a.m.
and from 1:30 to 5 p.m.
Sponsors and members of former
sorority groups who have requested
former residences have been
placed, Miss Doyle said. There are
34 undergraduate women now in
residence who remain in the draw
ing for a residence change. There
are also 31 girls from Redwoods
to be placed. Twenty women now
living off-campus have entered the
competition for full admission and
campus residence.
Since girls on the list to move
have given up their present rooms,
the number of vacancies has been
increased to 83. They are distrib
uted as follows:
Adelfa, 0; Branner, 10; Cubber
ley, 1; Durand, 1; Elm, 2; Eucalypto,
3; Granada. 4; Guthrie, 0: Haw
thorne, 0; Hurlburt, 0; Jordan, 0:
Lathrop, 0: Madrono, 1; Magnolia,
6; Manzanita, G; Mariposa, 2; Na
ranja, 0; Oaks, 1; Olivo, 1; Roble,
24; Roth. 0; Russell. 0: Storey, 4;
Union Residence, 8; and Casa Ven
tura, 8.
In houses in which there are four
or more vacancies, the ratio will be
half Upper Division and half Lower
Division.
