# Indian Netmen Sweep Santa Clara Matches
## 
### 
Sweeping nine out of ten matches, Stanford's netmen virtually blasted
J Santa Clara off the courts yesterday. Despite the fact that Stanford's
j tennis players were forced to move up a notch in singles competition
! due to the fact that Arnold Beisser, top Indian tennis star, had to M
■ tend classes, they copped six out of the seven singles matches.

| In the first match, Bub Rutledge,
i second ranking Indian player, dis-
I played a brilliant net game, and
after dropping the first match to j
top-seeded Roy Oaks of Santa Clara
j 4-6, he solved Oaks' deep - court
style and went on to win the final
two sets in easy fashion with the j
scores of 6-1 and 6-0.
Dave Basham defeated Bronco
Glenn Harris 6-3 and 6-4 in a hard
-1 fought contest. Basham's beautiful
i baseline game and his cannonball
i serves overpowered his Santa Clara
opponent.
Other singles matches saw Gro-
I ver Miller of Stanford defeat Jim
Gallagher in tin most exciting game
of the afternoon by the scores of
6-8, 6-2, 7-5. Roy Hughes of Stan
, ford defeated Juan Holm 5-7, 6-2.
6-3. Herle of Santa Clara defeated
Heath Schroeder 6-3, 6-3. Frost of
the Indians trounced Anderson 6-3,
6-2, and Stanford's Carman set back
Gray 6-4, 6-2.
In the doubles matches, Beiser

teamed up with Grover Miller to
win a seesaw game 6-1, 4-6, 6-1.
Beiser demonstrated his exceptional
control as he consistently placed his
shots on the opposite chalkmarks.
Beiser's disconcerting habit of look
ing in one direction and slamming
the ball in another direction un
nerved his opponents considerably.
Basham and Rutledge put on a co
ordinated offense to sweep past
Oaks and erle 6-3, 6-0. Basham's
great baseling game fitted in per
fectly with Rutledge's forecourt ex
pertness.
In the last match, Schroeder and
Hughes scored a decisive 6-1, 6-1
triumph.
