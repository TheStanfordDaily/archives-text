# Farm Net Squad Beats San Jose
## 
### 
Continuing an impressive early
season form, the Stanford varsity
tennis squad disposed of the San
Jose State Spartans, 8-1, on the
local courts yesterday. The Indian
netters rest today in preparation
for Friday's conference debut
against the powerful U.C.L.A. Bru
ins.
San Jose fought valiantly to stem
the Stanford tide but to no avail.
Sargent's third - place singles win
over Cardinal Roy Hughes, 7-9, 6-2,
6-2, stood as the lone Spartan tri
umpn.
Bob Rutledge fell heir to number
one seeding and proceeded to
trounce McKenzie of San Jose, 6-4,
6-3. Continuing the onslaught,
Dave Basham emerged the victor
over Franco, 6-2, 6-4.
Gordon Miller's 6-4, 6-1 win over
Landess furthered the Stanford
cause. In the fifth slot Morton of
San Jose fell prey to Indian Bob
Keenan in a rapid 6-4, 6-3 match.
Jack Frost of Stanford polished off
Payne, 6-3, 6-3, to climax singles
play for the afternoon.
In the feature meeting of the day,
the Farm doubles combo of Basham
and Rutledge rallied from apparent
defeat to whip McKenzie and Fran
co of San Jose, 5-7, 6-0, 8-6. Trail
ing 5-4 in the third set, Stanford
pulled the match out of the fire.
Arnold Boisser, Indian ace, made
a brief court appearance in the sec
ond doubles as he teamed with Roy
Hughes to trounce Landess and
Sargent, 6-1, 6-2. The long after
noon came to a close as Frost and

Keenan of Stanford crushed Spar
tans Morton and Payne, 7-5, 6-2.
