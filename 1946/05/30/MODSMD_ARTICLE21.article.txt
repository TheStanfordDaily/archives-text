# Harper's Editor to Address Amphitheater Commencement
## 
### 
Commencement exorcises June 16 at 4:30 p.m. in the Frost Amphithe
ater will climax the activities of the senior class. The speaker will be
Oscar Veto, editor of Harper's Magazine.
Highlight of the final three-day program will he the formal ball and
breakfast June 14, followed by the traditional pilgrimage of commemora-

tion to the tomb of Stanford's
founders, June 15 at 11 a.m.
At 11:15 ft the same day the dedi
cation of the class plate will take
place in front of the Memorial
Church. From 12 to 1:30 the class
will hold an outdoor program and
luncheon on the terraces of the
.Memorial Theater.
Marie Jo MeGervy and Lorna
Robertson have planned the Clas.s
Day exercises which will be held in
the Cubberley Auditorium at 2 p.m.
The day's activities will closc with
a reception for members of the
senior class at the home of Dr. and
Mrs. Donald Tresidder at 4 p.m.
Dr. Theodore Green, visiting
professor of humanities, will speak
at the Baccalaureate exercises in
the church June IK at 11 a.m.
Music for the service will be pro
vided by a choir made up of stu
dents remaining for the summer
session, and musical members of
the faculty stall and their wives.
Rehearsal will be held June 13 ;it
8 p.m. in the Choir Room. All
who would like to join the chu
ms are asked to give their names
to Dr. Warren D. Allen or notify
the music office, 259.
