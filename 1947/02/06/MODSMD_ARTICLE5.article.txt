# BULLETINS
## Hope for Future? 
### 
WASHINGTON, Feb. S.—(UP) —
President Truman told Congress to
day that the United Nations is a
going concern, but he warned that
justifiable hope and confidence in
the organization still "can be be
trayed and lost."
In his first report on UN activi
ties in 194(5—a 30,000 word docu
ment which emphasized that the
major problem of atomic energy and
its control still was unsettled —Mr.
Truman reviewed all phases of UN
activity. He pointed with pride to
United States leadership in all de
partments, warned of international
pitfalls ahead, but expressed over
all hope for the future of the World
Peace Organization.
