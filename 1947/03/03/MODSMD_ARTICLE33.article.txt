# UN Reformers To Meet Here
## 
### 
Stanford Faculty chapter of the
Citizens Committee for United Na
tions Reform has invited all inter
ested faculty members to a discus
sion Wednesday night at 7:30 in the
Men's Faculty Clubhouse.
"This organization," said B. Q.
Morgan, chairman of the meeting, of
relatively recent foundation pro
poses to meet the challenge of the
immediate future, without preju
dice to desirable long-range plans
for world betterment, by offering
three amendments to the Charter
of the United Nations:
1. Establishment of an effective
world authority, able to act by ma
jority vote (not veto) to prevent ag
gressive war and preparation for
such a war.
2. Control of the atomic threat
and prevention of armament for ag
gression.
3. Establishment of an effective
police force, powerful enough to
suppress aggression by major states.
