# IDG Talks On France
## 
### 
"France Today" will be the topic
discussed at the International Dis
cussion Group Wednesday at 7 p.m.
at Russell House.
Speakers for the evening include
Josie cle St. Blanquet, Lawrence
Grannis, and Bill Farrer.
Miss St. Blanquet comes from
France where she was a student at
the Sorbonne and was active in the
underground resistance during the
war. She recently transferred to
Stanford from Ml. Holyoke College
There she attended on a French
government scholarship.
Lawrence Grannis is a Phi Beta
Kappa, a member of the champion
debating squad that toured the
country a few years ago, and is now
in law school. Grannis will travel
to France this summer if the I.S.C.
is approved this week.
Bill Farrer was student body pres
ident of U.C.LA. in 1942-43, chair
man of the College Groups for War
ren in 1946, aide-de-camp for the
Commanding General of the 33rd
Division in the Philippines, and has
conducted tours for Secretary of
War Patterson, the House Naval
Affairs Committee, and a Russian
mission in Japan. Farrer is also a
law student and will travel to
France if the I.S.C. is approved.
