# 27 Vacancies On Rally Com; Tryouts Today
## 
### 
Tryouts for Rally Com will be
held today, tomorrow, and Friday
from 2 to 5 p.m. in room 13 of the
Old Union, announced Dave Hcyler,
newly appointed co - chairman of
Rally Com. The committee is being
organized now to make plans for
rallies and card stunts during the
football season fall quarter.
Bonnie Hanson, present Chairman
of Rally Com, urged all those who
have had any experience in work
ing with football games and rallies
to attend the interviews.
"Those who would be willing to
do manual labor, get up early Sat
urday mornings, and still have a lot
of fun are the type we need to form
next year's Rally Com," Miss Han
son said.
Heyler emphasized that tryoutees
need a "C average. Students with
artistic ability, card stunt ideas, and
rally ideas are all wanted to carry
out the functions of Rally Com, he
said.
Tryoutees will be interviewed by
Miss Hanson and by Barbara Carter
and Dave Heyler, newly appointed
co-chairmen. All the present mem
bers of the committee must try out
again for fall quarter.
According to Miss Carter there
are 15 positions' open on Rally Com
for men, 10 of which will be filled
by sophomores, and 12 positions
open for women, eight of which will
be filled by sophomores. The re
maining positions are open to either
freshmen or juniors. The only sen
iors eligible for Rally Com member
ship are the co-chairmen.
The main work of Rally Com is
during fall quarter when members
organize and put on rallies, work
out card stunts, usher at games, and
arrange rooting sections. Other ac
tivities of Rally Com are the Spring
Sing and the nomination of yell
leaders.
