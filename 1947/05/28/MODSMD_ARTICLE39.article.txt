# Annual WAA Field Day to Feature Archery, Tennis, Swimming, Dance
## 
### 
The annual WAA Field Day, sponsored by the Women's Physical Edu
cation Department, will be tomorrow at the Women's Gym, according to
Shirley Smith, WAA publicity manager.
There will be an archery exhibition with ten competitors at 2 p.m.
Beginners will participate in a Junior Columbia (24 arrows from 20-30-

40-yards), and the advanced archers
will shoot 48 from 60 yards. There
will also be a balloon shoot, accord
ing to Mrs. Margaret Barr.
Jerry Jackson will meet Betsy
Dunlap at 3:15 p.m. on the women's
courts to decide the winner in the
finals of the All-University women's
tennis singles.
At 4:15 p.m. competition in the
swimming meet will begin in the
women's pool with races in the 25-
yard freestyle, medley race, 50-yard
breaststroke, 50-yard back crawl,
25-yard backstroke, 100-yard crawl,
and 100-yard relay. Participants
will be: Joyce Pabst, Mary Wilbur,
Peggy Smith, Mary Kennedy, Ven
etia Taft, Shirley Coddington, and
Lydia Sinclaii'.
Concluding the Field Day will be
a dance exhibition at 5 p.m., which
will combine Mrs. Miriam Lidster's
classes in square and folk dancing
and the Hoedowners.
