NEWPORT, R. 1., Aug. 17 (UP).— |
In one of the season's most startling
■ upsets, Frank Parker of Los Angeles
| and Gardnar Mulloy of Coral Gables,
j Flarf trounced U.S. Davis cup cham-
I pions Ted Schroeder of Glendale,
Cal., and Jack Kramer of Monticello,
Cal., 3-6, 6-3, 6-3, 6-1, today to win
the doubles championship of the
i Newport Casino's 28th annual invi
| lution tournament.
Earlier, Schroeder had won the
! tourney's singles championship by
defeating William F. Talbert of New
i York and Wilmington, Del., 4-6, 6-3,
| 7-5, 6-3. He became the new holder
of the Mrs. Barger Wallach challenge
cup, succeeding Mulloy as champion.
In the semi-final round of the dou
bles. tourney, Mulloy and Frank
Parker of Los Angeles smothered
j Bob Falkenberg of Hollywood and
Tom Brown, Jr., of San Francisco,
6-0, 6-2, 6-3.

NEW YORK. Aug. 17 (UP).—For
the second straight year, a powerful
team of United States women tennis
players completed a 7-0 sweep of its
Wight man cup matches with Great
Britain by winning the remaining
three singles and one doubles con
tests today.
Rounding out America's 15th tri
umph in the series of 19 Wightman
meetings, the three top U.S. amateur
stars registered singles victories —
Margaret Osborne of San Francisco;
Louise Brough of Beverly Hills,
Calif.; and Doris Hart of Miami,
Fla.
Slender, brown-haired Miss Hart
registered today's first victory, beat
ing husky, brown-haired Mrs. Betty
Hilton of Birmingham, England, 4-6.
6-3, 7-5, before a small gallery of
500 who sat in the ivy-mantled For
est Hills stadium beneath leaden,
rain-threatening skies.
