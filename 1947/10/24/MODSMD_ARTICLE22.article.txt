# 'Journalists' To Visit Farm
## 
### 
High school journalists from ap
proximately 51 schools will gather
at Stanford Saturday for the 24th
annual convention of the Central
California Scholastic Press Associa
tion, it was announced early this
week by Dr. Chilton R. Bush, head
of Stanford's School of Journalism.
Tomorrow at 9:30 a.m. the visi
tors will meet in the Education Au
ditorium to hear welcoming ad
dresses by Dr. Bush, Division of
Journalism; John Turnblad, Sigma
Delta Chi; Nancy Bannick, Theta
Sigma Phi; and C. Russell Collier,
Alpha Delta Sigma. The addresses
will be followed by the showing of
a color movie, "Magazine Magic."
Panel discussions, dealing with the
problems of high school journal
ism, will be held from 10:45 to 11:45,
and a news-writing contest will be
held in room 53 of the Education
Building.
Saturday afternoon there will be
another general meeting in the Ed
ucation Auditorium, with an ad
dress by Dr. Bush and discussions
led by Professor D. W. Miller and
Thomas M. Nowell. Sigma Delta Chi
will then conduct the visiting jour
nalists on a tour of the campus.
Sigma Delta Chi and Theta Sigma
Phi will sponsor a jolly-up in the
Nitery at 4:15, while visiting advis
i ers will be served refreshments in
the lower lounge of the Women's
Clubhouse.
