# Frosh Classbook Out November 15
## 
### 
The first postwar edition of the
Freshman Classhook will be avail
able about Nov. 15, according to
Ted Off, managing editor of the
publication.
The names, home towns, and pic
tures of nearly 1,000 freshman men
and women will be included. They
will be classified into five residence
groups: Village, Encina, Branner,
Roble, and off-campus. This year's
edition will contain 24 pages.
It is mot too late to place an order
for the classbook. It may be ob
tained for one dollar per copy at the
Quad office. Any freshman who
has not returned his proofs should
do so at once.
