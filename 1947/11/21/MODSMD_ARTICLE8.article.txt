# Living Groups To Entertain Grads, Friends
## 
### 
In the spirit of Homecoming,
Stanford's living groups are plan
ning open houses and buffet lunch
eons for tomorrow, inviting friends
and old graduates to visit and eat
before going to the game.
Branner, Union, Lagunita, Roble,
and Women's Row will hold open
house from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m. for
grads and friends of the present
residents. The old-timers have been
sent invitations to return to their
former haunts to take a nostalgic
look around.
Luncheon will be served from
11:30 to 12:30, and tickets may l>c
purchased at meal time or in ad
vance by the women having guests.
The price is 75 cents.
Encina, Toyon, and Village will
also open the dining room doors
under a similar arrangement. En
cina open house will be held from
11 a.m. to 1 p.m. Toyon has no or
ganized open - house hours, but
alumni and friends will be welcome
to drop around.
The Village will follow a sched
ule similar to that of the women's
dorms. Open house will be held
from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m. Luncheon
will be served from 11 to 12:30, and
dinner from 5 to 6:30. The Village
Council hopes the alumni will fori
free to drop around and see the
new addition to the "Stanford Fam
ily"
The University dining halls will
all follow the same menu, offering
assorted salads, Italian spaghetti
v/ith cheese, French bread, relishes,
ice cream and cake, coffee, tea, and
milk. The public dining room at
Union will be open at noon, and in
the evening from 6 to 7. The Cel
lar and Snack Bar will also be
open at noon.
All fraternities are planning buf
fet luncheons for alumni and Cal
brothers. Alumni have also been
invited to house dances tomorrow
night.
