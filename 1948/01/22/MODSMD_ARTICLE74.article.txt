# UMT Issue To Be Aired On Tuesday
## 
### 
The pros and cons of Universal
Military Training will be aired next
Tuesday night at Cubberley Audi
torium at 7:30.
Dr. Philip W. Buck, professor of
political science at Stanford, will
serve as moderator. Colonel Nor
man E. Edwards of Palo Alto will
discuss the advantages of UMT
while the Rev. G. A. Casaday, also
of Palo Alto, will speak against
UMT. There will be a student par
ticipant on each side.
Colonel Edwards, who was a
chaplain with the army, is with the
Veterans Administration in Palo
Alto at the present time. He re
cently visited Fort Knox where he
saw trainees going through a typical
UMT training period.
Mr. Casaday, well known among
Stanford students, is minister for
the First Congregational Church in
Palo Alto.
This meeting is being sponsored
by a committee made up of repre
sentatives of the lIR, AVC, Student
Federalists, Village Christian Group,
and Debate Club. Ken Croft, Stu
dent Federalist head, has been ap
pointed chairman of the temporary
committee. Mary Virginia Pond is
in charge of securing the speakers.
The idea for this meeting was
suggested by a group of students
who attended the American Friends
Service Convention at Asilomar
during Christmas vacation.
