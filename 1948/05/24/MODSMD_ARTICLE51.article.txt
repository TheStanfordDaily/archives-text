faculty members were elected asso
ciates and members recently in
Sigma Xi, national honorary re
search society.
They will be honored at a banquet
at the Stantord Union tonight at
G:!5. The following new officers
will be installed at the meeting:
Dr. Lydik S. Jacobsen, president;
Dr. Harold M. Bacon, vice-presi
dent; Dr. Leland H. Brown, secre
tary-treasurer; and Dr. William A.
Bonner, assistant secretary.
Following the initiation. Dr. John
Lawrence, of the University of Cali
fornia. will speak in Cubberley Hall
on "Nuclear Physics in Biology and
Medicine."
The qualifications for full mem
bership for students and faculty
members are noteworthy achieve
ment in a scientific field and com
pletion of a piece of research wor
thy of publication.
Qualification tor associate mem
bership is marked excellence in one
or more departments of science, as
well as an aptitude for independent
research.
