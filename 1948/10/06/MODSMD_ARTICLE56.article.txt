# Woman Bequeaths Savings to Farm
## 
### 
The life savings of a 74-year-old
Austrian immigrant woman were re
ceived by Stanford today.
This fund, totaling $2,177, repre
sented years of purposeful frugality
by the late Susan Kohl Nagengast.
Miss Nagengast, before her death
in 1944, worked for more than 16
years as a baker at a women's dor
mitory on the Stanford campus.
Administrators of her will said
Miss Nagengast had wished to have
a part in helping to educate the
youth of her adopted country.
Also willed to the university was
Miss Nagengast's sole piece of jew
elry, a diamond ring valued at $100.
