RELIEF FOOD-Arrival in Honolulu of the food
laden Hawaiian Refiner (above) marked the 60th
day of the ILYVU longshoremen's strike in Hawaii.
By agreement between the governor's official food
committee and the ILYVU. the specially designated

"relief ship" was unloaded at prevailing longshore
rates and by union longshoremen. The ship's cargo
of 11.150 tons of food supplies was the largest relief
shipment to date. Here, 747 tons of potatoes are
piled on the freighter's decks.
