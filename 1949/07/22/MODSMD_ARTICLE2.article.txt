# Richard L. Balch Appointed New Chief Men's Counselor
## 
### 
Stanford's new chief counselor for men is Richard L. Balch,
war-time Navy torpedo boat skipper and a former football player
at Union College, where he has been assistant director of admis
sions for the past two and a half years.
President Wallace Sterling announced yesterday that Mr.

Batch would take over his new du
ties in mid-August as successor to
Shelton L. Beatty, who has been
appointed dean of men and lecturer
in English at Pomona College.
He will come to Stanford with a
wide range of experience in work
ing with students at Union College.
In addition to his work in the ad
missions field at the Schenectady,
N.Y., institution, he organized and
administered the foreign student
program with fraternities, assisted
in planning the freshman advisory
program, developed orientation pro
cedures, and served on the admin
istrative council which determined
general college policy.
He also found time to serve as
backfield coach for the college var
sity football team and to coach the
junior varsity lacrosse squad. He
played quarterback on the freshman
and varsity football teams at Un
ion, where he received the A.B. de
gree in psychology and government
in 1941.
Late in 1941 Mr. Balch volun
teered for Navy duty and was sent
to midshipmen's school where he

was commissioned as an ensign in
1942. He served with PT Boat
Squadron 11 for three years, taking
part in the Solomons, Bismarck Ar
chipelago, and Vella Lavalla cam
paigns before being returned to the
U.S. for duty in the Bureau of Ord
nance. He was assistant gunnery
officer on the aircraft carrier U.S.S.
Kearsarge at the time of his dis
charge as a full lieutenant in De
cember, 1945.
Alter the war Mr. Balch returned
to Union College as assistant direc
tor of admissions and assistant to
the dean of the college. He also
began work on his Master's de
gree, correlating results of student
achievement at Union College with
results of American Council on Ed
ucation tests on College Board ex
aminations.
Mr. Balch will serve under Dean
of Students Lawrence A. Kimpton
at Stanford. His new duties will
place him in charge of all counsel
ing work with men and the super
vision of fraternities and of men in
University residences.
The 30-year-old administrator is
married and has two children, Tim
othy, 4, and Pamela Jane, 2. His
wile, Patricia, a University of Cali
fornia graduate in the class of 1941,
is the daughter of Mrs. Jane Hartt
Richardson and the late Girard N.
Richardson, both Stanford gradu
ates. Mr. Richardson was city at
torney of Piedmont, California, for
34 years.
