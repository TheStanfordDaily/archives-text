# Who's Who: Tom Clark
## 
### 
Thomas Campbell Clark, nominated yesterday to the Supreme Court
by President Truman to replace the late Frank Murphy, has always been
active in legal service.
At present Clark is Attorney General of the United States, a position
he has held since June 1945.
In 1932 Clark was appointed to the United States Supreme Court by
President Roosevelt, but he resigned to become a special attorney for the
Department of Justice in 1937.
Clark received his law degree from the University of Texas in 1922.
While in college, he was a member of Delta Tau Delta.
He was born in Dallas, Texas, in 1899.
In the first World War, Clark served with the 153 rd Infantry Division.
Between 1937 and 1945, Clark served the Department of Justice in vari
ous capacities. He was named special attorney in charge of anti-trust
suits in 1938. In 1942, he became chief of the West Coast offices of the
Department. He worked with alien control and Japanese relocation during
the war, and was named in charge of the criminal division of the Depart
ment of Justice in 1943,
