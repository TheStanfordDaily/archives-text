# Schwartz Wins Grass Court Upset
## 
### 
SOUTH ORANGE, N.J., Aug. 2
(UP). —Sidney Schwartz, a promis
ing young tennis star from, New
York, brought about the first major
upset in the Eastern Grass Court
Tournament today when he dumped
eighth-seeded William Vogt of Phil
adelphia, 6-2, 4-6. 10-8, in a second
round match.
Earl Cochell of San Francisco,
however, drew the day's biggest
applause when he ripped Carlton
Hood of Brooklyn, N.Y., 6-1, 6-0,
in a first - round set - to. Cochell
Hew back to the U.S. last night
after competing at Munich Sunday.
He appeared none the worse after
the long journey, simply toying
with Hood throughout the match.

Billy Talbert of New York, win
ner of he Southampton tournament
last Sunday and one of the top
choices for the Eastern crown,
moved into the third round with a
6-4, 6-1 victory over Tony Trabe: t
of Cincinnati.
In other major second - round
matches, fourth - seeded GardnaP
Mulloy of Miami flattened Henry
von Kohorn of Scarsdale, N.Y.. 6-1,
6-3; sixth - seeded Herb Flam of
Beverly Hills, Calif., eliminated
Jack Geller of New York. 6-3, 6-3;
Jack Tuero. intercollegiate cham
pion from Tulane, dumped Arnold
Saul of Los Angeles, 6-3, 5-7. 6-3.
