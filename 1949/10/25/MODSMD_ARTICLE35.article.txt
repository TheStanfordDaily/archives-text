Honor Students.

of the University's Scholarship
committee, pointed out thai estab

scholastic accomplishments of stu

not received scholarships, but have
had a distinguishde academic rec
ord for the year. Some of them
graduated last June; others are still
in school.
With the adoption of this new
plan, Stanford will no longer award
Lower Division honors and will con
tinue to confer graduation honors.

Two hundred sixty-nine students
who had an average of at least A—
(3.5 or better) during the past aca
demic year were honored today by
being named on the Dean's List ol
Honor Students.
They represent the top 5 per cent
of the 5,030 undergraduate students
who attended the University din
ing the autumn, winter, and spring
quarters of 1948-45).
Dr. Hay D. Harriman, chairman
of the University's Scholarship
committee, pointed out thai estab
lishment of the Dean's List of Hon
or Students represented an innova
tion at the University.
It is an effort to recognize the
scholastic accomplishments of stu
dents who -many of them—have
not received scholarships, but have
had a distinguishde academic rec
ord for the year. Some of them
graduated last June; others are still
in school.
With the adoption of this new
plan, Stanford will no longer award
Lower Division honors and will con
tinue to confer graduation honors.
