# Play Tickets On Sale Friday
## 
### 
Tickets for the Stanford Players'
productions of "Boy Meets Girl"
and the musical comedy "Connecti
cut Yankee" go on sale tomorrow at
the Memorial Auditorium box of
fice.
"Boy Meets Girl," a farce dealing
with life in a Hollywood motion
picture company, is scheduled for
performance on February 9, 10, and
11 as a regular production of the
Stanford Players' subscription sea
son. Non-subscription tickets sell
for 90 cents.
"Connecticut Yankee," the musi
cal production of the subscription
season, will be presented on March
9, 10, and 11 in the Memorial Audi
torium. Tickets cost $1.20.
A l l - University tryouts for the
cast and chorus of "Connecticut
Yankee" will continue tonight and
tomorrow night in the Memorial
Auditorium from 7:30 to 10.
According to Director F. Cowles
Strickland, dancers, singers, and
musicians of all types are called for
in the script. "Trained voices are
not a requirement for trying out for
the show," Professor Strickland
emphasized. "Anyone who can carry
a tune is cordially invited."
Tryouts for the 25-piece orches
tra are scheduled for Friday night
at 7:30 in Woodpecker Lodge. Dance
tryouts will be held in the Memo
rial Auditorium Thursday afternoon
from-5 to 6.
