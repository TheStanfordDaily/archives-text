# Harvey Hall Chosen New Registrar
## 
### 
Harvey Hall returns to the Stan
ford faculty this quarter as the new
registrar, taking the old post of Dr.
Donald Winbigier, the new Dean of
Students.
Mr. Hall returns from Orange
Coast College in Southern Califor
nia, where for the past year he has
been chairman of the division of
language and literature. He worked
with Dr. Winbigier as assistant reg
istrar at Stanford during 1948-49.
His experience includes two years
during 1940-42 as assistant dean of
men at the College of the City of
New York. From 1932 to 1940 he
served as a teacher and counselor
at Sequoia Union High School in
Redwood City.
Hall returned to Sequoia after the
war to serve during 1946-48 as head
counselor and head of the English
department.
He spent four years in the Army
Air Force during World War 11, en
tering the service as a private in
1942 and being discharged as lieu
tenant colonel in 1945.
For three of the four years he
was group adjutant and executive
officer of a P-47 flight squadron.
The squadron served two years in
the European theater in close sup
port of Gen. George Patton's Third
Army.
Hall was graduated from the
University of California at Los An
geles in 1930. He was awarded a
general secondary teaching creden
tial at Stanford in 1931 and received
his MA. degree in 1941 at Colum
bia Teachers College, where he is a
candidate for the Ed.D. degree.
His wife is the former Marion
Dwight, a graduate of Stanford and
a former student in personnel ad
ministration at Columbia.
