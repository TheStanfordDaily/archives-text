# Exhibit Features Local Paper Mills
## 
### 
An exhibit on paper-making, in
cluding little-known information on
the history of mills in the Santa
Cruz Mountains, is now on display
> the Stanford Library.
The display has descriptions and
photographs of paper mills built
during pioneer days of the last cen
tury and since disappeared at Sara
toga, Soquel, and Corral itos.
W. B. Beatty, mining engineer on
the staff of the Stanford Research
Institute, whose hobby is paper, pre
pared the exhibit. He has also in
cluded books on papermaking, sam
[ pies of paper made in Japan, China,
j and European countries, and hand
made paper produced by artisans in
this country and abroad.
The exhibit is open to the public in
! the lobby on the second floor of the
library building. The library is open
from 8 a.m. to 10 p.m., Monday
through Friday; 8 a.m. to 4 p.m. on
Saturday; and 1 to 5 p.m. on Sun
day.
