# ELEVEN COLLEGES TO ATTEND
## Debate Society Will Sponsor First Student Model Congress 
### 
A model student congress, the
first of its kind and the climax of
this year's events in the Bay Area
Forensic League, will be held here
this Saturday from 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.,
sponsored by the Stanford Debate
Society.
The congress, composed of dele
| gates from 11 Bay Area colleges,
j will center debate on the national
topic of "Welfare Legislation,"
along the linev of health, education,
labor, social security, and agricul-
I ture.
Modeled alter the federal bicam
eral legislature, the student con
gress will consist of two houses:
Senate and House of Representa
tives. Each school participating will
contribute two members to the Sen
ate and six to the House, and each
delegate will participate in one of
five committees: Health, Labor, Ed-
I ucation, Social Security, and Agri
; culture.
Procedure
A minimum of two different bills,
! one in each house, will be submit
ted by each school. Bills submitted
jby the Stanford delegation to the

congress will concern: federal aid to
public schools, legalized gambling,
national standards for public school
education, and revised Taft-Hartley
Labor Law.
Party affiliation will be declared
by each member as either Republi
can. Democrat, or Independent.
Officers of the congress wiil in
clude a president of the Senate and
a Speaker of the House, elected by
the respective houses.
Wire to Iruman
The Stanford delegation to the
congress will include the following
students: Corinne Litvin, Elizabeth
Conser, Chandler Meyers, Jerry
Hosking, Arnold Gold, Harvey
Wagner, Rosemary Duggin, Bill
Fundenbcrg, Dick Konig, Duncan
Davidson, Don Gillan.
Results of the congress will be
wired to President Truman and tro
phies will be awarded to the four
top speakers in each house.
