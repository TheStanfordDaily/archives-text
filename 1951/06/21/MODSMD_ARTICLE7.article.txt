# Summer Registration Is Expected to Reach 2,700
## 
### 
Around 2,700 students are expected to register for studies this
summer, the Registrar's Office announced yesterday. That figure
will mean no change in the size of the student body over the last
two summers. Of that number, 1,790 are expected to be graduate
students.
The registration procedure will remain basically the same as

previous quarters, the only notable
change being that students' advisors
will be located in their respective of
fices instead of at the Pavilion.
Beginning at 8 a.m. students may
obtain their registration books at the
Memorial Auditorium, Room 11A or
112 A.
Registering students have been di
vided into three groups, each with a
different procedure.
Students who have registered pre
viously but not in spring quarter may
secure their registration books at the
Registrar's Office, Room 111 A. from 8
this morning to 12 noon, and from 1
to 5 this afternoon, irrespective of
alphabetical grouping.
Non-matriculated students may
obtain their registration books in the
Office of Admissions, Room 112 A,
from 8:30 to 12 and from 1 to 4:30.
There will be no alphabetical group
ing.
New matriculated students and
students who were here spring quar
ter may obtain their registration
books at the Memorial Auditorium by
alphabetical groupings.
The above group will register ac
cording to the following plan:
S to Z from 8 to 8:45 a.m.. east
entrance.
A to 1) from B:4j to 9:30 a.m., west
entrance.
Fto K from !):U() to 10:15 a.m., east
entrance.
1, to K from 10:15 to 11 a.m., west
entrance.
Students who are unable to report
in accordance with the schedule may
obtain their books at the Memorial
Auditorium from 11 to 11:45 a.m. and
from 1 to 4:30 p.m.
Lower Division students are re
quired to take physical education.
Women students must make appoint
ments to enroll in physical activity
courses in the Pavilion today.
Men will enroll at the P.E. Building
tomorrow. Upper Division students
wishing to take P.E. classes for credit
should enroll in the P.E. Department
tomorrow.
New students must have pictures
taken at the Pavilion before turning
in their registration books. They
must also report in the Pavilion for
physical examination appointments.
The registration books must be
stamped by the Health Service.
