# ILLUSTRATED LECTURE
## AUFS Speaker to Discuss 'Turkey in Global Polities' 
### 
"Turkey in Global Politics," a lecture illustrated by colored
slides, will be presented by Richard Robinson of the American
Universities Field Staff in Cubberley Auditorium at 4:15 p.m.
today.
The slides will show the prominent factors of topography and

population concentrations deter
mining the power position of Tur
key, which is now in possession of
the most effective land foi'ces in
continental, non-Soviet Europe.
Robinson has lived in Ankara
with his family and has studied
Turkish affairs for the past five
years. In 1950 he was a member of
the Economic Survey Mission sent
there by the International Bank for

Reconstruction and Development.
He is a graduate of the University
of Washington, with a Master's de
gree in business from Harvard.
This is the second in a series of
four public lectures to be presented
at Stanford by four international
affairs scholars of the AUFS who
have recently returned from the
trouble spots of the world.
Lawrence W. Witt will speak on
Brazil, Feb. 11, and Richard Nolte
on the Arab countries, Feb. 25. The
lectures will be held at 4:15 p.m.
in Cubberley.
