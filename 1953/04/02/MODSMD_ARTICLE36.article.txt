# WINS AWARD
## Farm TV Program To Spotlight U.N. 
### 
r c —
The final program in Stanford's
! award-winning television series will
I bring a graphic story of the United
Nations to Bay Area television
! viewers tomorrow at 7 p.m. on
| channel five.
The award for achievement in
local television was presented to
Stanford for its 13-week series on
"People, Places, and Politics" by the
San Francisco Television Academy
last week. It was one of two pro
gram awards made by the Acad
emy.
"The U.N. —Design for Peace" is
the title of this week's show.
Appearing on the final program
are Professors C. Easton Rothwell,
director of the Hoover Institute and
Library; James T. Watkins IV, act
ing head of the Political Science
Department; and Alfred R. Sumner
of the Geography Department.
