# TUESDAY SERIES
## Russia to Be Fisher's Topic 
### 
Dr. Raymond H. Fisher, scholar
in Russian history, will open the
spring quarter Tuesday Evening
Scries at Stanford tonight with the
topic "After Stalin, What?"
He will speak at 4:30 in Memo
rial Auditorium.
Dr. Fisher served as Russian spe
cialist with the U.S. State Depart
ment in Washington, D.C., from
1944 to 194(5. In 1944 the American
Historical Association awarded him
the Herbert Baxter Adams prize in
European history.
He has taught history at the Uni
versity of California at Los Ange
les since 1946, and has been an as
sociate professor sincc 1948. From
1937 to 1944 he taught at Humboldt
State College in Areata, California.
Dr. Fisher attended Yale for two
years, then transferred to the Uni
versity of California at Los Ange
les where he received his A.B. in
1929 and his M.A. in 1932. He re
ceived his Ph.D. degree at the Uni
versity of California at Berkeley in
1937.
In 1935 he was a Russian language
scholar at Columbia University.
Dr. Fisher will be introduced by
Dr. Harold M. Bacon of the Mathe
matics Department.
