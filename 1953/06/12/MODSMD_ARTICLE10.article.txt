# The 						Four Years Are Gone!
## 1,133 Seniors To Graduate 
### 
The Class of '53 will meet to
gether for the last time Sun
day at 5 p.m. when 2,263 senior
and graduate students will as
semble in Frost Amphitheater
for the Sixty-second Annual
Commencement Exercises.
A.B. and B.S. degrees will be
awarded to 1,133 Seniors, 707 men
and 426 women. Advanced degrees
will be given to 1,130 graduate stu
dents.
Sir Percy Spender, Australian am
bassador to the United States, will
deliver the Commencement address
entitled "The University and the
Community."
The Rev. Robert M. Minto will of
fer the invocation and benediction.
President J. E. Wallace Sterling will
welcome the graduates and their
friends and relatives before present
ing the diplomas.
The Baccalaureate Service will be
at 11 a.m. in Memorial Church.
"God Speaks for Man" will be the
title of the sermon delivered by The
Right Rev. Richard S. Watson, Epis
copal Bishop of Utah.
Seniors in the Stanford Air Force
ROTC will receive their commissions
in a short ceremony at 2:30 p.m. in
the Little Theater of Memorial Audi
torium. Lt. Col. Ralph W. Keller,
Professor of Air Science and Tactics
at Stanford, will speak.
The lessons from the old and new
testaments will be read by Pete
Grothe, Senior Class Historian, and
Ann Alabaster, Senior Vice-Presi
dent.
