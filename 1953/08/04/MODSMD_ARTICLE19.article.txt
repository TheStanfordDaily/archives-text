# Phillips Appointed To Brazilian Post
## 
### 
Richard B. Phillips, executive
secretary of Hispanic American
Studies at Stanford since 1951 and
assistant editor of the "Hispanic
American Report," has been ap
pointed director of the American
Cultural Institute in Sao Paulo.
The Institute, which is supported
by the U.S. Department of State,
offers to Brazilians instruction in
the English language, English and
American literature, United States
history, and related subjects deal
ing with American civilization. With
G.700 students it is the largest insti
tute of its kind in the world.
H. Leslie Robinson has succeeded
Phillips as executive secretary of
Hispanic American Studies. He re
ceived his A.B. in 1948 and A.M. in
19~>2 from Stanford.
