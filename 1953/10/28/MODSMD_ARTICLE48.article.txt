# Musicians Plan Free Concerts
## 
### 
Planning a busy season, the Stan
ford Music Department will give
several concerts during the remain
der of this quarter.
The first of these programs will
be a harpsichord recital by Putnam
Aldrich, associate professor of mu
sic, on Sunday, Nov. 8, at 4 p.m. in
Cubberley Auditorium. Featured
music will be works of Italian,
French, and German composers of
the sixteenth to eighteenth centu
ries.
On Sunday, Nov. 15, also at 4 p.m.
in Cubberley Auditorium, the sec
ond concert in the Chamber Music
Series will be heard. This concert
will be devoted entirely to the works
of Mozart.
The Stanford Symphony Or
chestra, directed by Sandor Salgo,
will appear on the Tuesday Eve
ning Series, Dec. 1, at 7:3 ft p.m.
The program will be in commem
oration of the 150 th anniversary
of Berlioz' birth and will include
several compositions by that com
poser as well as a performance
of Symphony No. 4 by Albert
liotissal, a work written in 1930
bill as yet seldom heard in this
country. Ruth Rochr, soprano,
will appear as soloist in this
country.
On Sunday, Dec. (>, the Stanford
University Chorus, under the direc
tion of Harold Schmidt, will present
the cantata "Saint Nicholas" by |
Benjamin Britten.
