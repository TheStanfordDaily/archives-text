# Row Hosts More Than 500 Freshmen; Open Houses Continue Through Sunday
## 
### JOHN McFEE 
Over 500 freshmen and other
rushees passed through the
doors of Stanford's fraternities
last night as 1954 rushing enthu
siastically got under way.
Handshaking, introductions,
punch sipping, and casual conversa
tion characterized the opening night
of the fraternity rushing season,
which this year lasts until Jan. 16.

"It's a mad rush," exclaimed John
Shepard, an Encina freshman, while
another was heard to say, "Rushing
is quite confusing at first and I'm
afraid it also will necessitate the
burning of the midnight oil for a
while."
Open houses will continue on the
Row for four more days ending
Sunday afternoon. For the first few
nights, freshmen will be divided
into two parts alphabetically ac
cording to last name, each group
alternating between Upper and
Lower Rows each night.
The open house schedule for the
rest of the week is as follows: To
night, 7:30-9:45 (last names begin
ning with A-M visit the Lower
Row while N-Z go to Upper Row);
tomorrow, 7:30-9:45 (A-M visit Up
j per Row, N-Z Lower Row); Satur
day, 2:30-5 (A-M go to Lower Row,
N-Z Upper Row); Saturday, 7:30-
9:45, and Sunday, 2:30-5 (rushees
may visit any houses they wish).
"Rushees are urged to see as
many houses as possible during the
open house period," said Gene Dils,
Interfraternity Council president.
Formal rushing commences Sun
day night at 7 after which time
there can be unlimited contact be
tween fraternity men and rushees.
Pledge Day will be Saturday,
Jan. 16, which closes formal rush
ing. Pledge cards will be signed
that morning at 11 in the Women's
Clubhouse.
Lower Row houses include Alpha
Kappa Lambda, Alpha Sigma Phi,
Beta Theta Pi, Chi Psi, Delta Upsi
lon, Kappa Sigma, Phi Delta Theta,
Phi Kappa Sigma, Sigma Alpha Ep
silon, Sigma Chi, Theta Chi, and
Zeta Psi.
Upper Row includes: Alpha Delta
Phi, Alpha Tau Omega, Delta Tau
Delta, Delta Chi, Kappa Alpha, Phi
Gamma Delta, Phi Kappa Psi, Phi
Sigma Kappa, Sigma Nu, Theta
Delta Chi, and Theta Xi.

Over 500 freshmen and other
rushees passed through the
doors of Stanford's fraternities
last night as 1954 rushing enthu
siastically got under way.
Handshaking, introductions,
punch sipping, and casual conversa
tion characterized the opening night
of the fraternity rushing season,
which this year lasts until Jan. 16.

"It's a mad rush," exclaimed John
Shepard, an Encina freshman, while
another was heard to say, "Rushing
is quite confusing at first and I'm
afraid it also will necessitate the
burning of the midnight oil for a
while."
Open houses will continue on the
Row for four more days ending
Sunday afternoon. For the first few
nights, freshmen will be divided
into two parts alphabetically ac
cording to last name, each group
alternating between Upper and
Lower Rows each night.
The open house schedule for the
rest of the week is as follows: To
night, 7:30-9:45 (last names begin
ning with A-M visit the Lower
Row while N-Z go to Upper Row);
tomorrow, 7:30-9:45 (A-M visit Up
j per Row, N-Z Lower Row); Satur
day, 2:30-5 (A-M go to Lower Row,
N-Z Upper Row); Saturday, 7:30-
9:45, and Sunday, 2:30-5 (rushees
may visit any houses they wish).
"Rushees are urged to see as
many houses as possible during the
open house period," said Gene Dils,
Interfraternity Council president.
Formal rushing commences Sun
day night at 7 after which time
there can be unlimited contact be
tween fraternity men and rushees.
Pledge Day will be Saturday,
Jan. 16, which closes formal rush
ing. Pledge cards will be signed
that morning at 11 in the Women's
Clubhouse.
