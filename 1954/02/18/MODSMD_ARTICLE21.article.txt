# WEEKEND SOCIAL CALENDAR
## Steinberg Will Conduct San Francisco Symphony 
### 
The San Francisco Symphony Or
chestra, conducted by William
Steinberg, will present concerts in
the Opera House tonight, tomorrow
afternoon, and Their
program will consist of "Symphony
of Psalms" by Stravinsky with the
San FYancisco State College A Ca
pella Choir, "Symphony No. 2 in B
Flat Major" by Schubert, and "Sym
phony No. 1 in C Minor" by Brahms.
Tickets are on sale at the Sherman
Clay box office.
Xenia Boodberg Lee, pianist, will
present a recital at the San Fran
cisco Museum of Art, Veterans'
Building, Civic Center, at 8:30 p.m.
Wednesday. She will play selections
by Bach, Beethoven, Schumann,
Bartok, Schoenberg, and Liszt.
Tickets are available at the Sher
man Clay box office at $1.50 for
general admission and $1 for stu
dents.
DRAMA
"The Shrike" by Joseph Kramm
will be presented tomorrow and
Saturday at 8:30 p.m. by the Menlo
Players Guild at the Burgess Park
Little Theater in Menlo Park. Tick
ets are available at the box office
for $ 1.
"The Affairs of Anatol" by Ar
thur Schnitzler will be performed
by the Playhouse Repertory Com
pany at Beach and Hyde Streets in
San Francisco during February and
March on Friday, Saturday, and
Sunday evenings, starting tomorrow
night.
A travel film-lecture, entitled
"The Rhineland," with Curtis Na
gel will be shown Saturday at 8
p.m. at the Theodore Roosevelt
High School, 19th and Santa
Clara, San Jose. Tickets cost 75
and 50 cents, and are on sale at
the Sherman Clay box officc.
"Time in the Sun," a tale of feu
dal Mexico, will be the Peninsula

Cinema Guild's presentation tomor
row night at 7:30 and 9:30 and Sun
day and Monday at 2:30, 7:30, and
9:30 p.m.
A motion picture, "Wilson," will
be shown Saturday at 2:30 p.m. at
the California Palace of the Legion
of Honor, Lincoln Park, San Fran
cisco. The film stars Alexander
Knox, Charles Coburn, and Geral
dine Fitzgerald,
SPECIAL EVENTS
The 28th annual San Francisco
Automobile Show is being held at
the San Francisco Civic Audito
rium, Civic Center, through Sun
day, from 10 a.m. to 11 p.m., with
stage shows at 3 and 9 p.m. It
will feature Jimmy Durante and
his Revue, and 1954 and experi
mental car models.

seum today and will continue to
March 28.

Steinberg, will present concerts in
the Opera House tonight, tomorrow
afternoon, and Their
program will consist of "Symphony
of Psalms" by Stravinsky with the
San FYancisco State College A Ca
pella Choir, "Symphony No. 2 in B
Flat Major" by Schubert, and "Sym
phony No. 1 in C Minor" by Brahms.
Tickets are on sale at the Sherman
Clay box office.
Xenia Boodberg Lee, pianist, will
present a recital at the San Fran
cisco Museum of Art, Veterans'
Building, Civic Center, at 8:30 p.m.
Wednesday. She will play selections
by Bach, Beethoven, Schumann,
Bartok, Schoenberg, and Liszt.
Tickets are available at the Sher
man Clay box office at $1.50 for
general admission and $1 for stu
dents.

SPECIAL EVENTS
The 28th annual San Francisco
Automobile Show is being held at
the San Francisco Civic Audito
rium, Civic Center, through Sun
day, from 10 a.m. to 11 p.m., with
stage shows at 3 and 9 p.m. It
will feature Jimmy Durante and
his Revue, and 1954 and experi
mental car models.
ART
The San Francisco Museum of
Art, Civic Center, will hold a free
lecture-tour by Annaliese Hoyer on
"Color, Line, and Form" Sunday at
3 p.m. and a free discussion on "The
Rental Gallery" by Mrs. E. Morris
Cox on Wedensday at 8 p.m. The
73rd Annual Painting and Sculpture
Exhibition of the San Francisco Art
Association will open at the mu
seum today and will continue to
March 28.

Museum at Golden Gate Park, San
Francisco, is showing two new ex
hibits: watercolors by William Fett,
a young modernist, and oil paintings
by David Friend, an abstract ex
pressionist.
