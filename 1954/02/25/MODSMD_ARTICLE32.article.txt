# Cultural Events
## 
### 
MUSIC
The Cosmopolitan Opera Com
pany will open its season at 8:20
p.m. on March 2 at the War Memo
rial Opera House in San Francisco
with Verdi's "La Traviata." Anton
Coppola, American-Italian conduc
tor, who has been with the San
Carlos Opera Company and the
Cincinnati Opera, will direct the
season's repertoire. Tickets, priced
from $1.50 to $3, are on sale at
Sherman Clay arid the Peninsula
Box Office.
DRAMA
"Death of a Salesman" l>y Arthur
Miller will be presented by the A"-
tors' Workshop of the San Francisco
Drama Guild at the Workshop's El
gin Street Theater at 8:30 p.m. to
morrow and Saturday and 7 p.m.
Sunday. Tickets may be obtained at
the office of the Drama Guild, 136
Valencia Street.
The Peninsula Little Theater will
produce "The Male Animal" by
Thurber and Nugent at the Hillbarn
Theater, 1800 El Camino Real. San
Mateo, at 8:30 p.m. tomorrow and
Saturday and 7:30 p.m. Sunday.
"The Shrike" by Joseph Kramm
will be given by the Burgess Park

Little Theater in Menlo Park at 8:30
p.m. tomorrow and Saturday. Tick
ets are available at the box office
for $1.
