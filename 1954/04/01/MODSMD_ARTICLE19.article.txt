# Two Silent Films Set for Tonight
## 
### 
The first program of the sprint;
quartei series of the Stanford Film
Society will lie presented at 7:30
p.m. tonight in Cubberley Audito
rium.
A double bill of silent films made
in 1921, "The Cabinet of Dr. Cali
cari" and"The Tell-Tale Heart,"
will bo shown.
"The Cabinet of Dr Caligari," to
which a sound track was added in
1040, is the most famous of the Gor
man expressionistic films. Its cubist
settings are early examples of a
trend in abstract stagina developed
in Germany over the past 30 years.
Edgar Allan Poe's "The Tell-Tale
Heart" is an atmospheric film with
abstract settings.
