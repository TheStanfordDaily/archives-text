MUSIC
Bruno Walter will conduct the
San Francisco Symphony Orchestra
in its next to last concert program
of this season tonight and Saturday
at the Opera House. The all-Bee
thoven program will consist of the
overture to "Egmont," Symphony
No. 2 in D Major, and Symphony
No. 3 in E Flat Major ("Eroica").
The Jean Leon Destine Haitian
dancers will appear Monday at 8:30
p.m. in the Veterans' Auditorium,
San Francisco. Among the dances
will be "Witch Doctor," "Creole Ma
zurka," "Street Scenes," "French
Court Dances," and "Revolt of the
Slaves." Tickets are on sale at the
Sherman, Clay box office for $3,
$2.40. and $1.80.
Two concerts will be given at the

Center: the Griller Quartet tomor
row at 8:30 p.m. and the California
String Quartet on Wednesday at
8:30 p.m. Prices -for the Griller re
cital are $1.75, $1.25, and 90 cents,

let, $1.85.
DRAMA
"The Lady's Not for Burning," a
comedy in verse by Christopher Fry,
will be presented by the Peninsula
Little Theater tomorrow and Satur
day at 8:30 p.m. and Sunday at 7:30
p.m. at the Hillbarn Theater, 1800
El Camino Real, San Mateo. The
performance will be repeated next
week end. Tickets are available by
writing Box 543, San Mateo.
