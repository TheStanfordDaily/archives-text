# Spring Show Rehearsals Go Into Last Week
## 
### 
Tickets for the Ram's Head Spring
Show, Cole Porter's "Anything
Goes," continue on sale today as the
production moves into its final week
of rehearsals. The show runs May
6, 7, and 8.
Tickets will be sold for 75 cents,
$1, and $1.25 at Engineering Cor
ner from 8 a.m. to 3 p.m., at Memo
rial Hall box office from 12:30 to 4:30
p.m., and at the Peninsula Box Of
fice in Palo Alto.
Lighting, sets, costumes, and
props were revealed at last night's
"tech" rehearsal. Five large, elabo
rate sets are used. The complex
lighting involves the use of over
100 different instruments.
The show has a cast of 60, includ
ing two champion pedigreed dogs, a
Pomeranian and a Chihuahua.
More members are needed for the
shift, fly, set construction, and cos
tume crews. Anyone interested may
report at Memorial Auditorium any
night between 7 and 10:30.
Crew heads are Rer.ee Reed,
props; Joan Bennett, makeup; Bill
Potter, lights; Rod MeDaniel, sound;
Phoebe Schrock, sets; Curt Pender
grass and Stan Curry, shift; Barbara
Spence, costumes; and Hal Tracy,
fly.
