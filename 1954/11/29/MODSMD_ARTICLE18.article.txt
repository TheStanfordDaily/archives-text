# U.D., Graduate Student Pre-Reg Opens Today
## Pre-Reg for LD Starts Wednesday 
### 
Pre-registration for upper divi
sion and graduate students will
be held today and tomorrow
from 8 a.m. until noon and from
1 to 5 p.m. in the Registrar's Of
fice, Room 111-A.
Students may obtain an official
study list card from their major
school or department, to be filled
out in ink for the approval and sig
nature of their advisers.
Courses marked with a in the
time schedule may be signed for in
the Registrar's Office.
Several changes and additions to
the winter quarter time schedule
have been announced. Social Sci
ences 120, Marriage and the Family,
will be given MTYVTh at 2 in Room
334 by Mrs. Mac Curdy. Students
may sign up for the class in Room
207-A.
The Germanic and Romanic Lan
guages Department announced that
class lists for conversation courses
12, 13, 27, and 28 in French and
Spanish will not be signed in the
Registrar's Office. The class lists will
be in Room 245 for French and
Room 246 for Spanish, and will bp
available as follows: French, 8 to 10
a.m. and 1 to 3 p.m. Dec. 1, 2, and
3. Spanish, 1 to 5 p.m. Dec. 1; 8 to
10 a.m. and 3 to 5 p.m. Dec. 2; and
8 to 10 a.m. and 1 to 5 p.m. Dec-. 3.
Women wishing to register for
N-196 under the School of Educa
tion may do so in the office of Miss
Christine Ricker in the Stanford
Union. Registration is open only by
arrangement.
Official study lists may be turned
in in person to a registration clerk
at the Registrar's Office. The dead
line is 5 p.m. tomorrow.

students will be held Dec. 1 through
