# Fraternity Rush Season Starts Tonight; Open Houses on Row
## Visitors Split Alphabetically 
### BILL WRIGHT 
Rushing, that giant squid
which which holds fraternity
men and rushees within its tena
cious grasp every winter, takes
charge again tonight at 7:30
when the 24 Stanford fraternities
swing open their doors to the
first wave of rushees—primarily
freshmen and transfers.
Tonight's open houses are the first
of five "get-acquainted"—or at least
"shake-hands"—sessions this week.
The fraternities will be open from
7:30 p.m. until 9:30 p.m. today, to
morow, and Friday. On Saturday
the houses will be open from 2:30
p.m. to 4:30 p.m. and from 7:30 p.m.
to 9 p.m.
Tonight and Friday rushe<fs whose
names come within the A-K alpha
betical division will visit Lower Row
while the L-Z group tours the Upper
Row. Tomorrow and Saturday af
ternoon the procedure will be re
versed. Saturday night rushees are
unrestricted and may visit any
unrestricted.
ROW DIVIDED
Lower Row houses are Alpha
Kappa Lambda, Alpha Sigma Phi,
Beta Theta Pi, Chi Psi, Delta Up
silon, Kappa Sigma, Phi Delta Theta,
Phi Kappa Sigma, Sigma Alpha Ep
silon, Sigma Chi, Theta Chi, Zeta
Psi.
Upper Row houses are Alpha
Delta Phi, Alpha Tau Omega, Delta
Chi, Delta Kappa Epsilon, Delta Tau
Delta, Kappa Alpha, Phi Gamma
Delta, Phi Kappa Psi, Phi Sigma
Kappa, Sigma Nu, Theta Delta Chi,
Theta Xi.
Under fraternity regulations tho
open houses will be the only periods
this week during which contact be -
tween fraternity men and rushees i
permitted. Formal, or unlimited,
rushing will begin at 9 p.m. Satur
day. At that time fraternities may
offer pins and rushees may accept.
The concentrated rushing period
will end Saturday, Jan. 15, with
pledge card signing and dinners for
the new pledges at their respective
houses. Until 5 p.m. that day the
no-contact rule will again be in ef
fect.
NEW POLICY
Under a new policy initiated by
freshman sponsors to protect dorm
frosh from constant invasion by fra
ternity men, "visiting hours" in En
cina and the Village have been es
tablished. Fraternity men wil] be
permitted in the living group wins;;-,
of the dorms during formal rushing
only from 11:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m.
and from 5:30 p.m. to 7:30 p.m.
Fraternity men may contact fresh
men in the dorm lobbies at other
times during the day. But all fra
ternity men must be out of the
dorms by 11 pjn.
The Interfraternity Council, the
body of house presidents which con
trols fraternity activities, has set a
limit of $700 on rushing expendi
tures. That does not include any in
crease in the commissary expense:
caused by hosting rushees at luncli
and dinner during formal rush week.
Rushees who have any problem. l
about rushing procedure may con
tact Otis Winters, IFC president, at
the IFC office in the Union or at the
Alpha Delta Phi house.
