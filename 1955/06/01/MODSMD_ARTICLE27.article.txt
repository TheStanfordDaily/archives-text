# Lower Division to Pre-Register Today
## 
### 
Autumn quarter pre-registration for lower division students
begins today. Lower division advisers are keeping special office
hours for conferences today, tomorrow, and Friday only, accord
ing to the Registrar's Office. Students must confer with their ad
visers before signing class lists.
The following lower division pre-registration procedure has

oeen announced Dy tne rcegistrar s
Office:
1. The student must have a con
ference in advance with his adviser.
The appointment time on the pre
registration permit is for signing
class lists, not for seeing advisers.
2. At the time of his conference,
the student must obtain an oificial
study list card from his adviser and
fill out his program in ink for his
adviser's approval and signature.
3. If the student's study list card
includes any courses marked with
an in the autumn quarter time
schedule, he must sign the appro
priate class lists at the information
desk, Registrar's Office, Room 111-A,
between 8 a.m. and noon or be
tween 1 and 5 p.m. tomorrow or
Friday. On Saturday the hours are
8 a.m. to noon only.
Appointments for signing class
lists begin tomorrow at 8 a.m. and
continue until Saturday noon. The
student may sign class lists at the

time given on the permit m#iled to
him or any time thereafter. His
name on the class list assures him of
admission to the class provided he i.s
present at the first class meeting. If
the student did not receive a permit,
he should go to the Lower Division
Office. Room 111-D.
4. The student must turn in his
official study list card, in person, to
the check-out clerk at the informa
tion desk before Saturday noon. Be
fore turning in the card, he must
have his picture taken.
Sign-up lists for French conver
sation courses 12, 13, 27, and 28 will
be available in Room 245 today and
Friday from 10 to 11 a.m. and from
noon to 12:45 p.m. Tomorrow the
lists will be available from 10 to
11:30 a.m. and from noon to 12:45.
Philosophy 1, Introduction to Log
ic, will be given autumn quarter by
Winst at 11 and by McNaughton at
1:15.
A new philosophy course. Phi
losophy 158, Computer Logic, will
be given at 2:15 MWF by McNaugh
ton for three units. It will deal with
the logical analysis of computing
machine circuits and the general na
ture of existing machines and ideal
machines.
