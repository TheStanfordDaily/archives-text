## Autumn Reg Procedure Announced 
### 
It's that time again, and Mon
day morning will find Stanford
students, old and new, joining
i lines that "couldn't possibly be
! that long" as registration gets
! under way.
Here is the official procedure, ac
cording to the Office:
G'n Monday, all students who were
registered either spring or summer
quarter and all new students may
pick up their registration books at
Memorial Auditorium, according to
the following schedule:
A-B 8-!) a.m.
C-D 9-10 a.m.
E-G 10-11 a.m.
11-K 1-2 p.m.
L-M 2-:} p.m.
N-R 3-4 p.m.
The Tuesday schedule will be:
S-T S-!t a.m.
U-Z 9-10 a.m.
A-D 10-11 a.m.
E-K 11-11:45 a.m.
L-R 1-1:45 p.m.
S-Z 1:45-2:30 p.m.
Students who have attended Stan
ford, but were not registered either
spring or summer quarter of this
year, may pick up their registration
books at the Registrar's Office, Room
111-A, on Monday or Tuesday from
8 a.m. to 12 noon or from 1 to 4
p.m., irrespective of alphabetic
grouping.
CONSULT ADVISERS
The next step, unless the student
has pre-registered, is a consultation
with the adviser, for completion of
the study list. Some advisers will
be stationed in the Pavilion; how
ever, most will he in their offices, as
listed in the time schedule, which is
available at the Registrar's Office
this morning.
Students who did not pre-register
will then sign class lists at the Pa
vilion. This assures admission to
the class, provided the student is
present at the first class meeting.
Lower Division students are re
quired to enroll for physical educa
tion courses, and all new students
are required to report to the Health
Service in the Pavilion for a health
examination appointment and to the
photographer there.
All students are required to make
arrangements for payment of tuition
or to pay it before turning in their
registration books. These are due at
the Pavilion before 5 p,m. on Tues
day. Otherwise, they will be sub
ject to late registration fees, which
are $2 until Oct. 4 and $10 there
after.
CARDS DUE
Study lists, as well as the IBM
cards issued in classes, are due at
the Registrar's Office on Oct. 7.
Veterans enrolling under Public
Law 346 may report to the Little
Theater in Memorial Auditorium,
with their registration books, be
tween 8 a.m. and 12 noon or from
1 to 5 p.m. either Monday or Tues
day.
Those enrolling under Public Law
16 may report to Room 129 in Me
morial Auditorium, with registration
books, at those times. Veterans en
rolling under either the California
State Program or the Canadian Pro
gram may report to Room 125. Me
morial Auditorium, at those times.
Veterans eligible for benefits un
der Public Law 550 are required to
report to the National Service Office,
Room 102-J. on or after Wednesday.

CONSULT ADVISERS
The next step, unless the student
has pre-registered, is a consultation
with the adviser, for completion of
the study list. Some advisers will
be stationed in the Pavilion; how
ever, most will he in their offices, as
listed in the time schedule, which is
available at the Registrar's Office
this morning.
Students who did not pre-register
will then sign class lists at the Pa
vilion. This assures admission to
the class, provided the student is
present at the first class meeting.
Lower Division students are re
quired to enroll for physical educa
tion courses, and all new students
are required to report to the Health
Service in the Pavilion for a health
examination appointment and to the
photographer there.
All students are required to make
arrangements for payment of tuition
or to pay it before turning in their
registration books. These are due at
the Pavilion before 5 p,m. on Tues
day. Otherwise, they will be sub
ject to late registration fees, which
are $2 until Oct. 4 and $10 there
after.

gram may report to Room 125. Me

der Public Law 550 are required to
report to the National Service Office,
Room 102-J. on or after Wednesday.
