# Married Women, Coeds Take Top Grade Honors
## 
### 
The women outdid the men, but
the University as a whole made a
solid C-f- average.
According to figures recently re
leased by the Scholarship oflice,
University grade point average for
the past year was 2.60, on the basis
of 4.0 for an A. Women had a 2.76
average, men 2.52. The greater num
ber of men causes the average score
to be lower than the mathematical
average of those two figures.
In spite of the lure of the beaches,
spring quarter averages showed a
considerable jump over the previous
quarters. All-campus standing, which
had been at 2.56 both autumn and
winter, leaped to 2.66.
Women, who had slipped from
2.76 in autumn to 2.73 in winter,
climbed to 2.81. Men had registered
2.47 and 2.48, but zoomed up to 2.59
in spring.
The highest average for a Univer
sity residence club was 3.13 achieved

by Sterling Hall, in the Village, a
dormitory for graduate and married
women. Hawthorne and The Oaks,
two Union Units, were also 4 stand
ard deviations above the University
average.
Breakers eating club made the
be.-,t showing among men's groups,
2.77, followed by the Alpha Kappa
Lambda fraternity with 2.75.
Other eating clubs which beat the
University average are El Tigre, El
Cuadro and El Campo. The other
fraternities with above - average
scholarship are Sigma Alpha Epsi
lon, Alpha Tau Omega, Kappa Alpha
♦und Delta Kappa Epsilon.
Among men's residences, Stern
was tops, with Toyon also above the
average.
Off-campus women made higher
grades than those in University resi
dences, but off-campus men scarcely
exceeded those in dorms, clubs or
fraternities.
