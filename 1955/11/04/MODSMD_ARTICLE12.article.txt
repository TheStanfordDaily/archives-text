# 1955 California-Stanford Agreement
## 
### 
In order to avoid any occurrence that might endanger the friendly
relations that have existed between our Universities for so many years,
and in order to maintain the fine sportsmanship that has been the
tradition of our "Big Game," we, the representatives of our student
bodies, do hereby resolve:
(a) That there shail be no "raids" by the students of either Uni
versity on the campus of the other, and that, students participating in
such violations will be subject to severe discipline. The term, raid,
.'■hall be understood to include the dropping of leaflets and other ma
terial from aircraft.
(b) That each student body will undertake to educate its members
in the weeks preceding the "Big Game" to observe the spirit of this
resolution, and to exercise every possible measure to prevent any in
cident arising that may lead to its violation.
HURFORD E. STONE H. DONALD WINBIGLER
Dean of Students Dean of Students
University of California Stanford University
ROBERT HAMILTON GEORGE W. RALPH
President, Associated Students President, Associated Students
University of California Stanford University


Jim Kt'iuH

NO AGGRESSION — Stanford and California representatives
yesterday signed a "non-aggression" pact at Berkeley in prepa
ration for this year's Big Game. The agreement provides that
each college will enforce "severe discipline" in the event that
members of either student body "raid" the other campus. Sign
ing the pact are (first row) Robert Hamilton, president of the
Associated Students of the University of California, and George
Ralph, ASSU president, and (back row) Stanford Dean of Stu
dents H. Donald Winbigler and Uiuveibily of California Dean
of Students Hurford E. Stone.
