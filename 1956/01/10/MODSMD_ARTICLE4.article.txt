# Copland to Speak Friday On Music in the Twenties
## Sunday Concert Will Feature Five Compositions by American Composer 
### 
Aaron Copland, famous American composer of such works as
"Appalachian Spring" and "Billy the Kid," v/ill be at Stanford this
weekend for a lecture and concert of his compositions.
Copland will speak on "Music in the Twenties" in Cubberley
Auditorium Friday at 4:15 p.m. The concert will' be in Cubberley

on Sunday afternoon at 4.
Both events are open to the public
free of charge under the auspices of
the Department of Music.
Copland is noted as a performer,
lecturer, author and teacher as well
as for his compositions. He has
taught at the New School for Social
Research, the Berkshire Music Cen
ter and Harvard University.
Books which he has written in
clude "What to Listen For in Music"
and "Our New Music."
The Sunday afternoon concert will
include two choruses from Copland's
recent opera "The Tender Land";
"Two Pieces for String Quartet"; the
choral piece "The Lark"; and "So
nata for Violin and Piano" to be
played by Professors Sandor Salgo

and William Loran Crosten.
Another feature of the concert will
be "Lincoln Portrait," a composition
for speaker and orchestra, with Rob
ert Loper of the Stanford Speech
and Drama Department in the
speaking role.
The Stanford Chorale, under the
direction of Harold C. Schmidt, and
the Stanford Symphony Orchestra,
with Professor Salgo conducting, will
participate.
Copland is noted for his ability to
portray the contemporary American
scene in his compositions. Such
works as "Rodeo" are examples of
this musical interpretation of typi
cally American phenomena.
