# Pre-Reg Com Tryouts Open
## 
### 
Tryouts begin tomorrow to
choose members for the pi e-regis
tration committee, whose main
job is planning activities for the
freshmen entering fall quarter.
Thirty-four positions are open
on the committee, which works
all year on every pro-registration
program. Plans will be made in
spring quarter for the fall pro-reg
week, according to Bryant Boat
wright, committee chairman.
Tryouts will be held tomorrow
from 1:30 to 5 p.m. and 7 to 10
p.m.; Friday from 1:30 to 3 p.m.;
and Saturday from 9 Jo 12 noon.
All interviews will he in the old
Excom room.
Qualifications
Candidates will be judged pri
marily on personality, ideas for
activities, availability (to come
early fall quarter) and previous
committee experience, according
to Boat wright.
Pro-reg committee handles all
aspects of greeting the incoming
freshmen. It will begin work ap
proximately a week before open
ing of fall quarter and finish up
on registration day.
Frmli Tours
Activities the group plans in
( lude tours of the campus. library
and Hoover Tower; a picnic and
songfest and faculty open houses.
Thry are iir charge of the Jolly
Up and freshman convocation.
The committee also has charge
of an orientation program for
transfer and graduate students.
