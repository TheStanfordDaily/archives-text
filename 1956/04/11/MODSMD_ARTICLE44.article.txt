schools open to American stu
dents has been published by the
Institute of International Educa
tion, 1 E. 67th St., New York City.
"Summer Study Abroad 1956"
lists 180 summer courses at edu
cational institutions in 22 coun
tries. It tells where to apply and
gives information on language re
quirements, a d m is si on proce
dures, credits, transportation and
costs.
In Latin America, courses are
offered at schools in Cuba, the
Dominican Republic, Guatemala,
Mexico and Peru.
Copies of the pamphlet may be
obtained free from the Informa
tion Division of the Institute or
from its regional office in San
Francisco.
