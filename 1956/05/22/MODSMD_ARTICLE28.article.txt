# TENNIS TOURNEY
## Card Netters Pull Upsets 
### 
Stanford tennis stars Jack Frost
scored impressive upset wins last
week in Berkeley in the California
State Tournament.
Frost upset Northern California
Intercollegiate Champion Jerry
DeWitts, 61, 6-4, in the quarter
finals.
In the semi-final round, the
Monterey slugger sped past the
Olympic Club's Harry Buttimer,
3-6, 7-5, 6-4, to reach the finals for
the third straight year.
Playing Alameda airman Whit
ney Reed in Ihe championship
match, Frost went down fighting,
6-1, 6-3, 3-6, 6-1.
Reed this year has won the U.S.
Air Force Championship and the
La Jolla Championship. He is
rated by Northern California Ten
nis President Jim Moffet as the
coming player of the 1956 season.
Douglas upset "Butch" Kriko
rian in the fourth round and
fourth-ranked Nick Carter.
Against Carter, the unseeded
Santa Monica ace was down, 4-1,
in the first set. But he rallied and
pulled it out by a decisive 7-5, 6-3
score.
This victory advanced Douglas
to the semi-finals, where he lost
a 7-5, 6-0, 6-3 match to Reed.
