# Japanese Sword Display Extended
## 
### 
Exhibition time for the collec
tion of Japanese swords at the
Stanford Museum has been ex
tended to the end of July.
The exhibit consists of 30 swords
and 50 tsuba loaned to the gallery
by the Japanese Sword Collectors
Club of Northern California.
The sword as a weapon and its
methods of manufacture were in
troduced into Japan from China
and Korea about the beginning of
the Christian era. The swords on
display at the museum range from
the 13th century through the 20th
century.
Museum hours are daily from 1
to 5 p.m. Admission is 25 cents
except for Stanford students and
faculty, who are admitted free of
charge.
