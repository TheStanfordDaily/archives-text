SENIOR EXCOM
A sign-up sheet for senior class
executive committee interviews
will be posted beginning today
outside the ASSU office. Inter
views begin Monday afternoon,
Oct. 1, and continue that evening
and the following afternoons and
evenings through Oct. 4.
Interviews will take place on
the third floor of the Women's
Clubhouse, according to Fred
For tine, class president. All sen
iors are eligible to try out for
excom.
11 It
Interviews to place students in
terested in the different depart
ments of lIR (SOIS, SIRO, Con
ference, Model UN, Host, Affilia
tions, UN Education) will be held
in the IIR shack across from Ge
ology Corner today and tomor
row from 4 to 6 p.m. All those
who signed the lIR list at the Pa
vilion and all others interested in
international relations are invited
to drop in and ask questions.
ELECTIONS COMMISSION
On Friday from 3 to 5 p.m. and
Monday from 4 to 6 p.m. in Ex
com room, and Tuesday from 7 to
0 p.m. in the Women's Clubhouse
there will be tryouts for the Elec
tions Commission. All students
are eligible. The signup list for
interviews is posted outside the
ASSU office. Former members
of the commission who wish to
work again this year on the AS
SU elections should contact Mike
Serlin this week or leave their
names on the bottom of the sign
up list outside the ASSU office.

CHAPPIE
The Stanford Chaparral will
hold tryouts and entertain pros
pective staffers at an open house
at 3:30 p.m. today in the Chappie
office across from Geology Cor
ner.
Both men and women students
are needed as artists, writers, of
fice workers and cartoonists, for
both business and editorial de
partments.
POLI UNION
The Stanford Political Union
will hold tryouts for publicity
chairman today from 1:30 to 5:30
p.m. at the Nitery. An executive
board meeting will be held the
same afternoon at 5:15 p.m. The
agenda for the meeting includes
planning a program schedule and
submitting ideas for student par
ticipation in political affairs.
