# Negro Leaders Call Meeting On Bus, School Integration
## 
### 
MONTGOMERY, Ala., Jan. 7 (UP) Negro leaders of drives to
end segregation on city buses in Montgomery, Tallahassee, Fla.,
and Birmingham, Ala., today called for an emergency meeting in
Atlanta Jan. 10 "to spur the campaign for integrated transporta
tion."
Negro integration ®aders here said that the two-day meeting
was planned during telephone conferences between the Rev. M. L.
King of Montgomery, the Rev. C. K. Steele of Tallahassee and the
Rev. F. L. Shuttlesworth of Birmingham, all leaders of drives for
mixed seating on city buses.
Earlier today, the City Commission in Tallahassee passed an
ordinance that would allow bus drivers to assign seats on city
buses, and bus segregation officially came to an end in Chatta
nooga, Tenn.
In Knoxville, Tenn., a group of Negroes late today filed suit in
federal court seeking racial integration of Knoxville city schools.
The lawsuit, entered by the National Association for the Advance
ment of Colored People, was filed in the same U.S. District Court
that last year ordered the high school at nearby Clinton to admit
Negro students.
