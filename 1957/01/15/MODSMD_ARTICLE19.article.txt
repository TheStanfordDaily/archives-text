# Eating Clubs End Open Rush Season; Houses on Sunday
## 
### 
Stanford's seven eating clubs,
through Interclub Council presi
dent Pete Mansfield, have an
nounced that open rushing for
1957 has ended. The clubs held
their final round of open houses
last night.
Open rushing ended for frater
nities Sunday night at 9 when the
formal rushing period was usher
ed in with a wave of house par
ties. The clubs and houses have
now set about organizing their
pledge classes.
Fraternity and eating club men
will be allowed in Wilbur lounges
between 7 a.m. and 11 p.m. until
after Thursday night parties. The
only hours they may visit fresh
man rooms are between 11:30
a.m. and 1:30 p.m. and between
5 and 7:30 p.m.
After Thursday night parties,
the no-contact rule will again be
in effect between fraternity men
and the frosh. At 5:15 Friday aft
ernoon no-contact will end in prep
aration for pledge card signing
dinners Friday evening.
No further "no contact" is en
countered by eating club mem
bers. Rushees may accept club
bids at any time.
