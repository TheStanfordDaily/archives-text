# House to Debate Post Office Bill
## 
### 
WASHINGTON, April 14 (UP)
- The House will he asked tomor
row to stamp its approval on a
$41 million "money order" needed
by the Post Office Department to
restore Saturday mail service.
Although favorable action was
indicated, the department went
ahead with plans to cut back other
postal services tomorrow until the
emergency money bill clears Con
gress and is signed by President
Eisenhower.
Meanwhile, the nation's letter
carriers faced the task of deliver
ing on Monday a great mass of
mail that accumulated Saturday
when Postmaster General Arthur
Summerfield stayed them from
their appointed rounds.
