# Racket Team Overpowers California, 7-2
## 
### 
Winning three of their matches
only after three sets, Stanford's
tennis team cut down California
Saturday in Berkeley, 7-2.
In tlie preliminary, the high
powered Papoose netmen squash
id the Baby Bears in an X-l rout.
The Indians clinched the var
sity till by copping the first five
singles matches, three of which
took three sets.
Jack Douglas ran away with
the first singles match with an
easy (>-4, 6-0 win over Lorin Denni
son. In second singles. Brooks
Rawlins rallied to upend Don Pim
ley in three sets, 3-6, 0-3, 6-3.
Dave Nelson eased by veteran
Larry Axtell in third singles, 6-4,
6-3, but Gil White needed the long
est set of the flay to beat Cal's
fourth singles man, Don Stensaas,
6-4, 12-1 1, 6-3.
Another extra-set win came to
Stanford in fifth singles as Low
ell Carruth outlasted Sheldon Dil
ler in a 6-4, 2-6, 6-4 struggle. Cal's
first win of the afternoon occur
red in the sixth match when Clay
Bradley slipped by A 1 Galloway,
6-4, 4-6, 6-3.
In first doubles, Douglas and
Nelson started it off with a bril
liant 6-1, 6-1 victory over Pimley
and Stensaas.
The third doubles duo of Bob
Bowden and Carruth turned in
the final Indian triumph as they
conquered Charles Raymer and
Bradley, 6-3, 7-5.
Axtell and Dennison, playing
their second year of intercollegi
ate competition together, ground
out a 6-3, 6-4 win over Stanford's
Rawlins and White.
