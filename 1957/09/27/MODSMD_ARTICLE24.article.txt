# Tryouts
## 
### 
AYVS
Women from all classes may
attend tryouts for Associated
Women Students standing com
mittees, which began yesterday
and will continue Monday, Tues
day and Wednesday. Tryouts are
being held from 7 to 9:30 p.m. in
Room 10 of the Women's Club
house.
lIR
Interviews for a political ana
lyst to act as special assistant to
the president and the policy
board will be hold next week by
the Institute of International Re
lations. Tryouts are open, the
only requirement being a work
ing knowledge of world affairs.
Students interested in an inter
view with the president should
call or stop by the lIR office be
tween 4 and 6 p.m. any day next
week except Thursday.
KZSU
Tryouts for KZSU announcers
will be held from 3 to 5 p.m. and
7 to 9 p.m. in the KZSU studios
in Memorial Hall. Tryouts for
maintenance and board engineers
will bo held from 5 to G and from
7 to 9 p.m. Those interested in
office work, producing or other
radio jobs may get information
on those at these times from ei
ther Steve Baffrey or Gary Tripp.
MEN'S GLEE CLUB
Glee Club auditions will, lie
held today from 4:30 to 5:30 p.m.
in the chorus rehearsal room at
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. All in
terested men may try out
PUBLIC RELATIONS
Wednesday, Thursday and Fri
day next week have been set as

dates for tryouts for Public Re
lations Board in the Public Rela
tions office from 1 to 5 p.m. Ten
to 15 positions are open for
freshman students. Public Rela
tions regulates campus publicity.
SENIOR ACTIVITY BOARD
Senior Activity Board, which
will work in co-operation with
the Senior Excom on the presen
tation and arrangement of sen
ior activities, is having tryouts
from 1 to 4 p.m. Sign-up sheet
is in the ASSU office today.
SEQUOIA

available to students interested
in working on Sequoia, the Stan
ford literary magazine. A sign
up list for interviews any time
this week will be aavilable in
temporary building IA, behind
Cubberley Auditorium.

will be held from 3 to 5 p.m. and
7 to 9 p.m. in the KZSU studios
in Memorial Hall. Tryouts for
maintenance and board engineers
will bo held from 5 to G and from
7 to 9 p.m. Those interested in
office work, producing or other
radio jobs may get information
on those at these times from ei
ther Steve Baffrey or Gary Tripp.
MEN'S GLEE CLUB
Glee Club auditions will, lie
held today from 4:30 to 5:30 p.m.
in the chorus rehearsal room at
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. All in
terested men may try out
PUBLIC RELATIONS
Wednesday, Thursday and Fri
day next week have been set as

dates for tryouts for Public Re
lations Board in the Public Rela
tions office from 1 to 5 p.m. Ten
to 15 positions are open for
freshman students. Public Rela
tions regulates campus publicity.
SENIOR ACTIVITY BOARD
Senior Activity Board, which
will work in co-operation with
the Senior Excom on the presen
tation and arrangement of sen
ior activities, is having tryouts
from 1 to 4 p.m. Sign-up sheet
is in the ASSU office today.
SEQUOIA
Editorial and art openings are
available to students interested
in working on Sequoia, the Stan
ford literary magazine. A sign
up list for interviews any time
this week will be aavilable in
temporary building IA, behind
Cubberley Auditorium.
STUDENT POLICE
Students interested in a job on
the Stanford Student Police force
should contact Don Potters be
tween 5 and 6 p.m. today at the
Theta Chi house.
AYVS
Women from all classes may
attend tryouts for Associated
Women Students standing com
mittees, which began yesterday
and will continue Monday, Tues
day and Wednesday. Tryouts are
being held from 7 to 9:30 p.m. in
Room 10 of the Women's Club
house.
lIR

lyst to act as special assistant to
the president and the policy
board will be hold next week by
the Institute of International Re
lations. Tryouts are open, the
only requirement being a work
ing knowledge of world affairs.
Students interested in an inter
view with the president should
call or stop by the lIR office be
tween 4 and 6 p.m. any day next
week except Thursday.
