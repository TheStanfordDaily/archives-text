# Tryouts Held For Pre-Reg
## 
### 
Tryouts begin today to choose
members for the Pre • Registra
tion Committee, whose main job
is planning activities for the
freshmen entering fall quarter.
Thirty-five positions are open
on the committee, which works
all year on every pre-registration
program. Plans will be made in
] spring quarter for the fall pre
reg week, according to John Phil
lips, committee chairman.
Tryouts will be held today
from 7:30 to 10:30 p.m., tomor
row from 3:30 to 5:30 p.m. and
from 7 to 8:30 p.m. and Wednes
day from 7 to 10:30 p.m. All in
terviews will be in .Room 12, third
; floor, Women's Clubhouse.
Candidates will be judged pri
marily on personality, ideas for
activities and availability (to
come early fall quarter), accord
ing to Phillips.
Pre-Reg Committee handles all
aspects of greeting the incoming
freshmen. It will begin work ap
proximately a week before open
ing of autumn quarter and finish
up on registration day.
The committee also has charge
of an orientation program for
transfer and graduate students.
