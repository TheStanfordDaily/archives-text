PRE REG
Persons interested in serving
on the Pre-Registration Commit
tee may try out today from 3 to
5:30 p.m. in Room 12 on the third
floor of the Women's Clubhouse.
Thirty-five positions are open
and candidates will be judged on
personality, new ideas for pre
reg and availability during the
pre-reg period.
The Pre-Reg Committee han
dles all aspects of greeting in
coming freshmen, and its work
begins approximately one week
before autumn quarter registra
tion. The committee also has
charge of the orientation pro
grams for graduate and transfer
students, according to committee
chairman John Phelps.
