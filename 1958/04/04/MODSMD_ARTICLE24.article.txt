# Ginzton Receives Liebmann Prize For Study of Microwave Frequency
## 
### 
Professor Edward L. Ginzton,
head of the Stanford Microwave
Laboratory, received the Morris
Liebmann Memorial Prize of the
Institute of Radio Engineers in
New York last Wednesday night
"for his creative contribution to
the generation and useful appli
cation of high energy at micro
wave frequencies."
Ginzton is currently on sabbati
cal leave at the European Coun
cil for Nuclear Research head
quarters in Geneva, Switzerland.
He directed the building of the
Mark 111 accelerator, developed
the klystron into the most power
ful vacuum tube in existence.


PROFESSOR GINZTON
Microwave Researcher
