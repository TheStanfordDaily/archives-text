# Douglas in Ojai Tennis Third Round
## 
### John Schwarz 
OJAl—Stanford comes a little
closer each time it meets SC and
UCLA, but the Card netters still
came off second best in the first
day of action here at the Ojai
Tennis Tournament.
Jack Douglas is the only Stan
ford man left in the competition.
He will play John Cranston of
UCLA in the singles quarter-fi
nals this morning. If Douglas
wins, ho will later today meet the
winner of the Mike Franks (UC
LA )-John Davis (USC) quarter
final match.
Douglas won his first-round
match from Jim Buck of USC,
12-10, 6-4. Douglas countered
Buck's excellent service with
good returns of his own through
out the match. There were only
two service breaks in the match,
each going to Douglas at the end
of a set.
Robin ITilgeman and Jim Jef
fries played the day's best doub
les for Stanford, finally losing to
the number one USC team of
Alex Olmedo and Brooks Atkin
son, 4-6, 6-3, 6-4. Hilgeman and
Jeffries had a 2-0 lead in the sec
ond set before they bowed.
In second-round results, Doug
las and Brooks Rawlins led 5-2 in
the third set, but Rawlins' in
jured leg finally wore down as
the UCLA team of Rohland and
Johnson won the final five games.
After one day of play, UCLA
has nine points to five for USC
and four for Stanford.
First round —Carruth d. Sis
son (C) 7-5, 6-4; Guzman (SC)
d. Rawlins 6-4, 6-2; Atkinson

(C) 6-4, 6-3; Douglas-Rawlins d.
Cohen-Sisson (C) 6-3, 6-3.
