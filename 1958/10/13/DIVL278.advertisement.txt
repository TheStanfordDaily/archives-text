Science Majors
Lockheed Missile Systems Division is systems manager for such major, long-term
projects as the Navy Polaris IRBM; Air Force Earth Satellite, H-5, X-7; Army
Kingfisher and other important research and development programs. The divi
sion was honored at the first National Missile Industry Conference as "the
organization that contributed most in the past year to the development of Ihe
art of missiles and astronautics."
Headquarters for the division are located at Sunnyvale, California, on the
San Francisco Peninsula. Research and development facilities are in the Stan
ford Industrial Park, in nearby Palo Alto. Other division locations are iti
Van Nuys, Santa Cruz and Santa Maria, California; Cape Canaveral. Florida;
Alamogordo. New Mexico; and Hawaii. Together, they provide complete
facilities with the latest scientific and technical equipment, including one of
the most advanced computing centers in the nation. Employee benefits are
among the best in the industry.
For those who qualify and desire tb continue their education, the Graduate
Study Program enables them to obtain advanced degrees at the University of
California at Berkeley or at Stanford University, while employed in their chosen
fields at Lockheed.
Our College Relations Administrator, Mr. R. C. Beverstock, will be avail
able on campus for interviews on October 15 and 16. For appointment, please
see your Placement Director now.
