# Class of '33 Will Return
## 
### 
More than 100 members of the
Class of 1933 return to Palo Alto
today to begin festivities marking
the class's 25th anniversary.
("lass members will spend the
day golfing on the Stanford
course. At 6 p.m. a cocktail party
and a buffet dinner have been
planned at Rickey's Studio Inn.
Following dinner the group will
attend Big Game Gaieties at Me
morial Auditorium.
Tomorrow 33'er.s will gather in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium at 9:30
a.m. to hear Dr. J. Wallace Ster
ling, whose talk will be followed
by a panel discussion of Stanford
affairs.
Participants include H. Donald
Winbigler, dean of students; Phil
ip 11. Rhinelandcr, dean of the
School of Humanities and Sci
ences; Rixford K. Snyder, direc
tor of admissions; Alf E. Brandin,
business manager; and Chuck
Taylor, assistant director of ath
letics.
Class members will adjourn to
Bowman Alumni House for lunch
and will then tour the campus,
stopping at Wilbur and Florence
Moore Hall, where they will be
taken on a tour of these newest
residences.
Tomorrow at 6 p.m. the group
will have coclftails at the Mark
Hopkins Hotel, San Francisco, fol
lowed at 8 p.m. by'the silver an
niversary banquet and dance in
the hotel's Peacock Court.
