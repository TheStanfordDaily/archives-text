# Frosh Rushing to Begin Today for Eating Clubs
## 
### 
.Rushing activities, conducted by Stanford's seven eating clus and
22 eligible fraternities, begin tonight with open houses at the eating
clubs for the four south houses of Wilbur.
Fraternity open houses start tomorrow.
The eating clubs announced the following schedule of open houses:

Tonight all clubs open from
7:30 to 9:30 for Arroyo, Cedro, Ju
nipero and Madera freshmen.
Tomorrow, all clubs open from
6 to 8 p.m. for Otero, Rinconada,
Soto and Traneos freshmen.
Friday, all clubs open to all
freshmen between 7:15 and 9:30
p.m.
Monday, April 6, all clubs will
be open to all freshmen between
7:15 and 10:15 p.m.
Rushing Dress
Bob Fuller, Interclub Council
publicity chairman, said that
dress for club rushing is "sport."
Refreshments will be served at
the open houses by hostesses, he
added.
"All of the eating clubs have
made great strides in the way of
physical improvements and better
social programs," Fuller said.
He had this suggestion for
freshmen: "The operation of a
club kitchen is highly interesting.
When you visit the clubs be sure
to inquire about these and the
many other aspects in the func
tioning of air eating club."
Fraternity Rush Program
Fraternities will conduct open
houses tomorrow through Thurs
day, and formal rush parties at
tended by invitation only will be
gin Friday and run through Mon
day. Tuesday night pledge cards
i will be signed at special card sign
ing dinners.
