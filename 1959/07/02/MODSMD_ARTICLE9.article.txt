# Terman Named University's Vice-President
## 
### 
Changes in the administrative
organization of Stanford Univer
sity were announced today by
President Wallace Sterling. They
were approved by the University's
Board of Trustees at their June 18
meeting and will become effective
September 1.
Provost Frederick E. Terman
becomes vice president and prov
ost.
Alf E. Brandin. business man
ager and executive officer for
land development, becomes vice
president for business affairs.
Kenneth M. Cuthbertson, assist
ant to ihc president, becomes vice
president for finance.
Frederic O. Glover, assistant to
the president, becomes Dr. Ster
ling's executive assistant.
Kesult of Study
The reorganization was the re
sult of an 18 months' study, in
recognition of the growth in size
and scope of Stanford's total oper
ation. It is designed to allow the
president to participate even more
directly than before in the devel
opment of Stanford's vigorous ac
ademic program.
Dr. Sterling will also preside
over a newly established Commit
tee on University Policy, which
consists of the deans of the vari
ous schools, representatives of the
faculty and University executive
officers.
Dr. Terman, internationally fa
mous electrical engineer and dean
of the University's school of engi
neering from 1945 to 19:18, will be
responsible for academic opera
tions. He will be assisted by Dr.
Robert J. Wert, newly appointed
vice provost.
Brandin, business manager since
1946, will continue his responsibil
ities for management of the Uni
versity's business operations and
development of University lands.
Added Duties
His added duties will include
supervision of planning for Uni
versity facilities, of non-academic
personnel programs, and of the
business functions of the Univer
sity Press.
Cuthbertson, assistant to the
president since 1954, will be re
sponsible for the planning and
management of the University's
financial affairs, including its
fund raising activities and public
information programs associated
with them.
David S. Jacobson, general sec
retary since 1952, and Peter C.
Allen, director of the News and
Publications Service, will retain
their respective positions.
Glover, assistant 1o the presi
dent since 1952, will assume great
er responsibilities in the presi
dent's office, especially in regard
to Stanford's educational associa
tions with other colleges and uni
versities.

President Wallace Sterling. They
were approved by the University's
Board of Trustees at their June 18
meeting and will become effective
September 1.

becomes vice president and prov
ost.

ager and executive officer for
land development, becomes vice
president for business affairs.
Kenneth M. Cuthbertson, assist
ant to ihc president, becomes vice
president for finance.
Frederic O. Glover, assistant to
the president, becomes Dr. Ster
ling's executive assistant.
Kesult of Study
The reorganization was the re
sult of an 18 months' study, in
recognition of the growth in size
and scope of Stanford's total oper
ation. It is designed to allow the
president to participate even more
directly than before in the devel
opment of Stanford's vigorous ac
ademic program.
Dr. Sterling will also preside
over a newly established Commit
tee on University Policy, which
consists of the deans of the vari
ous schools, representatives of the
faculty and University executive
officers.
