# Propaganda Films Planned
## 
### 
Four classics in the field of mo
tion picture propaganda will bo
shown in a special series this
quarter.
The films, in order of show
ing, include Sergei Eistenstein's
"Strike," 1925; Leni Riefenstahl's
"Triumph of the Will," 1934; D.
W. Griffith's "Birth of a Nation,"
1915; and Rene Clement's "Battle
of the Rails," 1945.
The presentations are on con
secutive Wednesdays beginning
Oct. 14 in Cubberley Auditorium
at 4:15 and 7:30 p.m. Tickets are
$2 for the series and may be ob
tained now at Memorial Audito
rium box office.
"Strike" deals with a Russian
pre-revolutionary strike and its
bloody post - revolutionary sup
pression.
"Triumph of the Will" was
filmed by 93 cameramen in a sup
posed documentary of the first
Nazi party conference at Nurem
burg.
"Birth of a Nation" pictures the
South during the Civil War and
Reconstruction.
"Battle of the Rails" dramatizes
the battle between French rail
road workers and Nazi occupa
tion forces.
