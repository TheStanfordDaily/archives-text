# Schedule 'Strike' Propaganda First
## 
### 
Sergei Eisenstein's "Strike,"
the first of four classics of motion
picture propaganda to be shown
this quarter, will be presented
Wednesday, October 14 at 4:15
and 7:30 p.m. in Cubberley Audi
torium. "Strike" deals with a
Russian pre-revolutionary strike
and its bloody post-revolutionary
suppression.
Tickets are $2 for the series
and may be obtained now at the
Memorial Auditorium box office.
Other films to be shown on suc
cessive Wednesdays are Leni Rie
fenstahl's "Triumph of the Will,"
a documentary of the first Nazi
party conference at Nuremburg,
D. W. Griffith's "Birth of a Na
tion," picturing the South during
the Civil War and Reconstruc
tion, and Rene Clement's "Battle
of the Rails," depicting the bat
tle between French railroad work
ers and Nazi occupation forces.
