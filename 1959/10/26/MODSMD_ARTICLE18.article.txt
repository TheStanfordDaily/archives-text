# Stanford Gets $225,000 in Aid
## 
### 
The University has received
two financial grants worth a total
of $225,000; one for new pro
grams in the School of Medicine
and the other for graduate engi
neering students.
The Ford Foundation granted
$200,000 to Stanford. $125,000 will
be used for fellowships and loans
leading to doctorates for students
planning to teach engineering.
$25,000 will go for additional
loans, and $50,000 for faculty ex
changes and lecturers from other
engineering schools.
The U.S. Public Health Service
gave a $25,000 to support a train
ing program in pharmacology.
The program wil be headed by
Dr. Avram Goldstein, professor
of pharmacology.
