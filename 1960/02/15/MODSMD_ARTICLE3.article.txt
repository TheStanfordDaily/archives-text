# Yugoslavian Envoy Slated
## 
### 
Now Yugoslavian Ambassador
to the United States, Marko Nikc
zic, who is on his first tour of the
West Coast, will speak today at
1:15 p.m. in Cubberly Auditorium
on "Contemporary Yugoslavia and
Her Foreign Policy."
Nike/ic. 38. attended high school
and studied architecture in Bel
grade. He fought the Nazis in
World War II and after the war
became deputy mayor of Belgrade
and a member of the Yugoslav
Parliament.
From 1953 to 1956 he was min
ister and later ambassador to
Egypt of the "Federal People's
Republic of Yugoslavia." He was
amba s s a dor to Czechoslovakia
from 1956 to 1958.
A reception will be held for
Nikezicat Bowman Alumni House
at 3 p.m. Admittance is by invi
tation only.
