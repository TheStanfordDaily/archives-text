### 
Stanford's baseball team con-1
tinned to have their troubles at
Ihe plate yesterday as they drop
ped a 8-5 decision to Long Beach
State at the Sunken Diamond.)

blows, as the Fehringmen left
twelve stranded on the base paths
in the loss.
Marv Mecklenberg was the los
ing pitcher, as he was hit with a
streak of wildness in a fateful
eighth inning which saw the Forty
Niners score three times to put

the game on ice. In the frame,
Steve Hart man lead off with a
walk, Bill Swanner followed with
a bunt that Mecklenberg couldn't
handle, and then a sharp single
by Gary Geagen scored them

both and tho Long Beach te;im
was on the way.
Geagen and Hartman were (he
big guns for I ho Forty Niners, as
each had three hits in the nine
inninger, all singles, and each had
two RBl's. Mike Filice and Mike
Magee each got three hits for
the Indians in the losing effort.
Center fielder Hal Schutzmann
went two for four in the losing
cause.
Herd Hyman relieved starter
Jerry Nicholson in tho fifth and
picked up the win for Long Beach
State.
