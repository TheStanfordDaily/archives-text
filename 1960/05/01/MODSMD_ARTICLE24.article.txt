## 
### 
Sparked by the steady pitching
of Jim Smith, Stanford's varsity
baseballers bounced back to de
feat Pepperdine 8-2 Saturday af
ter losing to the Waves IX-9 Fri
day.
Smith scattered seven hits and
struck out six while going the full
nine inning stint. Jim Burk was
the big gun for the Indians as he
rapped a double and triple to
drive in four runs.
Stanford erupted for five runs
in the sixth inning to blow the
top off what had been a light 3-2
contest. With one out Lee Moench
and Smitli singled and Mike Ma
gee walked to load the bases.
Skip Lawrence waited out reliever
Ray Sims for a walk and Burk
followed with a double. Burk
later scored on third baseman Boh
Hammett's throwing error.
Magee and Mike Filice contrib

uted heavily to the Stanford at
tack, each getting two hits.
Friday proved a nightmare for
the Indians as seven pitchers pa
raded to the mound, giving up 12
hits and IS Walks. The ball game
took three and one-half hours.
