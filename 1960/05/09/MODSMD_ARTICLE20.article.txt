# Card Nine Splits Two With Cal
## Daily Sports 
### Roy Schmidt 
Stanford's Jim Smith notched his third straight win Saturday en
abling the Indians to romp over California, 8-1. The win avenged a
10-1 shellacking at the hands of the Bears Friday.
Smith, though not as sharp as usual, was able to bear down in the
tight spots. Cal left 16 runners stranded and were retired three times
with the bases loaded. A tight Card defense added to his effective-

ness, as it turned in two double
plays and played errorless ball.
A four-run outburst in the sixth
turned a close 3-1 hall game into
a rout, as Bear pitcher Bill Oak
ley momentarily lost his control
and theCal defense disintegrated.
Oakley walked Jim Cowart to
start things off and then gave up
a single to Mike Filice. Jay Win
ter laid down a bunt which Oakley
grabbed, but his throw to third
goi by Kev Searpelli and two runs
scored.
Smith followed with a walk, and
Hiil Schutzmann bunted lie and
Filice along. After Mike Ma gee
lined out, Skip Lawrence hit a
sharp gVoiind ball which second
baseman Terry Johnson managed
lo spear, but his off-balance throw
got by Noel Barnes at first allow
ing Winter and Smith to score.
Magee and Jim Burke lead the
Stanford attack with three hits
each; Magee drove one Card run
and Burke pushed across two.
Friday. Cal starter Ted Settle
pitched four-hit ball, holding the
Indians to just one score, while
his teammates teed off on four
Stanford pitchers for 12 hits.
The Cards square off against
University of San Francisco this
afternoon on the Sunken Diamond

at 3:15 in their rescheduled rain
out game of April 2(5.

tight spots. Cal left 16 runners stranded and were retired three times
