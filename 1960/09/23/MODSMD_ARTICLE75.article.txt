# Five Faculty, Seven Students Given Fulbright Scholarships
## 
### 
Fuibright Scholarships for for
eign study and research have been
awarded to five Stanford faculty
members and seven Stanford stu
dents.
Faculty member Miss Georgia
Ray Williams, instructor in wom
en's physical education, will be
lecturing al the State Teachers
Training College, Rangoon, Bur
ma, until March.
ASSISTANT PItOFKSSOIt John
Thompson, of geography, will at
tend the University of Brazil in
Rio de Janeiro for his work. An
drew F. Burghardt, also an assist
ant professor of geography, will
attend the University of Vienna
where he will study the political
geography of Burgenland, an Aus
trian border area, after serving
as a faculty member at Stanford
in Germany.
Rome will be the headquarters
for Professor Mollis B. Chenery,
professor of economics, who will

study and evaluate the Italian
government program for the eco
nomic development of Italy.
Dr. Mitchel Weissbluth, assist
ant proiessor ol radiologic phys
ics at Stanford Medical School,
will study medical and biological
physics at the Weizmann Insti
tute of Science in Israel.

and philosophy at the University

Czamanske will do work in geol
ogy at the University of Oslo,
Norway, while Paul Random - will
bo studying international law at

ing will attend the University of

modate three of the Fuibright

son.
