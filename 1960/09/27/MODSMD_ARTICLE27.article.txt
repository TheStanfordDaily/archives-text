# 3-Play Season Set by Players
## 
### 
Beatrice and Benedick will ex
change wits in Memorial Audi
torium this quarter when the
Stanford Players launch their
23rd season with William Shake
speare's "Much Ado About Noth
ing."
Tryouts for the show are sched
uled for tonight, tomorrow and
Thursday at 7:30 p.m. in Me
morial Auditorium. A large cast
is needed, Director James Kerans
said, and urged everyone inter
ested to try out.
The show, the first of Will's
"joyous" comedies, is scheduled
for Oct. 2.5, 2!) and Nov. 3-5 in
Memorial Auditorium. II will he
staged and costumed in the style
of Vienna in the 1890's.
Jean Anouilh's "Waltz of the
Toreadors" will be the second pro
duction by the Players in Me
morial Auditorium on Feb. 3, 1,
9-1.0 p.m. Following this, Henrik
Ibsen's "Peer CJynt" will lie staged
in Frost Amphitheater. May 25-27.
Season tickets for the three
shows are now on sale at $1. $1.50,
$5.10, $5.85. $0.75 at Mem Aud
weekday afternoons between
12:30 and 1:30 p.m.
