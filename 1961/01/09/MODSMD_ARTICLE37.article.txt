# Earth Science Series Starts
## 
### 
A series on "The Earth Sciences,
Their Value Today" starts this
afternoon with two lectures on
"Mapping the Earth's Ancient
Magnetic Field."
Dr. Richard R. Doell, geophysi
cist with the U.S. Geological Sur
vey. will lecture on "Concepts and
History" at 4 p.m. and on "Prin
ciples and Problems" at 8 p.m. in
Room 100, Physics Lecture Hall.
The series sponsored by the
School of Mineral Sciences, open
to the public without charge,
brings nationally known experts
to the campus to describe current
research and problems confront
ing the earth sciences.
The second program, under
joint sponsorship with the Tues
day Evening Series, is "Hawaiian
Volcanics" by Dr. Gordon A. Mac
donald, senior professor of geol
ogy at the University of Hawaii,
at 7:30 p.m. in Memorial Audi
torium.
Macdonald will also lecture on
the geology of the Hawaiian Is
lands at 4 p.m. and on Hawaiian
petrology at 8 p.m. in the Physics
Lecture Hall Jan. 18.
More than a dozen other pro
grams on various topics in the
earth sciences complete the series
which runs through Feb. 27.
