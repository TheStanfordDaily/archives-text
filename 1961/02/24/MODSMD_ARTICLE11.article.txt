# Stanford Hosts Northern Cal Debate Contest
## 
### 
The winter tournament of the
Northern California Forensic As
sociation starts today with senior
division debates today and tomor
row on Ihe Stanford campus.
Debates, open to Stanford stu
dents and faculty, will be held to
day at 3:30 and 5 p.m. and tomor
row at 9 a.m., 10:30 a.m. and 1
p.m. Headquarters will be in the
Nitery.
Finals will be held at 2:30 p.m.
tomorrow in room 15, Cubberley.
Stanford teams entered in the de
bate are Bob Sayler and Bob Best,
Bill Dysart and Keith YVinsell,
Dick Weinig and Dick Noble. Mike
Hall and Jim Woolsey and Ken
Purcell and Dan Tarlock.
The junior division tournament
will be held on the College of San
Mateo campus. Freshmen and
sophomore debaters will enter in
dividual events in oratory, exposi
tory speaking, interpretative read
ing and impromptu speaking as
well as compete in debate.
A debate on compulsory health
insurance will bo held at 8:13 p.m.
Monday between Stanford and
Yeshiva University of New York
in room 61H. Stanford's Dysart
and Winsell will argue in favor of
compulsory insurance. It is free
to the public.
