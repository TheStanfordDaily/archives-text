# Card BaseBallers Open Season With Double Win
## 
### WARREN SCHLOAT 
The varsity baseball team
opened the 1961 season at home
with two wins in as many days,
beating Cal Poly, $-7, on Friday
and San Jose State on Saturday
by 4-1.
Against San Jose, on the com
bined four-hit pitching of Marv
Mecklenburg, Jim S m i th, and
Mark Miller, pitching three
innings apiece, the Tribe held the
visitors to a single tally.
Stanford opened up the scoring
with one run in the fourth inning
on a double by captain Mike Ma
gee, followed by a triple to deep
right by John Hurlbut. It was
Hurlbut's second triple in two
days.
Two more Card tallies in the
fifth inning came when, with Mike
Pi 1 ice on third as a result of a
walk, a sacrifice and an error, and
with Hal Sehutx.mann on first,
Skip Lawrence smashed a triple
to left center scoring both runners.
The final Indian tally crossed
the plate in the eighth inning as
Bob Overman, hitting for Law
rence, singled to light, advanced
to second on a force out, went to
third on a passed ball, and scored
on Rich Ferra/zano's single to
left.
In Friday's game against Cal
Poly, Stanford pulled ahead and
won the game with a six-run
splurge in the sixth inning.
Joe Schulman opened the inning
by drawing a walk and reached
second on Bob Hensler's single.
Following Shut/mann's base on
balls to load the bases, Skip Law
rence singled 1o right, scoring
Schulman and Hensler. Ma gee
then singled to left, driving
Shutzmann home and m o vi n g
Lawrence to .second.
