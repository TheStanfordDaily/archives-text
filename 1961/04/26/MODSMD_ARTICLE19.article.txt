# 7-2 VICTORY
## Hurlbut Leads Cards Past USF 
### ROY SCHMIDT 
Loosening up for their big series against USC this weekend, the
Stanford Indians romped over a hapless University of San Francisco
nine, 7-2, yesterday on the Sunken Diamond.
The Dons committed four errors afield and their generous pitchers,
A1 Kirkes and Ray Ware, allowed ten Stanford hits. Lefty Marv

Mecklenberg went six innings al
lowing two runs and three hits be
fore being lifted for a pinch hitter.
Terry Lindeman finished up.
THE TWO Card hurlers mowed
down 11 USF hitters, Mecklenberg
getting six and Lindeman whiff
ing five.
The Indians jumped off to a
1-0 lead in the third as slump
ridden Mike Magee smacked a
single to left scoring Skip Law
rence who got on via a fielders’
choice.

The Cards errupted for throe
more in the third when, with two
out, Lee Moench singled moving
Meeklenberg, who got on by an
error, to second. Lawrence then
walked to fill the bases.
THIS SET the stage for big
John Hurlbut, who promptly
knocked one off the bank in right
clearing the bases.
Bob Overman added another in
the seventh as he singled home
Lawrence who hurl banged a two
bagger.
With Ware definitely tiring.
Stanford put together three sin
gles and a USF error to score two
more.
The Dons capitalized on a walk
and a w'ell-hit double by catcher
John Petty to pick up a run in
the fourth. They added the other
in the sixth on a single, a hit bats
man, a wild pitch and a sacrifice
fiy.
