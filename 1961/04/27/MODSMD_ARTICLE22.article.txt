# Freshman Netmen Win Twice, Shutout San Mateo, San Jose
## 
### 
The freshman tennis team
scored two shutout wins this week
as they downed San Mateo Junior
College, 9-0, Monday and came
right hack Tuesday to blank the
San Jose State Spartans, 7-0, on
the Stanford courts.
First man John Isaacs led the
way for the frosh as he swept
both of his individual matches by
one-sided margins. Against San
Jose he brushed by Mel Higgin
son, 6-2. 6-1 and in the San Mateo
match he defeated Dave Dobri
ncn, 6-0, 6-1.

In the Spartan match Brad
Hodgman heat Bob Adams, 6-1,
6-1; Dick Wilson took John Hal
verson in straight sets, 6-0, 6-1;
Windy Nicolaus triumphed over
Kent Winslow, 6-0, 6-1, and John
Burt downed Ed Auger, 6-0, 61.

81-RON KIBBV and Nick Chick
ering teamed to win their doubles
match from Higginson and Ad
ams, 6-0, 6-2. Ed Ursin and Bruce
Bartley contributed the other
Stanford doubles win, 6-2 and 6-1,
over Winslow and Halvorson.
Hodgman, Wilson, Burt, Kibby
and Ursin also won their singles
matches against San Mateo. Hodg
man scored a 6-1, 6-0 victory over
Vic Rose; Wilson topped Bob
Wells, 6-1, 6-0; Burt bounced Dean
Hammer, 6-1, 6-2; Kibby took Bob
Roydon, 6-3, 6-0, and Ursin de
feated Leroy Friebel, 6-1, 6-2.
ALL of the doubles events
were won by the Papoose net men.
Nicolaus and Ursin took all but
one game while smashing Dobri

nen and Rose, 61, 60. Bartley
and Chickering defeated Wells
and Hammer, 6-0, 6-2, and Burt
and Kibby joined forces to handle
Roydon and Friebel, 6-1, 7-5.
Menlo College is the freshmen’s
next foe in a match to be played
next Monday afternoon on the
Farm courts.
