# String Quartet Ducats Available
## 
### 
Tickets are available at Dinkel
spiel Auditorium and at the Penin
sula Box Office in Town and Coun
try Village for the Walden String
Quartet concert to be held at 8:30
p.m. tomorrow.
The second in the music depart
ment’s subscription concert series,
the event will be in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium.
The quartet, comprised of Ho
mer Schmitt and Bernard Good
man, violins, John Garvey, viola,
and Robert Swenson, cello, comes
from the University of Illinois.
Stanford Music Prof. Leonard
Rainer’s composition, “Quartet,”
will be featured at the perform
ance, which will also include
standard as well as contemporary
works, such as Beethoven’s “Quar
tet No. 15,” “Opus 132 in A Minor,”
and Bartok's “Quartet No. 4.”
Ratner is the author of the text
book “Music: The Listener’s Art,”
used at colleges and universities
throughout the nation. His com
positions include “The Necklace,”
a chamber opera, and “Piano So
nata.”
