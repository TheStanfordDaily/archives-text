SAN FRANCISCO (UPI)
Snow was falling yesterday over
the Sierra Nevada and chains
were required on a number of
highways leading to ski areas.
The report from the California
State Automobile and the Ski
Tow Operators Associations by
highways;
HIGHWAY 40: Chains required
over Donner Summit with 12
inches of snow at 5,800 feet, 25
inches at 6,900 feet, and 21 inches
at 7,100 feet. Skiing good. Snow
ing.
HIGHWAY 50; Chains required
over Echo Summit with 7-12 inch-,
es of snow at 6,300 feet, and 13-31
inches at 7,400 feet. Skiing good.
Snowing.
HIGHWAY 89: Chains required

with 14-49 inches of snow at 6,600
feet. Skiing fair to good. Rain and
snow.
HIGHWAY 88: Chains required
with 15-25 inches of snow at 7,000
feet. Skiing on weekends only.
Snowing.
HIGHWAY 108: Chains re
quired with 20-32 inches of snow
at 6,600 feet. Skiing good. Snow
ing.
MT. SHASTA HIGHWAY 99
AND EVERITT HIGHWAY: No
chains required with 60 inches of
snow at 7,850 feet. Skiing fair to
good. Cloudy.
YOSEMITE HIGHWAYS 140
AND 41: No chains required with
icy spots on roads and 26 inches
of snow at Badger Pass. Skiing
excellent. Snowing.
