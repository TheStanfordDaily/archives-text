# Curtain Up Tonight On Modern Drama
## 
### 
“Six Characters in Search of an
Author,” the story of six charac
ters who are created by the im
agination of an author but whose
lives remain unfinished, opens
with the Stanford Players in the

Little Theater and will play to
morrow, Friday and Saturday
and Feb. 1-3.

The modern tragi-comedy by
Luigi Pirandello is centered on
the rehearsal of a play that is in
terrupted by the appearance of
characters from the author’s
imagination. They re-enact their
eternal torment and convince the
director of the play that their
lives arc dramatic. Then, to his
dismay, they take over the the
ater.

NSTANLEY WEESE as the fa
ther, Suzanne Payne as the step
daughter, Lisa Lauterere as the
mother, John McDaniels as the
son and Antony Alcock as the
director take the leading roles.
Other roles are played by Ellen
Cowley as Madame Pace, Samira
Kirollos as the Leading Lady,
Victor Emeljanow as the Load
ing Man, Yvonne Bonsall as the
Ingenue, David Miller as the Ju
venile. Nelda Halkett as the Sec
ond Lady, Diana Bischoff as the
Actress and Thomas Tully as the
Property Man.
Hunter Lynn, eight years old,
and William Wright, nine years
old, complete the cast.

DIRECTING PIRANDELLO’S
explosive and emotional drama
is Michael Addison.

Weese played a leading role in
this season’s Stanford production
of “The Master of Santiago.”
Miss Payne, formerly from Texas
Western College, has earned a
Woodrow Wilson Fellowship and
four consecutive Best Actress
Awards.

Prices arc $1.50 for the general
public on Wednesday and Thurs
day, $2 on Friday and Saturday;
$1 for students on Wednesday
and Thursday, $1.50 on Friday
and Saturday.
