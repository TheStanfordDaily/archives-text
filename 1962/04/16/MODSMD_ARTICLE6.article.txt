# Sterling to Speak On "State of SU'
## 
### 
President Wallace Sterling will
speak on “The State of the Uni
versity” at the Stanford chapter
annual dinner meeting of the
American Association of Univer
sity Professors Thursday night.
Faculty members and their
wives may make reservations by
phoning the Philosophy Depart
ment before 3 p.m. today. Choice
of dinner is meat or fish, and the
cost is $4.30 if arriving at 6 p.m.
or $3.50 at 7 p.m.
The meeting will be at the Uni
versity Club of Palo Alto, 3277
Junipero Serra Blvd. A brief
business meeting chaired by chap
ter president Prof. John Mother
shead will preceed President Ster
ling’s address. Standing commit
tees will report, and new chapter
officers for 1962-63 will be chosen.
