STANFORD'S FIRST VENTURE into high rise apartments for student living will form a part of the second
unit of Escondido Village. This is the architects' view of one of the three eight-story buildings to be
constructed for married students and their children. Work started April 5 on the new unit and com
pletion date is Sept, 9, 1963. The Alcan Pacific Co. of Sacramento has been awarded the $6,000,000
construction job. Funds have been loaned by the Federal Housing and Home Finance Agency. The
unit will consist of 459 furnished apartments and 44 buildings.
