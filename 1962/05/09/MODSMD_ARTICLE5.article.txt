# Stanford Hosts Folk Jamboree
## 
### 
Biting banjos and reverberant
guitars will invade the campus
this weekend for the Stanford
Folk Festival, Friday. Saturday,
and Sunday, May 11. 12, and 13.
The Thunder Mountain Tub
Thumpers will open the Festival
Friday night at 8 p.m. in Geology
Lecture Hall (southwest corner
of outer quad), with bluegrass
and other folk music.
Saturday Workshops will fea
ture Hedy West, Music of North
ern Georgia; Giles Gamble,
Southern Banjo Technique; and
Guy Carawan. Folk Music in
America Today. They will be held
in Room 61-H, 1-4 p.m. Saturday
evening two nationally known
folksingers and recording artists,
Hedy West and Guy Carawan,
will sing in Dinkelspiel Auditori
um at 8 p.m.
Sunday afternoon the Tim Har
ding Trio will sing Brazilian,
Spanish and Mexican songs, while
Dave Spence will do songs from
Ireland and Scotland; the concert
will be at the Little Theater, Me
morial Auditorium, 3:30 p.m.
Admission to all concerts is 50
cents; the workshops are free.
