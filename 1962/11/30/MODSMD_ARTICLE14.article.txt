# Carols, Concert To be Presented
## 
### 
The Christmas season will be
highlighted by a concert of
Christmas carols and a program
of traditional classical music.

Next Friday at 8 p.m. in Me
morial Church, there will lie a
service of Christmas carols sung
by the choir, with the congrega
tion participating. The choir
will also sing a special program
of music, both ancient and mod
ern, including “Laud to the Na
tivity” by Respighi. The choir is
under the direction of Prof. Har
old Schmidt.
On Sunday, Dec. 9, a Commu
nion Service at 9 a.m. and the
Service of Christmas Praise at
11 a.m. will be held in Memorial
Church.

Later that day, at 3:30, Prof.
Herbert Nanney, University Or
ganist, will present a concert of
Christmas music. He will be ac
companied by the Stanford Cham
ber Orchestra under the direction
of Prof. Sandor Salgo.
For those who will remain on
campus over the vacation there
will be a Christmas Eve service
at 11:30 p.m. and a Christmas
Day service at 11 a.m.
This Sunday the University
Chaplain, Rev. R. M. Minto, will
preach at the 11 a.m. worship
service.
