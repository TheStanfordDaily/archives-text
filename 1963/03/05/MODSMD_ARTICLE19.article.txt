## Netters Place Second to Cal 
### 
Stanford’s tennis team finished
a strong second to Cal’s netters in
the Northern California Intercol
legiate Tennis Tournament held
at Berkeley over the weekend.
Cal won the six-team meet, 34-10.
The Indian freshmen indicated
things will he different next year,
when they beat Cal in the frosh
events, 10-8.
Stanford’s Yoshi Minegishi
went all the way to the final
match before losing to Cal’s Jim
McManus, 6-1, 8-6. Minegishi then
teamed with Ron Preissmann in
the doubles, where they were
beaten by McManus and Don
Sykes, 6-2, 6-4.
Freshman Jim Best, whom
Stanford coach Bob Renker calls
the best prospect; he’s had here,
won the singles, beating Horse
Ritter of Foothill, 6-3, 6-4. Then
he teamed with Tom Maxeiner to
win the doubles.
Card Dick Wilson pulled a ma
jor upset when he beat third
seeded Barry Baskin of Cal, 3-7,
6-4, 6-4.

San Francisco today at 3 p.m. in
