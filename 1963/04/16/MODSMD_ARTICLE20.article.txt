# Indian Netters Lead Cal, 4-2
## 
### 
In a match unfinished Satur
day because of rain, Stanford’s
tennis squad look a commanding
lead over Cal. Before the rains
came, the Indian netters had a
4-2 lead in the singles, and were
leading 6-3, 3-1, in the third dou
bles match.
The Indians need only one dou
bles victory to assure a victory,
and will probably get it when
Cal meets Stanford on the Stan
ford home courts. May 2, to com
plete the match.
Stanford lost both the number
one and number two singles, but
swept the last four singles
matches for the lead. In the num
ber one singles Yoshi Minegishi
lost again to Cal’s Jim McManus,
6-3, 6-3. McManus, in the midst of
a great season, has yet to lose in
intercollegiate competition this
year. At number two singles, Ron
Preissman lost to Doug Sykes,
6-4, 6-1.
The last four matches of the
day were much more favorable.
John Issacs beat Dick Abramson,
6-3, 7-5; Dick Wilson defeated
Kent Olsen. 6-2, 6-2; John Burt
defeated Bob Harper, 6-1, 6-4; and
Brad Hodgeman defeated Tonv
Price, 6-2. 6-2.
The unbeaten Frosh host Foot
hill today at 3 p.m. on the Stan
ford Courts, and play Aragon
High School tomorrow also at 3
p.m.
