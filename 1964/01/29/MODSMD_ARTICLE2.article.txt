"Inside Castro's Cuba," a first
person account and color docu
mentary film, will be presented
by American correspondent Rob
ert Cohen at 7:30 p.m. in Memo
rial Auditorium. Commissioned at
various limes to work for NBC,
CBS. AP, Cohen received a pass
pott from the State Department
to spend 36 days filming Cuba
last fall.
His color movie and lecture will
cover such varied topics as food
rationing, agriculture, the Bay of
Pigs, the Cuban campaign to in
fluence Latin America, and other
controversial aspects' of Cuban
life today.
Tickets are on sale at Tresidder
Union Box Office: $1.25 for the
General Public, and $1 for stu
dents. The program is sponsored
by the Committee on Public Ex
ercises.
