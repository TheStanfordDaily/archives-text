rium.

SAN FRANCISCO
"A Thousand Clowns," running at the Curran Theater, is a com
edy about a television writer, his son, and a social worker who feels
that the boy is not being properly raised. Stars in the cast are Dane
Clark, Margaret O'Brien, and Barry Gordon.
The Actor's Workshop presents "The Firebugs," starring Robert
Haswell in a wild and comic fable about a man whose kindness to
arsonists fails to save his home. Other members of the mast are
Edward Winter. Glenn Mazen, and Herbert Propper. The play is
running at the Marines' Theater, nightly Wednesdays through Sun
days, with matinees on Saturday.
"The Ginger Man" is a new play concerning an aimiable moral
degenerate whose only pleasure in life is using and exploiting others.
The cast includes Tom Rosqui, Erica Rosqui, Robert Benson, and
Priscilla Pointer. The play is at the Encore Theater.
Coming to the Curran Theater on March 16 is "The Hollow
Crown" presented by members of the Royal Shakespeare Company
of Stratford-on-Avon and London. They bring to life some 900 years
of English royalty from William the Conqueror to Queen Victoria,
as known from personal and historical records.
