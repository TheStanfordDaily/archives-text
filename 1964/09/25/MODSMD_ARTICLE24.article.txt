# Daily Begins Volume 146 Housed In New Building
## 
### 
Published from the new $360,-
000 Storke Publications Building,
this special Freshman Edition of
The Daily begins the 146 th volume
of the Stanford newspaper.
With a press run of over 10,000
copies per day. The Daily reaches
more than 14,000 staff, faculty,
students and subscribers. It is
published Monday through Friday
except during dead week and
finals week.
PRESENTLY A $100,000 a year
business, the newspaper receives
over $75,000 from national and
local advertising.
It is entirely student run, is
completely free from administra

tive and faculty supervision, and !
is recognized as one of the most
independent collegiate papers in
the nation.
Complete state, national and in
ternational news comes to The j
Daily through the facilities of the
Associated Press.
THE EDITORIAL page features j
cartoons by Paul Conrad, col
lumns by internationally known J
political analysts such as Walter
Lippmann, and local columns and
editorial features.
Students working on the news
paper can receive up to seven
ci edits of academic work. Activ
ity credit is available to all staff
members.
Advertising salesmen also re
ceive commissions for their ad I
sales and photo technicians re
ceive compensation for each |
night's work in the Daily photo l
laboratory.
NO PREVIOUS experience is '
necessary for a staff position, but j
work on high school and other
publications is beneficial.
The paper first appeared on j
Dec. 9, 1891, in the form of the!
Sequoia. It became the ninth col- j
legiate daily in the nation the fol
lowing fall.
The new publication was called
the Daily Palo Alto until 1926
when it became the present Daily.
The first press was brought on
campus to print the Daily Palo
Alto, and the machine became the
seed for the Stanford Press.
The Stanford Press is now one j
of the largest book publishers on ;
the West Coast.
