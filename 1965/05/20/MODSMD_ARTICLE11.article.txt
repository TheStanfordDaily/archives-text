j "Perspective'* Series: Hole of Trustees
Mrs. Arthur Charles, a member of the Board of Trustees, w ill dis
cuss her role as a Trustee in the "University in Perspective" series
at ihe Stanford Y lounge, Women's Clubhouse, 3rd flood. 12-1 p.m.,
Friday. Lunch 50 cents.
Lowenstein To Speak 011 Freedom
Al Lowenstein concludes the "Freedom and Responsibility" lecture
series by speaking; at 1:15 p.m. in Tresidder Room 275. The series
has been aimed at deepening" philosophical discussion. The (Graduate
Coordinating Committee ami the Political Union have sponsored
the series.
French Slides To Be Shown Tonight
''Images of Provence," a slide tour of Southern France, will be
presented by the Associated Alumni of the Overseas Campuses this
Thursday, May 20. Michael Cartwright, Assistant Prof, of French,
; who took the slides, will narrate. The program will begin at 8:00 p.m.
in the I-Center. All those interested are invited to attend, particularly
those students about to depart for France and Italy.
Speech on Applying to Medical School
Donald L. Stiiwell, associate Professor of Anatomy, Stanford
School of Medicine, will speak on "Application to Medical School,"
at 7:30 p.m. in Tresidder Room 271. The talk, sponsored by Sigma Phi
Sigma, Stanford Pre-Medicai Society, will he repealed on Tuesday at
7:30 p.m. in Tresidder Room 274. Among the topics to be discussed
are: the admission procedure, grade point and medical board evalu
ation, costs, scholarships and loans, and many problems which the
pre-med must consider and solve.
