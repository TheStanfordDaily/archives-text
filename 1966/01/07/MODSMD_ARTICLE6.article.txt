# Speech Association Hosts National Debate Tourney
## 
### 
Today and tomorrow, the Stanford
Speech Association will host the First
Annual Redwood Invitational Inter
collegiate Debating Tournament.
An estimated 150 student debaters
will participate, representing the top
debating team* from over 30 schools
including Dartmouth, Georgetown, U.
of Illinois, Purdue, U. of Pennsylvania,
Middlebury College (Vermont), U.S.C.,
U.C.L.A., New Mexico, Loyola U., and
many others. Each of these 30 institu
tions will send two teams into compe
tition. Participating colleges and uni
versities represent 15 states.
The teams will be debating for and
against the resolution that law en
forcement officers in the United States
should have greater freedom in the
investigation and prosecution of crime.
Debating for Stanford will be the
team of Doug Thompson-Bob Nielsen,
who recently placed 3rd out of 70
teams at the difficult Rndlands Uni

versity debating tournament, and the
team of Rod Peck-Grant Wacker.
The first round begins at 3:15 p.m.
today. The second and third rounds are
at 4:45 p.m. and 6:15 p.m. On Satur
day, rounds will be held at 8:30 a.m.,
10.15 a.m. and 12 p.m. with the quar
ter, semi, and final rounds starting at
2:30 p.m., 3:45 p.m., and 5:00 p.m. re
spectively. The finals will be held in
Cubberley Auditorium. Tournament
headquarters will be in Cubberley Au
ditorium Lobby and information con
cerning locations of the various de
bates can be secured there before the
starting times of the rounds. The
preliminary rounds will be held in
rooms around the Quad.
Preparations for the tourney are
under the direction of Professor Ken
neth Moser of the Speech & Drama
Department at Stanford. He has in
vited the Stanford community to at
tend all rounds.
