Degree Deadline Nears
Monday, January 31, is the deadline for filing candidacy applica
tions for June A.M., M.S., and Engineer degrees. Applications must
be signed by major departments before being submitted to the
Graduate Study Office, Boom HUM, Registrar's Office.
'Prism' Organizes Tonight
All freshman interested in working on the editorial, art, or busi
ness boards of Prism, the freshman literary magazine, should attend
the organizational meeting scheduled tonight at 10 o'clock in room
52H. Prism publishes freshman poems; short stories, and essays.
Activity credit will be available for members of the staff. Any in
terested student who is unable to attend the meeting can call Tom
Sherman, Prism advisor, at the freshman English office, ext. 2722.
Rivers On LBJ, Press
Prof. William Rivers will speak o"n "The Presidents and the
Press" tomorrow night at 7:45 in Dinkelspiel Auditorium, as part
of the Tuesday Evening Series of the Committee on Public Exer
cises.
Rivers is an associate professor of communication and author of
an analysis of the Washington press corps, "The Opinionmakers."
He has been Washington correspondent for The Reporter maga
zine.
Museum Open Mornings
Dr. Lorenz Eitner, head of the Art and Architecture Department
has announced that Stanford Museum is now open from 10 a.m.
to 5 p.m. on weekdays, and 1 to 5 p.m. on Saturdays and Sundays.
The former hours were 1 to 5 p.m. daily.
Student members of the University's Guide Service will be on
duty weekdays from 10 a.m. to 1 p.m. and the Museum staff from
1 to 5 p.m.
