# Africa Week Schedule
## 
### 
Today: Mrs. Marian John
son will lead a panel discussion
on the "Role of Women in
Changing Africa" from 3 to 5
p. m. at the International Cen
ter.
High school student showing
of two African films, "Under the
Black Mask" and "Art of An
cient Egypt," starting at 4:15
p. m. in Cubberley Auditorium.
Repeat showing of the Afri
can plays.
Friday: Prof. David W. Ames
of the San Francisco State Col
lege Department of Anthropol
ogy, will lecture on "The Hausa
Musician and His Music," with
slides and original tapes at 4:15
p. m. at the International Cen
ter.

Public dinner of African food
at 6 p. m. at the International
Center. Repeat showing of Afri
can films for the general public
at 8 p. m. in Physics 100.
Saturday: Symposium "Afri
can Culture: Traditional or Mod
ern" at 9:30 a.m. in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium, moderated by Prof.
G. Wesley Johnson Jr., Stanford
Department of History, with
panelists Prof. Martin Klein, De
partment of History, Berkeley;
Prof. Robert R. Griffeth, Depart
ment of History, UCLA; and
Prof. Victor Uchendu, Food Re
search Institute, Stanford.
Reception and dance at 8:30
p. m. starring Saka Aquay and
His Highlife Orchestra from Los
Angeles.
