Project Seeks Bi-lingualTutors
An informational meeting for a new Stanford tutorial, the Sunny
vale Project, will be held tonight at 8:15 p.m. in Room 270 of Tresid
der Union.
This program will work with children of bi lingual families (pri
marily Spanish). The tutors work in teams of two, going to the
family home one evening each week. Most of the children are ele
mentary school age.
Places Open In "New Choir"
This year the choirs at St. Mark's Episcola Church, Palo Alto,
under the direction of Mr. C. Thomas Rhoacjs, Organist-Choirmas
ter, will be using a new method for training the male voice, de
veloped by the Robal School of Church Music.
The men rehearse on Thursday evenings at 8:00 p.m., and are
asked on occasion to join the boys who practice from 5:00 p.m. to
7:30 p.m. Anyone interested in joining either of these choirs may
call 326-3800 for further information.
Leppert To Speak To YD's
Dr. George Leppert, Democratic candidate for Congress in this
district, will speak in the Tresidder Large Lounge at 4:15 p.m. to
day. His appearance is sponsored by the Stanford Young Demo
crats. In his campaign Dr .Leppert, a professor of engineering at
Stanford, has been critical of parts of President Johnson's policy
in Vietnam. He opposes incumbant Charles Gubser, who has held
this office for seven terms.
Med Center Needs Volunteers
Students interested in working as volunteers at the medical cen
ter are invited to attend an orientation meeting today at 4:00 p.m.
in Room WOO 3 at the hospital. One unit of credit is given for thirty
hours of work.
