FRIDAY
AXE COMM: USC Kally at Searsville,
Friday, 3-6:00 p.m., 75c admission. Every
one welcome.
HILLEL FOUNDATION: 8 p.m. at
the Clubhouse. Sabbath Service and Oneg
Shabbat.
LUTHERANS AT STANFORD: 12
noon, at the Clubhouse. Luncheon and dis
cussion on "What's Happening: in the
Churches of Europe?"
PH.D. LANGUAGE EXAM : 8:30 p.m.,
Room 200 Q (History Corner). Exams in
French and Italian. Will be given again
October 28, same room, same time.
REV. HANS KUNG: 7:45 p.m Memor
ial Church. Lecture: "Whither the
Church? The Church and Catholicity."
RICHARD HOFSTADTER: 4:15
p.m., Dinkelspiel. Lecture: "The Paranoid
Style in American Politics."
'66 QUAD: Friday, Oct. 14 is the last
day previously purchased '66 Quads may
be picked up. Resinning Monday, Oct. 17,
remaining '66 Quads will be sold for $8.00
on a first-come first-serve basis.
STANFORD FOLK DANCERS: 7:30-
11:30 p.m., Women's gym. Beginners les
sons 7:30-8:30. Wear soft shoes. Every
one welcome.
STANFORD SKI CLUB EX COMM.:
Interviews for new members will be held
Thursday and Friday afternoon. Sign-up
now in the ASSU office.
SWAMI CHINMAYANANDA: 4:15
p.m., TMU Lounge. Lecture on
Vedanta Philosophy. Sponsored by Student
Religious Liberals.
TENNIS: 3-5:30 p.m., Women's courts.
Co-rec tennis mixed doubles. Come alone
or with a partner.
WAITING FOR GODOT: Theater
Series Friday, Oct. 14. Dinner in San
Francisco, performance at The Playhouse,
followed by discussion with the cast. Sign
up now at the I-Center upstairs reception
YOUNG PROHIBITIONISTS: Dry
in protest, noon today in White Plaza. Con
tributions are now being taken to send our
delegate to the national convention in 1968
in Chicken Scratch, Arkansas.

7:30 p.m., meet in Otero Lounge. 'All in

ing before dinner. Bring lunch. Destination:

welcome.
HILLEL FOUNDATION: 9 a.m. at

ing service.
HILLEL FOUNDATION: 12 noon, at

LUTHERANS AT STANFORD: Sun
day, 9 a.m. —Rolls and coffee at Lutheran
Center, 1611 Stanford Ave. at Bowdoin.
9:30 a.m. — Seminar — "The Searching
Church" at ;he Center. 9:30 a.m. — Uni

versity Lutheran Church School at Escon
dido School. 10:45 a.m. — Rides to Uni
versity Lutheran from Branner, Roble and
Bookstore parking lot. Rides to Trinity and
Bethany Churches from Bookstore parking
lot. 11 a.m. — worship service at Escondido
School. 12:30 p.m.—lunch at the Center.
STANFORD ISLAMIC SOCIETY:
Sunday, 8 p.m., I-Onter. Annual election
of officers for 1966-67. All old and new
members and interested members of S.I.S.
are encouraged to attend.
MISCELLANEOUS
ALL CAMPUS COFFEE HOUR:
MWF, 4-5 p.m., upstairs lounge of the
Clubhouse. Sponsored by the Stanford "Y".
AUSTRIA II: Tuesday, 9:30 p.m. in
TMU (room to be announced). There will
be a fact-seeking review of Stanford-in
Austria by the Overseas Campus (student)
Board with special emphasis on our groovy
"bad" group. This is the time to talk cur
riculum, rules, location, etc.
FASHION SHOW: Monday, 7:30-8:30
p.m., Tresidder Terrace. Stanford girls
will model Marimekko of Finland. Spon
sored by TMU Cultural Arts Division
and Design Research of San Francisco.
FREUD SEMINAR: Any student in
terested in participating in an all-student
seminar devoted to a careful reading of the
Interpretation of Dreams please call Marc
Shell or Bob Fishman, 327-2920, ext. 650.
HERITAGE FUND: Senior Class
scholarship drive. Interested seniors please
sign up for interviews (to be held Oct. 17-
22) at Axe Comm desk in ASSU.
CULTURAL ARTS DIVISION, TU
BOARD: Division meeting Tuesday, 5
p.m.
POLITICAL UNION PANEL: Tues
day, Oct. 18, 7:30 p.m., TMU, Rm. 270.
Paul Kreisberg, officer in charge of Main
land China Affairs with Department of
State, will speak on current internal state
of affairs in China and the Administration
policy on China. Also participating will be
two professors from the Institute of Politi
cal Problems.
SIDEWALK ART SHOW: To be held
Oct. 27 and 28 from 10:30 a.m. to 5 p.m.
Entry forms available at Information Desk
at Tresidder, due Oct. 24, 25, and 26. All
interested students urged to participate.

SHIP: Daily prayer meeting, 7-8 p.m.
