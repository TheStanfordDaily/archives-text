# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
AS-SUCH Committee To Meet
The committee to set up an ASSU coffee house (AS-SUCH) will
meet this afternoon at 5 p.m. in the third floor lounge of the
Women's Clubhouse.
Quakers To Present Talk On Drugs
An open meeting on drugs will be held this afternoon at 4:15 p.m.
in the Tresidder small lounge. Jan Marinissen will speak on "The
Grass Scene: Euphoria or Religious Response?"
Marinissen is supervisor of Austin McCormack House, a half
way house for ex-prisoners on parole.
The open meeting is sponsored by the Quaker Forum.
KZSU To Air Lane Versus Liebler
Radio station KZSU will air a two-part documentary about the
current debate over the Warren Commission report on the assassi
nation of President Kennedy. Part one is scheduled for tonight at
7 p.m., part two for tomorrow at 7 p.m.
The documentary was compiled from the recent speeches of
Commission attorney Wesley Liebeler and Mark Lane, author of
Rush To Judgment and critic of the commission.
I-Center Schedules So. African Film
Come Back, Africa, a film on Negro life in Johannesburg today,
will be shown tonight at 8 p.m. at the I-Center. Directed by Lionel
Rogosin, the film was made in secret with a non-professional cast.
The showing is sponsored by the International Film Forum of
the I-Center. Admission is open to I-Center members. Membership
is available at the door for $2.
YWCA Fair To Sell Foreign Gifts
The Stanford YWCA is sponsoring a gift fair today, tomorrow
and Friday in the Clubhouse on White Plaza. The fair, called the
"Gift Horse," will be open 11 a.m. to 5:45 p.m. daily and 7 to 9
p.m. tomorrow.
The "Gift Horse" will feature international gifts, coffee and for
eign pastry. The program tomorrow night will include folk dancers.
