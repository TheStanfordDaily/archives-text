# Stern Offers Cong. Conyers As G.I.R.
## 
### 
Congressman John Conyers of De
troit will be the Stern Hall Guest-in-
Residence today through Wednesday.
He is co-sponsored by the ASSU Po
litical Union as a part of the week
long Conference on Law Enforcement
and the Community.
Conyers is a graduate of Wayne
State University. A Democrat, he has
recently been returned to his second

term in Congress. During the summer
of 1963 he worked as civil rights law
yer in Mississippi. Last June he or
ganized and led a bipartisan delega
tion of six Congressmen who visited
Mississippi to witness the first pri
mary election held in the South since
passage of the Voting Rights Act of
1965.
Conyers is the only Negro member
of the House Judiciary Committee
which drafted the Voting Rights law.
Today at 1:15 p.m. Conyers will ap
pear before Professor Paul Gayer's
Labor Economics class, room 460, to
discuss the relationship of unions to
the political process.
At 2:30 p.m. he will meet with Stan
ford Committee for Peace in Vietnam,
the Students for a Democratic Society,
and other interested persons to dis
cuss Vietnam dissent and unification
of the peace and civil rights move
ments in Larkin Lounge of Stern.
Tonight at 9 p.m. he will participate
in a panel discussion with David
Guren, Executive Director of the West
Coast activities of the American Civil
Liberties Union, in Serra Lounge.
They will discuss "Open Housing in
the North."
Tomorrow Conyers will begin the
day with a press conference at 10 a.m.
in Room 269 of Tresidder. At 4:15 p.m.
he will discuss the internship pro
gram at a meeting of Stanford-in-
Washington.
At 8 p.m. in Donner Lounge of
Stern he will discuss the Vietnam war
and its effects upon domestic policies
and politics. Conyers is known for
his strong position against the war.

Also participating in the discussion
will be Professor Barton Bernstein.
Wednesday morning at 10 a.m. he
will hold a discussion with the Law
Students Civil Rights Research Group
in Lang Faculty Lounge of the Stan
ford Law School. At 7:30 p.m. he will
deliver his address for the Law En
forcement Conference in Tresidder
Large Lounge.

Congressman John Conyers of De
troit will be the Stern Hall Guest-in-
Residence today through Wednesday.
He is co-sponsored by the ASSU Po
litical Union as a part of the week
long Conference on Law Enforcement
and the Community.
Conyers is a graduate of Wayne
State University. A Democrat, he has
recently been returned to his second

term in Congress. During the summer
of 1963 he worked as civil rights law
yer in Mississippi. Last June he or
ganized and led a bipartisan delega
tion of six Congressmen who visited
Mississippi to witness the first pri
mary election held in the South since
passage of the Voting Rights Act of
1965.
Conyers is the only Negro member
of the House Judiciary Committee
which drafted the Voting Rights law.

the political process.
