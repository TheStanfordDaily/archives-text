'Black Power' Symposium Planned
A symposium on "Black Power," sponsored by the Northern
California chapter of the American Studies Association, will be held
Friday, December 9, at 8 p.m. in Tresidder 170-171. The moderator
will be Dr. Thomas Burbridge, Associate Professor of Pharma
cology, UC Medical Center, vice president of San Francisco chapter
of NAACP, and leader of Palace Hotel and Cadillac demonstra
tions. Speakers and commentators will be professors and instruc
tors from UC Santa Cruz, San Jose State, Stanford, and Experi
mental College of San Francisco State.
March For INon Violent Toys Set
The first of three parades to promote creative toys will be held
tomorrow, December 3, at 10:30 a.m. in the Stanford Shopping Cen
ter. Beginning from the Emporium, the parade will feature cos
tumned marchers, displays of constructive and creative toys, two
large rag dolls, made especially for the parades, and balloons for
children.
The parade will be repeated at 2 p.m. tomorrow in Downtown
Palo Alto, House of Today, 550 University Avenue. The last parai
will be held on December 10, at 10:30 a.m., in Town and Country
Village. The parades are sponsored by the Committee for Toys of
Peace, formed this fall in the Stanford-Palo Alto area.
British Expert On Africa To Speak
Professor Roger Anstey of the University of Durham, Englai J
will discuss "Capitalism and Slavery: A Critique" next Thursday,
December 8, at 4:15 p.m. in the Seminar Room (116) of the Hoover
Institution.
Dr. Anstey, presently Visiting Professor at the University of
British Columbia, is a British historian of Africa and the author of
two books on the Congo.

U Of Beirut To Celebrate 100 Years
A Lebanese "Evening Abroad" to celebrate the Hundredth An
niversary of the American University of Beirut will be held Sunday,
December 4, at 7:30 p.m. in Dinkelspiel Auditorium. The speaker for
the program will be Professor Constantino K. Zurayk of the Ameri
can University of Beirut.
The program will include films, narration of the University's his
tory, music and Lebanese folklore. A reception following the pr
gram will be held in the Large Lounge of Tresidder.
I-Center Schedules Holiday Activity
The Bechtel International Center will be open over the entire
Christmas vacation from 8 a.m. to midnight, except on Sundays,
when it will close at 10 p.m.
A full schedule of activities is planned, including a ski trip, a trip
to Los Angeles, Christmas parties, a New Year's Party, an Ice-
Skating Party, Games Night ,and movies. Refreshments will be
available during the holidays, everyone is invited to attend.
