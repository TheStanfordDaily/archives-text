Lawrence Schedules Organ Recital
Arthur Lawrence, assistant organist at Stanford, will present a
free recital at the Memorial Church at 3:30 p.m. Sunday.
Included on the program will be "Introduction and Passacaglia in
D Minor" by Max Reger; variations on a Chorale by Georg Bohm;
selections from the "Fiori Musicali" by Girolamo Frescobaldi;
"Prelude and Fugue in G Minor" by J. S. Bach; and a chorale by
Roger Sessions.
I-Center Plans Muslim Evening
Saturday, January 28, the I-Center program Evenings Abroad will
feature the Muslim Evening. It will include slides and musical items
from various Muslim countries and refreshments will be served.
Kamil T. Said will also give a short talk on "What Is God to a
Muslim."
SRT To Present Chekhov Plays
Tickets are available for Stanford Repertory Theater's special
off-campus production of three one-act plays by the Russian master
playwright, Anton Chekhov.
The plays, "The Boor," "The Marriage Proposal," and "Swan
Song," presented under the single title "Morning, Noon and Night
With Chekhov," will be shown at Sprangenberg Theater, Gunn High
School, 780 Arastradero Rd., Palo Alto, at 8:30 p.m. Friday, February
10, and at 2:30 p.m. and 8:30 p.m. Saturday, February 11.
Funds For War Protest Trip Sought
Today is the last day students will be collecting money to send
Robert McAfee Brown and Michael Novak to the war protest in
Washington, D.C., next week. They will represent Stanford stu
dents at the gathering to discuss our economic commitment in Viet
nam and communication of issues and concern to local communities
through the church.
Dean Napier will be attending the protest independently. Tables
will be set up in front of the Post Office from 11 a.m. to 2 p.m., or
donations may be brought to the Stanford Y and UCCM offices in
the Clubhouse before Tuesday.
