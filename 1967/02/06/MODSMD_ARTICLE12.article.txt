# Moot Court To Feature 'Jordan vs Weaver;' Justice William Brennan
## 
### 
Three judges, including Jus
tice William Brennan Jr. of the
U.S. Supreme Court, will portray
what might have happened had
the Supreme Court actually
agreed to review California's
pay-television proposal.
Sitting as the Supreme Court
in "Jordan vs. Weaver" with
Justice Brennan will be Chief
Justice Gordon Thompson of the
Nevada Supreme Court and
Judge Ben C. Duniway, judge of
the Ninth Circuit Court of Ap
peals, San Francisco, and a trus
tee of the University. Justice
Brennan will serve as "chief
justice" of the moot court.
The constitutional case in
volves California Secretary of
State Frank M. Jordan versus
Sylvester "Pat" Weaver, former
NBC vice-president who headed
the pay-television drive in Cali
fornia.
Representing Jordan will be
third-year law students William
McCarren and David Fox. Rep
resenting Weaver will be Kris
tina Hanson and Richard Whit
more.
The actual case was found un

constitutional by the California
Supreme Court in 1965 and was
appealed to the U.S. Supreme
Court, which denied cert.
The occasion marks the 15th
annual Marion Rice Kirkwood
Moot Court Competition of the
Stanford Law School, scheduled
for 8 p.m. Tuesday, February 7,
in Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Ad
mission is free and the public
is invited.
