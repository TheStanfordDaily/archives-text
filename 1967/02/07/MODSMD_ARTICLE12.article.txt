# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Art Show Initiates Negro Week
The Community Activities Committee of Palo Alto is sponsoring
a weeklong celebration of Negro History Week. There will be an
area-wide art show at Gunn High School, 780 Arastradero Road,
running from February 5 to February 12. The program will close
with a Lincoln's Birthday Tribute at 3 p.m. February 12, in Spangen
burg Auditorium at Gunn High School.
Dr. Jester Hairston will conduct members of the Ravenswood and
Menlo-Atherton choirs. Benefits from the program will aid Negro
graduates of the five local high schools in going to college.
Donner To Host Guest Poli Sci Prof
Dr. David Finley, visiting Assistant Professor of Political Science
from Colorado College, will speak tonight at 7 p.m. in the Donner
Lounge. His lecture is entitled "The Warsaw Pact as a Mirror of
NATO Today."
Dr. Finley received his M.A. and Ph.D. from Stanford, after un
dergraduate studies at the U.S. Military Academy.
'Experiment' To Run Film On War
Experimental film-maker Stan Brakhage will lecture and show
his latest work, "23rd Psalm Branch," an hour long epic on war to
morrow at 8:30 p.m. in The Experiment building. He also will pre
sent selections from his "Song" cycle, films dealing with such
themes as war, love, and fire. There will be a $1 admission charge.
