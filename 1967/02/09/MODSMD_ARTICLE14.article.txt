# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
I-Center To Present Chaplin Film
The I-Center will present the Charlie Chaplin film "Gold Rush"
tonight. The show will begin at 8:15 p.m. in TMU 274-5. Coffee and
discussion, with Professor Bliss Carnochan of the English Depart
ment, will follow at the I-Center.
Students To Begin Nitery Drama Show
The Master of Fine Arts students in theater are starting a weekly
program this Saturday night at 11:30 p.m. at The Nitery. The pro
gram is called "Midnight with the Mafia." It will consist of original
plays, avantgarde improvisations and other musical and theatrical
bits. Tickets will be on sale at the door for 50 cents. Free peanut
butter and jelly and crackers will be served. (Mafia is short for
Master of Fine Arts).
ASSU Concert Series Opens Tonight
The ASSU Concert Series is beginning its 41st season tonight
with the presentation of Bramwell Fletcher in the "George Bernard
Shaw Story." Fletcher does a characterization of the witty, cynical
George Bernard Shaw, much the same as Hal Holbrook has done
with Mark Twain. Tonight's performance is in Memorial Auditorium
at 8:30 p.m. Tickets are on sale at Tresidder Box Office and at the
door before the performance. Students can obtain discounts.
Mexicali Project To Meet Today
The HR Mexicali Project will meet today to discuss slites decided
upon for the project. The meeting will take place this afternoon at
5 p.m. in TMU 270. New participants are still welcome, "especially
if they have a car."
Talk To Focus On Classical Revival
Professor Antony Raubitschek of the Classics Department will
speak this afternoon on "The Revival of Classical Learning." The
talk is part of the Internezzo Series, and is sponsored by the Stan
ford Library's Division of Special Collections and the Archaeological
Institute of America.
The talk will begin at 3:30 p.m. in the Bender Room of the Main
Library.
Helen Chenowith To Discuss Haiku
The Los Altos Writers' Round Table will present a talk tonight
by Helen Chenoweth on the background and meaning of Haiku
poetry. The talk is scheduled for 4:15 p.m. in the Tresidder Small
Lounge, to be followed by a panel reading selected Haiku.
The group has recently published a collection of their Haiku en
titled Borrowed Water.

upon for the project. The meeting will take place this afternoon at
5 p.m. in TMU 270. New participants are still welcome, "especially
if they have a car."
Talk To Focus On Classical Revival
Professor Antony Raubitschek of the Classics Department will
speak this afternoon on "The Revival of Classical Learning." The
talk is part of the Internezzo Series, and is sponsored by the Stan
ford Library's Division of Special Collections and the Archaeological
Institute of America.
The talk will begin at 3:30 p.m. in the Bender Room of the Main
Library.
Helen Chenowith To Discuss Haiku
The Los Altos Writers' Round Table will present a talk tonight
by Helen Chenoweth on the background and meaning of Haiku
poetry. The talk is scheduled for 4:15 p.m. in the Tresidder Small
Lounge, to be followed by a panel reading selected Haiku.
The group has recently published a collection of their Haiku en
titled Borrowed Water.
Biz Conference To Hear Galbraith
John K. Galbraith, Milton Friedman, Otto N. Miller, and Eugene
M. Zuckert will lecture the 1967 Stanford Business Conference to
morrow at the Masonic Auditorium and Fairmont Hotel, in San
Francisco. The topic will be "Government and the Corporation:
Partners or Competitors?" The Business School has registration
forms on a first-come, first-served basis at $25.
I-Center To Present Chaplin Film
The I-Center will present the Charlie Chaplin film "Gold Rush"
tonight. The show will begin at 8:15 p.m. in TMU 274-5. Coffee and
discussion, with Professor Bliss Carnochan of the English Depart
ment, will follow at the I-Center.
Students To Begin Nitery Drama Show
The Master of Fine Arts students in theater are starting a weekly
program this Saturday night at 11:30 p.m. at The Nitery. The pro
gram is called "Midnight with the Mafia." It will consist of original
plays, avantgarde improvisations and other musical and theatrical
bits. Tickets will be on sale at the door for 50 cents. Free peanut
butter and jelly and crackers will be served. (Mafia is short for
Master of Fine Arts).
ASSU Concert Series Opens Tonight
The ASSU Concert Series is beginning its 41st season tonight
with the presentation of Bramwell Fletcher in the "George Bernard
Shaw Story." Fletcher does a characterization of the witty, cynical
George Bernard Shaw, much the same as Hal Holbrook has done
with Mark Twain. Tonight's performance is in Memorial Auditorium
at 8:30 p.m. Tickets are on sale at Tresidder Box Office and at the
door before the performance. Students can obtain discounts.
