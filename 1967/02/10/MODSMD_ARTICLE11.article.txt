# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Rep Theater To Present Chekhov
The Stanford Repertory Theater will present an off-campus per-
formance entitled "Morning, Noon and Night With Chekhov" today
and tomorrow.
"The Marriage Proposal," "The Boor," and 'Swan Song" will be
presented at Spangenberg Theater, Gunn High School in Palo Alto
8:30 tonight and at 2:30 and 8:30 tomorrow. Tickets are on sale in
Memorial Hall box office.
Russian Quartet Gives Concert
The Borodin String Quartet, foremost ensemble of its type in
Russia, will present a concert at 3:30 p.m. Sunday in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium.
The group will play 'Quartet No. 2 in D Major" by Borodin,
"Quartet in F Major, Opus 92, No. 2" by Prokofieff; "Five Pieces for
Quartet, Opus 5" by Webern; and "Quartet No. 4, Opus 83 in D
Major" by Shostakovich.
Tickets are available at the Tresidder box office.
Movie Features Alec Guinness
The Stanford Cricket Club under the sponsorship of the ASSU
will present a movie, "Father Brown, Detective," with Alec Gui-
ness, Saturday at 6 and 8:30 p.m. in Cubberley Auditorium.
Also included on the program are two documentaries: an art film,
"The Life of Dante," and a newsreel on the world cup soccer match
between England and Germany. Admission is 75 cents.
IIR China Conference To Begin
The IIR will present its conference on "China's Cultural Revolu-
tion" tomorrow from 9:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. in Dinkelspiel Audi-
torium.
Speakers include Stanford professors Michael Oksenberg, Mark
Mancall, Lyman Van Slyke, William Skinner, Bo Anderson, Robert
North, Claude Buss, and General Samuel Griffiths of the Hoover In-
stitute. Also featured will be Prof. Chalmers Johnson of Berkeley
and Lisa Hobbs of the San Francisco Examiner.
'Evening Abroad' In Brazil
A cultural program featuring slides and music of Brazil, fol-
lowed by refreshments and dancing to Brazilian music will be pre-
sented at 8 p.m. tomorrow at the International Center. Hosts of the
program will be Brazilian students on campus and students who
participated in last summer's Guanabara Exchange.

Movie Features Alec Guinness
The Stanford Cricket Club under the sponsorship of the ASSU
will present a movie, "Father Brown, Detective," with Alec Gui-
ness, Saturday at 6 and 8:30 p.m. in Cubberley Auditorium.
Also included on the program are two documentaries: an art film,
"The Life of Dante," and a newsreel on the world cup soccer match
between England and Germany. Admission is 75 cents.
IIR China Conference To Begin
The IIR will present its conference on "China's Cultural Revolu-
tion" tomorrow from 9:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. in Dinkelspiel Audi-
torium.
Speakers include Stanford professors Michael Oksenberg, Mark
Mancall, Lyman Van Slyke, William Skinner, Bo Anderson, Robert
North, Claude Buss, and General Samuel Griffiths of the Hoover In-
stitute. Also featured will be Prof. Chalmers Johnson of Berkeley
and Lisa Hobbs of the San Francisco Examiner.
'Evening Abroad' In Brazil
A cultural program featuring slides and music of Brazil, fol-
lowed by refreshments and dancing to Brazilian music will be pre-
sented at 8 p.m. tomorrow at the International Center. Hosts of the
program will be Brazilian students on campus and students who
participated in last summer's Guanabara Exchange.
Rep Theater To Present Chekhov
The Stanford Repertory Theater will present an off-campus per-
formance entitled "Morning, Noon and Night With Chekhov" today
and tomorrow.
"The Marriage Proposal," "The Boor," and 'Swan Song" will be
presented at Spangenberg Theater, Gunn High School in Palo Alto
8:30 tonight and at 2:30 and 8:30 tomorrow. Tickets are on sale in
Memorial Hall box office.
Russian Quartet Gives Concert
The Borodin String Quartet, foremost ensemble of its type in
Russia, will present a concert at 3:30 p.m. Sunday in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium.
The group will play 'Quartet No. 2 in D Major" by Borodin,
"Quartet in F Major, Opus 92, No. 2" by Prokofieff; "Five Pieces for
Quartet, Opus 5" by Webern; and "Quartet No. 4, Opus 83 in D
