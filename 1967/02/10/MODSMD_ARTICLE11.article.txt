# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Rep Theater To Present Chekhov
The Stanford Repertory Theater will present an off-campus p
formance entitled "Morning, Noon and Night With Chekhov"
and tomorrow.
"The Marriage Proposal," "The Boor," and 'Swan Song" will
presented at Spangenberg Theater, Gunn High School in Palo A 1
8:30 tonight and at 2:30 and 8:30 tomorrow. Tickets are on sale
Memorial Hall box office.
Russian Quartet Gives Concert
The Borodin String Quartet, foremost ensemble of its type
Russia, will present a concert at 3:30 p.m. Sunday in Dinkelsp
Auditorium.
The group will play 'Quartet No. 2 in D Major" by Borodi
"Quartet in F Major, Opus 92, No. 2" by Prokofieff; "Five Pieces 1
Quartet, Opus 5" by Webern; and "Quartet No. 4, Opus 83 in
Major" by Shostakovich-
Tickets are available at the Tresidder box office.
Movie Features Alec Guinness
The Stanford Cricket Club under the sponsorship of the ASf
will present a movie, "Father Brown, Detective," with Alec Gu
ness, Saturday at 6 and 8:30 p.m. in Cubberley Auditorium.
Also included on the program are two documentaries: an art fil
"The Life of Dante," and a newsreel on the world cup soccer mai
between England and Germany. Admission is 75 cents.
lIR China Conference To Begin
The lIR will present its conference on "China's Cultural Revo
tion" tomorrow from 9:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. in Dinkelspiel Au
torium.
Speakers include Stanford professors Michael Oksenberg, Mti
Mancall, Lyman Van Slyke, William Skinner, Bo Anderson, Rob<
North, Claude Buss, and General Samuel Griffiths of the Hoover !
stitute. Also featured will be Prof. Chalmers Johnson of Berkel
and Lisa Hobbs of the San Francisco Examiner.
'Evening Abroad' In Brazil
A cultural program featuring slides and music of Brazil, 112
lowed by refreshments and dancing to Brazilian music will be pi
sented at 8 p.m. tomorrow at the International Center. Hosts of t
program will be Brazilian students on campus and students u
participated in last summer's Guanabara Exchange.
