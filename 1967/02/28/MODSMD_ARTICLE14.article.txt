# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Composer To Cive Lecture-Concert
Karlheinz Stockhausen, considered to be one of the most inventive
contemporary composers, will present a lecture-concert at 8:30 p.m.
tonight in Dinkelspiel Auditorium under the sponsorship of the
Music Department.
The composer will illustrate his talk with several of his recent
compositions.
Tickets are available at the Tresidder Box Office.
Donner Forum W ill Discuss ROTC
The University's ROTC program is the topic of tonight's Donner
lecture series. The nature, purpose, and goals of ROTC will be
outlined in the forum, "The Justification of the Military in an
Academic Community."
The speakers will be Col. Nanny representing AROTC, Capt.
Brigham for NROTC, and Major Dodd for AFROTC.
The lecture will be held in the Donner Lounge at 7 p.m.
German Dept. Consultations Available
Students from the German Department will be holding office
hours Wednesday, Thursday, and Friday from 10 to 11 a.m. and
1 to 2 p.m. in room 241 G for anyone wishing to consult them about
pre-registration for courses in that department.
Student Abroad Applications Due
Friday, March 3, is the last day applications will be accepted for
the Student Abroad Home Welcome Program. Unlike most travel
plans, this program allows students four to five weeks of free
travel after profiting from the orientation session on campus.
This year the program offers students homes with families in six
European countries.
Anyone interested in this opportunity call apply at the Affilia
tions Division of the lIR in the ASSU office. For more information
contact Jim Knowles at 854-3635.
Medieval Arts Collector To Speak
Professor Mark Lansburgh will give an illustrated lecture en
titled "Collecting Medieval Manuscripts" at 8 p.m. in Room 320 in
the Geology corner on Wednesday.
Professor Lansburgh is from Colorado College in Colorado
Springs and is owner of an extensive collection of drawings and
medieval manuscripts. Color slides from his own collection will be
shown.
Experiment To Host Poetry Reading
The Experiment will present poetry reading by several students
this afternoon at 4:15 p.m.
Students appearing will be Don Patterson, author of Napa And
Beyond; Keith Howard, Sequoia editor; Bob Yaeger and Dick War
wick.
