# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Registration for spring quarter membership in The Experiment
will continue through this Friday from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. in The
Experiment building. There are still openings for approximately
50 persons in the original 27 seminars.
In addition, there are two new seminars; "The Media and the
Kennedy Assassination," led by Mark Lane, author of the best
selling Rush to Judgment, and"The Spiritual Strangulation of
Soviet Jewry," led by Rabbi Charles Familant.
Individuals who already have signed up but who have not yet
paid their $10 registration fee are requested to do so by Friday,
April 14.
★ ★ ★
The Overseas Campus Board is presently selecting its new mem
bers. Any returning alumnus of a Stanford Overseas Campus who
will be attending Stanford next year is eligible. Two members will
be selected from each campus.
The Board is an independently organized student group whose
functions include assisting the Overseas Campuses Office in the
operation of the Overseas Campus Program.
★ ★ ★
Ralph Harris, a noted British economist who has become dis
illusioned with the welfare state, will speak at the Western Politica
Society's first meeting of the quarter in the large lounge of Tresid
der at 7:45 this evening.
Harris is the Director of the Institute of Economic Affairs which
was formed as an educational trust in 1957 to raise the standard
of economic discussion by spreading knowledge of basic principles
and market analysis.
For his first trip to the United States, Harris will lecture on
"Problems and Prospects of the British Economy and the British
Welfare State".
Current issues of the Western Politica will be distributed at the
lecture.

A Phi O Book Exchange runs Tuesday through Friday from 1

★ ★ ★
Students for a Democratic Society will meet tonight to plan
the Spring program. The meeting will begin at 8:15 in Tresidder
275.
Most of the meeting will be devoted to discussion of the action
to be taken during the "Nationwide Mobilization Week to End the
War," April 8-15, protesting Stanford's involvement in the Vietnam
War. According to SDS spokesman Leonard Siegel, "Whatever
form of direct protest we take during mobilization week will also
be discussed tonight."
Other items on the agenda are participation in the mass peace
march in San Francisco on April 15 and the Conference on Cali
fornia Politics which regional SDS has set for April 8-9.

Professor Jan Kott, Visiting Professor of Polish Literature at
Yale University, will give two talks today. The first, sponsored by
the Department of Speech and Drama, will be on "Hamlet and
Orestes" and will be at 4 p.m. in the small lounge of Tresidder. The
second, entitled "Polish Theatre Today", is sponsored by the Com
mittee on Russian, East European, and Communist Studies. It will
be at 8 tonight, also in the small lounge of Tresidder.
