# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Exchange students from Keio University in Japan will present
their first "Culture and Coffee Hour" this afternoon at 4 in the
Bechtel I-Center. Students will demonstrate Japanese folk dances
and songs.
★ ★ ★
Voting by seniors continues today from 9 a.m. until noon at En
gineering Corner for the three "Outstanding Professors" who will
speak at the Class Day Exercises.
★ ★ ★
The Stanford Observer will be distributed with The Daily Mon
day morning.
★ ★ ★
On Saturday the California Committee To Legalize Abortion
(CCLA) will hold a Vigil on the steps of the State Capitol in Sacra
mento. Those interested should visit the CCLA desk in the ASSU
loft by Friday evening.
★ ★ ★
Artists, sculptors and poets from the community will show their
works Saturday and Sunday from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. in a Artists
Mobilize For Peace Exhibit. The event will help launch the Spring
Mobilization Week.
★ ★ ★
Stanford Medical Student Day will be held Saturday at 9 a.m. in
the Physics Lecture Hall. It will feature the second Lowell A. Rantz
Memorial Lecture to be delivered by Dr. Robert W. Berliner, di
rector of intramural research for the National Heart Institute. He
will speak on "The Regulation of Sodium Excretion."
★ ★ ★
A color film on four contemporary Belgian painters and another
short film on tourism in Belguim will be featured at the Belgian
Evening Abroad Program on Saturday at 8 p.m. in the I-Center. A
brief talk and Belgian waffles will follow.
★ ★ ★
Stanford's Department of Music will present a Concert by the
Houle Consort to benefit Florence Flood Relief at 3:30 p.m. Sunday
in Dinkelspiel. Tickets are available at Tresidder Box Office.
★ ★ ★
John Logan will read his poetry Monday, April 10 at 8 p.m. in the
small lounge at Tresidder Union.
