# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
TRYOUTS FOR THE WATER SKI SHOW, Hydrofollies, will be
held tomorrow, April 22, at 10 a.m. at Lake Lagunita. Further infor
mation is available from Randy Ferguson in the Boathouse or at
327-6812.
★ ★ ★
The next SENIOR HAPPY HOUR will be held this Sunday, April
23, at The Alley from 9 to 11 p.m. Pitchers are 90c, girls are 10c
a glass.
The third JAPANESE COFFEE & CULTURE HOUR will be pre
sented this afternoon at 4 p.m. in the I-Center. A demonstration of
Ikiru, the Japanese* art of self defense, will be given, and Keio ex
change students will be on hand for conversation.
★ ★ ★
There will be a STANFORD COMPUTATION CENTER USERS
MEETING this afternoon at 3:15 p.m. in Room 214, History Corner.
Dr. Edward Feigenbaum, Director, will describe the computing
services available at the Center, especially the recently arrived IBM
360/67 time-sharing system.
This is the last day for students, faculty and staff to purchase
STANFORD DISCOUNT TICKETS FOR THE PHILADELPHIA
ORCHESTRA CONCERT on May 28 before general sales are open
to the public. Tickets will be sold at the Post Office from 9:30 a.m.
to 3 p.m. and at the north side of the Faculty Club from 11:30 a.m.
to 1:30 p.m.
★ ★ ★
The AFRICAN ART DISPLAY will remain in Tresidder Union
until April 29. An exhibition of African History through books and
artifacts will be in the Hoover Institution until April 29 as well.
Africa Culture Week will begin next Monday, April 24, with a
speech in Dinkelspiel by the visiting Tanzanian Minister of State
for Foreign Affairs; on Tuesday there will be a symposium in
Cubberley on "Africa in the World Setting."

a concert of classical and Flamenco music at Memorial Auditorium
tonight at 8:30 for the benefit of the University Lacrosse team.
