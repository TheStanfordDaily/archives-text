# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The procedures and pit-falls of APPLYING TO MEDICAL
SCHOOL will be outlined by I>K. DONALD STILWELL, premedi
cal advisor tonight and Friday at 8:15 p.m. General discussion will be
open and all students planning to apply for 1968 admission to
any medical school should plan to attend. Tonight's meeting is
slated for TMU 275, Friday night's, TMU 282.
"At "A"
PROFESSOR GEORGE BACH of the Graduate School of Busi
ness and PROFESSOR BERT HICKMAN of the Economics Depart
ment will discuss the present stale of the economy and President
Johnson's proposal for an increase in the federal income tax at 8
p.m. tonight in Tresidder Union's Large Lounge. A question-and
answer period will follow the opening statements.
★ ★ ★
The role of political action in THE EXPERIMENT will be de
bated at an important meeting today at 4:15 in the Experiment
building. The future of the organization will also be discussed.
★ ★ ★
"THE CONCEPT OF HALLUCINATION" will be discussed by
D. THEODORE S ARB IN at 4:15 in room M-114. A continuation of
the Research Seminars in Biobehavioral Sciences, the seminar is
preceeded by a coffee hour.
★ ★ ★
AN ORGAN RECITAL will be presented tonight at 8:00 in Me
morial Church BY DOCTORAL CANDIDATE B. LYNN HERBERT.
The second in the Stanford Music Department's new Tuesday
evening series, his program will include "Sonata, Opus 65, No. 3"
by Felix Mendelssohn and two chorale preludes by J. S. Bach.
★ ★ ★
A symposium "AFRICA IN THE WORLD SETTING" will be
presented at 4:15 this afternoon in Cubberley Auditorium.
Sponsored by the African Studies Committee, the symposium
will be introduced by panel moderator Professor David Abernathy
who will discuss "Infiltration and Neglect." Other speakers are
Professor John Amoria, a Ghanaian now at Stanford, Prof. Cle
ment Moore of Berkeley, and Prof. Hans Weiler from Stanford.
★ ★ ★
Due to popular demand the Coffee House "MEM WHAT?" will
now be open Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday from 9:00 - 12:30
p.m., and Friday and Saturday from 10:00 • 1:30 a.m.
★ ★ ★
A program featuring the words of SHIBATA ZENSHIN, 15th
century Japanese artist and craftsman will be presented to the
Stanford Committee for Art this morning at 10:00, in the Midiel
Room of the Stanford Museum.
★ ★ ★

Hand", "Rhinoceros", "Alio! Hallo Alo!", "How to Have a Good
Child", and "Overture". Presented at CUBBERLEY at 7:30, the pro
gram is open to Film Series ticket-holders only.
