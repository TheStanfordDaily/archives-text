Any graduate students or young faculty members who are
interested in learning more about the possibilities of STUDY AND
RESEARCH IN THE SOVIET UNION, may do so today when Mr.
E. Willis Brooks, Deputy Chairman of the Inter-University Com
mittee on Travel Grants — which administers the official exchange
of scholars between the two countries — will be available to answer
questions. Mr. Brooks will be in the small lounge in Tresidder
from 2 to 3 p.m.
This exchange is not solely for Russian language or area speci
alists. Anyone whose scholarly interests might be furthered by
a semester or an academic year in the USSR is eligible to apply.

Dr. Robert Wark, Curate 1 of the Huntington Library Art Col
lections in San Marino will discuss ROWLANDSON'S DRAWINGS
for a Tour in a Post Chaise, Wednesday at 8:00 p.m. in the Physics
Lecture Hall. The lecture is the second in a series sponsored by the
Department of Art and Architecture.
Dr. Wark, who was formerly on the faculties of Harvard and
Yale, is the author of numerous books and articles on English
painting of the 17th and 18th centuries. His works include a new
edition of Sir Joshua Reynolds' "Discourses".
