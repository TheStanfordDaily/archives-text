# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The Stanford ANTI-DRAFT UNION will hold a meeting tonight
calling together "We Won't Go" signers, as well as ADU members.
Dave Harris will present ideas for nation-wide "non-cooperation" to
take place this fall. Members of the ADU have announced plans to
demonstrate at the Federal Building in San Francisco on May 8
in support of a non-cooperator due to be sentenced. A conference
on conscientious objection has been proposed for late in May. The
ADU hopes to campaign for the "anti-rank and grade release" ref
erendum. The meeting is at 8:15 in Tresidder 271.
★ * *
Sabicas, the famous GYPSY FLAMENCO GUITARIST, will close
the ASSU's 1967 concert season. He will appear at 8.30 p.m. today,
in Memorial Auditorium. Tickets are on sale at Tresidder Union box
office. Sabicas, a native of Pamplona, Andalusia, largely self-taught.
During his professional career he has accompanied dancer Carmen
Amaya, and appeared in many concerts both in America and Europe.
★ ★ ★
The keynote speech of AFRICAN CULTURE WEEK will be given
today at 4:15 in Cubberley by the Hon. Makame, visiting Minister
of State for Foreign Affairs of the United Republic of Tanzania.
★ ★ ★
Tickets for Synge's PLAYBOY OF THE WESTERN WORLD will
be on sale at the Tresidder Box Office for $1.00 starting today. It
will be staged Thursday, May 24 through Sunday, May 28 in the
Nitery Theatre at 8:30 p.m. This undergraduate production is spon
sored by the Department of Speech and Drama, and will be directed
by Glenn Cannon, a professional actor with the Stanford Repertory
Theater and currently an instructor in advanced acting in the de
partment.

There are currently three EXHIBITS IN TRESIDDER. "Indians of
San Sebastian," photographs by anthropology students Ken and
Kitty Harding, is in the lobby through May 7. A collection of African
art is in the south corridor until May 5. The exhibition of works by
contemporary artists from Los Angeles continues in the north corri
dor through May 4.
