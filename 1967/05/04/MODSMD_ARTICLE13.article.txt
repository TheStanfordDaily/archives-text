BENJAMIN F. I'AYTON, Executive Director of the Department
of Social Justice of the National Council of Churches will speak
this evening in Tresidder Small Lounge at 7:30 on "Race, Poverty,
and Peace."
★ ★ ★
Freshman women and seniors drawing into Hoskins House will
DRAW TODAY. Senior women who view Hoskins House as a
second alternative to OFF should participate in the draw. Seniors
drawing into Hoskins House should draw in pairs.
★ ★ ★
A public meeting concerned with the fight to preserve the RUM
FOItD OPEN HOUSING ACT will be held at eight o'clock in Fel
lowship Hall of the Palo Alto Presbyterian Church, 1140 Cowper
Street. Sponsor of the meeting is Mid-peninsula Citizens for Fair
Housing, a political action group that grew out of the local "No
on Proposition 14" campaign of 1964.
★ ★ ★
DEAN ROBERT HIND, Associate Dean of Undergraduate Edu
cation, will be the guest speaker today at the Stanford Y's lunch
discussion. He will be talking about education innovations being
put into effect at Stanford. Dean Hind has been closely associated
with curricular changes, and studies such as the Packer Commit
tee. This discussion is one of a series on the structure of the Uni
versity and its interaction with the local community. Lunches are
served in the Clubhouse basement for 50 cents at noon on Thurs
days.
■At "A
The I-Center will show a Russian film, "THE FORTY-FIRST,"
at 8:15 p.m., Tresidder Room 274-5. Grigori Chukhrai directed
the film. Coffee and discussion at the I-Center will follow with
Cesare Massarenti.
Ar "At
CHARLES LUCET, FRENCH AMBASSADOR TO THE UNITED
STATES, WILL SPEAK IN FRENCH at 4:15 p.m., on "The prin
cipal Intellectual Trends in France Today." Lucet, a career dip
lomat, has served for 13 years in the United States. A native of
Paris, he holds a degree in law from the University of Paris, and
a degree in political science from L'Ecole Libre des Sciences
Politiques.

FOItD OPEN HOUSING ACT will be held at eight o'clock in Fel
lowship Hall of the Palo Alto Presbyterian Church, 1140 Cowper
Street. Sponsor of the meeting is Mid-peninsula Citizens for Fair
Housing, a political action group that grew out of the local "No
on Proposition 14" campaign of 1964.
★ ★ ★
DEAN ROBERT HIND, Associate Dean of Undergraduate Edu
cation, will be the guest speaker today at the Stanford Y's lunch
discussion. He will be talking about education innovations being
put into effect at Stanford. Dean Hind has been closely associated
with curricular changes, and studies such as the Packer Commit
tee. This discussion is one of a series on the structure of the Uni
versity and its interaction with the local community. Lunches are
served in the Clubhouse basement for 50 cents at noon on Thurs
days.

Cesare Massarenti.
