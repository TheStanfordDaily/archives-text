# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
At 12:00 Friday free tickets to the MORT SAHL CONCERT will
be available at the Tresidder Box Office. The performance itself
will be held Tuesday, May 16, at 7:30 in Dinkelspiel.
★ ★ ★
A special exhibition of POLISH FOLK ART is on display dur
ing May in the Main Library. The exhibition, arranged by the
Polish Arts and Culture Committee, includes photographs, handi
crafts, jewelry, and dolls dressed in regional costumes.
★ ★ ★
The Tresidder Film Series will present tonight "UMBERTO D.,"
directed by Vittorio De Seca. The film, acclaimed by critics as
"... one of the three or four milestones in motion pictures," will
be shown at 7:30, 8:30, and 9:30.
★ ★ ★
THE ORGANIZATION OF ARAB STUDENTS at Stanford is
planning to present an evening of Arabian activities on Saturday
at 8:00 in Tresidder Large Lounge. The event will feature slides of
the Arab World, music, and a belly dance.
★ ★ ★
The Saturday night Tresidder film will be Philippe de Broca's
"FIVE DAY LOVER," starring Jean Seberg. The movie will be
shown Saturday at 7:30, 8:30, and 9:30.
★ ★ ★
SANDY BULL, electric guitar, oud, and sarod player, one of the
pioneers in the American Eastern music movement, will appear in
concert on Friday, May 19 at 8 p.m., Tresidder Large Lounge. Tick
ets are on sale at the Tresidder Box Office for $1.50.
★ ★ ★
The May issue of THE STANFORD OBSERVER will contain a
report from Nevitt Sanford's book "Why Colleges Fail", and will
include an interesting section on student activists and a story
on how Stanford picks its students.
★ ★ ★
The Stanford Speech Association has announced the SEVENTH
ANNUAL STANFORD AMERICAN ISSUES DEBATE TOURNA
MENT to be held on the Stanford campus on Friday and Saturday,
May 19-20. The topic is "Resolved that the United States should
withdraw from Vietnam." Any Stanford student may participate,
in either one-man or two-man teams, defending either the affirma
tive or negative side. Those interested in entering contact Prof.
Kenneth Mosier, Department of Speech, Mem Aud., Rm. 100, x 2647,
by Monday, May 15.
