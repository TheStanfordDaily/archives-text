# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
POLYCHROME SCULPTURE EXHIBIT in and around The Ex
periment Building is on display from now through May 29. Co
sponsored by The Experiment and the FUPA, the exhibit involves
app. 10 pieces of metal sculpture made by Ron Boise (a young
sculpture who died a couple of years ago) and painted by Joseph
Lysowski. The exhibit includes a thunder machine and some un
usual musical instruments.
★ ★ ★
PHILLIP LUCE, author of The New Left, a study of the rise of
ultra-radicalism among college students, will speak Monday at 7:45
p.m. in Tresidder.
Luce had been widely recognized as a leader and spokesman of the
New Left and of the Maoist-oriented Communist Party until his
break with them over a year ago.
In 1963 he lead a student trip to Cuba in violation of a State De
partment ban, and recently authored the first declaration calling men
to refuse to fight in Vietnam.
★ ★ ★
The annual CHINA NIGHT, sponsored by the Chinese Students
Club of Stanford, will take place Sunday in Dinkelspiel Auditorium
at 7:30 p.m.
The club will feature a movie on China, a folk chorus, and Chinese
classical music and dances. Professor Chin-ni Lin will give a Chinese
painting demonstration before the audience, and award his painting
as a prize.
Saturday afternoon and all day Sunday, Chinese art, literature
and paintings will be exhibited at the I-Center.
