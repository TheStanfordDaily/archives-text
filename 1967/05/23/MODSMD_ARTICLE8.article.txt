# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Open hearings continue today, tomorrow and Thursday for the
STUDY OF EDUCATION AT STANFORD. The steering committee
session today is from 7:30 to 10 p.m. in Room 143 of the Under
graduate Library. It will hear ideas on the Freshman year program,
and General Studies and Major requirements. The residence Policies
Committee will conduct its hearings from 7:30 to 10 p.m. tomorrow
and from 4 to 6 p.m. Thursday in the same room. All members of
the Stanford community have been invited to present their views.
Written statements are also welcome.
•k ★ ★
A transdenominational celebration of the birthday of BUDDHA
is planned for tonight at 9 in Memorial Church. The event, con
ceived and organized by students, will be composed of a light show,
live and recorded music, community chanting, the reading of poetic
works, and a consultation of the ancient Chinese oracle, the I Ching.
★ ★ ★
Former Student Body President DAVID HARRIS will show Felix
Greene's slides of North Vietnam tonight at 7:30 in the Madera
Lounge. He will also discuss non-cooperation with the United States
military establishment.
★ ★ *
The HISTORY DEPARTMENT will sponsor its last coffee hour of
the academic year this afternoon in room 228. The informal meeting
is open to all undergraduate students. Jack Trinklein, head of the
organizing committee, reports that usually professors outnumber
students two to one. * ★ ★
The weekly JAZZ TUESDAY will be held this evening at 10:30
in the I-Center. The program will include Wendy Weiner, singer;
Mike Menke, bass; Johnny Felder, drums; Mike Fuller, sax; Jeff
Stevens, piano; John Ferejohn, sax and flute. Other musicians are
invited to join this group for improvisation and jamming.
★ ★ *
The INTERNATIONAL CLUB is organizing a series of three or
four seminars entitled "THE BRAVE NEW WORLD" dealing with
effects of technology in the progress and alienation of society. Mr.
Vernon Thompson will be the main speaker and moderator. No
prior specialization is necessary. First of the seminars will be held
in Room 275 Tresidder at 8:15 tonight.
★ ★ ★
'♦A FESTIVAL OF SHORT FILMS," part of the University Film
Series, will be shown this evening at 7:30 in Cubberley Auditorium.
Admission is by series ticket only.
★ ★ ★
The SPEECH AND DRAMA Department will present its major
spring undergraduate production, "PLAYBOY OF THE WESTERN
'WORLD," by J. M. Synge, tomorrow through Friday at 8:30 p.m.
In the Nitery Theatre. This Irish comedy-drama is directed by Glenn
Cannon, professional member of the Stanford Repertory Theatre
and Lecturer in Acting in the Speech and Drama Department.
Tickets are one dollar, available at TMU Box Office, or at the theatre
t>n performance nights.
★ * ★
5 "THREATENING SKIES," a film produced in North Vietnam,
\vill be shown tonight in Tresidder, Room 270-271 at 8:15. The show
ing is sponsored by the Stanford Anti-Draft Union.
★ ★ ★

*afternoon in Frost Amphitheatre will be on sale today and tomorrow
'in front of the Post Office. The tickets are $3.00 and $1.50.

ceived and organized by students, will be composed of a light show,
live and recorded music, community chanting, the reading of poetic
works, and a consultation of the ancient Chinese oracle, the I Ching.

Former Student Body President DAVID HARRIS will show Felix
Greene's slides of North Vietnam tonight at 7:30 in the Madera
Lounge. He will also discuss non-cooperation with the United States
military establishment.

The HISTORY DEPARTMENT will sponsor its last coffee hour of
the academic year this afternoon in room 228. The informal meeting
is open to all undergraduate students. Jack Trinklein, head of the
organizing committee, reports that usually professors outnumber
students two to one. * ★ ★
The weekly JAZZ TUESDAY will be held this evening at 10:30
in the I-Center. The program will include Wendy Weiner, singer;
Mike Menke, bass; Johnny Felder, drums; Mike Fuller, sax; Jeff
Stevens, piano; John Ferejohn, sax and flute. Other musicians are
invited to join this group for improvisation and jamming.
★ ★ *
The INTERNATIONAL CLUB is organizing a series of three or
four seminars entitled "THE BRAVE NEW WORLD" dealing with
effects of technology in the progress and alienation of society. Mr.
Vernon Thompson will be the main speaker and moderator. No
prior specialization is necessary. First of the seminars will be held
in Room 275 Tresidder at 8:15 tonight.

Open hearings continue today, tomorrow and Thursday for the
STUDY OF EDUCATION AT STANFORD. The steering committee
session today is from 7:30 to 10 p.m. in Room 143 of the Under
graduate Library. It will hear ideas on the Freshman year program,
and General Studies and Major requirements. The residence Policies
Committee will conduct its hearings from 7:30 to 10 p.m. tomorrow
and from 4 to 6 p.m. Thursday in the same room. All members of
the Stanford community have been invited to present their views.
Written statements are also welcome.
