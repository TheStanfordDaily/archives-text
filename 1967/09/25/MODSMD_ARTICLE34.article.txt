# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Auditions for students interested in joining MUSICAL ORGANI
ZATIONS will begin today.
The schedule calls for Sandor Salgo of the Music Department to
hold auditions for the Opera Workshop today from 2 to 4 and
tomorrow from 10 a.m. to 12, and for the Symphony Orchestra,
Chamber Orchestra, and chamber music ensembles from 10 to 12
today and 2 to 4 p.m. tomorrow and Wednesday. The auditions will
be in Dinkelspiel Auditorium.
Harold Schmidt will hold auditions for the Chorus, Choir, and
Chorales today and tomorrow from 9 a.m. to noon and 2 to 4 p.m.
in Room 3, Dinkelspiel. Men's Glee Club auditions will be directed
by Robert MacKinnon tomorrow from 7 to 9 p.m. and Thursday
from 3:15 to 4 p.m. in the Rehearsal Hall in Dinkelspiel.
Arthur Barnes will hold auditions for Concert Band and Wind
Ensemble today and tomorrow from 10 a.m. to noon and 2 to 4
p.m. in Room 1, Dinkelspiel. Marching and Studio Band auditions
will be held during the same hours at the Band House, behind the
Basketball Pavillion.

All applications for the MEDICAL COLLEGE ADMISSIONS
TEST (MCAT) must be received in New York by October 6, ac
cording to the Counseling and Testing Center.
Students may pick up application forms in the Counseling and
Testing Center office on the third floor of the Old Union building.

Professor John W. Dodds was named winner of the annual $3,500
DINKELSPIEL AWARD for excellence in teaching at Commence
ment earlier this month. Undergraduate winners were Seniors
Mary Frances Swenson and Michael Roster.
