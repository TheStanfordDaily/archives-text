# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Inquiries about the DANFORTH GRADUATE FELLOWSHIPS,
to be awarded in March, 1968, are invited, according to Dr. David
Abernethy, Assistant Professor of Political Science, the local cam
pus representative (Office hours: Wednesday 2-4, Room 22 I.)
Applicants for the Danforth Graduate Fellowship must take
the Graduate Record Examination on October 28, 1967, if they have
not already done so. October 10 is the last date on which the Educa
tional Testing Service will accept applications for the October GRE.
★ ★ ★
Six to eight students are needed to sell refreshments at the
ARTHUR FIEDLER YOMIURI NIPPON SYMPHONY CONCERT
this Thursday at 2:30. Those interested should sign up on the list
posted on the Ram's Head office door, in the loft in the ASSU office.
Details will be available there. Call extension 4336 for questions.
★ ★ ★
The GOVERNMENT DOCUMENT LIBRARY in Room 101 of the
Main Library will be open the following hours: 8 a.m. to 10 p.m.,
Monday through Thursday; 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. Friday; 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.
Saturday, and 1 to 5 p.m. Sunday. There will be a professional ref
erence librarian on duty from 6 to 10 pm. Monday through Thursday
and from 1 to 5 p.m. on Saturday.
★ ★ ★
A printing error in the STANFORD REPERTORY THEATER
BROCHURE distributed at registration has caused some confusion
about ticket prices. The Tuesday through Thursday student price
for "Candaules, Commissioner," scheduled to play in the Nitery
January 9 through February 3 was incorrectly noted as $1.00. The
correct price is $1.50, which brings the student price for all four
shows up to $6.00, the printed subscription price.
