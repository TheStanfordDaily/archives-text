# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The Stanford ANTI-DRAFT UNION and STUDENTS FOR A
DEMOCRATIC SOCIETY, will hold a general meeting tor Stop
the- Draft Week tonight at 7:30 in Cubberley Auditorium for all
Stanford and Palo Alto participants.
The meeting is termed "imperative" by Anti-Draft Union Chair
man, Leonard Seigel. The strategies and tactical problems oi the
coming demonstrations will be explained. Legal advice and trans
portation information will be provided. Bus tickets will be sold after
the meeting.
★ ★ ★
LANCELOT LAW YVHYTE, acknowledged as one of the greatest
living philosophers, will present the second lecture in the Century
21 series this evening at 7:30 in the Tresidder Lounge. His topic
is "Science, Man and the Year 2000; From Knowledge to Under
standing."
Whyte has written fourteen books and his interests center on
man's relationship to the technical, the natural and the spiritual.
★ ★ ★
THE RESULTS OF SOCIAL CHANGE AND TECHNOLOGY
on theology will be the topic of discussion tonight at the Univer
sity Christian Movement seminar at 9 p.m. on the 3rd floor lounge
of the Clubhouse. Ralph Ellison's book. Invisible Man, will be used
as a case study.
★ ★ ★
Leading a FORUM DISCUSSION ON AFRICA from noon to 1:00
today in Tresidder will be B. L. K. Sanyang, Minister for Local Gov
ernment, Land, and Mines of Gambia.
Also present will be Arthur Davis of the U.S. State Department.
The discussion is sponsored by the Africa desk of the Institute of
International Relations. The group will meet regularly for luncheon
discussions.
★ ★ ★
A DISCUSSION OF WHITE RESONSE TO BLACK POWER will
take place this evening at 7:15 p.m. in Arroyo Lounge, Wilbur. A
tape of a speech by Stokeley Carmichael will be featured. Andy
Horowitz, member of the East Palo Alto Community House stu
dent project, will comment on local racial problems.
"THE END OF ST. PETERSBURG" will be shown tonight in
Tresidder at 8:15, room 274-5. The Russian-made film was created
in 1926 to dramatize, through the eyes of a peasant, the social up
heavel in St. Petersburg.
★ ★ ★
THE DEADLINE FOR THE RECEIPT OF APPLICATIONS for
the National Science Foundation Graduate Fellowships is December
8, 1967, and for postdoctoral fellowships, December 11. Selection
will be made by the Foundation, with awards to be announced on
March 15, 1968.

DEMOCRATIC SOCIETY, will hold a general meeting tor Stop
the- Draft Week tonight at 7:30 in Cubberley Auditorium for all
Stanford and Palo Alto participants.
The meeting is termed "imperative" by Anti-Draft Union Chair
man, Leonard Seigel. The strategies and tactical problems oi the
coming demonstrations will be explained. Legal advice and trans
portation information will be provided. Bus tickets will be sold after
the meeting.
★ ★ ★
LANCELOT LAW YVHYTE, acknowledged as one of the greatest
living philosophers, will present the second lecture in the Century
21 series this evening at 7:30 in the Tresidder Lounge. His topic
is "Science, Man and the Year 2000; From Knowledge to Under
standing."
Whyte has written fourteen books and his interests center on
man's relationship to the technical, the natural and the spiritual.
★ ★ ★
THE RESULTS OF SOCIAL CHANGE AND TECHNOLOGY
on theology will be the topic of discussion tonight at the Univer
sity Christian Movement seminar at 9 p.m. on the 3rd floor lounge
of the Clubhouse. Ralph Ellison's book. Invisible Man, will be used
as a case study.
★ ★ ★
Leading a FORUM DISCUSSION ON AFRICA from noon to 1:00
today in Tresidder will be B. L. K. Sanyang, Minister for Local Gov
ernment, Land, and Mines of Gambia.
Also present will be Arthur Davis of the U.S. State Department.
The discussion is sponsored by the Africa desk of the Institute of
International Relations. The group will meet regularly for luncheon
discussions.
★ ★ ★
A DISCUSSION OF WHITE RESONSE TO BLACK POWER will
take place this evening at 7:15 p.m. in Arroyo Lounge, Wilbur. A
tape of a speech by Stokeley Carmichael will be featured. Andy
Horowitz, member of the East Palo Alto Community House stu
dent project, will comment on local racial problems.
"THE END OF ST. PETERSBURG" will be shown tonight in
Tresidder at 8:15, room 274-5. The Russian-made film was created
in 1926 to dramatize, through the eyes of a peasant, the social up
heavel in St. Petersburg.
★ ★ ★
THE DEADLINE FOR THE RECEIPT OF APPLICATIONS for
the National Science Foundation Graduate Fellowships is December
8, 1967, and for postdoctoral fellowships, December 11. Selection
will be made by the Foundation, with awards to be announced on
March 15, 1968.
