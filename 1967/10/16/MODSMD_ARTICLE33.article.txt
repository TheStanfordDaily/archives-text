# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
JUDICIAL COUNCIL Chairman John Raskin
has announced that applications for the six open
positions on the ASSU Judicial Council must be
submitted by Wednesday morning at the ASSU
office.
Applications are available in the office. Inter
views will be held Wednesday and Thursday.
★ ★ ★
Information meetings on the BRITAIN and
ITALY OVERSEAS CAMPUSES will be held to
night in the Roble dining hall. The Britain meet
ing will be held at 7:30 p.m. followed by the Italy
session at 9 p.m.
"Alumni" from previous Italy and Britain
groups will be available for questions after a
short presentation which includes slides of the
campuses.
★ ★ ★
A talk on MEHER BABA, a God-realized Master
living in India, is planned for 8 p.m. in Tresid
der, Room 270, sponsored by the Free University.
The speakers will be Rick Chapman, who recent
ly returned from India where he was on a Ful

bright fellowship; and Bob Dreyfus, who has also
spent time in India. Dreyfus was one of the first
westerners to discuss the significance of drugs
with Baba.

STANFORD-IN-W ASHIN GTON (SIW), the
program which places Stanford students in gov
ernment offices for the summer, whill hold an
orientation meeting for all interested students
this afternoon at. 5 p.m. in the large lounge of

Greg Murphy, SIW president, also plans a
