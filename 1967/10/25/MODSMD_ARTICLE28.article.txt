# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Robert C. North's CENTURY 21 lecture on "The International
Political System," originally scheduled today, has been postponed
until Wednesday, Nov. 1, at 7:30 p.m. in Tresidder large lounge.
North is a Stanford professor of political science. His talk will
be the fourth in the Century 21 series on the impact of technological
change on society.
The lecture will be followed Thursday, Nov. 2, by Louis B. Sohn,
Harvard law professor, on "The World Rule of Law."
★ ★ ★
Anthropologist RICHARD ADAMS, of the University of Texas,
will speak on "Power and Development in Guatemala" this after
noon at 2 p.m. in Tresidder Room 282. A discussion period will fol
low Adams' lecture, sponsored by the Latin American Studies As
sociation.
★ ★ ★
The Keio Exchange Committee of the HE is accepting applica
tions from those interested in studying in Japan for one year. One
student will be selected to study at Keio University in Tokyo and
will be given a scholarship.
Applications are available at the upstairs office in the I-Center.
The deadline for applications is tomorrow at 5 p.m. Barry Moller
will answer questions at 327-1984.
★ ★ ★
Harvard linguist Professor ROMAN JAKOBSON will explore the
topic "Linguistics in its relation to other sciences" in a lecture this
afternoon at 4:15 in the Forum Room of the Meyer Undergraduate
Library.
★ ★ ★
The STANFORD CHAPARRAL requests cartoons, comic strips,
and straight as well as humorous articles, for its issue coming out
later this quarter. The deadline for material is Nov. 3. Material may
be left in envelopes distributed around campus, or brought to the
Chaparral office in the Student Publications building.
Staff meetings are held every Wednesday night at 7:30, and are
open to all interested students.
★ ★ ★
Professor J. HERMAN BLAKE will lecture this evening on "The
Negro Protest — Challenge to Values" as a part of the six-lecture
series sponsored by the AAUW. He will speak at the Ortega School
Multipurpose Room, 830 East Meadow, Palo Alto, at 7:45 p.m.
Professor Blake's interests include the problems of civil rights,
the black power movement, and Black Muslims. He graduated
from New York University and has served as Director of American
Studies for the Peace Corps training program.
