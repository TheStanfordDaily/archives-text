"GRAND CANYON OF THE LIVING COLORADO," a color
photographic exhibit, will be displayed on the second floor of Tre
sidder Union until November 6. This exhibit, sponsored by the
Stanford Conservation Group and produced by the Sieira Club,
Is one of four now being shown around the country. Using large
color photographs, it portrays the rock structure and life along the
river, now threatened by Bureau of Reclamation dams. The com
panion movie on the Grand Canyon, produced by the Sierra Club,
will be shown in connection with the exhibit; watch OATQ for
details.
★ ★ ★
ROBERT K. JAEDICKE, Stanford Graduate School of Business
professor of accounting, has been named co-recipient of an award
for "distinguished contributions to accounting literature for 1966"
by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants.
Prof. Jaedicke and former Stanford Business School Prof. Yuji
Ijlri won the award for research on "Reliability and Objectivity of
Accounting Methods", described in Accounting Review for July
1966. Prof. Jaedicke will receive a gold medal and share a $500
cash prize. The Jaedicke-Ijiri research provides a method by which
accountants can judge relative merits of two opposing accounting
theories: evaluating assets at their present value or at cost.

professor of accounting, has been named co-recipient of an award
for "distinguished contributions to accounting literature for 1966"
by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants.
Prof. Jaedicke and former Stanford Business School Prof. Yuji
Ijlri won the award for research on "Reliability and Objectivity of
Accounting Methods", described in Accounting Review for July
1966. Prof. Jaedicke will receive a gold medal and share a $500
cash prize. The Jaedicke-Ijiri research provides a method by which
accountants can judge relative merits of two opposing accounting
theories: evaluating assets at their present value or at cost.
