# Campus Roundup

Stanford's DEPARTMENT OF MUSIC is presenting a concert
tonight at 8:00 in Dinkelspiel Auditorium, featuring music "Then
and Now", directed by George Hould and Leland Smith. Admission
is $1.50; Students $1.00; all seats are reserved.
★ ★ ★
The FRESHMAN FILM SERIES is showing "Trouble is Para
dise", a 1932 comedy directed by Ernest Lubitsch. It was based on
the play "The Honest Finder" by Laszlo Aladar, and begins at 8:00
p.m. and 10:00 p.m. in 320, Geology Corner.
★ ★ ★
Everyone is invited to the I-CENTER JAZZ CASUAL at 9:00 p.m.
in the I-Center. Bring your own instrument; it will be informal and
spontaneous and coffee will be served.
★ ★ ★
Details and applications are available for the JOHN NOBLE
FOUNDATION LEADERSHIP GRANT for graduate study at
Columbia University. This award is open to male seniors who are
planning graduate work in the following fields: law, journalism,
medicine, social sciences, theology, business, and education; and
those who are interested in the international aspects of their pro
fessions. The grant carries a stipend of $2,500 for each year of
graduate work. Information is available in the Financial Aids
Office.
