# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Former SNCC field secretary and PALO ALTO RESISTANCE
member Dennis Sweeney will speak tonight in the Tresidder Large
Lounge. During the 7:30 meeting Sweeney will talk about his recent
discussions with the National Liberation Front in Prague. All stu
dents interested in the Resistance or in the Vietnam War are in
vited to attend.
★ ★ ★
PROFESSOR ROGER FREEMAN, an economist and Senior Staff
Member of the Hoover Institution will speak tonight at 7:30 in
Muir Lounge, Stern Hall. Freeman recently spent several months
in Russia observing the incentive system the Soviets think is the
answer to free competition. The meeting is sponsored by the Stan
ford Area Young Republicans who will hold a "Think and Drink"
session following the discussion.
★ ★ ★
A one day sale of original prints from the ROTEN GALLERIES
will be held tomorrow at Tresidder in the second floor lobby from
10 a.m. to 5 p.m. More than 500 original etchings, lithographs, and
woodcuts by Picasso, Chagall, Lautrec, Renoir, Goya, Baskin and
other modern artists and a selection of manuscript pages from
works of the 13th and 15th centuries will be offered for sale. Prices
start at $5.00.
★ ★ ★
Applications for the GRADUATE RECORD EXAMINATION to
be given Saturday, December 9, must be turned in by Monday,
November 14. Applications may be picked up in the counseling
and testing office on the third floor of the Old Union.
★ ★ ★
Italian archaeologist DR. BALDO CONTICELLO is this evening's
featured speaker at a public art lecture at 8:00 in the Physics Lec
ture Hall. Dr. Conticello is Archaeological Inspector for the De
partment of Antiquities at Rome, and Director of the National
Archaeological Museum of Sperlonga.
★ ★ ★
All MARINES, present, former, future and friends of the Corps
are invited to help commemorate its 192 nd Anniversary at the
ROTC Annex, Mahan Hall Friday at 2:30. Refreshments will follow.
★ ★ ★
DIS-JOINT, one of Stanford's coffee houses, has planned a three
evening presentation series, beginning tonight with "Surprise
Night" at the coffee house. Tomorrow evening Gray, Katie, John
and Company will do their musical "thing," according to Dis-Joint
Director Greg Yahna. A Friday dance on White Plaza will end the
series. Beginning at 9:30, the dance and refreshments will be "ab
solutely free." The Gyre will perform. Any other bands interested
in playing should contact John Hejl at 327-2145.
★ ★ ★
STUDENT-FACULTY BRIDGE NIGHT is tomorrow from 8-11:30
p.m. in Tresidder Large Lounge. Student sign-ups continue in front
of the post office. All interested faculty and teaching assistants
should call Jon Adams, 328-6529, to reserve tables.
★ ★ ★

FOUNDATION LEADERSHIP GRANT for Graduate study at
Columbia University. The $2,500 award is open to male seniors who
plan to do graduate work in law, medicine, social sciences, theology,
business, or education. Applications are available at the Financial
Aids Office in the Old Union and must be submitted by December 4.
