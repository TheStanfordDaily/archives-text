# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
A BE-IN is to be held Sunday from noon to six at El Camino Park
across from the Stanford Shopping Center. It is sponsored by the
Peace and Freedom Party. Entertainment will be provided by The
San Francisco Peace and Freedom Players and three bands — Kid
Africa, Lincoln Avenue Boy's Choir, and The Heatherstone. Voter
registrars will be operating there all day.
★ ★ ★
The Hillel Foundation will sponsor "SALLAH", an Israeli satiric
comedy, Sunday in Cubberley. The film was shown at the San
Francisco Film Festival in 1964 and was awarded two Golden Gate
Awards, two Golden Globe Awards, and was nominated for an
Academy Award in "The Best Foreign Film" category. It will be
shown at 5:30 and 8:00. Tickets are available at the Tresidder Box
Office or at the Hillel office in the Clubhouse. The price is $2.00
adults, $1.50 students.
★ * ★
The DEPARTMENT OF MUSIC will present a free concert of
chamber music at 3:30 Sunday in Dink. A newly formed student
quartet will play Haydn's "Quartet, Opus 76, No. 12." The Stanford
Wind Ensemble, under the direction of Prof. Arthur Barnes will per
form Richard Strauss' "Suite for 13 Wind Instruments." Several
part-songs will be presented by the Stanford Chorale under the
direction of Robert Rockbrand, D.M.A.
★ ★ ★
The SYMPHONY ORCHESTRA OF SAN FRANCISCO STATE
COLLEGE, under the direction of Laszlo Varga, will give its first
Fall Concert Sunday at 3 p.m. in the Main Auditorium of the School
of Creative Arts. The program will include "Consecration of the
House" overture, Opus 124 by Beethoven, Bartok's "Divertimento
for String Orchestra," and "Symphonie Fantastique" by Berlioz.
Reservations can be obtained through the creative Arts Box Of
fice, San Francisco State College, 1600 HoUoway, San Francisco,
94132. The phone number is 585-7174.
★ ★ ★
MARIE-CLAIRE ALAIN, French organist, will present a recital
tonight at 8:15 at the First Methodist Church, Palo Alto. She is
sponsored by the Friday Evening Concert Series, and the San Jose
Chapter of the American Guild of Organists. The program will
consist of numbers by Lubeck, J. S. Bach, Heiller, Franck, and Jehan
Alain. There is a $2.00 donation.
★ ★ ★
There will be an ALL-CAMPUS DANCE Saturday at 8:00 in
Otero (Wilbur). It is sponsored by the I-Club and the band will be
the Garlops, a Philippino band. The admission is 25c with girls and
I-Club members free. Refreshments will be served.

The QURANIC DISCUSSION CIRCLE will meet tonight from
7:30 to 9:00 in room 269 of TMU. Mr. Vicar Ahmed Seyed will speak

by a group discussion. It is sponsored by the Stanford Islamic So
ciety.
