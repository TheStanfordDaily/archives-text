# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Sunday at 8:30 p.m. in the Magnolia Lounge of Lagunita there
will bo a CONCERT OF NEW MUSIC. Drummer Oliver Johnson is
leading a group of San Francisco musicians including Raphael Gar
rett and Joe Halpin, basses, and Neal Jukes, tenor saxophone and
flute.
Johnson and Halpin are members of the Denny Zeitlen Trio.
Jewkes and Garrett have both played at Stanford in the past. Ad
mission is free for the concert, jointly presented by the ASSU and
Lagunita.
★ ★ ★
"MORMONISM, POLITICS, AND GEORGE ROMNEY" will be
the subject of a talk by David K. Hart, professor of political science
at San Francisco State and campaign consultant for the Republican
Party. The talk will be today at noon at the LDS Institute building,
865 Stanford Ave., Palo Alto. All are invited.
★ ★ ★
ENGINEERING STUDENTS will have an opportunity to give
their views and responses to the SES position paper at a meeting
Monday night. The meeting, sponsored by Tau Beta Pi, will be held
at 7:30 in the Large Lounge, Tresidder.
