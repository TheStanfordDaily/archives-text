# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The Arab view of the MIDDLE EAST CRISIS will be presented
today at the I-Center at 4:15, as part of the Current Events Coffee
Hour. This week the Arab Students at Stanford will lead the dis
cussion; next week the Israeli point of view will be given. Weekly
discussions of current problems and political crisis will continue
through out the year.
★ ★ ★
The COMMITTEE ON AFRICAN STUDIES, in cooperation with
the Hoover Institution will hold a seminar meeting at Tresidder
Union, Room 271, tonight at 8:00 p.m. Professor J. Clyde Mitchell,
the guest speaker, is a Professor of Urban Sociology at the Univer
sity of Manchester, Manchester, England. His topic will be "Urban
ization in Central Africa."
★ ★ ★
THE FRESHMAN FILM SERIES will be showing "Twentieth
Century" tonight at 8:00 and 10:00 p.m. in Room 320, Geology Cor
ner. The 1934 comedy, directed by Howard Hawks, stars John Barry
more, Carole Lombard, and Walter Connolly.
★ ★ ★
Stanford's SKI CLUB has announced plans for the coming Winter
Carnival, to be held the first weekend in January. Tickets will be
on sale the week after Thanksgiving. It will be held this year at
Heavenly Valley.
★ ★ ★
The I-CENTER JAZZ CASUAL will begin this evening at 9:00
at the I-Center. Coffee will be served during the informal session;
everyone is invited to bring their horn and blow.
