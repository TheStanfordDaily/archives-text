# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Women supporting draft and war resisters will discuss WOMEN'S
COMPLICITY in Grove House tomorrow at 7:30 p.m.
By Thanksgiving, twenty-six Stanford women had signed a state
ment which admits aiding and abetting men who disobey the Selec
tive Service Act by returning their draft cards. Women may pick
up complicity statements, return signed statements, or find out
more about the movement at tables in White Plaza from 10 a.m.
to 2 p.m. this week.

Registration and primary balloting begins today In the MOCK
PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION sponsored by the Stanford Area
Young Republicans. A "poll tax" of ten cents will benefit the Project
Help tutoring program at the Crittenden School in Mountain View.

Deadline for OVERSEAS CAMPUS APPLICATIONS for groups
leaving in June and September is 5:00 p.m. Thursday, November 30.
Overseas groups for Summer and Autumn quarters are Britain VI
and Germany XXI. Austria VII and France and Italy XVII will be
in Europe for Fall and Winter quarters. Applications, course lists,
and further information may be obtained at the Overseas Cam
puses Office, Room 1-C.
★ ★ ★
A panel will discuss STUDENT POWER at the Palo Alto-Stanford
Democratic Club tonight at 8 p.m. in the Multipurpose Room of
Ohlones Elementary School, 445 Charleston Road, Palo Alto. The
panel will include Mrs. Agnes Robinson, member of the Palo Alto
school board; Dr. Scott Thomson, principal of Cubberley High
School; Tom Grissom, doctoral candidate in education at Stanford;
and Chris Menchine and Tim Wise, students at Cubberley High
School.

Opportunities for OFFICER COMMISSIONS in the United States
Marine Corps will be outlined for interested students today and
tomorrow from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m. in the Placement Center. Major
G. M. Larson will explain both Aviation and Line Officer Programs.
