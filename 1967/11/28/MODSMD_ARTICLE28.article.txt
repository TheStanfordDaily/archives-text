# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
SKI CLUB will present seven Warren Miller films, including films
of Squaw Valley, "Mod Magic on Skis," and "Wild Wonderful World
of Skiing," tonight at 7:30 in Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Admission is
25 cents.
★ ★ ★
Tickets for WINTER CARNIVAL will be on sale from 1:00 to
5:00 p.m. beginning today and through this week, in front of the
ASSU office. The $18 will include lodging, food, and dances for the
weekend.
★ ★ ★
Because of the strike, there will be no Wednesday night SAN
FRANCISCO SYMPHONY concert this week.
★ ★ ★
FRESHMEN FILM SERIES will show "It's a Gift" tonight at
8 and again at 10:30, and "Two Seconds" at 9:20 only. "It's a Gift"
(1934) stars W. C. Fields and was directed by Norman Z. McLeod;
"Two Seconds" (1932) stars Edward G. Robinson and was directed
by Mervyn Leßoy. Admission is free in Room 320, Geology Corner.
★ ★ ★
Next Tuesday, Dec. 5, the FRESHMEN FILM SERIES will pre
sent "The Gay Divorcee," at 8 p.m., as the last of the series for this
quarter. There will be a special showing at midnight also. The 1934
musical stars Fred Astaire and Ginger Rogers. Admission is free
and coffee will be served in Room 320, Geology Corner.
★ ★ ★
THE CURRENT EVENTS COFFEE HOUR will take up the
Middle East Crisis today at 4:15 in the I-Center. Israeli students
will present their view for discussion. The discussion will be con
tinued next week with Arab students presenting their point of view.
★ ★ ★
THE GOSPEL ACCORDING TO ST. MATTHEW will be shown
on campus Sunday afternoon, sponsored by the University Christian
Movement (UCM). The film, currently playing in San Francisco,
will be presented to a limited audience at 2:30, Dec. 3, in Cubberley.
Tickets may be purchased for one dollar at the third floor of the
Clubhouse.
★ ★ ★
Today UCM hosts a discussion on SOCIAL CHANGE IN THE
PHILIPPINES. Senator Raul Manglapus of the Philippines will
meet with students at 4:15 this afternoon in the large lounge of
Tresidder.
★ ★ ★
FATHER PATRICK R. SHAULES S.J., founder of the Enter
prise, a self-help project in Asia, will speak tonight at 7:15 in Hurl
burt House. His topic will be the political situation in China, Macao
and Hong Kong.
★ ★ ★
DR. PHILIP H. SORENSON, research psychologist at Stanford
Research Institute, will discuss public educational requirements in
the Pacific Trust Territory tonight at 8 p.m. at the Human Factors
Meeting. The meeting will be in the banquet room of the Bold
Knight restaurant in Sunnyvale.
★ ★ ★
Cellist BONNIE HAMPTON will be soloist with Stanford Sym
phony Orchestra for a concert at 3:30 p.m. Sunday, Dec. 3, in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Tickets are available at TMU.

will present their view for discussion. The discussion will be con
tinued next week with Arab students presenting their point of view.
★ ★ ★
THE GOSPEL ACCORDING TO ST. MATTHEW will be shown
on campus Sunday afternoon, sponsored by the University Christian
Movement (UCM). The film, currently playing in San Francisco,
will be presented to a limited audience at 2:30, Dec. 3, in Cubberley.
Tickets may be purchased for one dollar at the third floor of the
Clubhouse.
★ ★ ★
Today UCM hosts a discussion on SOCIAL CHANGE IN THE
PHILIPPINES. Senator Raul Manglapus of the Philippines will
meet with students at 4:15 this afternoon in the large lounge of
Tresidder.
★ ★ ★
FATHER PATRICK R. SHAULES S.J., founder of the Enter
prise, a self-help project in Asia, will speak tonight at 7:15 in Hurl
burt House. His topic will be the political situation in China, Macao
and Hong Kong.
★ ★ ★
DR. PHILIP H. SORENSON, research psychologist at Stanford
Research Institute, will discuss public educational requirements in
the Pacific Trust Territory tonight at 8 p.m. at the Human Factors
Meeting. The meeting will be in the banquet room of the Bold
Knight restaurant in Sunnyvale.
★ ★ ★
Cellist BONNIE HAMPTON will be soloist with Stanford Sym
phony Orchestra for a concert at 3:30 p.m. Sunday, Dec. 3, in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Tickets are available at TMU.
SKI CLUB will present seven Warren Miller films, including films
of Squaw Valley, "Mod Magic on Skis," and "Wild Wonderful World
of Skiing," tonight at 7:30 in Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Admission is
25 cents.

weekend.
