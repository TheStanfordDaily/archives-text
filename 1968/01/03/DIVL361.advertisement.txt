I An Open Invitation To I
I INTERNATIONAL CENTER I
—You'll meet students from over 70 countries (Americans, too)
—You'll find a library full of periodicals from all parts of the world.
—You'll find a relaxing atmosphere, in which to talk, read, watch color-TV, listen to short
wave radio, or challenge a friend to chess, darts, ping pong or cards.
For your special interest, we have
JAZZ CASUALS—Tuesdays, 10 p.m. Come to listen, or to improvise!
INTERNATIONAL FILM FORUM—emphasizing cinema as an art medium. Thursdays, 8:15 p.m.
Winter Quarter will feature the films of Dovzhenko and Jean Renoir. Coffee and discus
sion follow each film. January 4: Zwenigora.
CURRENT EVENTS COFFEE HOUR—probing behind the headlines with students in the know-
Tuesdays, 4 p.m. January 9: Cuban Impact on Latin America with Prof. Fagan.
LUNCHEON DISCUSSION GROUPS—B delicious hot lunches in the company of an international
group. Topics such as Urban Crisis, Political Dissent, TV, Living in a Foreign Culture, Non
verbal Communication, China and her Neighbors, Men & Women, Prospects for World
Government. SIGN UP TODAY at I-Center booth near Pavillion.
SATURDAY EVENINGS ABROAD-every Saturday a new country presents its music, dance and
culture at the l-Center. January 6: A Gypsy Trio plays music from Hungary & Rumania.
ALL-CAMPUS COFFEE HOUR—every Friday, 4-6 p.m.
THEATER SERIES-d inner, a play & discussion with the cast. January 12: Endgame at ACT!
Many of the above programs are organized by the International Club. The l-Club is a group of foreign and American stu
dents working together to promote interests at Stanford in all kinds of international activities. If you are interested in this
area, we would be very pleased to hear from you—inquire at the l-Center.
