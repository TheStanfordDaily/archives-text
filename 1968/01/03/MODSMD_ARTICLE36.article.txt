# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
JACK VALENTI, president of the Motion Picture Association of
America, and Producer-director Stanley Kramer will be on campus
Jan. 4 and 5 for the northern California premiere of Kramer's film
"Guess Who's Coming to Dinner." The movie will be open to mem
bers of the Stanford community only. Admission is free, but tickets
must be picked up in advance at the Tresidder Union Box Office.
The movie stars Spencer Tracy, Katherine Hepburn, and Sidney
Poitier. The film will be shown in Mem Aud.
★ ★ ★
SIX $2500 SCHOLARSHIPS will be awarded by the Josephine de
Karman Scholarship Trust to graduate and senior undergraduate
students of exceptional ability in any discipline, with special con
sideration going to applicants in the humanities. The competition
is also open to foregn students already enrolled in a university in
the United States.
★ ★ ★
The WINTER CARNIVAL will be held in Heavenly Valley Jan.
5, 6, and 7. Tickets will be on sale today at the Pavilion from 8 a.m.
to 5 p.m. and again tomorrow in the ASSU office, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.
★ ★ ★
Qualified juniors interested in strengthening their language abil
ity and in researching a subject of their choice in LATIN AMERICA
arc eligible for an individual field research program offered by the
Committee on Latin American Studies. Outstanding students, inter
ested in planning and carryout out field research in Latin America
may be eligible for the program that begins Spring quarter 1968.
Detailed information is available from the Committee on Latin
America Studies, Bolivar House. Deadline for applications is Feb
ruary 1, 1968.
★ ★ ★
Applications are now being accepted for several positions soon
to be open on the ASSU JUDICIAL COUNCIL. The application
forms may be picked up at the Judicial Council office upstairs in
Tresidder Union and should be turned in before noon next Tuesday.
Interested students should also sign up for interviews when they
pick up application forms. The interviews will be held next week.
