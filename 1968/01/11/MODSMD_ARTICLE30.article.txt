# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
CLASS CARDS ARE DUE tomorrow not later than 5 p.m. at the
information desk of the Registrar's Office in the Old Union. After
study list class cards are turned in, programs may be changed only
by petition. A charge of $2 will be made for late turn-in, increasing
to $10 the following Monday.
★ ★ ★
The Lute class (72 g) taught by Stanley Buetens will also offer
INSTRUCTION IN CLASSICAL GUITAR. The class is open to all
Stanford students regardless of major. It will admit beginners
through advanced students of guitar. The class meets in Harmony
House on Tuesdays at 10 a.m. Class cards may be obtained from
the music department office in the Knoll. One unit of credit is avail
able.
★ ★ ★
Prof. Richard L. Trapp, chairman of the classics department at
S.F. State college will lead a discussion with the audience following
tonight's 8:00 performance of CANDAULES, COMMISSIONER at
the Nitery. The play, a politico-military contemporary satire by
Daniel C. Gerould, is based on Greek legend. Tickets are available
at the Tresidder box office.
★ ★ ★
A PAKISTAN EVENING ABROAD will present Pakistani music,
folk songs, dances, and the movie "Mountain Paradise" Saturday at
8 p.m. in Tresidder large lounge. Refreshments will be served. The
affair is sponsored by the Pakistani association at Stanford.
★ ★ ★
Stanford has awarded four news executives PROFESSIONAL
JOURNALISM FELLOWSHIPS for study in the academic area of
their choice spring quarter. They are: Robert C. King, assistant
managing editor of the Minneapolis Star; Theron C. Liddle, manag
ing editor of the Salt Lake City Desert News; James F. McDaniel,
assistant managing editor of the Memphis Commercial Appeal; and
Douglas L. Turner, city editor for special projects on the Buffalo
Courier-Express.
★ ★ ★
PROFESSOR NIKOLA TODOROV of Sofia, Bulgaria, will speak
on "The Genesis of Capitalism in the Balkan Provinces of the Otto

man Empire in the 19th Century," in Tresidder room 271, tonight
at 7:30.
★ ★ ★
APPLICATIONS FOB THE 1968 PBEJREGISTBATION COM
MITTEE are available in the ASSU office at the Pre-Reg desk. They
should be turned in by early next week. Interviews will be held dur
ing the next two weeks. Sign ups for interviews are to be made at
the Pre-Reg desk.
★ ★ ★
TOURS OF THE STANFORD LINEAR ACCELERATOR will be
given on January 18 and 24 with transportation provided. Buses will
leave Tresidder at 2:15 p.m. and return by 4:30 p.m. Interested stu
dents may sign-up at the second-floor information desk at Tresidder.
★ ★ ★
The AMEBIC AN FEDEBATION OF TEACHERS (Local 1816)
will meet today at 8:00 p.m. in Tresidder room 271, to set up an
executive committee and discuss policy and future activities. The
union is open to graduate students, library assistants, and faculty.

NEW DEPARTMENT HEAD—
Professor George Knoles has
been named executive head of
the history department. He re
places Professor David Potter,
who will take a leave of absence
next fall to use a senior fellow
ship from the National Endow
ment for the Humanities. Knoles
received his Pn.D. from Stan
ford in 1939 and began teaching
here In 1935. Currently the di
rector of the Institute of Ameri
can History, Knoles specializes
in Intellectual history of the
United States.
CLASS CARDS ARE DUE tomorrow not later than 5 p.m. at the
information desk of the Registrar's Office in the Old Union. After
study list class cards are turned in, programs may be changed only
by petition. A charge of $2 will be made for late turn-in, increasing
to $10 the following Monday.
★ ★ ★
The Lute class (72 g) taught by Stanley Buetens will also offer
INSTRUCTION IN CLASSICAL GUITAR. The class is open to all
Stanford students regardless of major. It will admit beginners
through advanced students of guitar. The class meets in Harmony
House on Tuesdays at 10 a.m. Class cards may be obtained from
the music department office in the Knoll. One unit of credit is avail
able.

S.F. State college will lead a discussion with the audience following
tonight's 8:00 performance of CANDAULES, COMMISSIONER at
the Nitery. The play, a politico-military contemporary satire by
Daniel C. Gerould, is based on Greek legend. Tickets are available
at the Tresidder box office.
