# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Seniors and graduate students are urged to file applications for
GRADUATE AWARDS (fellowships/scholarships and assistant
ships) by today, the general University deadline. Application forms
can be obtained in the Financial Aid Office, Room 214, Old Union.
Students already engaged in graduate study should check with
their departments to see if it is necessary to file an application. The
M.D. Program and the Schools of Law and Business also have dif
ferent procedures and deadlines.
★ ★ ★
The IRR-MEXICALI WORK PROJECT will hold its first organi
zational meeting today at 5 p.m. in Room 271 of Tresidder.
The project seeks to introduce students to the Mexican way of
life and the problems of border towns. The work project, which
takes place during Spring Vacation, is open to all members of the
Stanford community.
★ ★ ★
The Political Union will present a conference on the develop
mental problems of Latin America LATIN AMERICA: EVOLU
TION OR REVOLUTION today, Tuesday, and Wednesday at 4:15 in
the large lounge of Tresidder.
Today's program on "Law and Development" will include a speech
by Prof. John Merryman of the Stanford Law School and a panel
discussion with Profs. Robert Packenham of the Political Science
Department, Gerald Meier and Thomas Ehrlich of the Law School.
Dr. William McGreevey from the Center for Latin American
Studies at the University of California at Berkeley will discuss
"Economics and Development" on Tuesday.
On Wednesday Mr. John Gerassi, Latin America editor for
Ramparts and a member of the International Relations Department
at San Francisco State College, will discuss "The U.S. and Devel
opment."
★ ★ ★
THE COIVfM UNITY OF THE LEFT has cancelled its meeting
for tonight until Monday, Jan. 22. The group will continue its
organization of a coordinating committee for the various leftist
members of the campus.
A mailing will announce the time and place of the meeting, ac
cording to Mary Hanson, a steering committee representative.
★ ★ ★
THE STANFORD POLITICAL UNION, a non-partisan student
organization sponsored by the ASSU, is searching for people, ac
cording to President Tim Vidal, to create and organize programs.
Over half of its $5000 annual budget is still unspent or uncomitted.
Work will involve creating and organizing programs, drafting and
typing letters of invitation, or publicizing events.
In addition, a sign-up list will be posted on the Political Union
door of the ASSU office for election of officers for next year.
