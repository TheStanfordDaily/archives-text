# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
JOHN GERASSI, recently fired from his International Relations
post at San Francisco State, will conclude a series today on develop
mental problems of Latin America. Gerassi, who was charged with
being a leader of last month's campus disturbances, will speak as
part of the Latin America: Evolution or Revolution series. Gerassi
is a New York Times correspondent, editor for Ramparts, and the
author of five books on Latin America and Vietnam.
★ ★ ★
JOSHUA CHACO, visiting instructor in rehabilitation at Stanford
from Hebrew University, Jerusalem, will present the second "Prog
ress in Medicine" lecture for Winter Quarter tonight. He will speak
on "Social Policy and Medical Care, Israel" at 8 p.m. in room MlO6
of the Medical School.
★ ★ ★
Applications for future sessions at STANFORD'S OVERSEAS
CAMPUSES will be available on Monday, January 22, in the Over
seas Campuses Office, Room 1-C. This set of applications will in
clude Austria VIII, France XVIII, and Italy XVIII (March - Sep
tember, 1969 and Britain VII and Germany XXII (January - June,
1969). The deadline for submitting applications this quarter is
Thursday, March 7.
★ ★ ★
Tonight the SAN FRANCISCO SYMPHONY is opening its sea
son with the concert version of Gluck's Opera Orfeo. The perfor
mance begins at 8:30 at the opera house and includes the Stanford
Chorus and three soloists — Pilar Lorenger, soprano; Janet Baker,
messosoprano; and Sheila Marks, soprano. Student tickets will be
honored and refunds may be obtained for concerts not performed.
★ ★ ★
The Department of French and Italian is receiving applications
lor Group V of the STANFORD PROGRAM IN NANTES. Junior
and senior French majors are enrolled in the University of Nantes
and live there in the dorms. A Stanford professor will accompany
the group and will give a course in French and a Senior Colloquium
if necessary. Deadline for application is April 1.
★ ★ ★
Wednesday, Jan. 31, is the deadline for filing CANDIDACY AP
PLICATIONS for June A.M., M.S., and Engineer degrees, and for
September Ph.D. degrees. Applications must be signed by major
departments before being submitted to the Graduate Study Office,
Room 128, Old Union.
★ ★ ★
The MATHEMATICS DEPARTMENT will host Discussion and
Coffee Hours for undergraduates. These informal gatherings will
be held the first and third Wednesdays of each month at 4:15 in the
second floor lounge of the Mathematics Building.

mental problems of Latin America. Gerassi, who was charged with
being a leader of last month's campus disturbances, will speak as
part of the Latin America: Evolution or Revolution series. Gerassi
is a New York Times correspondent, editor for Ramparts, and the
author of five books on Latin America and Vietnam.

JOSHUA CHACO, visiting instructor in rehabilitation at Stanford
from Hebrew University, Jerusalem, will present the second "Prog
ress in Medicine" lecture for Winter Quarter tonight. He will speak
on "Social Policy and Medical Care, Israel" at 8 p.m. in room MlO6
of the Medical School.

clude Austria VIII, France XVIII, and Italy XVIII (March - Sep

Tonight the SAN FRANCISCO SYMPHONY is opening its sea
son with the concert version of Gluck's Opera Orfeo. The perfor
mance begins at 8:30 at the opera house and includes the Stanford
Chorus and three soloists — Pilar Lorenger, soprano; Janet Baker,
messosoprano; and Sheila Marks, soprano. Student tickets will be
honored and refunds may be obtained for concerts not performed.

The Department of French and Italian is receiving applications
lor Group V of the STANFORD PROGRAM IN NANTES. Junior
and senior French majors are enrolled in the University of Nantes
and live there in the dorms. A Stanford professor will accompany
the group and will give a course in French and a Senior Colloquium
if necessary. Deadline for application is April 1.

Room 128, Old Union.
