# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Tonight's FRESHMAN FLICK presents "Monkey Business"
(showings at 8:00 and 10:30 and"The Haven" (9:20 and midnight).
"Monkey Business" stars Groucho, Harpo, Chico and Zeppo Marx
and is directed by Norman Z. McLeod with music and lyrics by
Chico Marx and Sol Violinsky. "The Raven," based on Poe's "The
Pit and the Pendulum" and"The Raven," stars Boris Karloff and
Bela Legosi.
★ ★ ★
The I-CENTER JAZZ casual starts it series tonight at 9:30. These
Jazz evenings will occur the latter three Tuesdays of each month.
★ ★ ★
THE COMMITTEE ON AFRICAN STUDIES will hold a semi
nary meeting at Tresidder Union, Room 270 at 8:15 this evening.
Professor Sanford Dornbusch will be the guest speaker. His topic
for the evening is "Tribes and Bureaucracies."
★ ★ ★
This week's CURRENT EVENTS COFFEE HOUR will feature
a discussion on "National Liberation or World Communism, an
issue in Vietnam." Students from the Far East will lead this discus
sion at 4:15 in the I-Center.

7:00 tonight, Dr. Smith, director of the Haight-Ashbury medical
clinic, spoke on "Dilemnas in drug abuse, education and legislation."
★ ★ ★
The campus AMERICAN FIELD SERVICE club is having its first
social meeting of the quarter on January 28, in the Toyon Lounge
from 3:30 to 6:30. The program, called "Journey through Cuisine,"
will feature foods from various foreign countries as well as slides of
the annual AFS bus trip to New York. All those interested in
chaperoning this trip for foreign students please attend this meeting.
