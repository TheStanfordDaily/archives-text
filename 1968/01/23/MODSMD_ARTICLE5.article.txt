# Symphony Saturday
## 
### 
The end of the San Franci
sco Symphony strike has
marked the re-scheduling of a
concert by the orchestra at
Stanford to this Saturday, ac
cording to the Office of Public
Events.
Conductor Joseph Krips will
conduct an all-Beethoven pro
gram beginning at 8:30 p.m. In
Memorial Auditorium.
Tickets are all unreserved
and are on sale at the Tresld
er Union box office. Students
are offered a discount.
Krip's program will open
with the Leonore Overture No.
8, and follow with the Third
and Fourth symphonies.
Other cultural events spon
sored by Public Events during
the season will be the Julliard
Quartet at 8:30 p.m.., Feb 14
in Dinkelspiel Auditorium; the
Nomura Kyogen, Japanese
classical drama company, at
8 p.m., Feb. 19 in Memorial
Auditorium; and Actor Emlyn
Williams in a monologue, "Dy
lan Thomas Grows Up," at 8
Auditorium.
