The Stanford India Association presents AN EVENING OF IN
DIAN MUSIC AND DANCE tonight at 8 in Tresidder's largo lounge
to celebrate India's 18th Republic Day anniversary. Refreshments
and Indian documentaries follow at the Bechtel International Cen
ter.
★ ★ ★
The Election Commission will hold a BItIEF MEETING FOR ALL
VICE PRESIDENTIAL CANDIDATES Monday at 7 p.m. in Room
236, Tresidder. All candidates are required to attend the meeting.
No campaigning may begin until the meeting has taken place.
★ ★ ★
MIKE JAMES AND JUNEBUG BOYKIN FROM "JOIN" and the
National Community Union will speak today at 4:15 in Tresidder,
Room 274. The discussion will be on the formation of a national
network of radicals and the role of students in the movement; and
the political potentialities of the white working-class.
★ ★ ★
PUBLIC WARNING EQUIPMENT WILL BE TESTED in Santa
Clara and San Mateo Counties today at 11 a.m. The test pattern will
be a steady siren tone for one minute, a minute of silence, and a
wavering tone or series of short blasts for one minute.
★ ★ ★
The Keio Committee will present the Japanese movie "FIRES ON
THE PLAIN" tonight at 7:30 and 10 in Cubberley Auditorium. The
film was directed by Kon Ichikawa and won first prize at the Lo
carno Film Festival in 1961.
It is the story of a Japanese straggler on Leyte after World War
II and his desperate attempts to survive.

for June A.M., M.S., Engineer degrees, and for September Ph.D.

partments and submitted to the Graduate Study Office in Room
128, Old Union Building.

THE PLAIN" tonight at 7:30 and 10 in Cubberley Auditorium. The
