# Campus Roundup

### 
THE THEATER OF THE CONGRESSIONAL RECORD will
present a reenactment of the impeachment proceedings against
President Andrew Johnson on March 1. An all male cast of
fifty is needed. Tryouts will be held tonight and tomorrow at
7:30 p.m. in TMU small lounge. The public is invited to attend.

PROFESSOR PAUL R. EHRLICH, director of Graduate Studies
in the Department of Biological Sciences, will discuss the world
population crisis at a luncheon in the Colonial Room of the St.
Francis Hotel today at 12:30 p.m. His talk is entitled "Birth Con
trol: Too Little and Much Too Late." Reservations at $4.50 per
person, including tax and tip, may be obtained from Planned
Parenthood, 2340 Clay Street, San Francisco, 922-1720.

THE STANFORD ANTI-DRAFT UNION will meet tonight, not
last night as reported in yesterday's Daily. The meeting will be
held at 8:15 in Tresidder 267-8. New items for discussion will in
clude the identification and confrontation of draft board members
in the community, another attempt in April to shut down the
Oakland Induction Center, and a dorm speaking program.
