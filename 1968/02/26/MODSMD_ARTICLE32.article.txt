# Darley Leads Cal Net Win, Pasarell Sole Card Victor
## 
### FRED LEESON 
Led by freckled ace Chuck
Darley, the University of Cali
fornia belted its way to victories
in three of four divisions in the
finals Saturday to win the Nor
thern California Intercollegiate
tennis tournament on Stanford's
varsity courts.
The smooth, cool Darley eas
ily won the varsity singles title
and then teamed with Corky
Meinhardt to nab the doubles
crown as the Bears toppled de
fending champion Stanford 26-
22.
San Jose State was third with
17 points and Fresno State fol
lowed with eight.
While two Cal teams battled
for the freshman-junior col
lege doubles championship,
Stanford's top-seedeid Stan
Pasarell, never losing a set,
won the Indians' only title in
the freshman-junior college
singles.
Facing Cal's second-seeded
freshman Bob Alloo, Pasarell
Jumped out in front 3-1 in the
first set, faltered at 3-3, and fin
ished strongly, winning 6-4 and
6-2.
In the varsity singles, Dar
ley won an unprecedented
third title in the tourney's 22
year history by whipping
teammate Mike Gilfillen 6-3,
6-1.
Only San Jose State's John
Zweig, in the third round, was
able to force Darley into three
sets.
Stanford's second-seeded dou
bles team of Jamie Carroll and

Chuck Alloo survived a tough
7-5, 3-6, 7-5 third round battle to
meet Darley and Meinhardt in
the final.
The Bear duo claimed the first
set 6-4 and raced to a 5-0 lead
in the second, as Carrol and
Alloo suffered some serving
woes, before winning the title
6-1.
Stanford's Dean Schlobohm
and Brad Cornell gave the Dar
ley-Meinhardt team its hardest
match, losing 6-4, 2-6, 4-6 in the
third round.
In varsity singles, third
seeded John Spiegel lost to
Darley 2-6, 4-6 In the semi
finals and Schlobohm, seeded
fourth, fell to Gilfillen 4-6, 6-0,
4-6 in the quarter finals.
Bob Alloo and Larry Parker
won the freshman-junior college
doubles for Cal 6-2, 4-6, 6-2. Stan
ford's freshman double unit of
Pasarell and Paul Marienthal,
seeded second, was eliminated
in the semifinals 6-4, 4-6, 3-6.

Marienthal, seeded third in
singles, finished behind Alloo
and Parasell, who were picked
second and first.

6-3.

7-5, 6-3; Darley d. Spiegel 6-2, 6-4.
FlNAL—Darley d. Gilfillen 6-3, 6-1.

(UC) d. Schlobohm-Cornell (SU) 4-6,
6-3, 6-4; Alloo-Carrol (SU) d. Wald
man-Gilnllen (UC) 7-5, 3-6, 7-5.
FlNAL—Darley-Meinhardt d. Alloo-Car
rol 6-4, 6-1.

Led by freckled ace Chuck
Darley, the University of Cali
fornia belted its way to victories
in three of four divisions in the
finals Saturday to win the Nor
thern California Intercollegiate
tennis tournament on Stanford's
varsity courts.
The smooth, cool Darley eas
ily won the varsity singles title
and then teamed with Corky
Meinhardt to nab the doubles
crown as the Bears toppled de
fending champion Stanford 26-
22.
San Jose State was third with
17 points and Fresno State fol
lowed with eight.
While two Cal teams battled
for the freshman-junior col
lege doubles championship,
Stanford's top-seedeid Stan
Pasarell, never losing a set,
won the Indians' only title in
the freshman-junior college
singles.
Facing Cal's second-seeded
freshman Bob Alloo, Pasarell
Jumped out in front 3-1 in the
first set, faltered at 3-3, and fin
ished strongly, winning 6-4 and
6-2.
In the varsity singles, Dar
ley won an unprecedented
third title in the tourney's 22
year history by whipping
teammate Mike Gilfillen 6-3,
6-1.
Only San Jose State's John
Zweig, in the third round, was
able to force Darley into three
sets.
Stanford's second-seeded dou
bles team of Jamie Carroll and

Chuck Alloo survived a tough
7-5, 3-6, 7-5 third round battle to
meet Darley and Meinhardt in
the final.
The Bear duo claimed the first
set 6-4 and raced to a 5-0 lead
in the second, as Carrol and
Alloo suffered some serving
woes, before winning the title
6-1.
Stanford's Dean Schlobohm
and Brad Cornell gave the Dar
ley-Meinhardt team its hardest
match, losing 6-4, 2-6, 4-6 in the
third round.
In varsity singles, third
seeded John Spiegel lost to
Darley 2-6, 4-6 In the semi
finals and Schlobohm, seeded
fourth, fell to Gilfillen 4-6, 6-0,
4-6 in the quarter finals.
Bob Alloo and Larry Parker
won the freshman-junior college
doubles for Cal 6-2, 4-6, 6-2. Stan
ford's freshman double unit of
Pasarell and Paul Marienthal,
seeded second, was eliminated
in the semifinals 6-4, 4-6, 3-6.

Marienthal, seeded third in
singles, finished behind Alloo
and Parasell, who were picked
second and first.
