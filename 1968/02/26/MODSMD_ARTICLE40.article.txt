# Campus Roundup

Two free shows of Felix Greene's Inside North Vietnam plus
David Schoebrun on Vietnam will conclude the RINCONADA FILM
SERIES tonight, 7:30 and 9:30, in the Rinconada dining room, Wil
bur Hall.
★ ★ ★
What's happening In the PEACE & FREEDOM PARTY? For
those who are interested in or have registered in the party, there
will be speakers and discussion on the issues and candidates in the
party at 4:15 in Tresidder 270. Reese Ehrlich, a Stop the Draft Week
organizer suspended from UC Berkeley last quarter, is being sent
by the statewide office of the party to speak on the issues. Martin
Primack, one of the chief organizers in Santa Clara County, will
have information on candidates.
★ ★ ★
The movie TRIUMPH OF THE WILL will be shown tonight in
Cubberley Auditorium at 8:00. The screening is sponsored by Prof.
Gordon Craig's History 129 class. There will be no admission
charge, and it is open to the University community.
★ ★ ★
A collection of watercolors by RAUL PENNA FIRME is on dis
play this month at the Bechtel International Center. Penna Firme,
67, is professor of city planning at the Federal University of Rio
de Janeiro. He visited Stanford recently. The watercolors show
settings in Brazil and California, and are on sale for $10 and $25.

★ ★ ★
EDGAR S. BROOKS, distinguished South African senator and
critic of the apartheid policy, will discuss "Pan-Africanism, Nation
alism, and Liberalism" Monday at 4:15 in the small lounge of Tre
sidder Union. Brooks was one of the founders and, subsequently, a
president of the South African Institute of Race Relations. He
has also served as a senator of the South African states of Natal
and Zululand. The speech is sponsored by the Hoover Institution
and the Center for Research in International Studies.
