# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Tryouts for the Ram's Head Spring Show,
CARNIVAL, will be held this evening in rooms
129 and 205 of Memorial Auditorium at 7:30.
Actors, dancers, singers, and musicians are need
ed. The show will have six performances: May 16,
17, 18, 23, 24, and 25. Crew hands are also needed.
★ ★ ★
VASUDEVA'S RESTAURANT, in El Campo
Eating Club, will be serving crepes, omelettes, and
hamburgers Saturday from 9 p.m. to 2 a.m. Live
entertainment will be featured at the restaurant,
open to the Stanford community.
★ ★ ★
In observance of the Lenten Season the Me
morial Church Choir soloists and orchestra will
present BEETHOVEN'S MASS IN C MAJOR
at the 11:00 Morning Worship Service Sunday.
Soloists are Joyce Firstenburger, soprano ;Mar
jorie Tede, contralto; Nile Norton, tenor; and
George Gucker, bass. Professor, Harold Schmidt
will direct the choir. Dean of the Chapel, B. Davie
Napier, will speak on the Credo. The public is
invited.
★ ★ ★
Opportunities for officer commissions in the
U.S. Marine Corps will be outlined next week by
a MARINE OFFICER SELECTION TEAM on
campus. Both aviation and line officer programs
will be explained by Major G. M. Larson. The

team will hold interviews Wednesday, March 6,
in the Placement Service and Thursday, March 7,
in the business school from 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.
★ ★ ★
CHASTITY IS ITS OWN PUNISHMENT, an
original melodrama production by the members
of Soto's Creativity House, will begin a two
week performance at 8:30 tonight and tomorrow
night. The melodrama will be performed in the
Soto dining room, in the Wilbur Hall cafeteria.
Admission is 25c at the door.
★ ★ ★
DIS-JOINT, the Stanford coffee house, and
Grove House are co-sponsoring a dance tomorrow
from 9 p.m. to 1 a.m. in the Grove Lounge (at
Stern Hall). A live band, The Salt, will perform.
Boer, hot dogs, marshmallows and hot chocolate
will be served. The dance is open to all Stanford
students and friends. Admission is 50c.
★ ★ ★
Medieval and Renaissance music from England
and Germany will be featured in a concert by
the EARLY MUSIC QUARTET tomorrow night
at 8:30 in Dinkelspiel Auditorium. The quartet
includes two vocalists, mezo-soprano Andrea Von
Ramm and tenor Willard Cobb, and two instru
mentalists, Sterling Jones and Thomas Binkley,
who play bowed and plucked string instruments
and winds. Tickets are available at Tresidder box
office.
