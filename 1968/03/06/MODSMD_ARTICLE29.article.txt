# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Hugh B. Hester, a retired brigadier general, will speak on the
question, CAN WE WIN IN VIETNAM? today at 4 p.m. in TMU
270-71. Hester served for 34 years in the U.S. Army. He is, with
General James Gavin, one of the "Big Brass Lambs" featured in
the December issue of Esquire magazine. The program is spon
sored by the Faculty and Staff Committee for Peace in Vietnam.
★ ★ ★
The DEPARTMENT OF ANATOMY will sponsor a seminar en
titled "Fine Structure of Steroicigenic Cells of the Adrenal Gland,"
at 12:15 p.m. in the Anatomy Lecture Hall. The seminar will fea
ture John A. Long, Department of Anatomy, UC School of Medicine,
San Francisco.
★ ★ ★
C. W. McLean, regional economist of the FOOD AND AGRICUL
TURE ORGANIZATION OF THE UN will be on campus Friday,
March 8, and will be available to discuss SAO activities, including
employment and recruiting. He is interested in talking to graduates
in business administration, economics, and food research, and Pace
Corps returnees, and anyone else who is interested. He will be at
the placement office of the Graduate School of Business from 9 to
12 a.m.
★ ★ ★
Singers interested in performing in the opera chorus for Verde's
FALSTAFF, May 24-25 and June 1, should sign up outside room 3,
Dinkelspiel rehearsal area.
★ ★ ★
Cesare Massarenti, ASSU President, will speak in Junipero
Lounge on WHY STUDENTS ARE PEOPLE tonight following din
ner.
★ ★ ★

EAST OAKLAND SEMINAR PROJECT. Sponsored by the UCM,
this project offers opportunity for ten students to explore the Oak
land socio-political scene, especially through involvement in the
campaign of John George, young black Oakland attorney, who is
running in the Democratic primary for U.S. Representative. Call
Barbara Troxell, YWCA Director, for more information, or pick
up application in the Clubhouse, 3rd floor.

General James Gavin, one of the "Big Brass Lambs" featured in
the December issue of Esquire magazine. The program is spon
sored by the Faculty and Staff Committee for Peace in Vietnam.

EAST OAKLAND SEMINAR PROJECT. Sponsored by the UCM,
this project offers opportunity for ten students to explore the Oak
land socio-political scene, especially through involvement in the

Barbara Troxell, YWCA Director, for more information, or pick
up application in the Clubhouse, 3rd floor.
