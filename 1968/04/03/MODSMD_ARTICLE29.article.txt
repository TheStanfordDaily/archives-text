# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The McCarthy FOR PRESIDENT forces in
Palo Alto-Stanford are making arrangements for
a cavalcade to meet Senator Eugene McCarthy
on his arrival in S-in Francisco Thursday. The
cavalcade will be assembling in front of the
Quad on the Stanford campus directly in front
of the Law School library from 10:00 a.m. Sen
ator McCarthy's plane arrives at 11:35 a.m. at
San Francisco International Airport, United Air
lines, where a press conference will be held.
★ ★ ★
Students may sign up as a DELEGATE TO
THE CONSTITUTIONAL CONVENTION for
the Associated Students in their living units or
in the ASSU Loft in Tresidder. Subjects to be
considered are the role of the student in univer
sity government, the student judicial system, stu
dent finances, the graduate schools' relationship
to student government, and relations of the stu
dent government to affairs outside the univer
sity.
★ ★ ★
THE GUN FIGHTER, a western featuring Gre
gory Peck, will be shown tonight at 9:30 in room
320. The film is the first in the quarter's Fresh
man Film Series.
★ ★ ★
The LAW STUDENTS CIVIL RIGHTS RE
SEARCH COUNCIL is holding draft counseling
hours in room 33 in the basement of Crothers
Hall from 1-5 p.m. and 7-9 p.m. Monday through
Friday. They can be contacted at university ex
tension 3131.
★ ★ ★
The STANFORD AREA TUTORIAL needs
Stanford students to tutor Ravenswood High
School students in mathematics, sciences, En
glish, social studies, foreign languages, and some
high school business courses. Applications may
be picked up at the NSA office in the loft of Tre
sidder, Further information may be obtained by
calling Anthony Rogers at university extension
4824.
★ ★ ★
The ASSU is seeking APPLICANTS FOR THE
OFFICE OF SOCIAL SECRETARY. This posi
tion will entail coordinating and scheduling all
student-initiated events, the direction of ASSU

sponsored social and cultural activities, and prep
aration of the new ASSU calendar.
The office will carry a salary. Applicants must
be at least sophomores.
Application forms may be picked up in the
ASSU loft in Tresidder. Interviews will be held
and a selection made early next week. More in
formation may be obtained from Denis Hayes
in the ASSU loft (ext. 4331) daily between 1011
and 4-5:30.
★ ★ ★
The CLASSICAL GUITAR SOCIETY will hold
its first class instruction session at 7:30 p.m.
Wednesday April 3 in Harmony House. In addi
tion to the regular classes in Classical Guitar, a
class in beginning folk guitar will also be held
this quarter.
★ ★ ★
LAKE LAGUNITA is now open weekdays from
10:00 to 5:00 and weekends 9:00 to 6:00.
★ ★ ★
The STANFORD CHAPTER OF STUDENTS
FOR A DEMOCRATIC SOCIETY will hold its
first meeting of the Spring Quarter at 8:15 to
night in Tresidder 271. The "Ten Days in April,"
and the .spring educational program will be dis
cussed.

The INTERNATIONAL CENTER will spon
sor a series of informal discussion groups to con
front common problems of foreign students at
Stanford. Groups will be led by a member of
the Department of Psychiatry and will meet

p.m. To apply, call Mrs. Barbara Torrey, ext.
4181.
★ ★ ★
The Company and Freshman Drama are hold
ing TRYOUTS FOR "THE VISIT," a play by
Friedrich Ruerrenmatt, in Toyon lounge on Wed
nesday and Thursday evenings at 7:30. Scripts
can be checked out from the director in Toyon
Hall, room 238. The Visit will be presented for
the university public the last part of May.
★ ★ ★

topic of tonight's PROGRESS IN MEDICINE
lecture at Stanford Medical School. Speaker for
the lecture will be Dr. G. Robert Mason, assis
tant professor of surgery. He will speak at 8
p.m. in Room m-106 of the medical school.
