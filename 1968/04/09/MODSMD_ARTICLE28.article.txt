# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The Stanford Nixon Committee, in conjunction with the CHOICE
'6H campus poll, is presenting a halt hour film demonstrating the
qualifications of Richard Nixon for President. Literature will bo
available for all those interested in the views of the former Vice-
President abuut current crisis and problems. Two showings will
be at 7 and 7:30 tonight in room 247-248 of Tresidder Memorial
Union. All members of the Stanford community are welcome.
★ ★ ★
The first luncheon seminar for Spring quarter of the LATIN
AMERICAN STUDIES ASSOCIATION, scheduled for today, has
been cancelled. The featured speaker, Professor John Wirth, will
present his talk in the month of May. The next meeting of LASA
will be held Tuesday, April 16.
★ ★ ★
The ASSU office will be closed today from in to 12 a.m.
★ ★ ★
The STANFORD DISTAFF CLUB will not meet today as sche
duled. The noon meeting was cancelled in memory of Dr. King.
★ ★ ★
There will be a meeting of the ANTI-DRAFT UNION tonight at
8 p.m. in Tresidder Memorial Union. The meeting is for all those
who have signed the "We Won't Go" statement and for anyone
else who is interested. The goal is to get ten per cent of Stan
ford men to sign the statement.
★ ★ ★
Those wishing to express their sympathy for the death of Martin
Luther King, Jr. may do so in the form of a monetary contribu
tion at the Memorial Church vestry throughout the week. Checks
should be made payable to the SOUTHERN CHRISTIAN LEAD
ERSIILP FOUNDATION.
★ ★ ★
Out of respect for Rev. Martin Luther King's memory, the
FRESHMAN FILM SERIES has postponed showing The Philadel
phia Story until Thursday, April 11, at 8 and 10 p.m. in Geology
room 320.

THE PARAGONE: RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN SCULPTURE
AND PAINTING IN THE 14TII AND 15TH CENTURY ITALY,
will be the subject of Professor White's lecture on Wed., April 10.
This is the first lecture of the Wed. night Art Lecture Series and
will be held in the Physics Lecture Hall, at 8 p.m.
The Stanford Nixon Committee, in conjunction with the CHOICE
'6H campus poll, is presenting a halt hour film demonstrating the
qualifications of Richard Nixon for President. Literature will bo
available for all those interested in the views of the former Vice-
President abuut current crisis and problems. Two showings will
be at 7 and 7:30 tonight in room 247-248 of Tresidder Memorial
Union. All members of the Stanford community are welcome.

will be held Tuesday, April 16.
