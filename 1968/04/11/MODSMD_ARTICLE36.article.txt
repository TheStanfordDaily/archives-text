# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Checks for the MARTIN LUTHER KING MEMORIAL SCHO
LARSHIP should be made out to Stanford University, with a no
tation that they are to be used for the Fund. Checks may be turned
in at Robert Hind's office in the Law School Annex. Over $1,000
has been contributed so far.
★ ★ ★
The FRESHMAN FILM SERIES will present "The Philadelphia
Story" at 8:00 and 10:00 tonight in Room 320 of the Geology corner
of the Quad. Cary Grant, Katharine Hepburn and James Stuart
star in the 1940 comedy.
★ ★ ★
The SDS in cooperation with the Flick is presenting a LET'S
THINK ABOUT AGGRESSION NIGHT. Three films, Night & Fog,
Little Island and Vivre will be shown at 6:30, 8:00 and 9:00. Admis
sion is $1.00 for the benefit of the SDS.
★ ★ ★
A FESTIVAL OF CONTEMPORARY DUTCH FILM will be held
in Memorial Auditorium tonight. "Holland Terra Fertilia" will be
shown at 7:00, "The Whipping Cream Hero" at 7:25, "The Voice
of the Water" at 9:30 and"The Raid" at 11:30. Admission is free.
★ ★ ★
The International Films Forum will present Satyajit Ray's KAN
CHENJUNGA at 8:00 tonight in Room 274-5 of Tresidder. There
will be a discussion at the International Center led by Mr. Subir
K. Samyal immediately after the film.
★ ★ ★
The Organization of African Students will present DR. ALBERT
IMOHIOSEN of Ibadan University at noon today in the cafeteria
at Tresidder. His topic will be "Problems of National Integration
in Africa." Dr. Imohiosen is a visiting scholar at Stanford's Insti
tute of Political Studies.
★ ★ ★
BID CARDS may be signed in the office of the Dean of Students
beginning April 12 for fraternities and eating clubs.
★ ★ ★
There will be a noon rally at White Plaza explaining the reasons
for the upcoming (April 23) OAKLAND DEMONSTRATIONS. The
focus is dual: Free Huey and Stop the Draft. The rally will also
explore means of taking action against racism in America.
★ ★ ★
G. FREDERICK REINHARDT, who recently completed seven
years of service as U.S. Ambassador to Italy, will speak at 12:15
p.m. today in Bishop Auditorium of the Business School. His talk
is sponsored by the School's Symposium Committee. Reinhardt
is a former U.S. ambassador to Vietnam (1955-56) and to the
United Arab Republic (1960-61).
★ ★ ★
The Haight-Ashbury Modical Clinic is sponsoring an EASTER
BENEFIT ROCK DANCE CONCERT Sunday from 6:00 p.m. to
12:00 p.m. at the Palace of Fine Arts in San Francisco. Big Brother
and the Holding Co., the Quicksilver Messenger Service, the Intial
Shock and the Garden of Delights will be featured. Tickets will be
available at the door starting at 5:00 p.m. Sunday.
★ ★ ★
TITTICUT FOLLIES, a film documentary on psychiatric hospitals
will be shown Saturday night in Memorial Auditorium. Sponsored
by the Tresidder Union Program Division, the film will be screen
ed at 7:30 and 9 p.m. The film has recently been released after the
state of Massachusetts obtained an injunction against its showing.
