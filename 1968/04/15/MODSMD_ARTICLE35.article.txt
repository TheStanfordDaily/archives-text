# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
PETITION'S FOR ASST PRESIDENT AND VICE-PRESIDENT
may be picked up in the ASSU office after noon today. Petitions for
senior class and VVRA presidents and vice-presidents can also b
obtained today. The petitions are due April 25 at 5 p.m. The Spring
primary is slated for May 7th and Bth with run-offs May 14-15.
★ ★ ★
PROF. HOMER A. THOMPSON, classical archaeologist at the
Institute for Advanced Study, Princeton, will deliver the annual
Leon Sloss Memorial Lecture tonight at 8 p.m. in Room 100, Phy
sics Lecture Hall. His subject will be "The Agora: Civic Center of
Ancient Athens." Admission free and open to the public.
★ ★ ★
The newly-formed STANFORD CHAMBER PLAYERS, made up
of members of the Stanford music department and other area
players, will present a concert at 8 p.m. this evening in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium. Works of Mozart, Berg and Brahms will be presented.
Tickets are available at the Tresidder Box office.
★ ★ ★
THE AC ADEMY AWARD-WINNING DOCUMENTARY, THE
REDWOODS, will be screened this evening in room 320, geology
corner at 8. The program, sponsored by the Stanford Conserva
tion Group, coincides with the House Interior Committee's field
hearings on the proposed Redwoods National Park which begins
this week.
★ ★ ★
THE LATIN AMERICAN FILM SERIES starts this evening in
Tresidder at 8. The first film, El, is directed by Luis Bunuel and
is a work of withering social criticism.
★ ★ ★
THE FRENCH FILM SERIES continues this evening with "The
Idiot" featuring Gerard Philipe in the sereenpay of the Dostoevsky
novel. The film will be screened in the business school auditorium
at 7:30 and 9:30.
★ ★ ★
APPLICATIONS FOR THE OVERSEAS CAMPUS BOARD can
be obtained in the Overseas Campus office on the Quad. The Board
is made up interested undergraduates who are alumni of one of
the five campuses. Applications are due Friday.
★ ★ ★
KZSU RADIO will broadcast a fifteen minute roundup of the
California Primary Election every Monday evening at 11 p.m., be
ginning tonight. This will cover all the candidates and equal time
will be offered to any organization who feels their candidate was
slighted.

REDWOODS, will be screened this evening in room 320, geology

hearings on the proposed Redwoods National Park which begins
this week.

THE LATIN AMERICAN FILM SERIES starts this evening in
Tresidder at 8. The first film, El, is directed by Luis Bunuel and
is a work of withering social criticism.

THE FRENCH FILM SERIES continues this evening with "The
Idiot" featuring Gerard Philipe in the sereenpay of the Dostoevsky
novel. The film will be screened in the business school auditorium
at 7:30 and 9:30.

APPLICATIONS FOR THE OVERSEAS CAMPUS BOARD can
be obtained in the Overseas Campus office on the Quad. The Board
is made up interested undergraduates who are alumni of one of
the five campuses. Applications are due Friday.

KZSU RADIO will broadcast a fifteen minute roundup of the
California Primary Election every Monday evening at 11 p.m., be
ginning tonight. This will cover all the candidates and equal time
will be offered to any organization who feels their candidate was
slighted.
PETITION'S FOR ASST PRESIDENT AND VICE-PRESIDENT
may be picked up in the ASSU office after noon today. Petitions for
senior class and VVRA presidents and vice-presidents can also b
obtained today. The petitions are due April 25 at 5 p.m. The Spring
primary is slated for May 7th and Bth with run-offs May 14-15.

PROF. HOMER A. THOMPSON, classical archaeologist at the
Institute for Advanced Study, Princeton, will deliver the annual
Leon Sloss Memorial Lecture tonight at 8 p.m. in Room 100, Phy
sics Lecture Hall. His subject will be "The Agora: Civic Center of
Ancient Athens." Admission free and open to the public.
