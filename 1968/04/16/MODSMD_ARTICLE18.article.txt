# Campus Roundup

The STANFORD ANTI-DRAFT UNION will provide general
draft counseling on Mondays from 11 a.m. to 2 p.m.; on Tuesdays
from 1 p.m. to 4 p.m.; on Wednesdays from 11 a.m. to 4 p.m and
on Thursday and Fridays from 12 noon to 2 pm. For draft counsel
ing dealing with specifically legal questions, law students will
counsel weekdays in room 33 in the basement of Crothers Hall from
1 p.m. to 5 p.m. and from 7 p.m. to 9 p.m.

THE MOTHERS' CLUB OF STANFORD UNIVERSITY is pre
senting Matthew S. Kahn, Professor of Art, who will lecture in

The Freshman Film Series will present two films, THE BARBER
SHOP, starring W. C. Fields, and SHADOW OF A DOUBT, di
rected by Alfred Hitchcock. The Fields flick will be shown at 8:00
and 10:30, and The Hitchcock production will follow at 8:21 and
10:51 this evening. Admission is free, and both films will be shown
in Geology Corner, room 320.

Representatives of VISTA (Volunteers In Service To America)
will be on White Plaza April 15-20. They are seeking qualified can
didates for assignments to projects which have asked for help
throughout the country. Headquarters for the six day drive will
be at the Volunteers' Service Center in the Club House.
