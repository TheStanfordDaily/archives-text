# Guest Blues Group To Play In Dorms
## 
### 
The Cecil Taylor Sextot, a ma
jor representative and contribu
tor to Afro-American culture,
will be guests-in-residence at
Stanford for 17 days.
The group, sponsored by the
ASSU Newly Organized Instru
ment of Student Expression, will
give their first performance this
afternoon at 4:15 p.m. in the
Trosidder large lounge. During
their stay, the group will pre
sent one major concert on April
27 at 8 p.m. in Dinkelspiel Audi
torium.
In addition, they will give
thirteen small programs at var
ious living groups. The purpose
of these small concerts is to eli
minate the usual distance be
tween performers and their pub
lic. In these informal sessions,
students will have an opportu
nity for close contact with the
musicians.
Five of these sessions will be
open to the Stanford commu
nity at large: April 29, 7 p.m. at
Beta Chi; April 30, 7 p.m. at
Roble; May 1, 7 p.m. at Florence
Moore; May 2, 7 p.m. at Lambda
Nu; and May 3, 7 p.m. at Phi
Kappa Sigma.
Taylor is widely known for
his ability to combine classical
and modern Western composi
tion into his own distinctive
blues style. He also improvises
extensively and seeks maximum

audience involvement in his mu
sic.
Taylor has lectured at Ben
nington, Brandeis and New York
Universities, at the University
of Pittsburgh, and in Copenhag
en. He has performed at the
Five Spot Club, Philharmonic
Hall, Carnegie Hall, and Town
Hall in New York. In addition he
has given television lecture per
formances for NBC and NET.
