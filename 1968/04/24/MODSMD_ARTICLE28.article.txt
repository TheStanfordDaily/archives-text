# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The campus MrCARTHY for-President organization announces
two of its supporters, Peter Isard and Alan Abrams, will be on
White Plaza today from 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. to answer points raised in
a recent KENNEDY-for-President advertisement in The Daily.
★ ★ ★
Prof. Robert A. Pahl of Vale will speak on the MeCARTHY CAN
DIDACY today at 2 p.m. in TMU room 270. Dahl, who considers
McCarthy's candidacy crucial to the future of American policy, will
discuss the policy implications of the 1908 elections. Questions will
be entertained.
★ ★ ★
The Netherlands Information Agency is presenting the last half
of A FESTIVAL OF CONTEMPORARY DUTCH FILM at Memor
ial Auditorium tonight in a late showing at 11 p.m. The showing
includes "A Sunday on the Island of the Grande Jatte," "The Not-
So-Fortunate Return of Joszef Katus to the Land of Rembrandt"
and"The Gangster Girl." Admission free.
★ ★ ★
Tonight at 7:30 p.m. THE BLACK POWER LECTURE SERIES
features Stanley Paryear, president of the Palo Alto NAACP and
Charles Harlins, deputy director of the San Francisco branch of
the Urban League, speaking on "The Changing Pace of Traditional
Leadership." TMU Large Lounge.
★ ★ ★
The films HIGH NOON and THE WORLD OF APU will be screen
ed and coffee served at the Beta Chi house (664 San Juan, Stanford)
at 7:30 tonight. No admission charge, all members of the Stanford
community welcome.
★ ★ ★
Bill Shurtleff, a founder and director of the Esalen program at
Stanford will lead AN INTRODUCTION TO THE I CHING tonight
at 8 p.m. at TMU. Participants will learn and use the ancient tech
nique for consulting the oracle book as well as the history behint
it. If possible bring Bollingen edition and three Chinese coins.
★ ★ ★
The WEDNESDAY LECTURES IN ART series features Prof.
David Irwin of Glasgow University tonight at 8 p.m. in the Physics
Lecture Hall. His subject will be "Gothic Neoclassicism — a Con
tradiction?"
★ ★ ★
SPACE MEDICINE — A REVIEW OF AMERICAN AND RUS
SIAN MEDICAL OBSERVATIONS will be this week's Progress in
Medicine lecture. Dr. William M. Helvey, of Lockheed Missies &
Space Co., will speak at 8 tonight in room M-100 of the Medical
School.
★ ★ ★
LEWIS W. SPITZ, professor of history at Stanford, will deliver
a lecture today entitled "The Power of the Word" in the Albert
M. Bender Room at 3:30 p.m. The lecture will open an exhibition,
"The Sixteenth Century, New Acquisitions," planned and arranged
by Dr. Susan V. Lenkey. The exhibition will illustrate various as
pects of 16th century civilization.
