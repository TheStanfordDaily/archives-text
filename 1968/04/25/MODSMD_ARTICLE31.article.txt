# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Marvin Adelson of the Systems Development Corporation in Los
Angeles will give a CENTURY 21 SPEECH tonight titled "The
Forecasting of Technology and the Technology of Forecasting." It
will begin at 7:30 in Tresidder Union.
★ ★ ★
The International Film Forum will present BIZARRE BIZARRE,
directed by Marcel Carne, tonight at 8 in Tresidder 274-5. Coffee will
be served in the I-Center after the movie.
★ ★ ★
Professor James Billington of Princeton will give a lecture on
INTELLECTUAL FERMENT IN THE U.S.S.R. this afternoon in
Tresidder lounge at 4:15. Billington is a professor of history at
Princeton and as associate of the Woodrow Wilson School of Pub
lic and International Affairs.
★ ★ ★
The Sunday morning UNIVERSITY PUBLIC WORSHIP SER
VICE will present the university chorus under the direction of Pro
fessor Schmidt in two sections from the "Paukenmesse" by Franz
Joseph Haydn and two movements from the motet "Warum ist
das Licht gegeben dem Muhseligen?" by Johannes Brahms.
★ ★ ★
Joseph Katz, associate director of the Institute for the Study
of Human Problems, will lead the discussion and play analysis fol
lowing tonight's performance of THE INSPECTOR GENERAL,
Nikolai Gogol's political satire. Curtain time is 8 p.m. in the Little
Theater. Tickets are available in Tresidder Box Office.
★ ★ ★
The LASSU BY-LAWS COMMITTEE will hold an open hearing
tonight at 7:30 in Tresidder room 263 on the proposed changes in
the organization of the Stanford Daily and the ASSU Travel Ser
vice.
★ ★ ★
The Stanford Dressage Society will present WILLIAM HART'S
TUMBLEWEEDS and "Corral", as award-winning western with
musical accompaniment by Pete Seeger. The films will be shown
at 6:30, 8:15 and 9:30. Student admission is $1.
* ★ ★
TRIBE AND TRADITIONALISM IN MODERN AFRICA will
be the subject of a lunch discussion sponsored by the Organization
of African Students. The speaker will be Prof. W. H. Friedland,
chairman of the African Studies Committee of Cornell University.
★ ★ ★
Prof. Robert McCloskey of Harvard will give a talk on THE
WARREN COURT IN PERSPECTIVE tonight in Room 228 at 7:30.
McCloskey is on campus as a lecturer in American Democracy.
★ ★ ★
THE CECIL TAYLOR UNIT will give a special performance of
music and dance at Dinkelspiel Auditorium on Saturday April 27
at 8 p.m. Tickets are on sale outside of Tresidder, at the Post Office,
and at the door. Price is $1 for students, $2 others.
