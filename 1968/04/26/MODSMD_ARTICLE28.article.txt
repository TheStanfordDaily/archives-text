# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
* ~— —
The Stanford YWCA holds its annual STRAW BERRY BRUNCH
today from 10:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. on the lawn in front of Tresid
der. Strawberry shortcake, milk and coffee will be sold for 50 cents
a serving. Proceeds go to the East Palo Alto project, the Okinawa
Conference, and other University Christian Movement - YWCA*
sponsored projects.

MUSLIM EVENING ABROAD, sponsored by the Stanford Is
lamic Society, will be held tomorrow evening at 8:00 in the Tresid
der Main Lounge. Dr. Jamil Diab of Phoenix, Ariz.., is the main
speaker. Refreshments from Muslim countries will be served.
★ ★ ★
"The Flick." a new Stanford Community film society, presents
an evening of EXPERIMENTAL AND UNDERGROUND FILMS
tonight in Cubberley. Films include "Blinkity Blank," "A Chairy
Tale," "Happy Anniversary," "Moviola Blues," "The Tender Game,"
"Thirteen Cantos of Hell,' and "Up Tight . . . L.A. Is Burning.'
★ ★ ★
Mrs. Lenore D. Hanks, C.5.8., of Portland, Ore., will lecture on
the topic "THE NEW MORALITY" in the Tresidder Large Lounge
tonight at 8:00. The lecture is sponsored by the Christian Science
Organization. All are welcome.
★ ★ ★
The Cultural Arts Division of Tresidder invites students and fac
ulty to a DEMONSTRATION BY CHINESE ARTIST CHOW
CHIAN-CHIU in the Tresidder Large Lounge Sunday evening at
7:30. A reception for the artist will follow.
★ ★ ★
April 29 is the application deadline for women interested in the
Stanford in GERMANY, GROUP XXI, for June to December. Wom
en who have attended any Overseas Campus and qualify with
German may reapply as well as other late applicants who have not
been overseas. Additional information is available at the Overseas
Campuses Office, Room 1-C.
★ ★ ★
The BAY AREA AFRICANISTS MEETING begins at 9:00 Sat
urday morning with registration in front of Building 300. Morning
panels will last from 9:30 to 11:30 on "Application of Social Re
search Findings in Certain Independent African States" and "Bantu
Origins." Following luncheon from 12:00 to 3:00 additional panels
will be held on "Problems of Research in Africa," "Science and
Science Training in Modern Africa," and "Black Revolution and
African Studies." For further information contact Dr. Joseph
Greenberg in the Anthropology department.
★ ★ ★
Undergraduates in the Latter Dav Saints Church are holding a
"DAY OF EXAMINATION" Saturday at the LDS Institute of Reli
gion, 865 Stanford Avenue, 8:45 a.m. to 5:15 p.m. The purpose of
the seminars, panels, and discussions is to consider ways of acting
on moral commitments as citizens of the world and a Mormon.

PROF. GEORGE WEBER of the Union Theological Seminary
will speak Sunday morning at 11 in Memorial Church. Prof. Weber
is the former director of the East Harlem Protestant Parish, and is
currently the director of the Metropolitan Urban Service Training.
