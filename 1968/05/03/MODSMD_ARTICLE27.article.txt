# Alumni Organize Largest Program
## 
### 
The Stanford Alumni Associa
tion's biggest-ever campus con
ference is scheduled for Satur
day, May 18.
Some 1300 alumni, students
and administrators are serving
on committees to make the 35th
annual event a success. Payment
of a $1 registration fee entitles
anyone, alumnus or not, to at
tend.
President Wallace Sterling
of Stanford will make his last
talk prior to retirement to the
annual campus gathering. His
speech to combined reunion
classes and parents is sched
uled for 1:15 p.m., following
luncheon in the Bowman
Alumni House patio, to allow
those attending department
luncheons elsewhere to hear
him.
Two special University pro
grams are planned on the theme
of "The Provost and Business
Affairs."
The Music Department will
.offer an "open rehearsal" per
formance of the third act of
Verdi's "Falstaff," which will
begin at 3:15 p.m: in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium.
The Stanford Repertory Thea
ter will present "Sergeant Mus
grave's Dance" at 8 p.m. both
nights in The Nitery. Ram's
Head is offering the recent musi
cal, "Carnival," in Memorial
Auditorium at 8:30 p.m.
At the Stanford Shopping
Center, Stanford seniors and
Cardinals Board will sponsor
a "Con Home" Carnival on
behalf of the Stanford Chll
drens Convalescent Hospital,
with rides, games, and booths
manned by various living
groups.

On Saturday night, the Stan
ford Men's Glee Club will pre
sent "Up, Up and Away," which
begins at 8 p.m. in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium.
