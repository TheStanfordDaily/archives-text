# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The annual INTERCOLLEGIATE FOLK DANCE FESTIVAL,
presented by the Stanford International Folkdancers, the Women's
Recreational Association, and the Stanford Folkdancers Club, will
be held on Sunday, May 5, from 1 to 5 p.m. on the Women's Gym
lawn. Dancers and spectators invited. Admission free.

WORKERS FOR Mc.CARTHY will meet in the Physics Tank (not
Cub. Aud. as previously announced) at 9:30 or 11 a.m. for orien
tation and precinct assignments on Saturday, May 4. Canvassing
of California voters goes into full swing this weekend.

Dis-Joint and TMU present JAZZ IN THE SHADE today from 2
to 4 p.m. on the Tresidder front terrace. This is the first of a series
of afternoon concerts featuring local musicians.

Their stand in White Plaza will be manned from 11:30 a.m. to 3
p.m. The fee of 50c is used to support a Stanford Scholarship Fund.

Movement and the Mid-Peninsula Christian Ministry. Interested

James M. Woods of the Studio Watts Workshop returns to Serra
House bringing JEAN-PAUL SARTRE'S "NO EXIT" with him.
The play will be presented tonight and tomorrow night at 8 in the
Serra House courtyard. Students 50c, adults $1.00. Bring pillows or
blankets to sit on in case of a shortage of chairs.
