# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The STANFORD POPULATION FORUM will present the third
In a series of five lectures dealing with population and environ
mental problems. Dr. Melvin Reder, professor of economics, will
speak in Tressider, Room 270 tonight at 8.
★ ★ ★
Reservations are still being taken for the UNIVERSITY CHRIST
IAN MOVEMENT WEEKEND in the Santa Cruz Mountains May
17-19 with JefTery Smith and Michael Novak. For information call
325-2762 or 321-2300 ex. 3114 or see the USC Secretary on the
third floor of the Clubhouse. The cost of the weekend will be $15.
★ ★ ★
Dr. William P. Cregr, head of the Hematology Division of the
School of Medicine will speak on ETHNOLOGY AND BLOOD
GROUPS tonight at 8 in Room M-106 of the medical school.
★ ★ ★
A conference and five workshops on the ways business can re
spond to the RACIAL CRISIS will take place in Bishop Auditorium
of the Business School today from 9:30 to 12:30.
★ ★ ★
The Stanford YWCA lunch forum on the Educated Woman will
hear Miss Joyce Passetti, Women's Page Editor of the Palo Alto
Times, discuss the ROLE OF WOMEN IN JOURNALISM and the
place of women in the Middle East in the Clubhouse at noon today.
★ ★ ★
The Communications Department and the Union Board will spon
sor a continuous showing of STUDENT-PRODUCED FILMS in
the Tresidder patio after dark tonight.
★ ★ ★
Today is the last day for submitted PROPOSALS FOR UNDER
GRADUATE SPECIALS and classes for next fall. Suggestions
should be made in Robert Hind's office, Room 107 of the Law An
nex.
★ ★ ★
Today is the last day of the DRAW FOR UNIVERSITY HOUS
ING for the next academic year. Students who wish to change their
residence or obtain University housing may draw on the first
floor of the Old Union from 9-12 and 14:30. Postcards notifying
results will be sent out May 20.
★ ★ ★
The Western Civ Film Series will present "MEIN KAMPF" at 4:15
and 7:30 in Cubberley.
★ ★ ★
The I-Center will sponsor a trip of SYNANON, a center for the
treatment of drug addicts, on Saturday. For information call uni
versity ext. 4181.
★ ★ ★
The committee of the ARTS FOR McCARTHY will have refresh
ments and entertainment at the Stanford Campus Recreation As
sociation tonight between 8:30 and 11. A drawing for works of
art will take place at 9:30.
★ ★ ★
Charlotte Steiner, a lifelong vegetarian, will discuss VEGETAR
IAN DIET AND BREATHING tonight at 8 in Tresidder.
★ ★ ★
There will be a send-off rally for the Bay Area Caravan of the
POOR PEOPLE'S CAMPAIGN tonight at 7:30 at the Oakland
Auditorium Arena at 10th and Fallon Sts. Those who would like
rides or can take riders should meet behind the Bookstore at 6:15
p.m.
