The STANFORD MKN'S GLEE CLUB and ORCHESTRA will pre
sent a full concert of music including works by Handel and Brahms
and excerpts from the Broadway hit "Mame!" on Saturday, May 18,
at 8:30 in Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Students, $1.00; General, $2.00.
★ ★ ★
TIIE INTERNATIONAL FILM FORUM presents Ivan the Ter
rible, Part Two, tonight at 8:00 in TMIJ 274-75. Col Tee will be served
at the I-Center after the showing.
★ ★ ★
The OPERA CHORUS will rehearse tonight at 8:45 on stage in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium.
★ ★ ★
The ORGANIZATION OF AFRICAN STUDENTS presents Pro
fessor Victor Uchendu, who has just returned from a year of re
search in a number of African countries. He will be speaking on
"Cultural Constraints in Agrarian Economic Development in Af
rica" at noon at the Africa Table in Tresidder Cafeteria.
★ ★ ★
"Am I My Brother's Keeper?" will be the topic of an informal
talk by BART LYTTON tonight at 7 p.m. in Hurlburt House.
All members of the Stanford Community are invited.
★ ★ *
Philip Stone, Fellow at the Center for Advanced Study in the
Behavioral Sciences, will speak on "Monitoring and Influencing
Public Opinion: Implications for a Democratic Society" tonight for
CENTURY 21. The speech will be held at 7:30 in the Tresidder
large lounge.
★ ★ ★
Mike Rolle will give a PIANO RECITAL at 7:30 Sunday night in
the Tresidder small lounge. He will play Goldberg variations by
J. S. Bach.
★ ★ *
A program of SONGS FOR THE THEATER will be presented by
the Department of Speech and Drama at 2:30 p.m. Sunday, May
19, in Tresidder 281. Participants in the program, directed by
Evelyn Draper, are first and second year Master of Fine Arts
candidates in acting and the Acting Fellows of the Stanford Reper
tory Theater. The performance will include songs of the theater
from the second half of the sixteenth century to current musicals.
Lois Kulsar will accompany the actor-singers. Admission is free.

A program of SONGS FOR THE THEATER will be presented by
the Department of Speech and Drama at 2:30 p.m. Sunday, May
19, in Tresidder 281. Participants in the program, directed by
Evelyn Draper, are first and second year Master of Fine Arts
candidates in acting and the Acting Fellows of the Stanford Reper
tory Theater. The performance will include songs of the theater
from the second half of the sixteenth century to current musicals.
Lois Kulsar will accompany the actor-singers. Admission is free.
