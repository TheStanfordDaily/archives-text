# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
There will be a very important meeting of the MCCARTHY FOR
PRESIDENT COMMITTEE at 9 tonight in the ASSU office. Plans
are being made for pre-primary functions, and all McCarthy sup
porters are urged to come.
★ ★ ★
THE LATIN AMERICAN STUDIES ASSOCIATION luncheon
seminar will feature Prof. Russell Davis of Harvard's Center for
Studies in Education and Development, discussing his work in
Mexico, Central America, and Venezeua. The talk will be at noon,
and all are invited.
★ ★ ★
This week's Current Events Coffee Hour will feature French stu
dents discussing "RECENT DEVELOPMENTS IN FRANCE" at
the I-Center at 4:15 today. The originally scheduled discussion will
be postponed. All are invited.
★ ★ ★
"OCEAN FLOOR SPREADING: A DISCOVERY OF MAJOR
SIGNIFICANCE" will be the topic of the annual Sigma Xi lecture
to be presented at 8 p.m. in the Physics lecture hall by Prof. Allen V.
Cox. The public is invited.
★ ★ ★
All sophomores must declare a major to be eligible for PRE
REGISTRATION. Signing of class lists begins May 27. Preliminary
time schedule for fall quarter, Summer Bulletin, General Studies
Bulletin for 1968-69, and the Courses and Degrees pamphlet will
be available at the information window in the Old Union lobby.
★ ★ ★
The FRESHMAN FILM SERIES will feature "Ride the High
Country," a 1962 color western starring Randolph Scott and Joel
McCrea. The film will be shown at 8 and 10.

verdi e il suo tempo" by Luigi Magnani. The talk will be in TMU
