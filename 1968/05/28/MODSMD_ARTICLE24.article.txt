# Campus Roundup

"THE AMERICAN IMAGE OF THE CRISIS IN THE MIDDLE
EAST" will be the topic of a lecture by Prof. J. Neyer of Rutgers
University. The Israeli Students Organization is presenting the
talk at 8:15 in the Clubhouse.
★ ★ ★
The West German TV documentary POLIZEITSTAATSBESUCH
(Police State Visit) will be shown tonight at 7:30 and 8:15 in TMU
274. It depicts the police precautions taken during the Shah of
Iran's visit in 1967. It is sponsored by Otero and German Students
Association.
★ ★ ★
Project Director Singh Wallia will give "A REPORT ON CEN
TURY 21 PROJECT" this evening at 7:30 in the TMU lounge.
★ ★ ★
Three dignitaries from Nigeria will talk on the NIGERIAN CIVIL
WAR in TMU 274 at 4:15. The speakers will be Alii Ekineh, Chairman
of the Nigerian Commission on Human Rights and Secretary of
the Nigerian Bar Association, chief Lanlehin and chief I. I. Murphy.
★ ★ ★
The FRESHMAN FILM SERIES will feature "Easter Parade,"
a Technicolor musical starring Fred Astaire and Judy Garland.
The Series will recess next Tuesday by showing "The Red Balloon"
and "North by Northwest," starring Cary Grant. They will be
shown in Geology 320, and the Series resumes June 25 for the
summer quarter.
★ ★ ★
BLACK PANTHER PARTY Minister of Education, George Mur
ray, will speak at 8 in the Branner Dining Hall on the Panther
Party Platform. Murray was born in Pafaguola, Mississippi and
teaches in the English Department at San Francisco State.
★ ★ ★
The LATIN AMERICAN STUDIES ASSOCIATION luncheon
seminar will feature Prof. John Houston of Knox College who will
discuss "Some Latin American Views of the O.A.S. and Cuba."
Everyone is invited at noon in Bolivar House.
