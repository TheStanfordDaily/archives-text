# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
A UNIVERSITY COMMITTEE is being established to seek a
solution to campus parking problems. It will be a student-faeulty
administration committee with four students.
Students interested in serving on this committee should leave
their name, address, and phone number for Denis Hayes in the As
sociated Students' Office on the second floor of Tresidder Union.
★ ★ ★
Alpha Phi Omega, national service fraternity, is holding its quar
terly BOOK EXCHANGE in the Alvarado House daily this week, 11
a.m. to 4 p.m.
Students can find good used textbooks at low prices, and sell their
old books at their own prices. Students often save 50% or more of
the new price of textbooks.
★ ★ ★
The SEQUOIA, Stanford's literary magazine, will hold an organi
zational meeting Wednesday, at 8:30 in the Storke Publications
Building. A proposed art series and work on the magazine will be
discussed.
★ ★ ★
ANNA CHRISTIE, a 1930 drama starring Greta Garbo and
Charles Bickford, will be shown at 8:00 tonight in Geology room 320.
INTERNATIONAL HOUSE, a 1933 comedy starring W. C. Fields,
will be shown in the same room at 9:45. Admission to both is free.
