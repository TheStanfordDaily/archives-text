# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The SEQUOIA, Stanford's literary magazine, will hold an or
ganizational meeting tonight at 8:30 in the Storke Publications
Building.
The meeting will concern the magazine's procedures and policy
and a proposed series of Sequoia-sponsored poetry readings, art
shows, and concerts.
★ ★ *
A basic African language, YORUBA, is being offered for the
first time this year in a course taught by Mr. Olasope Oyelaran.
Yoruba is one of the three most important languages of Nigeria.
Students desiring further information should go to the Linguistics
Office, Building 100, Room 101-G today at 2:15.
★ ★ ★
STUDENT ASSOCIATES program arranges for upperclassmen
to eat one or more meals with freshmen in a particular house. A
sign-up list for those interested in being associates is available in
the Wilbur Office until Friday.
