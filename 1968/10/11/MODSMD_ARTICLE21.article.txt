# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
An organizational meeting of the PALO ALTO
FRIENDS will be held Sunday, at the home of
Mr. Ronald Sax, 3181 Emerson in Palo Alto. Ha
rumi Befu, Assistant Professor of Anthropology
at Stanford will discuss recent student activism
in Japan. All are invited.
★ ★ ★
Stanford seniors planning to take the ADMIS
SION TEST FOR GRADUATE STUDY IN BUSI
NESS on November 2 should file applications to
take the test with the Educational Testing Ser
vice by October 19. Applications are available at
the Stanford Graduate School of Business.
★ ★ ★
The AFRICAN TABLE will meet at noon today
at the International Center. Mr. James E. Craw
ford will speak on "Military Intervention in Poli
tics: the Case of Sierra Leone." All are invited.
Bring your own lunch. Coffee will be provided.
★ ★ ★
AN EVENING ABROAD—BRAZIL will be held
Saturday, at 8 p.m. at the International Center.
There will be music, dancing, and food in the
spirit of Rio's Carnival. All International Asso
ciation members are welcome.
★ ★ ★
Polling places for the forthcoming CONSTITU
TIONAL ELECTION on October 14 and 15 will
be located at the Post Office, the Engineering
Corner, and the Physics Tank. The polls will be
open from 9:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. at all locations.
A Student Body Card is required.

Ralph Metzner, editor of tho PSYCHEDELIC
REVIEW, will speak on "Recent Developments in
Psychedelic Drugs" today at 8:00 p.m. in the large
lounge of Tresidder Union.
★ ★ ★
A LASER LIGHT SHOW involving flowing pat
terns set to music will be presented today at
noon in Physics Rm. 101. A make-your-own lunch
will be available.
★ ★ ★
"FIRE BULLS" a Chinese Mandarin classic
film with Chinese and English subtitles, will be
shown on Sunday at 1:00 p.m. in Memorial Audi
torium. It is open to the public for an admission
fee of $1.

PIJVY will be held today at 4:00 p.m. at 829
University Ave., Palo Alto.

SMOKER on Sunday at 3 p.m. in the Tresidder
