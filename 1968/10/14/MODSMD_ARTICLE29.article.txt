# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Graduates and undergraduates interested in a summer in Japan
through the KEIO EXCHANGE PROGRAM should meet for slides
and discussion with last summer's exchange members at 8 p.m. in
Tresidder.
★ ★ ★
First casting calls for Stanford's THEATER OF THE CON
GRESSIONAL RECORD will be conducted from 8 to 10 p.m., Oct.
15-17, at Bishop Auditorium in the Graduate School of Business.
Fifty congressmen's roles will be filled.
★ ★ ★
Applications and course lists for OVERSEAS CAMPUSES are now
available for summer-fall Germany and Britain and fall-winter Aus
tria, France and Italy. Deadline for applications is Thursday,
November 28.
★ ★ ★
Volunteers to distribute UNITED FUND DRIVE leaflets and
pledge cards to faculty and staff are asked to contact department
heads for further information.
★ ★ ★
Student applications for the OUTDOOR CAMPUS SCULPTURE
COMMITTEE are being taken in the ASSU office. The committee
recommends acceptance of sculpture and art works for outdoor
display on the campus.
★ ★ ★
Polling places for the CONSTITUTIONAL ELECTION will be
open today and tomorrow, 9:30 to 4:00, at the Post Office, Engi
neering Corner, and the Physics Tank. A student body card is
required to vote.
