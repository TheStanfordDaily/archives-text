The International Association and Adelpha are sponsoring their
WEEKLY T.G.I.F. (i.e. beer and coffee hour) tomorrow from 4-6
p.m. in the Adelpha lounge. Everyone is welcome.
★ ★ ★
The STANFORD COMMUNITY COFFEE HOUSE plans to broad
en its program to include conversations with faculty members and
campus guests, dialogues on campus and world issues, experimental
movies, and after-play discussions. Anyone interested in serving on
a program committee should contact Dick Roe, 321-2300 X 3114. The
first meeting will be this afternoon at 4:15 in the Coffee House.
★ ★ ★
OVERSEAS CAMPUS BOARD APPLICATIONS will be available
in the Overseas Campus Office until Wednesday, November 13. The
Board consists of Overseas Campus alumni interested in working
on the program. Details are on the application.

SERIES is tonight at 8 p.m. in TMU small lounge. Professor Phillip
Damon, Chairman of the Department of Comparative Literature at
Berkeley will discuss "Sir Gawain and the Structure of Oral Narra
tive." Coffee will be served.

for Fair Housing, will speak at noon today in SLAC's main audito
rium, 2575 Sand Hill Road, Menlo Park. Everyone is invited to at
tend.
★ ★ ★
Dr. Hildegard Goss-Mayr, internationally famous Viennese lec
turer will speak on THE CHRISTIAN AND REVOLUTION today
at 4:15 p.m. in TMU, room 275. As a leader of the International
Fellowship of Reconciliation, Dr. Goss-Mayr has traveled exten
sively in Latin America and Africa to promote non-violent social
revolution and has organized Christian-Marxist conferences in
Eastern and Western Europe.
