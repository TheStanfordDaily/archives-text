# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The deadline for filing new 1909-70 STATE SCHOLARSHIP AP
PLICATIONS is Dec. 10, 1968. Undergraduates under 21 years of
age who are legal California residents may obtain applications Ironi
the Financial Aids Office.
★ ★ ★
OK. ,1. W. GKISHAM, of Washington University Medical School,
St. Louis will be interviewing applicants on November 1.3, all day,
and Nov. 14, morning. Dr. J. R Schofield, of Baylor University
Medical College, will be on campus all day Nov. 14. Contact the
Biology office for appointments.
★ ★ ★
THE FRESHMAN FILM SERIES tonight will feature Topper
at 8 p.m., starring Constance Bennett and Cary Grant, and Shall
We Dance" at 10 p.m., starring Fred Astaire and Ginger Rogers.
★ ★ ★
Harold Pinter's "THE BIRTHDAY PARTY" opens Wednesday at
8 p.m. in The Nitery and will continue its run Wednesdays through
Saturdays until Nov. 23. Discussions led by the director, I rofessor
vVilliam Sharp, will follow Thursday performances. Ushers, needed
for all performances, may sign up at the Memorial Hall Box Office
or call 321-2300 x 2934.
★ ★ ★
The OVERSEAS CAMPUSES OFFICE is continuing to accept
applications for the next Autumn-Winter groups in Austria, France,
and Italy, as well as Summer-Autumn groups in Britain and Ger
many. Applications, available in the Overseas Campuses Office and
living groups are due Nov. 27.
★ ★ ★

Center is "Student Genocide in Mexico." Mexican students will
lead discussions on recent Mexico City events.
