# Campus Roundup

ALEX HALEY, internationally known writer and the "as-told-to"
author of the AUTOBIOGRAPHY OF MALCOLM X, will be speak
ing tomorrow at 4:15 in Dinkelspiel on "The Virus of Violence."
★ ★ ★
Application's for next year's BERLIN EXCHANGE SCHOLAR
SHIP are in the I-Center Upstairs office. The scholarship offers one
full year of study at the Free University of Berlin. More Informa
tion can be obtained from Tom Gede, Chairman, Berlin Exchange,
at 327-2920 X 241.
★ ★ *
THE STANFORD MANAGEMENT INTERN PROGRAM is hold
ing its annual orientation program this afternoon at 4:00 in TMU
large lounge. All prospective applicants for summer junior manage
ment positions in industry are urged to attend.
★ ★ ★
The Tresidder Film Series will present PHANTOM OF THE
OPERA on Friday night and SWEET LIGHT IN A DARK ROOM
on Saturday night. Showings will start at 7:30, 8:30 and 9:30 and
will cost 50c.
★ ★ ★
Three newsreel films, "TROUBLEMAKERS" (riots in Newark),
"PANTHERS" and "HAIGHT" will be shown tonight in Cardinal
Lounge, Florence Moore. An informal discussion will be held after
the films.
★ ★ ★
Following tonight's performance of Pinter's "THE BIRTHDAY
PARTY" in the Nitery, Professor William Sharp, the director, will
lead a discussion with the audience.
k ★ ★
Tryouts for Moliere's "THE LEARNED LADIES" will be held
tomorrow afternoon at 3:30 in room 129, Memorial Hall. The pro
duction will be presented in January in Dinkelspiel.
★ ★ ★
CO-ED BADMINTON is scheduled for Thursday nights at 7:30-
9:30 in the Women's Gym. Equipment will be provided and every
one is invited. There is a 25c cover charge for boys.
★ ★ ★
The DISCOTHEQUE will be open every Friday night at 8:30 in
the I-Center Patio. There will be dancing, a light show, and re
freshments. Everyone is welcome. Anyone Interested in working
on the Discotheque is encouraged to leave his name at the I-Center
or call Dianne Cameron at 327-2920 X 429.
