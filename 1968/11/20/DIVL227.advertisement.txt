This study of Europe during the Second World War is
not another military history of the conflict; the theme
is a broader one: an examination of the various ways
in which the European states adapted themselves to
the demands of total and protracted war, with a sum
mary of the War's immediate and longer-term impact
on European political and social institutions, on atti
tudes, and on forms of intellectual and cultural ex
pression. Complete in itself, the book is one of a
number in "The Rise of Modern Europe" series.
