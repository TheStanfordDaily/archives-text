# Campus Roundup

### 
Two African plays, "The Virtues of Adultery" and"The Silhouette
of God" by Nigerian playwright Joseph Okpaku will open a seven
performance run tomorrow night at 8 p.m. in the Little Theater. The
plays are sponsored by the Dept. of Speech and Drama and The
Journal of the New African Literature and the Arts. Student admis
sion is $1.

Stanford-ln-Washington application deadline has been extended to

* * *
Interviews are being held Tues., Jan. 14, and Thurs., Jan. 1 6 to fill
student vacancies of the Subcommittee on Overseas Campuses. This
committee is not the student-organized Overseas Campuses Board, but
is a subcommittee of the University Committee on General Studies
and is responsible for academic policies and programs at the Overseas
Campuses. Other than the six faculty members of the subcommittee,
there are four student positions, no two of which can be filled by
alumni of the same Overseas Campus. The committee usually meets
from 12:00 noon to 2 p.m., five or six time a quarter. Interested
students should sign up for an interview in the Overseas Campuses
Office, Room IC, Bldg. 1.
