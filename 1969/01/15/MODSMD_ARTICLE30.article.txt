The following FRESHMAN SEMINARS have
openings: 1, 18, 22, 37, 51, 93, 94, 169. See Fresh
man Seminar Bulletin for course descriptions. Call
Miss Ilene Weeks, ext. 2131 or 2356 to sign up.

The first of a series of EXPERIMENTAL FILMS
to be' shown every Fri. at 11 p.m. in room 320, geo.
corner, will begin on Jan. 17 with "Cabinet of Dr.
Calgari," "Trip to the Moon," "Gertie the Dino
saur," "Dream of a Rarebit Fiend."
* * ♦
MAYNARD PARKER, veteran correspondent
covering Southeast Asia, will be on campus this
week as a Guest-in-Residence. He will speak tonight
at 7 p.m. at Branner on "Southeast Asia after Viet
nam."
* ♦ ♦
There will be a CANDLELIGHT SERVICE of
meditation tonight in Mem Chu at 9:30. The theme
will be daily corporate existence as expressed in big
city newspaper columns.
* * ♦

ford School of Law, and third year Law Students

Leroy Bobbit and Louis Nogales will be at TMU
267-8 Jan. 20 at 3 p.m. to talk with all Stanford
Minority Students interested in attending Stanford
School of Law.
