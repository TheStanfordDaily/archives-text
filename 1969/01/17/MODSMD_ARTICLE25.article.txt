\ See a LIVE CHAPARRAL. The SCG will sponsor
I a hike in Coe Street Park tomorrow morning, rain or
shine. No experience is necessary. Meet at the His
tory Corner at 9 a.m.
* * *
Anyone interested in working on the STAFF of
ASSU PRESIDENT Denis Hayes is asked to pick up
an application at the ASSU office.
* ♦ *
PRESIDENT PITZER will speak to the student
body in Dinkelspiel Auditorium Monday at 4:15.
His speech will be followed by a panel discussion on
the Study of Education at Stanford. The panelists
will be Dr. Robert Walker, Director of General
Studies; Herbert Packer, Vice Provost; Norton
Batkin, undergraduate on SES Steering Committee;
David Pugh, SDS; and Sanford Dornbush, President
of AAUP. Denis Hayes will moderate.
* * *
IA DISCOTHEQUE Friday night at 8:30 at the
international center. Come for a wild evening of
wine, women and bright lights.
* * *
Friday night 9:00 - 11:00 p.m. Jan. 17, THE
CLEANLINESS AND GODLINESS SKIFFLE

BAND, appearing at the coffeehouse-cover charge
50c .
* * *
A COMMITTEE TO REVIEW AND DECIDE
UPON THE DRAW has been formed with the Dean
of Students office. Interested students please apply
as soon as possible to the ASSU office.
* * *
The Stanford Committee on Radical Education
(SCORE) will sponsor the showing of the Beatles
film MAGICAL MYSTERY TOUR Sunday morning
at 9 a.m. and 10:30 a.m. Admission $2.50, Students
$2.00. The public is invited.
* * ♦
Beginning this Sunday, Jan. 19, the COMMUN
ION SERVICE in the Round Room of Memorial
Church will begin at 8:50 a.m. instead of 9:00.
* # *

the registrars. Class cards which have not been made
available by instructors can be obtained at the regis
trars. Starting Monday fee for late registration will
be $2, starting a week from Monday it will be in
creased to $10 and following tnat a students regis
tration will be subject to cancellation.
