# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Ram's Head needs a COSTUME EXECUTRIX for its winter pro
ductions "Tom Paine", "Play," and "She Got Out." If you know how
to sew, want to meet friendly people, and have some free time for the
next month please contact Ram's Head, ext. 4336, or Kathy Williams,
328-4084.
* * *
LAMB, a young classical jazz blues sound produced by four musi
cians: one vocalist, two classical guitarists, and an upright bass, will
appear at the Stanford Community Coffee House tonight at 9 and
10:30 p.m. and Midnight. There will be a 50 cent cover charge.
♦ * *
The Tresidder MAGICAL MYSTERY TOUR will present four films
and three shorts tonight at 11 p.m. in the Geology Corner (320.)
Included are "Moses," "Quixote,'' "Cosmic Ray,' and' Vivian.
Admission is 60 cents.
* * *
Members of the faculty of the STANFORD MUSIC DEPARI
- will present a concert tonight a 8:30 p.m. in Dinkelspiel Aud
itorium. Included are selections for piano, strings, clarinet, and voice.
* # *
Nominating petitions for students desiring td run for the ASSU
EDUCATION COUNCIL are now available in the ASSU Office. The
council will be the student group which will meet with the Faculty
Senate to consider the 'educational' reports of the S.E.S. The Blue
applications which were distributed earlier will also constitute valid
nominating petitions, provided they contain ten signatures from the
candidate's own electorate.
* * *
STUDENT APPLICANTS are being sought for a university com
mittee to evaluate the role of the Teaching Assistant at Stanford. To
date, only a few applications from graduate students in engineering
and the sciences who have T.A. experience have been received. Appli
cations are due in the ASSU office Monday at noon.
♦ * *
THE COMPANY will hold a meeting Sunday at 7:30 p.m. in Ar
royo Lounge to promote comradeship and to do some scenes from the
plays being prepared this quarter. Please come.
* * *
The Stanford chapter of STUDENTS FOR A DEMOCRATIC
SOCIETY will meet Sunday at 8 p.m. in Tresidder. Under discussion
will be further strategy regarding SDS's campaign to get Stanford out
of Southeast Asia, including the possibility of formulating an ulti
matum for the meeting of the SDS's nine demands.
* # *
Monday is the DEADLINE FOR FILING candidacy applications
for June A.M., M.S., and Engineer degrees, and for October Ph.D.
degrees. Applications must be signed by major departments before
being submitted to the Graduate Study Office, Room 117, Old Union.
