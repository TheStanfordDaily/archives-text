# WEEKEND SCHEDULE
## 
### 
Numerous Stanford sports squads will see action over the weekend,
both home and away:
SWIMMING—Perhaps the finest group of college swimmers in the na
tion today, the varsity meets UCLA at 2:00 p.m. tomorrow in Encina
Pool. The Stanford Frosh, also outstanding, take on College of the
Sequoias at 10:00 a.m.
iiASEBALL--The always torgh Card baseball nine plays a make-up
contest today against the Philadelphia Rookies. The game, originally
set for yesterday was rained out. Tomorrow, the team plays the first
in a series of double-headers, this against USF starting at 12:30 p.m.
RUGBY With a 36-0 victory under their belts, the rugby team opens
league play and defense of their unofficial national title at USC to
morrow at 1 :30 p.m. Last week the Capitol Rugby Club got clobbered
by the huge score.
FROSH BASKETBALL-Coach Paul Neumann's five little Indians go
against the University of Santa Clara Frosh tonight at 6:00 p.m. in an
away game. Claude Terry leads the successful squad, which remains
above the .500 mark.
GYMNASTICS- The improving team takes on both UC schools to
morrow night in Berkeley at 8:00 p.m. Cal is the defending national
champion, while UCLA always fields a strong team. Craig Dickson is
Stanford's main upset hope.
GOLF- The varsity squad opens their season today at 1:00 p.m. on
the Stanford course versus San Jose City College. Tomorrow, they
face Santa Clara at noon.
TENNIS—The <JV squad opposes Cal Poly of San Luis Obispo at 3:00
p.m. this afternoon, at home.
