# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
The STUDENT CONDUCT LEGISLATIVE Council will have an
open meeting in Tresidder, Thursday from 4 to 6 p.m. and will have
hearings on the Campus Disruption Policy at 8:30 p.m. on Sunday
night at Tresidder.
* * *
The PSYCHODRAMA MARATHON led by the Human Institute,
scheduled for this weekend Feb. 14-16, has places open for students,
faculty, and administrators. The cost is $13. For information contact
Macia Sloane, Roble Hall, 326-2520 x 228.
* * *
To encourage the Sharon Heights Safeway store to join the other
Midpenisula Safeway stores in boycotting California table grapes, the
STANFORD PTA will picket and shop-in there at noon, Wed., Feb.
12. The boycott has been called by the AFL-CIO Farmer Workers
Union to gain collective bargaining rights, union recognition, and a
minimum wage. The Stanford community is invited to participate.
* * *
The final lecture of the Winter series of the Wednesday Night Art
Lectures will be given by Prof. Standish Lawder, film history and 20th
Century Art professor at Yale University. He will lecture in Bishop
Auditorium Wed., Feb. 12 at 8:00 on "The German Expressionist
Cinema" and show the film "The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari". The
lectures are free and open to the public.
* * *
MAJORS IN SPANISH and allied disciplines may spend two
quarters at the University of Salamanca. Students will reside in
residential de estudiantes and attend courses both at the University
and with Professor Gustavo Alfara who will accompany the group.
Application forms and additional information may be obtained from
the Department of Spanish and Portuguese.
* * *
The STANFORD REPERTORY THEATER presents Ugo Betti's
contemporary drama, "The Queen and the Rebels" at 8:00 tonight in
Memorial Auditorium. Performances will continue Wednesdays
through Saturdays until Mar. 1. Call 321-2300 x 2934 for
reservations.
* * *
RABBI JOSEPH GLASER, Regional Director of the Union of
American Hebrew Congregations will speak on "World Jewry", Wed.,
Feb 12 at 8 p.m. in Tresidder, Room 270. Rabbi Glaser is
Vice-chairman of the San Francisco Conference on Religion and
Peace. He was a Leading mediator in the Pereli-Minetti-United Farm
Workers—Teamsters Union dispute, guiding it to a successful
conclusion.
* * *
Come and see the film HUELGA at 7:30 p.m., Thurs., Feb. 13, at
251 Castro Street, Mt. View. The program will include a side show of
the great Sacramento march and a discussion of the new tactics in the
Grape War. Donation $1.00 at the door.
* * *
There will be a meeting of the POLITICAL SCIENCE GRADUATE
AND UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS in Tresidder Room 274-275
Thurs. at 4:15 to consider and appropriate response to recent
developments in the political science curriculum, especially regarding
the proposal to allow T.A.'s to teach various courses. All Political
science students are urged to attend.

There will be a meeting of the POLITICAL SCIENCE GRADUATE
AND UNDERGRADUATE STUDENTS in Tresidder Room 274-275
Thurs. at 4:15 to consider and appropriate response to recent
developments in the political science curriculum, especially regarding
the proposal to allow T.A.'s to teach various courses. All Political
science students are urged to attend.
The STUDENT CONDUCT LEGISLATIVE Council will have an
open meeting in Tresidder, Thursday from 4 to 6 p.m. and will have
hearings on the Campus Disruption Policy at 8:30 p.m. on Sunday
night at Tresidder.
* * *
The PSYCHODRAMA MARATHON led by the Human Institute,
scheduled for this weekend Feb. 14-16, has places open for students,
faculty, and administrators. The cost is $13. For information contact
Macia Sloane, Roble Hall, 326-2520 x 228.
* * *
To encourage the Sharon Heights Safeway store to join the other
Midpenisula Safeway stores in boycotting California table grapes, the
STANFORD PTA will picket and shop-in there at noon, Wed., Feb.
12. The boycott has been called by the AFL-CIO Farmer Workers
Union to gain collective bargaining rights, union recognition, and a
minimum wage. The Stanford community is invited to participate.
* * *
The final lecture of the Winter series of the Wednesday Night Art
Lectures will be given by Prof. Standish Lawder, film history and 20th
Century Art professor at Yale University. He will lecture in Bishop
Auditorium Wed., Feb. 12 at 8:00 on "The German Expressionist
Cinema" and show the film "The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari". The
lectures are free and open to the public.
* * *
MAJORS IN SPANISH and allied disciplines may spend two
quarters at the University of Salamanca. Students will reside in
residential de estudiantes and attend courses both at the University
and with Professor Gustavo Alfara who will accompany the group.
Application forms and additional information may be obtained from
the Department of Spanish and Portuguese.
* * *
The STANFORD REPERTORY THEATER presents Ugo Betti's
contemporary drama, "The Queen and the Rebels" at 8:00 tonight in
Memorial Auditorium. Performances will continue Wednesdays
through Saturdays until Mar. 1. Call 321-2300 x 2934 for
reservations.
* * *
RABBI JOSEPH GLASER, Regional Director of the Union of
American Hebrew Congregations will speak on "World Jewry", Wed.,
Feb 12 at 8 p.m. in Tresidder, Room 270. Rabbi Glaser is
Vice-chairman of the San Francisco Conference on Religion and
Peace. He was a Leading mediator in the Pereli-Minetti-United Farm
Workers—Teamsters Union dispute, guiding it to a successful
conclusion.
