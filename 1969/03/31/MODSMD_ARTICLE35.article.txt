# Varied Season
## Six Plays In Repertoire 
### 
A varied season of plays will be
presented by the Department of
Speech and Drama this spring.
Two Stanford Repertory Thea
ter shows are planned. "Blood
Wedding" by Federico Garcia
Lorca will play in the Little Thea
ter at 8 p.m. April 2-5, 9, 11, 12,
16-19 and at 2 p.m. April 5 and
12.
In this modern Spanish classic
family feuds, conflict erupts when
human desire comes face to face
with the unbending code of the
Spanish culture.
"The Jew of Malta" by
Christopher Marlowe, a classic of
the English Renaissance, will play
in Memorial Auditorium at 8 p.m.
May 7-10 and 14-17 with a
matinee at 2 p.m. May 10, to be
followed by a special workshop
on "Marlowe and His Times."
In addition to its SRT plays,
the Speech and Drama Depart
ment has four additional offer
ings.
An evening of two one-acts will
be presented at 8 p.m. April 23-26
in the Nitery. The first of these is
"The Marriage of Heaven and
Hell," a dramatization with mime
and music of William Blake's
visionary poem. It is directed by
Jay Morse, an undergraduate in
drama.
The second is "The Wager," an
original script by Mark Medoff.

"The MaFiA Sings" is the title
of an evening of songs for the
theater. The program is under the
direction of Evelyn Draper, voice
coach for MFA drama students,
and will be held at 7:30 p.m. May
11 in Tresidder Memorial Union,
Room 281.
" 19-Ajax-69," a new trans
lation and adaptation of the
"Ajax of Sophocles" play, will be
given at 8 p.m. May 21-14 in the
patio of Bechtel International
Center. Robert P. Walter directs
this show using the "Water"
ensemble that appeared in his
recent production of "Tom
Paine."
Another original script,
"Mourning Hercules" by MFA
student Howard Brown, will play
at 8 p.m. May 28-31 in the Little
Theater. Brown will direct his
own script.
The West Premiere of
"The Electric Nligger," a recent
off-Broadway hit by Ed Bullins,
will be presented the first week in
June in the Nitery. The pro

duction is a joint effort of John
Cochran and the African and
Afro-American Studies program
with the MaFiA.
A varied season of plays will be
presented by the Department of
Speech and Drama this spring.

A varied season of plays will be
presented by the Department of
Speech and Drama this spring.
Two Stanford Repertory Thea
ter shows are planned. "Blood
Wedding" by Federico Garcia
Lorca will play in the Little Thea
ter at 8 p.m. April 2-5, 9, 11, 12,
16-19 and at 2 p.m. April 5 and
12.
In this modern Spanish classic
family feuds, conflict erupts when
human desire comes face to face
with the unbending code of the
Spanish culture.
"The Jew of Malta" by
Christopher Marlowe, a classic of
the English Renaissance, will play
in Memorial Auditorium at 8 p.m.
May 7-10 and 14-17 with a
matinee at 2 p.m. May 10, to be
followed by a special workshop
on "Marlowe and His Times."
In addition to its SRT plays,
the Speech and Drama Depart
ment has four additional offer
ings.
An evening of two one-acts will
be presented at 8 p.m. April 23-26
in the Nitery. The first of these is
"The Marriage of Heaven and
Hell," a dramatization with mime
and music of William Blake's
visionary poem. It is directed by
Jay Morse, an undergraduate in
drama.
The second is "The Wager," an
original script by Mark Medoff.

"The MaFiA Sings" is the title
of an evening of songs for the
theater. The program is under the
direction of Evelyn Draper, voice
coach for MFA drama students,
and will be held at 7:30 p.m. May
11 in Tresidder Memorial Union,
Room 281.
" 19-Ajax-69," a new trans
lation and adaptation of the
"Ajax of Sophocles" play, will be
given at 8 p.m. May 21-14 in the
patio of Bechtel International
Center. Robert P. Walter directs
this show using the "Water"
ensemble that appeared in his
recent production of "Tom
Paine."
Another original script,
"Mourning Hercules" by MFA
student Howard Brown, will play
at 8 p.m. May 28-31 in the Little
Theater. Brown will direct his
own script.
The West Premiere of
"The Electric Nligger," a recent
off-Broadway hit by Ed Bullins,
will be presented the first week in
June in the Nitery. The pro

duction is a joint effort of John
Cochran and the African and
Afro-American Studies program
with the MaFiA.
