# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
Professor Stuart R. Schram of the University of London, a leading
biographer of Mao Tse-tung, will speak on "THE SOCIAL AND
CULTURAL REVOLUTIONS IN ASIA" tonight at 8:15 p.m. in
Bishop Auditorium.
* # *
AIK POLLUTION caused by automobile exhaust will be the topic of
a panel discussion sponsored by the Population and Environment
Forum tonight at 7:45 in the large lounge of Tresidder Union.
* * *
A MAUNDY THURSDAY SERVICE will be held tonight at 9 p.m.
in Memorial Church. It will include sharing of communion, readings
from the Scriptures and contemporary sources, and a meditation by
Dean of the Chapel B. Davie Napier.
* * *
"PARABLES: A RELEVANT MEANS oV COMMUNICATION?"
will be the topic of Dr. Kenneth Pike, professor of linguistics at
Michigan State, tonight at 7 :30 p.m. in Tresidder, Rm. 275.
* * *
Mr. William Keogh, Esq., Stanford Law School, an authority on
military justice, will be featured tonight at 7 p.m. in the Lassen House
(Stern) Lounge for a discussion on "MILITARY JUSTICE AND THE
PRESIDIO MUTINY TRIAL." Refreshments will be served.
* * *
"MEMORANDUM," a Resistance-SDS sponsored film on Nazi
Germany and Germany today will be shown tonight at 7:30 p.m. in
Cubberly, 50c.
* * *
Dr. Klaus Meschkat, visiting professor of sociology at NYU and
ex-leader of the German SDS will speak tomorrow on "FASCISM AND
THE NEW LEFT" in Cubberly at 4:15 p.m.

"BLOOD WEDDING," Federico Garcia Lorca's poetic-drama plays
tonight at 8 p.m. in the Little Theater. Call the Memorial Hall Box
Office for reservations.
* * ♦
Try-outs for "THE DEVILS," the spring show of The Company will
be held today from 3:30 p.m. to 5 p.m. and tonight from 7:30 p.m. to
9 p.m. in Toyon Lounge. Substantial parts are open for both men and
women, and creative technical people are especially needed.
* * *
The T.MU CULTURAL ARTS DIVISION is looking for groups,
musical, dramatic or otherwise, to perform informally during spring
quarter. Call Helen Shannon, ext. 4311 at TMU.
* * *
APPLICATIONS are solicited for the following positions: one
student member for the Committee on a New Undergraduate Dorm,
one student member for the General Studies Committee, and one
graduate student member and one undergraduate member for the
University Library Council. Contact ASSU office.
* * *
Applications for the position of STUDENT FINANCIAL
MANAGER are available in the ASSU office and are due Monday, April
7, by 4 p.m. Interested applicants should talk to David Swift
concerning the position.
