# Many Activities
## Africa Culture Week 
### 
"Africa Culture Week" will be
sponsored by the Stanford
Organization of African Students
April 14-20, including art and
literature exhibitions, discussions,
film showings, and an African
dinner and dance.
The Culture Week coincides
with the "Afro-American Cultural
Festival," being sponsored by the
Black Student Union of Stanford
from April 13-30.
The program opens Tuesday,
April 15, with a keynote speech
by the Hon. Ebenezer Akuete,
Senior Secretary of the Ministry
of External Affairs of Ghana, at
4:15 p.m. in Cubberley
Auditorium.
Displays of African literature,
pictures and posters, old and
contemporary wood and ivory
carvings and sculpture, and
weaving will be shown from April
14 to 20 in Hoover Institution, at
the Main Library, at Meyer
Undergraduate Library, and at
Tresidder Memorial Union.
Movies made in Africa will be
shown at 7:30 p.m. Wednesday,
April 16, in Tresidder, Rooms
270-271.
At 4:15 p.m. Thursday, April
17 in Dinkelspiel Auditorium, a
panel discussion on "Higher
Education and Economic
Development in Africa" will be
moderated by Prof. Ulf
Himmelstrand from Sweden, now
at the Center for Advanced Study
in the Behavioral Sciences on the
Stanford campus.
Panelists will include Dr. Jacob

Oloya of Kenya, visiting
economist at Stanford's Food
Research Institute; and Albert
Wright, Ghanaian educator doing
advanced work at UC-Berkeley.
The African dinner, with dishes
from various section of the
African continent, and the movie
and dance are scheduled for
Saturday, April 19, at Bechtel
International Center. Dinner is at
6:30 p.m. (with a $1.50 charge).
The movie follows at 8 p.m. and
the dance at 9:30 p.m.
Joe Folayan of Nigeria,

graduate student in civil
engineering, is president of the
35-member Organization of
African Students at Stanford. He
has been assisted in arranging the
week-long program by the offices
of the Dean of Students, Public
Events, ASSU, Hoover Institution,
and the I-Center.
Folayan urges attendance and
the opportunity it affords for his
organization "to inform Stanford
and its community about
Africa—its people, culture, and
development."

Joe Folayan of Nigeria,

graduate student in civil
engineering, is president of the
35-member Organization of
African Students at Stanford. He
has been assisted in arranging the
week-long program by the offices
of the Dean of Students, Public
Events, ASSU, Hoover Institution,
and the I-Center.
Folayan urges attendance and
the opportunity it affords for his
organization "to inform Stanford
and its community about
Africa—its people, culture, and
development."
"Africa Culture Week" will be
sponsored by the Stanford
Organization of African Students
April 14-20, including art and
literature exhibitions, discussions,
film showings, and an African
dinner and dance.
The Culture Week coincides
with the "Afro-American Cultural
Festival," being sponsored by the
Black Student Union of Stanford
from April 13-30.
The program opens Tuesday,
April 15, with a keynote speech
by the Hon. Ebenezer Akuete,
Senior Secretary of the Ministry
of External Affairs of Ghana, at
4:15 p.m. in Cubberley
Auditorium.
Displays of African literature,
pictures and posters, old and
contemporary wood and ivory
carvings and sculpture, and
weaving will be shown from April
14 to 20 in Hoover Institution, at
the Main Library, at Meyer
Undergraduate Library, and at
Tresidder Memorial Union.
Movies made in Africa will be
shown at 7:30 p.m. Wednesday,
April 16, in Tresidder, Rooms
270-271.
At 4:15 p.m. Thursday, April
17 in Dinkelspiel Auditorium, a
panel discussion on "Higher
Education and Economic
Development in Africa" will be
moderated by Prof. Ulf
Himmelstrand from Sweden, now
at the Center for Advanced Study
in the Behavioral Sciences on the
Stanford campus.
Panelists will include Dr. Jacob

Oloya of Kenya, visiting
economist at Stanford's Food
Research Institute; and Albert
Wright, Ghanaian educator doing
advanced work at UC-Berkeley.
The African dinner, with dishes
from various section of the
African continent, and the movie
and dance are scheduled for
Saturday, April 19, at Bechtel
International Center. Dinner is at
6:30 p.m. (with a $1.50 charge).
