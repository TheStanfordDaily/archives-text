# Celebrate Culture
## BSU Hosts Festival 
### 
The University's Black Student
Union is hosting an Afro-American
Cultural Festival from April 13 to
20 with films, music, lectures, and
a fashion show—and an exhibition
at Stanford Art Gallery from
April 15 to 30—"to celebrate our
culture."
The Festival coincides with
"Africa Culture Week," being
sponsored by the Organization of
African Students at Stanford from
April 14-20.
The Art Gallery show, titled
African and Afro-American
Art," will include prints and
drawings by contemporary
California Black artists and by
guest artist Calvin Burnett of
Boston, Mass.; and
religious-historic African carvings
and sculpture.
In addition, a similar exhibition
will be displayed at Tresidder
Memorial Union where, in the
large lounge at 4:15 p.m.
Wednesday, Apr. 16, Dr. Samella

Lewis of Los Angeles will discuss
"Afro-American Art Today."
Prof. Herman Blake from
UC-Santa Cruz will be the
keynote speaker for the cultural
festival at a 4:15 p.m. program
Monday, Apr. 14, in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium. It is expected he will
discuss the ethnic studies program
at that campus.
English Prof. Ishmael Reed
from UC-Berkeley, a novelist, will
give a talk on Afro-American
literature at 4:15 p.m. Friday,
Apr. 18, in Tresidder large lounge.
The festival music programs
include a 2:30 p.m. performance

Sunday, Apr. 13, in Memorial
Auditorium with Oscar Brown Jr., J
singer-composer, and the 'Mighty
Mystics,' Bay Area vocal group.
Another program, "Music of
the Afro-American Community,"
will be presented at 8 p.m. Friday,
Apr. 18, in Tresidder Memorial
Union large lounge. On Sunday,
Apr. 20, Sun Ra and his "Solar
Arkestra' will explore the worlds
of heliocentric music at 2:30 p.m.
in Memorial Auditorium.
An African Fashion Show is
scheduled for 2:30 p.m. Saturday,
Apr. 19, at Tresidder large lounge.
