STANFORDâ€”Five members of
the Stanford Law School Class of
1969 have been elected to the
Order of the Coif, a national legal
honor society.
Nine other members of the
Class of 1969 were elected in June
of this year.
The Order of the Coif is to
legal education substantially what
Phi Beta Kappa is to
undergraduate education.
The new members are:
Joel Norton Klevens, Bruce
Weimer Sattler, James McClain
Hoak, Jr., Fredericka Paff, and
Sandra Smiley.
* * *
Readings for the Stanford
Repertory Theater's production
of "Androcles and the Lion" by
Bernard Shaw will be held
Wednesday and Thursday at 7
p.m. in Room 205 of Memorial

Hall and Friday at 7 p.m. in the
Nitery. Shaw's wicked satire on
fanaticism and martyrdom will
play Dec. 3-6, 10-13, and 19-20 in
Memorial Hall. The two final
performances are scheduled for
the week-end following exam
week.
Try-outs are open to all
Stanford students, staff, and
faculty. Undergraduates are
especially encouraged to try-out.
Academic credit is available.
"Androcles and the Lion" will
be directed by Denis Carey, guest
lecturer in the Department of
Speech and Drama. Carey has
been an actor and director with
major companies over the past 20
years in England, Canada,
Belgium, Yugoslavia, and the U.S.
Those interested in assisting
the technical director may contact
Derek Hunt, Room 143, ext.
2572.
