# Campus Round-Up
## 
### 
THE UCM is sponsoring a conference on a critique of student
service projects this Friday, November 7, from 3:00 p.m. into che
evening. There is no cost and supper is provided free. There is still
room for a limited number-of additional students. Call x 3114 if
interested.
♦ ♦ ♦
SEQUOIA, Stanford's Literary Magazine, is collecting material for
its Winter Issue. Short stories, poems, and other writing would be
greatly appreciated. Any contributions should be left either at the
Sequoia office in the Storke Publications Building or in the mailbox
downstairs. Art work and photographs are also being solicited.
* * *
BETA CHI COMMUNITY FOR THE PERFORMINC ARTS invites
all recent alumni of Beta Chi or Sigma Nu to a buffet supper and
preview performance of Rochelle Owen's BECLCH Saturday,
November Bth, Cocktails at 6:30. R.S.V.P. 328-9986.
* * *
THE STUDENT FACULTY LIAISON COMMITTEE of the School
of Engineering will meet today at 4:15 in Room 274, Engin. Corner.
All engineering students and prospective engineering urged to attend.
* * *
All those interested in discussing and planning a peaceful and
symbolic STUDENT STRIKE AT STANFORD in solidarity with
international strikes on Nov. 14th, please contact Debby Shenfeld or
Bertie Anderson, 327-2920, ext. 6139.
* * *
All those interested in founding a chapter of the STUDENT
MOBILIZATION COMMITTEE AT STANFORD to carry on regular,
comprehensive, nonpartisan, nonviolent antiwar activities, please
contact Debby Shenfeld or Bertie Anderson, 327-2920, ext. 6139.
* * *
THE BERNIE TRILLING QUINTET, well known campus jazz
group, will perform tonight at the Wilbur Coffee House.
♦ ♦ *
ECOLOGY ACTION. Join the new peninsula chapter now. First
general meeting tonight at 7:30 p.m. at the Menlo Park Recreation
Center to discuss and plan immediate and future activities.
♦ * *
