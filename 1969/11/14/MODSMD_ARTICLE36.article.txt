# Campus Round-Up
## 
### 
Application deadline for winter quarter
UNDERGRADUATE SPECIAL COURSES is
today. Forms may be picked up from Mrs. Main,
Bldg. 1.
* * *
"BLOW-UP" will be presented Saturday night at
Cubberley at 7 and 9, and 11 p.m. by the Celluloid
Syndicate. Admission is $1.00.
* * *
There will be a MOBILIZATION MONITORS
MEETING tonight at 7 in the ASSU office. This
meeting is extremely important, so everyone must
attend. Monitors may pick up their materials from
4-6 p.m. Friday at the ASSU office.
* * *
December Ski Trips are being planned by the
International Center for BEGINNING AND
EXPERIENCED SKIIERS alike. Come in this
week for more information or call ext. 4181.
* * *
The NAIROBI BOOK DRIVE needs help in
collecting donated books from San Francisco, San
Jose, and the Palo Alto vicinity. To help, call
327-7577 or 321-2300, ext. 4330.
* * *
All undergraduate PSYCHOLOGY MAJORS are
encouraged to sign up for membership on various
departmental committees at the Department
office, Room 13 Cubberley. Those students
interested in serving on the Faculty or Curriculum
Committees are urged to enlist as soon as possible.
The deadline is 5:00 p.m. on Thursday, November
20.
* * *
"BEYOND PROTEST," a Mixed Media
presentation by the Bahais of Stanford, will
happen Saturday night at 8:30 at the Stanford
Club House. Talk, music, sound and admission
free.
* * *
Tonight at 7:30 and 9:30 in Cubberley,
Volunteers in Asia will present Michael Roemer's
film "NOTHING BUT A MAN." 75c will get you
in.
* * *
Coffee break discussions of the VIETNAM
WAR will be held in Escondido Village today.
Apartments 58A and 104 A will begin discussions
at 10:30 a.m. Apartment 54D will open at 12:30
p.m. All are welcome.
* * *
Applications are available in the ASSU office

for students interested in the position of ASSU
FLECTIONS COMMISSIONER and are due
Tuesday, November 18.
* ♦ *
Drop by the Tresidder Cafeteria today for
INFORMAL TALK SESSIONS with resource
leaders on the Vietnam war, American foreign
policy, civil disobedience, or your own topic.
Sessions are planned for 9-10:30 a.m. and
beginning again at 2:00 p.m.
* * #
One student will be selected from the student
body at large by the ASSU Committee on
Nominations to serve as one of three students on
the FACULTY SENATE without vote.
Applications are available in the ASSU Office and
are due Wednesday, Nov. 19.
* * *
Anyone who would like to participate in
today's PEACE STRIKE please come to the
coordinating table at White Plaza for directions.
Volunteers are needed for picketing, leafleting,
joining discussion and guerilla theatre groups, and
relaying messages from the coordinating tables.
* ♦ *
The deadline for APPLICATIONS for the
Committee on Research of the Academic Council,
the Dean's Advisory Committee, the
Student-Faculty Employment Committee, and the
Department of Political Science on Undergraduate
Studies has been extended to Wednesday,
November 19.
* * *
CELEBRATE FRIDAY at the I-Center! Happy
Hour will start at 4 p.m. and last until ?
International Association and Graduate Student
Association are co-sponsors. All welcome.
* * *
Sunday there will be a walk of SAN
FRANCISQUITO CREEK before it is paved in the
name of flood control. Meet at Sunset Magazine
(Willow and Middlefield Roads, Menlo Park) at 12
noon.
* * *
Applications are now available for five standing
committees on the BOARD OF TRUSTEES:
FINANCE (2 students), ACADEMIC AFFAIRS (2
students), BUILDINGS AND GROUNDS (2
students), PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT (2
students), and LAND DEVELOPMENT (1
student). Completed applications are due next
Monday, Nov. 17.
