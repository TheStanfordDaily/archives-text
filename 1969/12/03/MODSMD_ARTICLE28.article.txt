# Tanner Takes Texas Tourney
## 
### HALL DAILY 
Last weekend in a spectacular
televised final, Stanford's
freshman star Roscoe Tanner
captured the National Junior
Indoor Tennis Championships
held in Dallas, Texas. In order to
win his second national
tournament title this year, Roscoe
defeated Jim Connors of
Belleville, Illinois by a score of
6-3, 10-8.
Stanford also sent two other
ace freshmen to the Dallas
competition—Gerry Groslimond
and Rick Fisher. In the round of
sixteen, Fisher pulled off the
upset of the tournament and the
biggest win of his tennis career.
He defeated top-seeded Dick
Stockton of New York City,
playing as a sophomore for the
University of Trinity (Texas), in a
two-set match, 10-8, 6-4.
In the same round, Groslimond
fell before fourth-seeded Harold
Solomon of Silver Spring, Md.,
6-4, 6-4. Tanner breezed past
Bruce Nagel of lowa City, 6-4,
6-2.

In the quarter-finals, number
two seeded Tanner whipped Dan
Birchmore, National Junior Clay
Courts Champion from the
University of Georgia, in a quick
7-5, 6-3. Meanwhile, Fisher also
advanced by clouting his
opponent, Howard Butt from
Corpus Cristi, 6-2, 6-4.
In other quarter-final matches,
Solomon outlasted Brian
Gottfried, a Trinity freshman
from Fort Lauderdale, Fla., in a
marathon, 3-6, 6-4, 6-2. Connors
crushed Alex Mayer of
Wood mere, N.Y., 6-4, 6 1.
The semi-finals pitted Tanner
against Solomon and Fisher versus
Connors. Stanford's Tanner
drubbed Solomon, 6-1, 6-4.
However, Connors proved too
tough for Fisher in a 7-5, 6-4
outcome.
For Tanner, who didn't lose
one set at Dallas last week, this is
his second national junior title
this year.
Farlier last spring, he garnered
the National Hardcourts Junior
Championships at Burlingame.
