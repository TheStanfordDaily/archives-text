# Tanner on Top
## Tennis Team Triumphs 
### HALL DAILY 
Coach Dick Gould's varsity
tennis team opened its season
yesterday with an impressive
victory over San Fernando Valley
State College, 9-0. After sweeping
the six singles matches, Gould
rested his top racketeers and
substituted other players who
proved how deep the Tribe netters
really are this year.
Roscoe Tanner led the Indian
assault by whipping 1969 NCAA
College Division Singles Champ
Steve Messmer, 6-3, 6-4. At
second singles slot, Rick Fisher
disposed of George Benedict in
straight sets, 6-2, 6-0.
Rich Evans overcame a poor
start in defeating Tighe Taylor,
6-8, 6-2, 6-3. In fourth singles,
Rob Rippner stopped Bris
Eisendrath, 6-1, 6-0. Chris Chapin,
playing fifth, put away Del

Newquist 6-2, 6-2. In the last
singles spot, Gerry Groslimond
scalped Jeff Marshall 6-0, 6-0.
In an exhibition singles match,
Larry Stecumest of Stanford beat
Tom Conover of Valley State in
two sets, 6-4, 6-1.
Without using his first four
doubles teams, Coach Gould gave
six varsity squad members some
valuable experience and playing
time. In first doubles, Robin Fry
and Paul Marienthal were
victorious over Messmer and
Benedict, 6-4, 6-3. Messmer and
Benedict were NCAA College
Division doubles champions last
year.
In the number two doubles,
Rob Daugherty and Larry
Stecumest outplayed Taylor and
Eisendrath, 6-3, 6-4. In the final
doubles competition, transfer A 1
Margot and freshman Wes

Hampton swamped Newquist and
Marshal, 6-0, 6-0.
San Fernando Valley coach
Pat Dixon said he was impressed
by Stanford's depth. Valley State
is the reigning NCAA College
Division team champion. Missing
from yesterday's action were two
notable Indian net stars, Stan
Pasarell and Mac Claflin.
