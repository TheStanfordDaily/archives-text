# Southland Depth Scuttles Net Hopes
## 
### HALL DAILY 
Last weekend, Stanford's
highly touted varsity tennis team
fell before the mighty Bruins of
UCLA and the tenacious Trojans
of USC. The Indians lost to the
Uclans 8-1 and suffered a 6-3
setback from Troy.
On Friday at UCLA's home
courts, Stanford was able to put
away only one match, second
doubles. In singles, Roscoe Tanner
dropped a 6-4, 9-7 decision to Jeff
Borowiak. Tanner had not lost a
singles match yet this season.
Both Rick Fisher and Rick
Evans were beaten in straight sets.
Playing fourth singles Gerry
Groslimond was outlasted by
Steve Cornell 6-3, 8-10, 6-4.
In the fifth slot, Paul
Marienthal lost to Doug Smith
3-6, 6-4, 6-4. Rob Rippner lost to
Ron Cornell 6-1, 6-1.
In doubles action, Tanner and
Rippner were defeated by

Borowiak and Jeff Austin in a
titanic three-setter 4-6, 9-7, 6-1.
The freshmen duo of
Groslimond and Fisher captured
Stanford's lone victory by
overwhelming the Cornell pair
7-5, 14-12.
In the last doubles, Evans and
Mac Claflin lost to Vasquez and
Smith 6-4, 9-7.
Saturday, the Tribe tennis
squad was overwhelmed by USC's
tremendous depth. The Indians
were victorious in only one singles
fray. Tanner whipped top Trojan
Tom Leonard 6-4, 8-6.
Fisher was aced by Eric Van
Dillen 6-4, 7-5. Evans lost his
struggle with Steve Avoyer 6-2,
1-6, 6-3.
USC's George Taylor
continued the string by defeating
Groslimond 6-2, 6-4. Marienthal
was outlasted Mike Machette 6-4,
7-9, 6-2. Rippner also fell before
the SC charge 6-3, 6-3.

Stanford captured two of the
three doubles contests. Tanner
and Rippner garnered victory over
Leonard and Avoyer 1-6, 6-3,6-4.
Van Dillen and Taylor
managed to conquer Fisher and
Groslimond 6-3, 6-3. In third
doubles, Evans and Claflin beat
Dick Bohrnstedt and Fernando
Gentil 2-6, 6-4, 6-3.
Monday, the Indian racketmen
expanded their season record to
8-2 over-all by vanquishing UC
Santa Barbara 7-2. Stanford
exhibited their usual tenacity in
winning all six singles matches
without dropping one set.
Doubles action saw Rob
Daugherty and Bill Atkins in the
second slot with freshmen Wes
Hampton and Jim Healy playing
third.
This Thursday, the Tribe tennis
team faces rugged defending
NAIA National Champs,
Redlands. Saturday will bring Cal
to Stanford's varsity courts for an
important match at 1 o'clock.

fell before the mighty Bruins of
UCLA and the tenacious Trojans
of USC. The Indians lost to the
Uclans 8-1 and suffered a 6-3
setback from Troy.
On Friday at UCLA's home
courts, Stanford was able to put
away only one match, second
doubles. In singles, Roscoe Tanner
dropped a 6-4, 9-7 decision to Jeff
Borowiak. Tanner had not lost a
singles match yet this season.
Both Rick Fisher and Rick
Evans were beaten in straight sets.
Playing fourth singles Gerry
Groslimond was outlasted by
Steve Cornell 6-3, 8-10, 6-4.
In the fifth slot, Paul
Marienthal lost to Doug Smith
3-6, 6-4, 6-4. Rob Rippner lost to
Ron Cornell 6-1, 6-1.
In doubles action, Tanner and
Rippner were defeated by

Borowiak and Jeff Austin in a
titanic three-setter 4-6, 9-7, 6-1.
The freshmen duo of
Groslimond and Fisher captured
Stanford's lone victory by
overwhelming the Cornell pair
7-5, 14-12.
In the last doubles, Evans and
Mac Claflin lost to Vasquez and
Smith 6-4, 9-7.
Saturday, the Tribe tennis
squad was overwhelmed by USC's
tremendous depth. The Indians
were victorious in only one singles
fray. Tanner whipped top Trojan
Tom Leonard 6-4, 8-6.
Fisher was aced by Eric Van
Dillen 6-4, 7-5. Evans lost his
struggle with Steve Avoyer 6-2,
1-6, 6-3.
USC's George Taylor
continued the string by defeating
Groslimond 6-2, 6-4. Marienthal
was outlasted Mike Machette 6-4,
7-9, 6-2. Rippner also fell before
the SC charge 6-3, 6-3.

Stanford captured two of the
three doubles contests. Tanner
and Rippner garnered victory over
Leonard and Avoyer 1-6, 6-3,6-4.
Van Dillen and Taylor
managed to conquer Fisher and
Groslimond 6-3, 6-3. In third
doubles, Evans and Claflin beat
Dick Bohrnstedt and Fernando
Gentil 2-6, 6-4, 6-3.
Monday, the Indian racketmen
expanded their season record to
8-2 over-all by vanquishing UC
Santa Barbara 7-2. Stanford
exhibited their usual tenacity in
winning all six singles matches
without dropping one set.
Doubles action saw Rob
Daugherty and Bill Atkins in the
second slot with freshmen Wes
Hampton and Jim Healy playing
third.
This Thursday, the Tribe tennis
team faces rugged defending
NAIA National Champs,
Redlands. Saturday will bring Cal
to Stanford's varsity courts for an
important match at 1 o'clock.
