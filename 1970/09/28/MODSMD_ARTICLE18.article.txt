U.S. Congressman Charles S.
Gubser, whose district includes
Stanford, will be on campus
September 28 at 4 p.m. in White
Plaza to talk informally with
students. Gubser will also attend
the Monday night meeting of the
Stanford Young Republicans
which will be held at 6 :45 p.m. in
Tresidder Union.

A new lecture course in
Byzantine Art (Art 105 D) will be
taught by Professor Lewis during
fall quarter. The class will meet
Mondays, Wednesdays and
Fridays at 11 a.m. in Room 4 of
the Art Bldg. Class discussions will
center on the art of the capital of
the Eastern Empire at
Constantinople from 330 A.D. to
the Turkish Conquest in 1453
with special emphasis on the
Great Palace, Hagia Sophia and
the Kariye Camii. Special lectures

will be given on parallel
developments in Jewish art,
Coptic, Egypt, Armenia and the
rock-cut churches of Cappadocia.

* * *
Students interested in
participating in the meal associate
program during winter quarter
should sign up for the program
between 8 a.m. and 5 p.m. in the
Old Pavilion on Serra Street
September 28 or 29.
The program is designed to
provide an opportunity for
non-residents of university
residence halls to affiliate with a
specific residence. The purpose of
the program is to bring individuals

representing a wide variety of
viewpoints and backgrounds into
direct contact with residents.
However, persons accepted in the
program are expected to become
active participants.
In making assignments to a
specific residence, attention will be
given to assigning students to
those residences listed as
preferences on the application
form. Selection for the program is
on a random draw basis.
Assignments will be posted in the
upstairs lobby of Tresidder Union
between 10 a.m. and 10 p.m. on
Wednesday, September 3 0.
