# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
WORKERS ACTION CAUCUS will meet today
from 12:15 p.m. to 1 p.m. in TMU 274 to discuss
the effects of NLRB decisions on workers' right to
organize.
* * *
THIRD WORLD STUDENTS UNION will hold
an informational meeting tonight at 8 p.m. in the
International Center. Everyone is invited.
* * *
Classes in effective READING AND STUDY
SKILLS will be offered every Tuesday and
Thursday at 4:15 p.m. and 7 p.m. during fall
quarter. Registration for both classes is at the first
meeting in Room E-15 of the Education Bldg. The
classes are non-credit fee courses $25 for students
and $50 for faculty and staff.
* * *
There are still openings in a 3-unit course on
tutoring, UGS 134 : PRACTICAL EXPERIENCE IN
EDUCATION. The class meets today at 4:15 p.m.
in Art 4.
* * *
WEDNESDAY NIGHT AT THE TUBE: Movies
featuring Hopalong Cassidy, Ozzie and Hariet and
W.C. Fields will be shown Wednesday at 7 :30 p.m.,
9 p.m., and 10:30 p.m. in Cubberly.

The School of Education will present a
colloquium, THE DRUG SCENE: IMPLICATIONS
FOR THE TEACHER, tonight at 7 :30 p.m. in TMU
182.
* * *
Plans for the frosh sponsored BIG GAME
BONFIRE are now being made. An organizational
meeting for all interested freshmen will be held
October 8 at 7:30 p.m. in TMU 271. For more
information, call Kelly Cawley at 491 -31 76 or Vol
VanDalsem at 382-9 637.
* * *

TROUPE will be held October 6 at 10 p.m. in the
Women's Clubhouse. Those interested in
participating as dancers or technical staff for a
concert should attend. For more information, call
328 -4153.
* * *

SOCIETY will be held this evening at 7 :30 p.m. in

Stilwell, pre-med advisor; and Ben Lusk, medical
student, will all be present to talk to members of
the society. Old members as well as frosh, transfer
students and upperclassmen are invited.

An orientation meeting of the PRE-MEDICAL
SOCIETY will be held this evening at 7 :30 p.m. in
the large lounge at Tresidder. Dr. John L. Wilson,
Dean of the Stanford Medical School; Dr. Donald L.
Stilwell, pre-med advisor; and Ben Lusk, medical
student, will all be present to talk to members of
the society. Old members as well as frosh, transfer
students and upperclassmen are invited.
