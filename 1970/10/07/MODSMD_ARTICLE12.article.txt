# Campus Roundup
## 
### 
THE WORKERS ACTION CAUCUS MEETING will be held
today at noon on the third floor of the Clubhouse. All University
workers are urged to come.
* * *
WEDNESDAY NIGHT AT THE TUBE: Movies featuring Hopalong
Cassidy, Ozzie and Harriet, and W.C. Fields will be presented tonight
in Cubberley Auditorium. Showings will be at 7 :30, 9 :00, and 10 :30.
* * *
SKI TEAM will meet tonight at 7:00 p.m. in TMU 271. People
with extensive skiing experience are urged to attend.
* * *
CANADA AND THE DRAFT will be the subject of a program this
afternoon at 4:15 p.m. in the third floor lounge of the Clubhouse.
The Rev. Mr. Richard Killmer will discuss the reasons why more than
4 0 ,000 draft-age American men have moved to Canada, the problems
they face there, and their future relationship with the United States.
* * *
STUDENT MEAL ASSOCIATE PROGRAM: Students interested
in associating on a lunch only basis in Lagunita, Florence Moore,
Stern Hall, Wilbur Hall, and the Row should contact John Grube, ex.
3045. Students who wish to change their meal plan preference should
do so by Friday. Students who have been selected for the program
must buy their meal tickets by 4 :30 p.m. on Friday.
* * *
MEETING OF WHITE STUDENTS to discuss lack of white
response to Black Student Union program offered last week.
Confusion in publicity? Apathy on part of whites? What action should
be taken? Branner Lounge 7 :00 tonight.
* * *
There will be a BARBEQUE for ATO alumni, families, and friends
at the new house after the USC game.
* * *
IN THE YEAR OF THE PIG, the first American feature film on
the Indochina war, will be shown tonight in Memorial Auditorium.
There will be two showings, at 7:30 p.m. and 9:30 p.m. The film is
being presented by Concerned Asian Scholars at Stanford, who are
also holding an open house in the Business School Lounge.
* * *
OPEN PANEL DISCUSSION AND MEETING for all Stanford
women will be held tonight at 7:30 p.m. in Tresidder Large Lounge.
Speakers will include members of Open Spaces Women's Commune,
Stanford Community Day Care Center, and Stanford Women's
Liberation.
* * *
Comrade Sahli of the DEMOCRATIC POPULAR FRONT for the
Liberation of Palestine (Marxist-Leninist) will speak this evening at 8
p.m. in Room 270 of Tresidder Union. Sahli will discuss the position
of the Palestinian people and will speak on features which distinguish
the Democratic Popular Front from other organizations of the
Popular Front.
