Lectures, Recitals Slated
The painter Rembrandt will be the topic of the Leon Sloss Jr.
Memorial Lectures delivered on April 4,5 and 6.
Dr. Julius S. Held, professor emeritus of art history- at Barnard
College, N.Y., will first discuss "Rembrandt and the Spoken
Word," at 3:00 p.m. Sunday, April 4 in Cummings Art
Auditorium.
The authority on Dutch and Flemish painting will speak on
"Rembrandt and the Ancient World" on April 5. This will be
followed by "Rembrandt Problems" on April 6, after which Dr.
Held will lead audience discussion. Both of these lectures will be at
8 :00 p.m. in the Art Auditorium.
Admission is free and the public is welcome at all three talks.
* * *
Jeremy Levin, sophomore, will present a piano recital at 8:00
p.m. Sunday, April 4, in Dinkelspiel Auditorium. He will play
Sonata no. 20 in F Major of Haydn, Partita no. 1 of Bach, Scherzo
no. 1 in B Minor, G Minor Ballade, Nocturne Op.55 no. 2 and
Etude Op.25 no. 1, all by Chopin, and 7th Sonata of Prokofiev.
He will make his New York debut on April 18.
Soprano Melinda Atkins will perform at 8 :00 p.m. tonight in
Dinkelspiel, assisted by an instrumental ensemble.
Admission is free for both recitals.
