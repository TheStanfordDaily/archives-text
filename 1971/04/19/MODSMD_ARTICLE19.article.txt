# Skunked By Uclans
## Racketmen Down Troy, 6-3 
### HALL DAILY 
Stanford's netmen repeated their earlier season
performances against UCLA and USC here last
weekend by dumping Troy, 6-3, and falling before
the Bruins, 9-0.
On coid, windy Friday, the Indians were unable
to score a single point against the defending NCAA
champs from Westwood. In the nine matches, eight
were close with three of them going the full distance
of three sets. The only convincing victory came at
fifth singles as sophomore Bruin Jeff Austin
dropped frosh Paul Sidone, 6-4, 6-2.
In the premier singles attraction, defending
NCAA singles champ repeated his NCAA finals
victory over Roscoe Tanner, 6-3, 3-6, 6-4.
Pakistani Davis Cupper Haroon Rahim defeated
Stanford's courtwise freshman Alex Mayer, 6-4, 7-5.
Top frosh in the nation Jimmy Connors downed
Rick Fisher at third singles, 7-5, 6-4.
In other singles play, Lito Alvarez aced Stan
Pasarell 6-2, 7-6, while Elio Vasquez defeated Gery
Groslimond, 7-5, 3-6, 6-1.
Preview Feature
In what may have been a preview of this year's
NCAA doubles final, Borowiak and Rahim beat
Tanner and Mayer in three sets, 6-4, 4-6, 6-4. In
further pairs action, Austin and Connors outscored
Fisher and Pasarell, 6-3, 7-6, and Vasquez and
Alvarez downed Groslimond and Mac Claflin, 6-4,
6-4.
The loss to the Bruins, Stanford's second in Pac-8
and over-all season play, left the Indians with a 12-2
record entering the second meet of the year with
Trojans on Saturday.

The Tribe's previous meeting with USC had
ended in a 6-3 triumph for the Indians at Los
Angeles. A second victory over Troy would establish
Stanford as number two in the Pac-8 for the first
time in over 13 years.
The first four singles gave the Tribe a 3-1 lead.
Roscoe Tanner drew a default from top Trojan
Marcelo Lara in the second set. Lara dropped the
first set- 6-2, then complaining of a bruised thumb,
defaulted.
Fernando Gentil picked up the first sc point by
beating Groslimond, 6-3, 6-4.
Rick Fisher added another tally, disposing of
Dick Bornstedt, 7-5, 6-4. Senior Stan Pasarell made
it 3-1 with a 6-3, 6-4 win over Mike Machette.
Freshmen Clinch Match
In the two crucial singles matches of the
afternoon, Alex Mayer outpointed highly-rated
Southern Cal senior Tom Leonard, 6-7, 6-2, 6-3.
Paul Sidone clinched the match with a 2-6, 6-3, 6-2
marathon victory over Indian Junior Chajpion Sashi
Menon.
In doubles competition, Tanner and Mayer
whipped Menon and Jim Hobson, 6-2, 6-4. Leonard
and Bornstedt beat Fisher and Pasarell, 7-6, 6-4,
while Gentil and George Taylor defeated Rick Evans
and Claflin, 7-6, 6-4.
Today, Stanford plays an exihibition match
against the La Jolla High School tennis team. Led
by the two top-ranked high school players in the
country, the squad also boasts four other
highly-rated netmen.
