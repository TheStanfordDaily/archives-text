# Plays, Musical Slated To End Spring Term
## 
### 
Students will have a wide range
of dramatic productions to choose
from in the next two weeks as the
quarter ends with a theatrical
nourish.
A kaleidoscope of 14 playlets,
entitled "Limited Engagements,"
will open in the Little Theater at
8:00 p.m. on Wednesday, May 19.
John Olon-Scrymgeour has
selected and directed plays by
Edward Albee, Samuel Barber and
Gian-Carlo Menotti, Samuel
Beckett, Eugene lonesco,
Federico Garcia Lorca, Harold

Pinter, William Saroyan, Murray
Schisgal and Gertrude Stein.
Ricardo Diaz, of the
Department of Communication,
has been commissioned to
produce a film version of Lorca's
"Bitter Dialogue." Hal Peterson of
the Music Department has written
original music for the film.
Newly-written plays by
Rosalyn Drexler, Jeanne Doron,
David Campton, and Stephen
Vinaver round out the evening.
Director Scrymgeour explains,
"because of the brevity of these
plays and their vast technical
demands, they are rarely seen."
The show runs on May 19-22 and
26-29 at 8:00 p.m. with a 2:00
p.m. matinee Saturday, May 22.
"Toad of Toad Hall," a musical
version of "Wind in the Willows,"
is also opening this week. The
Company production will be
shown Thursday, May 20 at
Branner, Friday at Lagunita,
Saturday at Loro. It will also run
on Tuesday, May 25 at Old Union
Courtyard, Wednesday at Grove
House, Thursday at Roble and
Friday at Serra. Admission is free.
"Exit the King," by Eugene
lonesco will play in Memorial
Church from May 19-22 at 8:00
p.m. The Company show is free.
The Company will also present
Antoine de Saint-Exupery's "The
Little Prince." The play will be
shown free in Branner on, May
22-29 at 8:30 p.m.
The Black Drama Workshop
will perform "Experiences in
Black:: II," an evening of three
plays complemented with dance,
on May 27-31 at 8:00 p.m. in the
Art Auditorium.
