# Tolly's Follies
## Off The Farm This Weekend 
### Don Tollefson 
This will be an outstanding weekend for on-campus music with
three performances remaining for the Preservation Hall Jazz Band
and Sunday's big rock concert at Frost. But if you want something
more, there are a number of off-campus events which should provide
enjoyment for almost anybody.
3 For the Show Plus 6 will be presented for the last two times
tonight and tomorrow at 8:30 p.m. at the Palo Alto Children's
Theater, 1305 Middlefield Road. The show features excerpts from a
number of musicals as well as Leonard Bernstein's semi-serious
opera. Trouble in Tahiti. Tickets are $3.00 for adults and $2.00 for
students under 19.
Also in Palo Alto, the Illegitimate Theater will present four
improvisational shows in their next-to-last weekend in Palo Alto.
Shows will be presented tonight and tomorrow night and 9:00 p.m.
and 11:00 p.m. and tickets are $3.00 for adults and $2.50 for
students. After next weekend, the troupe will begin performing at
Ghiradelli Square in San Francisco. The shows will be at the Los
Robles Art Gallery, 520 Ramona Street.
On these same nights, the Hillbarn Theater in Foster City will
present Night Must Fall. The suspense drama will be shown nightly
at 8:30 p.m. Admission is $2.75 for adults and $2.25 for students.
Also in the Fast Bay, the Berkeley Repertory Theater is
presenting The Fantastiks. The musical is presented Tuesday through
Friday at 8:00 p.m. with Saturday night shows at 7:30 and 9:30
p.m. Tickets are $3.00 on weeknights and $4.00 on weekends.
Shakespeare Shows
A couple of Shakespeare productions will be staged in the Bay
Area this weekend. Tonight and Saturday night at 8:00 p.m. the
College of Marin will present A Midsummer Night's Dream. Tickets
are $2.00 for adults and 75 cents for students.
And in Cupertino, the New Shakespeare Company at De Anza
College will perform As You Like It, also tonight at 8:00 p.m.
Tickets are $2.50 for adults $1.50 for students.
If free dramatic productions interest you, there will be at least
two of note this weekend in San Francisco. Tonight and tomorrow
night at the Intersection, 756 Union Street, there will be a satirical
revue presented by the Pitschel Players.
Tonight at 8:00 p.m. at the Bethany United Methodist Church,
the San Francisco State College Barter Theater will perform
Christopher Fry's Sleep of Prisoners. Admission to this is also free.
Musical Things
Moving into the realm of music, the City again provides good free
entertainment this weekend. On Sunday afternoon at Stern Grove,
Sloat Blvd. and 19th Ave. the Preservation Hall Jazz Band will give a
free concert. The show starts at 2.00 p.m. but many people come
early and bring a picnic lunch.
At In Your Far in Palo Alto, Bola Sete and The Gypsy Band will
be featured this weekend. Both groups are jazz oriented and both
will appear six times with shows tonight and Saturday night at 9:00
and 11:30 p.m. and on Sunday at 3:00 and 8:00 p.m. There is a
$2.00 cover charge and you must be 21 or older, except at the
Sunday afternoon show.
Bill Graham is presenting two big shows at the Berkeley
Community Theater this weekend. Tomorrow night, Lee Michaels,
Brewer & Shipley and Jackie Lomaz will appear, with Emerson,
Lake & Palmer and Edgar Winter scheduled for Sunday night. Both
shows will begin at 8:00 p.m. and tickets are $4.50 in advance and
$5.50 at the door.

In a more classical vein, the 34th annual Carmel Bach Festival
begins tonight. All shows except morning recitals on the 17th, 19th,
24th and one afternoon recital on the 19th are sold out. Tickets for
the recitals are $3.00 and the morning concerts begin at 11:00 a.m.
with the one afternoon one at 3:00 p.m.
Tomorrow night, the Lenox Quartet will play at the Montalvo
Music Festival in Saratoga. The concert is scheduled for 8:30 p.m. in
the Carriage House Theater at the Villa Montalvo Cultural Center.
Ticket information is available by calling (408) 867-3421.
Back on campus, The Bridge Over The River Kwai, will be shown
Sunday night in Cubberley at 7:00 p.m. and 9:45 p.m.
And on Monday night the Philosophical Film Series will present
International House with W.C. Fields, Grade Allen, George Burns,
Bella Lugois and Rudi Vallee. It will also be in Cubberley at 7:00,
9:00 and 11:00 p.m. with tickets available for $ 1.00.
Finally the Joan Baez concert mentioned in Tuesday's column
will be on Saturday July 24th rather that Sunday July 25th as
previously announced.

Sloat Blvd. and 19th Ave. the Preservation Hall Jazz Band will give a
free concert. The show starts at 2.00 p.m. but many people come
early and bring a picnic lunch.
At In Your Far in Palo Alto, Bola Sete and The Gypsy Band will
be featured this weekend. Both groups are jazz oriented and both
will appear six times with shows tonight and Saturday night at 9:00
and 11:30 p.m. and on Sunday at 3:00 and 8:00 p.m. There is a
$2.00 cover charge and you must be 21 or older, except at the
Sunday afternoon show.
Bill Graham is presenting two big shows at the Berkeley
Community Theater this weekend. Tomorrow night, Lee Michaels,
Brewer & Shipley and Jackie Lomaz will appear, with Emerson,
Lake & Palmer and Edgar Winter scheduled for Sunday night. Both
shows will begin at 8:00 p.m. and tickets are $4.50 in advance and
$5.50 at the door.

In a more classical vein, the 34th annual Carmel Bach Festival
begins tonight. All shows except morning recitals on the 17th, 19th,
24th and one afternoon recital on the 19th are sold out. Tickets for
the recitals are $3.00 and the morning concerts begin at 11:00 a.m.
with the one afternoon one at 3:00 p.m.
Tomorrow night, the Lenox Quartet will play at the Montalvo
Music Festival in Saratoga. The concert is scheduled for 8:30 p.m. in
the Carriage House Theater at the Villa Montalvo Cultural Center.
Ticket information is available by calling (408) 867-3421.
Back on campus, The Bridge Over The River Kwai, will be shown
Sunday night in Cubberley at 7:00 p.m. and 9:45 p.m.
And on Monday night the Philosophical Film Series will present
International House with W.C. Fields, Grade Allen, George Burns,
Bella Lugois and Rudi Vallee. It will also be in Cubberley at 7:00,
9:00 and 11:00 p.m. with tickets available for $ 1.00.
Finally the Joan Baez concert mentioned in Tuesday's column
will be on Saturday July 24th rather that Sunday July 25th as
previously announced.

ROCK OUT—The first summer rock concert at Frost Ampitheater this
Sunday will feature Elvin Bishop, Sopwith Camel and Lamb, as well as
Cold Blood (above). Tickets at $2.00 will be available at the gate and
the show begins at 1:00 p.m. Preservation Hall Jazz Band will also be
playing on campus this weekend. Tickets for tonight's shows at 8:00
and 10:00 p.m. and tomorrow night's show at 8:00 p.m. are scarce,
but some may be available at Dinkelspiel Auditorium.
This will be an outstanding weekend for on-campus music with
three performances remaining for the Preservation Hall Jazz Band
and Sunday's big rock concert at Frost. But if you want something
more, there are a number of off-campus events which should provide
enjoyment for almost anybody.
3 For the Show Plus 6 will be presented for the last two times
tonight and tomorrow at 8:30 p.m. at the Palo Alto Children's
Theater, 1305 Middlefield Road. The show features excerpts from a
number of musicals as well as Leonard Bernstein's semi-serious
opera. Trouble in Tahiti. Tickets are $3.00 for adults and $2.00 for
students under 19.
Also in Palo Alto, the Illegitimate Theater will present four
improvisational shows in their next-to-last weekend in Palo Alto.
Shows will be presented tonight and tomorrow night and 9:00 p.m.
and 11:00 p.m. and tickets are $3.00 for adults and $2.50 for
students. After next weekend, the troupe will begin performing at
Ghiradelli Square in San Francisco. The shows will be at the Los
Robles Art Gallery, 520 Ramona Street.
On these same nights, the Hillbarn Theater in Foster City will
present Night Must Fall. The suspense drama will be shown nightly
at 8:30 p.m. Admission is $2.75 for adults and $2.25 for students.
Also in the Fast Bay, the Berkeley Repertory Theater is
presenting The Fantastiks. The musical is presented Tuesday through
Friday at 8:00 p.m. with Saturday night shows at 7:30 and 9:30
p.m. Tickets are $3.00 on weeknights and $4.00 on weekends.
Shakespeare Shows
A couple of Shakespeare productions will be staged in the Bay
Area this weekend. Tonight and Saturday night at 8:00 p.m. the
College of Marin will present A Midsummer Night's Dream. Tickets
are $2.00 for adults and 75 cents for students.
And in Cupertino, the New Shakespeare Company at De Anza
College will perform As You Like It, also tonight at 8:00 p.m.
Tickets are $2.50 for adults $1.50 for students.
If free dramatic productions interest you, there will be at least
two of note this weekend in San Francisco. Tonight and tomorrow
night at the Intersection, 756 Union Street, there will be a satirical
revue presented by the Pitschel Players.
Tonight at 8:00 p.m. at the Bethany United Methodist Church,
the San Francisco State College Barter Theater will perform
Christopher Fry's Sleep of Prisoners. Admission to this is also free.
