# Tolly's Follies
## Good Music At Cal 
### don tollefson 
Large university campuses are
natural places for a large number
of varied entertainment events.
Even though the Stanford Sum
mer Festival is no longer, this
campus has still had some fine
summer entertainment this quar
ter, ala Bob and Ray and Preser
vation Hall.
The same can be said for the
Berkeley campus of the University
of California where a great num
ber of top entertainment events
have been and will continue to be
presented this summer. Especially
outstanding have been the dance
programs which have already
brought the Alvin Ailey and
Merce Cunningham companies
and which will present Cunning
ham again next weekend and later
in August, the Joffrey Ballet.
But this weekend the spotlight
at Cal falls on classical music.
Three concerts of note will be

given this weekend. Tonight at
8:00 p.m. in Hertz Hall, the
widely acclaimed Lenox Quartet
will perform works of Haydn,
Bartok and Brahms. Tickets have
been selling quickly but should
still be available at the door.
Admission is $3.00 general and
$1.50 for students.
On Sunday afternoon there will
be a concert of Indian music at
2:30 p.m. in the Hearst Greek
Theatre. Sitarist Nikhil Banerjee,
labia player Kanai Dutta and a
tamboura player will be the
featured performers. Tickets are
$1.00 for the general public and
50c for students.
Then on Sunday night again at
8:00 p.m. in Hertz Hall there will
be an organ concert featuring
Edwin Flath. Flath will perform
works of Bach and Messiaen.
Tickets for this will be $2.00
general and $1.00 for students.
The bluegrass team of Vern and
Ray will be appearing tonight and
tomorrow night at 9:00 p.m. at
The Tangent. Admission is $1.50.
And tonight at 9:00 and 11:00
p.m. the Illegitimate Theater will

perform for the last time in Palo
Alto before moving to Ghiradelli
Square in San Francisco. Tickets
are $3.00 for adults and $2.50 for
students with the shows at the
Los Robles Art Gallery, 520
Ramona Street.
On campus, tonight's Com
munications Dept. Film Series
movie, Wages of Fear will be
shown once only at 8:00 p.m. in
Cubberley Auditorium Admission
is $1.00. Tomorrow Joan Baez
will perform in Frost Ampitheater
at 3:00 p.m. Tickets for this are
$2.00.
Tomorrow night at 7:00 and
9:30 p.m. in Cubberley, the
Stanford Forensic Union will
present The Flim-Flam Man star
ring George C. Scott. Admission is
$1.00. Also, that night the Mecha
film series is presenting The Marx
Brothers in the film, At The
Circus, in Bishop Auditorium in
the Biz School, at 8:00 and 9:40
p.m.
On Monday night in Cubberley,
the Philosophical Film Series
movie will be Psycho. Shows are
7:30 and 9:45 p.m. and tickets
are $1.00.

$1.50 for students.

$1.00 for the general public and
50c for students.

Edwin Flath. Flath will perform
