# Tolly's Follies
## Bring Gus A Bone 
### 
A few thousand students will
make their annual migration down
to Los Angeles this weekend for
the USC football game, but just
like last weekend, those staying
up here won't be lacking things to
do.
A voice concert, an appearance
by the San Francisco Mime
Troupe, a birthday party for a dog
and the usual number of movies
fill up this weekend's campus
entertainment schedule.
One quick note for those of
you going to LA. There will be an
all-nite party following the game
Saturday night at the Central
Produce Market at 11th and San
Pedro in downtown Los Angeles.
Buses will leave from the Beta
Fraternity house on the USC
campus for the market following
the game.
Back on campus, tonight's
Volunteers in Asia film will be
Hiroshima, Mon Amour. It will be
shown at 7:30 and 9:30 p.m. in
Bishop Auditorium and admission
will be $1. Fellini's Satyricon will
be screened by the Contemporary
Cinema Society tonight at 7:30
and 10 p.m. in Memorial
Auditorium. Admission will also
be $1.
The final movies being shown
tonight will be Film and Chafed
Elbows, which will be screened in
Cubberley Auditorium at 7:30
and 9:30 p.m. Tickets again go for
$1. The ASSU Sunday Flick this
week will be West Side Story. It
will be shown in Memorial
Auditorium at 7 and 9:45 on
Sunday night. Admission is 50
cents.
Mime Troupe
Tomorrow night at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium, the
Association of Young Crows will
present the San Francisco Mime
Troupe in a performance of The
Dragon Lady's Revenge.
Admission will be $1.50.
Also on Saturday night at 8
p.m., there will be an all-campus
birthday party at Beta Chi for Gus
the Dog. All students are invited
and there is no admission, but try

to bring a bone for Gus.
On Sunday night at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium, there will
be a vocal recital by soprano
Evelyn Lear and baritone Tom
Stewart. The husband and wife
team from the Metropolitan
Opera will perform works by
Purcell, Handel, Haydn, Schubert,
Mozart and others. Admission is
$4.50 general and $3.75 for
students.
Up at the University of
California in Berkeley, there will
also be quite a lot going on this
weekend. The ETC La Mama/New
York continues their run with
performances tonight and Monday
night of Gertrude (Or Would She
be Pleased to Receive it) and
Demon (The Damask Drum).
Both performances will be at 8
p.m. in Zellerbach Auditorium
with ticket prices ranging from
$1.50 to $4.50 for the general
public and from $1 to $3.50 for
students.
Joan Baez will be appearing
tonight and tomorrow night at
8:30 p.m. at Cal's Greek Theater.
Tickets are priced at $2. Both
shows are sold out.
Classical Music
Tomorrow night at 8 p.m. in
Cal's Hertz Hall, the Vienna
Chamber Orchestra will perform
works of Mozart and Haydn.
Tickets are $4 general and $2.50
for students. Sunday night in
Hertz at 8 p.m., organist Anton
Heiller will perform an all-Bach
program. Tickets are $3 general
and $1.50 for students.
Moving out of the academic
realm, there is also a lot of good
music going on elsewhere in the
Bay Area this weekend. The
Credence Clearwater Revival show
tomorrow night at the Oakland
Coliseum is sold out, as is the
David Crosby —Graham Nash
show at Flint Center in Cupertino.
Sunday night.
Tickets were going fast for
tonight's Crosby—Nash show at
the Berkeley Community Theater,
but it was not sold out at last
report. Judee Sill will also be on
the bill and tickets for the 8 p.m.
show are priced at $3.50, $4.50
and $5.50. To check on the latest
ticket information call 845-2308.
Gordon Lightfoot will be
appearing tomorrow night at the
San Jose Civic Auditorium and
Sunday night at the Berkeley
Community Theater. Both shows
begin at 8 p.m. and tickets are
priced at $3, $4 and $5.
Sonny & Cher
At the Circle Star Theater in
San Carlos, Sonny and Cher and
Hamilton, Joe Frank & Reynolds
will be featured this weekend.
There will be performances
tonight and tomorrow night at
7:30 and 10:30 p.m. and on
Sunday at 3 and 7:30 p.m. Ticket
prices range from a low of $3.50
for the cheapest seats on Sunday
afternoon to $7.50 for the most
expensive seats on a weekend
night.
Tonight only at Winterland in
San Francisco, Pink Floyd will be
appearing at 8 p.m. Tickets are
$4.
The Broln Czechoslovakian
Folk Dance Company will be in
the Bay Area this weekend.
Tonight at 8:30 p.m. and Sunday
at 2:30 p.m. they will be at the
Masonic Auditorium in San
Francisco and tomorrow night at
8:30 p.m. they will be at Flint
Center in Cupertino. Ticket prices
range from $3.50 to $6.50.

highlights. Gus the Dog (left) will be celebrating his birthday at an all-campus party tomorrow night at

A few thousand students will
make their annual migration down
to Los Angeles this weekend for
the USC football game, but just
like last weekend, those staying
up here won't be lacking things to
do.
A voice concert, an appearance
by the San Francisco Mime
Troupe, a birthday party for a dog
and the usual number of movies
fill up this weekend's campus
entertainment schedule.
One quick note for those of
you going to LA. There will be an
all-nite party following the game
Saturday night at the Central
Produce Market at 11th and San
Pedro in downtown Los Angeles.
Buses will leave from the Beta
Fraternity house on the USC
campus for the market following
the game.
Back on campus, tonight's
Volunteers in Asia film will be
Hiroshima, Mon Amour. It will be
shown at 7:30 and 9:30 p.m. in
Bishop Auditorium and admission
will be $1. Fellini's Satyricon will
be screened by the Contemporary
Cinema Society tonight at 7:30
and 10 p.m. in Memorial
Auditorium. Admission will also
be $1.
The final movies being shown
tonight will be Film and Chafed
Elbows, which will be screened in
Cubberley Auditorium at 7:30
and 9:30 p.m. Tickets again go for
$1. The ASSU Sunday Flick this
week will be West Side Story. It
will be shown in Memorial
Auditorium at 7 and 9:45 on
Sunday night. Admission is 50
cents.
Mime Troupe
Tomorrow night at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium, the
Association of Young Crows will
present the San Francisco Mime
Troupe in a performance of The
Dragon Lady's Revenge.
Admission will be $1.50.
Also on Saturday night at 8
p.m., there will be an all-campus
birthday party at Beta Chi for Gus
the Dog. All students are invited
and there is no admission, but try

to bring a bone for Gus.
On Sunday night at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium, there will
be a vocal recital by soprano
Evelyn Lear and baritone Tom
Stewart. The husband and wife
team from the Metropolitan
Opera will perform works by
Purcell, Handel, Haydn, Schubert,
Mozart and others. Admission is
$4.50 general and $3.75 for
students.
Up at the University of
California in Berkeley, there will
also be quite a lot going on this
weekend. The ETC La Mama/New
York continues their run with
performances tonight and Monday
night of Gertrude (Or Would She
be Pleased to Receive it) and
Demon (The Damask Drum).
Both performances will be at 8
p.m. in Zellerbach Auditorium
with ticket prices ranging from
$1.50 to $4.50 for the general
public and from $1 to $3.50 for
students.
Joan Baez will be appearing
tonight and tomorrow night at
8:30 p.m. at Cal's Greek Theater.
Tickets are priced at $2. Both
shows are sold out.
Classical Music
Tomorrow night at 8 p.m. in
Cal's Hertz Hall, the Vienna
Chamber Orchestra will perform
works of Mozart and Haydn.
Tickets are $4 general and $2.50
for students. Sunday night in
Hertz at 8 p.m., organist Anton
Heiller will perform an all-Bach
program. Tickets are $3 general
and $1.50 for students.
Moving out of the academic
realm, there is also a lot of good
music going on elsewhere in the
Bay Area this weekend. The
Credence Clearwater Revival show
tomorrow night at the Oakland
Coliseum is sold out, as is the
David Crosby —Graham Nash
show at Flint Center in Cupertino.
Sunday night.
Tickets were going fast for
tonight's Crosby—Nash show at
the Berkeley Community Theater,
but it was not sold out at last
report. Judee Sill will also be on
the bill and tickets for the 8 p.m.
show are priced at $3.50, $4.50
and $5.50. To check on the latest
ticket information call 845-2308.
Gordon Lightfoot will be
appearing tomorrow night at the
San Jose Civic Auditorium and
Sunday night at the Berkeley
Community Theater. Both shows
begin at 8 p.m. and tickets are
priced at $3, $4 and $5.
Sonny & Cher
At the Circle Star Theater in
San Carlos, Sonny and Cher and
Hamilton, Joe Frank & Reynolds
will be featured this weekend.
There will be performances
tonight and tomorrow night at
7:30 and 10:30 p.m. and on
Sunday at 3 and 7:30 p.m. Ticket
prices range from a low of $3.50
for the cheapest seats on Sunday
afternoon to $7.50 for the most
expensive seats on a weekend
night.
Tonight only at Winterland in
San Francisco, Pink Floyd will be
appearing at 8 p.m. Tickets are
$4.
The Broln Czechoslovakian
Folk Dance Company will be in
the Bay Area this weekend.
Tonight at 8:30 p.m. and Sunday
at 2:30 p.m. they will be at the
Masonic Auditorium in San
Francisco and tomorrow night at
8:30 p.m. they will be at Flint
Center in Cupertino. Ticket prices
range from $3.50 to $6.50.
