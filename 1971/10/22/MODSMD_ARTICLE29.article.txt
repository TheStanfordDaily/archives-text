# Tolly's Follies
## Much More Music 
### Don Tollefson 
In a big musical weekend, the classical sound is featured
on-campus, while rock and jazz sounds dominate the off-campus
highlights. Movies are as saturated as ever on campus, but drama
suffers everywhere from an early season light schedule.
Tonight at 8 p.m. in Dinkelspiel Auditorium, senior music major
Sylvie Ollivier will give a piano recital featuring works of Brahms,
Beethoven, Mozart, Mussorgsky and Blach. The concert is free.
Sunday afternoon, university organist Herbert Nanney will give a
free recital in Dinkelspiel beginning at 3 p.m. Nanney will perform a
program of Renaissance and Baroque music including works by Bach,
Boehm, Buxtehude, Muffat and Krebs.
In the only other non-cinematic on-campus event of the weekend,
the San Francisco Free Theater will perform William Russo's The
Bacchae tomorrow night at 8 and 10 p.m., again in Dinkelspiel.
Tickets are priced at $ 1.5 0.
Tonight the Volunteers in Asia Film Series will present Breathless
at 7:30 and 9:30 p.m. in Bishop Auditorium. Admission to this
Jean-Luc Godard directed film which stars Jean-Paul Belmondo and
Jean Seberg, will be $1.
Tonight also at 7 :30 and 9:30 p.m. in Cubberley Auditorium, the
Beta Chi Film Series will feature Bruce Bailee's Quick Billy and David
Holzman's Diary. Admission will be a buck.
Tomorrow night the Contemporary Cinema Society will show
Tricia's Wedding and Belle de Jour. The Cockettes are the stars of
Tricia's Wedding while Catherine Deneuve stars in Belle de
Jour. Tickets go for a hundred pennies.
The ASSU Sunday Flick will be MASH. It will be shown along with
a short, The Further Adventures of Uncle Sam, at 7 :00 and 9:4 5 p.m.
in Memorial Auditorium. Admission is 50cents.
Moving out into the big wide world, we find a lot of good shows,
especially musical ones. Locally, the Vince Guaraldi Quartet will be at
In Your Ear through Sunday night. Tonight and tomorrow night's
shows begin at 9 p.m., with the Sunday show starting at 8 p.m.
Admission is $2.50 and minors are admitted only on Sunday nights.
Also on Sunday night at 8 p.m., Donovan will be at the San
Francisco Civic Auditorium. Tickets for this show are priced at
$3.50-$6.50. James Brown will be at the San Francisco Civic
Auditorium tomorrow night, at the San Jose Civic Auditorium
Sunday night and at the Berkeley Community Theater on Monday
night. For ticket information call the separate auditoriums.
Tonight and tomorrow night, Dave Van Ronk will be at the Lion's
Share in San Anselmo. He will give shows at 9 and 11 p.m. each night.
There will be a $2.50 cover charge. On Sunday night, Van Ronk will
be at the Bear's Lair in Berkeley.
The EST Company's production of Tommy closes
out its regular run October 30 at the Friends and Relations Hall
on the beach in San Francisco. Two extra shows, have been added
tomorrow night at 10:45 and next Saturday night at 10:45. Tickets
are $3.50 and a student rush ticket for the last regular performances
will go for $2.50.
The big event at Cal this weekend, will be an appearance by the
Black Light Theatre of Prague. The troupe is on their debut tour of
North American and will appear in Zellerbach Auditorium tonight and
tomorrow night at 8 p.m. Tickets are scaled from $1.50 to $4.50 for
the public and from $1 to $3.50 for students.
Two final events up in the City deserve mention. Tonight at 9 p.m.
at Project Artaud People's Hall, 2800 Mariposa Street, City Lights
presents Andrei Voznesensky with translations read by Lawrence
Ferlinghetti. Tickets are $2.
Also up in San Francisco, the Lamplighters continue to present
lolanthe at the Presentation Theatre. There will be performances
tonight and tomorrow night at 8 :30 p.m. and on Sunday at 2 :30 p.m.
Tickets are $3.50 and the theater is located at 2350 Turk Street.
