TO: Senators Cranston and Tunney; Representative McCloskey; The San Francisco Chronicle; The Palo Alto
Times; and The Stanford Daily.
The undersigned administrators, faculty, students, and staff at Stanford Law School wish to express our outrage at the current

We call simply for cessation of bombing throughout Vietnam and for resumption of peace negotiations in Paris.
Candidates for public office in the upcoming primary and general elections should be aware of the deep concern many Californians
share about this tragic re-escalation of the Indo-China war.

321 MEMBERS OF THE STANFORD
LAW SCHOOL COMMUNITY EXPRESS
OUTRAGE AT THE NIXON BOMBING.
TO: Senators Cranston and Tunney; Representative McCloskey; The San Francisco Chronicle; The Palo Alto
Times; and The Stanford Daily.
The undersigned administrators, faculty, students, and staff at Stanford Law School wish to express our outrage at the current
massive bombing attacks by the American government in Vietnam. We depiore this senseless renewal of brutality upon a people
entrapped in a political, military, and moral vacuum not of their making.
We urge political spokesmen and those who mold public opinion to dissociate themselves immediately and unequivocally from the
present policy of terror and destruction. This appeal transcends party loyalty or political faction.
We call simply for cessation of bombing throughout Vietnam and for resumption of peace negotiations in Paris.
Candidates for public office in the upcoming primary and general elections should be aware of the deep concern many Californians
share about this tragic re-escalation of the Indo-China war.
