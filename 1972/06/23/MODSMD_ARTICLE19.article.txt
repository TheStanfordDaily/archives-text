# The 						Stanford Daily
## Entertainment Happening: This Week Symphony Meets Diller 
### 
"Happenings'' is,
unfortunately, not a complete
listing of all local activities in the
arts. However, we try to include
as much information as possible in
hopes of having something of
interest for everyone.
We solicit listings for the
column, which will be a
semi-regular feature of the Daily.
Please address all communications
to Rich Jaroslovsky,
entertainment editor, in care of
the Stanford Daily, Storke
Building, Stanford, California,
94305.
ONGOING HAPPENINGS
"THE ROTHSCHILDS," the
current musical production of the
San Francisco Civic Light Opera,
closes tomorrow night at the
Curran Theater in the City.
THE TEMPTATIONS close
their act Sunday night at the
Circle Star in San Carlos. Also on
the bill are the Honey Cone and
the Robinson Family Plan.
Tickets: $7.50-$3.75.
"SLEUTH," a production of
the American Conservatory Theater
(ACT), continues its run through
July 1 at the Geary Theater.
Tickets are still available. Student
Rush Tickets $3.
PRISON LIFE photographs by
Danny Lyon and drawings by
Billy McCune are part of
"Conversations with the Dead,"
currently on display at 1952
Union Street in San Francisco.
There is no charge for admission.
The showing is co-sponsored by
Palo Alto's Institute for the Study
of Nonviolence.
JOHN LEE HOOKER and
Banana and the Bunch (of the
Youngbloods) will be at the
Lion's Share in San Ansel mo,
Marin County, through tomorrow
night.
THE BERKELEY
REPERTORY THEATRE
continues its production of "She
Stoops to Conquer." Opening
June 30: Moliere's "The
Imaginary Invalid." At the
playhouse, 2890 College Ave.
TODAY
MICHAEL LORIMER tonight
at Dinkelspiel Auditorium on
campus, at 8 p.m.
CHEECH AND CHONG and
the Elvin Bishop Group, two
shows tonight (7:30 and 10 p.m.),
at Memorial Auditorium on
campus. Tickets $3.50 at the
door, $2.50 in advance.
THE AMPHION ENSEMBLE,
8 p.m., Hertz Hall on the campus
of UC-Berkeley. Tickets $2.50,
students $1.50.
SHAN O'CASEY's "The
Shadow of a Gunman" at Mercy
High, 3250-19 th Avenue in San
Francisco, opens tonight at 8:30
p.m.

SHAKESPEARE'S "A
Midsummer Night Dream," Old
Town Theater in Los Altos, opens
tonight at 8:30 p.m.
SATURDAY
MUSIC AT THE
VINEYARDS, concert today at
3:30 p.m. at Paul Masson
Vineyards in Saratoga, Ca.
Another concert tomorrow.
SUNDAY
SIX PERFORMERS from the
San Francisco Opera's Dollar
Opera Season will appear at the
Windsor Winery near Santa Rosa
today at 2 p.m. Cost is $5.
THREE COLLEGE JAZZ
BANDS will give a free concert at
San Francisco's Sigmund Stern
Grove at 2 p.m. The Grove is at
the intersection of 19th Avenue
and Sloat. Colleges represented
include Valley State, Cabrillo
College, and the University of
Nevada.
THE SAN JOSE BOYS'
CHORUS at De Anza College,
8:15 p.m. Tickets are $1.
THE FINE ARTS QUARTET
UC-Berkeley's Hertz Hall at 8
p.m. Ticket's $2.50 general, $1.50
students. Also Tuesday night.
TUESDAY
"ROMANCE AND REALITY"
is tonight's episode of the
Civilization television program to
be shown at Cubberley
Auditorium at 8 p.m. Admission
is free.
CHAMBER MUSIC WITH
BAROQUE GUITAR at 8 p.m. at
Dinkelspiel Auditorium.
Admission is $2 general, $1
students. Robert Strizich directs.
WEDNESDAY
PHYLLIS DILLER A
SOLOIST with the San Francisco
Symphony (would I lie to you?),
8 :30 p.m. at the Opera House in
S.F. Skitch Henderson is guest

conductor. Tickets $6.50-$4 from
the Opera House box office.
THURSDAY
LUTENISTS Stanley Butens,
Joan Myers, Peter Danner, and
George Olczak at Dinkelspiel, 8
p.m., tickets $2 general and $1
students.
"CIVILISATION," screens
"Man, Measure of All Things" at
Cubberley Auditorium at 8 p.m.
There is no charge.
PHYLLIS DILLER brings her
Symphony concert to Flint
Center at DeAnza College in
Cupertino at 8:15 p.m. Tickets
available from the Flint Box
Office, $3.50-$6.50.

closes tomorrow night at the
Curran Theater in the City.
THE TEMPTATIONS close
their act Sunday night at the
Circle Star in San Carlos. Also on
the bill are the Honey Cone and
the Robinson Family Plan.
Tickets: $7.50-$3.75.
"SLEUTH," a production of
the American Conservatory Theater
(ACT), continues its run through
July 1 at the Geary Theater.
Tickets are still available. Student
Rush Tickets $3.
PRISON LIFE photographs by
Danny Lyon and drawings by
Billy McCune are part of
"Conversations with the Dead,"
currently on display at 1952
Union Street in San Francisco.
There is no charge for admission.
The showing is co-sponsored by
Palo Alto's Institute for the Study
of Nonviolence.

night.
