# Dance Group Opens Season
## 
### 
The Inner City Repertory
Dance Company will appear at
Memorial Auditorium tomorrow
and Friday as the first event in the
Stanford Office of Public Events
season.
And the Stanford Department
of Music will sponsor the first of
six campus appearances by the
Francesco Trio chamber ensemble
next Sunday, Oct. 8.
The Inner City company will
have one show each on Oct. 5 and

6 at 8 p.m. Tickets are on sale, if
still available, at the Tresidder
Ticket Office. Admission is
$2.75-$4.75.
The Francesco Trio will appear
at Dinkelspiel Auditorium at 3
p.m. Admission is non-reserved,
$3 general and $1 student.
The Inner City company
performance is the first in a long
series of events planned for the
forthcoming year by the Office of
Public Events. Among

forthcoming events are:
—the New York Chamber
Soloists at Dinkelspiel
Auditorium, Oct. 13 and 24 at 8
p.m.;
—the Modern Jazz Quartet,
Oct. 20, 8 p.m. at Mem Aud;
—Emlyn Williams in "Dylan
Thomas Growing Up," Nov. 14 at
Dinkelspiel;
— Harp si c hordist-organist
Anthony Newman at Dinkelspiel,
Jan. 26;
—"The Ballad of Sacco and
Vanzetti" at Dinkelspiel, Feb. 6;
—Prof. Peter Schickele with his
"The Intimate P.D.Q. Bach," Feb.
23 at Mem Aud.
The Stanford Drama
Department has also announced
its first production of the year,
Henrik Ibsen's "Ghosts." The
show will run Oct. 24-28, and
Oct. 31-Nov. 4, at the Little
Theater in Memorial Auditorium.
Tickets for "Ghosts"
performances on Friday and
Saturday evenings are $3 general,
$2 students. Other performances
are $2.50/sl.
Later Drama productions will
include Shakespeare's "Twelfth
Night," George Farquhar's
"Beaux' Stratagem," and
Euripides' "Orestes."
