# Big Daddy Serves Up Weekly Menu
## 
### DAVE McCULLUM 
MONDAY
Iximb stew, green salad, fresh
fruit, beverage
Lamb Stew
2 pounds stewing lamb
Vi pound carrots
6 small potatoes
2 cloves of garlic
V2 pound peas
2 onions
Slice the onions and crush the
garlic. Cut meat into cubes and
brown in a little fat. Add the
onion and garlic and brown. Add
water to cover, salt and pepper to
taste, and simmer on a low flame
until tender. About 3 A of an hour
before end of cooking time, add

the potatoes, carrots and peas and
a little more water if necessary.
TUESDAY
Savory liver, steamed rice, leaf
spinach, tomato and cucumber
salad, caramel custard, beverage
Savory Liver
4 servings
1 pound liver
french dressing
flour seasoned with salt and
pepper
2 tablespoons fat
1 green pepper, sliced
1 medium onion, sliced
1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
'/2 cup water
Cut the liver into small pieces.
Marinate for several hours in the

French dressing, then sprinkle
with seasoned flour and brown in
hot fat. Remove from pan and
add green pepper and onion to
fat, adding a little more fat if
needed. Brown lightly. Add
Worcestershire sauce, water, and
stir until combined and thickened
slightly. Season to taste then add
liver long enough to heat.
WEDNESDAY
Chicken cacciatore, spaghetti,
fresh kale, lettuce and green
pepper salad, fresh fruit, beverage
Chicken Cacciatore
4-5 servings
Use a 2Vi to 3'/2 pound chicken
disjointed. Brown chicken in Vi
cup fat. Add 1 medium onion,
sliced thin and 1 clove garlic,
minced and cook until onion is
golden. Add 1 can tomatoes, V/ 2
teaspoons salt, Vi teaspoos
pepper, V2 teaspoon oregano and
cover. Simmer 40 to 50 minutes
until chicken is tender and sauce
thickened. Add V2 cup white wine
the last 15 minutes of cooking.
THURSDAY
New England boiled dinner,
radishes and ripe olives, fruit
compote
New England Boiled dinner
6 servings
3 pounds corned beef
6 medium turnips, whole
6 medium onions, whole
4 potatoes, quartered
6 medium carrots, whole
1 head cabbage, cut in wedges
1/8 teaspoon pepper
Place meat in a kettle, cover
and simmer 3 hours or until
tender. One hour before serving

time add turnips and onions.
When these have cooked 15
minutes add potatoes, carrots
and cabbage. Sprinkle with
pepper. Cover and cook 45
minutes longer. Serve with
horse-radish sauce made by
combining V2 cup salad dressing, l A
cup whipped cream, V2 teaspoon
salt and 2 tablespoons prepared
horseradish.
FRIDAY
Baked stuffed Fish, scalloped
tomatoes, succotash, lemon
meringue pie, beverage
Baked Stuffed Fish
6 pounds dressed fish
salt
3 A cup butter or margarine
6 cups toasted bread crumbs
4 tablespoons lemon juice
3 tablespoons chopped onion
2 tablespoons capers
2 tablespoons parsley flakes
3 A teaspoon each, salt, celery seed,
thyme, marjoram
V2 teaspoon pepper
Wash the fish. Pat dry with
paper towels. Sprinkle inside and
out with salt. Melt butter and toss
lightly together with remaining
ingredients. Stuff fish loosely and
close with skewers and string.
Place fish on a shallow greased
baking dish. Brush with melted
fat. Bake for 1 .to IV2 hours in a
350 degree oven, basting
occasionally with melted fat.
SATURDAY
Round steak with vegetables,
steamed rice, green salad, ice
cream, beverage
Round Steak with Vegetables
2 pounds round steak

flour
1 teaspoon salt
8 carrot strips
8 celery strips
4 dill pickles, cut in half
lengthwise
3 tablespoons fat
1 cup beef bouillon
l A teaspoon pepper
l A teaspoon onion salt
Cut steak in 8 pieces. Pound in
flour with edge of heavy saucer.
Sprinkle on salt. Lay 1 strip each
of carrot, celery and dill pickle on
each piece. Fold meat in half.
Fasten with pick. Brown meat in
hot fat in a heavy skillet. Add
remaining ingredients. Cover and
simmer about VA hours or until
tender.
SUNDAY
Roast pork loin, baked sweet
potatoes, buttered green beans,
broiled grapefruit, beverage
Roast Pork Loin
4 pounds pork loin
1 teaspoon salt
l A teaspoon pepper
V2 teaspoon sage
Sprinkle meat with seasonings
and bake at 350 degrees for about
2Vi to 3 hrs. Serve with savory
apples, made by placing cored
thick apple slices in a shallow pan
and topping with a mixture of 2
tablespoons lemon juice, 4
tablespoons brown sugar, 4
tablespoons catsup, 2 tablespoons
melted butter and 2 tablespoons
prepared horse-radish. Bake with
the roast for the last 30 minutes,
adding a bit of water if needed.

Vi pound carrots
6 small potatoes
2 cloves of garlic
V2 pound peas
2 onions
Slice the onions and crush the
garlic. Cut meat into cubes and
brown in a little fat. Add the
onion and garlic and brown. Add
water to cover, salt and pepper to
taste, and simmer on a low flame
until tender. About 3 A of an hour
before end of cooking time, add

the potatoes, carrots and peas and
a little more water if necessary.

Savory liver, steamed rice, leaf
spinach, tomato and cucumber
salad, caramel custard, beverage
Savory Liver
4 servings
1 pound liver
french dressing
flour seasoned with salt and
pepper
2 tablespoons fat
1 green pepper, sliced
1 medium onion, sliced
1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
'/2 cup water
Cut the liver into small pieces.
Marinate for several hours in the

French dressing, then sprinkle
with seasoned flour and brown in
hot fat. Remove from pan and
add green pepper and onion to
fat, adding a little more fat if
needed. Brown lightly. Add
Worcestershire sauce, water, and
stir until combined and thickened
slightly. Season to taste then add
liver long enough to heat.

Chicken Cacciatore
4-5 servings
Use a 2Vi to 3'/2 pound chicken
disjointed. Brown chicken in Vi
cup fat. Add 1 medium onion,
sliced thin and 1 clove garlic,
minced and cook until onion is
golden. Add 1 can tomatoes, V/ 2
teaspoons salt, Vi teaspoos
pepper, V2 teaspoon oregano and
cover. Simmer 40 to 50 minutes
until chicken is tender and sauce
thickened. Add V2 cup white wine
the last 15 minutes of cooking.

New England Boiled dinner
6 servings
3 pounds corned beef
6 medium turnips, whole
6 medium onions, whole
4 potatoes, quartered
6 medium carrots, whole
1 head cabbage, cut in wedges
1/8 teaspoon pepper
Place meat in a kettle, cover
and simmer 3 hours or until
tender. One hour before serving

time add turnips and onions.
When these have cooked 15
minutes add potatoes, carrots
and cabbage. Sprinkle with
pepper. Cover and cook 45
minutes longer. Serve with
horse-radish sauce made by
combining V2 cup salad dressing, l A
cup whipped cream, V2 teaspoon
salt and 2 tablespoons prepared
horseradish.

Baked Stuffed Fish
6 pounds dressed fish
salt
3 A cup butter or margarine
6 cups toasted bread crumbs
4 tablespoons lemon juice
3 tablespoons chopped onion
2 tablespoons capers
2 tablespoons parsley flakes
3 A teaspoon each, salt, celery seed,
thyme, marjoram
V2 teaspoon pepper
Wash the fish. Pat dry with
paper towels. Sprinkle inside and
out with salt. Melt butter and toss
lightly together with remaining
ingredients. Stuff fish loosely and
close with skewers and string.
Place fish on a shallow greased
baking dish. Brush with melted
fat. Bake for 1 .to IV2 hours in a
350 degree oven, basting
occasionally with melted fat.

Round Steak with Vegetables
2 pounds round steak

flour
1 teaspoon salt
8 carrot strips
8 celery strips
4 dill pickles, cut in half
lengthwise
3 tablespoons fat
1 cup beef bouillon
l A teaspoon pepper
l A teaspoon onion salt
Cut steak in 8 pieces. Pound in
flour with edge of heavy saucer.
Sprinkle on salt. Lay 1 strip each
of carrot, celery and dill pickle on
each piece. Fold meat in half.
Fasten with pick. Brown meat in
hot fat in a heavy skillet. Add
remaining ingredients. Cover and
simmer about VA hours or until
tender.

Savory Liver
4 servings
1 pound liver
french dressing
flour seasoned with salt and
pepper
2 tablespoons fat
1 green pepper, sliced
1 medium onion, sliced
1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
'/2 cup water
Cut the liver into small pieces.
Marinate for several hours in the

French dressing, then sprinkle
with seasoned flour and brown in
hot fat. Remove from pan and
