One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest
continues at the Little Fox
Theater Tuesday through
Saturday at 8:30 and Sunday at
7:30. For information call
434-4738.
Joe Orton's Loot appears at the
Berkeley Repertory Theater
tonight through Friday at 8.
Tickets are $3, $1.50 for students.
The Company Theater, 2314
Bancroft, performs its last
weekend of King Lear starting
tonight at 8. Tickets are $2.50
and $1.50.

performance of songs and arias
tonight only at 8:30 in the Opera
House. Seats are $5.50 to $9.50.

presents Carmen, with Ariel
Bybee at the Curran Theater at 8.
This is the last performance of the
opera, and it is SOLD OUT.

Take It With You goes on at the
Geary Theatre tonight at 8:30.
Tickets are $4.90 to $6.90.

play at the Boarding House, 960
Bush St. Call 441-4333 for dinner
reservations. Show ends March 4.

Memorial Theater. Tickets
Tuesday through Thursday at
8:30 and Sunday at 3 are $5.50 to
$7.50, for Friday at 8:30 and
Saturday at 6 and 9, $6.50 to
$8.50, and for Wednesday at
2:30, $4.50 to $6.50.

with guest conductor Rafael
Fruhbeck de Burgos performs
Haydn's Symphony No. 44 and
Falla's opera, 'La Vida Breve'
tonight at 8 in Zellerbach
Auditorium and tomorrow at
8:30 in the Opera House. Tickets
for the Thursday show are SOLD
OUT. Call the Opera House for
availability on Friday night.

Tuotti continues at the Nitery at
8 tonight through Saturday.
Tickets are $2 general admission,
$1 for students.
The Stanford Opera Theater
presents Benjamin Britten's Albert
Herring in Dinkelspiel Auditorium
at 8 tonight through Saturday.
Tickets are $1.50 to $3.50, with a
50 cent discount for students.

Press Books opens today in the
Bender Room of the Main
Library. The show traces the press
from 1494 to 1598.

at 8. Admission is $1.

Deathwatch tonight through
Saturday in Roble. Admission is
free.

lonesco's The Bald Soprano
tonight through Saturday in the
Twain East Lounge at 8.
Admission is free.
The I-Toyon Theater puts on The
Dark of the Moon, by Howard
Richardson and William Berney
tonight through Saturday in the
Toyon Hall Lounge at 8.
Admission is 50 cents.

presents A Midsummer Night's

Art, History, and Humanities
109A screens Durer and the
Renaissance in Annenberg
Auditorium at 12. Free admission.
The Stanford Wind Ensenble,
directed by Arthur Barnes,
performs tonight at 8 in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium.
Admission is free.
A benefit premiere showing of
Sounder, starring Academy Award
nominee Cicely Tyson, will be
held at the Guild Theater in
Menlo Park at 5, 7 and 9. All
profits will go to the Citizens'
Committee for Medical Aid to the
People of Indochina. The
Committee will distribute
proceeds to aid victims on all sides
of the Indochina war, through the
Children's Medical Relief
International, the American
Friends Service Committee, and
the Medical Aid for Indochina.
Tickets for the 5 o'clock show are
$3 for adults and $1.50 for
children. All tickets for the
evening shows are $5. Call
961-2875 or 326-3116 for tickets.
