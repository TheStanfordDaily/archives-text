THURSDAY, MARCH 8
One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest
continues at the Little Fox
Theater Monday through
Saturday at 8:30 and Sunday at
7:30. For information call
434-4738.
The Berkeley Repertory Theater
presents Dance of Death at 8
tonight and tomorrow, and at 7
on Sunday. Tickets are $3, $1.50
for students.
Jane Russell stars in Mame, at the
Circle Star Theater through March
18. Tickets for shows Tuesday
through Thursday at 8:30 and
Sunday at 7:30 are $3.50 to
$5.50; on Friday and Saturday at
8:30, $4.50 to $6.50; and on
Sunday at 2:30, $3 to $5.
The American Ballet Theater
presents 'Coppelia' with Natalia
Makarova at the Opera House at
8:30. SOLD OUT.
The San Francisco Symphony,
with guest director James Levine,
performs Mendelssohn's Fifth
Symphony and Stravinsky's
'Variations for Orchestra' and
other works at 2 in the Opera
House. It will repeat the concert
Friday and Saturday at 8:30. The
weekend shows are SOLD OUT.
Call 397-0717 for information on
ticket availability for the matinee.
The Royal Shakespeare Company
presents 'A Midsummer Night's
Dream' at the Geary Theater
through March 26. Only second
balcony seats ($4.90 weeknights,
$5.90 weekends) are left. Call
673-6440 for tickets.
Jean Aenet's Death watch
continues tonight through
Saturday in Roble at 8. There will
be a special midnight performance
on Friday. Admission is free.
Godspell plays on at the Marines'
Memorial Theater. Tickets for
shows Tuesday through Thursday
at 8:30 and Sunday at 3 are $5.50
to $7.50, for Friday at 8:30 and
Saturday at 6 and 9, $6.50 to
$8.50, and for Wednesday at 2,
$4.50 to $6.50.
The New Riders of the Purple

Sage, Ramblin Jack Elliott, and
Commander Cody appear at the
Berkeley Community Theater at
8. Tickets are $3.50 in advance,
$4 at the door.
Alan Bates stars in Butley playing
at the Curran Theater through
March 17. Tickets for shows
Monday through Thursday at
8:30 are $4.50 to $7.50, for
Friday and Saturday at 8:30, $5
to $8, and for Saturday and
Sunday at 2:30, $3.50 to $6.50.
The life-size puppets from Japan,
Bunraku, continue their run at the
Nourse Auditorium tonight
through Saturday at 8:30. Tickets
for the Thursday show are $3.50
to $6.50, and for the weekend
shows, $4.50 to $7.50.

Thoreau Spent in Jail. POST

The Topeng Masked Dance
Theater of Bali dances in
Zellerbach Playhouse at 8.
Admission is $3.50, $2.50 for
students.
Guitarist Ernesto Bitetti performs
tonight at Flint Center at 8:30,
Saturday at the Veterans
Auditorium at 8:30, and Sunday
at 2 in Zellerbach Auditorium.
Tickets are $2.50 to $5.50.
A TV Retrospective plays tonight
and Saturday at 7 and 10 in the

Palo Alto High Auditorium.
Shows screened include 'Howdy
Doody's trip to Finland,' 1950
Rosebowl highlights, and Mickey
Mouse Club selections. Admission
is $1.
Ravi Shankar and Ali Akbar Khan
perform in Masonic Auditorium
tonight at 8:30. Tickets are $3.75
to $5.75.
Sharmalee Edirisinghe exhibits
Ceylon batiks at the Montessori
International Center in the
Teledyne Isotope Building, Palo
Alto, today and tomorrow. The
show is open today from 9 till 6
and tomorrow from 10:30 to 1.
There will be free demonstrations,
today at 4 and tomorrow at
11:30. Admission is free.

The Stanford Quarterly Review
presents Antonioni's film,
Blow-Up, tonight at 7 and 9,
together with road runner
cartoons, in Cubberley
Auditorium. Admission is $1.
The Ceylon National Dance
Comapny performs in Spangenberg
Auditorium. Gunn High School,,
tonight at 8 and Saturday at 3.
Admission is $1.50-$3.50.
The High Chapparal Film Series
screens Singing in the Rain,
starring Gene Kelley at 7:30 and
two Little Rascals shorts at 9:15
in Bishop Auditorium. Admission
is $1.
The Committee on Black
Performing Arts sponsors a free
performance of the Watumbe
Dance Troupe of San Francisco in

Dinkelspiel Auditorium at 8.

Young play at the Marin Veterans

free.

Sometimes a Great Notion,
starring Paul Newman, in
Memorial Auditorium at 7 and
9:30. Admission is 50 cents.

Romanesque sculpture tonight at
8 in Annenberg Auditorium. The
lecture is free to all members of
the Committee for Art at
Stanford University and to all
students. There will be a slight fee
for other listeners.

Joan Hitlin opens today at the
»Artifactory, 226 Hamilton, in
Palo Alto. The show is open from
10 to 6.
