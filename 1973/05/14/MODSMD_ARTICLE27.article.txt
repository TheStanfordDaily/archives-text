# Women Netters Finish Second In Pac-8 Play
## 
### Patricia Lowdon 
Stanford's women's tennis
team finished second last weekend
in the Pacific-8 collegiate
tournament in Seattle. UCLA
took top honors winning both the
singles and doubles titles.
Stanford's Tracy MacNair,
fourth seed, lost to top seeded
Linda Lewis from UCLA in the
semis 6-2, 6-2. Lewis went on to
defeat a UCLA teammate Dawson
6-1, 6-4, for the championship.
The Card's other singles
representative Andrea Barnes lost
to Dawson in a tight 6-4, 7-5
quarter finals match. Barnes
trounced Oregon State's
Thompson 6-0, 6-0 in her first
round match.
Stanford's doubles team
Whitney Grant and Terry Albert
lost to top-seeded UCLA's
Godwin-Harter 6-2, 6-3, in the
finals. In a thrilling semi-finals
match Grant and Albert defeated
UCLA's Pifferini and Ley 6-3, 4-6,

6-3. The Stanford duo ousted
Cal's Sorensen-Gonzales 6-0, 6-2
in the quarters.
Godwin-Harter eliminated
fourth seeded Maureen O'Keefe
and Karen Wright from Stanford
in the semis 6-2, 6-1. Previously,
the Card's team defeated
Washington State's Lantz-Morgen
6-2, 6-1 in the quarters.
•Freshmen and sophoiu> v
netters finished third behind Davis
and Berkeley in the Modesto
tournament also last weekend.
Stanford's number one singles
Ann Feeley lost in the semi-finals
to Davis' Sara Follen 6-0, 6-2.
Playing number two for the Card's
Cyndy Blees was defeated 6-0, 6-2
by Davis' Carolun Ishii in the
quarters.
Stanford's Meredith Brittain
and Kathy Capps lost in the
semi-finals of the doubles to Julie
Garcie and Lisa Bauer from Davis,
6-2, 6-0.
