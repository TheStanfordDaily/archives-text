# 'Daily' Entertainment Spectrum

THURSDAY,OCTOBER 18
DEBORAH KERR stars in the
Civic Light Opera production of
THE DAY AFTER THE FAIR at
the Curran Theatre thru Oct. 20.
Call 474-4790 for ticket
information. (For a review of the
play, see opposite page.)
The Montgomery Playhouse in
San Francisco continues to
present EL GRANDE DE
COCA-COLA, a comedy about a
"tacky spectacular being
produced in a nightclub in
Honduras." Call 788-8282 for
information.
THE ALVIN AILEY CITY
CENTER DANCE THEATER
performs "The Lark Ascending,"
"Love Songs," and "Carmina
Burana" tonight at 8 p.m. in
Memorial Auditorium. The group
also performs tomorrow night and
Saturday night at Stanford.
Tickets for tonight's performance
are $2.75 to $4.75; for the
weekend performances, $3.25 to
$5.25.
The Boarding House in San
Francisco presents THE
POINTER SISTERS thru Oct. 21.
Call 441-4333 for information.
Painist GARY PALMER performs
tonight in Tresidder's small lounge
at 8 p.m. He will play music of
Mozart, Beethoven, Chopin and
others. Admission is free.
The Matrix at 412 Broadway in
San Francisco presents THE
WAILERS, a group from
Kingston, Jamaica, tonight at 9
p.m. Call 434-2444 for
information.
The SAN FRANCISCO
INTERNATIONAL FILM
FESTIVAL continues thru Oct.
28 at the Palace of Fine Arts
Theatre in San Francisco.
Reserved seats for evenings are
$3.75. Call 775-2021 for
information.

FRIDAY, OCTOBER 19
The Circle Star Theatre in San
Carlos presents comedian
JOHNNY CARSON tonight thru
Sunday night. Call 364-2550 for
information.
The Tresidder Film series screens
THE 400 BLOWS, written and
directed by Francois Truffaunt,
tonight at 8 and 10 p.m. in the
lounges upstairs. Admission is $1.
The Office of Public Events
presents THE CIRCUS, starring
CHARLIE CHAPLIN, tonight at 7
and 9:15 p.m. in Cubberley
Auditorium. Admission is $1.50
general, $1.25 for students.
SLY AND THE FAMILY STONE
perform tonight with GIDEON
AND POWER at 8:30 p.m. at the
Santa Clara County Fairgrounds
in San Jose. Tickets are $4.75 in
advance, $5.50 at the door.
THE J. GEILS BAND, SLADE
and SPOOKY TOOTH perform
tonight and tomorrow night at
Winterland. Tickets are $4 in
advance, $4.50 at the door.
The Martin Luther King
Community Center in San Mateo
presents a dance concert entitled
"The Total Black Experience"
tonight at 9 p.m. Admission is
$1.50 general, $1 for students.
SATURDAY, OCTOBER 20
Creature Features, with host Bob
Wilkins, screens NIGHT OF THE
LIVING DEAD at 9 and
ATTACK OF THE MAYAN
MUMMY at 11 p.m. tonight on
Channel 2.
U.C. Berkeley presents Mikis
Theodorakis' ZORBA DANCES
tongith at 8 p.m. in Zellerbach
Auditorium. Call 642-2561 for
information.
The Law School Film Society
screens THE FIXER, directed by

John Frankenheimer, tonight at
7:30 and 9:45 p.m. in Cubberley
Auditorium. Admission is $1,
with proceeds going to law
scholarships.
ACT (the American Conservatory
Theatre) in San Francisco opens
its new season tonight with THE
TAMING OF THE SHREW,
premiering at 8:30 p.m.
Call 771-3880 for information.
SUNDAY, OCTOBER 21
THE STANFORD CHAMBER
ORCHESTRA, conducted by
Sandor Salgo, plays Haydn's
Symphony no. 95 and other
pieces in a free concert today at 3
p.m. in Dinkelspiel Auditorium.
Three members of Lawrence
Welk's musical family —
champagne lady NORMA
ZIMMER, vocalist JIM ROBERTS
and pianist 808 RALSTON —
perform at San Jose City College
tonight at 8 p.m. Call 298-2181
for information.
ASSU SUNDAY FLICKS screens
THE EMIGRANTS, with Liv
Ullmann, tonight at 7 and 9:45
p.m. in Memorial Auditorium.
Admission is 50 cents.

information.
