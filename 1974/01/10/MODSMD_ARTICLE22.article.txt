CONTINUING EVENTS
Ink paintings of SOHAKU NASU
will be shown through the end of
January at the Bechtel
International Center. This is the
first U.S. exhibit of his
landscapes, which have been
widely shown in Japan.
The SAN FRANCISCO
SYMPHONY season continues
with guest cellist Ken Yasuda
performing Schumann's Cello
Concerto. Also on the program,
directed by Seiji Ozawa, is Hoist's
The Planets and Haydn's
Notturno No. 1. Performances are
at the War Memorial Opera House
at 8:30 p.m., except Thursday's
which is at UC Berkeley's
Zellerbach Hall. Next week's
program, which includes Mahler's
Five Late Songs, J.S. Bach's
Sinfonia in E-flat Major for
Double Orchestra and Richard
Strauss' Ein Heldenleben, begins
Wednesday.
ACT continues its 1973-74 San
Francisco season with Moliere's
THE MISER tonight and
Thursday, and matinees Saturday
and Wednesday; THE HOUSE OF
BERNARDA ALBA tomorrow
night; and THE TAMING OF
THE SHREW Saturday and
Wednesday nights. Evening
performances at the Geary are at
8:30 p.m., matinees, 2:30 p.m.
THE TRIAL OF JAMES
McNEILL WHISTLER, a new

play by Jon Phillip Palmer,
continues at the On Broadway
Theater, 435 Broadway in San
Francisco. Showtimes are 8:30
p.m. Tuesdays through Fridays, 7
and 10 p.m. on Saturdays and
7:30 p.m. on Sundays. Tickets are
available through Ticketron.
Comedian DAVID STEINBERG
appears through Sunday at the
Boarding House on Bush St. in
San Francisco. Call 441-4333 for
reservations and showtimes.
Robert Sherwood's play THE
PETRIFIED FOREST, subject of
a Bogart film classic, is the current
offering of the Berkeley
Repertory Theatre, 2980 College
Ave. Performances are
Wednesdays through Saturdays at
8 p.m. and Sundays at 7 p.m.
through Feb. 10. For informaiton,
call 845-4700.
The Manhattan Playhouse in Palo
Alto presents Noel Coward's
PRIVATE LIVES weekends
through Jan. 25. For ticket
information, call 322-4589.
FRIDAY, JANUARY 11
Tresidder screens Ingmar
Bergman's THE HOUR OF THE
WOLF, with Liv Ullmann and
Max von Sydow, tonight at 8 and
10 p.m. in the lounges. Admission
is $1.
ALEXANDRA HAWLEY, flute,

of the Music Department faculty,
and Kenneth Ziegenfuss, piano,
will perform works of C.P.E.
Bach, Mozart, Schubert, Martin
and Bartok at 8 p .m. in
Dinkelspiel. Admission is $2,
students $1.
GOYA: THE GRAPHIC WORK is
the title of a major comprehensive
exhibit, including etchings,
aquatints, lithographs and the
well-known Disasters of War
series. The show opens today at
the Art Gallery, and continues
through March 3.
Bill Graham presents JERRY
GARCIA-MERLE SAUNDERS,
WAYLON JENNINGS and ALICE
STUART AND SNAKE tonight
and tomorrow at 8 p.m. at
Winterland. Tickets are available
through Ticketron.
THE AMAZING KRESKIN,
mentalist, will appear at Masonic
Auditorium at 8:30 p.m. Tickets
are available at Macy's and most
agencies.
SATURDAY, JANUARY 12
Tresidder presents a program of
TV OLDIES, including "Sergeant
Bilko," Burns and Allen, Nixon's
Checkers speech, Groucho Marx
in "You Bet Your Life," Elvis on
"The Ed Sullivan Show," "The
Mickey Mouse Club,"and more.

Shows are at 7:30 and 10:30 p.m.
in the lounges, with admission
$1.50.
SUNDAY, JANUARY 13
ASSU Sunday Flicks presents
multi-Oscar-winning CABARET
tonight at 6, 8:15 and 10:30 p.m.
in Mem Aud. Admission is a mere
50 cents.
Organist JAMES WELCH will play
music of Bach, Handel, Mozart,
Peeters, Heiller and others in a
free recital at 3 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel.
MONDAY, JANUARY 14
DR. ALBERT J. LUBIN will
present a lecture on Vincent Van
Gogh at the general meeting of
the Stanford Faculty Women's
Club. The 2:15 meeting in
Annenberg Auditorium is open to
the public.

TUESDAY, JANUARY 15
MICHELE MILAND, soprano,
and MARIE BIRD, flute and
piano, will give a free recital of
songs and arias by Schumann,
Respighi, Mozart, Bartok, Barber,
Rodrigo and Roussel at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel.
VOYAGE EN FRANCE, a slide
presentation of a tour of southern
France, will be shown at 8 p.m. in
Annenberg Auditorium. Art Profs.
Albert Elsen and Paul Turner will
give commentary on the slides,
taken on a department-sponsored
tour. The program is free and
open to the public.
WEDNESDAY, JANUARY 16
JAY WINOGRAD will give a free
trumpet recital, accompanied by
brass and string ensembles, at 8
p.m. in Tresidder's large lounge.
Featured composers include
Copeland, Vivaldi and Ewald.


IN CONCERT—Flutist Alexandra Hawley and pianist Kenneth
Ziegenfuss, both of Stanford's music faculty, will perform at 8 p.m.
Friday night in Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Admission is $2, $1 for
students.
