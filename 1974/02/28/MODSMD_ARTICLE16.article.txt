# 'Daily' Entertainment Spectrum

CONTINUING EVENTS
ACT continues its season with
TONIGHT AT 8:30, tonight,
Tuesday, Wednesday evening and
Wednesday matinee; THE MISER,
Friday evening; CYRANO DE
BERGERAC Saturday matinee
and evening; no performances
Sunday. All evening performances
are at 8:30 p.m., all matinees at
2:30 p.m. at the Geary Theatre.
WILL ROGERS' U.S.A. starring

James Whitmore continues at
Marines' Memorial Theatre
through March 24.
THE CREDIBILITY GAP, a
musical satire group, and
musicians TREVOR VEITCH
AND ANDY KULBERG continue
at" the Boarding House through
Sunday. HOYT AXTON,
composer, and THE
BUCKINGHAM NICKS begin
Tuesday.

THURSDAY, FEBRUARY 28
George Brown will read Old
English and translations from Old
English in the Branner Lounge at
7:30 p.m. This reading will also be
presented at 3 p.m. Sunday on
KZSU 90.1 FM's VOICES AND
PASSAGES.
FRIDAY, MARCH 1
Pianist SEYMOUR BERNSTEIN

will perform works of Beethoven
and Moussorgsky as well as his
own compositions in an 8 p.m.
concert at Dinkelspiel
Auditorium. Admission is $4.
The Larkin Players present
FRANNY AND ZOOEY, a free
play adapted from the book by
J.D. Salinger as part of a SCIRE
project. Curtain is at 8 p.m. in
Toyon Lounge tonight, Saturday
and Sunday.
The Manhattan Playhouse,
Manhattan Ave. and W. Bayshore,
Palo Alto, present Moliere's
comedy, THE MISER. Curtain
time is 8:30 p.m. Friday and
Saturdays through March.
The Tresidder Film Series presents
PASSAGE TO MARSEILLE, with
Humphrey Bogart, Claude Rains,
Peter Lorre, and Sydney
Greenstreet. Shows are at 8 and
10:30 p.m. and admission is $1.
History of Film presents Carol
Reed's THE THIRD MAN, plus a
complete episode of
BULLWINKLE at 7 and 9:15
p.m. in Jordan 040, the
psychology auditorium.
Admission is $1.
Bill Graham presents POCO and
STRAWBS at Winterland. This
show continues tomorrow night.
JONI MITCHELL is SOLD OUT.
SATURDAY, MARCH 2
The chorus and orchestra of the
University of California, Berkeley,

perform Stravinsky's "Les Noces"
sung in Russian, Debussy's
"Nocturnes" and Schoenberg's
"Variations" at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Tickets
are $2, students $1.
THE SHELLBACK ROSE will
play at a dance with the acoustic
group EASY VIRTUE. The dance
is at All Saints Auditorium at the
corner of Waverly and Hamilton,
Palo Alto, at 8:30 p.m.
Former Stanford faculty member
Gerald Hiken will speak at a
program entitled ALL YOU
EVER WANTED TO KNOW
ABOUT HOLLYWOOD BUT
WERE AFRAID TO ASK. Hiken
will discuss his experiences as a
movie and television actor. In
addition the film HOLLYWOOD,
THE DREAM FACTORY,
narrated by Dick Cavett will be
shown. This show to benefit
paraplegic Israeli soldiers will be
presented tonight and tomorrow

at 8 p.m. at Mitchell Park Center,
3800 Middlefield Rd., Palo Alto.
SUNDAY, MARCH 3
THE FRANCESCO TRIO -
David Abel, violin; Bonnie
Hampton, cello; and Nathan
Schwartz, piano, with guest Rolf
Persinger, viola — play an all
Beethoven program at 3 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Tickets
are $2 general, $1 student.
ASSU Sunday Flicks presents
Ingmar Bergman's CRIES AND
WHISPERS with the short subject
THE MEN IN THE PARK. Shows
are at 7 and 9 p.m., admission is
50 cents.
MONDAY, MARCH 4
The Raymond Fred West
Memorial Lecture Series presents
Professor Jurgen Moltmann
speaking on HUMAN IDENTITY
IN CHRISTIAN FAITH. This
lecture will continue Tuesday and
Wednesday at 8 p.m., Bishop
Auditorium. Admission is free.
History of Film presents Billy
Wilder's SUNSET BOULEVARD
and an MGM cartoon at 7 and
9:15 p.m. in Jordan 040, the
psychology auditorium.
Admission is $1.
TUESDAY, MARCH 5
ALEA 11, The Ensemble for New
Music, will give a free concert in
Memorial Church at 8 p.m.
playing music and theater pieces
tyy Stockhausen, Bresnick, Kagel,
McNabb and others.

WEDNESDAY, MARCH 6
A free noon concert in Tresidder
large lounge features old and new
music for brass quintet performed
by Carole Klein and James
Duncan, trumpets; John Burton,
horn; Ryan Krebs, trombone; and
Ted Szatrowski, tuba.
Sharon Polk, pianist, will play
partitas by J.S. Bach in A minor,
G major, and E minor in a free
recital at 8 p.m. in Tresidder large
lounge.
The JSSU Film Series presents
Humphrey Bogart in THE CAINE
MUTINY, with co-stars Van
Johnson, Jose Ferrer, Fred
Mac Murray, and E.G. Marshall.
Second feature is THE
UNANSWERED QUESTION.
Shows are at 7 and 9 p.m. in
Cubberley Auditorium, and
admission is $1.
LUCIA, a Cuban film depicting
the life of a Cuban woman before,
during, and after the revolution,
will be shown by the Women's
International League for Peace
and Freedom. The show is at 8
p.m. at Gunn High School
auditorium, 780 Arastradero Rd.,
Palo Alto.


—Photo courtesy University News and Publications

which runs through March 9 with Saturday and Sunday matinees at 2 p.m. and nightly performances at 8
p.m. Tickets are $3.50 general, $2 for students.

Music, will give a free concert in
Memorial Church at 8 p.m.
playing music and theater pieces
tyy Stockhausen, Bresnick, Kagel,
McNabb and others.

WEDNESDAY, MARCH 6
A free noon concert in Tresidder
large lounge features old and new
music for brass quintet performed
by Carole Klein and James
Duncan, trumpets; John Burton,
horn; Ryan Krebs, trombone; and
Ted Szatrowski, tuba.
Sharon Polk, pianist, will play
partitas by J.S. Bach in A minor,
G major, and E minor in a free
recital at 8 p.m. in Tresidder large
lounge.
The JSSU Film Series presents
Humphrey Bogart in THE CAINE
MUTINY, with co-stars Van
Johnson, Jose Ferrer, Fred
Mac Murray, and E.G. Marshall.
Second feature is THE
UNANSWERED QUESTION.
Shows are at 7 and 9 p.m. in
Cubberley Auditorium, and
admission is $1.
LUCIA, a Cuban film depicting
the life of a Cuban woman before,
during, and after the revolution,
will be shown by the Women's
International League for Peace
and Freedom. The show is at 8
p.m. at Gunn High School
auditorium, 780 Arastradero Rd.,
Palo Alto.

PUELIE OPENS—The West Coast premiere of the Broadway musical Purlie is Friday at 8 p.m. in the
Little Theater. Phil McGee and Anne Kenney star in the Committee on Black Performing Arts production
which runs through March 9 with Saturday and Sunday matinees at 2 p.m. and nightly performances at 8
p.m. Tickets are $3.50 general, $2 for students.

Former Stanford faculty member
Gerald Hiken will speak at a
program entitled ALL YOU
EVER WANTED TO KNOW
ABOUT HOLLYWOOD BUT
WERE AFRAID TO ASK. Hiken
will discuss his experiences as a
movie and television actor. In
addition the film HOLLYWOOD,
THE DREAM FACTORY,
narrated by Dick Cavett will be
shown. This show to benefit
paraplegic Israeli soldiers will be
presented tonight and tomorrow

at 8 p.m. at Mitchell Park Center,
3800 Middlefield Rd., Palo Alto.
