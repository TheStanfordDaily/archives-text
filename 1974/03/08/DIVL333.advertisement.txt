AGENDA DIGEST
The Council of the City of Palo Alto will meet on Monday, March 11,
1974 at 7: 30 p.m. in the Council Chamber of the Civic Center, 250
Hamilton Avenue. The complete agenda for the meeting is
available at the Civic Center, all City libraries, the Community
Center, Police and Fire Stations. In addition, packets containing
the Agenda and supporting informative material may be consulted
at the Clerk's Office, 7th floor, and the libraries. Below is a brief
digest of the items which have been submitted. Inquiries con
cerning details of any items should be directed to the City Clerk
(329 2226). Palo Alto City Council Meetings are broadcast live via
KZSU, 90.1 on the FM dial.
1. Finance and Public Works Committee recommends re Jenks &
Adamson contract.
2. Policy and Procedures Committee recommends re Booz, Allen
& Hamilton Report
3. Policy and Procedures Committee recommends re Palo Alto's
support, in principle, of a Bay Area Regional Planning Agency.
4. Policy and Procedures Committee recommends re land use
legislation.
5. Planning Commission recommends re reclassification of
certain properties in Fire Zone I.
6. Planning Commission recommendations in response to Council
referrals of 1/7 / 74 re Fire Zone I.
7. Planning Commission recommends re application ot Dividend
Industries.
8. Planning Commission recommends re application of Schwarz
Construction Company.
9. Planning Commission recommends re application of Thomas
Harrington.
10. Human Relations Commission recommends re SB 1288, a
Landlord ' Tenant Bill.
11. Human Relations Commission recommends re City acting as
fiscal agent for application for funding to Luke B. Hancock
Foundation.
12. Ordinance amending Section 16.20.280 of Palo Alto Municipal
Code to require conformance of signs upon change of face of such
signs.
13. FBI National Academy — Leave of Absence Approval for
Police Chief.
14. Cultural Center Garden Patio Project: Rejection of Bids.
15. Mitchell' Park Improvements: Bid Award for Construction.
16. Grant of Easement to Santa Clara Valley Water District.
17. Study df Speed Control Devices
18. Alma Street Improvements — Engineering Services.
19. Menlo Park Municipal Transit System.
20. Request of Vice Mayor Pearson re Task Force for Integrated
Education.
