' SHORT COMPUTER COURSES "
Offered Spring Quarter
by
Academic Computing Services
Stanford Center for Information Processing
All interested students, faculty and staff are invited to attend these free, non-credit
computer courses taught by the staff of Academic Computing Services. Introductory as well
as advanced courses describe the service?, programming languages and applications programs
available on Stanford's IBM 360/67 computer located in Pine Hall, Jordan Quad. A special
series of seminars on numerical analysis is being offered; lecturers for these seminars will
be graduate students from the Computer Science Department. In most of these courses, free
computer time is provided for students to do assignments. All classes meet in Polya Hall
(next to Pine Hall).
Registration is required for all courses. You may register now for the Introduction to
Academic Computing Services course by calling Ext. 4877 or by going to the Document Sales
Office in Pine Hall during tlie hours 10 a.m. - Noon and 1:15 - 5 p.m. Registration for all
other courses opens April 1 and must be made in person at the Document Sales Office. Early
registration is advisable as enrollment is limited.
For a further description of course content, materials used or prerequisites, call Ext. 4877
or see the Academic Computing Services Bulletin available at the Document Sales Office.
