AH interested students, faculty arid staff are Invited to attend these free, non-credit
computer courses taught by the staff of Academic Computing Services. Introductory as well

available on Stanford's IBM 360/67 computer located in Pine Hall, Jordan Quad. A special
series of seminars on numerical analysis is being offered; lecturers for these seminars will
be graduate students from the Computer Science Department. In most of these courses, free
computer time is provided for students to do assignments. All classes meet in Polya Hall
(next to Pine Hall).
Registration is required for all courses. You may register for the Introduction to Academic Com
puting Services course by calling Ext. 4877 or by going to the Document Sales Office in Pine Hall
during the hours 10 a.m. - Noon and 1:15-5 p.m. Registration for all other courses must be made in
person at the Document Sales Office. Early registration is advisable as enrollment is United.
For a further description of course content, materials used or prerequisites, call Ext 4877 or see the
Academic Computing Services Bulletin available at the Document Sales Office.
