*4.75, $3.75, $2.75.
BALLO DI DONNE TURCHE for
dancers, singers, and
instrumentalists will be staged in
costume by students of early
music under the direction of
Meredith Little. The Ballo and
music by Dufay comprise a free
concert at 8 p.m. in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium.
The Tresidder Film Series presents
THE LOVED ONE (1965)
directed by Tony Richardson and
starring Jonathan Winters, Robert
Morse and Sir John Gielgud.
Showings are at 8 and 10:30 p.m.
and admission is $1.
A CANADIAN FILM FESTIVAL
sponsored by the National Film
Board of Canada and the De Anza
College Film Guild will be held at
Flint Center in Cupertino starting
at 8 p.m.
SATURDAY, APRIL 6
LE ROI SE MEURT will be
presented by LE TRETEAU DE
PARIS in French at 8 p.m. in
Memorial Auditorium. Tickets are
$4, $3, $2.
THE FRANCESCO TRIO,
Stanford's resident ensemble,
present A ROMANTIC REVIVAL
featuring music of Glinka,
Smetana, Liszt, Mendelssohn and
Faure, at 8 p.m. in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium. Admission is $2
general, $1 student.
TV OLDIES featuring Superman,
Elvis, The Beatles, Father Knows
Best, and Dragnet '56 will be
presented in the Tresidder lounges
at 8 and 10:30 p.m. Admission is
$1.50.

LES MENESTRIERS perform
ancient French music at 2:30 p.m.
in Dinkelspiel Auditorium.
Admission is $4.50.
Bill Graham presents at San Jose
Civic Auditorium BOZ SCAGGS
without his tails and with his
band.
ASSU Sunday Flicks presents
DELIVERANCE with the short
film FARM WORKERS at 7 and
9:30 p.m. Admission is 50 cents.
The Palo Alto Women's Coalition
film series features THE QUEEN,
a documentary of a drag beauty
contest. Screenings are at 7:30
and 9:30 in Room 1-2 at
Cubberley High School.
Three films and a lecture will be
presented by the Cucaracha Film
Center of El Centro Campesino,
the Farmworker's Cultural Center

at 1:30 p.m. in the Palo Alto
Cultural Center Auditorium. The
auditorium is located at 1313
Newell Road, and admission will
be charged.
MONDAY, APRIL 8
JESSE COLIN YOUNG will
perform at 7:30 and 10 p.m. in
Memorial Auditorium. Tickets are
$3.50.
MUSIC TO THE QUEEN'S
TASTE, a concert of 12th
through 18th century music for
soprano and viola da gamba will
feature Janet Wheeler, Martha
Blackman, and a viol consort. This
free concert starts at 8 p.m. in
Annenburg Auditorium.
TUESDAY, APRIL 9
A concert of German baroque
music, with Leta Miller, flute;
Peter Shelton, cello; Jeanne
Ashby, violin; and David Sheetz,
harpsichord will be presented at 8
p.m. in Tresidder large lounge.
Admission is free.
WEDNESDAY, APRIL 10
The San Francisco Symphony
conducted by Seiji Ozawa will
perform THE PASSION
ACCORDING TO ST. MATTHEW
by J.S. Bach through Saturday at
the Opera House.
A free noon concert will feature
music for flutes and piano by
Vivaldi and Faure, and music for
women's chorus by Bach, Casals,
Vaughn Williams and Persichetti.
The performance is at 12:15 p.m.
in Tresidder large lounge.
David Oppenheim, organist, will
perform music of Bach, Pachelbel,
Brahms, Franck, Hindemith and
Charpentier in a free recital at 8
p.m. in Memorial Church.
FUTURE EVENTS
The Tresidder Program Board
presents a NAPA VALLEY
WINETASTING TRIP on
Saturday, April 13, 8 a.m. to 8
p.m. Transportation will be by
bus from the Tresidder lot and
will cost $4.50, plus a box lunch
if desired. Tickets on sale this
week at Tresidder Information
Desk.
DISNEYLAND is the destination
of another Tresidder trip planned
for Friday, May 3 through
Sunday, May 5. Tickets are
$31.90 for a four-person room, or
$34.90 for a two-person room.
Price includes transportation by
chartered bus, two nights lodging,
admission to Disneyland, tickets
to 11 attractions, and shuttle bus
rides to Disneyland. Tickets go on
sale this week at the Tresidder
Information Desk.

Proctor—in random arrangement above—perform tonight under their

Firesign's outstanding accomplishments include eight albums and
the foundation in 1972 of the Natural Surrealistic Light People's
Party, headed by unsuccessful presidential candidate George Papoon.
Tickets for tonight's performance are $4.

music, with Leta Miller, flute;

The San Francisco Symphony
conducted by Seiji Ozawa will
perform THE PASSION
ACCORDING TO ST. MATTHEW
by J.S. Bach through Saturday at
the Opera House.
A free noon concert will feature
music for flutes and piano by
Vivaldi and Faure, and music for
women's chorus by Bach, Casals,
Vaughn Williams and Persichetti.
The performance is at 12:15 p.m.
in Tresidder large lounge.
David Oppenheim, organist, will
perform music of Bach, Pachelbel,
Brahms, Franck, Hindemith and
Charpentier in a free recital at 8
p.m. in Memorial Church.
