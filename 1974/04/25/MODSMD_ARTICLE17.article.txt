CONTINUING EVENTS
ROSENCRANTZ AND
GUILDENSTERN ARE DEAD by
Tom Stoppard continues in the
Little Theater through Saturday,
and Monday, April 29 through
Saturday, May 4. The April 29
performance has been added
becuase of a large pre-sale for the
other performances. Student
tickets for all performances are
$1, with tickets for the Monday
performance being sold only at
the door beginning at 7 p.m. the
day of the performance. All
performances are at 8 p.m., and
all other tickets are available at
Tresidder Box Office.
UNSEEN FLOWERS OF THE
DESERT, a photographic exhibit
by graduate Robert Gilbreath will
continue through Tuesday on the
second floor of Tresidder.
The screenprints and etchings of
Max Klager continue through
Tuesday at Bechtel International
Center.
ACT continues its season with
BROADWAY tonight, Friday,
Saturday, Tuesday and
Wednesday; THE CHERRY
ORCHARD Saturday matinee,
Wednesday matinee; and
CYRANO DE BERGERAC
Monday evening. All evening
performances are at 8:30 p.m., all
matinees at 2:30 p.m. at the
Geary Theatre.
HORACE SILVER continues at
Keystone Korner, 750 Vallejo St.,
San Francisco, through Sunday.
His quintet is replaced Tuesday by
AIRTO AND FINGERS.
The Odyssey Room, 799 E. El
Camino Real, Sunnyvale, presents
continuous music from 5 p.m. to

2 a.m. as part of their fifth
anniversary party Friday and
Saturday nights. BUTCH
WHACKS AND THE GLASS
PACKS are the featured band
Monday.
KATHI McDONALD and LEO
SAYER continue through Sunday
at the Boarding House, 960 Bush
St., San Francisco.
LINDA EDMISTON AND
WESTERN ELECTRIC and
LAWRENCE HAMMOND AND
THE WHIPLASH BAND star in a
live KEST country music
broadcast from the Orphanage,
4034 20th St., San Francisco,
tonight. PETER SPELMAN AND
SPELBOUND appear Friday and
Saturday. AZTECA performs
Sunday, and is replaced Monday
by MAGIC BUS. SAPO and
SPECTRUM appear Tuesday.
Cal Tjader continues through
Saturday at El Matador, 492
Broadway, San Francisco.
THURSDAY, APRIL 25
The Stanford chapter of the
College Opera Association will sell
SAN FRANCISCO OPERA
TICKETS for next fall at half
price at 12:30 p.m. today in
Room 267-268 of Tresidder. The
operas include: Puccini's Manon
Lescaut with Leontype Price;
Wagner's Parsifal with Jess
Thomas; Rossini's La Cenerentola
with Fredericka von Stade;
Massenet's Esclarmonde with Joan
Sutherland; Verdi's Luisa Miller
with Katia Ricciarelli and Luciano
Pavarotti; and Puccini's Madame
Butterfly with Renata Scotto or
Pilar Lorengar. Tickets are for
balcony seats and will be sold for
$2.88 each.

The Stanford Film Society and
the D epartment of
Communication present Don
Siegel's INVASION OF THE
BODY SNATCHERS and Harold
Lloyd in NEVER WEAKEN.
Showings are at 7 and 9:30 p.m.
in Cubberley Auditorium, and
admission is $1.
The MENDICANTS, Stanford's
all-male singing group, will give a
free concert at Alpha Delta Phi at
10 p.m.
A free BLUEGRASS JAM
SESSION with all musicians and
listeners welcome is offered from
7 to 10 p.m. at Gryphon Stringed
Instruments, 4041 El Camino,
Palo Alto. For more information
call 493-2131.
FRIDAY, APRIL 26
THE EARLY MUSIC CONSORT
OF LONDON will perform at 8
p.m. in Dinkelspiel Auditorium.
Admission is $4.50 general, $2
student.
The Tresidder Film Series presents
KING OF HEARTS with Alan
Bates and Genevieve Bujold at 8
and 10 p.m. in Cubberley
Auditorium. Admission is $1.
THE NEW PORT COSTA
PLAYERS will perform at 8 p.m.
on Friday, April 26 in the Palo
Alto Cultural Center, 1313 Newell
Rd.
THE POINTER SISTERS will
perform for one night only at 8
p.m. at the San Jose Civic
Auditorium.
IN CONCERT features LOGGINS
& MESSINA, BACHMAN
TURNER OVERDRIVE,
BROWNSVILLE STATION, and

THE POINTER SISTERS at
11:30 p.m. on Channel 7.
SATURDAY, APRIL 27
JON HENDRICKS will perform
the music of -Duke Ellington
tonight at 8 p.m. in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium. Tickets are $3
general, $2 students.
Bill Graham presents at
Winterland DAVE MASON,
ELVIN BISHOP, AND SUZI
QUATRO.
SUNDAY, APRIL 28
A ROBERT FROST
CENTENNIAL CELEBRATION
will begin with a talk at 2:30 p.m.
today by Associate Professor
Albert Gelpi in the forum room of
Meyer Library. A reception will
follow in the Bender Room of the
Main Library where a selection of
Frost's books and manuscripts
will be on display through August
31.
A Sunday Afternoon Series
concert features the Stanford
Chamber Orchestra, Jay Klein,
conductor, and small ensembles
playing music of Handel,
Schubert, Scarlatti and Persichetti
at 3 p.m. in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium. Admission is free.
The Tresidder Program Board
presents a trip to the
GIANTS-METS DOUBLE
HEADER including round trip
bus fare and a box seat at
Candlestick Park for $7. Buses
will leave the Tresidder parking
lot at 10:30 a.m. Tickets may be
purchased at the Tresidder
Information Center.
ASSU SUNDAY FLICKS presents
THE POSEIDON ADVENTURE

and the short subject SOLO at 7
and 9:40 p.m. in Memorial
Auditorium. Tickets are 50 cents
for Stanford students, faculty,
staff, and their families.
WEDNESDAY, MAY 1
Gilbert and Sullivan's
IOLANTHE, or THE PEER AND
THE PERI, will be presented by
the Stanford Savoyards tonight at
8 p.m. as well as Friday and
Saturday, May 3 and 4, in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Tickets
are $2.50 general, $1 student.
A free noon concert at 12:15 p.m.
in Tresidder large lounge features
Kenneth Smith, flute, Scott Kim
and Chris Hardy, piano,
performing music of Telemann,
Davidovsky and Beethoven.
John Robinson, recorder, and a
baroque ensemble will play music
of Couperin, Croft, Telemann and
Handel in a free concert at 8 p.m.
in Tresidder large lounge.
San Jose State University presents
the NITTY GRITTY DIRT
BAND, JOHN HARTFORD, and
JERRY JEFF WALKER tonight
at 7 and 11 p.m. in the men's
gym. Tickets are $3 for the
general public.
YOU'RE A GOOD MAN,
CHARLIE BROWN opens tonight
at 8 p.m. in the student union of
Menlo College. The performances
will be repeated May 2, 7 and 8.
There is a small admission charge.
The SAN FRANCISCO
SYMPHONY ORCHESTRA with
Maestro Seiji Ozawa is performing
tonight, Friday, and Saturday at
the Opera House, and tomorrow
night at Zellerbach Hall at
Berkeley.
