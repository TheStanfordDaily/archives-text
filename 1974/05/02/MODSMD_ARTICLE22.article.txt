CONTINUING EVENTS
ROSENCRANTZ AND
GUILDENSTERN ARE DEAD by
Tom Stoppard finishes its run
with performances nightly
through Saturday, May 4. Student
tickets for all performances are
$1, and are available at Tresidder
Box Office. All performances are
at 8 p.m.
ACT continues its season with
THE CHERRY ORCHARD

tonight and Friday matinee;
BROADWAY Friday, Saturday,
Tuesday, Wednesday matinee and
night; and THE TAMING OF
THE SHREW Monday evening.
All evening performances are at
8:30 p.m., all matinees at 2:30
p.m. at the Geary Theatre.
The San Francisco Symphony
Orchestra with Maestro Seiji
Ozawa continue their
performances of works by

DVORAK, BARTOK, and CAGE
tonight at 8 p.m. in Zellerbach
Hall on the Berkeley campus, and
Friday and Saturday at 8:30 p.m.
in the Opera House.
DOC WATSON AND SON, and
SKUNK CABBAGE continue
through Saturday at the Boarding
House, 960 Bush St., San
Francisco. MERLE TRAVIS and
THE GREENBRIAR BOYS start
Tuesday, May 7.
AIRTO AND FINGERS:
FEATURING FLORA PURIM
AND BUSTER WILLIAMS
continue through Sunday at
Keystone Korner, 750 Vallejo St.,
San Francisco. The duo is
replaced Tuesday by the AHMAD
JAMAL TRIO.
THIRD WORLD PHOTOS
exhibit, by Norman Chandler is
continuing upstairs at Tresidder.
Black and White photographs by
Bill Respini are being exhibited in
the Coffee House.
THURSDAY, MAY 2
Violinist Jeanne Ashby and
ensemble will perform Biber's
MOUNT OF OLIVES SONATA
and music of Webern, Ives, and
Brahms at 8 p.m. in Tresidder
large lounge. Admission is free.
The Stanford Film Society and
the D epartment of
Communication present THE
BEST YEARS OF OUR LIVES
with Fredric March, Dana
Andrews and Myrna Loy; directed
by William Wyler. 7:00 only.
Cubberley Auditorium. Tickets
$1.
Foothill College Drama
Department presents THE MAN
WHO CAME TO DINNER by
George S. Kaufman and Moss Hart
tonight at 8:15 p.m. in the
Campus Theatre. The production
will be repeated May 3,4,9,10,
and 11. Tickets are $2.50 and
$1.50 at box office.
JOEY HEATHERTON opens
tonight in the Venetian Room of
the Fairmont Hotel and Tower.

Information about the show,
which runs through May 12th, can
be obtained at 362-8800.

or THE PEER AND THE PERI

281 at 8:30 and 10:30. Paul

ASSU Special Events presents:

FESTIVAL, a tradition at

Women's Recreation Association.
It is free and open to the public.

QUARTET will present a concert
in Dinkelspiel Auditorium at 2:30
p.m. Their presentation will
include works by ARRIAGA,
BERG, and BEETHOVEN.

$3.50 for students, $4.50 general.

recital for the San Francisco
Symphony at 3 p.m. at the Opera
House, San Francisco. Tickets for
the recital are on sale at $4,

$5.50, $6.50, $7.50, and $8.50
for a single box seat.

Admission is 50 cents.

tonight and Friday matinee;
BROADWAY Friday, Saturday,
Tuesday, Wednesday matinee and
night; and THE TAMING OF
THE SHREW Monday evening.
All evening performances are at
8:30 p.m., all matinees at 2:30
p.m. at the Geary Theatre.
The San Francisco Symphony
Orchestra with Maestro Seiji
Ozawa continue their
performances of works by

DVORAK, BARTOK, and CAGE
tonight at 8 p.m. in Zellerbach
Hall on the Berkeley campus, and
Friday and Saturday at 8:30 p.m.
in the Opera House.
DOC WATSON AND SON, and
SKUNK CABBAGE continue
through Saturday at the Boarding
House, 960 Bush St., San
Francisco. MERLE TRAVIS and
THE GREENBRIAR BOYS start
Tuesday, May 7.
AIRTO AND FINGERS:
FEATURING FLORA PURIM
AND BUSTER WILLIAMS
continue through Sunday at
Keystone Korner, 750 Vallejo St.,
San Francisco. The duo is
replaced Tuesday by the AHMAD
JAMAL TRIO.
THIRD WORLD PHOTOS
exhibit, by Norman Chandler is
continuing upstairs at Tresidder.
Black and White photographs by
Bill Respini are being exhibited in
the Coffee House.

$1.
