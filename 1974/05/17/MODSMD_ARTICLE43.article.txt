# Tennis Team Blanks WSU
## 
### 
Stanford's tennis team easily
advanced to the semifinals of the
Pacific Eight tennis championship
tournament in Seattle yesterday,
defeating hapless Washington
State, weakest team in the league,
9-0.
The Cardinals face California
today at 9 a.m. The Bears, fourth
seeded, beat host Washington 7-2.
In other matches, UCLA
crushed Oregon 9-0 and USC beat
Oregon State 8-1. The Trojans,
whose sole loss yesterday came
when first singles entrant John
Andrews was beaten, and Bruins
square off in a 2 p.m. match
today.
Jim Delaney, who has taken
over the number one position for
the Cards, beat Tim Randolph
6-1, 6-3. At second singles, John
Whitlinger topped Steve Crow 6-0,
6-2, while at third, Chico Hagey
downed Craig Clayton 6-1, 6-1.
The newly-revised bottom half
of the lineup did similarly well.
Gene Mayer, now out of his big
brother's shadow (Alex quit the
team Tuesday), beat Bill Kodama
6-2, 6-2, Nick Saviano downed Sid
Korten 6-2, 6-2 and Mark Mitchell
jolted Don Judy, 6-0, 6-2.
At first doubles, the hastily
prepared team of Whitlinger-
Delaney beat Randolph-Crow,
6-4, 6-1. The more experienced
(by the length of the season)
teams of Mitchell-Saviano and
Hagey-Mayer also won.
