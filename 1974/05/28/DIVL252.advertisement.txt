Election to Phi Beta Kappa represents high scholastic achievement. In selecting its members the
Society seeks to uphold the traditional values of the Liberal Arts. The basis for selection is
evidence of performance of high distinction in an academic program that exhibits depth and
breadth and in which each of the disciplinary areas — humanities, sciences, social sciences — is
well represented.

Election to Phi Beta Kappa represents high scholastic achievement. In selecting its members the
Society seeks to uphold the traditional values of the Liberal Arts. The basis for selection is
evidence of performance of high distinction in an academic program that exhibits depth and
breadth and in which each of the disciplinary areas — humanities, sciences, social sciences — is
well represented.
10 percent of the Senior Class and 2.5 percent of the Junior Class were elected to membership in
the Society at the Chapter meeting of May 21, 1974. No more than 12 percent of each graduating
class can be elected to membership in the Chapter. The new electees for 1974 are:
