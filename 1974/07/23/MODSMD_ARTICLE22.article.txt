# Hagey Wins Major U.S. Net Tourney
## 
### 
Stanford junior Chico Hagey
won the USLTA Men's National
Amateur Grass Courts
Championships Sunday at
Newport, R.I.
Hagey beat Peter Fleming of
Michigan 6-5, 3-6, 6-2, 6-3 in the
finals of the event. Hagey got a
key service break midway through
the third set and coasted home.
Hagey succeeds teammate Jim
Delaney as national grass
champion.
Another Stanford tennis
player, Chip Fisher, who suffered
a horrendous slump this past year,
showed signs of returning to his
game, finishing second in the
Pacific Northwest Tennis
Championships. The unseeded
Fisher reached the finals before
bowing to top-seeded Keith
Hancock of Australia, 6-3, 6-4.
Another Stanford player, Mark
Mitchell, reached the semis before
losing to Hancock. Both Mitchell
and Fisher are Palo Alto natives.
Still another Stanford player,
Gene Mayer, reached the finals of
the St. Louis Tennis
Tournament's Boys' 18's. Mayer
lost to Bruce Manson, who'll go to
USC, 6-4, 3-6, 6-4 in the finals.
