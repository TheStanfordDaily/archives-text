# News Notes
## 
### 
Craig Haney, Russel Sage
Resident in Law and Psychology
at the Stanford Law School, will
speak Thursday at 8 p.m. in the
Tresidder Lounges on "People in
Prison: An Experiment in Human
Nature." His talk will be
accompanied by slides.
Haney's lecture is part of the
Tresidder Program Board's "Seven
Conversations" series.
* * *
Dr. Robert T. Jones, senior
staff scientist at the NASA-Ames
Research Center, will speak on
"Oblique Wings for Noise-Free
Flight at Transonic Speeds,"
Thursday at 4:15 p.m. in Skilling
Auditorium.
The lecture is sponsored by the
Department of Aeronautics and
Astronautics and is part of the
1974 ASEE-NASA summer
Faculty Institute.
* * *
"Who Shot J.F.K.?," a lecture
on the Kennedy Assassination,
will be held Wednesday at 8 p.m.
at Spangenberg Auditorium, 780
Arastradero Rd., Palo Alto.
Admission is $1.25.
The program will include slides
and a home movie of the
assassination. It is sponsored by
the Assassination Information
Bureau.
