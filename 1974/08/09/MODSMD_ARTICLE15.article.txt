# Ford Cancels Visit
## 
### 
Vice President Gerald Ford has cancelled his speaking
engagement at the centennial commemoration of President
Herbert Hoover's birthday here. Ford, who will be attending
to Presidential matters, had planned to speak in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium at 8 p.m. Saturday.
Sen. Mark Hatfield, R-Ore., will address a convocation in
Memorial Auditorium at 4:30 p.m. as planned. Hatfield's
speech is closed to the public, but will be broadcast live over
KZSU 90.1 FM.
Hatfield's visit to Stanford will include dinner with
President and Mrs. Richard Lyman Friday night, breakfast
with students Saturday morning and a guided tour of the
Hoover Institute for War, Revolution and Peace with Institute
director W. Glenn Campbell Saturday afternoon.
Ironically, President Richard Nixon cancelled a similar
speaking engagement here in 1967 to dedicate the Lou Henry
Hoover building in the Hoover Institute. Nixon did not appear
due to the death of his mother.
