# Bay Area Spectrum
##

CONTINUING EVENTS
Recent sculptures by artist
CARRIE ABRAMOVITZ are on
display in the 2nd floor gallery of
Tresidder Union. Works in bronze,
slate, alabaster and wood can be
seen through the month of
October.
PRINTS FROM CIRBUS
EDITIONS, a collection of 70
pieces by such artists as Joe
Goode, Tom Holland, Ed Moses,
Ed Ruscha and Bruce Nauman,
will be exhibited through October
31 in the Euphrat Gallery, De
Anza College. The gallery is open
from 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. Monday
through Friday, and 11 a.m. to 2
p.m. Saturdays.
ORIENTAL RUG EXHIBITION,
assembled from 25 collections in
the Bay Area and the Robert H.
Lowie Museum of Anthropology
in Berkeley, continues through
November 10 at the Palo Alto
Cultural Center.
An exhibit of 25 pieces created by

artist MATT KAHN runs through
Oct. 25 at the Syntex Gallery,
3401 Hillview Ave., Palo Alto.
The gallery is open from 10 a.m.
to 4 p.m. Monday through Friday,
and 1 to 4 p.m. on Saturday.
GLADY KNIGHT & THE PIPS
continue their run at the Circle
Star Theatre in San Carlos. Ticket
prices for their performances
range from $4.50 to $7.50. For
further information, contact the
Circle Star Theatre.
TRAFFIC continues at
Winterland tonight top billed with
LINDISFARNE. Tickets to the 8
p.m. performance are $6 at the
door.
The ACT season continues with
KING RICHARD 111 being
performed at 8:30 p.m. tonight,
tomorrow, Monday and Tuesday
night, and at 2:30 p.m. on
Saturday. PILLARS OF THE
COMMUNITY will be performed
on Wednesday night at 8:30 p.m.
EVOLUTION OF THE BLUES

continues this week at the On
Broadway Theatre, 435
Broadway, San Francisco. For
showtimesand ticket information,
call the theatre.
COLD BLOOD with LYDIA
PENSE continues tonight at The
Orphanage, 807 Montgomery
Street, San Francisco. There is a
21 year age minimum. For further
information, call 863-8622. On
Friday and Sunday, CISUM is
playing, and starting Monday, the
AVERAGE WHITE BAND starts.
JOHNNY NASH continues
through Sunday night at the
Boarding House. Replacing them
next Wednesday is COMMANDER
CODY AND HIS LOST PLANET
AIRMEN. The Boarding House is
located at 960 Rush St., San
Francisco. For further
information, call 441-4333.
THURSDAY, OCT. 3
Another GALLERY
WALKAROUND will be
sponsored by CAPA, Council for
the Arts, Palo Alto, from 7:30 to
10 p.m. The evening's walkaround
will feature entertainment,
strolling musicians, refreshments
and art-in-action.
CHUCK MANGIONE performs
tonight at the Great American
Music Hall. Friday and Saturday
night, LARRY CORYELL and
the ELEVENTH HOUSE take
over, followed by JERRY
GARCIA/MERL SAUNDERS on
Sunday night. TRACY NELSON
and MOTHER EARTH are
playing on Tuesday and
Wednesday.
John Pasqualetti's PACIFIC
BALLET performs tonight,
tomorrow and Sunday night at
the Marin Veteran's Auditorium.
For further information, call
472-3500.
FRIDAY, OCT. 4
The Stanford Film Society
presents Sergei Eisenstein's
STRIKE at 7:30 p.m.; and Erich
von Stroheim's FOOLISH WIVES
at 9 p.m. in Cubberley
Auditorium. Tickets are $1 for
one or both features.
The Lively Arts at Stanford
present the PRESERVATION
HALL JAZZ BAND at 8 p.m. in

Memorial Auditorium. Tickets are
$4.75 general admission and
$2.75 for students.
PAUL GARRISTON, clarinet, and
MARIE BIRD, piano, will
perform in a noon concert at
Tresidder Union Large Lounge.
Works of Wanhal, Brahms, and
Berg will be heard.
Stanford India Association
presents AN EVENING OF
INDIAN DANCES, featuring
artist Astaad Deboo; at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Tickets
are on sale at the l-Center, $2.25
general admission and $1.75 for
students.
SHADOW OF A DOUBT, directed
by Alfred Hitchcock, will be
shown in the Tresidder Lounge,
2nd floork at 8 and 10 p.m.
Admission is $1.
The Paramount Theatre presents
JAZZ AT THE PARAMOUNT,
featuring Freddie Hubbard
Quintet and the All Stars, Stanley
Turrentine, Sonny Stitt, Kenny
Burrell and others. Tickets for the
8:30 p.m. show are $5 and $6.
FESTIVAL OF JAZZ, a two-night
show, will be held at theTeleport
Lounge in Berkeley, 2445
Telegraph Ave. Friday night will
feature the groups of Woody
Shaw, Julian Priester, and Michael
White. Saturday night's line-up
has the Eddie Henderson Group,
the Bobby Hutcherson Group,
and Infinite Sound. All seats are
$3 and the first show starts at
8:30 p.m.

SATURDAY, OCT. 5
THE PHILIPPINE DANCE
COMPANY will trace the cultural
heritage of their nation in an hour
film called BAYANIHAN. A
dance troupe of young members
from the Filipino community and
Foothill students will perform
additional folk dances in person.
The show starts at 10:30 a.m. at
Foothill Theatre and tickets are
50 cents at the door.
STANFORD NIGHT AT
MARINE WORLD features music
from 7-12 p.m. by The Shellback
Rose in addition to a full Marine
World Show. Tickets are on sale
for $4 and transportation by bus
may be obtained for $1.50.
SUNDAY, OCT. 6
MARK TWAIN IN ITALY, by
Dick Reddy, is this week's feature
at the De Anza College Sunday
evening film series. Program starts
at 7:30 p.nV. at the Flint Center
and tickets are $2 general
admission, $1 students.
A free recital will be given by
Vicki Hale Riley, organist, today
at 2:30 p.m. in Memorial Church.
Her program includes works by

Frescobaldi, Walther, Sweelinck,
Bach and others.
JOAN BAEZ & MIMI FARINA
appear today at Frost
Ampitheatre in an afternoon
concert. All tickets are $3.50 for
the 2 p.m. performance and the
gates open at noon.
The ASSU Sunday Flicks presents
Woody Allen in SLEEPER tonight
at 7 p.m. and 9 p.m. Also
included is the short flick, Pigskin
Capers. Tickets, 50 cents.
Keyboard inventions with
personal reminiscences of Louis
Armstrong, Fats Waller, Charlie
Parker and Duke Ellington will be
the content of a solo concert by
pianist Eart 'Fatha' Hines today at
Foothill College Theater. Tickets
for the 7:30 p.m. performance are
$3.50 and are available through
the Foothill College and Flint
Center box offices.
TUESDAY, OCT. 8
The Lively Arts at Stanford will
present the NEW YORK
CHAMBER SOLOISTS tonight at
8 p.m. in Dinkelspiel Auditorium.
The ensemble of two voices, two
winds, five strings, and two

keyboards will perform
"Selections from the Songbook of
Anna Magdalena Bach" and other
"Musical Offerings." Tickets are
$.75 general admission, $2.75 for
students.
FRENCH CANCAN, a film about
Parisian night life in the 1880s, is
this week's offering of the French
Cine-Club at Stanford. Showtimes
in Bishop Auditorium are 7:30
and 9:30 p.m. Admission is $1.
ALICE STUART, CLOVER and
STEAMING FREEMAN appear
tonight at Winterland. Tickets are
$6 at the door.
WEDNESDAY, OCT. 9
The Departments of
Communication, English, and Art
present the first of a series of
personal appearances by leading
American experimental
filmmakers with an appearance by
James Broughton. Broughton will
present a selection of his films
tonight in Bishop Auditorium at
7:30 p.m. Admission is $1.
ELTON JOHN and KIKI DEE
perform to a sellout crowd
tonight at the Cow Palace.
Showtime is 8 p.m.
