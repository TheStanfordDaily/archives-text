Computer User Forum
Academic Computing Services is
sponsoring an open meeting at noon
today in Room 111 Polya Hall to
inform the computer community of
new developments at the 360/67
facility.
The forum will cover new rates, the
undergraduate computing experiment,
improvements in the system, the
upgrading of current terminal
equipment and a live demonstration.
Famine Relief Dance
The ASSU and the African Famine
Relief Fund are co-sponsoring a dance
to welcome new students tonight from
7 to 9:30 p.m. in the courtyard behind
Tresidder. There will be free food and
the band "Pure Gold" will perform.
Cranston Party Tomorrow
A campaign party for Sen. Alan
Cranston (D-Calif.) will be held
tomorrow from 3 to 6 p.m. at the
home of Dr. and Mrs. Robert Ziegler,
1052 Edgewood Road, Redwood City.
The event is open to the public and
students can attend for a $2.50
contribution. Adults must contribute
$5, $10 or $15.
The party will feature swimming,
volleyball, ping-pong, apple-bobbing,
live music, refreshments and a no-host
bar. Cranston is scheduled to arrive at
4 p.m. to meet and talk with the
guests.
Speakers For Amnesty
Mrs. Patricia Simon, national
coordinator of Gold Star Parents for
Amnesty, and David Harris, former
draft resister and former Stanford
student body president, will speak
tomorrow at 7:30 p.m. at Kepler's
Book Store at 825 El Camino Real in
Menlo Park.
Simon, who comes from Boston, is
touring the West as part of a national
campaign to overturn President Gerald
Ford's conditional amnesty program.
Harris is currently a writer for Rolling
Stone.
Malcolm Boyd To Preach
Malcolm Boyd, Episcopal priest,
peace activist, church reformer and
author, will deliver a sermon entitled
"Superstate with a Superchrist"
Sunday at Memorial Church at 11 a.m.
He will also preach at the Episcopal
Eucharist at the University Lutheran
Church Sunday at 4 p.m.
The public is invited to talk with
Boyd at 4 p.m. Monday at a sherry
hour in the Memorial Church Round
Room and at 8 p.m. at 610 Gerona
Road on the Stanford campus.

Famine Relief Dance
The ASSU and the African Famine
Relief Fund are co-sponsoring a dance
to welcome new students tonight from
7 to 9:30 p.m. in the courtyard behind
Tresidder. There will be free food and
the band "Pure Gold" will perform.
Cranston Party Tomorrow
A campaign party for Sen. Alan
Cranston (D-Calif.) will be held
tomorrow from 3 to 6 p.m. at the
home of Dr. and Mrs. Robert Ziegler,
1052 Edgewood Road, Redwood City.
The event is open to the public and
students can attend for a $2.50
contribution. Adults must contribute
$5, $10 or $15.
The party will feature swimming,
volleyball, ping-pong, apple-bobbing,
live music, refreshments and a no-host
bar. Cranston is scheduled to arrive at
4 p.m. to meet and talk with the
guests.
Speakers For Amnesty
Mrs. Patricia Simon, national
coordinator of Gold Star Parents for
Amnesty, and David Harris, former
draft resister and former Stanford
student body president, will speak
tomorrow at 7:30 p.m. at Kepler's
Book Store at 825 El Camino Real in
Menlo Park.
Simon, who comes from Boston, is
touring the West as part of a national
campaign to overturn President Gerald
Ford's conditional amnesty program.
Harris is currently a writer for Rolling
Stone.
Malcolm Boyd To Preach
Malcolm Boyd, Episcopal priest,
peace activist, church reformer and
author, will deliver a sermon entitled
"Superstate with a Superchrist"
Sunday at Memorial Church at 11 a.m.
He will also preach at the Episcopal
Eucharist at the University Lutheran
Church Sunday at 4 p.m.
The public is invited to talk with
Boyd at 4 p.m. Monday at a sherry
hour in the Memorial Church Round
Room and at 8 p.m. at 610 Gerona
Road on the Stanford campus.
Computer User Forum
Academic Computing Services is
sponsoring an open meeting at noon
today in Room 111 Polya Hall to
inform the computer community of
new developments at the 360/67
facility.
The forum will cover new rates, the
undergraduate computing experiment,
improvements in the system, the
upgrading of current terminal
equipment and a live demonstration.
