ASSU Voting Continues
balloting for 20 ASSIJ Senate*
positions concludes today.
Polling places will be open from 10
a.m. to 2 p.m. at the history corner of
the Quad, the Medical School and the
physics tank. Polls will be open from
10 a.m. to 4 p.m. at the post office
and at the Quad's engineering corner.
'Science And Government'
Speech
Wolfgang Panofsky, director of the
Stanford Linear Accelerator Center,
will speak tonight at 8 p.m. in the
Tresidder lounges on "Science and
(lovernment."
Bern To Speak On Sex-Typing
Asst. Psychology Prof. Sandra Bern
will discuss her research on sex-typing
in the first Center for Research on
Womens (CROW) lecture of the year
at noon today in room 86 of the
business school.
Bern has been researching sex roles
for several years and has developed an
"androgyny Scale," which measures a
person's sex-typing.

Poster Design Competition
The Drama Department is holding a
campus-wide poster design
competition in connection with its
upcoming production of Hugo von
Hofmannsthal's Electra.
The entry which the panel
considers most appropriate will be
reproduced by the Stanford
Reprographic Services and distributed
on campus and throughout the Bay
Area.
An informational meeting will be
held tomorrow in room 125, Memorial
Auditorium, at noon. Director Ellen
Mease and Set Designer Richard
Riddell will explain their
interpretation of the play and faculty
designer William Eddelman will
describe the graphics process to be
used.
Panel To Probe U.S. Role In Chile
And Vietnam
The impact of American
Involvement on the recent histories of
Chile and Vietnam will be the subject
of a panel discussion by Indochina and
Latin American experts, tonight at
7:30 p.m. in Bishop Auditorium.
