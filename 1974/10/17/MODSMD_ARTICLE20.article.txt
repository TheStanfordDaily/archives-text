# Entertainment
## Bay Area Spectrum

CONTINUING EVENTS

The ACT season continues with
KING RICHARD 111 playing
tonight, tomorrow, Monday and
Tuesday at 8:30 p.m. There is a
matinee performance on Sunday
at 2:30 p.m. PILLARS OF THE
COMMUNITY plays Saturday and

Wednesday nights at 8:30 p.m.
For further information, call the
Geary Theatre in San Francisco.
The GRATEFUL DEAD continue
their five night stint at
Winterland. The DEAD play at
Winterland every night through
Sunday. Showtimes are at 7 p.m.

rather than the usual Winterland
time of 8 p.m.
JIMMY BUFFET and JERRY
RIOPELLE play through Sunday
night at the Boarding House, 960
Bush St., San Francisco.
Following them at the club on
Tuesday and playing through

November 3 is BO DIDDLEY
with RAW SOUL. For further
information, call 441-4333.
An exhibition of eight paintings
and 46 drawings by Genoan
painter LUCA CAMBIASO is now
on view at the Stanford Art
Gallery and will continue through
Oct. 27.
Ink drawings and airbrush
impressions produced by
PHYLLIS SHUGER of Palo Alto
are on display through October at
the Bechtel International Center.
Exhibit theme is "Transition to
Phantasmagoria," reflecting
successive changes in the artist's
style from sharp line and detail to
an illusory technique of
fluctuating and receding figures.
An exhibition of 25 pieces created
by artist MATT KAHN runs
through Oct. 25 at the Syntex
Gallery, 3401 Hillview Ave., Palo
Alto. The gallery is open from 10
a.m. to 4 p.m. Monday through
Friday, and 1 to 4 p.m. on
Saturday.
THURSDAY, OCT. 17
THE SAN FRANCISCO FILM
FESTIVAL starts today (for the
public) and continues throughout
the week with 14 different movies
being presented at nighttime
screenings. Admission price is $4
for the evening programs at the
Palace of Fine Arts Theatre,
Marina Boulevard at Lyon Street
in the Marina. For further
information, call the Peninsula
Ticket Agency in Los Altos.
JOEL GREY opens in the
Venetian Room of the Fairmont
Hotel tonight and will perform
twice nightly through Oct. 27.
Showtimes are 9:30 p.m. and
midnight (except Monday). For
further information, call the
Fairmont Hotel at 362-8800.

A lecture on TRANSCENDEN
TALISM IN AMERICA by
Professor Albert Gelpi will be
given today at 2:15 p.m. in
Annenberg Auditorium. This is
part of the Committee for Art at
Stanford program entitled ART
PLUS.
BUTCH WHACKS and THE
GLASS PACKS perform tonight
at the Great American Music Hall,
859 O'Farrell, San Francisco. No
minors are allowed tonight. For
further information, call the hall
at 885-0750.
FRIDAY, OCT. 18
The PAUL ANKA show opens
tonight and runs through Sunday
night. Prices range from $4.50 to
$7.50 for his three evening
performances, and his Sunday
matinee. Tickets are available at
the Circle Star Theatre ticket
office.
SARAH VAUGHN performs
tonight and tomorrow night at the
Great American Music Hall.
Following her at the hall on
Monday and Tuesday is TRACY
NELSON and MOTHER EARTH.
On Wednesday and Thursday,
JERRY JEFF WALKER takes
over. Minors are welcome to all of
these performances.
The De Anza Friday Night Film
Series offers WOMAN IN THE
DUNES, a Japanese film probing
the fundamental human questions
of identity and freedom.
Showtime is 8 p.m. in De Anza
Forum Building 1 and tickets are
$1 at the door.
The Stanford India Association
presents the Hindu movie
YADON Kl BARRAT (with

English subtitles) tonight at 8
p.m. in Bishop Auditorium.
Tickets are $1.75 for members
and $2.25 for non-members.
JAMES WELCH, Stanford's
Assistant Organist and master's
candidate, will present a musically
contrasting program at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Included
in the program is Bach's "Prelude
and Fugue in D major" and the
six "Schuebler Chorales," and
music of contemporary Austrian
composers. Admission is free.
The BIG SLEEP with Humphrey
Bogart and Lauren Bacall is this
week's offering of the Tresidder
Film Series. Showtimes are 8 and
10 p.m. in the 2nd floor lounges
at Tresidder and tickets are $l.
The Lively Arts at Stanford
present THE ROMEROS tonight
at 8 p.m. in Memorial
Auditorium. Tickets for the event
are $3.50-$5.50 for general
admission and $2.75 for students.
The Stanford Film Society
presents Alfred Hitchcock's
PSYCHO at 7 and 10:30 p.m. and
Fritz Lang's M with Peter Morre
at 9 p.m. in Cubberley
Auditorium. Tickets are $l.
SATURDAY, OCT. 19
A STANFORD BRASS
ENSEMBLE, led by James
Duncan, will present an evening of
chamber music at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. The
program is diverse, including
music by Britten, D'lndy, Stevens,
Nelhybel and Hindemith.
Admission is free.
The Law School Film Society
presents SUNDAYS AND
CYBELE at 8 p.m. and 10 p.m. in

Bishop Auditorium. Tickets are
$l.
A CONTEMPORARY ORGAN
CONCERT with organist Barbara
Harbach-George will be held at All
Saints Episcopal Church, 555
Webster St. in Palo Alto. An
admission will be charged for the
concert. For information, call
329-2106 or 322-3811.
LIVE WIRE, an electric vaudeville
musical, will be performed at
midnight at the New Varsity
Theatre in Palo Alto. Advance
tickets are on sale at the New
Varsity.
SUNDAY, OCT. 20
A special free Sunday concert
celebrating the 100 th birthday of
the American composer, Charles
E. Ives, will be held at 3 p.m. in
the Tresidder Union lounges. His
music will be presented by Sondra
Rae Clark, piano, Ronald Erikson,
violin, and Helene Joseph Weil,
soprano.
The ASSU Sunday Flicks present
Franco Zeffirelli's ROMEO AND
JULIET tonight at 7 and 9:30
p.m. in Memorial Auditorium.
Tickets are 50 cents.
MONDAY, OCT. 21
The French Cine Club offers Eric
Rohmer's MA NUIT CHEZ
MAUD, a subtle and
unconventional tale about love.
Showtimes are 7:30 and 9:30
p.m. and tickets are $l.
ANNE OSHETSKY, recent
Stanford graduate, will return to
campus for her fifth annual piano
recital at 8 p.m. tonight in the
Tresidder Union lounges.

Professor Albert Gelpi will be
given today at 2:15 p.m. in
Annenberg Auditorium. This is
part of the Committee for Art at
Stanford program entitled ART
PLUS.
BUTCH WHACKS and THE
GLASS PACKS perform tonight
at the Great American Music Hall,
859 O'Farrell, San Francisco. No
minors are allowed tonight. For
further information, call the hall
at 885-0750.
FRIDAY, OCT. 18
The PAUL ANKA show opens
tonight and runs through Sunday
night. Prices range from $4.50 to
$7.50 for his three evening
performances, and his Sunday
matinee. Tickets are available at
the Circle Star Theatre ticket
office.
SARAH VAUGHN performs
tonight and tomorrow night at the
Great American Music Hall.
Following her at the hall on
Monday and Tuesday is TRACY
NELSON and MOTHER EARTH.
On Wednesday and Thursday,
JERRY JEFF WALKER takes
over. Minors are welcome to all of
these performances.
The De Anza Friday Night Film
Series offers WOMAN IN THE
DUNES, a Japanese film probing
the fundamental human questions
of identity and freedom.
Showtime is 8 p.m. in De Anza
Forum Building 1 and tickets are
$1 at the door.
The Stanford India Association
presents the Hindu movie
YADON Kl BARRAT (with

English subtitles) tonight at 8
p.m. in Bishop Auditorium.
Tickets are $1.75 for members
and $2.25 for non-members.
JAMES WELCH, Stanford's
Assistant Organist and master's
candidate, will present a musically
contrasting program at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Included
in the program is Bach's "Prelude
and Fugue in D major" and the
six "Schuebler Chorales," and
music of contemporary Austrian
composers. Admission is free.
The BIG SLEEP with Humphrey
Bogart and Lauren Bacall is this
week's offering of the Tresidder
Film Series. Showtimes are 8 and
10 p.m. in the 2nd floor lounges
at Tresidder and tickets are $l.
The Lively Arts at Stanford
present THE ROMEROS tonight
at 8 p.m. in Memorial
Auditorium. Tickets for the event
are $3.50-$5.50 for general
admission and $2.75 for students.
The Stanford Film Society
presents Alfred Hitchcock's
PSYCHO at 7 and 10:30 p.m. and
Fritz Lang's M with Peter Morre
at 9 p.m. in Cubberley
Auditorium. Tickets are $l.
SATURDAY, OCT. 19
A STANFORD BRASS
ENSEMBLE, led by James
Duncan, will present an evening of
chamber music at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. The
program is diverse, including
music by Britten, D'lndy, Stevens,
Nelhybel and Hindemith.
Admission is free.
The Law School Film Society
presents SUNDAYS AND
CYBELE at 8 p.m. and 10 p.m. in

Bishop Auditorium. Tickets are
$l.
A CONTEMPORARY ORGAN
CONCERT with organist Barbara
Harbach-George will be held at All
Saints Episcopal Church, 555
Webster St. in Palo Alto. An
admission will be charged for the
concert. For information, call
329-2106 or 322-3811.
LIVE WIRE, an electric vaudeville
musical, will be performed at
midnight at the New Varsity
Theatre in Palo Alto. Advance
tickets are on sale at the New
Varsity.
SUNDAY, OCT. 20
A special free Sunday concert
celebrating the 100 th birthday of
the American composer, Charles
E. Ives, will be held at 3 p.m. in
the Tresidder Union lounges. His
music will be presented by Sondra
Rae Clark, piano, Ronald Erikson,
violin, and Helene Joseph Weil,
soprano.
The ASSU Sunday Flicks present
Franco Zeffirelli's ROMEO AND
JULIET tonight at 7 and 9:30
p.m. in Memorial Auditorium.
Tickets are 50 cents.
MONDAY, OCT. 21
The French Cine Club offers Eric
Rohmer's MA NUIT CHEZ
MAUD, a subtle and
unconventional tale about love.
Showtimes are 7:30 and 9:30
p.m. and tickets are $l.
ANNE OSHETSKY, recent
Stanford graduate, will return to
campus for her fifth annual piano
recital at 8 p.m. tonight in the
Tresidder Union lounges.

JIMMY BUFFET and JERRY
RIOPELLE play through Sunday
night at the Boarding House, 960
Bush St., San Francisco.
Following them at the club on
Tuesday and playing through

November 3 is BO DIDDLEY
with RAW SOUL. For further
information, call 441-4333.
An exhibition of eight paintings
and 46 drawings by Genoan
painter LUCA CAMBIASO is now
on view at the Stanford Art
Gallery and will continue through
Oct. 27.
Ink drawings and airbrush
impressions produced by
PHYLLIS SHUGER of Palo Alto
are on display through October at
the Bechtel International Center.
Exhibit theme is "Transition to
Phantasmagoria," reflecting
successive changes in the artist's
style from sharp line and detail to
an illusory technique of
fluctuating and receding figures.
An exhibition of 25 pieces created
by artist MATT KAHN runs
through Oct. 25 at the Syntex
Gallery, 3401 Hillview Ave., Palo
Alto. The gallery is open from 10
a.m. to 4 p.m. Monday through
Friday, and 1 to 4 p.m. on
Saturday.
