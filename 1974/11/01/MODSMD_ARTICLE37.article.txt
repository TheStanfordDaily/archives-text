Chinese Lecture
Berkeley Profs. Tuen Ron Shen and
Chang-Lin Tien will speak on "Science
and Engineering in China" today
at 7:30 p.m. in Jordan Hall 040. Both
have visited China within the last year.
Admission is free.
Leakey Symposium
Dr. Jane Goodall, director of
Stanford's Outdoor Primate Research
Facility, will be featured at an L.S.B.
Leakey Foundation Symposium "In
Search of Man" tomorrow at San
Francisco Masonic Auditorium from
8:30 a.m. to 5 p.m.
Also featured will be Beatrice and
Allen Gardner of the University of
Nevada, Harvard Anthropology Prof.
Irven Devore, Phillip Tobia? from the
Anatomy Department of the
University of the WitWatersrand in
South Africa and Berkeley
Anthropology Prof. Sherwood
Washburn.
Os Guinness
Author, critic and theologian Os
Guinness will speak on "Confession in
Celluloid" tomorrow at 8 p.m. in
Cubberley Auditorium. In his speech,
Guinness will explore a Christian view
of Ingmar Bergman. He will also give
the sermon at 11 a.m. Sunday in
Memorial Church.
Mental Research Benefit
An art show, exhibit and art sale
under the title "Excellence in* the
Arts" will be open from 9 a.m. to 9
p.m. beginning tomorrow through
Nov. 7 at the Oak Creek Tennis Club,
at 1600 Willow Rd. The show is a
benefit for the Mental Research
Institute of Palo Alto which conducts
research into schizophrenia. There is
no charge for the show itself.
There will be a preview party today
from 6 to 9 p.m. featuring music and
refreshments for a $5 charge.
