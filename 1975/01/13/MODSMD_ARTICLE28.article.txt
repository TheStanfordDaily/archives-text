Organizational Decision-Making
James G. March, David Jacks
Professor of Higher Education, will
examine how organizations make
decisions and will look into some of
the problems of leadership in his talk
"Leaders, Decisions and Other Sacred
Myths" today at 3 p.m. in the
Tresidder Large Lounge.
Nutrition Education
Sharon Elliot, a graduate student at
the School of Education, will teach a
course entitled "Foods for a Healthy
Family" beginning this week. The class
will end the week of March 10 and
will feature a lecture-discussion
format. It is offered at two times:
Monday from 9:30-11 a.m. at the
YWCA, 4161 Alma Street, Palo Alto
for a fee of $11; and Wednesdays from
9:30-11:30 a.m. at Stanford's Village

Center for a fee of $l6. Call 324-1831
for more information.
Chinese Art Lecture
Art Prof. Michael Sullivan will
discuss recent archaeological
discoveries in the Peoples' Republic of
China tonight at 8 in Annenberg
Auditorium, Cummings Art Building.
Conservative Forum
Prof. Thomas Molnar, who most
recently taught at Long Island
University and Brooklyn College, will
discuss "Religion and the Sick
Society" tonight at 7:30 in Tresidder
270-271.
The address, sponsored by the
Stanford Conservative Forum, will be
followed by a panel discussion
featuring Classics Prof. Antony
Raubitschek and English graduate
student Philip Palmer.
