YOSEMITE WILDERNESS TREK
February 14-17 Washington's Birthday weekend $l2O per person,
($9O Stanford Students, faculty, staff) includes transportation
from Stanford to Yosemite and return. Repeating last year's sold
out program, we spend three nights at the Yosemite Institute's
Crane Flat camp (bunkhouse style) for a fascinating nature study
of the high country and Yosemite Valley. Instruction will be
provided by Professor Benjamin Page of the Geology Dept., and
the staff of the Yosemite Institute. This instruction includes
winter ecology, geology, cross-country skiing with optional snow
camping and survival training classes. Tuition includes in
struction, cross-country skis, snowshoes, lodging, and deluxe
meals. Bring a sleeping bag and warm clothing. Limited to 45;
space still available.
EXPEDITION TO THE PACIFIC ISLANDS
AND LAGOONS OF DAJA CALIFORNIA
March 10-17 7 days at $550. This trip will be an ocean cruise
from San Diego to Guadalupe, San Benitos, Cedros and San
Martin Islands, and to Baja lagoons on the deluxe vessel Qualifier
105. Traveling by night and exploring by day, shore parties will
investigate the flora, fauna, geology, and history of the region. We
will explore the Baja coastline when wildlife activity is at its
peak—elephant seals and gray whales in their mating season;
killer whales, dolphins, migratory shorebirds, and waterfowl at
their greatest number. Included in the faculty will be Dr.
Raymond Gill More, well-known authority on the gray whale. A
unique classroom for the naturalist and nature photographer.
Limited to 32; few spaces still available.
MEXICO SUITCASE SEMINAR
March 21-April 5 14 days at approximately $1,090 from Los
Angeles. A second offering of this popular in-the-field study of the
art, architecture, history, and culture of the ancient Mayan and
Aztec civilizations. Four days in cosmopolitan Mexico City: the
National Palace, Ballet Folklorico, Tula, Teotihuacan, famous
National Museum of Anthropology. Two-day excursion to
Xochicolco, Taxco, and Cuernavaca. By air to the Yucatan
Peninsula. The spectacular empire city, Chichen Itza, dating
from 435 A.D. Climb the enormous 1,000-columned Temple of the
Warriors pyramid. On to magnificent Uxmal, founded in 1007
A.D., where 20,000 handcut stones adorn the palace. By air to the
lush Caribbean isle of Cozumel for snorkeling in the world's
clearest waters. Ferry boat to the spectacular site of Tulum.
Lectures will be given by Ezra Zubron and James Fox from the
Dept. of Anthropology. Limited to 55; few spaces still available.

MEXICO SUITCASE SEMINAR
March 21-April 5 14 days at approximately $1,090 from Los
Angeles. A second offering of this popular in-the-field study of the
art, architecture, history, and culture of the ancient Mayan and
Aztec civilizations. Four days in cosmopolitan Mexico City: the
National Palace, Ballet Folklorico, Tula, Teotihuacan, famous
National Museum of Anthropology. Two-day excursion to
Xochicolco, Taxco, and Cuernavaca. By air to the Yucatan
Peninsula. The spectacular empire city, Chichen Itza, dating
from 435 A.D. Climb the enormous 1,000-columned Temple of the
Warriors pyramid. On to magnificent Uxmal, founded in 1007
A.D., where 20,000 handcut stones adorn the palace. By air to the
lush Caribbean isle of Cozumel for snorkeling in the world's
clearest waters. Ferry boat to the spectacular site of Tulum.
Lectures will be given by Ezra Zubron and James Fox from the
Dept. of Anthropology. Limited to 55; few spaces still available.

YOSEMITE WILDERNESS TREK
February 14-17 Washington's Birthday weekend $l2O per person,
($9O Stanford Students, faculty, staff) includes transportation
from Stanford to Yosemite and return. Repeating last year's sold
out program, we spend three nights at the Yosemite Institute's
Crane Flat camp (bunkhouse style) for a fascinating nature study
of the high country and Yosemite Valley. Instruction will be
provided by Professor Benjamin Page of the Geology Dept., and
the staff of the Yosemite Institute. This instruction includes
winter ecology, geology, cross-country skiing with optional snow
camping and survival training classes. Tuition includes in
struction, cross-country skis, snowshoes, lodging, and deluxe
meals. Bring a sleeping bag and warm clothing. Limited to 45;
space still available.
EXPEDITION TO THE PACIFIC ISLANDS
AND LAGOONS OF DAJA CALIFORNIA
March 10-17 7 days at $550. This trip will be an ocean cruise
from San Diego to Guadalupe, San Benitos, Cedros and San
Martin Islands, and to Baja lagoons on the deluxe vessel Qualifier
105. Traveling by night and exploring by day, shore parties will
investigate the flora, fauna, geology, and history of the region. We
will explore the Baja coastline when wildlife activity is at its
peak—elephant seals and gray whales in their mating season;
killer whales, dolphins, migratory shorebirds, and waterfowl at
their greatest number. Included in the faculty will be Dr.
Raymond Gill More, well-known authority on the gray whale. A
unique classroom for the naturalist and nature photographer.
Limited to 32; few spaces still available.
