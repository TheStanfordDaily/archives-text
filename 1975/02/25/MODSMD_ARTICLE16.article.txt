# Briefly...
## 
### 
Meet The Candidates
Tonight at 7:30 the League of
Women's Voters is sponsoring Palo
Alto Candidates Night at Palo Alto
High School. Candidates for all local
offices in the March 1 election will
speak and answer questions. The
meeting is free and open to the public.
Goddesses, Etc.
Dr. Kathleen Cohen, head of the art
department at San Jose State, will
present "Goddesses, Witches and
Mortals: A Slide Presentation" today
at noon in Room 86 of the Graduate
School of Business. The free lecture is
sponsored by the Center for Research
on Women.
Political Discussions
Anita Miller, chairwoman of the
California Commission on the Status
of Women, will lead an informal
symposium on "Experiences in
Politics" at 4 p.m. today at Storey
House. Then at 8 p.m. "Sissy"
Faronthold, head of the National
Women's Political Caucus, Law Prof.
Barbara Babcock and Miller will lead a
similar discussion in Donner Lounge.
Renaissance Universal
Renaissance Universal, an
organization trying to create "a new,
universalistic, humanistic world
society," will hold its second meeting
tonight at 7:30 in the Alumna Lounge
of the Women's Clubhouse in the Old
Union. All are welcome to this
meeting of the Stanford chapter of the
world-wide organization.
Jobs In Britain
Students interested in summer
employment in Britain can get help
from the Council on International
Educational Exchange (CIEE). The
service charges $75 for a pre-arranged
job, and $25 to secure working papers.
Jobs usually pay enough for living
expenses. Applications are available

from CIEE, Hotel McAlpin, Suite
2200, Broadway and 34th Street, New
York, New York 10001.
Shock Tube Speaker
The Paul Vielle lecture, traditionally
the keynote address, will be delivered
at the 10th International Shock Tube
Symposium by Aeronautics and
Astronautics Prof. Daniel Bershader.
The Symposium will be held July
14-17 at Kyoto University in Japan.
Bershader was recently elected
chairman of the American Physical
Society's Division of Fluid Dynamics.
Shock tube technology deals with
certain problems of space flight
re-entry into the Earth's atmosphere.
Uridergrads To Vote
Undergraduates may vote today and
tomorrow in the referendum on
proposed changes in the grading
system. The polling places are: the
History Corner and Physics Tank, both
open.from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. today;
and the Post Office and Engineering
Corner, both open from 10 a.m. to 4
p.m. today. There will be different
hours tomorrow.
