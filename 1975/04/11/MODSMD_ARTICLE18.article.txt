11. Stanford Judicial Council
A. Jurisdiction
1. The Stanford Judicial Council (SJC) shall have jurisdic
tion over all student disciplinary cases, (except for those
cases which follow the procedure of E. 2) and its decisions
shall not be appealable except in the sense that all its
decisions must be recommendations to the President of
the University. (Charter Section A.l)
2. Purpose and Scope of These Procedures—The Proce
dure described herein will be used to adjudicate:
a) any case in which the University administration Files
a complaint charging that a student has violated a
University regulation governing student conduct;
b) any case in which a student Files a complaint
charging that a member of the faculty or administra
tion has taken disciplinary action against him without
his consent and without adjudication under the judi
cial system in effect at the time of the alleged
disciplinary action; (CJP Section 1)
c) any case duly pending before the Campus Judicial
Panel.
3. Th» SJC shall not have jurisdiction over offenses
committed outside the University campus except during
participati
3. The SJC shall not have jurisdiction over offenses
committed outside the University campus except during
participation in a University activity, unless such conduct
is a violation ot a specific University regulation. However,
if a student's conduct on or off campus calls into question
the student's membership in the educational community,
either because he has grossly violated elemental standards
of behavior requisite to the maintenance of an education
al community or because his continued presence would
adversely affect the ability of others to pursue their
educational goals, he may be found ineligible to continue
as a member of the University community only by the
SJC.
This provision shall not be interpreted to apply when an
individual's eligibility to continue as a student is put in
question because of academic or medical difficulties.
(Charter Section A.2)
B. Composition
1. Ihe Stanford Judicial Council shall be composed of the
following:
a. A faculty cocliairman appointed by the President
after consultation with the Dean of the Law School.
The faculty cocliairman shall preside over and vote
only to break a tie in cases other than those involving
violations of the Honor Code. (C-15)
b. A law student cochairman appointed by the Presi
dent after consultation with the Dean of the Law
School who shall consult with appropriate law student
groups. The law student cochairman shall preside over
and vote only to break a tie in cases involving
violations of the Honor Code. (C-15)
c. Three faculty members appointed by the Senate of
the Academic Council. (Charter Section B.l(b)
d. Three student members and three student alternates
who are appointed in the following manner:
1. The Dean of Student Affairs shall establish a list
of twenty-four registered students and submit it to
the ASSU Senate. That list shall be established
from a roster of registered students generated at
random and shall consist of the first twenty-four
students on that roster who declare a willingness to
serve.
2. The ASSU Senate shall select three student
members and and three student alternates from
that list.
3. In the case of vacancies declared under Section
B. 3(b) the above process shall be repeated with
the ASSU selecting from a list of four students, for
each such vacancy. (C-15)
e. Should the ASSU Senate not select such student
members and alternates by the last day of spring
quarter preceding the fall quarter in which they shall
commence service, or not fill a vacancy within 15
working days of being notified of such vacancy as
provided in Section 8.3(c), student members shall be
selected at random from the student body under a
system established by the Dean of Student Affairs.
(C-15)
2. Term of Office-The Term of office for each member
and the cochairmen of the SJC (other than those
appointed to fill vacancies declared under Section 8.3)
shall commence on the first day of the fall quarter
following the quarter in which he was appointed. The
term of office of all members shall expire on the day
preceding the first day of the fall quarter following the
quarter in which the member commenced service. (C-15)
3. Vacancy on the Stanford Judicial Council
a. The Faculty Cochairman shall declare a vacancy to
exist when he or a faculty member submits his
resignation from the Stanford Judicial Council, ceases
to be a member of the Stanford faculty, takes a leave
of absence or sabbatical leave from the University,
leaves for an overseas campus, or ceases to perform his
duties as a member of the Stanford Judicial Council.
(CJP Section 19)
b. The Student Cochairman shall declare a vacancy to
exist when he or a student member submits his
resignation from the Stanford Judicial Council, from
the University, is suspended or placed on disciplinary
probation, leaves for an overseas campus, ceases to
perform his duties as a member of the Stanford
Judicial Council, or fails to register within the first
week of any quarter during this term of office-except
that if a student member fails to register for the
summer quarter, his position shall not be declared
vacant for that reason if he has agreed to remain
available to carry out his duties as a member of the
Stanford Judicial Council during that summer quarter.
(CJP Section 19)
c. In the event that such a vacancy is declared, and
alternates are not available to fill the vacancy, the
chairman shall promptly notify the appropriate body
which must repeat the selection process described in
Section B(l) to the extent necessary to fill said
vacancies. (C-15)
C. Appointment of Hearing Officers-The President of the
University will appoint a number of Hearing Officers
consisting of attorneys who are not members of the
faculty, staff, or student body of Stanford. (CJP Section
2(a))
D. Complaint and Notice of Charges-The complaint shall
be filed with the Chairmen of the SJC and the Dean of
Student Affairs before the end of th< academic quarter
following the academic quarter in -vhich the alleged
offense or disciplinary action was, or should reasonably
have been discovered. The complaint shall contain a
specification of the act upon which the charge is based
and the regulation allegedly violated, or a specification of
the disciplinary action taken and the judicial precedures
which were not followed. (CJP Section 3)
Upon receipt of the complaint the Dean shall promptly
send notice by mail to the person charged. The notice
shall instruct the person charged to meet with the Dean of
Student Affairs or his designee for an explanation of the
judicial procedures. (C-15)
E. Alternative Judicial Procedures
1. A person charged may request the Dean of Student
Affairs to refer the case to the presiding cochairman of
the SJC in order to set a hearing. Upon such referral, the

presiding chairman shall proceed as follows:
a. If the complainant and the person charged agree to
a stipulation of all relevant facts, the Chairman may
omit the hearing before a Hearing Officer and submit
the stipulation to the SJC to be acted upon under
these procedures as would a report from a Hearing
Officer. (CJP Section 4)
b. If no such agreement on the relevant facts is
possible, the Chairman shall set the case before a
Hearing Officer for proceedings as provided in Section
F.
2. A person charged who admits to a violation of a
University regulation as charged may have his case heard
entirely by the Dean of Student Affairs or his designee if
the student so requests and the Dean of Student Affairs
agrees, in that event, the determinations of the Dean or
his designee shall not be appealable, except in the sense
that they are recommendations to the President The
Dean of Student Affairs or his designee shall inform the
SJC of his determinations, and the President shall inform
the SJC of his action on the recommendation, and both
shall give their reason therefor in writing. (CJP Section
20, Charter 1.3. C-15)
F. Proceedings of Hearing Officers
1. Calendar of Proceedings-The complainant and the
person charged must be prepared to proceed with a
hearing within a reasonable time after the complaint is
filed. The presiding Chairman of the Stanford Judicial
Council will set a time and place at which a hearing shall
be held by a Hearing Officer for the purpose of deter
mining the facts relevant to the charge and for the
purpose of making a preliminary recommendation as to
whether or not those facts constitute a violation of a
University regulation as charged or disciplinary action
without consent and without adjudication under the
applicable judicial system. The Hearing Officer will be
selected by the Chairman from the panel appointed by
the President. Notice of the time, place, and Hearing
Officer selected will be mailed by the Chairman to the
person charged at least seven (7) days prior to the date of
the first scheduled hearing on the charges. (CJP Section 5)
A challenge to the timeliness of the complaint or request
for a change in the time, place, or Hearing Officer selected
for any hearing or for the consolidation or severance of
any cases must be presented to the presiding Chairman,
together with any supporting written material, at least
forty-eight (48) hours prior to the scheduled hearing. The
Chairman will sustain such a challenge or request if he is
persuaded that there are substantially compelling reasons
therefor. However, a request for postponement made at
any time prior to a hearing shall be sustained by the
Chairman if he is persuaded that the person charged is
unable to attend the scheduled hearing for good cause.
Grounds for such good causc shall consist only of those
set forth in Section F. 4 of these procedures. The Chair
man may in his discretion consult with the Hearing
Officer prior to ruling on such a challenge or request, but
his riiing may not be reconsidered by the Hearing Officer
or the Stanford Judicial Councfl-except during a hearing
to show cause as provided in Section F. 4 of these
procedures. (CJP Section 5)
2. Public or Private Nature of Proceedings Before Hearing
Officers-Proceedings conducted by the Hearing Officer
shall be open to the public unless the person charged and
the Chairman of the Stanford Judicial Council agree that
they shall be private and confidential, or unless the
Hearing Officer, in his discretion, deems it necessary to
exclude the public or otherwise restrict attendance in
order to maintain adequate decorum. All proceedings,
whether public or private, will be recorded on tape, and
the recordings will be retained by the Chairman of the
Stanford Judicial Council or his designee for a minimum
of six months. (CJP Section 6)
3. Conduct of Proceedings Before Hearing Officers—
During the hearing before a Hearing Officer:
a) The hearing shall be conducted in a fair and
expeditious manner. (CJP Section 7)
b) The person charged and/or the complainant may be
represented by counsel, and each may present relevant
evidence and question witnesses presented by the
other. (CJP Section 7)
c) Facts alleged by the complainant must be estab
lished by the complainant beyond a reasonable doubt
(CJP Section 7)
d) The person charged may refuse to give testimony
himself. If the person charged elects to give testimony
he shall be subject to examination on his credibility
and on all matters relevant to the charges against him.
(CJP Section 7)
e) The Hearing Officer shall make all ruling on all
matters relating to the conduct of the hearing before
him including the admission of evidence-but ex
cluding any matter which, under these procedures,
must be presented to and decided by the Chairman of
the Stanford Judicial Council. While technical rules of
evidence shall not apply, the Hearing Officer will
exclude irrelevant or unduly repetitious evidence. (CJP
Section 7)
0 The Hearing Officer may question witnesses for the
purpose of cLuifying matters adduced during the
hearing and may request such additional information
as. in his judgment, will aid him in making his findings
and recommendation. In the absence of counsel for
the complainar t, or the person chargcd, the Hearing
Officer shall ask such questions as may be necessary to
satisfy the Hearing Officer that each witness has been
appropriately questioned on matters concerning the
credibility and all substantially relevant issues of fact
(CJP Section 7}
g) The Hearing Officer may not postpone the com
mencement of a hearing-except that the initial
hearing on the charge shall be postponed if a student
charged docs not appear. See Section F. 4 for the
applicable procedures in the event of such a postpone
ment. (CJP Section 7)
h) The Hearing Officer may, upon a request after a
hearing has commenced, grant a continuance only for
strongly persuasive reasons and only when it is not
otherwise inconsistent with these procedures. The
absence or departure from the hearing of counsel for
the complainant or for the person charged shall not be
grounds for such a continuance, and the departure of
the complainant or the person charged shall be
grounds for such a continuance only if the Hearing

Officer is persuaded that the complainant or person
charged is, for good cause, unable to remain at the
hearing. Grounds for good cause shall consist only of
those set forth in Section F. 4 of these procedures.
(CJP Section 7)
4. Appearance Before the Hearing Officer-When the
complaint falls wi:hin Section A.2(a) of these procedures,
the student charged must appear in person at the com
mencement of his initial hearing before the Hearing
Officer-whether or not his counsel appears. For this
purpose, physical presence constitutes an appearance.
(CJP Section 16)
In the event that the student charged does not appear at
the commencement of his initial hearing, the Hearing
Officer will postpone the hearing and report that fact to
the Chairman of the Stanford Judicial Council. The
Chairman shall then mail notice to the student charged of
the time and place a further hearing before a Hearing
Officer to determine whether or not the student was, for
good cause, unable to appear. If the student charged does
not appear at this further hearing, or upon appearing does
not persuade the Hearing Officer that he was, for good
cause, unable to attend the hearing, the student will be
suspended until such time as hearings on the charges are
held by a Hearing Officer and final recommendations are
submitted to the President by the Stanford Judicial
Council. The Hearing Officer's determination on this
matter shall not be appealable, except in the sense that his
determination shall be a recommendation to the Presi
dent (CJP Section 16)
If the complaint falls within the provisions of Section
A.2(b) of these procedures and the person charged does
not appear before the Hearing Officer, the hearing shall
proceed in his absence—unless the person charged informs
the Chairman of the Judicial Council, in advance of the
hearing, that he will not be able to attend and the
Chairman is persuaded that there is good cause therefor.
(CJP Section 16)
For purposes of these procedures good cause shall consist
only of illness or unavoidable and extraordinary circum
stances, and the fact that a civil or criminal action against
the student charged is pending shall not be considered as
such extraordinary circumstances unless the actual
hearing or trial date of the civil or criminal action
conflicts with the date set for the hearing before the
Hearing Officer or before the Stanford Judicial Council.
(CJP Section 16)
5. Report of the Hearing Officer—The Hearing Officer,
within ten (10) days after the conclusion of the proceed
ings before him, shall submit a report of his findings,
recommendations, and reasons therefor to the person
charged, the complainant, the President of the University,
and the Chairman of the Stanford Judicial Council. The
findings of fact contained in this report are not appeal
able, except in the sense that they are recommendations
to the President. (CJP Section 8)
G. Supplemental Report from Dean of Student Affairs-
In any case which is referred to the Chairman of the SJC
under Section E.l the complainant or the student charged
may petition the presiding Chairman to seek the assis
tance of the Dean of Student Affairs or his designee in
ascertaining relevant background facts which might bear
on any determination of an appropriate penalty. The
Chairman shall, if such a petition is denied, state the
reasons for that action in writing. The Chairman may, on
his own motion, obtain such assistance from the Dean.
The Dean or his designee shall provide the requested
information in writing to the Chairman and to the parties.
(C-15)
H. Preservation of Testimony—lf a student charged is
suspended for failing to appear at his hearing, the Chair
man of the Stanford Judicial Council shall, upon request
of the complainant and after mailing notice to the student
charged, arrange for a proceeding conducted by a Hearing
Officer for the purpose of preserving testimony relevant
to the charges. If, at any subsequent hearing on the
charges, a witness is not available despite a reasonable
effort by the complainant to make him available, the
preserved testimony of that witness will be given the same
weight as would such testimony adduced from the witness
in person at the hearing. (CJP Section 18)
I. Proceedings of the Stanford Judicial Council
1. Calendar of Proceedings-After receipt of the Hearing
Officer's report, the presiding Chairman of the Stanford
Judicial Council shall set a time and place at which a
hearing shall be held before that panel for the purpose of
determining whether or not the facts as found by the
Hearing Officer constitute a violation of a University
regulation as charged, or constitute disciplinary action
without consent and without adjudication under the
applicable judicial system, and for the purpose of deter
mining the appropriate sanction or remedial action, if
any. Notice of the hearing shall be mailed to the person
charged at least five (5) days in advance of such a hearing,
and the person charged shall present to the Chairman any
requests for changes in the time or place of the hearing or
for disqualification of any member of the Stanford
Judicial Council at least forty-eight (48) hours prior to
the time at which the hearing is scheduled. The Chairman
shall grant such a request if he is persuaded that there are
substantially compelling reasons therefor. (CJP Section 9)
2. Quorum-Unless expressly waived by a person charged,
the Stanford Judicial Council will not conduct a hearing
when less than one-half of its members in attendance
(excluding the presiding Chairman) are students, and in
no case will the Stanford Judicial Council conduct a
hearing with less than four (4) of its members plus the
presiding Chairman in attendance. The Chairman, may if
necessary to satisfy either of the proceeding two require
ments, appoint one or more of the student alternates to
attend a hearing. A majority vote of the members
attending a hearing and voting shall be necessary for any
determination by the Stanford Judicial Council. The
Cochairmen may participate in the discussions and delibe
rations of the Stanford Judicial Council, but only the
presiding Chairman shall vote and then only in the event
of a tie. (CJP Section 10)
3. Appearance Before the Stanford Judicial Council-If a
person charged does not appear at the hearing before the
Stanford Judicial Council, or if he departs from the
hearing, the hearing will be conducted in his absence. If,

within three (3) days after such a hearing, the person
charged requests the presiding Chairman to reopen the
hearing, and the Chairman is persuaded that the person
was, for good cause, unable to appear or remain and
unable to so notify the Chairman in advance of the
hearing, the Chairman shall reopen the hearing to receive
such oral argument, motivational statement, pleas in
mitigation and responses thereto, as are permissible under
these procedures. Grounds for good cause shall consist
only of those set forth in Section F. 4 and the Chairman's
determination on this matter shall not be reviewable by
the Stanford Judicial Council. (CJP Section 17)
4. Public or Private Nature of Hearing-Hearings con
ducted by the Stanford Judicial Council shall be open to
the public unless the person charged and the presiding
Chairman agree that they shall be private and confiden
tial; or unless the Chairman, in his discretion, deems it
necessary to exclude the public or otherwise restrict
attendance in order to maintain adequate decorum. All
hearings, whether public or private, will be recorded on
tape, and the recording will be retained by the faculty
Chairman of the Judicial Council or his designee for a
minimum of six months. (CJP Section 11)
5. Conduct a Hearings-During hearings before the Stan
ford Judicial Council:
a) The hearing; shall be conducted in a fair and
expeditious manner.
b) The complainant and/or the person charged may be
represented by counsel.
c) Only written and oral argument relevant to the
questions before the Stanford Judicial Council may be
presented to the panel, and this argument may be
presented only by the complainant and the person
charged.
d) As part of his argument, the person charged may
make a motivational statement and plea in mitigation
of sentence-subject to questioning by members of the
panel.
e) The presiding Chairman of the Stanford Judicial
Council may set time limits on oral argument, may
establish deadlines for receipt of written material and
will make rulingp relating to the conduct of the
hearing before the Stanford Judicial Council. The
Chairman will also rule on motions made during the
hearing—subject to being over-ruled by a majority of
the members of the panel sitting on that case. (CJP
Section 12.(e»
6. Necessity for Further Determination of Fact-In the
event that the Stanford Judicial Council decides that the
resolution of additional disputed facts is necessary before
it can make a determination as to whether or not the facts
as found by the Hearing Officer constitute a violation of
University regulation as charged or disciplinary action
without consent and without adjudication under the
applicable judicial system, the presiding Chairman shall
remand the case to a Hearing Officer for purposes of
making the necessary additional findings. The additional
findings will be submitted to the Stanford Judicial
Council, which may, in its discretion hold another hearing
to permit further oral argument before it in accordance
with these procedures. (CJP Section 13)
7. Determination of the Stanford Judicial Council—Upon
a determination as to whether or not the facts as found
by the Hearing Officer constitute a violation of a Univer
sity regulation as charged, or constitute disciplinary
action without consent and without adjudication under
the applicable judicial system, the Stanford Judicial
Council will recommend the appropriate sanction or
remedial action, if any. (CJP Section 14)
The Stanford Judicial Council shall promptly issue a
written opinion containing its determinations and the
reasons therefor, and shall mail a copy thereof to the
person charged, the complainant, and the President. These
determinations shall not be appealable, except in the
sense that they are recommendations to the President.
(CJP Section 14)
J. Sanctions—Sanctions shall be recommended according
to the circumstances of each individual case and must
conform to restrictions on sanctions promulgated in
accordance with the Legislative and Judicial Charter. In
recommending an appropriate sanction, the Stanford
Judicial Council shall take into account the nature and
seriousness of the offense, the knowledge and intent of
the student, prior violations of University regulations and
prior violations of the criminal law committed on the
campus, and the likelihood, or lack thereof, of similar
misconduct by the student in the future. If in a State or
Federal Court, civil or criminal penalties have already
been assessed against a student in a case arising out of an
incident which is the subject of the case before the
Stanford Judicial Council, the council shall recommend
such additional sanctions, if any, which it believes neces
sary to protect the persons, property, and functions of
the University. The pendency of such a case in a State or
Federal Court shall not, however be cause for delaying
final recommendations from the Stanford Judicial
Council to the President. (CJP Section 21)
K. Action by the President-A claim that the procedures
herein were not properly followed by a Hearing Officer or
the Stanford Judicial Council and the basis of such a
claim, must be submitted in writing to the President
within (4) days of the issuance of the Stanford Judicial
Council report The President will report his action on
any such claim and on the recommendations of the
Stanford Judicial Council to the presiding Chairman, the
person charged, and the complainant. (CJP Section 15) •
L. Defendant Rights Before the Stanford Judicial Council.
1. To be informed of the charge and of the alleged act
upon which the charge is based;
2. To request that the Dean of Students decide the case;
3. To be allowed a reasonable time in which to prepare a
defense;
4. To hear all evidence upon which charges are based,
and to answer this evidence through rebuttal;
5. To call witnesses before the Hearing Officer and to
confront any other witness before the Hearing Officer;
6. To be assured that, unless the defendant asks for an
open hearing, any matters of facts which would tend to
identify the person or persons involved in a case would
be kept confidential;
7. To request that any member of the Council be
disqualified because of prejudice;
8. To be informed of the above rights by proper
summons;
9. To be considered innocent until proved guilty beyond
a reasonable doubt;
10. To be assured that no record of the case is placed on
his transcript;
11 • To have no person presenting evidence against him sit
in judgment upon him. (Charter, Section F.)
12. A student may refuse to incriminate himself. (Charter
Section E. 2)
M. Defendant Cooperation-A student does not have the
right to refuse to cooperate at any phase of the judicial
process described herein. Except where otherwise pro
vided in these procedures, sanctions for refusal to co
operate will consist (1) of placing a hold on next quarter
registration (2) of refusal of graduation in the case of
students in their last quarter. (Charter Section E.)
N. Relation to University Policy-Kach member of the
Stanford Judicial Council is obligated to enforce the
regulations of the Student Conduct Legislative Council or
the legislative product of the President acting after consul
tation with the SCLC as provided in Article I, section D,
2,b. Until such time as the SCLC changes present policy,
the SJC will be obligated in the case of conflicts of law to
enforce the University policy. (Charter Section D)
O. House Councils Subordinate councils will be
abolished with the beginning of the Stanford Judicial
Council (except for overseas campuses or other groups
not regularly in attendance at the home campus.) The
SCLC shall have the right to establish subordinate or
coordinate councils, including house councils, overseas
campus councils, and school councils. The limits to
jurisdiction of these councils, and the array of penalties
available to these councils, shall be specified by the SCLC.
The SCLC will also determine, in advance, which types of
cases may be appealed to the SJC. (Charter Section H).
