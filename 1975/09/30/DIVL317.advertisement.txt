SHORT COMPUTER COURSES
Offered Autumn Quarter
by
Academic Computing Services
Stanford Center for Information Processing
All interested students, faculty and staff are invited to attend these non-credit
computer courses taught by the staff of Academic Computing Services. Introductory as
well as advanced courses describe the services, programminq languages and applications
programs available on Stanford's IBM 360/67 computer located in Pine Hall, Jordan
In most of these courses, computer time is provided for students to do assignments.
Classes meet in Polya Hall (next to Pine Hall) unless otherwise noted.
You may register for these courses by going to the Document Sales Office in Pine Hall
during the hours 10 a.m. - Noon and 1:15 - 5 p.m. Registration for the Introduction to
Academic Computing Services course and the IOPROGM seminar will be accepted by phone
(Ext. 74877). Early registration is advisable as enrollment is limited. Also, a course
will be cancelled if fewer than fifteen persons are enrolled.
For further information on course content, fees, materials used or prerequisites, call
Ext. 74877 or see the Academic Computing Services Bulletin available at the Document
Sales Office.
