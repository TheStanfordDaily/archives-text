# Briefly...

Boat dance
The senior class boat dance is tonight from 8 p.m. to 12
midnight. The boat leaves from Pier 43 V2 (near the wharf).
A few tickets still remain and are now available to all
students. They may be purchased at the ASSU office.
Politics in Korea
Nicola Geiger, formerly a professor at the Friends World
College in Japan will speak on the political situation in
Korea today at 1:30 p.m. in the Junipero Lounge, Wilbur
Hall.
Job fair
The Society of Black Scientists and Engineers will hold
its annual job fair tomorrow at 270 Tresidder Union from
10 a.m. to 12 noon.
Representatives from several science - and engineer
ing - related companies have been invited to interview
students for summer, parttime and full time employment,
as well as answer questions about their companies.
ASSU Senate
The senate will meet on Monday at 7:30 p.m. in the
Business School. Provost William Miller will discuss var
ious topics there.
China talk
Felix Greene, a former Palo Alto resident and frequent

visitor to the People's Republic of China will talk on "Chi
na's Perspective on the World Crisis" tomorrow at 8 p.m.
in Memorial Church.
Greene's film, "The People's Communes," will be
shown at 7:15 p.m.
CORRECTION
A concert of choral and organ music in celebration of
Founder's Day, featuring the Stanford Chorale, will be
held Sunday afternoon at 2:30 in Memorial Church, not
3:30 as listed in yesterday's Daily.
