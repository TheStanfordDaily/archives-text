# USC, UCLA tie for 1st
## Cards 3rd at Ojai; only Forood wins 
### Jill Lieber 
Stanford's Lele Forood de
feated USC's Barb Halquist, 6-1,
6-3, yesterday to win the wo
men's singles championship at
the 77th annual Ojai Intercol
legiate Tennis Tournament.
The Card men and women
sent entries into a total of four
finals yesterday, but Forood was
the only titlist.
In the open division women's
singles finals, Stacy Margolin of
Beverly Hills High School beat
Barb Jordan, 6-1, 6-3.
Bruce Manson and Chris
Lewis of USC won the men's
doubles title by defeating Mark
Mitchell and Gene Mayer, 6-1,
6-4.
In the men's open doubles fi
nal, Stanford's John Rast and
Craig Johnson lost to 1959
Wimbledon champion Alex Ol
medo and Tom Leonard, 6-3,3-6,
6-3.
The Cards finished third in the
team standings with 12 points.

UCLA and USC tied for first with
15 points each.
Pat DuPre, No. 1 on Stanford's
ladder, advanced to Saturday's
semifinals where he was elimi
nated by Peter Fleming, No. 1 for
top ranked UCLA. 6-1, 5-7, 6-4.
Fleming went on to win the
men's singles championship,
defeating USC's Bruce Manson
in the finals, 3-6, 6-2, 6-2.
Three of the four teams in the
men's doubles semifinals were
from Stanford. Mark Mitchell
and Mayer advanced tothefinals
by beating teammates Matt
Mitchell and Perry Wright, 6-2,
5-7, 7-6. In the other semifinal
match, Lewis and Manson top
ped Bill Maze and DuPre, 6-3,
7-5.
Cards Susie Hagey and Diane
Morrison got as far as the semifi
nals in women's doubles, but
were stopped by Dede Cronk and
Penny Johnson of Long Beach,
6-7, 6-2, 6-2.
