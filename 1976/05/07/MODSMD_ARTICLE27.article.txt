# Only Hagey survives
## Ten Card netters fall at Cal States 
### 
Stanford sent 11 players to the
California State Tennis Champ
ionships at Pebble Beach earlier
this week and, as play moves
into thefourth round today, only
one is still in the tournament.
Card freshman Susie Hagey
stayed alive yesterday by beating
Helle Viragh, 6-4, 6-1.
Cards Perry Wright and Craig
Johnson cruised to the third
round, but then came up short as
the draw got noticeably tougher.
Wright, who upset ex-Card
Chico Hagey in the second
round, was eliminated by Brian
Teacher, the third seed and the
top-ranked player in college,
6-3, 6-3.
Johnson lost to pro Steve
Krulevitz, the fifth seed, 6-3, 5-7,
6-4.
Bill Maze said goodby to
Pebble Beach after dropping a

second-round match to Joao
Soares, No. 1 for eighth-ranked
Pepperdine, 6-2, 6-4.
Matt Mitchell won his open
ing match and then lost to New
Zealand's Chris Gunning, 6-4,
6-4. Mark Mitchell was beaten in
the first round by 17-year-old
Gary Stansbury, 6-3, 6-4.
Stanford's Alex Cortes suc
cessfully qualified for the tour
nament and then had the misfor
tune to meet Jim McManus, the
No. 2 seed. McManus won, 4-6,
6-0, 6-3.
Steve Valelly lost in the qual
ifying round, while Card team
mate Dave Becker got as far as
the first round after qualifying.
Card women Joy Hahn and Pat
Varga both lost in second-round
play. Hahn won her first round
match by default and then fell to
Marisa Louis of San Francisco,
6-7, 6-2, 6-0. Varga was defeated
by Cathie Anderson, 6-2, 7-6.
The Cal States, being played at
the Beach and Tennis Club in
Pebble Beach, will continue
through this Sunday.
This is the 76th year the Cal
States have been held. Play on
Wednesday was delayed for
about four hours, due to rain.
On Wednesday, the No. 1 seed
in the championships, Bob Car
michael of Australia, was de
feated by North Hollywood's
Gene Malin in three sets, 6-2,
3-6, 6-3.
Also playing in the champion
ships are UCLA's Brian Teacher
and Ferdi Taygan.
