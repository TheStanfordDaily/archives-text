# University Facilities and Services Available During Summer Quarter, 1976
## 
### 
The following information describes the facilities available during the Summer Session and the
pertinent regulations governing their use. Your identification card entitles you to use these
facilities on a special basis during the period of your participation at Stanford.

BECHTEL INTERNATIONAL CENTER (l-CENTER)
Table tennis .. . pool. .. color TV .. . volleyball.. . etc. Available all summer. Everyone
welcome. Open until midnight except Sunday when it closes at 10 p.m.
BOOKSTORE
The Bookstore is located on White Plaza adjacent to the post office. The hours are
Monday through Saturday, 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. The store has a large supply of books in
general reading as well as the texts currently being used on campus. In addition, it stocks
school supplies, gifts, greeting cards, and souvenir items. Come in and browse while you
are here. When you have returned home, Susan Page, the store's shopper, will personally
handle your mail-order requests. (497-3661)
THE BRIDGE
The Bridge is a counseling and drop-in center, located at 640 Campus Drive, offering free
sevices to the Stanford community and the Palo Alto area. Its diverse volunteer staff
consists of both students and non-students who are trained in listening and non-directive
counseling skills. During the summer the staff maintains an open telephone line each night
after 10 p.m. for anyone seeking assistance with a specific issue or problem, or who simply
wants to talk. This number is 497-3392.
The Bridge is open during the day this summer from 10 a.m. to 10 p.m. on Monday,
Wednesday and Friday. All are welcome during those hours and are invited to drop in . . .
for counseling services, for information and referral services, to browse in our small
reading room for humanistic psychology and personal growth, and to meet some of our
staff persons and to learn more about on-going Bridge groups, classes and other activities.
Specific events scheduled for this summer at The Bridge include weekly workshops in
massage, meditative techniques, and exploring states of consciousness with music, as well
as beginning and advanced counseling classes. The Bridge will also conduct Wednesday
Evening Lecture Series, consisting of films or demonstrations accompanied by small group
discussions on a wide range of topics relating to persona! awareness and growth, lifestyle
alternatives, etc.
Visit The Bridge this summer between 10 a.m. and 10 p.m. on Monday, Wednesday or
Friday ... or call 497-3392. Summer quarter coordinator is Sherry Brainerd ... co
ordinators for 1976-77 are Sue Crissman and Don Gallagher.
PHYSICAL RECREATION
Badminton
Courts are available at the Roble Gym, however, specific times must be arranged by
interested groups. Furnish own rackets and shuttlecocks; clean-soled tennis shoes must be
worn.
Golf
Current green fees are in effect at the Golf Course and Driving Range. Your identification
card, when presented at the Course, will entitle you to play at guest rates. Regular Golf Club
members, faculty and staff, have preference at specified hours. The Driving Range is also
available at reasonable rates.
Handball/Squash
Courts are open only to persons with Stanford ID's. For information and reservations,
call 327-9966, Monday through Friday, after 11 a.m. Courts are adjacent to deGuerre Pool
Complex.
Recreational Swim Program
The deGuerre Pool, open Summer Quarter to Autumn Quarter, is available to faculty,
staff, summer registered students, conference participants, and their immediate families.
Open Monday through Friday, 2 p.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturday, Sunday and Holidays, Noon to 6
p.m. For students registered for Summer, no charge with identification card. Fees for all
other eligible swimmers will be as follows: family season ticket — $30.00; individual
season ticket — $10.00; weekly swim card — $2.50. Summer Recreational Swim Cards can
be purchased only at the Department of Athletics during business hours starting June 7,
1976. No tickets will be sold at the pool. (Special events may occasionally interupt
schedule.)
A special program open to faculty, staff, conference participants and summer registered
students will be open Monday through Friday, Noon to 2 p.m. There is no charge. ID
required. Co-ed. Adults only.
The Lagunita Pool is available to faculty and staff. Monday through Friday, Noon to 2
p.m. ID required. Adults only. Closed weekends.
Table Tennis
Tables are available for use at Roble Gym upon request. Furnish own balls and rackets.
Tennis
Courts near the Encina Gym and adjacent to the Roble Gym are available for recreation
except when in use by regular classes. Tennis shoes must be worn. Furnish own balls and
rackets. No charge. Playing rules are posted at the courts.
Volleyball
Courts are open for co-recreational games except during scheduled classes and/or in
tramural tournaments. The Encina Gym volleyball courts are available daily (outside).
Other Recreational FacMties
Inquiries regarding the use of other recreational facilities may be directed to departmen
tal secretaries of the Department of Athletics, Physical Education and Recreation. (497-
4895 or 497-4591)
RELIGION AT STANFORD
Memorial Church, the center for Protestant and Catholic worship on campus, is situated
at the center of the campus on the Inner Quadrangle. Regular Sunday services continue
throughout the Summer. University Public Worship, which often brings preachers from

throughout the nation representing various denominations, is held at 11 a.m. Evening
Communion is held at 7 p.m. in the Round Room on Sundays when the Eucharist has not
been celebrated at 11 a.m. Theological discussion groups and individual counseling are
also available during the summer.
The Clubhouse, located on White Plaza, is the location for a Friday evening informal
Jewish Service, a Thursday noon Episcopal Communion Service, and the Campus Ministry
offices.
STANFORD UNIVERSITY MUSEUM OF ART
The Museum features permanent collections, including the Rodin sculptures given by
the B.G. Cantor Art Foundation in Los Angeles, and a variety of temporary exhibitions. The
Museum is located at Lomita Drive and Memorial Way. Open Tuesday through Friday, 10
a.m. to 4:45 p.m.; Saturday and Sunday, 1 p.m. to 4:45 p.m. Closed on Mondays.
Tours (Gather in the Museum Lobby)
General interest tours: Sundays at 2 p.m. and Tuesdays at 12:30 p.m.
Rodin collection tours: Wednesdays at 12:15 p.m. and Saturdays at 1:30 p.m.
Special interest tours, or group tours, may be arranged by calling 497-3469 in advance to
make reservations.
Stanford Art Gallery
The Gallery is adjacent to the Cummings Art Building. There will be a special summer
exhibition. The Gallery is open Tuesday through Friday. 10 a.m. to 4:45 p.m.; Saturday and
Sunday, 1 p.m. to 4:45 p.m. Closed on Mondays.
Committee for Art Bookshops I and II
Located in the Museum and Art Gallery, and run by the Committee for Art at Stanford.
Open at the same times as the Museum and Gallery, the Bookshops are well stocked with
art books (including many publications by the Art Department's faculty), posters, post
cards, reproductions, calendars and many other items. The proceeds from these two
Bookshops, which are organized and staffed by the Committee for Art Volunteers, benefit
Art Department and Museum special projects.
TRESIDDER MEMORIAL UNION
The Union provides a variety of facilities and services which include:
Food
The COFFEE HOUSE features made-to-order sandwiches, homemade soup, pastries,
and fine coffees, plus an informal atmosphere and spontaneous entertainment. Open
Monday through Thursday, 11 a.m. to Midnight; Friday, 11 a.m. to 1 a.m.; Saturday, Noon
to 1 a.m.; and Sunday, Noon to Midnight. Hours of operation subject to change without
notice. Call 497-1311 for up-to-the-minute information.
The TRANSITION, located on the ground floor, offers hamburgers, hot dogs, pizza, and a
salad bar daily. Open Monday through Sunday, 11 a.m. to 7:30 p.m.
The COMMONS is in the arcade to the rear of Encina Hall. It offers hot meals as well as
sandwiches, short orders, desserts, ice cream and a salad bar daily. Open Monday through
Friday, 7 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.
MARCEL'S PASTRY SHOP offers fine pastries and desserts, made-to-order cakes and
breads, pies, cookies, ice cream and continental breakfasts. Located in the TRANSITION
dining area. Open Monday through Friday, 7 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Saturday and Sunday, 8 a.m. to
7 p.m.
Performing Arts and Exhibits
Exhibits, including paintings, photography, and other craft by well-known artists and
talented students, are continuously on display on the second floor. There is music and
reading in a secluded place on the second floor, just for relaxing. Enjoy your own kind of
music in one of our listening rooms with over 900 selections to choose from. Or browse
through our newspapers and magazines, study, watch television, or take a nap. Contact
Jean Martin regarding exhibits and programs. Open Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5
p.m. (497-4311)
Recreation
Tresidder's BOWLING CENTER on the ground floor next to the COFFEE HOUSE has
fourteen bowling lanes, six pool tables, and a rack of pinball machines. Open Monday
through Thursday, 10 a.m. to 10:30 p.m.; Friday and Saturday, 10 a.m. to 11:30 p.m.; and
Sunday, Noon to 8 p.m. (497-4316)
Shops and Services
SEQUOIA TRAVEL CENTER offers complete travel service: reservations for all airlines,
steamships and tours; youth fare tickets; Eurail and Britrail passes; hotel reservations, and
traveler's insurance. Located on the ground floor. Open Monday through Friday, 8:30 a.m.
to 5 p.m. (4 97-9401)
The STORE sells magazines, groceries, tobacco, sundries and health and beauty aids.
Open Monday through Saturday, 8 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sunday, 9 a.m. to 7 p.m. (497-4319)
The TICKET OFFICE, located on the second floor, is on the national Ticketron network,
making tickets available for events all over the Bay Area as well as on campus. Tours to
Hearst Castle and campsite reservations for state parks also can be arranged. Contact Susie,
497-4317. Open Monday through Friday, 10:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.
If your hair would like some attention, you can visit the BARBER SHOP on the first floor.
Open Monday through Friday, 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; Saturday, 8:30 a.m. to 5 p.m.
(327-9664)
For convenient check cashing, loans, and other banking services, see the people at the
WELLS FARGO BANK on the second floor, or call 327-6762. Open Monday through
Thursday, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Friday, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Walk-up windows open Monday
through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.

Look for the special events schedule which will periodically be distributed around the

The following information describes the facilities available during the Summer Session and the
pertinent regulations governing their use. Your identification card entitles you to use these
facilities on a special basis during the period of your participation at Stanford.

BECHTEL INTERNATIONAL CENTER (l-CENTER)
Table tennis .. . pool. .. color TV .. . volleyball.. . etc. Available all summer. Everyone
welcome. Open until midnight except Sunday when it closes at 10 p.m.
BOOKSTORE
The Bookstore is located on White Plaza adjacent to the post office. The hours are
Monday through Saturday, 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. The store has a large supply of books in
general reading as well as the texts currently being used on campus. In addition, it stocks
school supplies, gifts, greeting cards, and souvenir items. Come in and browse while you
are here. When you have returned home, Susan Page, the store's shopper, will personally
handle your mail-order requests. (497-3661)
THE BRIDGE
The Bridge is a counseling and drop-in center, located at 640 Campus Drive, offering free
sevices to the Stanford community and the Palo Alto area. Its diverse volunteer staff
consists of both students and non-students who are trained in listening and non-directive
counseling skills. During the summer the staff maintains an open telephone line each night
after 10 p.m. for anyone seeking assistance with a specific issue or problem, or who simply
wants to talk. This number is 497-3392.
The Bridge is open during the day this summer from 10 a.m. to 10 p.m. on Monday,
Wednesday and Friday. All are welcome during those hours and are invited to drop in . . .
for counseling services, for information and referral services, to browse in our small
reading room for humanistic psychology and personal growth, and to meet some of our
staff persons and to learn more about on-going Bridge groups, classes and other activities.
Specific events scheduled for this summer at The Bridge include weekly workshops in
massage, meditative techniques, and exploring states of consciousness with music, as well
as beginning and advanced counseling classes. The Bridge will also conduct Wednesday
Evening Lecture Series, consisting of films or demonstrations accompanied by small group
discussions on a wide range of topics relating to persona! awareness and growth, lifestyle
alternatives, etc.
Visit The Bridge this summer between 10 a.m. and 10 p.m. on Monday, Wednesday or
Friday ... or call 497-3392. Summer quarter coordinator is Sherry Brainerd ... co
ordinators for 1976-77 are Sue Crissman and Don Gallagher.
PHYSICAL RECREATION
Badminton
Courts are available at the Roble Gym, however, specific times must be arranged by
interested groups. Furnish own rackets and shuttlecocks; clean-soled tennis shoes must be
worn.
Golf
Current green fees are in effect at the Golf Course and Driving Range. Your identification
card, when presented at the Course, will entitle you to play at guest rates. Regular Golf Club
members, faculty and staff, have preference at specified hours. The Driving Range is also
available at reasonable rates.
Handball/Squash
Courts are open only to persons with Stanford ID's. For information and reservations,
call 327-9966, Monday through Friday, after 11 a.m. Courts are adjacent to deGuerre Pool
Complex.
Recreational Swim Program
The deGuerre Pool, open Summer Quarter to Autumn Quarter, is available to faculty,
staff, summer registered students, conference participants, and their immediate families.
Open Monday through Friday, 2 p.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturday, Sunday and Holidays, Noon to 6
p.m. For students registered for Summer, no charge with identification card. Fees for all
other eligible swimmers will be as follows: family season ticket — $30.00; individual
season ticket — $10.00; weekly swim card — $2.50. Summer Recreational Swim Cards can
be purchased only at the Department of Athletics during business hours starting June 7,
1976. No tickets will be sold at the pool. (Special events may occasionally interupt
schedule.)
A special program open to faculty, staff, conference participants and summer registered
students will be open Monday through Friday, Noon to 2 p.m. There is no charge. ID
required. Co-ed. Adults only.
The Lagunita Pool is available to faculty and staff. Monday through Friday, Noon to 2
p.m. ID required. Adults only. Closed weekends.
Table Tennis
Tables are available for use at Roble Gym upon request. Furnish own balls and rackets.
Tennis
Courts near the Encina Gym and adjacent to the Roble Gym are available for recreation
except when in use by regular classes. Tennis shoes must be worn. Furnish own balls and
rackets. No charge. Playing rules are posted at the courts.
Volleyball
Courts are open for co-recreational games except during scheduled classes and/or in
tramural tournaments. The Encina Gym volleyball courts are available daily (outside).
Other Recreational FacMties
Inquiries regarding the use of other recreational facilities may be directed to departmen
tal secretaries of the Department of Athletics, Physical Education and Recreation. (497-
4895 or 497-4591)
RELIGION AT STANFORD
Memorial Church, the center for Protestant and Catholic worship on campus, is situated
at the center of the campus on the Inner Quadrangle. Regular Sunday services continue
throughout the Summer. University Public Worship, which often brings preachers from

throughout the nation representing various denominations, is held at 11 a.m. Evening
Communion is held at 7 p.m. in the Round Room on Sundays when the Eucharist has not
been celebrated at 11 a.m. Theological discussion groups and individual counseling are
also available during the summer.
The Clubhouse, located on White Plaza, is the location for a Friday evening informal
Jewish Service, a Thursday noon Episcopal Communion Service, and the Campus Ministry
offices.
STANFORD UNIVERSITY MUSEUM OF ART
The Museum features permanent collections, including the Rodin sculptures given by
the B.G. Cantor Art Foundation in Los Angeles, and a variety of temporary exhibitions. The
Museum is located at Lomita Drive and Memorial Way. Open Tuesday through Friday, 10
a.m. to 4:45 p.m.; Saturday and Sunday, 1 p.m. to 4:45 p.m. Closed on Mondays.
Tours (Gather in the Museum Lobby)
General interest tours: Sundays at 2 p.m. and Tuesdays at 12:30 p.m.
Rodin collection tours: Wednesdays at 12:15 p.m. and Saturdays at 1:30 p.m.
Special interest tours, or group tours, may be arranged by calling 497-3469 in advance to
make reservations.
Stanford Art Gallery
The Gallery is adjacent to the Cummings Art Building. There will be a special summer
exhibition. The Gallery is open Tuesday through Friday. 10 a.m. to 4:45 p.m.; Saturday and
Sunday, 1 p.m. to 4:45 p.m. Closed on Mondays.
Committee for Art Bookshops I and II
Located in the Museum and Art Gallery, and run by the Committee for Art at Stanford.
Open at the same times as the Museum and Gallery, the Bookshops are well stocked with
art books (including many publications by the Art Department's faculty), posters, post
cards, reproductions, calendars and many other items. The proceeds from these two
Bookshops, which are organized and staffed by the Committee for Art Volunteers, benefit
Art Department and Museum special projects.

The Union provides a variety of facilities and services which include:
Food
The COFFEE HOUSE features made-to-order sandwiches, homemade soup, pastries,
and fine coffees, plus an informal atmosphere and spontaneous entertainment. Open
Monday through Thursday, 11 a.m. to Midnight; Friday, 11 a.m. to 1 a.m.; Saturday, Noon
to 1 a.m.; and Sunday, Noon to Midnight. Hours of operation subject to change without
notice. Call 497-1311 for up-to-the-minute information.
The TRANSITION, located on the ground floor, offers hamburgers, hot dogs, pizza, and a
salad bar daily. Open Monday through Sunday, 11 a.m. to 7:30 p.m.
The COMMONS is in the arcade to the rear of Encina Hall. It offers hot meals as well as
sandwiches, short orders, desserts, ice cream and a salad bar daily. Open Monday through
Friday, 7 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.
MARCEL'S PASTRY SHOP offers fine pastries and desserts, made-to-order cakes and
breads, pies, cookies, ice cream and continental breakfasts. Located in the TRANSITION
dining area. Open Monday through Friday, 7 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Saturday and Sunday, 8 a.m. to
7 p.m.
Performing Arts and Exhibits
Exhibits, including paintings, photography, and other craft by well-known artists and
talented students, are continuously on display on the second floor. There is music and
reading in a secluded place on the second floor, just for relaxing. Enjoy your own kind of
music in one of our listening rooms with over 900 selections to choose from. Or browse
through our newspapers and magazines, study, watch television, or take a nap. Contact
Jean Martin regarding exhibits and programs. Open Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5
p.m. (497-4311)
Recreation
Tresidder's BOWLING CENTER on the ground floor next to the COFFEE HOUSE has
fourteen bowling lanes, six pool tables, and a rack of pinball machines. Open Monday
through Thursday, 10 a.m. to 10:30 p.m.; Friday and Saturday, 10 a.m. to 11:30 p.m.; and
Sunday, Noon to 8 p.m. (497-4316)
Shops and Services
SEQUOIA TRAVEL CENTER offers complete travel service: reservations for all airlines,
steamships and tours; youth fare tickets; Eurail and Britrail passes; hotel reservations, and
traveler's insurance. Located on the ground floor. Open Monday through Friday, 8:30 a.m.
to 5 p.m. (4 97-9401)
The STORE sells magazines, groceries, tobacco, sundries and health and beauty aids.
Open Monday through Saturday, 8 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sunday, 9 a.m. to 7 p.m. (497-4319)
The TICKET OFFICE, located on the second floor, is on the national Ticketron network,
making tickets available for events all over the Bay Area as well as on campus. Tours to
Hearst Castle and campsite reservations for state parks also can be arranged. Contact Susie,
497-4317. Open Monday through Friday, 10:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.
If your hair would like some attention, you can visit the BARBER SHOP on the first floor.
Open Monday through Friday, 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; Saturday, 8:30 a.m. to 5 p.m.
(327-9664)
For convenient check cashing, loans, and other banking services, see the people at the
WELLS FARGO BANK on the second floor, or call 327-6762. Open Monday through
Thursday, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Friday, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Walk-up windows open Monday
through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.
SPECIAL NOTE
Look for the special events schedule which will periodically be distributed around the
campus.
The following information describes the facilities available during the Summer Session and the
pertinent regulations governing their use. Your identification card entitles you to use these
facilities on a special basis during the period of your participation at Stanford.

BECHTEL INTERNATIONAL CENTER (l-CENTER)
Table tennis .. . pool. .. color TV .. . volleyball.. . etc. Available all summer. Everyone
welcome. Open until midnight except Sunday when it closes at 10 p.m.
BOOKSTORE
The Bookstore is located on White Plaza adjacent to the post office. The hours are
Monday through Saturday, 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. The store has a large supply of books in
general reading as well as the texts currently being used on campus. In addition, it stocks
school supplies, gifts, greeting cards, and souvenir items. Come in and browse while you
are here. When you have returned home, Susan Page, the store's shopper, will personally
handle your mail-order requests. (497-3661)
THE BRIDGE
The Bridge is a counseling and drop-in center, located at 640 Campus Drive, offering free
sevices to the Stanford community and the Palo Alto area. Its diverse volunteer staff
consists of both students and non-students who are trained in listening and non-directive
counseling skills. During the summer the staff maintains an open telephone line each night
after 10 p.m. for anyone seeking assistance with a specific issue or problem, or who simply
wants to talk. This number is 497-3392.
The Bridge is open during the day this summer from 10 a.m. to 10 p.m. on Monday,
Wednesday and Friday. All are welcome during those hours and are invited to drop in . . .
for counseling services, for information and referral services, to browse in our small
reading room for humanistic psychology and personal growth, and to meet some of our
staff persons and to learn more about on-going Bridge groups, classes and other activities.
Specific events scheduled for this summer at The Bridge include weekly workshops in
massage, meditative techniques, and exploring states of consciousness with music, as well
as beginning and advanced counseling classes. The Bridge will also conduct Wednesday
Evening Lecture Series, consisting of films or demonstrations accompanied by small group
discussions on a wide range of topics relating to persona! awareness and growth, lifestyle
alternatives, etc.
Visit The Bridge this summer between 10 a.m. and 10 p.m. on Monday, Wednesday or
Friday ... or call 497-3392. Summer quarter coordinator is Sherry Brainerd ... co
ordinators for 1976-77 are Sue Crissman and Don Gallagher.
PHYSICAL RECREATION
Badminton
Courts are available at the Roble Gym, however, specific times must be arranged by
interested groups. Furnish own rackets and shuttlecocks; clean-soled tennis shoes must be
worn.
Golf
Current green fees are in effect at the Golf Course and Driving Range. Your identification
card, when presented at the Course, will entitle you to play at guest rates. Regular Golf Club
members, faculty and staff, have preference at specified hours. The Driving Range is also
available at reasonable rates.
Handball/Squash
Courts are open only to persons with Stanford ID's. For information and reservations,
call 327-9966, Monday through Friday, after 11 a.m. Courts are adjacent to deGuerre Pool
Complex.
Recreational Swim Program
The deGuerre Pool, open Summer Quarter to Autumn Quarter, is available to faculty,
staff, summer registered students, conference participants, and their immediate families.
Open Monday through Friday, 2 p.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturday, Sunday and Holidays, Noon to 6
p.m. For students registered for Summer, no charge with identification card. Fees for all
other eligible swimmers will be as follows: family season ticket — $30.00; individual
season ticket — $10.00; weekly swim card — $2.50. Summer Recreational Swim Cards can
be purchased only at the Department of Athletics during business hours starting June 7,
1976. No tickets will be sold at the pool. (Special events may occasionally interupt
schedule.)
A special program open to faculty, staff, conference participants and summer registered
students will be open Monday through Friday, Noon to 2 p.m. There is no charge. ID
required. Co-ed. Adults only.
The Lagunita Pool is available to faculty and staff. Monday through Friday, Noon to 2
p.m. ID required. Adults only. Closed weekends.
Table Tennis
Tables are available for use at Roble Gym upon request. Furnish own balls and rackets.
Tennis
Courts near the Encina Gym and adjacent to the Roble Gym are available for recreation
except when in use by regular classes. Tennis shoes must be worn. Furnish own balls and
rackets. No charge. Playing rules are posted at the courts.
Volleyball
Courts are open for co-recreational games except during scheduled classes and/or in
tramural tournaments. The Encina Gym volleyball courts are available daily (outside).
Other Recreational FacMties
Inquiries regarding the use of other recreational facilities may be directed to departmen
tal secretaries of the Department of Athletics, Physical Education and Recreation. (497-
4895 or 497-4591)
RELIGION AT STANFORD
Memorial Church, the center for Protestant and Catholic worship on campus, is situated
at the center of the campus on the Inner Quadrangle. Regular Sunday services continue
throughout the Summer. University Public Worship, which often brings preachers from

throughout the nation representing various denominations, is held at 11 a.m. Evening
Communion is held at 7 p.m. in the Round Room on Sundays when the Eucharist has not
been celebrated at 11 a.m. Theological discussion groups and individual counseling are
also available during the summer.
The Clubhouse, located on White Plaza, is the location for a Friday evening informal
Jewish Service, a Thursday noon Episcopal Communion Service, and the Campus Ministry
offices.
STANFORD UNIVERSITY MUSEUM OF ART
The Museum features permanent collections, including the Rodin sculptures given by
the B.G. Cantor Art Foundation in Los Angeles, and a variety of temporary exhibitions. The
Museum is located at Lomita Drive and Memorial Way. Open Tuesday through Friday, 10
a.m. to 4:45 p.m.; Saturday and Sunday, 1 p.m. to 4:45 p.m. Closed on Mondays.
Tours (Gather in the Museum Lobby)
General interest tours: Sundays at 2 p.m. and Tuesdays at 12:30 p.m.
Rodin collection tours: Wednesdays at 12:15 p.m. and Saturdays at 1:30 p.m.
Special interest tours, or group tours, may be arranged by calling 497-3469 in advance to
make reservations.
Stanford Art Gallery
The Gallery is adjacent to the Cummings Art Building. There will be a special summer
exhibition. The Gallery is open Tuesday through Friday. 10 a.m. to 4:45 p.m.; Saturday and
Sunday, 1 p.m. to 4:45 p.m. Closed on Mondays.
Committee for Art Bookshops I and II
Located in the Museum and Art Gallery, and run by the Committee for Art at Stanford.
Open at the same times as the Museum and Gallery, the Bookshops are well stocked with
art books (including many publications by the Art Department's faculty), posters, post
cards, reproductions, calendars and many other items. The proceeds from these two
Bookshops, which are organized and staffed by the Committee for Art Volunteers, benefit
Art Department and Museum special projects.

BECHTEL INTERNATIONAL CENTER (l-CENTER)
Table tennis .. . pool. .. color TV .. . volleyball.. . etc. Available all summer. Everyone
welcome. Open until midnight except Sunday when it closes at 10 p.m.
BOOKSTORE
The Bookstore is located on White Plaza adjacent to the post office. The hours are
Monday through Saturday, 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. The store has a large supply of books in
general reading as well as the texts currently being used on campus. In addition, it stocks
school supplies, gifts, greeting cards, and souvenir items. Come in and browse while you
are here. When you have returned home, Susan Page, the store's shopper, will personally
handle your mail-order requests. (497-3661)
THE BRIDGE
The Bridge is a counseling and drop-in center, located at 640 Campus Drive, offering free
sevices to the Stanford community and the Palo Alto area. Its diverse volunteer staff
consists of both students and non-students who are trained in listening and non-directive
counseling skills. During the summer the staff maintains an open telephone line each night
after 10 p.m. for anyone seeking assistance with a specific issue or problem, or who simply
wants to talk. This number is 497-3392.
The Bridge is open during the day this summer from 10 a.m. to 10 p.m. on Monday,
Wednesday and Friday. All are welcome during those hours and are invited to drop in . . .
for counseling services, for information and referral services, to browse in our small
reading room for humanistic psychology and personal growth, and to meet some of our
staff persons and to learn more about on-going Bridge groups, classes and other activities.
Specific events scheduled for this summer at The Bridge include weekly workshops in
massage, meditative techniques, and exploring states of consciousness with music, as well
as beginning and advanced counseling classes. The Bridge will also conduct Wednesday
Evening Lecture Series, consisting of films or demonstrations accompanied by small group
discussions on a wide range of topics relating to persona! awareness and growth, lifestyle
alternatives, etc.
Visit The Bridge this summer between 10 a.m. and 10 p.m. on Monday, Wednesday or
Friday ... or call 497-3392. Summer quarter coordinator is Sherry Brainerd ... co
ordinators for 1976-77 are Sue Crissman and Don Gallagher.

Recreational Swim Program
The deGuerre Pool, open Summer Quarter to Autumn Quarter, is available to faculty,
staff, summer registered students, conference participants, and their immediate families.
Open Monday through Friday, 2 p.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturday, Sunday and Holidays, Noon to 6
p.m. For students registered for Summer, no charge with identification card. Fees for all
other eligible swimmers will be as follows: family season ticket — $30.00; individual
season ticket — $10.00; weekly swim card — $2.50. Summer Recreational Swim Cards can
be purchased only at the Department of Athletics during business hours starting June 7,
1976. No tickets will be sold at the pool. (Special events may occasionally interupt
schedule.)

Open Monday through Friday, 2 p.m. to 6 p.m.; Saturday, Sunday and Holidays, Noon to 6
p.m. For students registered for Summer, no charge with identification card. Fees for all
other eligible swimmers will be as follows: family season ticket — $30.00; individual
season ticket — $10.00; weekly swim card — $2.50. Summer Recreational Swim Cards can
be purchased only at the Department of Athletics during business hours starting June 7,
1976. No tickets will be sold at the pool. (Special events may occasionally interupt
schedule.)
A special program open to faculty, staff, conference participants and summer registered
students will be open Monday through Friday, Noon to 2 p.m. There is no charge. ID
required. Co-ed. Adults only.
The Lagunita Pool is available to faculty and staff. Monday through Friday, Noon to 2
p.m. ID required. Adults only. Closed weekends.
Table Tennis
Tables are available for use at Roble Gym upon request. Furnish own balls and rackets.
Tennis
Courts near the Encina Gym and adjacent to the Roble Gym are available for recreation
except when in use by regular classes. Tennis shoes must be worn. Furnish own balls and
rackets. No charge. Playing rules are posted at the courts.
Volleyball
Courts are open for co-recreational games except during scheduled classes and/or in
tramural tournaments. The Encina Gym volleyball courts are available daily (outside).

Inquiries regarding the use of other recreational facilities may be directed to departmen

4895 or 497-4591)
RELIGION AT STANFORD
Memorial Church, the center for Protestant and Catholic worship on campus, is situated
at the center of the campus on the Inner Quadrangle. Regular Sunday services continue
throughout the Summer. University Public Worship, which often brings preachers from

throughout the nation representing various denominations, is held at 11 a.m. Evening
Communion is held at 7 p.m. in the Round Room on Sundays when the Eucharist has not
been celebrated at 11 a.m. Theological discussion groups and individual counseling are
also available during the summer.
The Clubhouse, located on White Plaza, is the location for a Friday evening informal
Jewish Service, a Thursday noon Episcopal Communion Service, and the Campus Ministry
offices.
STANFORD UNIVERSITY MUSEUM OF ART
The Museum features permanent collections, including the Rodin sculptures given by
the B.G. Cantor Art Foundation in Los Angeles, and a variety of temporary exhibitions. The
Museum is located at Lomita Drive and Memorial Way. Open Tuesday through Friday, 10
a.m. to 4:45 p.m.; Saturday and Sunday, 1 p.m. to 4:45 p.m. Closed on Mondays.
Tours (Gather in the Museum Lobby)
General interest tours: Sundays at 2 p.m. and Tuesdays at 12:30 p.m.
Rodin collection tours: Wednesdays at 12:15 p.m. and Saturdays at 1:30 p.m.
Special interest tours, or group tours, may be arranged by calling 497-3469 in advance to
make reservations.
Stanford Art Gallery
The Gallery is adjacent to the Cummings Art Building. There will be a special summer
exhibition. The Gallery is open Tuesday through Friday. 10 a.m. to 4:45 p.m.; Saturday and
Sunday, 1 p.m. to 4:45 p.m. Closed on Mondays.
Committee for Art Bookshops I and II
Located in the Museum and Art Gallery, and run by the Committee for Art at Stanford.
Open at the same times as the Museum and Gallery, the Bookshops are well stocked with
art books (including many publications by the Art Department's faculty), posters, post
cards, reproductions, calendars and many other items. The proceeds from these two
Bookshops, which are organized and staffed by the Committee for Art Volunteers, benefit
Art Department and Museum special projects.
TRESIDDER MEMORIAL UNION
The Union provides a variety of facilities and services which include:
Food
The COFFEE HOUSE features made-to-order sandwiches, homemade soup, pastries,
and fine coffees, plus an informal atmosphere and spontaneous entertainment. Open
Monday through Thursday, 11 a.m. to Midnight; Friday, 11 a.m. to 1 a.m.; Saturday, Noon
to 1 a.m.; and Sunday, Noon to Midnight. Hours of operation subject to change without
notice. Call 497-1311 for up-to-the-minute information.
The TRANSITION, located on the ground floor, offers hamburgers, hot dogs, pizza, and a
salad bar daily. Open Monday through Sunday, 11 a.m. to 7:30 p.m.
The COMMONS is in the arcade to the rear of Encina Hall. It offers hot meals as well as
sandwiches, short orders, desserts, ice cream and a salad bar daily. Open Monday through
Friday, 7 a.m. to 3:30 p.m.
MARCEL'S PASTRY SHOP offers fine pastries and desserts, made-to-order cakes and
breads, pies, cookies, ice cream and continental breakfasts. Located in the TRANSITION
dining area. Open Monday through Friday, 7 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Saturday and Sunday, 8 a.m. to
7 p.m.
Performing Arts and Exhibits
Exhibits, including paintings, photography, and other craft by well-known artists and
talented students, are continuously on display on the second floor. There is music and
reading in a secluded place on the second floor, just for relaxing. Enjoy your own kind of
music in one of our listening rooms with over 900 selections to choose from. Or browse
through our newspapers and magazines, study, watch television, or take a nap. Contact
Jean Martin regarding exhibits and programs. Open Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5
p.m. (497-4311)
Recreation
Tresidder's BOWLING CENTER on the ground floor next to the COFFEE HOUSE has
fourteen bowling lanes, six pool tables, and a rack of pinball machines. Open Monday
through Thursday, 10 a.m. to 10:30 p.m.; Friday and Saturday, 10 a.m. to 11:30 p.m.; and
Sunday, Noon to 8 p.m. (497-4316)
Shops and Services
SEQUOIA TRAVEL CENTER offers complete travel service: reservations for all airlines,
steamships and tours; youth fare tickets; Eurail and Britrail passes; hotel reservations, and
traveler's insurance. Located on the ground floor. Open Monday through Friday, 8:30 a.m.
to 5 p.m. (4 97-9401)
The STORE sells magazines, groceries, tobacco, sundries and health and beauty aids.
Open Monday through Saturday, 8 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Sunday, 9 a.m. to 7 p.m. (497-4319)
The TICKET OFFICE, located on the second floor, is on the national Ticketron network,
making tickets available for events all over the Bay Area as well as on campus. Tours to
Hearst Castle and campsite reservations for state parks also can be arranged. Contact Susie,
497-4317. Open Monday through Friday, 10:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.
If your hair would like some attention, you can visit the BARBER SHOP on the first floor.
Open Monday through Friday, 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; Saturday, 8:30 a.m. to 5 p.m.
(327-9664)
For convenient check cashing, loans, and other banking services, see the people at the
WELLS FARGO BANK on the second floor, or call 327-6762. Open Monday through
Thursday, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Friday, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Walk-up windows open Monday
through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.

BOOKSTORE
The Bookstore is located on White Plaza adjacent to the post office. The hours are
Monday through Saturday, 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. The store has a large supply of books in
general reading as well as the texts currently being used on campus. In addition, it stocks
school supplies, gifts, greeting cards, and souvenir items. Come in and browse while you
are here. When you have returned home, Susan Page, the store's shopper, will personally
handle your mail-order requests. (497-3661)
THE BRIDGE
The Bridge is a counseling and drop-in center, located at 640 Campus Drive, offering free
sevices to the Stanford community and the Palo Alto area. Its diverse volunteer staff
consists of both students and non-students who are trained in listening and non-directive
counseling skills. During the summer the staff maintains an open telephone line each night
after 10 p.m. for anyone seeking assistance with a specific issue or problem, or who simply
wants to talk. This number is 497-3392.
The Bridge is open during the day this summer from 10 a.m. to 10 p.m. on Monday,
Wednesday and Friday. All are welcome during those hours and are invited to drop in . . .
for counseling services, for information and referral services, to browse in our small
reading room for humanistic psychology and personal growth, and to meet some of our
staff persons and to learn more about on-going Bridge groups, classes and other activities.
Specific events scheduled for this summer at The Bridge include weekly workshops in
massage, meditative techniques, and exploring states of consciousness with music, as well
as beginning and advanced counseling classes. The Bridge will also conduct Wednesday
Evening Lecture Series, consisting of films or demonstrations accompanied by small group
discussions on a wide range of topics relating to persona! awareness and growth, lifestyle
alternatives, etc.
Visit The Bridge this summer between 10 a.m. and 10 p.m. on Monday, Wednesday or
Friday ... or call 497-3392. Summer quarter coordinator is Sherry Brainerd ... co
ordinators for 1976-77 are Sue Crissman and Don Gallagher.
