Although it doesn't charge an
annual membership fee other
than tuition, Stanford may just
be the greatest country club in
the Bay Area. The recreational
lacilities on the Farm rival those
of any club you care to name.
In the morning you can get in a
quick nine holes of golf and then
cool off with a swim in deCuerre
pools in the afternoon.
There's something for every
taste, and new students will
probably want to avail them
selves of the recreational
lacilities available right from the
start. The following is a list of
those available for use by mem
bers of the Stanford community:
Basketball: Maples Pavillion
is open from noon until 1 p.m.

Tennis: All tennis courts are

Softball: The IM softball fields

Golf: The Stanford golf course

Hiking: If you don't mind

Horseback Riding: Check

Sailing: Lake Lagunita is av

Cycling: There are many
bicycle paths on campus and in
the Stanford area.
