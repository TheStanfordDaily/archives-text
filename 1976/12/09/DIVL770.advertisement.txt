. THE STANFORD HONOR CODE I
I The Honor Code dates from 1921, and is: I
I A. An undertaking of the students, individu- I
I ally and collectively: 1
I (1) That they will do their share and take an I
I active part in seeing to it that others as well as I
I themselves uphold the spirit and letter of the I
Honor Code;
I (2) That they will not give or receive aid in I
examinations; that they will not give or receive I
unpermitted aid in class work, in the prepara-
I tion of reports, or in any other work that is to be I
I used by the instructor as the basis of grading. I
I B. The faculty on its part manifests its confi- I
I dence in the honor of its students by refraining I
from proctoring examinations or otherwise tak-
I ing special steps to prevent the forms of dishon- I
esty mentioned above.
I C. Provided that alternate seats are available, I
I tests will be taken from the classroom only with I
I the consent of the instructor (adopted by student I
I referendum in 1955). I
I D. The students and the faculty will work I
I together to establish those conditions under I
I which optimum academic performance can be I

of the Associated Students
