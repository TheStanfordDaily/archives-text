This weekly listing is an attempt to give amateur
and pro sports people as complete a re
view as possible of area recreational oppor
tunities. Send all notices ol forthcoming events
to Recreation Listing, Sports Department, Stan
ford Daily, Storke Student Publications Bldg.,
Stanford, CA 94305. Notices must arrive at the
Daly the Friday preceding the week ol publica
tion.

Continuing
Kayak Pool Sessions. Introduction to
kayaking with basic padding techniques,
safety, the eskimo-roll and surfing
techniques will be covered. Taught by
Bob Barnes, Millberry Union Pool,

Each session is three weeks long. Sign up
at Outdoors Unlimited/Wilderness Ex
change Center, 1309 3rd Ave., San Fran
cisco. Call 666-2078 tor information.

BACKPACKING
Saturday, May 7
Point Reyes. A weekend exploring three
different natural environments of Point
Reyes; Beach and Esteros, Chaparral,
and Evergreen Forests. Stay at Laguna
Ranch Hostel ($4), so all that is needed is
a sleeping bag and daypack. Bring
cameras; wildflowers will be out. Contact
David Friedman, Wilderness Exchange,
S.F., 666-2078.*
Continuing
Alpine Survival Training. Course at
Yosemite covers food gathering and pre
paration, fire, shelters, river crossings,

map and compass, snowfield travel. Write
to Lloyd Price, Director of Yosemite Moun
taineering, Yosemite National Park, CA
95389 or call 209-372-4611.
BICYCLING
Saturday, May 7
Tunftas Creek. One of the most scenic
rides in the Stanford area and one of the
toughest: it has one 2-mile long steep sec
tion ir the middle of a milder climb. Meet at
Kings Mountain and Skyline; then, nde will
go to Half Moon Bay and back up Tunitas
Creek. Lunch at Alex's or bring lunch.
Rides will be moderate to brisk, with
steep, long grades. Led by Lucy Grey,
Western Wheelers (589-4406). 29 miles.
10 a.m.
Sunday, May 22
the Sequoia Canfery. The peninsula's
scenic bicycle tour through redwood
forests and along the Pacific. Half century
and quarter century routes too (50 and 25
miles, respectively). Secure application
forms from Western Wheelers, P.O. Box
183, Menlo Park, CA or call Menlo Park
Recreation Dept. (325-3211). Begins at
780 Arastradero Road, Palo Alto at 5:30
a.m. Last century starting time at 8 a.m.

RUNNING
Saturday, May 7
6th Annual Angel Island Run, 4.78
miles. Race begins at 11:30 a.m. on Angel
Island. Phone 781-6785. $3. Half hilly, all
pavement.
Sunday, May 15
07th Annual Bay-to-Breakers Cross
City Run, 7.6 miles. Begins at 10 a.m. at
the foot of Howard Street, cuts across
Market Street, crosses the Panhandle,
winds by Golden Gate Park and ends by
the Cliff House. Entries were due April 28
although runners should feel free to run
unofficially.
Sunday, May 22
Golden Gate Charity Race, 6 miles.
Starts at Ft. Baker Parade Grounds next
to Sausalito, crosses the Golden Gate
Bridge to the Marina Green. Begins at
noon. One hill, paved. Entry fee: $3, $6 for
family. Contact Pax Beale, 567-6100.
Sunday, May 29
TOAC 6-Mle Race. Hellyer Park, San
Jose. Begins at 10 a.m. Entry fee: $1.50.
Flat, pavement. Contact Ray Smith (408)
253-0296.
Sunday, June 5
57th Annual Statuto Races, 4.29 and
8.58 miles. Race begins at 9 a.m. in front
of the San Francisco Athletic Club at 1630

Stockton St. Four-mile loop course,
pavement. Entry fee $1. Phone 986-
9650.
Sunday, June 12
Woodminster Run. 9 25 miles Starts at
10 a.m. in Joaquin Miller Park in Oakland.
Very hilly trails. Entry fee: $2. Phone
937-0806.
Continuing
Sunday Fun Runs from one-quarter to
six miles every Sunday at 10:30 a.m. at
Foothill Junior College off of 1-280 in Los
Altos.
