# An introduction to the campus facilities and services
## 
### 
The following area among the facilities available to visitors
during the summer session:
TRESIDDER MEMORIAL UNION
The Union provides a variety of facilities and services which
include:
Food
The COFFEE HOUSE features made-to-order sandwiches,
homemade soup, pastries and fine coffees, plus an informal
atmosphere and live entertainment. Open Monday through
Thursday, 11 a.m. to Midnight; Friday, 11 a.m. to 1 a.m.; Satur
day, Noon to 1 a.m.; and Sunday, Noon to Midnight. Hours of
operation subject to change without notice.
The TRANSITION, located on the ground floor, offers ham
burgers, hot dogs, pizza, ice cream, frozen yogurt, and
sandwiches daily.
The COMMONS is in the arcade to the rear of Encina Hall. It
offers hot meals as well as sandwiches, short orders, desserts,
ice cream and a salad bar daily. Open Monday through Friday, 7
a.m. to 3:30 p.m.
MARCEL'S PASTRY SHOP offers fine pastries and desserts,
made-to-order cakes and breads, pies, cookies, ice cream and
continental breakfasts. Located in the TRANSITION dining
area. Open Monday through Friday, 7 a.m. to 9 p.m.; Saturday
and Sunday, 8 a.m. to 7 p.m.
Performing Arts and Exhibits
Exhibits, including paintings, photography and other craft
by well-known artists and talented students, are continuously
on display on the second floor. There is music and reading in a

secluded place on the second floor, just for relaxing. Enjoy your
own kind of music in one of the listening rooms with over 900
selections to choose from. Or browse through newspapers and
magazines, study, watch television, or take a nap. Open Mon
day through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. (497-4311).
Recreation
Tresidder's BOWLING CENTER on the ground floor next to
the COFFEE HOUSE has 14 bowling lanes and six pool tables.
Open Monday through Thursday, 10 a.m. to 10:30 p.m.; Friday
and Saturday, 10 a.m. to 11:30 p.m.; and Sunday, Noon to 8 p.m.
(497-4316).
Shops and Services
SEQUOIA TRAVEL CENTER offers complete travel service:
reservations for all airlines, steamships and tours; youth fare
tickets; Eurail and Britrail passes; hotel reservations and
traveler's insurance. Located on the ground floor. Open Mon
day through Friday, 8:30 a.m. to 5 p.m. (497-9401).
The STORE sells magazines, groceries, tobacco, sundries and
health and beauty aids. Open Monday through Saturday, 8 a.m.
to 9 p.m.; Sunday, 9 a.m. to 7 p.m. (497-4319).
The TICKET OFFICE, located on the second floor, is on the
national Ticketron network, making tickets available for events
all over the Bay Area as well as on campus. Open Monday
through Friday, 10:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.
If your hair would like some attention, you can visit the
BARBER SHOP on the first floor. Open Monday through Fri
day, 8:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.; Saturday, 8:30 a.m. to 5 p.m. (327-
9664).
For convenient check cashing, loans and other banking ser
vices, see the people at the WELLS FARGO BANK on the
second floor, or call 327-6762. Open Monday through Thurs
day, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.; Friday, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Walk-up
windows open Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.
PHYSICAL RECREATION
Badminton
Courts are available at Roble Gym. Specific times must be
arranged by interested groups. Furnish your own raquets and
shuttlecocks; clean-soled tennis shoes must be worn.
Golf
Current Green Fees are in effect at the Golf Course and Driv
ing Range. Your identification card, when presented at the
course, entitles you to play, but there are no special rates.
Regular Golf Club members, faculty and staff have preference
during some hours. The Driving Range is also available.
Handball/Squash
For information and reservations, call 497-1588, Monday
through Friday, after 11 a.m. Courts are adjacent to the de-
Guerre pool complex.
Recreational Swim Program
The deGuerre pool is available for faculty, staff, summer
registered students, conference participants, and their im
mediate families. Open Monday through Friday, 2 p.m. to 6
p.m.; Saturday, Sunday and Holidays, noon to 6 p.m. For stu
dents registered for summer, no charge with identification
card. Fees for all other eligible swimmers are as follows: $3O for
a family season ticket, $lO for an individual season ticket and
$2.50 for a weekly swim card. Summer Recreational Swim
Cards may be purchased at the department of athletics during
business hours. No tickets will be sold at the pool. Special
events may occasionally interrupt the schedule.
Table Tennis
Tables are available for use at Roble Gym upon request.
Furnish own balls and rackets.
Tennis
Courts near the Encina Gym and adjacent to the Roble Gym

are available for recreation except when in use by regular clas
ses. Tennis shoes must be worn. Furnish own balls and rackets.
No charge. Playing rules are posted at the courts.
Volleyball
Courts are open for co-recreational games except during
scheduled classes and/or intramural tournaments. The Encina
Gym volleyball courts are available daily (outside).
Other Recreational Faciities
Inquiries regarding the use of other recreational facilities
may be directed to departmental secretaries of the Department
of Athletics, Physical Education and Recreation (497-4895 or
497-4591).
BOOKSTORE
The Bookstore is located on White Plaza adjacent to the post
office. The hours are Monday through Saturday, 8:30 a.m. to
5:30 p.m. The store has a large supply of books in general
reading as well as the texts currently being used on campus. In
addition, it stocks school supplies, gifts, greeting cards and
souvenir items. Come in and browse while you are here.
RELIGION AT STANFORD
Memorial Church, the center for Protestant and Catholic wor
ship on campus, is situated at the center of the campus on the
Inner Quadrangle. Regular Sunday services continue through
out the Summer. University Public Worship, which often
brings preachers from throughout the nation representing vari
ous denominations, is held at 11 a.m. Evening Communion is
held at 7 p.m. in the Round Room on Sundays when the
Eucharist has not been celebrated at 11 a.m. Theological dis
cussion groups and individual counseling are also available
during the summer.
The Clubhouse, located on White Plaza, is the location for a
Friday evening informal Jewish Service, a Thursday noon Epis
copal Communion Service and the Campus Ministry offices.
