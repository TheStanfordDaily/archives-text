# Welcome to Tresidder Union
## 
### 
recreation
The Bowling Center offers 14 bowling lanes and a
roomful of pinball machines and pool tables, bowling
classes, league play, intramurals, tournaments. 497-
4316.
meetings, events
Six air-conditioned meeting rooms and lounges
equipped with blackboards, screens, sound systems,
for groups up to 500. Facilities are free of charge to
student organizations and for official departmental
functions. For reservations, call 497-4314, 9 am-4 pm
weekdays. Rooms are open Monday through Friday,
8am-10pm; Saturday and Sunday, 10am-10pm (early
openings or late closings may be requested).
office of student
activities (osa)
OSAis responsible for advising the ASSU, registering
and advising student organizations, and providing
administrative support for various student programs.
In addition, OSA funds student-initiated activities
and assists student organizations with their funding
requests. 497-3542.
assu
The ASSU complex, located on the second floor, is
the headquarters of the student government officers,
the Student Financial Manager, senior class officers,
and the committee staff assistants. The business of
fice provides banking services for on-campus student
organizations, maintains a coin-operated Xerox
machine, and provides information about ASSU ac
tivities. Project offices include the Travel Service,
Special Events, Sunday Flicks, Course Guide, Lecture
Notes Service, ARLO, Refrigerator Rental Service,
and T-Shirt Shop. 497-4331.

food
Coffee House. Made-to-order sandwiches,
homemade soups, cheese boards, pastries, fine cof
fee drinks, live entertainment. 497-3592.
The Transition. Hamburgers, hot dogs, soups, salad
bar, pizza, hot breakfasts, hofbrau, yogurt bar, ice
cream soda fountain, hot entrees at lunch and dinner
hours. 497-4315.
Marcel's Pastry Shop. Fine pastries and desserts,
breads, continental breakfasts, special-occasion
cakes and pies made to order, 497-3521.
Catering. Experienced, professional service for lunc
heons, banquets, conferences, parties, weddings,
meetings—at Tresidder, other campus locations, or
in your home. 497-4324.
The Commons. Breakfast and lunch service nestled
behind Encina Hall, serving hot and cold sandwiches
(made to order), fresh fruit plates, salad bar, hot
breakfasts, short orders, desserts, and a soda foun
tain with Dreyer's ice cream. 497-3409.

shop and services
Wells Fargo Bank. Check cashing (no account neces
sary, with Stanford I.D.), Federally Insured Student
Loans, special student accounts, and all other bank
ing services. 855-7605.
The Store. Snacks, soft drinks, dairy and delicatessen
items, sundries, groceries, magazines, newspapers,
tobacco. 497-4319.
Ticket Office. Tickets for campus and Bay Area enter
tainment, Ticketron service, campsite reservations,
Hearst Castle tours, Bay Area sporting events. 497-
4317.
Sequoia Travel Center. Complete travel service: re
servations for all airlines, hotels, steamships, tours,
youth fare tickets, Eurail and Britrail passes, traveler's
insurance. 323-9401.
Stanford Barber Styling. Cuts, trims, shampoos, styl
ing for men and women. Redken and Jhirmack pro
ducts are available. For appointments, call 327-9664.
performances, exhibits
Paintings, photography, and other works by profes
sionals and Stanford students, staff, and faculty are
continuously displayed on the second floor as well as
in the Coffee House gallery. Over 400 framed art
prints are available to rent in the second-floor music/
reading room. Stereo listening rooms with over 500
record albums to choose from, newspapers,
magazines, and color television are available for use
in the music/reading room. Concerts, films, lectures,
and panels are offered throughout the year, and are
planned and executed by the Tresidder Program
Board with the assistance of the Programs Manager.
For information, call 497-4311.

servations for all airlines, hotels, steamships, tours,

Stanford Barber Styling. Cuts, trims, shampoos, styl
ing for men and women. Redken and Jhirmack pro
ducts are available. For appointments, call 327-9664.
performances, exhibits
Paintings, photography, and other works by profes
sionals and Stanford students, staff, and faculty are
continuously displayed on the second floor as well as
in the Coffee House gallery. Over 400 framed art
prints are available to rent in the second-floor music/
reading room. Stereo listening rooms with over 500
record albums to choose from, newspapers,
magazines, and color television are available for use
in the music/reading room. Concerts, films, lectures,
and panels are offered throughout the year, and are
planned and executed by the Tresidder Program
Board with the assistance of the Programs Manager.
For information, call 497-4311.
recreation
The Bowling Center offers 14 bowling lanes and a
roomful of pinball machines and pool tables, bowling
classes, league play, intramurals, tournaments. 497-
4316.
meetings, events
Six air-conditioned meeting rooms and lounges
equipped with blackboards, screens, sound systems,
for groups up to 500. Facilities are free of charge to
student organizations and for official departmental
functions. For reservations, call 497-4314, 9 am-4 pm
weekdays. Rooms are open Monday through Friday,
8am-10pm; Saturday and Sunday, 10am-10pm (early
openings or late closings may be requested).
office of student
activities (osa)
OSAis responsible for advising the ASSU, registering
and advising student organizations, and providing
administrative support for various student programs.
In addition, OSA funds student-initiated activities
and assists student organizations with their funding
requests. 497-3542.
assu
The ASSU complex, located on the second floor, is
the headquarters of the student government officers,
the Student Financial Manager, senior class officers,
and the committee staff assistants. The business of
fice provides banking services for on-campus student
organizations, maintains a coin-operated Xerox
machine, and provides information about ASSU ac
tivities. Project offices include the Travel Service,
Special Events, Sunday Flicks, Course Guide, Lecture
Notes Service, ARLO, Refrigerator Rental Service,
and T-Shirt Shop. 497-4331.
