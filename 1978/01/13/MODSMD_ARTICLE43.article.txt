# Across the sports desk
## 
### 
IM referees
Referees are needed for in
tramural contests. An ex
panded winter sports schedule
has created a demand for offi
cials. Intramural basketball
games will be played on week
nights, Saturdays and Sundays
beginning Sunday, Jan. 15.
Soccer games are played
weekdays at 3 and 4 p.m. and
Saturdays at 9, 10 and 11 a.m.
beginning Monday, jan. 16.
Officials are paid $2.70 per
hour. If you are interested in
officiating, please call the IM
office at 497-1081.
Women's basketball
Stanford's women's basket
ball team takes on Fullerton

State and the University of
Pacific this weekend in hopes
of improving on its 6-4 season
record.
Stanford will meet Fullerton
State, who handed the Cards a
73-69 defeat last weekend in
Los Angeles, at 7:30 p.m. today
in Maples Pavilion.
The contest against UOP will
begin after the Stanford vs.
Washington men's basketball
game on Saturday. The tipoff
should be at approximately
6:30 p.m.
Fencing
The Stanford fencing team
will meet UC-Santa Cruz for its
first match of the new year on
Saturday at 11 a.m. in Roble
Gym. The Cards, currently 5-3

on the season, dropped a split
decision on the basis of bouts
to UC-Santa Cruz on Nov. 5.
Women's gymnastics
Stanford's newest varsity
sport makes its debut when the
women's gymnastics team
opens its 1978 season against
Northridge State on Saturday
at 1:30 p.m. in Encina Gym.
The Cards are coached by
Jackie Walker, who comes to
Stanford after winning AIAW
Region four Coach of the Year
honors at Louisiana State Uni
versity the last three seasons.
Top performers for Stanford
this year should be Sam
Muramatsu and transfer )uli
Banfe, a balance beam
specialist.
