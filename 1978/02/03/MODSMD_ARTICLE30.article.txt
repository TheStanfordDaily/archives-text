# Women romp 59-43
## 
### Sukie Jackson 
Chalk up another Northern
California Athletic League
game victory for the Stanford
women's basketball team.
With last night's defeat of the
University of Pacific Tigers, the
Cards improve their league re
cord to 5-1, second in the Nor
Cal League behind undefeated
San lose, who boast a 5-0 mark.
"The game was close the
whole way," assistant coach
Sue Rojcewicz said. "We didn't
break it open until seven or
eight minutes before the end
of the game."
Bright spots in the Card play
include Maggie Nelson's 30
points (13 for 18 from the field
and four for five from the line),
the team's excellent defense
and an all time low 14 Stanford

turnovers.
After winning two games on
the road, the Cards return
home to meet Berkeley Satur
day night at 8 at Maples Pavil
ion.
