# Dichters to perform
## 
### 
Misha and Cipa Dichter,
pianists, open the Lively Arts at
Stanford winter quarter
schedule with a concert at 8
p.m. Friday, Jan. 12, in Memo
rial Auditorium.
The Dichters will perform
two works together: Brahms'
Variations on a Theme by
Haydn and Rachmaninoff's
Suite No. 2, Op. 17.
Misha will solo in Liszt's
Sonata in B minor.
Misha Dichter began his in
ternational career after win
ning the Tchaikovsky Competi
tion in Moscow in 1966. By the

early 1970s he had established
himself as one of America's
leading young artists, perform
ing alone and with orchestras
here and abroad.
Cipa was born in Brazil. She
made her professional debut at
the age of 16 with the Sym
phony Orchestra of Brazil. Her
recent concert performances
include appearances with the
Los Angles Philharmonic at the
Hollywood Bowl, at the
Meadowbrook and Ravinia
Festivals, and at the Mostly
Mozart Festival in New York's
Lincoln Center.
Other Lively Arts January
events include performances
in Memorial Auditorium at 8
p.m. Saturday, Jan. 20, by the
Pittsburgh Symphony
Chamber Orchestra with Barry
Tuckwell conducting and solo
ing; at 8 p.m. Tuesday, |an 23,
by Guitarist Christopher Par
kening; and at 8 p.m. Friday,
Jan. 26, by AMAN, the Interna
tonal Folk Ensemble.
