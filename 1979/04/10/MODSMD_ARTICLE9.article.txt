# Environment Month
## 
### 
Tuesday, April 10: Keynote address by David Brower,
president of Friends of the Earth, a leading environmen
talist and Nobel Peace Prize nominee. Kresge Auditorium, 8
p.m.
Wednesday, April 11: "Economics of Environmental Regu
lation panel discussion with Provost William Miller
serving as moderator; )im Harding, Friends of the Earth
economist; Robert Scherr, an engineer for Getty Oil; and
other speakers. Kresge Auditorium, 8 p.m.
Thursday, April 12: Murray Bookchin, environmental pro
fessor and author. Terman Auditorium, 8 p.m.
Monday, April 16: Slide show and talk by Peter Calthorpe,
solar architect, and Charles Eley of the American Institute of
Architects speaking on "A Nation of Energy-Efficient Build
ings." Tresidder Rm. 270, 8 p.m.
Tuesday, April 17: Slide show and talk by Judy Corbett, an
ecological community developer and designer of Village
Homes in Davis, Ca. Annenburg Auditorium, 7:30 p.m.
Wednesday, April 18: "The Future of Nuclear Power" —
panel discussion with Dr. Edward Teller, nuclear physicist
and Hoover fellow; Peter Faulkner, former nuclear power
systems engineer, guest professor and author of The Silent
Bomb; Dr. Bertram Wolfe, general manager of General
Electric's Nuclear Division; and Harding. Kresge Au
ditorium, 7:30 p.m.
Wednesday, April 18 to April 20: California Office of Ap
propriate Technology display trailer. White Plaza.
Thursday, April 19: Mike McCloskey, Sierra Club director,
at sherry hour. American Studies House, 5 p.m.
Thursday, April 19 and Friday April 20: PG&E energy
trailer. White Plaza.
Friday, April 20: "Local Day" — alternative energy fair in
White Plaza 10 a.m. to 4 p.m., including tours of the Roth
house solar system. Talk by Dr. Helen Caldicott, anti
nuclear physician, on the effects of radiation from nuclear
power. Fairchild Auditorium, 8 p.m.
Tuesday, April 24: Talk on Alaska by Dr. Edgar Wayburn,
Alaska expert for the Sierra Club. Time and place to be
announced.
Wednesday, April 25: "Forest Use Alternatives" — panel
discussion with George Craig of Western Timber Associa
tion, John Amadio of the Sierra Club, and representatives
from the California Department of Resources and the U.S.
Department of the Interior. Tresidder Rm. 270, 8 p.m.
Thursday, April 26: "Recycling and the Ecology Move
ment" by Cliff Humphreys of Ecology Action in Palo Alto.
Time and place to be announced.
