# Booters to hold exhibition
## 
### Templin Dowling Staff Writer 
Hard to believe as it may be,
the Stanford men's soccer team
will be in action this weekend,
acting as hosts of the NorCal In
vitational tournament.
Billed by the Stanford athletic
department as the "Stanford
Spring Soccer Showcase," the
tournament takes place Saturday
and Sunday at Harry Maloney
Field.
Eight of the best teams in the
West are scheduled to compete,
beginning at 10:15 a.m. Satur
day and 1 1:45 a.m. Sunday. The
University of San Francisco
Dons, NCAA champions three

of the past four years, headline
the exhibition. They will be
joined by Pacific Soccer Confer
ence teams San Jose State. Santa
Clara, Fresno State, University
of Pacific and California, as well
as the Cardinals.
In addition, the University of
Colorado will send its squad to
the seven game competition.
Each team will play a shortened
45-minute exhibition against the
Cards, with the Stanford-Col
orado match leading off the un
usual tournament.
The Cal Bears then meet the
Cards at 12:30 p.m. Saturday,
followed by San Jose State-
Stanford at 1:15 p.m. and
Stanford-USF at 2:45 p.m.

Sunday's schedule begins with
Stanford-UOP at 1 1:45 p.m.,
which will be followed by Fresno
State-Stanford at 1:15 p.m. and
Santa Clara-Stanford at 2:45
p.m.
Last fall, Stanford narrowly
missed the NCAA playoffs,
compiling a 14-5-2 record over
all and a 5-2 PSC mark. Stanford
was led by sophomore midfielder
Willie Giucci of Uruguay (20
goals, five assists) and junior
Dan McNevin (11 goals, 17 as
sists).
There will be no admission
charge at this weekend's tour
nament.
