Lawyer chosen for Billy probe
WASHINGTON — The Senate subcommittee
investigating Biilv Carter yesterday selected
Chicago attorney Philip Tone, a former federal
judge,to be its outside counsel.
Tone, a Republican who was appointed to the
federal bench by President Nixon, "will lend the
subcommittee and its already aggressive and able
staff of investigators an important element of non
partisanship," said Sen. Birch Bayh (D-Ind.).
Dow Jones closes with late gain
NEW YORK — The stock market, paced by

data processing and computer stocks, extended its
rally for the fourth consecutive session yesterday in
moderate trading.
The Dow Jones average of 30 industrial stocks
which showed little change through the early hours
of trading began to climb during the afternoon and
closed with a 9.39 point gain at 964.08. That was
the average's highest close since March 1977.
