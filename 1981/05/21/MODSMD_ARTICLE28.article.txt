# BAY AREA EVENTS
## ART 
### 
Campus
Masters of Photography: Special
Exhibition in Stanford Museum of
Art with selections from Bay Area
collections, including Fox Talbot,
Fenton, Cameron, Bisson Freres, Em
erson, Atget, Stieglitz, Strand, and
Minor White. Docent tours at 12:15
p.m and Sundays at 3 p.m. Museum
open Tues.-Fri., 10 a.m. to 5 p.m.,
and weekends 1 to 5 p.m.
Japanese Prints: Images of For
eigners: Special Exhibition in
Miedel Room of Stanford Museum of
Art May 5 through June 14. Docent
Tours Thursdays at 12:15 p.m. and
Sundays at 2 p.m. Prints from the
McGovern Collection illustrate,
often in a fanciful way, how the
Japanese viewed foreigners from
1615 to 1912.
Master of Fine Arts Graduate Ex
hibition: Will be presented at Stan
ford Museum of Art, May 10 through
June 14. Exhibiting artists will be
Nevin Berger, printmaker; Paul
Gleason, painter; and Richard Putz,
Sculptor. The show consists of
selected works completed during the
artists' two years residence at Stan

ford.
Bay Area
Seipp Gallery: Peninsula Land
scapes by artists Fletcher and
Kampmann will be on exhibit at the
Seipp Gallery at Castillejo School,
1310 Bryant, Palo Alto through May
28. Hours are Monday through Fri
day, 1 to 4 p.m.
Montalvo Center for the Arts: Ex
hibits by artists Joy Stocksdale, tex
tiles,- Jean Mooney, acrylic paint
ings,- and Jane Lidz, architectural
photography, through June 14.
Artists in the American Desert: An
exhibition of paintings, works on pa
per and photo documentation of
land works, will be on veiw at Kaiser
Center Art Gallery, Kaiser Building
Mezzanine, 300 Lakeside Drive,
Oakland. Gallery open from 9 a.m.
to 5 p.m., Monday through Friday.
La Raza Art Exhibit: Exhibit at
Foothill College Hubert H. Semans
Library, viewing hours from 8 a.m. to
10 p.m. Mondays through
Thursdays; 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.
Fridays; and 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. Satur
days. Oakland artist Malaquias
Montoya will display his artwork.
WORKS: A solo exhibition by
sculptor, Eva Bovenzi, will be pre
sented at WORKS-San Jose, from
May 9 through June 5. Bovenzi's on
the-wall wood frame structures wet
painting and sculpture. Gallery

hours from noon to 4 p.m., Tuesday
through Saturday. More info at
(408) 295-8378.
