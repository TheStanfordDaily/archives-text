# BAY AREA EVENTS
## Art 
### 
Campus
Masters of Photography: Special
Exhibition in Stanford Museum of
Art with selections from Bay Area
collections, including Fox Talbot,
Fenton, Cameron, Bisson Freres, Em
erson, Atget, Stieglitz, Strand, and
Minor White. Docent tours at 12:15
p.m and Sundays at 3 p.m. Museum
open Tuesday through Friday, 10
a.m. to 5 p.m., and weekends 1 to 5
p.m.
Japanese Prints: Images of For
eigners: Special Exhibition in
Miedel Room of Stanford Museum of
Art through June 14. Docent tours
Thursdays at 12:15 p.m. and Sun
days at 2 p.m. Prints from the Mc-
Govern Collection illustrate, often in
a fanciful way, how the Japanese
viewed foreigners from 1615 to
1912.
Master of Fine Arts Graduate Ex
hibition: Will be presented at Stan
ford Museum of Art, through June
14. Exhibiting artists will be Nevin
Berger, printmaker; Paul Gleason,
painter; and Richard Putz, sculptor.
The show consists of selected works
completed during the artists' two
years residence at Stanford.
The Future That Was Going To
Be: Photographic images depicting
future technology, produced by the
students of VTS 125. Tresidder 2nd
floor lobby. Through June 9.
Bay Area
Fans in Fashion: An exhibition of
fans from the Fine Arts Museum of
San Francisco through Aug. 30.

Exhibits/Projects: Alice Malloy
and Karen Skladany: Presented at
Fiberworks, 1940 Bonita Ave,
Berkeley, June 2 through 19. Gallery
Hours Tuesday through Friday, noon
to 5 p.m., Saturday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.
Volcanic Landscapes: Photo
graphic Exhibit featuring works by
Mothias Van Hesemans will run at
the Syntex Gallery, 3401 Hill view
Ave. Palo Alto, June 1 through July
Lunar Rock: Sample moon rock col
lected from Apollo 15 mission will be
exhibited June 1 though 30 at De
Anza College Learning Center. Mon
day through Thursday, 7:30 a.m. to
10 p.m., Friday 7.30 a.m. to 4.30
p.m., Sunday, 1 to 5 p.m.
Miniature Needleworks: Will be
on display at the Triton Museum of
Art through July 12. Museum Hours
are noon to 4 p.m. Tuesday through
Friday, Noon to 5 p.m., Saturday
and Sunday. More info at 248-4585.
Museum at 1505 Warburton Ave
nue, Santa Clara.
Tunisian Artifacts: Will be dis
played at the Triton Museum of Art
through July 12. Admission is free,
(see above for info).
