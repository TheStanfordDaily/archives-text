# Wonder what happened here the day YOU were born?
## 
### 
j —
It's easy to find out. Just come into the Daily office any
afternoon this week and ask to see the Daily that appeared
on the day you appeared.
Then sit down. Look around the office. Get to know us.
We need writers, reviewers, columnists, photographers,
artists, and production people.
You don't need experience. You don't need a lot of
spare time. You don't need to be an English major.
You just need to be interested in working for an inde
pendent student newspaper that reaches 22,000 people
every day.
You are invited to one of our special Orientation meet
ings this week at the Daily office (across from Terman Engi
neering Center.) Meet the Daily today at 10 a.m. and 2
p.m., tomorrow at 3 p.m., Thursday at 10 a.m. and Friday at
2 p.m.
