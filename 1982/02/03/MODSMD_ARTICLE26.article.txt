# Netmen edge USF
## 
### 
The defending national
champion Stanford men's ten
nis team survived a brief scare
by the University of San Fran
cisco before eking out a 6-3 win
at the Varsity Courts yesterday.
Stanford, heavily favored to
win their opening match,
limped out to a 4-2 lead after
the singles.
Scott Davis gave Stanford the
margin by edging Mike How
ard 6-4, 2-6, 7-6 in a tense third
set tie-breaker.
USF's Eddie Barretto slipped
past Scott Bondurant 6-4, 6-4 in
No. 2 singles while Jeff Arons
defeated John Huebner 6-2,
6-3 at No. 3.
Stanford's John Corse
whipped Mike Jee 6-2, 6-1 and

USF's Byron Neponuceno
edged Mark McKeenn 7-6, 6-3.
Freshman walk-on Paul Rob
inson of Stanford ripped Eddie
James 6-3, 6-0 to round out the
singles.
Baretto-Howard won the
No. 1 doubles in a 7-6, 6-3
squeaker over Davis-Arons.
Stanford won the other two
double matches to secure the
win.
Bondurant-Corse edged Jee-
Huebner 6-4, 7-5 and Rob
inson-Jeremy Semple out
lasted Neponuceno-James 7-6,
6-4.
Stanford travels to the Na
tional Team Indoor Tour
nament in Memphis Tennessee
Thursday.
