# Burgin falters in singles
## Moulton-Copeland win in Ojai 
### Sid Spaeth 
Alycia Moulton and Caryn Copeland, regaining the form
that made them the No. 1 doubles team in the nation last
year, swept through the Ojai Intercollegiates to claim first in
the intercollegiate doubles division.
Moulton also reached the semi-finals of the in
tercollegiate singles before falling to Kelly Henry of USC
6-2,7-5.
No team totals were kept in the women's division.
Moulton and Copeland cruised to an early lead over Tina
Rimer and Julie Newell of Arizona in the finals before falter
ing and dropping the second set 5-7. The defending AIAW
champs regained their concentration and won the third set
6-2.
To reach the final, Moulton and Copeland beat Marie
Cheshire and Chris Downing of Biola 6-1, 6-0, Maria and
Chris Myers 6-0, 6-3 of UC-lrvine, and Karen Huebner and

Helena Manset 6-2, 7-5.
Moulton smothered Kathy Sturdevant of UC-Santa
Barbara in the first round of the singles and then edged
Maria Myers of UC-lrvine 7-6,6-4 to reach the quarterfinals
on Saturday.
Moulton edged Kathy O'Brien 6-2, 7-6 in the quarters
before falling to Henry.
Elise Burgin, rated as the top collegiate singles player in
the nation, crushed UC-lrvine's Cindy Keeling 6-1, 6-1 in
the first round, but was upset by San Diego State's Micki
Schillig 6-3, 7-6 in the second.
In open doubles, Alison Hardey and Sue Rasmussen beat
Gail Miller and Beth Rushing 6-2,6-2 before falling in three
sets to Kathy Bryan and Michelle Beck 4-6,6-3,6-3.
Linda Gates lost to Carol Heynen of USC in open singles
6-4,6-1.
