# Bay Area exhibits variety
## 
### Karen Springen 
The Louvre is in Paris, and
the Metropolitan is in New
York, but many smaller
museums with high quality,
specialized collections are
in the San Francisco Bay
Area.
Stanford Museum of Art
The Museum of Art, right
off Palm Drive, opened in
1892. It is the oldest museum
west of the Mississippi
River. Highlights include
the Rodin collection, on
permanent exhibit, and the
Gold Spike which united
the first transcontinental
railroad. The museum is
open 10 a.m. to 4:45 p.m.,
Tuesday through Friday and
1:00 to 4:45 Saturday and
Sunday. The museum is
closed Mondays. It is also
usually closed during Au
gust.
Stanford Art Gallery The
Thomas Welton Stanford
Art Gallery, which holds
smaller collections and ex

hibits, is located across from
the history corner of the
quadrangle. It is open dur
ing the same hours as the art
museum. Funds from Sena
tor Stanford's younger
brother enabled the gallery
to open in 1917. The gallery
features changing exhibits
and a small store which sells
prints, art books, and post
cards.
San Francisco Museum of
Modern Art The San Fran
cisco Museum of Modern
Art, located at Van Ness and
McAllister, features rotating
exhibits. It is open from 10
a.m. to 6 p.m. Tuesday,
Wednesday, and Friday, 10
a.m. to 10 p.m. Thursday,
and 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Satur
day and Sunday.
Asian Art Museum After
enjoying the roller skating
at Golden Gate Park one
Sunday, stop by the nearby
Asian Art Museum at Eighth
Avenue and Kennedy Drive.
It is open daily 10 to 5.
