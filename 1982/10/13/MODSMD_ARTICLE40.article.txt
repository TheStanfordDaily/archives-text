# J. HENRY MEYER MEMORIAL LIBRARY
## 
### 
The J. Henry Meyer Memorial Library houses
a collection of more than 155,000 books and
audio/visual materials, selected primarily in
support of the undergraduate curriculum. Also
included are items of general interest and a
limited amount of materials for recreational
reading and listening.
Reserves* tor most undergraduate courses
are kept in Mever. Loan periods are two hours,
or one, two, or three days, according to the
recommendation of the instructor. Books not
on reserve circulate for one month and may be
renewed if not needed bv another borrower.
Students must use audio/visual materials with
in the library, but audio/visual materials do
circulate to instructors for classroom use.
Serials* and microforms are limited to building
use only.
Mever does not have a card catalog, nor are
its holdings listed in the card catalog in the
Green Library. Instead, Meyer has a computer
produced microfiche catalog. Fiche readers and
copies of the Meyer catalog are located in
Mever, Green, and other library locations
throughout campus. Library staff will gladly
show patrons how to use the fiche catalog and
its supplements. RLIN* can be searched on-line
for more detailed information on Meyers hold
ings or orders for new materials.
Mever librarians provide a variety of refer

ence* and bibliographic services, including gen
eral reference assistance, term paper advice,
tours, lectures to classes, and Library 1, a 3-unit
course. Users may inquire about these or other
services at the Reference/Information Desk on
the second floor.
A self-guided tour of the Meyer Library is
available with other library guides on the sec
ond floor. Reference materials, periodicals, and
newspapers are shelved on this level.
Circulating books are arranged by Library of
Congress classification* number on the second,
third, and fourth floors and audio/visual mate
rials are available on the first floor.
Mever has plenty of carrel and lounge seat
ing, photocopiers, electric typewriters, and cal
culators. For those with valid C.I.T. accounts,
computer terminals are provided on the fourth
floor.
FM radio and special audio programs can be
piped to carrels along the perimeter of the three
upper floors during most of the hours the
Libraiy is open. Earphones mav be checked out
at the Audio/Visual Service Desk
The Mever Library welcomes comments
from its users and encourages suggestions for
items to add to the collections. Suggestions and
purchase request blanks are available at the
Librarv s second floor "Action Box."
