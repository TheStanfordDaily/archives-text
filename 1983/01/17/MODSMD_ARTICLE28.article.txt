# SMU wins Adidas Classic
## 
### 
Southern Methodist edged
three second-place teams by one
point to win the Adidas-MGM
Grand Collegiate Tennis Classic
in Reno, Nev. last weekend
while Stanford was well back
"with a disappointing four
points."
Stanford coach Dick Gould
admitted he was disappointed in
some areas, "but I think it was an
excellent learning experience."
Utah, Trinity and USC tied for
second with 12 points, California
had 11 and Stanford and Pep
perdine each had four.
"I thought Mark McKeen and
Scott Davis played pretty well,"
Gould said. "I was really pleased
with McKeen in both singles and
doubles. He* and (Jim) Grabb
look like they are going to make
a pretty good doubles team."
The first singles division of the

tournament consisted of the
team's No. 1 and No. 2 players.
No. 3 seed Davis defeated Chris
tian Schultes of Utah, 6-4, 6-2 in
the first round before being
upset by Charles Honey of Trin
ity, 6-4, 6-2 in the quarterfinals.
I Greg Homes of Utah beat Eric
Rosenfield of Stanford 6-3, 6-1
in the first round.
The second division, consist
ing of No. 3 and 4 players, saw
both No. 4 seed Dan Goldie and
No. 6 seed John Letts of Stan
ford win the first set only to lose
their matches. Goldie was
beaten by Utah's Joe Russell,
2-6, 7-6, 6-4 and Letts was
beaten by John Tsumas 4-6,6-4,
6-3.
In the third division, consisting

of No. 5 and 6 players, McKeen
battled his way to the finals with
wins over USCs Jim Agate, 7-6,
6-4, Utah's Lasse Mannisto, 6-3,
6-3, and No. 2-seeded Mark
Styslinger of SMU 6-3, 7-5.
Cal's Chris Schoop outlasted
McKeen 6-2, 5-7, 6-3 in the fi
nal.
USCs Tim Pawsat beat Stan
ford's Grabb 6-4, 6-3 in the first
round.
Stanford was beaten in the
first round in all three doubles
divisions.
Pawsat and Jorge Losana of
USC defeated Davis-Letts 7-5,
7-6 in No. 1, Honey and David
Eddes of Trinity nipped
McKeen-Grabb 6-3, 5-7, 7-6 in
No. 2 and Mark Wooldridge and
Peter Wright of Cal beat
Goldie-Rosenfield 6-3, 6-4 in
No. 3.
