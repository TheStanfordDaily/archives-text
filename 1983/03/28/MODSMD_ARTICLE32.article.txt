# Five frosh pace netmen
## 
### SID SPAETH Senior sports writer 
Those "damn rookies" were
all Dick Gould could rely on as
the Stanford men's tennis team
entered a "key point" of the
season.
With No. 1 player Scott Davis
sidelined indefinitely with
mononucleosis, Could packed
five freshmen into the line-up
for weekend duals at Arizona
and Arizona State and they re
sponded with two wins.
Stanford, now 9-1, fought off
three rain and hail delays and
finished indoors Friday, beat
ing Arizona 6-3 and then
stormed past Arizona State
Saturday 8-1.
"Those damn rookies,"
Could said. "They just keep
getting better and better and
better. I have no idea when
Scott (Davis) will be back, but
we're doing a pretty good job
without him.
"We're making a big push
right now too because this is a
key point in the season."
The Arizona matches, along
with a home match against the
University of California-Santa
Barbara at 1:30 p.m. Wednes
day and a match at Cal Friday,
will tell Could a lot about his
No. b-ranked team, he said.
"Those four matches, of
which we are half done,"
Gould said, "will let us know
whether we are a pretty good
team or not."
The Arizona match was the
first match at another school
for many players and the blus
tery conditions repeatedly
started and stopped play until
the match finished at 7:30 p.m.
on indoor courts.
Stanford picked up singles
victories in the No. 3 through
No. 6 matches and won the
second and third doubles
matches to clinch the win.
Eric Rosenfeld beat Randy
Vigmostid 2-6, 6-1, 6-4; Mark
McKeen edged Ken Mac-
Dougall 3-6, 7-5, 6-2; )im Grabb
beat Mike Lee 6-3, 4-6, 6-1; and
Geordie McKee dropped Seth
Bowan 6-3, 6-1.
Arizona's Andia Luters

edged John Letts 6-7, 6-2, 6-4 at
No. 1 and Paul Chamberlain
knocked off Dan Coldie 6-3,
6-2 at No. 2.
Chamberlain and Luters
picked up the Wildcat's only
doubles win with a 3-6, 6-3, 7-6
win over Letts and leremy
Semple. McKeen and Crabb
beat Vigmostid and Bowen 5-7,
6-4, 6-2; and Coldie and Rosen

feld edged McDougall and
Todd Mason 6-7, 6-4, 6-3.
Stanford had an easier time
with Arizona State, sweeping
the singles and continuing on
for a 8-1 win.
Letts beat |im Baumann6-3,
6-3; Coldie eked by Stan Perry
7-6,6-3; Rosenfeld started slow
but came back to rip Alex Levie
1-6, 6-1, 6-2; McKeen dropped

Jim Settles 6-1, 2-6, 6-1; Grabb
beat Bert Royden 6-7, 6-2, 6-0;
and McKee nipped Mark Weiss
6-7, 6-2, 6-3.
