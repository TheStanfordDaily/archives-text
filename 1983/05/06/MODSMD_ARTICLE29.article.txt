# SPORTS
## Card crushes Cal NCAAs next for netmen 
### TOM MOORE 
Relying on the strength of its
singles play, the men's tennis
team defeated UC-Berkeley
Wednesday at Stanford
Stadium 5-4.
In the match's most exciting
and emotional duel, Mark Mc-
Keen outlasted Cal's Henry
Hecht 4-6, 6-3, 6-4. Ahead 5-2 in
the final set, McKeen allowed
Hecht back into the match 5-4,
but then with an encouraging
crowd urging him on, held
serve to clinch the early victory
for Stanford.
"Mark kept his poise to get
that final game," said Stanford
coach Dick Gould. "All our
guys have done a super job all
season."
In the other singles matches,
Scott Davis had little trouble
dispatching Randy Nixon 6-3,
6-3; John Letts lost in straight
sets to Mark Woolridge 0-6,
1-6; Dan Coldie defeated Doug
Stone 6-2, 6-2; Eric Rosenfeld
held off Chris Schoop 6-3, 7-6;
and |im Crabb beat Henry
Somrnerville in three sets 4-6,
6-3, 6-4.
With the victory already
tucked away, Stanford lost ail
its doubles matches in three
sets.
Cal's Woolridge/
Somrnerville defeated Davis/
Letts 6-2, 6-7, 6-4; Nixon/Stone
dropped the McKeen/Grabb
duo 6-4, 5-7, 6-4; and the
Hecht/Schoop pair completed
the sweep by edging Goldie/
Rosenfeld 0-6, 6-4, 7-5.
"The letdown in doubles was
a little disappointing, but Cal
played a tough match," Gould
said.
Owning the best record in
the nation at 20-1, Stanford
now looks ahead to the NCAA
tournament May 14-22 in
Athens, Ga. As a final tune-up,
Stanford again faces Cal in a
men's and women's indoor
exhibition match at Maples
Pavilion Sunday, May 8.
Men's singles, women's
singles, and mixed doubles
matches are scheduled to
begin at 6 p.m.
