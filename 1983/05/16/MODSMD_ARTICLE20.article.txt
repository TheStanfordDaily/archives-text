# SPORTS
## Netters advance in early rounds of NCAA tournament Men ace out Harvard and Arkansas over weekend 
### TOM MOORE 
The Cardinal men's tennis
team defeated Harvard 6-1
Saturday and Arkansas 5-4 in
the quarter finals yesterday to
advance to the NCAA semi
finals today against third
ranked USC.
Yesterday marked the Card's
21st straight win this year,
bringing it to 22-1 overall.
Despite their inexperience
in NCAA play, the Cardinal had
little trouble with the un
seeded Crimson. At No. 1,
Davis defeated Sands, 6-0, 6-4;
at No. 2 Harvard's Grossman
beat Letts 4-6, 6-3, 6-3; and at
the other slots Goldie de
molished Beckman 6-4, 6-2;
Rosenfeld squeaked past
Beren 6-4, 2-6, 6-2; and at Nos.
5 and 6 McKeen and Grabb
turned in their usual stellar
performance, with McKeen

thrashing Loud in two sets 6-4,
6-4; and Grabb beating the
Crimson's Scott 6-2, 6-4. In
doubles, the Rosenfeld-Goldie
pair beat the Beren-Beckman
duo 6-4, 6-2.
"That win will give us the
confidence we need," said
coach Dick Gould after the
match. "Our singles play was
very strong." The Card
promptly continued its win
ning ways yesterday as well,
thrashing seventh seeded Ar
kansas behind inspired singles
efforts. Its only defeat again
came at the No. 2 position.
"We had another good effort
out there again today," re
marked Gould, "and we came
out on top of some tough
matches."
The Card won five ot six sing
les matches against Arkansas.

It defaulted the doubles
matches to save its players'
energies for tomorrow's match
against USC, said head coach
Dick Gould.
In the five Stanford wins over
Arkansas, Scott Davis defeated
Duhan 6-4, 6-2; Dan Goldie de
feated Pat Serret 6-0, 5-7, 7-6;
Eric Rosenfeld defeated Joe
Lambert 6-4, 6-4; Captain Mark
McKeen defeated Tim Siegel
7-6, 2-6, 6-1 and ]im Grabb de
feated Bobby Bank 6-3, 6-2.
In the Cardinal's only loss,
John Letz fell to Kelly Evernden
2-6, 6-5.
Now 22-1 on the year, the
Cardinal faces third-seeded
USC today at 10 a.m. (EDT) in
the semifinals. The winner will
face the victor of the other
semifinals match tomorrow af
ternoon.
