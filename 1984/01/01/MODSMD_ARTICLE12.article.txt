# Olympic soccer at Stanford
## 
### JULIE RYAN 
If you get a kick out of soc
cer, Stanford is the place to be
this summer. I he Stanford
Stadium will host all Olympic
soccer matches except the
finals.
The games at Stanford will
begin on July 29 with a
torchlighting and opening cer
emony. The U.S. team wiil face
the Costa Ricans that night at a
game scheduled from 7 to 9
p.m.
I he rest of the schedule is as
follows: July 30: West Ger
many vs. Morocco from 7 to 9
p.m.; July 31: Egypt vs. Costa
Rica from 7 to 9 p.m.; Aug. 1:

West Germany vs. Brazil from
7 to 9 p.m.; Aug. 2: Egypt vs.
the United States from 7 to 9
p.m.; Aug. 3; Saudi Arabia vs.
West Germany from 7 to 9
p.m.
The outcomes of these
matches will determine the
teams for the playoffs. The
quarterfinals will be held Aug.
6 and 9 from 3 to 5 p.m. and 7
to 9 p.m. The semifinals are
scheduled for Aug. 8 from
8:30 to 10:30 pm. The final
match will be held in the Rose
Bowl Aug. 1 1.
ABC will be filming the soc
cer matches as part of their
Olympic coverage, and will be

installing lights at the Stadium
so that night games can be
played. As with all Olympic
coverage, most of the soccer
competition will be cut out so
your only sure bet is to be there
in person.
Approximately 140 Olympic
team members, coaches and
trainers will arrive on campus
around July 20 to start practic
ing. They will be housed in
Branner Hall where a special
security system, including
guards and fencing around the
dorm, will be installed as a
safety measure. The alterations
will be funded by the Interna
tional Olympic Committee.
