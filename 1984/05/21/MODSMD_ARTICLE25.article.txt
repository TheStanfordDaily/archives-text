# Individual play ends in L.A.
## 
### JOHN LIS Staff sportswriter 
Following the 6-0 victory over USC that gave
the Stanford women's tennis team the NCAA
I earn Championship Tuesday, six members of the
team moved into the individual portion of the
tournament last weekend.
Linda Gates had the most successful individual
performance. In addition to the doubles champi
onship she won with Elise Burgin, Gates finished
second in the singles competition, losing yesterday
in the finals to Lisa Spain of Georgia, 7-5, 3-6, 6-
2.
Spain raced out to a 3-0 lead in the first set, but
Gates came back to tie it at 3-3. Spain then broke
Gates' serve to take a 5-3 lead in the first set, but
Gates came back to tie it at 5-5. Spain then held
service to take a 6-5 lead and then broke Gates
again to win the set.
Gates led all the way in the second set, but in
the third, Spain broke her service at love, as well
as in the third and fifth games to close out the
match.
Stanford Head Coach Frank Brennan said,
"I've seen the last five finals, and they're usually
anti-climactic. This one was great. They both

played really good, exciting tennis."
Gates had won five matches to reach the finals.
The No. 4 seed opened Thursday with a first
round 6-3. 6-3 win over Marlin Noriega of Miami
(Fla.). followed by a 4-6. 6-0, 6-4 second-round
victory over Oklahoma State's Kcnata Mar
cinowska.
Friday. Gates defeated Hclianc Steden of USC,
7-5. h-2. in the third round and then defeated San
Diego State's Cynthia MacGrcgor. the No. 6 seed.
6-1, 6-4, in a quarterfinal match. Gates advanced
into the finals with an extremely close 6-7. 7-6, 7-
5 win over No. 13-seeded Ann Hulbert of South
ern Methodist in Saturday's semifinal.
Two other Stanford netters made it into the
quarterfinal round of eight. Leigh Anne Eldredge
upset No. I seed Kathleen Cummings of Texas in
the third round, 7-6. 6-1, to advance. In the quar
terfinal she met Hulbert, who defeated her, 6-4.
6-4.
Eldredge had defeated Florida's Kim Clingan,
6-2. 6-1. and SDSU's Linda Howell, 6-4, 2-6, 6-
2, in the first two rounds. Eldredge was the No. V
seed in the tournament.
Kate Gompert also made it
into the quarterfinals before she
fell to No. 2 seed Gretchen
Rush of Trinity, 6-3. 7-5. Gom
pert had defeated UCLA's
I .ynn Lewis, 6-1, 4-6, 6-3, in the
third round. Her first two wins
were over No. 7 seed Beverly
Bowes of Texas, 6-4, 6-2, and
Mary MacTaggart of Michigan,
6-4, 6-3.
Patty Fendick had been Stan
lord's highest seed at No. 3, but
the freshman fell on the first day
of competition to Margaret
Redfearn of Houston, 6-4, 1-6,
6-0. in a second-round match.
Fendick had defeated Cheryl
Jones of USC, 6-4, 4-6, 6-4, in
the opening round.
Michelle Weiss also fell in the
second round. She defeated In
diana's Tracy Hoffman, 6-4, 6-
0, in the opening round, but

then lost to Steden, 7-6, 7-5.
Burgin and Gates won the
doubles competition, but an
other Stanford team had been
favored to do so. Fendick and
Eldredge were seeded No. 1,
but fell in Friday's second round
to Miami's Ros Riach and Cathy
Richman. The pair had earlier
defeated Clemson's Jane For
man and Jodv Trucks, 6-7, 6-1,
6-4.
The third doubles team of
Gompert and Weiss was
eliminated early, falling in the
first round to Pepperdine's Pam
Jung and Akemi Nishiya, 3-6,
6-4, 6-4.

two years of eligibility when she
joined the professional circuit
last year. Gates, Fendick and
Eldredge will return and will
form the nucleus of next year's
team.
