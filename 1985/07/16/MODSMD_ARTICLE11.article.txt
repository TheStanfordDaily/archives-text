# Opinions
## The 						Stanford Daily Live Aid's success 
### 
Saturday's Live Aid benefit concert raised 55 million
dollars to feed the hungry in Ethiopia and other African
nations. The 60-odd rock superstars who performed at the
historic event avoided a seemingly inevitable clash of egos,
instead displaying great selflessness and determination in
making the 14-hour concert a success.
Even more laudable is the accomplishment of the or
ganizers and technicians — Live Aid was broadcast to an
estimated two billion TV sets around the world. Live Aid
used an unprecedented 10 satellites, compared to the four
used for the Olympic Games.
Usually, technological acheivements of this magnitude
result from greed — in the case of business/consumer in
novations — or fear — in refinement of military technol
ogy-
