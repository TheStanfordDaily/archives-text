Lively Arts at Stanford: Presents The Paul
Dresher Ensemble, performing the Northern
California premiere of "Slow Fire," a "micro
opera" about a mental patient's mixed reac
tions to a "banal" environment. Saturday,
Feb. 1, 8:30 p.m., Dinkelspiel Auditorium.
Tickets at Tresidder Ticket Office: $9 and
$10 public, $6 and $7 students. Student and
senior rush tickets $3.50. Free
lecture/demonstration of "Slow Fire" today in
Dinkelspiel at 12:15.
Stanford Drama Department and the Com
mittee on Black Performing Arts: Present
"The Amen Corner," a play by James
Baldwin about a black fundamentalist church.
Performances through Saturday and Feb. 5-8
at 8 p.m. The Nitery. 497-2576.
Elsewhere
American Conservatory Theatre: Presents
"You Never Can Tell," a 1897 satire on sex
roles and the family. Performances Mondays
through Saturdays at 8 p.m. and matinees on
Wednesdays and Saturdays at 2 p.m. Runs
through Feb. 22. 450 Geary St., San Fran
cisco. 771-3880.
Magic Theatre: Presents "Wild Indian," a
play based on the true story of Ishi, the sole
surviving Yahi Indian who stumbles into the
world of the Saldu, or white man. Perfor
mances Wednesdays through Saturdays at
8:30 p.m. and Sundays at 2 p.m. and 7:30
p.m. Runs through March 2. Magic Theatre
Northside, Building D, Fort Mason, San
Francisco. Call for times, 441-8001.
Nova Theatre: Presents "Sarah and the Sax,"
a drama centering around an encounter be
tween a Jewish matron and an embittered
black street musician. Last show Saturday at
8:30 p.m. 347 Dolores, San Francisco. 346-
5337.

Saratoga Chamber Theatre: Presents "And
Miss Reardon Drinks a Little," by Paul Zin
del. Performances Fridays and Saturdays at
8:30 p.m. Runs through Feb. 22. Azule Mall,
Saratoga. 996-9835.
Eureka Theatre Company: Presents
"Gardenia," a drama exploring the devasta

Wednesdays through Sundays at 8 p.m. Runs
through March 9. 2730 16th St., San Fran
cisco. 558-9898.
