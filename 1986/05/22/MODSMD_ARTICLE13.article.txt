# Get the most from exercise
## 
### 
PACE YOURSELF
You get the benefits of a
workout by exercising longer
rather than harder or faster.
Slow down when you need to.
STOP EXERCISING
• when you are extremely out
of breath. Keep moving gently
though; never stop an aerobic
activity abruptly.
• when you experience a
sharp pain in a joint or muscle
Slow down and join in again
when the symptoms disap
pear. If pain persists, go to
Cowell.
STRETCH
Always, before and after your
workout. (See article on
stretching, page 3) This will
help you avoid injury.
• Don't bounce while stretch
ing. Stretching should be
smooth and sustained.
LAND SMOOTHLY
Never land flat footed.
DRINK WATER
• before, during and after
your exercise session to avoid
dehydration.
WHAT TO WEAR
• Athletic shoes are a must!!
Use shoes appropriate to your
activity (e.g. aerobic shoes for
aerobic dance, running shoes
for jogging). • Women should
wear a supportive bra during
each workout.
A WORD OF CAUTION
• Do not eat a meal for 2 hours

before you exercise. You may
experience nausea and
cramps otherwise.
• If you are over age 35 and
not accustomed to vigorous
exercise, consult with a
physician before you start.
• If you are pregnant, consult
with your physician to receive
some guidelines on exercise
during pregnancy.
TARGET HEART RATE
Training at your target heart
rate increases your fitness
without causing extreme
fatigue or stress on body func

tions. During each workout
take your pulse several times
to check if you are within your
target heart rate range.
TO FIND YOUR PERSONAL
TARGET RANGE
USE THIS FORMULA:
(220 - age) (.7) to (220 - age)
(.85)
then divide by 6 to get your
ten second count.
EXERCISE PRESCRIPTION
To obtain maximum health
benefits, exercise at target heart
rate 3 to 4 times a week for 20
to 30 minutes each session.
