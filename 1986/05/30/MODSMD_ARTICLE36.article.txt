# Edberg ousted at French; Wilander survives a scare
## 
### 
u i Defending champion Mats
Wilander barely escaped an upset by American Aaron
Krickstein yesterday, while four seeded players
tumbled out of the French Open tennis championships.
And a trio of youngsters — Mary Joe Fernandez
third-seeded Steffi Graf and No. 9 Gabriela Sabatini
joined Chris Evert Lloyd among the winners on the
red clay courts of Roland Garros.
But Stefan Edberg, the men's No. 5 seed, fell to fel
low Swede Mikeal Pernfors, twice an NCAA champion
at Georgia, 6-7, 7-5, 6-3, 2-6, 6-4; Italy's Claudio
Panatta defeated No. 101 hierry Tulasne of France 0-
6, 3-6, 6-2, 6-4, 6-4; Argentina's Mercedes Paz ousted
No. 8 Manuela Maleeva of Bulgaria 5-7, 7-6, 7-5; and
Fernandez, a Miami high school freshman, eliminated
No. 14 Andrea Temesvari of Hungary 5-7, 6-2, 6-3.
"I was very lucky in the fifth set," Wilander said
after edging Krickstein 6-1, 3-6, 5-7, 6-3, b-4 in a
four-hour slugfest on center court. The No. 2 seed
from Sweden converted his only break point of the
decisive set, while Krickstein failed to convert on six
break points.
"Except for the stretch from 2-all to 5-2 in the third
set, he controlled the whole match," Wilander ad

mitted.
Lloyd, the defending women's champion and also
seeded No. 2, advanced into the fourth round of this
Grand Slam tournament by crushing fellow American
Anne Smith 6-3, 6-1.
On Day 4 of this two-week tournaent — a chilly,
windy day when ran briefly interrupted play — Graf
defeated Marcella Mesker of the Netherlands 6-2, 6-1;
No. 5 Hana Mandlikova stopped fellow Czechoslovak
Regina Marsikova 6-1, 6-2; Sabatini downed Ivanna
Madruga-Osses of Aigentina 6-3, 6-3; and No. 13
Carling Bassett of Canada eliminated Australia's
Jenny Byrne 6-3, 6-2.
In men's play, third-seeded Boris Becker of West
Germany, the reigning Wimbledon champion, ousted
Bruno Oresar of Yugoslavia 6-2, 6-0, 6-7, 6-3; No. 8
Henri Leconte of France downed Israel's Amos
Mansdorf 7-5, 6-3, 4-6, 6-0; No. 11 Martin Jaite of
Argentina outlasted Czechoslovakia's Miloslav Mecir
7-6, 1-6, 2-6, 6-3, 6-1; and No. 14 Emilio Sanchez of
Spain defeated Michiel Schapers of the Netherlands 6-
3, 6-2, 6-2.
Fernandez's victory moved her into a third-round
clash aeainst Britain's Anne Hobbs.
