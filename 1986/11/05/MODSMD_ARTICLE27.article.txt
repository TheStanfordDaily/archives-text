# BAY AREA EVENTS
## 
### 
Performances
Here
The Stanford Drama Department
presents: Arthur Schnitzler's "La
Ronde," tonight through Saturday at 8
p.m. in the Little Theatre. Set in fin-de
siecle Vienna, the play is described by
director Oliver Gerland as "10 glitter
ing scenes of seduction." Tickets are
$7, $3 off for students, available at
Tresidder Ticket Office (723-4317).
Seating in the Little Theatre is limited.
The Stanford Savoyards present:
"The Sorcerer", an operetta by Gilbert
& Sullivan, Friday and Saturday at 8
p.m., Sunday at 2:30 p.m., in Dinkel
spiel Auditorium. Tickets are available
at Tresidder Ticket Office for $6.50,
$2.50 off for students.
The lively Arts at Stanford pres
ent: The Washington Ballet from
Washington D.C. — "one of the
country's finest professional ballet
companies" — Tuesday Nov. 11 at 8
p.m. in Memorial Auditorium. Tickets
are $14.50, $13, $11.50, and $10.50, $3
off for students, available at Tresidder
Ticket Office. The company will also
present a lecture-demonstration at
noon on Nov. 11 in Memorial
Auditorium; donations are requested.
Elsewhere
The Magic Theatre presents:
"Visions of Beckett," a showcase of two

rarely performed Samuel Beckett
pieces as well as two English-language
stage premieres. Plays Wednesdays
through Saturdays at 8:30 p.m., Sun
days at 2 and 7:30 p.m., through Dec.
14. At the Magic Thetare, Fort Mason
Center, Building D, San Francisco.
Tickets are $15 and $12.50, $9 for stu
dents, available at all Ticketron outlets
or by calling the box office at 441-8822.
The San Jose Civic Light Opera

presents: "Oliver!" Plays this Friday
and Saturday at 8:30 p.m., Sunday at 7
p.m., weekend matinees at 2 p.m. At 18
Paseo de San Antonio, San Jose. Ticket
prices range from $10 to $25 and can
be charged by phone at (408) 971-
1212.
Theatre Works presents: Neil
Simon's "Brighton Beach Memoirs,"

evenings at 8 p.m. and Sundays at 2:30
p.m., through Nov. 15. At the Burgess
Theatre, 601 Laurel Street, Menlo Park.
For tickets or more information, call
the CAPA Box Office at 329-2623
The San Jose Repertory Company
presents: "GocLspell," the family mu
sical based on the Gospel according to
St. Matthew. Plays Saturday and Tues
day at 8 p.m., Sunday at 2 and 7 p.m., at
the Montgomery Theatre, located at S.

Market and W. San Carlos Streets in
San Jose. Ticket prices range from $11
to $25, and are available at the San Jose
Rep. Box Office, 476 Park Avenue, or
by calling (408) 294-7572.
The Palo Alto Players present:
"Footlight Frenzy," an evening of
"frenetic comedy" about a PTA group
of questionable talent that tries to stage

a fund-raising play. Plays Friday and
Saturday at 8 p.m., Sunday at 2:30 p.m.,
at the Lucie Stern Theatre, 1305 Mid
dlefield Road, Palo Alto. Tickets are
$8.50 and $10.50; student discounts are
available. Call the CAPA Box Office at
329-2626.
The Berkeley Repertory Theatre
presents: Charles Dickens' "Hard
Times," Wednesday through Saturdays
at 8:30 p.m., Sundays at 2:30 and 7:30

p.m., through Nov. 16. Ticket prices
range from $10 to $14, with student
discounts available. Tickets can be pur
chased at the Berkeley Rep. Box Office,
2025 Addison Street, Berkeley (845-
4700).
Classical Music
Here
The Stanford Music Department

presents: Soprano Doris Willaims and
accompanying vocalists, in a free reci
tal of works by John Dowland.
Madrigals by Monteverdi and others
will also be performed. Today at 8 p.m.
in Campbell Recital Hall.
The Stanford Department of Music
presents: Harpists Ruth Bonaparte
and Maria Mendieta, performing works
by Ravel and others, tomorrow at 8
p.m. in Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Tick-

ets are $5, $2 off for students, available
at the door and at Tresidder Ticket Of
fice (723-4317).
The Lively Arts at Stanford pres
ent: The Guarneri String Quartet and
the Kalichstein-Laredo-Robinson Trio
together, performing an afternoon of
chamber music. This Sunday at 2:30
p.m. in Memorial Auditorium. Tickets

are $16.50, $15.50, $14.50, and $13 50,
$3 off for students. Tickets are available
at Tresidder Ticket Office and all BASS
outlets.
Elsewhere
The School of Creative Arts
presents: World-renowned Swiss
chamber orchestra "The Mas
terplayers." Works by great masters of
the past as well as several contempo
rary pieces will be performed. This
Sunday at 3 p.m. in McKenna Theatre at
San Francisco State University, 1600
Holloway Street, San Francisco. Admis
sion is free.
Pop/Jazz/Rock
Elsewhere

Steamroller, the "grandfathers of
electronic music," performing music
from their "Fresh Aire" album series,
Thursday through Saturday at 8 p.m.,
Sunday at 2 and 7 p.m., at the Warfield
Theatre in San Francisco. Tickets are
available at all BASS outlets or by call
ing 762-2277.
The Pena Moai Cultural Center
presents: "Blazing Redheads," an all
female group that combines fusion,
Latin, jazz, rock and progressive music.
"High-powered and highly danceable."
This Saturday at 7:30 p.m. at the Pena
Moai Center, 1944 University Avenue,
Palo Alto. Admission is $5.
