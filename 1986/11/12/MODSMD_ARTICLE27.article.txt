Performances
Here
The Stanford Drama Department
presents: Arthur Schnitzler's "La
Ronde," tonight through Saturday at 8
p.m. in the Little Theatre. Set in fin-cle
siecle Vienna, the play is described by
director Oliver Gerland as "10 glitter
ing scenes of seduction." Tickets are
$ 7 , $3 off for students, available at
Tresidder Ticket Office (723-4317).
Seating in the Little Theatre is limited.
The Stanford Savnvard* nre«s*»«t
"The Sorcerer," an operetta by Gilbert
and Sullivan, Friday and Saturday at 8
p.m. in Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Tick
ets are available at Tresidder Ticket Of
fice for $6.50, $2.50 off for students.
The Stanford Residential Arts Pro
gram and Rape Education Project
present: "Moonlight Serenade," by
Toni Press, about the family of an AIDS
victim. The play is described as both a

comedy and a drama, and features
Stanford students. Plays at the Elliot
Program Center tomorrow through
Saturday at 8 p.m., Sunday at 3 p.m.
Tickets are $3, free to Stanford stu
dents, and can be reserved by calling
723-1521. Tickets will be held at the
entrance and must be claimed 30
minutes before showtime.
Elsewhere
The Magic Theatre presents:
"Visions of Beckett," directed by Stan
Gotarski, a showcase of two rarely per
formed Beckett pieces as well as two
English-language premieres. Wednes
days through Saturdays at 8:30 p.m.,
Sundays at 2 p.m. and 7:30 p.m.,
through Dec. 14. At the Magic Theatre,
Fort Mason Center, Building D, San
Francisco. Tickets are $15 and $12.50,
$9 for students, available at all Tick
etron outlets or by calling the box of
fice at 441-8822.
Theatre Works presents: Neil

Simon's "Brighton Beach Memoirs,"
evenings at 8 p.m. and Sundays at 2:30
p.m., through Nov. 15. At the Burgess
Theatre, 601 Laurel St., Menlo Park.
For tickets or more information, call
the CAPA Box Office at 329-2623.
Classical Music
Here
The Lively Arts at Stanford pres
ent: "Three Generations of Music,"
featuring violinist David Abel, pianist
Julie Strindberg, percussionist William
Winant, and guest artists Lou Harrison
and William Colvig, performing works
by three generations of experimental
American composers, including John
Cage and Charles Ives. This Friday at 8
p.m. in Kresge Auditorium; tickets are
$13 50, $3 off for students, and are
available at Tresidder Ticket Office
(723-4317). The musicians will also
hold a free rehearsal on Friday 2-5
p.m., also in Kresge.

The Stanford Music Department
presents: "Renaissance to Rameau,'
an evening of music for viols, featuring
music from the English Renaissance
and the French Baroque periods. The
concert will be held on Friday at 8 p.m.
in Campbell Recital Hall. There is no
admission charge.
The Stanford Department of Music
presents: A recital by cellist Irene
Sharp and pianist Naomi Sparrow, per
forming 20th-century works for cello
and piano, at 8 p.m. this Sunday in
Campbell Recital Hall. Sparrow is the
coordinator of the Stanford Music De
partment's piano program. Tickets are
$5, $2 off for students, and are
available at Tresidder and at the door.
The Stanford Department of Music
presents: The Stanford Symphonic
Band, conducted by Arthur Barnes,
performing 19th and 20th-century
music, this Sunday at 2:30 p.m. in Dink
elspiel Auditorium. Tickets are $5, $2
off for students, and are available at
Tresidder and at the door.
The Stanford Department of Music
presents: The Stanford Chorale and
the Stanford Contemporary Dance En
semble, in a joint performance of Gian
Carlo Menotti's madrigal fable, "The
Unicorn, the Gorgon and the Man

ticore" at 8 p.m. Tuesday in Memorial
Church. The Chorale wili also perform
Ottorino Respighi's "Laud to the Na
tivity." Tickets are $6, $3 off for stu
dents, and are available at Tresidder
and at the door.
