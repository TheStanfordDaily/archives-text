WIN $$$
Programming Contest
The Basic Research Sleep Laboratory of the Stanford Sleep
Disorders Center is offering a prize of $300 for the best
computer program producing a pedigree tree. The program will
be required to graphically depict the lineage of up to 200
animals. Working programs will be evaluated on the aesthetics of
the resulting tree.
Programs must be written in C or Pascal and run on IBM PC or
Macintosh computers. Tor complete details, pick up the contest
rules and specifications at Margaret Jacks Halls, LOTS or contact
Rand at 725-6510, the Sleep Lab, basement of the Anatomy
Building. Deadline for submission is May 15, 1987.
Mixed Company presents
FEMALE TROUBLES
a mad theatrical review of women and medical care
Wednesday, April 15, 7:30 p.m.
Cubberley Auditorium
Donations accepted
This performance is part of the Jing Lyman Lecture Series "Women's Humor
and Social Satire," sponsored by the'lnstitute for Research on Women and
Gender, the Women's Center, the Program in Feminist Studies, and the
Graduate Women's Network.
