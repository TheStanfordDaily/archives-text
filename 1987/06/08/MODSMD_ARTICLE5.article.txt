# THEY PHONED HOME ...
## 
### 
They phoned home, they phoned friends and they phoned alumni all over the United States
to raise money for Stanford at the Fifth Annual Spring Telethon. They phoned for fun as well
as for funds.
More than 300 Stanford students, alumni and friends gathered amidst balloons and bells for
a solid week of calling between May 9 and May 16,1987. Spurred by the Centennial
celebrations of Cornerstone Weekend, enthusiastic volunteers reached more than 5000
alumni.
Their valiant efforts were rewarded by a grand total of $189,000 in specified pledges to
Stanford—enough to earn easily the $100,000 matching grant offered as a challenge by
alumnus Ralph P. Davidson, '50, former chairman of Time, Inc.

Individuals and groups vied for numerous prizes donated by many generous Bay Area
merchants and organizations, including lunches hosted by Stanford faculty members and a
hot-air balloon ride offered by alumnus Al Amsbaugh, 60. Callers walked away, not only
with a tangible prize, but also with the satisfaction of knowing that they had contributed to
something important.
The Office of Development wishes to thank all those who helped make the Fifth Annual
Telethon a resounding success. In particular, grateful thanks to members of the student
organizing committee, the callers, clerks and other Telethon helpers for the many hours
they donated to support Stanford.

They phoned home, they phoned friends and they phoned alumni all over the United States
to raise money for Stanford at the Fifth Annual Spring Telethon. They phoned for fun as well
as for funds.
More than 300 Stanford students, alumni and friends gathered amidst balloons and bells for
a solid week of calling between May 9 and May 16,1987. Spurred by the Centennial
celebrations of Cornerstone Weekend, enthusiastic volunteers reached more than 5000
alumni.
Their valiant efforts were rewarded by a grand total of $189,000 in specified pledges to
Stanford—enough to earn easily the $100,000 matching grant offered as a challenge by
alumnus Ralph P. Davidson, '50, former chairman of Time, Inc.

Individuals and groups vied for numerous prizes donated by many generous Bay Area
merchants and organizations, including lunches hosted by Stanford faculty members and a
hot-air balloon ride offered by alumnus Al Amsbaugh, 60. Callers walked away, not only
with a tangible prize, but also with the satisfaction of knowing that they had contributed to
something important.
The Office of Development wishes to thank all those who helped make the Fifth Annual
Telethon a resounding success. In particular, grateful thanks to members of the student
organizing committee, the callers, clerks and other Telethon helpers for the many hours
they donated to support Stanford.

They phoned home, they phoned friends and they phoned alumni all over the United States
to raise money for Stanford at the Fifth Annual Spring Telethon. They phoned for fun as well
as for funds.
More than 300 Stanford students, alumni and friends gathered amidst balloons and bells for
a solid week of calling between May 9 and May 16,1987. Spurred by the Centennial
celebrations of Cornerstone Weekend, enthusiastic volunteers reached more than 5000
alumni.
Their valiant efforts were rewarded by a grand total of $189,000 in specified pledges to
Stanford—enough to earn easily the $100,000 matching grant offered as a challenge by
alumnus Ralph P. Davidson, '50, former chairman of Time, Inc.

Individuals and groups vied for numerous prizes donated by many generous Bay Area
merchants and organizations, including lunches hosted by Stanford faculty members and a
hot-air balloon ride offered by alumnus Al Amsbaugh, 60. Callers walked away, not only
with a tangible prize, but also with the satisfaction of knowing that they had contributed to
something important.
The Office of Development wishes to thank all those who helped make the Fifth Annual
Telethon a resounding success. In particular, grateful thanks to members of the student
organizing committee, the callers, clerks and other Telethon helpers for the many hours
they donated to support Stanford.
