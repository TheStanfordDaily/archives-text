# The 						Fred Astaire Film Festival July 10 through July 23 at the Stanford Theater Entire Box Office Proceeds Donated to The Children's Hospital at Stanford
## 
### 
Daily Admission $4.00 (double feature); Festival Pass $20.00 (two weeks).
The Stanford Theater is located at 221 University Avenue in downtown Palo Alto.
The following tentative show dates are subject to change. For confirmation
please call the box office at (415) 324-3700. All films shown in 35mm prints!
Produced with the support of the David and Lucile Packard Foundation with
special thanks to RKO Pictures and the UCLA Film Archive.

Top Hat (RKO 1935)
Music and lyrics by Irving Berlin. Fred Astaire, Ginger Rogers,
Eric Blore, Edward Everett Horton, Helen Broderick, Erik Rhodes.
Directed by Mark Sandrich. Songs include No Strings; Isn't This
a Lovely Day?; Top Hat, White Tie, and Tails; Cheek to Cheek;
The Piccolino.
The most famous Astaire-Rogers film. If you haven't seen it
on a big screen, don't miss this chance!
Friday (7/10) 7:00; Saturday (7/11) 1:30; Tuesday (7/14) 7:00.
The Gay Divorcee (RKO 1934)
Music and lyrics by Cole Porter and others. Fred Astaire, Ginger
Rogers, Eric Blore, Edward Everett Horton, Alice Brady, Erik
Rhodes, Betty Grable. Directed by Mark Sandrich. Songs include
Night and Day; A Needle in a Haystack; The Continental.
The first film in which Fred and Ginger were given top billing.
"The Continental" won the first Oscar ever given for best song.
Friday (7/10) 5:00, 8:50; Saturday (7/11) 3:20; Tuesday (7/14) 5:00.
Shall We Dance (RKO 1937)
Music by George Gershwin, lyrics by Ira Gershwin. Fred Astaire,
Ginger Rogers, Eric Blore, Edward Everett Horton. Directed by
Mark Sandrich. Songs include Let's Call the Whole Thing Off;
They All Laughed; They Can't Take That Away from Me.
The Gershwin songs all became standards.
Sunday (7/12) 7:00; Monday (7/13) 4:50.
Follow the Fleet (RKO 1936)
Music and lyrics by Irving Berlin. Fred Astaire, Ginger Rogers,
Randolph Scott, Harriet Hilliard. Directed by Mark Sandrich.
Songs include Let's Face the Music and Dance; Let Yourself Go.
Fine musical numbers make up for a somewhat weak plot. You will
recognize twenty-one year old Harriet Hilliard (Nelson) of Ozzie
and Harriet.
Sunday (7/12) 9:10; Monday (7/13) 8:30.

Swing Time (RKO 1936)
Music by Jerome Kern, lyrics by Dorothy Fields. Fred Astaire,
Ginger Rogers, Eric Blore, Helen Broderick, Victor Moore.
Directed by George Stevens. Songs include Pick Yourself Up; The
Way You Look Tonight; Waltz in Swing Time; A Fine Romance;
Bojangles of Harlem; Never Gonna Dance.
Swing Time contains the most sublime dances ever recorded on film.
Saturday (7/11) 7:00; Sunday (7/12) 1:30; Tuesday (7/14) 8:50.
Roberta (RKO 1935)
Music by Jerome Kern, lyrics by Otto Harbach, Dorothy Fields,
Bernard Dougal. Fred Astaire, Ginger Rogers, Irene Dunne,
Randolph Scott. Directed by William A. Seiter. Songs include
I'll Be Hard to Handle; Yesterdays; I Won't Dance; Smoke Gets in
Your Eyes; Lovely to Look At.
This film has a fine musical score by Jerome Kern, and Astaire's
tap solo "I Won't Dance" is one of his very best.
Saturday (7/11) 8:55; Sunday (7/12) 3:25.
Flying Down to Rio (RKO 1933)
Music by Vincent Youmans, lyrics by Edward Eliscu and Gus Kahn.
Dolores Del Rio, Gene Raymond, Ginger Rogers, Fred Astaire.
Directed by Thornton Freeland. Songs include Music Makes Me;
Orchids in the Moonlight; The Carioca; Flying Down to Rio.
The first film in which the public saw Fred and Ginger dancing.
Saturday (7/11) 5:20; Sunday (7/12) 5:20.
Carefree (RKO 1938)
Music and lyrics by Irving Berlin. Fred Astaire, Ginger Rogers,
Ralph Bellamy, Luella Gear, Jack Carson. Directed by Mark
Sandrich. Songs include Change Partners; I Used to Be Color
Blind; The Yam.
This enjoyable film, in which Fred plays a psychiatrist, is
seldom shown.
Monday (7/13) 7:00.
A Damsel in Distress (RKO 1937)
Music by George Gershwin, lyrics by Ira Gershwin. Fred Astaire,
George Burns, Gracie Allen, Joan Fontaine. Songs include A Foggy
Day in London Town; Nice Work If You Can Get It. Wednesday
(7/15) 7:00.
Second Chorus (Paramount 1941)
Featuring Artie Shaw's band, Fred Astaire, Paulette Goddard.
Even if Astaire once termed this "the worst film I ever made," it
does offer the chance to hear Artie Shaw's band at its peak.
Wednesday (7/15) 5:25, 8:50.
Holiday Inn (Paramount 1942)
Music and lyrics by Irving Berlin. Fred Astaire, Bing Crosby,
Marjorie Reynolds, Virginia Dale. This is the film in which
Crosby sings White Christmas and in which Astaire does his
famous firecracker dance. Thursday (7/16) 7:00.
Blue Skies (Paramount 1946)
Music and lyrics by Irving Berlin. Fred Astaire, Bing Crosby,
Joan Caulfield. Best number is Astaire's dance "Puttin' on the
Ritz." Thursday (7/16) 5:05, 8:50.
You Were Never Lovelier (Columbia 1942)
Music by Jerome Kern, lyrics by Johnny Mercer. Fred Astaire,
Rita Hayworth, Adolphe Menjou. Directed by William A. Seiter.
Rita Hayworth called this film "one of the only jewels in my
life." Songs include Dearly Beloved and I'm Old Fashioned.
Friday (7/17) 7:00; Saturday (7/18) TBA.
You'll Never Get Rich (Columbia 1941)
Music and lyrics by Cole Porter. Fred Astaire, Rita Hayworth.
The film that made Rita Hayworth a star. Friday (7/17) 5:20,
8:50.
Broadway Melody of 1940 (MGM 1940)
Music and lyrics by Cole Porter. Fred Astaire, Eleanor Powell,
George Murphy, Frank Morgan. Songs include Please Don't Monkey
With Broadway; I've Got My Eyes on You; I Concentrate on You;
Begin the Beguine. Saturday (7/18) TBA, 9:05.
The Band Wagon (MGM 1953)
Music by Arthur Schwartz, lyrics by Howard Dietz. Fred Astaire,
Cyd Charisse, Jack Buchanan, Oscar Levant, Nanette Fabray.
Directed by Vincente Minnelli. Songs include By Myself; A Shine
on Your Shoes; That's Entertainment; Dancing in the Dark;
Triplets; I Guess I'll Have to Change My Plan. This is one of
the greatest of Astaire's films. Saturday (7/18) 7:00; Sunday
(7/19) 4:55.
Easter Parade (MGM 1948)
Music and lyrics by Irving Berlin. Fred Astaire, Judy Garland,
Ann Miller, Peter Lawford. Directed by Charles Walters. Songs
include Stepping Out With My Baby; We're a Couple of Swells;
Easter Parade. Sunday (7/19) 1:15, 7:00.
Ziegfeld Follies (MGM 1945)
A revue consisting of fourteen skits and musical numbers of
varying quality. Astaire dances once with Gene Kelly (The Babbit
and the Bromide) and twice with Lucille Bremer (This Heart of
Mine, and Limehouse Blues). This Heart of Mine is one of Astaire's
best numbers. Monday (7/20) 5:00, 9:00.

Royal Wedding (MGM 1951)
Music by Burton Lane, lyrics by Alan Jay Lerner. Fred Astaire,
Jane Powell, Peter Lawford, Sarah Churchill. This story of a
brother-sister song and dance team was inspired by the early
adventures of Fred and his sister Adele. The film contains two
of Astaire's best known numbers, dancing with a hat rack and on
the ceiling. Sunday (7/19) 3:10, 8:55.
Yolanda and the Thief (MGM 1945)
Music by Harry Warren, lyrics by Arthur Freed. Fred Astaire,
Lucille Bremer, Frank Morgan, Mildred Natwick. Directed by
Vincente Minnelli. Musical fantasy about con man Astaire trying
to swindle heiress Bremer by posing as her guardian angel.
Although this film failed at the box office, many persons
(including the organizers of this festival) regard the film with
great affection. Monday (7/20) 7:00.
Three Little Words (MGM 1950)
Music by Harry Ruby, lyrics by Bert Kalmar. Fred Astaire,
Vera-Ellen, Red Skelton, Arlene Dahl. The story, partly true, of
songwriters Harry Ruby and Bert Kalmar. This film was one of
Fred Astaire's favorites. Songs include Where Did You Get That
Girl?; Who's Sorry Now?; I Wanna Be Loved by You; Nevertheless;
Thinking of You. Tuesday (7/21) 7:00.
The Belle of New York (MGM 1952)
Music by Harry Warren, lyrics by Johnny Mercer. Fred Astaire,
Vera-Ellen, Marjorie Main, Keenan Wynn. One of Astaire's lesser
films, but in Astaire's own words "there were some awfully good
dance numbers in that thing." Tuesday (7/21) 5:25, 8:55.
Silk Stockings (MGM 1957)
Music and lyrics by Cole Porter. Fred Astaire, Cyd Charisse,
Janis Paige, Peter Lorre. Directed by Rouben Mamoulian. Songs
include All of You; Satin and Silk; Siberia; The Ritz Roll and
Rock; Too Bad (You Can't Go Back to Moscow). Astaire's last
great musical movie. Wednesday (7/22) 7:00.
Daddy Long Legs (Twentieth Century-Fox 1955)
Music and lyrics by Johnny Mercer. Fred Astaire, Leslie Caron,
Fred Clark, Thelma Ritter. Story of playboy Astaire sponsoring
college education of orphan Caron. Best song is Something's
Gotta Give. Wednesday (7/22) 4:45, 9:10.
Funny Face (Paramount 1957)
Music by George Gershwin, lyrics by Ira Gershwin. Fred Astaire,
Audrey Hepburn, Kay Thompsoi . Directed by Stanley Donen. Astaire
a.s fashion photographer, modeled on Richard Avedon (who served
as consultant for the film). The photography is the most
visually stylish of any Astaire film. We hope to be able to show
a genuine IB Technicolor print. Thursday (7/23) 7:00.
Finian's Rainbow (Warner 1968)
Music by Burton Lane, lyrics by E. Y. Harburg. Fred Astaire,
Petula Clark, Tommy Steele, Keenan Wynn. As Leonard Maltin's TV
movie guide says, "Try to see it in a theater." Here is your
chance! Thursday (7/23) 8:55.
