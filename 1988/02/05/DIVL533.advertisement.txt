Five Weeks of Classic Films at the Stanford Theater
Honoring Three Directors:
Ernst Lubitsch (1892-1947) ♦ Rouben Mamoulian (1898-1987)
Josef von Sternberg (1894-1969)
As a Benefit for The UCLA Film and Television Archives
With emphasis on films from the golden age of Paramount Pictures, starring
Jeanette Mac Donald, Maurice Chevalier; Miriam Hopkins, Gary Cmpei;
Fredric March, Marlene Dietrich, Carole Lombard, and the Marx Brothers.
Also three films with Greta Garbo, three with Audrey Hepburn, and an
. evening of newsreels.
This five-week festival is presented by The David and Lucile Packard
Foundation in cooperation with the UCLA Film Archives. All films will be
shown in 35mm prints with carbon arc projectors. General admission is $4.00
per day (double feature). All box office pr<xeeds go directly to UCLA for film
preservation. Free festival pass (for two persons) if you donate at least $100.00
to the UCLA Film Archives (we can forward your check).
Open Night Ceremony on Friday, February 5 at 7:00 pm, with antique cars.
The Stanford Theater is Uxated at 221 University Avenue in downtown Palo
Alto. Telephone (415) 324-3700 to verify schedule, which is subject to change.
Love Me Tonight Fri. (2/5) 7:30, Sat. (2/6) 3:15; Tue. (2/9) 7:30.
The Love Parade Fri. (2/5) 9:30, Sat. (2/6) 5:20.
Lecture by Miles Kruger on Rouben Mamoulian and the American stage and
screen musical Sat. (2/6) 2:15, followed by Love Me Tonight at 3:15.
Admission ticket for the film includes this lecture.
