# Bentsen to speak tomorrow
## VP hopeful will tour SLAC after early morning speech 
### Jill Daniels Staff writer 
The presidential campaign will
hit Stanford bright and early to
morrow morning when Democra
tic vice-presidential candidate Sen.
Lloyd Bentsen speaks in Kresge
Auditorium at 8 a.m.
The Texan's speech, entitled
"Technology, Education, and the
Future" is not an ordinary cam
paign speech, according to Subodh
Chandra, chair of the Stanford
Democrats.
Bentsen's visit was announced
yesterday evening at a meeting of
the Stanford Democrats, who are
sponsoring the event. The ar
rangements were finalized yester
day afternoon.
Space is limited in Kresge Au
ditorium, and only 500 students
will be admitted on a first-come
first-serve basis. For reasons of
security, no overflow will be al
lowed, and those who wish to at
tend should arrive early in order
to ensure their admittance. Doors
will open at 7:30 a.m.
The speech will not be simul
taneously broadcast over
loudspeakers because classes will
be in session.
Following Bentsen's speech, he
will tour the Stanford Linear Ac
celerator Center with Sen. Sam
Nunn, D-Ga., Sen. Barbara
Mikulski, D-Md., and Sen. Ben
nett Johnston, D-La.
Afterward, Nunn, Mikulski and
Johnston will participate in a dis
cussion entitled "Serving Your

to advocate Democratic ideals,"

votes, is a priority state, and that
the 12th district that Stanford is
in is a priority. Stanford can pro
vide the margin of victory (in the
presidential election). That's why
Bentsen's coming."

ure, Justice Anthony Kennedy,
will speak at Kresge at 12:15 p.m.
Wednesday. Kennedy, a Law
School alum, was appointed to the
