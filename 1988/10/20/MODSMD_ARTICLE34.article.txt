# Readings Worth Hearing
## 
### 
Floyd Salas, director of the San Quentin Poetry Workshop, will
read a selection of prison poetry Thursday, Oct. 20 from 4 to 6
p.m., Fort Mason Conference Hall, Building A, Room 2, San
Francisco. The reading is part of National Poetry Week 11. Call
621-3073 or 776-6602 for details. Student tickets are $3 each.
Events continue through Sunday, Oct. 23.
Lee Brandenburg and Andrew Lewis Shepherd will sign copies
of "The Captive American" Thursday, Oct. 20 from 1 to 3 p.m.
hi the Bookstore. "The Captive American" has received a fair
amount of attention recently, and the story behind its publication
is as interesting as the book itself, so it's worth your while to
stop by.
Josiah Thompson, master of the detective story and author
of the best-selling book "GumShoe," will speak Thursday, Oct.
20 at 6:30 p.m. at Branner lounge.
Erotic Poetry: Love in the Afternoon, Friday, Oct. 21 from 4
to 6:30 p.m. in Fort Mason Conference Hall, Building A, San
Francisco. Includes Mary Mackey, Ivan Arguelles, Jane Hirshfield,
Patrick Finley, Lynn Londadier, Viola Weinderg and Jeremy Lamer.
The Liberated Muse, an evening of readings focusing on
poetry of gay and lesbian liberation, Friday, Oct. 21 from 8 to
10:30 p.m., Fort Mason Conference Hall, Building A, San Francisco.
Includes Neeli Cherkovski, Judy Grahn, Susan Griffin and Harold
Norse.
Gretel Ehrlich, author of "The Solace of Open Spaces," will
read from her latest novel, "Heart Mountain" Friday, Oct. 21 at 8
p.m. at Printers Inc. (310 California Ave.).
Nancy Packer, professor of English at Stanford, will sian copies
of her latest book, "In My Father's House: Tales of an Unconfor
mable Man," Friday, Oct. 21 from 1 to 3 p.m. at the Bookstore.
Poetry in Work and Play, poems of longshormen, truckers,
cowboys and unions, Saturday, Oct. 22 from 1 to 4 p.m., Fort
Mason Conference Hall, Building A, San Francisco.
An evening of readings by Israeli and American Jewish
poets will be held Sunday, Oct. 23 at 8 p.m. in Tresidder Union,
Oak West. The event is part of a larger Bay Area celebration of
"The Writer in the Jewish Community." Participants include Pulitzer
Prize-winner Stanley Kunitz, Dalia Ravikovitch, Harvey Shapiro
and Meir Wieseltier.
Heinz-Otto Peitgen, coauthor of "The Beauty of Fractals," will
give a visual presentation about fractals Tuesday, Oct. 25 from
7:30 to 9:30 p.m. at the Bookstore. Peitgen is one of the most
important mathematicians working with fractals, so expect a
packed house for this major, exciting event.
Meg Morris, columnist for The Times Tribune, will speak on
Tuesday, Oct. 25 at 8 p.m. at Printers Inc. (301 Castro St.,
Mountain View). Her topic: "Demystifying New Age Books."
Wallace Stegner, founder of the Stanford Creative Writing Pro
gram and recipient of the Pulitzer Prize and the National Book
Award, will sign copies of his new novel, "Crossing to Safety,"
Wednesday, Oct. 26 from 1 to 3 p.m. at the Bookstore.
Cynthia Ozick, novelist, essayist and short story writer of in
ternational acclaim, will read from and speak about her work
Wednesday, Oct. 26 at 8 p.m. in Cubberley Auditorium. This
important event is sponsered by the Program in Jewish Studies
and the Jewish Community Endowment Fund. Call 723-7589 for
more information.
