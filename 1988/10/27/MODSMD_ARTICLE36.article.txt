# Readings Worth Hearing
## 
### 
Edward Abbey, environmentalist, anarchist, activist and author,
will read from his novel, "The Fool's Progress" tomorrow at 8
p.m. at Kepler's Books (821 El Camino Real).
Paul Ehrllch and Darryl Wheye will sign copies of "A Birder's
Handbook" tomorrow at 1 p.m. at the Stanford Bookstore. Ehrlich
is a professor of biological sciences at Stanford and the founder
and director of Stanford's Center for Conservation Biology. Wheye
is a biological consultant and writer.
Ruthane Lum McGunn, author of "Chinese American Portraits"
will speak Tuesday, Nov. 1 at 8 p.m. at Printers Inc. (301 Castro
St., Mountain View).
Donald Knuth, Oren Patashnik and Ronald Graham will sign
copies of "Concrete Mathematics: A Foundation for Computer
Science" Wednesday, Nov. 2 at 12 p.m. at the Stanford Bookstore.
Kneuth is Fletcher Jones Professor of Computer Science at Stan
ford.
Victoria Nichols and Susan Thompson, authors of "Silk Stag
ings: When Women Write of Murder," will speak Thursday, Nov.
3 at 8 p.m. at Printers Inc. (310 California Ave., Palo Alto).
Matt Groening, creator or "Life in Hell" will sign his latest book,
"Childhood is Hell," on Thursday, Nov. 3 from 1:30 to 5 p.m. at
the Stanford Bookstore. Binky says: "Get there before the lines
get too long!"
