# Readings Worth Hearing
## 
### 
Matt Groaning, creator of "Life in Hell" and author of "Love is
Hell," "Work is Hell" and "School is Hell," will sign his fourth and
latest book, "Childhood is Hell" today from 1:30 to 5 p.m. at the
Stanford Bookstore.
Victoria Nichols and Susan Thompson, authors of "SILK
STALKINGS: When Women Write of Murder" will speak tonight
at 8 p.m. at Printers Inc. (310 California Ave.)
Arturo Islas, Stanford professor and author of "The Rain God"
and "Migrant Souls" will read tonight at 6:30 in the Adams House
Lounge, Sterling Quad.
Bay Area mystery writers. Eight Bay Area mystery writers will
sign their works and speak with both the converted and the
curious at the Stanford Bookstore on Friday, Nov. 4 from 1 to 3
p.m.. If you love mystery, and are interested in local writing talent,
don't miss this.
Audre Lorde, poet, writer and activist in the black, women's,
and gay/lesbian movements will read Wednesday, Nov. 9 at 8
p.m. in Kresge Auditorium. Contact the Graduate Women's Net
work for tickets.
