The Carnegie Endowment for International Peace is sponsoring the
Student Intern Program which is designed to provide substantive work
experience in international affairs. Those eligible are:
• seniors graduating in the 1988/89 academic year
• graduates from the class of 1988.
Nationally, 10 students with serious career interests in international
affairs will be hired to work at the Endowment on a full-time basis for
six months. Internship begins in June or September 1989.
The Carnegie Endowment for International Peace is an educational
organization based in Washington D.C. It examines international
relations and U.S. foreign policy through research, discussion and a
variety of publications.
For an information packet or more information, contact Sandra Viera at
725-2830 from 8 am to 12 pm; or go to the internship counter at the
Career Planning and Placement Center.
