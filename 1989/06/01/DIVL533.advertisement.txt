WE SUPPORT YOU!
The 55 students who were arrested in the protest at President Kennedy's office were motivated by a strong
commitment to building a multi-cultural Stanford. These students should not be charged with violating the
Fundamental Standard; and no individual students should be singled out for their particular roles in the protest.
Charging students who took action in order to improve Stanford while racial incidents go unpunished is
hypocritical and wrong. Furthermore, the administration's statement that the students are charged under the
Campus Disruption Policy, not the Fundamental Standard, is a misrepresentation of the relationship of the two
policies and seems to be simply an unsuccessful attempt to avoid the appearance of hypocrisy.
From all across Stanford, we support these students . ..
The following Faculty and Staff members signed this statement to President Kennedy:
"We are writing to encourage you to exercise leadership in focusing the attention of the University on
addressing the concerns raised by the Agenda for Action Coalition ... We ask that the administration
not prosecute the students who participated in the occupation of your office under the
Fundamental Standard... we earnestly ask that individual students not he singled out for the
particular roles they played in the occupation ... A spirit of generosity and conciliation will do a lot to
restore trust and rebuild channels of communication that will allow us to get on with the tasks of addressing
the students' concerns and implementing the vision of the UCMI report in the fullest possible manner."
