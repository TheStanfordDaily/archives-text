Drama Droppings
Even thought the year is ending there's still plenty of
drama to see courtesy of the Stanford Department of Drama.
"Antigone/Cassandra," a performance art piece incorpo
rating music, dance and theater, shows tonight through
Sunday at 8 p.m. in the Nitery at Old Union. Tickets for
this reinterpretation of mythological texts by Bertolt Brecht
and Chnsta Wolf are available at the Tresidder Ticket
Office or at the door.
And tonight through Saturday at 8 p.m. in Studio 205 in
Memonal Hall the Drama Department will present two
one-act comedies: "The Shadow of the Glen," by John
Millington Synge, and "Self Torture and Strenuous Exer
cise," by Harry Kondoleon. Admission is free.
Openly graphic
Starting this Sunday you can check out an exhibition of
graphic design by Barbara Mendelsohn at the Bechtel
International Center.
Mendelsohn, a senior graphic designer with the Stanford
Publication Services has contributed to the visual impact
of publicity and publications for many University depart
ments, including graphics for posters for several Jing Lyman
lecture senes.
The exhibit can be viewed whenever the I-Center is
open, and admission is free.
