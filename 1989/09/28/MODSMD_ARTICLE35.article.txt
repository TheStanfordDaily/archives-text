# One last look at summer
## 
### 
Popular demand has brought back "Jacques and His Master,"
the Drama Department's summer season smash, for two encore
performances tonight and tomorrow night at 8 p.m. in the Little
Theatre.
A tale of friendship, fate and sexual exploits, "Jacues and His
Master" was written by Milan Kundera, who also authored "The
Unbearable Lightness of Being."
"Jacques and His Master" is every bit as hot as a genuine Indian
Summer.
You can't beat this hot Indian Summer deal — admission is free
to students with valid ID.
And "Jacques and His Master" is but a prelude to the
department's fall season. The first featured performance this
quarter will be "The Seagull" by Anton Chekov.
Stanford's own Allen Blue, a fifth-year senior in the department,
designed the set of "The Seagull."
