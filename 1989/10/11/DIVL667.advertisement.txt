EQUAL JUSTICE UNDER THE LAW
We, the undersigned, oppose the prosecution of Louis Jackson. The manner in which Louis was singled out for his role in the May 15th protest at President Kennedy's
office is unfair and makes a mockery of the justice system. The facts speak for themselves - Louis is being singled out for selective punishment.
• While 55 students were arrested on the day of the protest, a warrent for Louis' arrest was issued 3 weeks after the protest, on the day before final exams.
• Louis is the only person who was present on the 15th to be charged with six charges: Obstructing Police Justice, Unlawful Assembly, Inciting to Riot, Battery,
Trespassing, and Refusal to Disperse. These charges carry a maximum penalty in excess of 2 years in jail.
• The police report says that several other students "continued to incite the crowd," but Louis is the only one who was arrested because the police "couldn't identify"
the other students.
Law enforcement officials in this countruy have a long and well-documented history of targetting, harassing, and attacking Black leaders - such as Martin L. King, Jr -
who challenged the status quo. Given the recent wave of racist violence in Bensonhurst, New York, Virginia Beach, and the UC Berkeley Alpha Phi Alpha fraternity party,
it is imperative that all concerned people take a stand against these attacks on African American people.
Whether intentional or not, the prosecution of Louis Jackson has ugly racial overtones, and we charge Stanford University, the Stanford Police Department, and the
Santa Clara County District office with contributing to a pattern of racist treatment of Black leaders. In the past, University Presidents - including President
Kennedy - have intervened in cases where there was a question of selective or racist treatment of Black Stanford sutdents at the hands of county law enforcement officials
(the 1971 Leo Bazile case and the 1985 Robby Perkins case). We, therefore, call on President Kennedy to make sure that all of the charges are dropped against Louis
Jackson. We further call on Stanford University to take active steps to create a campus climate that is free from racial harassment, and where all people are protected and
respected.

EQUAL JUSTICE UNDER THE LAW
We, the undersigned, oppose the prosecution of Louis Jackson. The manner in which Louis was singled out for his role in the May 15th protest at President Kennedy's
office is unfair and makes a mockery of the justice system. The facts speak for themselves - Louis is being singled out for selective punishment.
• While 55 students were arrested on the day of the protest, a warrent for Louis' arrest was issued 3 weeks after the protest, on the day before final exams.
• Louis is the only person who was present on the 15th to be charged with six charges: Obstructing Police Justice, Unlawful Assembly, Inciting to Riot, Battery,
Trespassing, and Refusal to Disperse. These charges carry a maximum penalty in excess of 2 years in jail.
• The police report says that several other students "continued to incite the crowd," but Louis is the only one who was arrested because the police "couldn't identify"
the other students.
Law enforcement officials in this countruy have a long and well-documented history of targetting, harassing, and attacking Black leaders - such as Martin L. King, Jr -
who challenged the status quo. Given the recent wave of racist violence in Bensonhurst, New York, Virginia Beach, and the UC Berkeley Alpha Phi Alpha fraternity party,
it is imperative that all concerned people take a stand against these attacks on African American people.
Whether intentional or not, the prosecution of Louis Jackson has ugly racial overtones, and we charge Stanford University, the Stanford Police Department, and the
Santa Clara County District office with contributing to a pattern of racist treatment of Black leaders. In the past, University Presidents - including President
Kennedy - have intervened in cases where there was a question of selective or racist treatment of Black Stanford sutdents at the hands of county law enforcement officials
(the 1971 Leo Bazile case and the 1985 Robby Perkins case). We, therefore, call on President Kennedy to make sure that all of the charges are dropped against Louis
Jackson. We further call on Stanford University to take active steps to create a campus climate that is free from racial harassment, and where all people are protected and
respected.

EQUAL JUSTICE UNDER THE LAW
We, the undersigned, oppose the prosecution of Louis Jackson. The manner in which Louis was singled out for his role in the May 15th protest at President Kennedy's
office is unfair and makes a mockery of the justice system. The facts speak for themselves - Louis is being singled out for selective punishment.
• While 55 students were arrested on the day of the protest, a warrent for Louis' arrest was issued 3 weeks after the protest, on the day before final exams.
• Louis is the only person who was present on the 15th to be charged with six charges: Obstructing Police Justice, Unlawful Assembly, Inciting to Riot, Battery,
Trespassing, and Refusal to Disperse. These charges carry a maximum penalty in excess of 2 years in jail.
• The police report says that several other students "continued to incite the crowd," but Louis is the only one who was arrested because the police "couldn't identify"
the other students.
Law enforcement officials in this countruy have a long and well-documented history of targetting, harassing, and attacking Black leaders - such as Martin L. King, Jr -
who challenged the status quo. Given the recent wave of racist violence in Bensonhurst, New York, Virginia Beach, and the UC Berkeley Alpha Phi Alpha fraternity party,
it is imperative that all concerned people take a stand against these attacks on African American people.
Whether intentional or not, the prosecution of Louis Jackson has ugly racial overtones, and we charge Stanford University, the Stanford Police Department, and the
Santa Clara County District office with contributing to a pattern of racist treatment of Black leaders. In the past, University Presidents - including President
Kennedy - have intervened in cases where there was a question of selective or racist treatment of Black Stanford sutdents at the hands of county law enforcement officials
(the 1971 Leo Bazile case and the 1985 Robby Perkins case). We, therefore, call on President Kennedy to make sure that all of the charges are dropped against Louis
Jackson. We further call on Stanford University to take active steps to create a campus climate that is free from racial harassment, and where all people are protected and
respected.

CONTINUING THE STRUGGLE
FOR A TRULY MULTICULTURAL UNIVERSITY
CONSCIENCE, COMMUNITY, and JUSTICE
:v, . ,
An appeal to the Stanford community to stand for justice for a fellow student, Louis Jackson, and to continue to work towards the
creation of a campus based on the principles of mutual respect and appreciation for people of all cultures.
EQUAL JUSTICE UNDER THE LAW
We, the undersigned, oppose the prosecution of Louis Jackson. The manner in which Louis was singled out for his role in the May 15th protest at President Kennedy's
office is unfair and makes a mockery of the justice system. The facts speak for themselves - Louis is being singled out for selective punishment.
• While 55 students were arrested on the day of the protest, a warrent for Louis' arrest was issued 3 weeks after the protest, on the day before final exams.
• Louis is the only person who was present on the 15th to be charged with six charges: Obstructing Police Justice, Unlawful Assembly, Inciting to Riot, Battery,
Trespassing, and Refusal to Disperse. These charges carry a maximum penalty in excess of 2 years in jail.
• The police report says that several other students "continued to incite the crowd," but Louis is the only one who was arrested because the police "couldn't identify"
the other students.
Law enforcement officials in this countruy have a long and well-documented history of targetting, harassing, and attacking Black leaders - such as Martin L. King, Jr -
who challenged the status quo. Given the recent wave of racist violence in Bensonhurst, New York, Virginia Beach, and the UC Berkeley Alpha Phi Alpha fraternity party,
it is imperative that all concerned people take a stand against these attacks on African American people.
Whether intentional or not, the prosecution of Louis Jackson has ugly racial overtones, and we charge Stanford University, the Stanford Police Department, and the
Santa Clara County District office with contributing to a pattern of racist treatment of Black leaders. In the past, University Presidents - including President
Kennedy - have intervened in cases where there was a question of selective or racist treatment of Black Stanford sutdents at the hands of county law enforcement officials
(the 1971 Leo Bazile case and the 1985 Robby Perkins case). We, therefore, call on President Kennedy to make sure that all of the charges are dropped against Louis
Jackson. We further call on Stanford University to take active steps to create a campus climate that is free from racial harassment, and where all people are protected and
respected.

EQUAL JUSTICE UNDER THE LAW
We, the undersigned, oppose the prosecution of Louis Jackson. The manner in which Louis was singled out for his role in the May 15th protest at President Kennedy's
office is unfair and makes a mockery of the justice system. The facts speak for themselves - Louis is being singled out for selective punishment.
• While 55 students were arrested on the day of the protest, a warrent for Louis' arrest was issued 3 weeks after the protest, on the day before final exams.
• Louis is the only person who was present on the 15th to be charged with six charges: Obstructing Police Justice, Unlawful Assembly, Inciting to Riot, Battery,
Trespassing, and Refusal to Disperse. These charges carry a maximum penalty in excess of 2 years in jail.
• The police report says that several other students "continued to incite the crowd," but Louis is the only one who was arrested because the police "couldn't identify"
the other students.
Law enforcement officials in this countruy have a long and well-documented history of targetting, harassing, and attacking Black leaders - such as Martin L. King, Jr -
who challenged the status quo. Given the recent wave of racist violence in Bensonhurst, New York, Virginia Beach, and the UC Berkeley Alpha Phi Alpha fraternity party,
it is imperative that all concerned people take a stand against these attacks on African American people.
Whether intentional or not, the prosecution of Louis Jackson has ugly racial overtones, and we charge Stanford University, the Stanford Police Department, and the
Santa Clara County District office with contributing to a pattern of racist treatment of Black leaders. In the past, University Presidents - including President
Kennedy - have intervened in cases where there was a question of selective or racist treatment of Black Stanford sutdents at the hands of county law enforcement officials
(the 1971 Leo Bazile case and the 1985 Robby Perkins case). We, therefore, call on President Kennedy to make sure that all of the charges are dropped against Louis
Jackson. We further call on Stanford University to take active steps to create a campus climate that is free from racial harassment, and where all people are protected and
respected.

CONTINUING THE STRUGGLE
FOR A TRULY MULTICULTURAL UNIVERSITY
CONSCIENCE, COMMUNITY, and JUSTICE
:v, . ,
An appeal to the Stanford community to stand for justice for a fellow student, Louis Jackson, and to continue to work towards the
creation of a campus based on the principles of mutual respect and appreciation for people of all cultures.
EQUAL JUSTICE UNDER THE LAW
We, the undersigned, oppose the prosecution of Louis Jackson. The manner in which Louis was singled out for his role in the May 15th protest at President Kennedy's
office is unfair and makes a mockery of the justice system. The facts speak for themselves - Louis is being singled out for selective punishment.
• While 55 students were arrested on the day of the protest, a warrent for Louis' arrest was issued 3 weeks after the protest, on the day before final exams.
• Louis is the only person who was present on the 15th to be charged with six charges: Obstructing Police Justice, Unlawful Assembly, Inciting to Riot, Battery,
Trespassing, and Refusal to Disperse. These charges carry a maximum penalty in excess of 2 years in jail.
• The police report says that several other students "continued to incite the crowd," but Louis is the only one who was arrested because the police "couldn't identify"
the other students.
Law enforcement officials in this countruy have a long and well-documented history of targetting, harassing, and attacking Black leaders - such as Martin L. King, Jr -
who challenged the status quo. Given the recent wave of racist violence in Bensonhurst, New York, Virginia Beach, and the UC Berkeley Alpha Phi Alpha fraternity party,
it is imperative that all concerned people take a stand against these attacks on African American people.
Whether intentional or not, the prosecution of Louis Jackson has ugly racial overtones, and we charge Stanford University, the Stanford Police Department, and the
Santa Clara County District office with contributing to a pattern of racist treatment of Black leaders. In the past, University Presidents - including President
Kennedy - have intervened in cases where there was a question of selective or racist treatment of Black Stanford sutdents at the hands of county law enforcement officials
(the 1971 Leo Bazile case and the 1985 Robby Perkins case). We, therefore, call on President Kennedy to make sure that all of the charges are dropped against Louis
Jackson. We further call on Stanford University to take active steps to create a campus climate that is free from racial harassment, and where all people are protected and
respected.
