Periodicals and Newspapers
Socrates provides bibliographic informa
tion about which periodicals are kept by Green,
Meyer, the Branch Libraries and most of the
Coordinate Libraries (but NOT about the indi
vidual articles). You should contact the appro
priate library to determine where they are
shelved. In Green, current issues of periodicals
are shelved in the Current Periodicals & Micro
texts Room as well as current and back issues
of newspapers (East Wing, Lower Level,
Michael Ballard, 725-1041, cn.cpm@forsythe).
The libraries of the Hoover Institution also
maintain a large collection of both domestic
and foreign newspapers. Current issues are
available in the Current Periodicals and
Newspaper Reading Room (Hoover Tower,
Room 15, Viveca Seymour, 723-2070).
Social Science
Machine-Readable Data Files
The machine-readable data files cover a
wide range of topics and time periods, and
include census records, election returns, public
opinion polls, and statistics of all types. They
have been acquired from the Inter-university
Consortium for Political and Social Research
(ICPSR), the Roper Center, other data archives,
and government agencies.
For details about requesting, locating, and
using these files, consult the document, "Aca
demic Data Service User's Guide," available
at Green and Jonsson Reference Desks or from
Academic Information Resources (AIR),
Sweet Hall, 3rd loor. For more information,
call Eddy Hogan, Green Library (725-1054,
cn.dat@forsythe) or Felix Limcaoco, AIR
(723-4952, fkl@jessica).

Media Collections
The Media Center for the University
Libraries (Meyer Library, First Floor, Michael
Miller, 725-1187, cn.mdm@forsythe) houses a
wide variety of media including films, video
tapes, sound recordings, audio tapes, and
slides. These materials are available for indi
vidual use and for instructional support to
classrooms. The Language Laboratory (Meyer
Library, First Floor, Renate Albrecht, 725-5523,
cn.rwa@forsythe) also has a variety of media
to support language learning.
Other valuable audiovisual materials can
be found at the Archive of Recorded Sound,
the Music Library, the Engineering Library, the
Mathematical & Computer Sciences Library,
Lane Library, the University Archives, the
Hoover Institution and Archives, and
Fleishmann Learning Resource Center at
the Medical Center.
Manuscripts
The Department of Special Collections
(West Wing, Green Library, 723-4054,
cn.spc@forsythe) houses important collections
of special materials like manuscripts and rare
books including the papers and letters of
Stanford faculty members and presidents.
Hoover Institution Archives (Herbert
Hoover Memorial Building, Room W)M,
723-3563) holds over 40 million historical
documents in 4,(XX) archival collections on
twentieth-century history, politics, and
international relations. Coverage is global in
scope with significant collections on Africa,
Asia, Europe, Latin America, the Middle East,
the Soviet Union, and the United States.
Maps
Branner Earth Sciences Library maintains a
large collection of maps from all over the world
including natural resource and natural hazards
maps and the topographical quadrangles of the

U.S. Geological Survey (725-1103, j.K. Herro,
cn.ear@forsythe).
The Central Map Collection (Green
Library, West Wing, 2nd Floor, Karyl Tonge,
723-4054) contains the general collection of
topographic maps, ethnolinguistic maps, street
maps, maps showing population distributions
and natural vegetation, aeronautical and
nautical charts, gazetteers of place names, and
cartographic reference works. Few maps are
represented in the catalogs, so it is wise to
consult with the map specialist.
Microforms
Green Library has the largest collection
of microforms in SUL, but most of the Branch
libraries have extensive collections in the sub
ject areas of their disciplines. In Green, the
microforms are housed in the Current Periodi
cals and Microtext Room (East Wing, lower
level, Michael Ballard, 725-1041, cn.cpm) and
the Jonsson Library of Government Documents
(723-2727, cn.gov). Equipment for reading and
printing microforms is available in the Micro
text Room and Jonsson Library; portable
readers are available for loan. The Hoover
Institution Library also has a substantial
microform collection.
Printed guides which provide detailed
access to the microform collections in Green
Library are available in the Microtexts Room
and the Jonsson Library. Check with your
Branch Library reference staff to discover what
microform collections and equipment they
have available.

Maps
Branner Earth Sciences Library maintains a
large collection of maps from all over the world
including natural resource and natural hazards
maps and the topographical quadrangles of the

U.S. Geological Survey (725-1103, j.K. Herro,
cn.ear@forsythe).
The Central Map Collection (Green
Library, West Wing, 2nd Floor, Karyl Tonge,
723-4054) contains the general collection of
topographic maps, ethnolinguistic maps, street
maps, maps showing population distributions
and natural vegetation, aeronautical and
nautical charts, gazetteers of place names, and
cartographic reference works. Few maps are
represented in the catalogs, so it is wise to
consult with the map specialist.
Microforms
Green Library has the largest collection
of microforms in SUL, but most of the Branch
libraries have extensive collections in the sub
ject areas of their disciplines. In Green, the
microforms are housed in the Current Periodi
cals and Microtext Room (East Wing, lower
level, Michael Ballard, 725-1041, cn.cpm) and
the Jonsson Library of Government Documents
(723-2727, cn.gov). Equipment for reading and
printing microforms is available in the Micro
text Room and Jonsson Library; portable
readers are available for loan. The Hoover
Institution Library also has a substantial
microform collection.
Printed guides which provide detailed
access to the microform collections in Green
Library are available in the Microtexts Room
and the Jonsson Library. Check with your
Branch Library reference staff to discover what
microform collections and equipment they
have available.


Periodicals and Newspapers
Socrates provides bibliographic informa
tion about which periodicals are kept by Green,
Meyer, the Branch Libraries and most of the
Coordinate Libraries (but NOT about the indi
vidual articles). You should contact the appro
priate library to determine where they are
shelved. In Green, current issues of periodicals
are shelved in the Current Periodicals & Micro
texts Room as well as current and back issues
of newspapers (East Wing, Lower Level,
Michael Ballard, 725-1041, cn.cpm@forsythe).
The libraries of the Hoover Institution also
maintain a large collection of both domestic
and foreign newspapers. Current issues are
available in the Current Periodicals and
Newspaper Reading Room (Hoover Tower,
Room 15, Viveca Seymour, 723-2070).
Social Science
Machine-Readable Data Files
The machine-readable data files cover a
wide range of topics and time periods, and
include census records, election returns, public
opinion polls, and statistics of all types. They
have been acquired from the Inter-university
Consortium for Political and Social Research
(ICPSR), the Roper Center, other data archives,
and government agencies.
For details about requesting, locating, and
using these files, consult the document, "Aca
demic Data Service User's Guide," available
at Green and Jonsson Reference Desks or from
Academic Information Resources (AIR),
Sweet Hall, 3rd loor. For more information,
call Eddy Hogan, Green Library (725-1054,
cn.dat@forsythe) or Felix Limcaoco, AIR
(723-4952, fkl@jessica).

Media Collections
The Media Center for the University
Libraries (Meyer Library, First Floor, Michael
Miller, 725-1187, cn.mdm@forsythe) houses a
wide variety of media including films, video
tapes, sound recordings, audio tapes, and
slides. These materials are available for indi
vidual use and for instructional support to
classrooms. The Language Laboratory (Meyer
Library, First Floor, Renate Albrecht, 725-5523,
cn.rwa@forsythe) also has a variety of media
to support language learning.
Other valuable audiovisual materials can
be found at the Archive of Recorded Sound,
the Music Library, the Engineering Library, the
Mathematical & Computer Sciences Library,
Lane Library, the University Archives, the
Hoover Institution and Archives, and
Fleishmann Learning Resource Center at
the Medical Center.
