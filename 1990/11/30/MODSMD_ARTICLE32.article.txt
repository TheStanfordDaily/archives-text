# The 						Walt Disney Company
## 
### 
The Walt Disney Company is looking for highly qualified Bachelor's Degree
candidates with excellent academic records, demonstrated quantitative abilities, and
outstanding leadership skills to fill positions in Strategic Planning, Treasury, Disney
Development Company, and Walt Disney Studios Business Planning. Each of these 2-3
year opportunities develops excellent business and analytical skills while providing
significant exposure to senior corporate and divisional executives within one of
America's most successful companies.
Interested candidates are invited to send their resumes, with cover letter and transcript,
to one or more of the four departments by January 16, 1991.

Strategic Planning
The Strategic Planning Department is responsible for
the identification, analysis, and development of
Disney's new strategic and financial initiatives.
Specific activities include acquisitions and joint
ventures, new business development, corporate
growth strategy, and brand management. The group's
activities and accomplishments have contributed to
the rapid growth of each of the operational divisions
of the Company: Attractions & Resorts, Studios,
Consumer Products, and Records. This unique
opportunity enables the Strategic Planning Analyst to
develop both the business experience gained at a
consulting firm and the M&A transaction experience
gained at an investment bank.
Contact: Melanie Ramberg
Strategic Planning Analyst
The Walt Disney Company
Team Disney Building 310J
500 South Buena Vista Street
Burbank, CA 91521
Disney Development Company
The Disney Development Company (DDC) is the
real estate development division, working with other
divisions to master plan Disney's extensive domestic
and overseas land holdings. DDC is directly
responsible for developing and managing the
construction of the hotel, retail, and residential
components of these projects. In addition, DDC
develops new corporate office and studio-related
facilities. The DDC team applies business,
architectural, planning, and construction skills to
major development opportunities. The Development
Analyst will have a significant role as a member of
this team and can expect to acquire a strong
understanding of the development process.
Contact: Marty Hartigan
Development Analyst
Disney Development Company
Olive Building
500 South Buena Vista Street
Burbank, CA 91521

Treasury
The Treasury Department is responsible for Disney's
corporate financial management. The department
develops, structures, and executes the Company's
corporate finance transactions. Specific activities
include financings at both corporate and project
specific levels, cash and investment management,
investor relations, and foreign exchange
management. Providing exposure to investment
banking transactions as well as to trading/investment
activities, the Treasury Analyst position develops
expertise in financial strategy and an understanding
of its role in general corporate management.
Contact: Andrew Sun
Treasury Analyst
The Walt Disney Company
Team Disney Building 301 C
500 South Buena Vista Street
Burbank, CA 91521
The Walt Disney Studios
Business Planning
A leader in the filmed entertainment industy, The
Walt Disney Studios produces and distributes motion
pictures and television product worldwide, and owns
and operates broadcast and cable television
properties. Recently, the Studio has experienced
unparalleled growth with such successes as Little
Mermaid, Pretty Woman, and The Golden Girls.
Business Planning works closely with senior
management to develop financial and business
strategies for the Studios' operating units, and plays a
central role in the Studios' annual operating and
strategic planning process. A Business Planner gains
thorough knowledge of the entertainment industry as
well as general business and management skills.
Contact: Rosemary Acquaviva
Business Planner
