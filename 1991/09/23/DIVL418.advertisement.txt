ME99 - Mechanical Dissection
How does a sewing machine create stitches?
How does a bike shift gears?
Drjvi Wheel
Feed-Doc
Needi.e -
Hook
9
Bobiiin
ME99 allows students to explore answers to these and
many other questions through a series of mechanical
dissection labs. A byproduct of this experience is greater
confidence in "hands-on" skills. Lab time will be spent
disassembling various artifacts. Assignments will include
formal and informal oral presentations and a final paper
concerning function and history.
Prerequisites: A keen sense of curiosity. No prior
dissection experience required.
ME99 — Mechanical Dissection. 3 units, Sheppard,
Autumn, MW 1:15-3:00, Building 530, Room 534.
Sign class list in 500-500 A on Sept. 23 or 24.
Instructor: Sheri Sheppard, ME Design Division, 525 Terman, 725-
1590, sheppard@sierra. Available for advising on this course on
Monday, September 23,1:00-4:30, Terman 503.
