THE FACULTY SEMINAR
ON PEACE STUDIES
announces a
Peace Studies Fellows Program
for the study of topics related to the
Creation and Maintenance of Peace
The Faculty Seminar on Peace Studies will select 6-8 graduate students
to be Peace Studies Fellows for one year beginning January 1, 1992.
Graduate Students who are interested in researching topics relevant to
the creation and maintenance of peace are eligible to apply. The Seminar
will award an honorarium of $500 for the year's appointment.
Each Fellow is expected (1) to participate in the biweekly Faculty
seminar, (2) to give a presentation to the seminar during the yeai; and (3)
submit a written report on his/her research. Fellows will also be invited to
meet with visiting scholars who will give special seminars at various times
throughout the year. Each Fellow will be paired with a faculty member
with whom he/she will collaborate.
For more information on how to apply, please call or write Byron
Bland, Center for International Security and Arms Control, 320 Galvez
House, Stanford, CA, 94305-6167; Phone: 415-723-3114.
Application deadline is December 1.
