# What's Up
## 
### 
Kulintang Arts, an Asian-American dance company current
ly teaching dance at Stanford, will perform Friday, Saturday and
Sunday in Oakland. The composition, "Cycles," combines Filipino
and American themes. Tickets are $12 in advance; phone (510)
535-0374.
World-famous lute player Hamza El-Din, who has come to
the Bay Area to play with the Grateful Dead, will perform at
Arroyo tonight at 9. Thank Res Ed for this one!
There's plenty of music on campus this weekend, including
combined chorus and orchestra performances Friday and
Saturday and a Stanford Jazz Ensemble concert Sunday. All
shows are at 8 p.m. at Dinkelspiel Auditorium; student tickets are
$4 through Tresidder Ticket Office or at the door.
The Order of Things in Motion, a series of original dance
pieces choreographed and performed by two Stanford undergrads,
will be shown Friday at 8 p.m. at Roble Dance Studio. Admission
is free.
