# Theater
## Whats 
### 
The Stanford University Museum of Art presents an exhibit of
works by Master of Fine Arts students. The show runs through
June 14 at the Stanford Art Gallery near Hoover Tower. Hours are
Tuesdays through Fridays from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m., Saturdays and
Sundays from 1 to 5 p.m. Admission is free.
Open-air performances of Caryl Churchill's comedy "Cloud 9"
will take place Thursday through Saturday, May 28-30, at the
"Hoop," that fountain-turned-sculpture in front of Green Library.
Shows are at 6 p.m.; admission is free.
The Stanford String Quartet will give its final concert of the sea
son this Sunday at 2:30 p.m. in Dinkelspiel Auditorium. The per
formance features a quartet by Darius Milhaud and also includes
works by Beethoven and Brahms. Student tickets are $5.
If you have time to get away this weekend, check out the San
Jose Greek Festival. The festival, which costs a mere dollar for stu
dents, features food, music and dancing courtesy of San Jose's
Greek-American community. This is a great way to experience
Greek (or Cretin, if you prefer) life without having to go through
Rush.
