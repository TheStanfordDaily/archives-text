The RAND
Graduate School
RGS
invites
applications for its doctoral degree program in
Public Policy Analysis. Deadline for academic
year 1993-1994 is February 1, 1993. An integral
part of RAND, RGS is fully accredited by the
Western Association of Schools and Colleges.
Curriculum consists of rigorous multidisciplinary
course work, including quantitative methods,
economics, and on-the-job training (OJT), lead
ing to the dissertation and award of the Ph.D. in
Public Policy Analysis. Subfields of specializa
tion include health policy, national security poli
cy, and Russian and Eurasian studies. Students
typically receive OJT support equivalent to doc
toral fellowships. Fellowships are also available
for applicants with a special interest in educa
tion. A master's degree, or equivalent post
bachelor's degree training and experience, is
required for admission.
A representative of The RAND Graduate School
will be at the Career Planning (Si Placement
Center on Monday, November 9, 1992.
