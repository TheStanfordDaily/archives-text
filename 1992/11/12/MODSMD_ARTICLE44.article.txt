# Soap operetta
## 
### 
It's not quite a soap opera, but it's close. It's an op
eretta.The Stanford Savoyards' production of Gilbert
and Sullivan's "The Mikado,"opens Friday at Dinkel
spiel Auditorium.
Set in the mythical town of Titipu, Japan, "The
Mikado" is a love triangle farce. Ko-Ko, the Lord High
Executioner of Titipu, loves one of his three wards,
Yum-Yum. But Nanki Poo, son of the Mikado (Em
peror of Japan), loves her as well.
"The Mikado" plays at Dinkelspiel Auditorium
Nov. 13,14, 20 and 21 at Bp. m., with a 2:30 p.m. mati
nee performance Sunday, Nov. 15. Tickets ($ 10 for the
general public and $6.50 for students) are available
through Tresidder Ticket Office.
