Analyst Position
Trammel Crow Company will be recruiting for
the position of Analyst. This entry level posi
tion offers an intensive, hands-on experience in
the commercial real estate industry with the
leading firm in the field. The Analyst develops
financial models, completes special projects,
reviews due diligence materials, and interacts
with all levels of the organization.
Qualified candidates will have strong quantita
tive ability, good communication skills, and a
strong work ethic. For further information, see
the job posting at the CPPC.
Send resume and cover letter to:
Stephen M. Moyski
Trammel Crow Company
7995 E. Prentice Avenue — Suite 300
Greenwood Village, CO 80111
Qualified candidates will be contacted to schedule an off-campus
interview in late January 1993.
THIS YEAR A LOT OF COLLEGE
SENIORS WILL BE GRADUATING
INTO DEBT.
Under the Army's
Loan Repayment
program, you could get
out from under with a
three-year enlistment.
Each year you serve
on active duty reduces
your indebtedness by one
third or $1,500, which
ever amount is greater,
up to a $55,000 limit.
The offer applies to Perkins Loans, Stafford Loans,
and certain other federally insured loans, which are not
in default.
And debt relief is just one of the many benefits
you'll earn from the Army. Ask your Army Recruiter.
Call 1 -800-USA-ARMY

South African Teaching Fellowship
for Stanford Graduate Students
Sponsored by the Provost's Committee on
Faculty Initiatives on South Africa
Funding has been allocated for a new program to send two Stanford graduate
students to teach at a traditionally black South African university for an academic
year, beginning as early as July 1993. Interested students will be invited to submit
applications in a university-wide competition. The fellowship committee encourages
students from all Stanford schools (Business, Earth Sciences, Education,
Engineering, Humanities & Sciences, Law, Medicine)
to apply.

January 14, 1993
Where: Bechtel International
Center Assembly Room

Stanford University; 723-0295.
