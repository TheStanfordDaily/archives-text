This weekend,
The Warfield
presents
back-to-back
nights of modern
rock hoe-downs with
Ned's Atomic Dust
bin, Goo-Goo Dolls,
Supreme Love Gods
and Flowerhead to
morrow night, as well
as Suzanne Vega with
Kitchens of Distinc
tion on Saturday.
Ned's Atomic
Dustbin's latest
album, "Are You Nor-

mal?" and Suzanne
Vega's "99.9 F n both hold top-10 positions
on the college charts, and with opening acts
such as these, well, what can we say?
The Ned's show starts at 8 p.m., with the
doors opening at 7 p.m., and tickets are
$15.50. Suzanne Vega and Kitchens of Dis
tinction tickets are $19.50 for the main
floor, $18.50 balcony, with the doors open
ing at 7 p.m. for an 8 o'clock show. Call the
Warfield 775-7722 for more info.
■ The player...
You've got to know when to hold 'em,
know when to fold 'em, know when to walk
away, know when to run. You better take
your honey, to go see Kenny Rogers. What
better thing to do now, for Valentine's Day?
Yes, friends, the master of lusciousness
himself Kenny Rogers will be performing
three shows this weekend at the Circle Star
Center. Saturday at 7:30 p.m. and Sunday at
3:30 p.m. and 7:30 p.m., Kenny will be per
forming with Martina Mcßride. Tickets are
available at the Circle Star Box Office, 366-
7100, or at BASS.
■ Hit it maestro...
Baritone William Ramsey, accompanied
by pianist Stephen Sano, will be presenting
a recital this Saturday at 8 p.m. in Dinkel
spiel Auditorium.
Ramsey, the director of choral activities at
Stanford, will be performing songs by Schu
bert, Dowland, Brahms and others.Tickets
for the performance are $7 for general ad
mission, $4 for students and are available at
the Tresidder Ticket Office or at the door.
For more info, call the Music Department at
(415)723-3811.
—Joshua Walker
