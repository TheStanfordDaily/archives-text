# Women's tennis blows by Arizona competition
## Wildcats fall 7-2, Sun Devils follow suit 5-1 
### Mike McDonough Senior staff writer 
The Valley of the Sun it
wasn't.
Nevertheless, the Stanford
women's tennis team sloshed
their way through Arizona
last weekend to defeat two of
its Pacific 10 Conference
rivals.
On Saturday, the Cardinal
handed No. 12 Arizona (5-3, 1-3
Pac-10) a 7-2 loss, then com
pleted a rain-delayed dual
match yesterday against No. 8
Arizona State, downing the Sun
Devils 5-1.
Stanford's three individual
match losses this weekend were
its first of the season.
Due to heavy rain in Tempe,
Ariz, on Friday, the Cardinal (9-
0, 4-0 Pac-10) saw its first action
of the weekend on Saturday in
Tucson, Ariz, against the Wild
cats.
In the No. 1 match, senior
Laxmi Poruri crushed Arizona's
Alix Creek by a 6-2, 6-0 score,
while No. 2 Heather Willens
breezed by Michelle Oldham, 6-
3, 6-0.
Sophomore Kim Shasby man
aged to get by Celine Verdier in
the No. 3 match, 6-4, 7-6, but
No. 4 Kristine Kurth was not so
lucky, falling to the Cats' Miko
Hemsi, 6-3, 6-7, 6-1.
Kurth returned to play after
being sidelined last week with a
lung infection.
In the No. 5 match, freshman
Beth Berris defeated Angela
Bernal, 6-4, 6-0, while No. 6
sophomore Amy Chiminello
topped Arizona's Allison Grace,
6-2, 6-1.
In doubles action, Stan
ford's Kurth and Berris played

in the No. 1 spot for Poruri
and Willens, overcoming the
Wildcats' Creek and Oldham,
7-5, 6-3.
The No. 2 tandem of
Chiminello and Shasby sur
vived a three-set scare against
Hemsi and Verdier, pulling out
a 6-4, 5-7, 7-6 victory.
Stanford's No. 3 team of
Wendy Sacks and Emma Wood
house, however, lost in
straight sets to Bernal and
Grace, 7-5, 6-3.
The Cardinal returned to
Tempe on Sunday to complete
its rain-delayed dual match
against the Sun Devils.
No. 1 Poruri led the charge
against ASU with a 6-4, 6-0
victory over Joelle Schad, but
Willens dropped a three-set
nailbiter against Kori David
son in Willens' first dual
match loss of the year, 7-5, 4-
6, 7-5.
No, 3 Shasby defeated Paige
Bartelt by a 6-3, 6-2 score, and
No. 4 Kurth rebounded from
Saturday's loss with a 6-4, 6-2
win over the Sun Devils' Kara
Schertzer.
Berris wrapped up the No. 5
match with a 6-3, 6-1 victory
over Meredith Geiger, 6-3, 6-1,
and No. 6 Chiminello rounded
out the dual match victory for
Stanford, beating Julie Cop
pinger 7-5, 6-1.
Due to the rain delay, Stan
ford and Arizona State cancelled
the doubles portion of the com
petition.
The Cardinal travels to Wis
consin this Thursday to compete
in the USTA/ITA Women's
National Indoor Intercollegiate
Team Championship, where it
will meet Virginia in the first
round.
