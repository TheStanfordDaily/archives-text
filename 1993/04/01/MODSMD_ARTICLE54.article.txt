# out 'n about
## A 						fitting tribute 
### 
Commemorating the 25th anniversary of the assas
sination of Martin Luther King, Jr., this weekend a
new play highlighting the famed civil-rights leader
will be coming to campus.
Written by Stanford's own Clayborn Carson, the
multi-media event entitled "Passages of Martin
Luther King" includes rare footage of the King family
and King's dealings with Malcolm X., John F. Kennedy
and Stokely Carmichael.
The play will be performed on Friday and Saturday,
April 2 and 3 as well as April 9 and 10, at 8 p.m. in
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. "Passages" is presented by
the Martin Luther King, Jr., Papers Project, the Stan
ford Department of Drama and the Committee on
Black Performing Arts.
Tickets for "Passages" are $12 general admission, $10
for Stanford faculty and staff and $5 for students, and are
available at the Tresidder Ticket Office, (415) 725-APTS.
