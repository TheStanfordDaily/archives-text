Stanford
Drama De
partment pre
sents two senior pro
jects: "Invisiblemen"
and "Streetcar: Rod
ney King and the
L.A. Insurrection" in
the Eleanor Prosser
Studio upstairs in
Memorial Hall.
"Invisiblemen," a
collection of mono
logues portraying
eight young urban
black men, is a one
man show written

and performed by
Gregory Cass. "Streetcar," written by Donald
Klein and Clark Jackson, is an 80-minute
multimedia adaptation which juxtaposes the
plot of Tennesee Williams' "A Streetcar
Named Desire" with events spanning from
the Rodney King beating to the trial and sub
sequent riots.
The double bill will be presented Thurs
day through Saturday evenings at 8 p.m,
April 29 through May 1. Admission for all
shows is $3 at the door only
■ Spring Choral Concert
On May 6, expand your Thursday-night
horizons by attending a Spring choral con
cert, featuring the Stanford University Singers.
Performing works by Corigliano and Rut
ter, two brilliant 20th-century composers,
the Singers will be directed by Amy Lieber
man and Stephan Sano. Tickets cost $4 for
students, $7 general, and are available at the
door or through the Tresidder Ticket Office.
The show starts at 8 p.m. and will be per
formed in beautiful Memorial Church.
■ Savoyards Performance
Mark tomorrow evening, Saturday
evening or a Sunday matinee on your calen
dar. This weekend, the Stanford Savoyards
will perform their spring show, "H.M.S.
Pinafore," a Gilbert and Sullivan classic.
There's also a Sing-Along of favorite G&S
songs Sunday in Tresidder at 12:30 p.m. The
show starts at 8 p.m. Friday and Saturday,
and 2:30 p.m. Sunday.
— Alex Wiesen
