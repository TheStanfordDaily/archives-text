The RAND Graduate School (RGS)
RGS invites applications for its doctoral degree program in Public
Policy Analysis. The deadline for academic year 1994-1995 is
February 1, 1994. An integral part of RAND, RGS is fully accredited by
the Western Association of Schools and Colleges. Curriculum consists
of rigorous multidisciplinary coursework, including quantitative
methods, economics, social sciences, technology and policy
workshops, and on-the-iob training (OJT), leadina to the dissertation
and award of the Ph.D. in Public Policy Analysis. Subfields of
specialization include health policy, national security policy, and
Russian and Eurasian studies. Students typically receive OJT support
equivalent to doctoral fellowships. Fellowships are also available for
applicants with a special interest in education and health policy. A
master's degree or equivalent post-bachelor's degree training and
experience is required for admission.
A representative of The RAND Graduate School will be at the Career
Planning and Placement Center on Wednesday, November 10, 1993.
For additional information, send for bulletin:
The RAND Graduate School
P.O. Box 2138
Santa Monica, CA 90407-2138
or telephone: (310) 393-0411, ext. 7690
