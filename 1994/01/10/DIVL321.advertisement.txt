# Adv. 11 Page 5
##

The Stanford Center on Conflict and Negotiation Presents the
INTERDISCIPLINARY SEMINAR ON
CONFLICT AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION
Winter 1994
Econ. 386/ Law 325/ O.R. 366/ Psych. 283*
All sessions meet from 4:10 p.m. to 5:30 p.m., on Tuesdays.
Seminar meetings will be held in Room 180 of the Law School.
January 11: Professor Richard Thaler (Cornell University)
—Fairness and Economics
January 18: Professor Jennifer F3rown (Univ. of Santa Clara Law School)
Professor lan Ayres (Stanford Law School) — discussion leader
—Economic Rationales for Mediation
January 25: Professor Susan Okin (Stanford University)
—Justice, Gender and Power in Families
February 1: Professor Amos Tversky (Stanford University)
—Topic to be announced
February 8: Professor Robert Gertner (University of Chicago)
—Settlement Escrows
February 15: Professor Roger Noll (Stanford University)
—Contract Negotiations under Free Agency in Professional Sports
February 22: Galina Starovoitova (U.S. Institute of Peace)
Leila Alieva (Harvard Center for Middle Eastern Studies)
—Ethnic Disputes in the Former Soviet Union
March 1: Professor Lee Ross (Stanford University)
—Psychological Barriers to Conflict Resolution
March 8: Daniel Magraw, Esq. (Environmental Protection Agency)
—Issues of Negotiation in International Environmental Law
All sessions are open to the public.
This schedule subject to change.
For more information, contact SCCN Deputy Director Melanie Greenberg (723-2574).
'Graduate students may elect a one credit (pass/fail) option, or a two credit option
for which a research paper is required. Law students must take the two credit option.
