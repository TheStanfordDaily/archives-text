NEW COURSE I
The Political Economy of Integration
In the Americas
(Economics 135/392)
Open to Advanced Undergraduates and Graduate Students
in Political Science, Public Policy; and Economics
This new course will address issues of bargaining and power
relations in the integration of asymmetrical economics and political
systems. The approaches drawn from the expertise of faculty at
Stanford (and visitors) are to be applied to issues in the Americas,
including NAFTA and the pros and cons of a Western Hemisphere
Free Trade Area. Guest lecturers will include Judith Goldstein,
Stephen D. Krasner, Paul Krugman, Roger Noll, Clark W. Reynolds,
Julie Schaffner, and others.
First meeting on Thursday, March 31 in FRII
(basement of Food Research Institute) at 3:15 p.m.
