# Men shut out at Pac-10s
## Singles, doubles bounced in semis 
### Mike Langford Senior staff writer 
The Stanford men's tennis team finished off an
impressive weekend among extremely difficult com
petition at the Pacific 10 Conference Individual Ten
nis Championships at Ojai, Calif., last weekend.
The Cardinal's top two players, No. 3-ranked
senior Michael Flanagan and No. 51-ranked sopho
more Jeff Salzenstein, made it all the way to the
semifinals of the singles draw. However, Flanagan
lost to USC's No. 2-ranked Wayne Black, 7-6, 7-6,
and Salzenstein fell to Arizona State's No. 4-ranked
Sargis Sargisian by a score of 6-1, 3-6, 0-6.
To reach the semis, Flanagan defeated USC's
Adam Peterson by default. Also in the quarterfinals,
Salzenstein beat Stanford teammate senior Chris
Cocotos by a score of 3-6, 6-0, 6-3.
Cardinal senior Robert Devens lost to Sargisian in
the second round, while sophomore Jim Thomas lost
in the first round to California's Brian Eagle.
Stanford senior Billy Wright made it all the way to
the finals of the Open Invitational, losing to UCLA's
Eric Lin by a score of 3-6, 7-6, 2-6.
