COMMUNITY SERVICE FELLOWSHIPS
THROUGH THE HAAS CENTER
FOR PUBLIC SERVICE
COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT FELLOWSHIP PROGRAM
This fellowship provides students with the experience and support necessary to
affect social change at the community level. A small number of undergraduates
spend their summer in internships with community development
organizations followed by academic year activities with local
grassroots community organizations.
Open to all sophomores, juniors, and co-terminal students returning to
campus in the fall.
$2,000 stipend
Deadline: February 28
Contact Sue Gray at 725-7408 for more information.
ECHOING GREEN PUBLIC SERVICE FELLOWSHIP FOR GRADUATING
SENIORS
Provides graduating seniors with the opportunity to develop and implement an
innovative community servicc project.
One-year seed grant of up to $15, 000
Deadline: February 6
Contact Sue Gray at 725-7408 for more information.
JOHN GARDNER PUBLIC SERVICE FELLOWSHIP PROGRAM
Provides graduating seniors at Stanford and UC Berkeley with the opportunity to
work with a mentor in a government or non-profit agency.
$15,000 stipend for 11 months plus appropriate travel expenses
Deadline: February 13
Information meeting: January 23, 4:30 pm., Haas Center
Contact Jeanne Wahl Halleck at 725-2870 for more information.
DONALD KENNEDY PUBLIC SERVICE SUMMER FELLOWSHIP PROGRAM
Provides funding to students who develop innovative, collaborative community
scrvice projects that address needs locally, nationally or internationally.
Open to all undergraduate and graduate students.
$2,000+ grant plus financial aid for eligible students
Deadline: April 5
Contact Sue Gray at 725-7408 for more information
STANFORD IN GOVERNMENT (SIG) FELLOWSHIPS
Summer fellowships in Washington, D.C., Sacramento, and state government are
available in the areas of education, housing, the environment, health policy,
international development and other topics.
Open to all freshmen, sophomores, juniors, and co-terminal students returning to
campus this fall. International fellowships arc available to undergraduate and
graduate students
$1,250-$ 1,500 stipend
Deadline: February 24
Contact the SIG office at 723-4151 for more information.
For more Information about these fellowships, stop by the Haas Center for Public Service.
