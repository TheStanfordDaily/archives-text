The Stanford Directory is now accepting applications
for the positions of
Project Director and
Business Manager
The Stanford Directory is Stanford's official phone book and resource guide - you'll
find one on every desk on campus. I lie Directory is a special business project owned and operat
ed by the Associated Students of Stanford University. I he project's primary goal is to produce a
publication that maintains a high level of accuracy, organization and design. Expenses are to be
met through advertising sales and an annual contribution from the University. Surplus revenues
are used to benefit other student publications and activities.
As Stanford's most widely used resource guide (circulation 32,000), the Stanford
Directory contains over 8f>() pages of campus information, maps, listings, advertising, and
coupons. In addition, our fledgling web site, the Stanford Virtual Directory (http://www-directo
ry.stanford.edu/), provides on-line information resources and has already been featured as a hot
pick by Netscape, Yahoo & the San Jose Mercur\ Center.
These positions offer an excellent opportunity to gain valuable managerial, organiza
tional, design and business skills and to gain "real world" experience in publishing, marketing
and information technologies. Here is your opportunity to leave your mark at Stanford: a copy of
your Directory will be found on every desk on campus.
JOB DESCRIPTIONS:
The PROJECT DIRECTOR heads the Directory's 15-person staff and is ulti
mately responsible for the project's success. He or she establishes long-range and short
term goals for the staff and ensures that the project progresses according to
schedule. Extensive experience in publishing and knowledge of page-layout design
(Quark XPress & Adobe Photoshop) are strongly recommended though not required.
The BUSINESS MANAGER oversees all financial aspects of the project and
shares responsibility with the project director for the bottom line success of the project.
Along with the Project Director, the Business Manager manages the Directory's nine
person sales staff and participates in all hiring decisions. Previous sales and managerial
experience is strongly recommended though not required.
QUALIFICATIONS:
Both the Project Director and the Business Manager need impeccable organiza
tional skills and an excellent eye for detail. They must also possess the ability to see the
"big picture;" outstanding communication and interpersonal skills; the ability to resolve
conflicts and crises quickly; an enthusiastic, friendly personality; and the ability to work
well under stress.
Both the Project Director and the Business Manager must be a Stanford
undergraduate during the 1995-96 school year; thus graduating seniors are eligible
(and encouraged) to apply.
Applications are now available in the ASSU office on the second floor of
Tie si tide r Union and are due Friday, February 16. For more info, email
directory@virtual.
