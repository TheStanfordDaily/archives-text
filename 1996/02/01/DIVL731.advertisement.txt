Black Community Services Center and
The Real News are proud to present:
1996 tflack Liberation Month
Poetryltssay Writing Contest
This year's theme for BLM is:
"DIVERSITY IN OUR
COMMUNITY: WEAVING A
FABRIC OF UNITY"
All interested poets and writers are welcome to com
pete for scholarship prizes by submitting work which
embodies the theme of BI.M. Personal essays and
poetry are limited to I,(XX) words. Contestants must submit entries by 5:(X) pm
on February 12,1996 on a Libeled diskette along with a hard copy to The Real
News drop box at the Black Community Services Center.
The three winners will be awarded monetary prizes of $150, $100, and $50
respectively, and will be announced at the Black Student Union's Soul Food
Dinner to be held on February 23,19%. In addition, all winning and honorable
mention entries will be published in Stanford's black community newspaper
The Real News. All currently registered students are welcome to enter.
One entry per person. For more information contact Johnathon Briggs
or Sophia Whyte at: 723-1587.
