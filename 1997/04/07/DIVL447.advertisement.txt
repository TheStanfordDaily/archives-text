1997 Rebele Journalism
Internships
Work at a weekly newspaper in California and earn a reporter's salary! Students can write and report
the news, or work in advertising, circulation, or production. Internships generally last about 12 weeks.
Here arc some comments from past interns:
Intern for the Pacifica Tribune
"The editor let me try a little of everything, including news, features, sports, and even a person
al column. The staff at the Tribune made me feel welcome immediately, and from them I learned
a lot about community journalism. "
Intern for the Forum Community News
"My internship gave me my first real chance to see what it is like to be a journalist. My intern
ship was an invaluable educational experience, regardless of whether or not / choose a career
in journalism. "
Intern for the Sonoma Index-Tribune
"I've done features, covered two fatal car crashes, and interviewed local officials on a wide
range of subjects. There's nothing like the experience of working at a community-oriented
weekly newspaper, and I love it...l've been hired as a reporter. "
For more information, contact either Marion Lewenstein, Professor of Communication at 723-0703 or
Stephanie Young. Internship Coordinator at 725-2239.
Deadline: April 11
