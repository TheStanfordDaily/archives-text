PHI BETA KAPPA
ACHING FEU,
The Stanford Chapter of Phi Beta Kappa is currently soliciting nominations for this year's Phi Beta Kappa
Teaching Prize. The Prize, which carries a substantial cash award, recognizes excellence in teaching as well as
effectiveness in eliciting high student achievement in liberal arts and sciences scholarship. The Prize is
awarded during the annual spring initiation ceremony which will be held this year on Friday, June 13 from
7:30-9:00 p.m.
Previous winners are:
1991 - Professor David Brady, Political Science
1992 - Professor Jodie Maxmin,. Art
1993 - Professor Robert Waymouth, Chemistry
1994 - Professor Donald Kennedy, Biological Sciences and Human Biology
1996 - Professor Robert McGinn, Industrial Engineering and Engineering Management
Nominations of tenured, tenure-track, or non-tenure-track instructors or professors are invited from
undergraduate members of Phi Beta Kappa. Students should write their nomination in the form of a short
letter describing why the instructor is particularly deserving. Letters should be sent by April 18, 1997 to: PBK
Teaching Prize Committee, 119 Birch/Oak, Jordan Quad, Stanford, CA 94305-4128; or via electronic mail to
jmarcus@leland.stanford.edu.
For more information, please contact Dr. Jane Marcus, PBK Secretary, at the above email address or by phone
at 723-1659.
