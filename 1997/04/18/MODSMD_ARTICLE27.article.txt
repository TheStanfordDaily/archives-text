Earth week activity
The environmental representatives are co
sponsoring, with Herstory, a talk on the interna
tional society for ecology and culture, by Helena
Norberg-Hodge. At 7 p.m. in the Branner
Lounge.
Applications for URO grants
Undergraduates are invited to apply for Golden
Grants( Humanities and Creative Arts) and
small grants in all fields by 5 p.m. Forms and in
formation at 122 Sweet Hall or call 723-3828.

Festival and democracy: Athenian Pana
thenaea
Lecture given by W. Robert Connor, director,
National Humanities Center Research, Triangle
Park, North Carolina. At 3:30 p.m. in the Hu
manities Center Annex, 579 Alvarado Row.
Matthew Cavicke, baritone
A senior recital: "Songs of Love and War."
Works by Schubert, Schumann, Handel, Mozart
and Britten. At 8 p.m. in Campbell Recital Hall.
Herstory opening ceremonies
Student performance groups, including Jam
Pac'd, will provide entertainment for this intro
duction to two weeks of Herstory events. At
noon in White Plaza. Then at 4 p.m. in the
Woman's Center a forum will be held dis
cussing: "Is a painting of a nude woman sexist?"
What if it is painted by a woman?" a relaxed dis

cussion about many issues of woman and
image in contemporary culture.

Earth week festival

live-music, organic food, eco-vendor fest to fi
nalize our week of Earth Day celebrations.

Discussions of careers in film
