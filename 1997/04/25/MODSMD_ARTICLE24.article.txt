# Men succeed In Ojai
## 
### Jeremy Stone Editorial staff 
The men's tennis squad fared
well in the opening day of the
Pacific 10 Conference Champi
onships in Ojai, Calif. Three of
four entrants in the singles draw
advanced to today's quarterfinals.
Second-seeded Ryan Wolters
advanced past Arizona's Jerome
Oliveri 7-5, 6-1, and Gustavo Mar
culato of Arizona State 6-3, 6-2 in
the second round. The sophomore
faces UCLA's Alex Ducret today.
Freshmen Bob and Mike Bryan
also advanced. Bob Bryan dropped
just four games all day, crushing

California's David Sutton 6-1, 6-2
and Washington's Nick Williams 6-
0, 6-1. He will next go up against
USC's Patrick Gottesleben.
Mike Bryan won by 6-4, 6-3
scores against Washington's Sasha
liampazzo in the first round and
Arizona State's Sergio Elias in the
second. Top-seeded Eric Taino of
UCLA will battle him next.
Senior captain Grant Elliott fell
to UCliA's Kevin Kim 6-3, 6-1 in
the second round. Elliott defeated
Wildcat Henrik Wagner 7-6, 6-0 in
opening-round play.
In the invitational portion of
the tourney, freshman Geoff
Abrams received a first-round bye
and got by Husky Chad Raymond
6-1, 6-3 in the second. He awaits
Nick Quatrochi today.
Junior Charles Hoeveler got by
Oregon's Miguel Arriaga 6-2, 6-3
in the opening rouna and followed
that with a 6-3, 7-6 triumph over
Cal's Adam Gross. USC's Akram
Zaman will duel Hoeveler today.
Junior Misha Palecek was elim
inated yesterday in the second
round by Cal's Ming Le 6-3, 7-6
after an easy win over Washing
ton's Martin Gamling 6-2, 6-1
