# Track excels at Invite
## 
### Becky Unruh Contributing writer 
The Cardinal track and field team had
some impressive performances over the
weekend. On Friday, Stanford hosted the
Cardinal Invitational, a meet open to
both collegiate and postcollegiate ath
letes. Saturday, a few Cardinal athletes
traveled to Modesto to compete at the
Modesto relays.
There were several notables compet
ing at the Cardinal invite, which helped
bolster the competition. Dan O'Brien, an
Olympic gold medalist and world-record
holder in the decathlon won the 110-
meter high hurdles. PattiSue Plumer,
Class of '85 and a three-time Olympian,
won the women's 1,500 meters in 4 min
utes, 19.43 seconds. Chris Huffins, a
decathlete with his sights set on the 2000
Olympics, won the 200 in 21.58 seconds.
The winner of the men's discus is
someone known quite well to the Stan
ford throwers. Cardinal assistant coach
Robert Weir won the event with a throw
of 190» feet, 8 inches. Junior Justin
Strand followed his coach's example,
winning the hammer throw with a toss of
222-8.
Junior Jeff Allen recorded another
first in the 400-meter hurdles with a time
of 51.54, just one-hundredth of a second
slower than his season best set against
California at Big Meet.
Freshman Toby Stevenson won the
men's pole vault competition. En route to
his first-place finish, he set a new school
record in the event, leaping 17-8 3/4. The
old mark of 17-8 1/2 which Stevenson
had matched earlier in the season was set

The women's team put in sev

Junior Sarna Renfro won the
3,000 meters with an unofficial
time of 9:29.8. Junior Kortney
Dunscombe won the 5,000
meters with a time of 16:52.03.
Junior Sara Moore finished
third in the 1,500 meters just a
second behind Plumer, crossing
the line in 4:20.81. Moore
improved her season best by near
ly 3 seconds and narrowly missed
the NCAA qualifying mark of
4:19.5. Moore did meet the NCAA
provisional qualifying standard.
Junior Heather Miller won
the 200 meters for the Cardinal
in a time of 24.71. Fellow junior
Bernadette Armand finished
third in the 100-meter hurdles,
crossing the line in 14.03.
Freshmen Summer Pierson
and Maggie Isaac finished 2-3 in
the discus with throws of 168-1
and 157-3 feet, respectively.
Pierson improved her previous
season best by almost 2 feet.
Freshman Cassie Romuld fin
ished second in the shot put

with a throw of 45-7 1/4. Fellow
freshman Allison Beatty added a
third-place finish in the javelin
with a throw of 141-2.
Sophomore Tracye Lawyer
won the high jump with a leap of
5-10. Freshman Christina Batas
tini placed second with a 17-foot
leap in the long jump, and junior
Rhonda Witmer finished third in
the triple jump .leaping 36-9 3/4.
At Modesto, Allen finished
first in the Section 1 preliminar
ies in the 400-meter hurdles
with a time of 52.73.
Junior Jeff McCarty finished
10th in the Section 1 discus pre
liminaries, tossing the disc 155-
8.
Section 1 preliminary results
show Romuld and Isaac finished
eighth and ninth in the women's
shot put with puts of 44-11 and
42-10 1/4, respectively.
The Cardinal is gearing up for
the Pacific 10 Conference Champi
onships, which begin Saturday
with the Pac-10 Decathlon / Hep
tathlon Championships. Pac-lOs
will continue May 24-25 in Seattle.
