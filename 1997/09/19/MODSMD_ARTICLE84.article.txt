Books
Mon *22
When he was 12 years old, author Danny Seo
founded Earth 2000, a national environmen
tal group with more than 20,000 members.
He will read from his book "Generation React:
Activism for Beginners," which covers how to
start an activist group, how to organize a
protest and how to host other world-changing
activities. Admission is free. Printer's Inc. Book
store, 310 California St., 8 p.m. (650) 327-
6500.

Film
Wed *24
The French Cin6-Club of Palo Alto will show
the 1977 "Pardon Mon Affair," in which four
friends undergo a series of romantic antics.
This 102 minute French-language film with
English subtitles was directed by Yves Robert
and is rated PG. Admission is $6 Cultural
Center Auditorium, 1313 Newell Road, 8 p.m.
(650)325-3831 or (650) 329-2366.

Drama
Wed • 24, Thurs • 25, Sat • 27
The Stanford Gilbert and Sullivan Company,
also known as the Savoyards, will hold open
auditions for its fall production of "H.M.S.
Pinafore," in Braun Rehearsal Auditorium.
Bring a prepared song in English to the audi
tions. 7 to 9:30 p.m. on the 24 and 25,
10 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. on the 27.

Music
Wed-24
The L.A. post-punk rockers Goldfinger play at
the Edge, 260 California Ave. in Palo Alto
Tickets are $10 in advance, $12 at the door
Doors open at 8 p.m., show starts at 9 p m
(650) 324-3343.
