NEW COURSE
Genetic Testing and Alzheimer Disease:
Has the Time Come?
One Unit S/NC Med 253
Advances in genetics offer the hope for more effective diagnosis, treatment and identification of
those at risk For developing Alzheimer disease. At the same time, each new discovery raises complex
ethical, legal, and social questions.
Please join us for this special symposium, when leaders in the fields of medicine, genetics,
neuroscience, law, psychiatry, humanities, ethics, decision analysis, biotechnology, and insurance
review the issues, with special attention to the recommendations developed by the Working Group
of the Stanford Program in Genomics, Ethics, and Society.
Saturday, October 25, 1997, 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m., Kresge Auditorium. Please consult our Web
site for details, http://www-leland.stanford.edu/dept/scbe/adconf.htm
The Stanford Program in Genomics, Ethics and Society gratefully acknowledges unrestricted funding for this symposium by
the SmithKJine Beecham Corporation.
