Music
Fri • 17
A Brahms Celebration will be held by the
Stanford String Quartet at Dinkelspiel Au
ditorium. Tickets are available at Tresidder
ticket office or the door — $12 general,
$ 10 Music Guild members, $6 students,
free for Stanford students. 8 p.m., with a
pre-concert lecture at 7:15 p.m.
Tues • 21
Virtuoso American organist Marilyn Keiser

will perform works of J.S. Bach, Mozart,
Mendelssohn and others at Memorial
Church on campus. Tickets are available
at Tresidder ticket office or the door — $8
general, $4 students, free for Stanford
students. 8 p.m.
Wed • 22
Do you sing, play, write, read or dance?
Then get to the open microphone night at
the Coffee House and share your passion.
9:30 p.m.

Books
Sun*l9
The legendary Gus Vant Sant — director
of "Drugstore Cowboy" and "My Own
Private Idaho" among others — will read
from his novel titled "Pink." Filmmakers
from another dimension recruit a mid
dle-aged maker of infomercials for a new
adventure. Printer's Inc. Bookstore, 310
California Ave in Palo Alto. 327-6500.
5 p.m.

Film
Wed *22
The French Cine-Club of Palo Alto pre
sents "Ridicule," a 1996 film by Patrice
Leconte that mocks contemporary French
government and was nominated for mul
tiple Academy Awards. In 1783, at the
court of Louis XVI, wit is the ultimate
weapon. In French with English subtitles.
Cultural Center Auditorium, 1313 Newell
Road in Palo Alto. Tickets are $6 general,
$4 members. 325-3831. 8 p.m.
