On or about December 1910 the human eharacter changed, Virginia Woolf remarked, and
well she might have. The company she kept, the Bloomsbury circle, took shape before the
coming of World War I, and had a lasting impact on English society and culture after the
war. Drawing upon his historical and literary talents, Stanford Professor Peter Stansky
captures the dazzling world of early Bloomsbury. Just released in a beautiful paperback
edition, this book illustrates the political and social contexts of a truly fascinating time in
the not so distant past.
