# Wrestling deaths spur rule changes
## 
### 
OVFRI AND PARK. Kan
(AP) Ihe NCAA, jarred by the
recent deaths of three wrestlers
trying to make their weight classes,
announced rule changes vesterday
that put new restrictions on the
ways wrestlers shed pounds to
make weight.
Among the changes, effective
immediately
Wrestlers ma\ not use saunas
for water loss, and rubber suits and
diuretics cannot be used for am
reason. NCAA rules already bar
laxatives, fluid restriction, self in
duced vomiting, hot rooms, hot
boxes and steam rooms for dehy
dration I~he NC \ A defined a hot
room as a room with a temperature
above degrees.

was prett\ arbitrary 1 mean, you
always think of wrestling rooms as
110 degrees." s.ud Stanford sopho

For the rest of this season
only, a seven-pound weight al
lowance has been added to .ill
weight classes, which already ha\c
a one-pound allowance.
For example, for the 1 18-pound
class, wrestlers may weigh as much
;is 126 pounds. But as of Feb. 1,
when the one-pound allowance
ends, seven pounds will he added
to each weight class, making 125
pounds the upper limit of the 118-
pound class. The rule is effective
onl\ for the rest of this season and

is expected to he reviewed when
NCAA rules officials meet in
April.
"They're basically moving up
the weight classes seven pounds."
Kendall said. "But anyone who's
wrestled at a higher weight class
before can't just shift down to the
lower one and take advantage of
the rule."
Wrestlers may compete only in

weight classes in which they com
peted on or before Jan. 7 of this
season.
All weigh-ins will be held no
more than two hours before a
match, cutting down the time ath
letes have to lose weight.
The deaths alarmed coaches
and prompted calls for the NCAA
to review its rules.
The most recent victim was 21-

year-old Jeff Reese of the Univer
sity of Michigan. He died in De
cember of kidney failure and a
heart malfunction while wearing a
rubber suit and riding a stationary
bike to lose weight.
