Course Offering
Spanish 150:
Spanish Literature
Introduction to Spanish
Peninsula Literature
Taught by
Professor Edwin Williamson
TTH 2:15-4:00
Room 260-241
3-5 units
(register as 298-522-150-0-01)
Questions:
Call Meredith Dyer at 725-9226
The aim of this course is to explore the
culture of Spain in its Golden Age ( a time
when the Spanish Monarchy had become
the foremost power in the world. It will
consist of a study of how authors repre
sent the dominant values of Spanish
society in poetry, fiction and drama, and
how they address the conflicts it sought to
resolve. Texts will be studied as literary
artifacts in their own right and as products
of the culture of their time. Close reading
of "Lazarillo de Tormes" and works by
Garcilaso, Cervantes, Lope de Vega,
Tirso de Molina, Calder6n de la Barca.
