NOMINATIONS ARE BEING ACCEPTED
from the CLASS OF 1999 for the
PHI BETA KAPPA
TIE ACHING IPEH
The Stanford Chapter of Phi Beta Kappa is currently soliciting nominations for this
year's Phi Beta Kappa Teaching Prize. The Prize, which carries a cash award, recognizes
excellence in teaching as well as effectiveness in eliciting high student achievement in
liberal arts and sciences scholarship. The Prize is awarded during the annual spring
initiation ceremony which will be held this year on Friday, June 11 from 7:30-9:00 p.m.
Previous winners are:
1991 - Professor David Brady, Political Science
1992 - Professor Jodie Maxmin, Art
1993 - Professor Robert Waymouth, Chemistry
1994 - Professor Donald Kennedy, Biological Sciences and Human Biology
1996 - Professor Robert McGinn, Industrial Engineering and Engineering Management
1997 - James Watanabe, Lecturer, Hopkins Marine Station
1998- Professor Thomas Wandless, Chemistry
Nominations of tenured, tenure-track, or non-tenure-track instructors or professors are
invited from members of the Class of 1999 ONLY. Seniors should write their
nomination in the form of a short letter describing why the instructor is particularly
deserving. Specifics rather than generalizations will best serve a person s candidacy.
Letters of nomination, in paper format ONLY, should be sent by February 1, 1999 to:
PBK Teaching Prize Committee, 119 Birch/Oak, Jordan Quad, Stanford, CA 94305-4128.
For more information, please contact Dr. Jane Marcus, PBK Secretary, at
jane.marcus@stanford.edu or by phone at 723-1659.
