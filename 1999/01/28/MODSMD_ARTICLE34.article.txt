Film
THUR/28
The Stanford Film Society presents "Beginnings," a film festival featuring stu
dent films of now-famous directors George Lucas, Robert Zemeckis, Jane
Campion and Martin Scorsese. See how it all began at 8 p.m. in Cubberly
Auditorium.
Theater
THUR/28
Jeannie Barroga presents her one-woman show, "A Good Face." The perfor
mance is at 7:30 p.m. at the A3C Ballroom.
Art
NOW — FEB / 21
Now on exhibit is the "Stanford Art Faculty Exhibition," featuring work in a
range of media. Admission is free. The works are on display from 10 a.m. to
5 p.m. Tuesday through Friday, and 1 to 5 p.m. on Saturday and Sunday at
the Stanford Art Gallery.
SUN/31 — MAY/2
"Impressionists in Winter: Effets de Neige" is the first exhibition to focus exclu
sively on Impressionist winter landscape paintings. This display of 55 paint
ings by Monet, Pissarro, Sisley, Gaillebotte, Renoir and Gaugin presents a

wide range of luminous winter scenes. Although Paris and its surrounding
countryside are not known for snowy winters, an unusual winter pattern pre
vailed during the period represented in this exhibtion. Between 1865 and
1894, the impressionists produced hundreds of paintings that they titled or
subtitled"effet de neigeT (literally, "snow effect"). Tickets are $12, $9 students.
Note that reservations are required for attendance on Saturdays and
Sundays. The exhibition is open Tuesday, Saturday and Sunday from 10 a.m.
to 6 p.m., and Wednesday to Friday from 10 a.m. to 8 p.m. at Yerba Buena
Center for Arts, 701 Mission St., San Francisco. Call (415) 978-ARTS for
advance tickets and information.
Music
SAT/30
The SFS Great Performers Series continues with a recital by French pianist
Jean-Yves Thibaudet. He will perform works by Debussy, Rachmaninoff and
Liszt. Praised by the press as "one of the most exciting talents before the pub
lic today," pianist Thibaudet is renowned for his eloquent phrasing, lustrous
colors and brilliant technique. Elegant and engaging, Thibaudet is a sought
after soloist and collaborator, with over 20 classical CDs to his credit. The pro
gram will include works by Debussy, Rachmaninoff, Liszt, Wagner, and Verdi.
Tickets are $12 to $50. The performance is at 8 p.m. at the Davies Symphony
Hall, 201 Van Ness Ave., San Francisco. Call (415) 864-6000 for tickets and
information.
