Literature
THUR/4
Local author and winner of the 1998 John Simmons
Short Fiction Award Kathryn Chetkovich explores how
the young women of her eleven short stories are
brought down, sometimes willfully, by those they love.
Admission is free. The reading is at 7:30 p.m. at
Kepleris Books, 1010 El Camino Real, Menlo Park.
327-4321.

Film
THUR/4
Synergy launches a weekly series of "political films."
This week: "Salt of the Earth," directed by Herbet
Biberman. The screening is at 10 pm in the Synergy
House Lounge (on San Juan, off Campus Drive East).
SUN/7 - MO N/8
Sadie Benning Film Festival: Sunday will feature
Benning's older shorts, while Monday will screen her
newest work, including "Flat is Beautiful," "Judy Spots"
and "German Song." Tickets are $7, $3.50 for students;
arrive early for tickets. 7:30 pm, the San Francisco Art
Institute, 800 Chestnut (between Jones and

Leavenworth), San Francisco. (415) 558-8129.
THUR/4
The Stanford Film Society's 18th Annual Black Maria
Film and Video Festival, featuring a selection of 49
award-winning pieces. Film-makers and the festival
programmers will be in attendance to present their
works and speak with the audience. The program will
be divided into two screenings: "Human Truths and
Follies" at 7 pm, and "Strange Love and Modern
Society" at 9 pm. Free for undergraduates, $3/program,
$5 for both, for graduate students, staff and faculty, and
$5/program, $8 both for general audience. Annenberg
Auditorium, in the basement of Cumming Art Building.
