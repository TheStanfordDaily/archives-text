George P. Shultz Fund for Canadian Studies
The Canadian Studies Committee announces a fellowship for
advanced undergraduate and graduate students specializing in
Canadian Studies. Awards are typically under $1,000. Applicants
are asked to provide:
• a 2-3 page proposal;
• a detailed,one-page budget (indicating other sources of
funding);
• a short statement from a faculty advisor confirming the
relevance of the travel/research to applicant's academic
work;
• an up-to-date curriculum vita.

Join Us in An Exciting New Educational Venture:
The Edison
Partnership School
The Edison Project is seeking individuals with a demonstrated
commitment to teaching for the 1999/2000 school year. Founded in 1991,
The Edison Project is the country's leading private manager of public
schools. We currently provide an outstanding education to students in 51
public charter and partnership schools in 12 states and 25 cities across the
nation. As The Edison Project continues to grow, so do opportunities for
educators.
Opportunities exist for teachers with a range of classroom experi
ences, as well as school-based Community Resource Directors, Directors of
Technology and Special Education Teachers.
Why teach at Edison school? The Edison Project offers teachers an
exciting opportunity to work in a school community that values creativity,
diversity, professionalism, technological sophistication, motivation, and
accountability.
