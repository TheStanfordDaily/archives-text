Heart disease more prevalent
among ethnic minorities
Blacks 45 to 64 years old are twice as likely
to die from heart disease than their white
counterparts
6- to 9-year-old black and Chicana girls are
more likely to be overweight and to show
early signs of diabetes than white girls
Black and Chicana girls obtain a higher per
centage of their energy from fat and tend to
have higher blood pressure than white girls.
Black and Chicano boys are at higher risk
of diabetes than white boys, and black boys
have higher fat intake than white boys.
One particular risk factor — smoking — is
more prevalent among whites from lower
socioeconomic status than for either of the
two minority groups.
