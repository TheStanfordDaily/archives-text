Prof. Van Valkenburgh is principal of Michael Van
Valkenburgh Associates, Cambridge,
Massachusetts, a landscape architecture firm
founded in 1982. He attended Cornell University
and studied photography at the Boston Museum
School. Mr. Van Valkenburgh completed a Master
- V.v of Fine Arts in Landscape Architecture from The
University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in
Among his most recent award-winning projects we
find: The Vera List Courtyard in New York
(American Society of Landscape Architects Merit Award in 1998); Allegheny
Riverfront Park in Pittsburgh (Progressive Architecture Award Citation 1997); 50
Avenue Montaigne Garden, Paris (Boston Society of Landscape Architects Merit
Award 1995); and Harvard Yard Restoration (National Trust for Historic
Preservation Honor award 1994)
Mr. Van Valkenburgh is Charles Eliot Professor of Landscape Architecture at
Harvard University's Graduate School of Design, where he was chairman of the
Landscape Architecture Department from 1991 to 1996. He has taught at the
GSD since 1979. Mr. Van Valkenburgh is an Advanced Fellow at the American
Academy in Rome, and a Fellow of the American Society of Landscape
Architects.
