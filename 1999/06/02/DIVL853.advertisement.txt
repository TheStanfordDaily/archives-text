> WORK WITH THE AFRICAN IMMIGRATION AND
REFUGEE RESOURCE CENTER
The African Immigrant and Refugee Resource Center (AIRRC), a nonprofit in San
Francisco that helps immigrants and refugees with job placement and training, is
looking for summer interns and/or volunteers to help with capacity building, grant
writing and cultural awareness events. The AIRRC has been in operation for 17 years
and has served more than 700 immigrants and refugees from 23 nations (mostly
African). The center has limited staff, so interns would be extremely involved and
given lots of responsibility. The location is great: Market St. between Sth A 6th, right
near Union Square. Interested? Want to hear more? Contact Stephanie Kehrer asap at
497-4160 or skehrer@leland.

* INTERN WITH A WELFARE-TO-WORK
ORGANIZATION IN OAKLAND OR BERKELEY
The Comprehensive Care Team, a group of multidisciplinary health care professionals,
needs volunteer advocates who can assist the team in providing services to seriously
ill outpatients receiving care at UCSF clinics. Volunteer advocates provide non-med
ical services: home visits, telephone follow-ups and reassurance, shopping errands,
respite visits for caregiver relief. Volunteers are offered training, on-going support
and have the opportunity to help individuals live as fully as possible during their
experience of serious illness. For more information, call Ann Bednarczyk, Volunteer
Coordinator, ast (4IS) 626-5900.

* WORK IN SOUTHERN AFRICA THIS SUMMER!
Southern African Student Volunteers, SASVO, is a program of the United Nations
Development Program. SASVO works with Southern African college students to orga
nize three-week development projects to make a concrete difference in the well-being
of their society. At the same time, students are able to see new places and meet new
people from other parts of the region. SASVO recruits and operates primarily in 14
different nations in Southern Africa. Students from all over the world are welcome to
join. For more information, email sasvol@iafrica.com, or check out the website:
www.sasvo.co.za
For more information, call the Haas Center for Public Service
at 723-0992 or stop by the Center between 9:OOAM and SrOOPM.
