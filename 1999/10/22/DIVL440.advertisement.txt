Undergraduate Advising Center
Are You Interested in an
Individually Designed Major in
Humanities and Sciences?
Come in to The Undergraduate Advising Center (UAC) and meet with
the Program Advisor, Susan Zare, to discuss your interests.
Keep in mind the following:
Each student submitting a new proposal must have at least three
(3) full quarters of undergraduate work remaining at Stanford.
Each major shall consist of at least 60 units, at or above the 100 level.
A maximum of 15 of these 60 units may be taken as pass/no credit.
A maximum of 8 of these 60 units inay be taken in individual study or directed
reading.
None of these 60 units may count towards another major, minor, or other special
program.
At least one course must satisfy the Writing in the Major (WIM) requirement.
The proposed major must constitute a coherent program that achieves a clear
academic goal.
The proposed major must be comparable in quality and academic rigor to
degrees obtained in other degree-granting programs offered at Stanford.
The proposed major cannot duplicate or be achievable through a major offered
by another degree-granting program or department.
The proposed major must include a senior capstone experience integrating the
interdisciplinary I DM.
Students must present evidence that demonstrates their ability to do
independent work.
Proposals are accepted once per quarter.
