Cultural and Social Anthropology Department
presents:
On the Subject of Narrative
Talks on narrative and social analysis.
Renato Rosaldo
Monday, November 22, 1999
3:15 - 5:00 p.m., Bids. 110, Rm. 1110
Narrative Truths
Renato Rosaldo is a Lucie Stern Professor in Social Sciences at
Stanford University. He has done field research among the Ilongots of
northern Luzon, Philippines. He has published many books including
Ilongot Headhunting, 1883-1974: A Study in Society and History
(1980) and Culture and Truth: The Remaking of Social Analysis
(1989). Recently, he has been conducting research on cultural citizen
ship in San Jose, CA. and published Claiming Identity \ Space, and
Rights (1997).
James Fernandez
Monday. November 29, 1999
3:30-5:00 p.m., Bids. 110, Rm. 1110
"On Capturing the Imagination" and
Other Ironies of the Narrated World
James Fernandez is a professor of anthropology at the University of Chicago since
1986. His work has focused on various anthropological issues in Africa;
Microcosmogeny and Modernization in Africa (1969), Bwiti: An Ethnography
of the Religious Imagination in Africa (1982) and Persuasions and
Performances: the Play of Tropes in Culture (1986). His more recent research
deals with field work he is doing Spain. His presentation for the colloquium will
address three different kinds of narratives, including genealogical narratives,
chronicle narratives and development narratives.
