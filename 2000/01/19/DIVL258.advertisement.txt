NOMINATIONS ARE BEING ACCEPTED
from the CLASS OF 2000 for the
PHI BETA KAPPA
TEACHING PRIZE
The Stanford Chapter of Phi Beta Kappa is currently soliciting nominations
for this year's Phi Beta Kappa Teaching Prize. The Prize, which carries a cash
award, recognizes excellence in teaching as well as effectiveness in eliciting
high student achievement in liberal arts and sciences scholarship. The Prize
is awarded during the annual spring initiation ceremony which will be held
this year on Friday, June 9 from 7:30-9:00 p.m.
Previous winners are:
1991 - Professor David Brady, Political Science
1992 - Professor Jodie Maxmin, Art
1993 - Professor Robert Waymouth, Chemistry
1994 - Professor Donald Kennedy, Biological Sciences and Human Biology
1996 - Professor Robert McGinn, Industrial Engineering and Engineering
Management
1997 - James Watanabe, Lecturer, Hopkins Marine Station
1998- Professor Thomas Wandless, Chemistry
1999- Christopher Edwards, Assistant Professor, Mechanical Engineering
Nominations of tenured, tenure-track, or non-tenure-track instructors or
professors are invited from members of the Class of 2000 ONLY. Seniors
should write their nomination in the form of a letter describing why the
instructor is particularly deserving. Specifics rather than generalizations will
best serve a person's candidacy. Letters of nomination should be submitted
by February 4, 2000. Mail nominations to: PBK Teaching Prize Committee,
c/o Pam Hansson, Building 590, 514 Lausen Mall, Room 202, Stanford, CA
94305-4128, or e-mail, to Pam.Hansson@Stanford.Edu.
For more information, please contact Pam Hansson, PBK Co-Secretary, at
pam.hansson@stanford.edu or by phone at 725-0319.
