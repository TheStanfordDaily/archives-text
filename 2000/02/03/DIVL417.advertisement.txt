Cultural and Social Anthropology Department
presents:
Materializing Identities
The CASA colloquium series in Winter 2000 will explore various processes of identity formation in terms of individuals, gender, and community.
In particular the central role of material culture in making identities will be examined. In this way, even if not concerned with the distant past,
all tfie contributions are 'archaeological', in that they deal with the materialisation and objectification of social processes.

Rosemary Joyce
Monday, February 7, 2000
3:30 - 5:00 p.m. • Bldg 110 •Rm 1110
Performance and Inscription: Sex, Gender
and Sexuality in Prehispanic Mesoamerica
Rosemary Joyce is a professor of anthropology at the University of California at Berkeley. Her studies have involved
symbolism and social organization in complex societies, settlement patterns, and the analysis of archaeological ceramics.
Bob Preucel
Monday, February 28, 2000
3:30 - 5:00 p.m. • Bldg 110 •Rm 1110
Making Communities: Discourses of Identity
and Meaning at Kotyiti, A Revolt Period Pueblo
Village in the Northern Rio Grande
Bob Preucel is a professor of anthropology at the University of Pennsylvania and Associate Curator of
North American archaeology at the University Museum. His research interests center on understanding the ways
in which archaeology intersects with the natural and social sciences.
Tom Laquer
Monday, March 6, 2000
3:30 - 5:00 p.m. • Bldg 110 •Rm 1110
Places of the Dead in Modernity
Tom Laquer is a professor of history at the University of California at Berkeley. His published works include
Religion and Respectability: Sunday Schools and Working class Culture, 1/80-1850 (19760 and Making Sex:
Body and Sender from the Greeks to Freud (1990).
