UNDERGRADUATE ADVISING CENTER
Are You Interested in an
Individually Designed Major in Humanities and Sciences?
Come in to the Undergraduate Advising Center (UAC) and meet with the
Program Advisor, Susan Zare, to discuss your interests. Keep in mind the
following:
• Each student submitting a new proposal must have at least three
(3) full quarters of undergraduate work remaining at Stanford.
• Each major shall consist of at least 60 units, at or above the 100
level.
• A maximum of 15 of these 60 units may be taken as pass/no credit.
• A maximum of 8 of these 60 units may be taken in individual study
or directed reading.
• None of these 60 units may count towards another major, minor, or
other special program.
• At least one course must satisfy the Writing in the Major (WIM)
requirement.
• The proposed major must constitute a coherent program that
achieves a clear academic goal.
• The proposed major must be comparable in quality and academic
rigor to degrees btained in other degree granting programs offered at
Stanford.
• The proposed major cannot duplicate or he achievable through a
major offered by another degree-granting program or department.
• The proposed major must include a senior capstone experience
integrating the interdisciplinary IDM.
• Students must present evidence that demonstrates their ability to do
independent work.
WINTER QUARTER DEADLINE
TYiesday, February 22, 2000 y
Proposals are accepted once per quarter. Check with UAC in Sweet Hall, Ist Floor,
for exact dates or phone
723-2426 for more information.
