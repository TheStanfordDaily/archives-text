						History of Struggle
## 
### 
• The Kurds are the largest ethnic population in
the world without a homeland. They make up
20 to 25 million people, spread out over
Turkey, Iran, Iraq, Syria and the former Sovi
et Union.
• Up until World War I, the Kurds lived in a loose
federation of tribes under the Ottoman Km
pire.
• Last year, the leader of the Kurdish resistance,
Abdullah Ocalan, was captured by the Turk
ish government, tried for treason and sen
tenced to death. His case is being appealed.
• Just last month, Kurdish guerillas announced
their transition from armed struggle to poli
tics.
