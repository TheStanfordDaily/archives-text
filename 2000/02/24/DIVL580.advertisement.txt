Department of Physics, Stanford University presents
The Robert Hofstadter Memorial Lectures
Free Public Lectures — No charge to attend
February 28 and 29, 2000
Invited Speaker: Professor Douglas Hofstadter
Professor of Computer Science and Cognitive Science,
Indiana University
Public Lecture: Monday, February 28, 2000
at 8:00 PM in the Regional Teaching Facility,
370 Serra Mall, TCSEQ, Room 201,
Stanford University
Musings and Reminiscences of an Ex-Quasi-Physicist"
An autobiographical talk describing the speaker's arduous journey from mathematics to physics to cognitive science.
Afternoon Colloquium: Tuesday, February 29, 2000
4:15 pm (at the same location as above)
"The Übiquity and Power of Analogies in Physics"
This lecture will focus on how a familiar idea in one area of physics can be transported into another area
and yield important new insights.
Professor Douglas Hofstadter (Professor of Computer and Cognitive Science, Indiana University) is the son of the late Nobel Laureate and
Stanford Physics Professor, Robert Hofstadter. Douglas is the recipient of numerous awards, including the Pulitzer Prize for his book, Godel.
Escher. Bach: an Eternal Golden Braid. He has an unusually wide range of interests and a diverse background in science.
Please call (650) 723-4347 for more information
