Public Service Opportunities'
+ PUBLIC SERVICE ADVISING
FELLOWSHIP PROGRAM
Here is a chance to advise students about service
opportunities, create resources for students doing
service, develop and present outreach programs and
workshops across campus and serve as a liaison
between the Haas Center and a community center.
Earn $650/ per quarter. Applications area available at
www.haas.sUmford.edu. Applications are due April
21, 2000. Contact avila@stanford.edu for more
information.
+ INTERNATIONAL SUMMER
PROGRAM ASSISTANT
Work as a Program Assistant for this exciting educa
tional exchange program involving Stanford,
Japanese and Taiwanese university students!
Introduce these guests to America while learning
about their cultures! Room, board, stipend provid
ed, or forgo the stipend, and opt for a trip to Asia
instead!. No prior background necessary for this
educational exchange; 16 open positions.
Info meeting 4/13, noon, at Haas Center for Public
Service, 3rd floor. Or call, email, or drop by:
Volunteers In Asia/Trans Pacific Exchange /
mailto:bmurphy@igc.org / 3-3229 / http:www.vola
sia.org / Apps. due 4/20, 5:00 PM.
+ CELEBRATE LIFE...THROUGH MUSIC!
Would you like to make someone's day? Then join
Young at Heart, a community service group devoted
to bringing joy to the elderly through song. Each
week, we perform a show filled with showtunes and
old standards from America's Golden Era (19205-19505)
1950s) for the residents of a local nursing home.
Contact Matthew Wenger at 7-5498 or
mwenger@stanford.edu if you'd like to find out
more about Young at Heart or see one of our perfor
mances. You can also visit our web page at
http://www.stanford.edu/group/yah/
+ TUTORS NEEDED!
East Palo Alto Tennis and Tlitoring (EPATT) is look
ing for tutors willing to commit three to four hours a
week to do one-on-one literacy and homework help
with youth ages K-12. Afternoon and evening times
available. On campus. No tennis is required. Contact
Aisha Lowe (aisha@leland) for more information.
Presented by the Haas Center for Public Service, a unit of Student Affairs.
Located across from the Bike Shop; open 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Call 723-0992.
The Culture of the /Yew Economy
Monday, April 24 • 7:30 PM
j|| Dr. Curtis R. Carlson
9L President and CEO of SRI International, Dr. Carlson was a
1 member of the team that won an Emmy for outstanding
{ , JHjM technical achievement for his development of digital high
definition television (HDTV) which became the U.S.
business strategist, futurist, and author
of The Art of the Long We wand, most recently, co-author of \
The Long Boom, Mr. Schwartz is co-founder and chairman
of the Global Business network.
In an informal setting, the two speakers will outline their
rationale for why they believe we are at the beginning of a "long I
boom." They are MOT primarily speaking of an economic boom,
but rather a continuing deep transformation in all aspects of life and culture. Mr.
Schwartz will offer his understanding of the forces that will continue to drive a boom
he thinks has just begun and suggest a set of principles that he believes are fundamen
tal to promoting positive rather than destructive manifestations of the boom. Dr. Carlson
will talk about his experiences in creating an enterprise that is responsive to the oppor
tunities of this time and speak about the changes he is actively implementing at SRI to
enable it to become an incubator for innovative and world-changing technologies.
