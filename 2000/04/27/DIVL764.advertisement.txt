Are You Interested in an
Individually Designed Major in Humanities and Sciences?
Come in to The Undergraduate Advising Center (UAC) and meet with the Program
Advisor, Susan Zare, to discuss your interests. Keep in mind the following:
• Each student submitting a new proposal must have at least three
(3) full quarters' ol undergraduate work remaining at Stanford.
• Each major shall consist of at least 60 units, at or above the 100 level.
• A maximum of 15 of those 60 units may he taken as pass/no credit.
• A maximum of 8 of these 60 units may be taken in individual study or
directed reading.
• None of these 60 units may count towards another major, minor, or
other special program.
• At least one course must satisfy the Writing in the Major (WIM)
requirement.
• The proposed major must constitute a coherent program that
achieves a clear academic goal.
• The proposed major must be comparable in quality and academic
rigor to degrees obtained in other degree-granting programs ollered
at Stanford.
• The proposed major cannot duplicate or be achievable through
major olfered by another degree-granting program or department.
• The proposed major must include a senior capstone experience
integrating the interdisciplinary IDM.
• Students must present evidence that demonstrates their ability to do
independent work.
