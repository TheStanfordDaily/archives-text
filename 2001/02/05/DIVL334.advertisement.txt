"A Quest (or Dignity:
A History ol African-American Physicians"
Vanessa Northington Gamble, A\.D., Ph.D.
Vice President for Community anil Minority Programs, AAMC
Thursday, February 15, 2001
Medical School Classroom M-106
noon
Vanessa Northington Gamble, M.D., Ph.D. is the Vice President of the Division of Community and Minority
Progiams at the Association of American Medical Colleges (AAMC) At the AAMC she leads activities in diversity in
the medical workforce, cultural competence, health Previously Dr. Gamble was Director of the Center for the Study
of Race and Ethnicity in Medicine and Associate Professor of History of Medicine and Family Medicine at the
University of Wisconsin School of Medicine. She was the first black woman tenured at the medical school
An internationally recognized scholar hei research interests include the history of American medicine and public
health, health policy, and issues of race and racism in American medicine
Her book, Making a Place for Outselves Jhe Black Hospital Movement, 1920-1945, was named an outstanding
academic book by Choice, the |ournal of ocademic librarians. Dr Gamble recently received a Robert Wood Johnson
Foundation Health Investigator Award to write a book of essays on race and racism in American medicine. She is
also conducting research on the history of black women physicians.
Dr. Gamble has served as a consultant oi committee Alan Guttmacher Institute, the Agency for Healthcare Research
and Quality, the Institute of Medicine, the National Institutes of Health, the Centeis for Disease Control and
Prevention. American Foundation foi AIDS Research, the American Association for the History of Medicine, and the
Reproductive Health Technologies Proiect
She has also served as chair of the Tuskegee Syphilis Study legacy Committee. This committee took a lead role
in the successful campaign to obtain a presidential apology for the syphilis study
Dr. Gamble received her B.A from Hampshire College and her M D., and her Ph.D. in the History and Sociology of
Science, from the University of Pennsylvania.
Sponsored by The Student National Medical Association, the Center of Excellence,
and the Gustavus and Louise Pfeiffer Research Foundation
For more information, please contcct the Center of Excellence at (650) 725-0403
