The Ai Isayama Scholarship
I'lie \i Isayama Scholarship was established by the Asia/Pacific Research Center in 1991 to pro-
Mile support to .i qualified Stanford undergraduate to conduct research in Japan in preparation for
writing a senior honors thesis related to Japan or I S.-Japan relations. The scholarship is normal
ly gixen for summer support between the awardee's third and fourth years of undergraduate work.
I'lie scholarship was established in memory of Ai Isayama, the daughter of close friends of the
\si.iT.ii ifii Research Center, who died of leukemia in 1990 at the age of sixteen.
Hie Ai Isayama Scholarship provides economy-class air travel and living expenses for up to ten
weeks in Japan. The maximum amount available to each awardee is $4,000 (including travel); the
total amount awarded depends on the proposed length of stay.
Students majoring in am discipline are eligible to apply. Applicants need not be proficient in the
Japanese language but must demonstrate that their time in Japan will contribute substantially to the
completion of their proposed project.
Hie application deadline is \pril .SO, 2001. To apply, please submit a written proposal of no more
than five pages in length, along with your resume, current Stanford transcript, two letters of refer
ence. and a budget for the proposed period of stay in Japan, to the Isayama Scholarship Committee
at the address below. I'lie proposal should describe \our research topic in detail and explain why
you need to spend time in Japan in pursuing this research.
