Aw ar d -winning author, William E. Burrows,
discusses the Blind Man's Bluff" of aerial espionage with
his latest book, By Any Means Necessary
•MW99
Unknown to the public and cloaked
inutniost secrecy, the L'.S. flew missions
against the Communist bloc almost
continuously during the Cold War
in a desperate effort to collect
intelligence and find targets tor all-out
nuclear war. Many air force and
navy flyers were killed on these
missions. Now, award-winning
historian and author o(Deep Black:
Space Espionage and National Security,
William E. Burrows, shows that others
were captured, tortured and imprisoned
while their loved ones grieved and their
government looked the other way.
Burrows has interviewed scores of
men who flew these "black missions",
as well as the widows and children
of those who never returned, all of
whom want the full story told. By
Any Means Necessary is not about
airplanes, but about the people
who've sacrificed their lives in
the interest of national security.
