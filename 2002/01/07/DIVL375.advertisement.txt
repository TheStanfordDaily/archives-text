The Beinecke
Scholarship Program
If you are a junior planning to attend graduate school in the arts, humanities,
or social sciences you may be eligible fora Beinecke Scholarship. The
program seeks to encourage and enable highly motivated students to pursue
opportunities available to them and to be courageous in the selection of a
graduate course of study.
Information Meeting
Thursday, January 10, 2002
4:00 pm
403 Sweet Hall
Eligibility
1) A college junior pursuing a bachelor's degree during the 2001-2002 academ
ic year. "Junior" means a student who plans to continue full-time undergraduate
study and who expects to receive a baccalaureate degree between December
2002 and August 2003. 2) Be a United States citizen or a United States
national from American Samoa or the Commonwealth of the Northern
Mariana Islands. 3) Have a documented history of receiving need-based finan
cial aid during his or her undergraduate years.
A Beinecke Scholar receives a total of $32,000 in support of his or her graduate
education.
How to Apply
Pick up application materials at the Undergraduate Research Programs offices
on the fourth floor of Sweet Hall. For further information, contact us at 724-
7562; urp@vpue.stanford.edu; or http://urp.stanford.edu/
Application Deadline: Friday, February 8, 2002 by 5:00 pm
