A Unique Opportunity For Summer 2002
K
"Beagle II Awards"
Thanks to a special alumni gift, the Department ot Anthropological Sciences is pleased to offer
again the Beagle II Awards for the summer of 2002, open to Stanford undergraduates of all
years and majors.
• Awards of up to $4000 will he made to the hest 1-3 proposals for a summer "voyage" of
scientific discovery to any appropriate location in the world.
• The applications ask for a proposed itinerary of travel (it need not he by ship) and study,
together with a statement of the ways in which a self-styled Beagle "voyage" would con
tribute to the applicant's undergraduate science education. Students can apply singly or
in groups of 2 or 3.
• Applications will be screened by an interdisciplinary committee.
Informational Meeting:
Thursday - January 17- 4:00 pm - Bldg. 370-Rm. 370
Meeting will include:
• a brief introduction to the goals of the Beagle 11 program,
• presentations by last year's Beagle II awardces.
Students considering applying for the Beagle II Award this year are encouraged to attend.
Applications:
Online at the Beagle II website at http://www.stanford.edu/group/beagle2 or paper copies at
Anthro Sciences, Bldg 360, Room 361-.361F. Due by 5 pm, Tuesday, February 19, 20002.
Questions can be directed to Mary Cahill, Student Services Coordinator, mcahill@stanford.
Awards to be announced Friday, March 1, 2002.
