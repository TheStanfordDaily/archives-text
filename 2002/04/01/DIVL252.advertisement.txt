Public Service Opportunities
+ COME TO COMMUNITY DAY AT STANFORD
I Community Day, Sunday, April 7, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m., is an open
house—a free, family-oriented, fun-filled, jam-packed day for our com
munity neighbors. Choose from more than 50 activities for young and
* old. Explore the university's classrooms, labs and museums. Learn more
about scientific research on campus and at Jasper Ridge Biological
> Preserve. Meet Stanford athletes and sign the kids up for sports clinics.
Enjoy dance and music performances by Stanford students. Browse the
1 health fair and check the safety of child car seats. Information:
bttp://neighbors. Stanford, edu
' ♦ RAVENSWOOD READS FOR THE KIDS...AND FOR YOU
Interested in working with elementary schoolers to enhance their read
, ing skills? lie a part of Ravenswood Reads: One-to-one literacy tutoring
with students (2nd-5th grades) from East Palo Alto. Flexible limes.
Transportation, curriculum and training provided. Work-Study available.
Contact Jesse Moya, 723-5786, moya@stanford.edu.
' ♦ STANFORD IN WASHINGTON
ENVIRONMENTAL AND HEALTH POLICY PROGRAM
Stanford in Washington is an academic residential program for juniors
and seniors to study and work for a quarter in our nation's capital.
> INFORMATION MEETING for Winter 2003, in which all program compo-
I nents will focus on environment and health policy, will be held
Thursday, April 11, 4:30 p.m. at the Haas Center. Applications due:
* Tuesday, May 7. For more information, email jhalleck@stanford.edu
> or call 725-2870.
+ NEW STUDENT-INITIATED URBAN STUDIES CUSS FOR
SPRING 2002
Housing and Homelessness in the Bay Area, first class:
Wednesday, April 3,4 p.m., Building 260, meet at the staircase on the
first floor. In the last few decades, local social and economic changes
have thrown thousands of Bay Area residents into poverty and unstable
housing situations. Through readings, dialogue, and guest speakers, find
out how communities and individuals are dealing with these issues and
how students can affect change. Contacts: katiecam@stanford.edu or
cbrawner@stanford. edu.
+ VOLUNTEER AT LYTTON GARDENS SENIOR COMMUNITY
♦Help shop at Safeway and deliver to residents, 3 hours every other week
or once a month. These residents have no one else to shop for them
since they lost their delivery service. * Initiate new Alzheimer's activities.
As little as one hour a week, any shift, to do simple exercises or activi
ties with the patients. * Volunteer for front desk in Assisted Living, 2-hour
shifts. Interact with residents, schedule transportation to doctors,
answer phones. Contact: Jon McConnell, jottmc@stanford.edu.
+ VOLUNTEER OPP FOR HISTORY BUFFS
The Los Altos History Museum is looking for museum docents, store
volunteers, administrative support to the staff and others, to fill a wide
variety of jobs. Docents and sales volunteers are needed for four shifts
on Thursdays through Sundays, noon to 4 p.m. Contact: Marion Grimm,
(650) 948-4042, FAX (650) 949-4042, or robgrimm@aol.com
Presented by the Haas Center for Public Service, a unit of Student Affairs.
Located across from the Bike Shop; open 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Call 723-0992.
