Debora Norris
Spring Quarter Visiting Mentor: April 10-12, 2002
The Visiting Mentor
Program is designed
to bring distinguished
professionals in
public service to
campus to spend time
meeting and working
with students, faculty,
and staff. The Visiting
Mentor Program is
jointly sponsored by
the Haas Center for
Public Service and
the Stanford Alumni
Association.
Debora Norris '93, is a third term
State Representative, and recently
announced her candidacy for the US
Congress in Arizona's new
Congressional District 1. She is the
first Native American woman in the
Arizona Legislature and would be
the first Native American woman in
the US Congress.
the following events are free and open to the public
Women of Color in Politics
Wednesday, April 10, 12:00 -1:15 p.m.
Women's Community Center
Lunch provided. Please RSVP: jennycwu@stnnford.edu
Reflections on Stanford and Beyond
Wednesday, April 10, 6:00 - 7:15 p.m.
Muwekma Talt-Ruk
Dinner served. For more information: nevets@stanford.edu
Ethics at Noon
Debora will lead a conversation on an ethical topic of her choosing.
Thursday, April 11,12:00 -1:00 p.m.
Building 100, Room 101K
Perspectives on Native American Culture and Politics
Come to an open-house reception to meet Debora.
Friday, April 12,9:30 -11:00 a.m.
Native American Cultural Center
Breakfast provided. Please RSVP: ggraves@stanford.edu
Stanford-in-Government Forum
Join Debora in an open conversation with students of SIG.
Friday, April 12,12:15 -1:30 p.m.
Donald Kennedy Conference Room, Haas Center
Lunch provided. Please RSVP: curiousg@stanford.edu
Haas Center for Public Service
