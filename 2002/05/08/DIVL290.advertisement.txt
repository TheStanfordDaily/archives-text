A conference and film festival commemorating the work of filmmakers
Fernando Birri and Lourdes Portillo.
Presented by The Center for Latin American Studies, Stanford University
The conference and festival will include screenings of several of each director's
films, as well as roundtable discussions about their lasting influence on cinema, both in
Latin America and throughout the world.
TP -n 1r\ "O- A world renowned filmmaker who revolutionized the use of cinema to
IlHitlQU Ol. J. 1 p romo te social justice, Fernando Birri began his career with the ground-break
ing documentary Tire Die, which focused on the lives of poor barrio residents on the
"■fit edges of modern urban Argentina. Birri also founded and directed a documentary film
sc h°°' " first of its kind in Latin America - at the Universidad National del Litoral
,, in Argentina, and co-founded and directed the Escuela International de Cine y
H Televisi ° n de San Antonio de Los Barios in Cuba. A recent recipient of the "Golden
I Gate Persistence Of Vision" award at the San Francisco International Film Festival, Birri
XS. ' s currently a Tinker Visiting Professor at the Center for Latin American Studies.
Filmmaker Lourdes Portillo has produced films on a range of |||
Award nominated documentary Las Madres: The Mothers of
the Plaza de Mayo, which focuses on the mothers of the disappeared in Argentina, and
the more recent documentary Corpus: A Home Movie for Selena. Her most recent docu
mentary, Seiiorita Extraviada, examines the mysterious killing of hundreds of young H
women in the border town of Ciudad Juarez. Portillo is currently a visiting professor in HE
the Department of Spanish and Portuguese. \
