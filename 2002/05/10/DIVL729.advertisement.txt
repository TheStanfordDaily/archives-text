Organized by the University Architect/Planning Office and

Annenberg Auditorium - Cummings Art Building
Stanford University - 7:30 p.m
Free Admission
Wednesday, May 15'
Shigeru Ban established his architectural practice in 1985 in Tokyo. He received his degree in architecture from Cooper Union
in 1984. He received the World Architecture Awards in 2001 for his work on the Japan Pavilion at the Expo 2000 Hannover
World's Foir. Publications include the "Shigeru Ban/Projects in Process" and the "Paper Tube Architecture from Rwanda to
Kobe."
