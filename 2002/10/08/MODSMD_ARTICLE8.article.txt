# Campus Watch
##

Researchers conduct first-ever trial of kleptoma
nia drug
Led by Psychiatry Prof. Lorrin Koran, researchers at Stanford Med
ical Center are beginning the first placebo-controlled trial of a drug
treatment for kleptomania.
The illness, which is characterized by uncontrollable urges to steal, af
fects an estimated 1.2 million people in the United States.
The study will examine whether stealing can be reduced by a selec
tive serotonin reuptake inhibitor that is currently on the market to treat
depression. Participants in the study will be asked to keep a journal of
incidents of kleptomania and record their urges to steal.
Koran has also led several studies examining a similar drug and its af
fect on compulsive shoppers, finding that nearly 75 percent of patients
significantly reduced their shopping while taking the drug.
Visiting poet Komunyakaa to speak at Kresge
Yusef Komunyakaa, the Lawrence and Nancy Mohr Visiting Poet
who is teaching an undergraduate workshop this fall, will read from his
work tomorrow at 8 p.m. in Kresge Auditorium.
Komunyakaa is a professor of creative writing at Princeton Universi
ty and a chancellor of the Academy of American Poets. He has received
numerous awards including the Pulitzer Prize, William Faulkner Prize
from the University of Rennes, the Thomas Forcade Award and a fel
lowship from the National Endowment for the Arts.
Technology Open House aims to help computer
illiterate
Sponsored by Information Technology Systems and Services and
Stanford University Libraries / Academic Information Resources, a
technology open house hopes to help answer the questions of Stan
ford faculty and staff concerning computer-related technology. The
event will be held tomorrow in the Meyer Library lobby from 11
a.m. to 1:30 p.m.
Organizers will be on hand to aid professors in finding solutions for
using computer equipment and software in the classroom, to set up
course Web sites, and to direct visitors to other available resources.
Catherine Knipe of the ITSS Planning, Technology and Consulting
Office, said that she was thrilled to offer direct support.
"That's what technology is supposed to be doing," she said. "Being a
help, not a hindrance."
Rep jresentatives from more than 40 technology service groups will
participate in the open house.
Peace Corps volunteer to speak at Bechtel
Returned Peace Corps volunteer Mona Nyandoro will share her ex
periences as a volunteer in Sao Tome and Principe in a General Infor
mation Meeting held at the Bechtel Center General Assembly Room this
evening at 6 p.m.
Nyandoro will also answer questions regarding current Peace Corps
service opportunities around the world.
Representatives from the Peace Corps will be on campus for the Ca
reer Fair held today from 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. in White Plaza.
