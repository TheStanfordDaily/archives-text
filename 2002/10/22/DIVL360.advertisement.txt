Discover What Matters to Luis Fraga. Luis Ricardo Fraga is Associate Professor in the Department of Political Science
at Stanford University. He has published widely in scholarly journals and edited volumes. He is co-editor of Ethnic and
Racial Minorities in Advanced Industrial Democracies (1992), and his co-authored book, The Politics of Education in
Multiethnic Cities will be published hy Duke University Press. He is currently completing another hook manuscript
entitled The Changing Urban Regime: Toward an Informed Public Interest. He is a past president of the Western
Political Science Association and has served on the Executive Council of the American Political Science Association.
Fraga has received a number of teaching and advising awards at Stanford including the Rhodes Pri:e tor Excellence in
Undergraduate Teaching (1993), the Dinkelspiel Award for Distinctive Contributions to Undergraduate Education
(1995), the Allan V. Cox Medal for Faculty Excellence Fostering Undergraduate Research (1997), the Faculty Award
from the Chicano/Latino Graduating Class (1993, 1996, 1997, 1999, 2000, 2001), and the Undergraduate Faculty
Advisor of the Year Award (2001). He was also given the Adaljiza Sosa-Riddell Award for Exemplary Mentoring of
Graduate Latina/o Students by the Committee on the Status of Latinos in the Profession of the American Political
Science Association (2001).
What Matters to Me and Why encourages reflection within the Stanford community on matters of personal values, beliefs and
motivations in order to better understand the lives and inspirations of those who shape the University. For more information,
