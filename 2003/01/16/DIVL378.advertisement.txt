You thought about dropping out of school to start
your own company, but the market crashed. You
looked at those Wall Street jobs, but weren't sure
they were right for you. You'd like to get an MBA,
but you're too young. What can you do?
Be CEO. As the head of Stanford Student
Enterprises, you have an unparalleled opportunity
to learn management skills, financial analysis, and
strategic decision-making. SSE is the only place
you can evaluate 80-year-old businesses and
analyze brand new ventures on a daily basis.
SSE is the financial branch of the ASSU. Our
portfolio of businesses includes the Stanford
Directory, Flicks, the Stanford Store, and the
Technology Group. SSE's Investments team also
manages over $8 million in assets, including the
ASSU Endowment.
Why wait? Be CEO.
The CEO position requires 20 hours per week for
training during spring quarter. As CEO, you will
work full-time during the summer and the
following academic year. Compensation is
competitive.
All majors are welcome to apply. Preference will
be given to students graduating by June 2003.
Applicants should have significant independent
leadership experience and demonstrated skills in
project management, numerical analysis, and
team building.
Interested candidates should consult the job
posting on Monstertrak / Cardinal Recruiting.
First-round interviews will be conducted on
January 30th and will be scheduled on a first
come first-served basis. To interview for the
position, you must submit a resume and cover
letter and sign up (on Monstertrak) between
January 16th and 26th.
Resume drop and interview sign-up January 16th - 26th
Manager and Director positions are also available.
For more information visit our website:
http://sse.sta nford.ed u
Questions? Contact Current CEO Dave Endelman at 725-1461
orceo@sse.stanford.edu.
