Recent research suggests that summertime lightning over the United States has a
significant impact on regional pollution, surpassing pollution caused by human
activities. Human activities dominate the creation of these gases near the Earth's
surface, but lightning plays a bigger role in the free troposphere. The findings
were released in a recent paper by researchers at Texas A&M.
