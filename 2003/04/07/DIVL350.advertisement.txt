The Institute for Research on Women and Gender
Stanford University
announces...
The Marjorie Lozoff Graduate Essay Prize
The Marjorie Lozoff Prize of $750 is awarded annually by the Maijorie Lozoff Fund for Research
on Women and Gender to promote scholarship in areas that further women's development. The
prize will be awarded to a graduate student fora scholarly essay not more than 35 double-spaced
pages in length that addresses some aspect of women's development. Contestants must be current
registered students at Stanford University. Graduate/professional students from any academic or
professional discipline are welcome to apply.
This prize honors the memory of Marjorie Morse Lozoff (September 30, 1916 - April 18, 2002).
Maijorie Lozoff served as a model for the idea that most deeply absorbed her: furthering women's
development for the benefit of women, men, children, and society.
Application Procedure: Six copies of the essay and of the entry form must be submitted to the
office of the Institute for Research on Women and Gender at Serra House, 556 Salvatierra Walk,
Mail Code: 8640. Applications can be found at "Funding Opportunities" on the Institute web site
at http://www.stanford.edu/group/IRWG/. The deadline is 5 p.m. on Friday, April 12, 2003. The
prize winner will be announced in mid-May.
For additional information, please contact Ali Abdollahi at 723-1994 or <ali.abdollahi@stanford.edu>
Taube Center for Jewish Studies
announces
The Donald And Robin Kennedy
Jewish Studies
Undergraduate Award
The Taube Center for Jewish Studies at Stanford University announces the annual Donald
and Robin Kennedy Undergraduate Award. The competition honors the best essay written
by an undergraduate at Stanford on a Jewish theme during the 2002-03 academic year.
One prize of $500 will be awarded. Essays must be typed, double-spaced, and accom
panied by a cover letter with the name of the contestant. The contestant's name should
not appear on the essay itself.
Please submit three (3) copies to the Taube Center for Jewish Studies, Building 240, room
103, Stanford University, Stanford, CA 94305.
Deadline for submission
of the essay is
May 12, 2003.
