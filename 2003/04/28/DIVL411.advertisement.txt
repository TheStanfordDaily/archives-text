All the Rave:
The Rise and Fall of
Shawn Fanning's
Napster
by Joseph Menn
Wednesday, April 30, 2003
7pm
Stanford Bookstore Art Alcove
At age seventeen, Shawn Fanning designed a computer
program that transformed the Internet into an unlimited
library of free music. Tens of millions of young people
quickly signed on, Time magazine put Fanning on its
cover, and his company, Napster, became a household
name. It did not take long for the music industry to
declare war, one that has now engulfed the biggest
entertainment and technology companies on the planet.
