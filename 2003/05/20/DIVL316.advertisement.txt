What Matters to Me and Wto
Nadinne Cruz
Director of the Haas Center for Public Service
Wed. May 21
Noon - 1 p.m.
Memorial Church's Side Chapel
Cruz, a Filipina-American educator, traces her involvement with public service over 30 years ago to her
college student volunteer experiences with peasants in the Philippines. Under her leadership, the Haas
Center has become a preeminent public service center, currently ranked * 1 for service-learning among
colleges and universities by the U.S. News and World Report. Cruz also founded and directed the year
long Public Service Scholars Program for seniors who develop honors theses into research as a form of
public service. As a lecturer in Urban Studies, she taught service-learning courses and has served as
Resident Fellow for the Okada Asian American Theme House. After nine years on campus, Cruz will
be leaving Stanford next month: "1 want to take time to practice what I preach about living a sustain
able life, spend more time with my family and friends to nourish my soul and spirit, renew my commu
nity involvement, do public speaking, and work on a community service textbook, a sequel to Servict'-
Learning: A Movement's Pioneers Reflect on Its Origins, Practice.''
Discussion and lecture series encourages reflection within the Stanford, community on matters of personal values,
beliefs, and motivations in order to better understand the lives and inspirations of those who shape the University.
Open to all. For more information, contact Rabbi Patricia Karlin-Neumann at rabbihkn@Suinford.EDU .
