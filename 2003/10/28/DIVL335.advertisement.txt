The Gustavus and Louise Pfeiffer Research Foundation
in conjunction with The Stanford University School of Medicine and Center of Excellence
Cordially Invite You To Attend
The 2003 Fall Quarter Visiting Professor Lecture Series
Improving the Health of Latino Children: One Minority Physicians Research and Career Path
Glenn Flores, M.D., F.A.A.P.
Associate Professor of Pediatrics, Epidemiology, and Health Policy; Director of Community Outcomes;
Associate Director of the Center for the Advancement of Urban Children, Department of Pediatrics,
Medical College of Wisconsin
Tuesday, October 28,2003
Medical School Classroom M-112* 12 noon
Glenn Flores founded and was the co-director of the Pediatric Latino Clinic at Boston Medical Center. He is a Robert Wood
Johnson Generalise Physician Faculty Scholar and a former Robert Wood Johnson Minority Medical Faculty Scholar, he has
published over 40 articles and book chapters on a variety of topics, including "Errors in medical interpretation and their
potential clinical consequences in pediatric encounters (Pediatrics 2003) and "The health of latino children: Urgent priorities,
unanswered questions, and a research agenda (Journal of the American Medical Association 2002).
Sponsored by
the Latino Medical Students Association, the Center of Excellence,
and the Gustavus and Louise Pfeiffer Research Foundation
For more information, please contact the Center of Excellence at (650) 725-0403
