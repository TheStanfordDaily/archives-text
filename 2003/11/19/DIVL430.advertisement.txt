The Institute for Research on Women and Gender
Stanford University
announces...
The Marjorie Lozoff Graduate Essay Prize
The Marjorie Lozoff Prize of $750 is awarded annually by the Marjorie Lozoff Fund for
Research on Women and Gender to promote scholarship in areas that further women's devel
opment. The prize will be awarded to a graduate student for a scholarly essay not more than
35 double-spaced pages in length that addresses some aspect of women's development.
Contestants must be current registered students at Stanford University. Graduate/profes
sional students from any academic or professional discipline are welcome to apply.
This prize honors the memory of Marjorie Morse Lozoff (September 30, 1916 - April 18,
2002). Marjorie Lozoff served as a model for the idea that most deeply absorbed her: fur
thering women's development for the benefit of women, men, children, and society.
Application Procedure: Six copies of the essay and of the entry form must be submitted to
the office of the Institute for Research on Women and Gender at Serra House, 556 Salvatierra
Walk, Mail Code: 8640. Applications can be found at "Funding Opportunities" on the
Institute web site at http://www.stanford.edu/group/IRWG/. The deadline is 5 p.m. on Friday,
February 20,2004. The prize winner will be announced April 1.
For additional information, please contact Courtney Bennett at 723-1994 or
<bennettc@stanford.edu>
