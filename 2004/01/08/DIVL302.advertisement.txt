Anatomy of Movement: an Interdisciplinary Course
Ortho 222 Winter Quarter 2004 http://move.stanford.edu
3-4:15 p.m.-M,W,F
Clark Center, room 5362 (Mon), S-360 (Wed & Fri)
Interested in Human Movement?
Consider Anatomy of Movement, a cross-discipline, project-based course!
Lecturers include Stanford and internationally renowned physicians and
surgeons, engineers, animators, visual and performing artists, and others who
study the marvel of motion. Team projects will simulate human movement.
Who Should Enroll?
Undergraduates—concentration or interest in Humßio, Biology, Visual & Performing
Arts, anthropology, computer Science/Symbolic Systems, Engineering.
Graduate Students —medical and engineering students
Course Requirements:
Team project of different backgrounds, creating a physical or
computational model of human movement.
3 unit course
Course Director
Amy L. Ladd, MD
alad@stanford.edu
