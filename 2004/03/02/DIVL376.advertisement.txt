The Office for Religious Life presents:
What Matters to Me and Why
Howard Wolf
President of the Stanford Alumni Association
Wednesday, March 3
12:00-1:00 P.M.
Side Chapel in Memorial Church.
Please feel free to bring your lunch.
What Matters to Me and Why is a discussion and lecture series that encourages reflec
tion within the Stanford community on matters of personal values, beliefs, and moti
vations in order to better understand the lives and inspirations of those who shape the
University. All students, faculty, staff and friends are welcome!
Wolf has led the Stanford Alumni Association since 2001 and earned his bachelor's
degree in psychology at Stanford in 1980 and his MBA at Harvard in 1985. He also
has worked as both an entrepreneur and business manager in both the publishing and
commercial real estate development and management industries. An active alum
and volunteer, as well as an avid Stanford athletics fan, he received the Stanford
Associates Outstanding Achievement Award in 2000. In addition to his alumni
association role, Howard is both an officer of the university and part of its senior
management team, with particular responsibility for advising the university's presi
dent and provost on alumni affairs.
For info., call the Office for Religious Life at 723-1762 or visit our website.
http://religiouslife.stanford.edu
