What happens if
you get sick in an African village?
Will you be able to access the archives you're interested in?
How do you gather specimens and get them back into the country?
How do you plan interviews to obtain the information you really need?
Find out at the
Undergraduate
Research Programs
Pre-Field Preparation Conference
Saturday, April 3, 2004
8:45 a.m. - 3:30 p.m.
Get answers to these questions and much more at the annual Pre-Field Preparation
Conference on Saturday, April 3, from 8:45-3:30. Stanford faculty and other campus
experts will prepare you to make the most of your summer research through plenary
talks and small workshops on methodologies, best practices, human subjects protocols,
contingency plans, and other issues. There will also be a special breakout session for
students doing public service. Discuss your plans with faculty members and students
who have already done research in specific parts of the world.
This event will help you to attain
your research and public service goals.
You are strongly urged to attend if you have already received funding or are in the
process of applying for an Undergraduate Research Opportunities Major or Small
Grant. Your participation in pre-field preparation will be strongly considered in the
review of your grant application.
Breakfast and lunch will be served. Fora detailed agenda and registration for this event,
visit our website at http://urp.stanford.edu. For further information or help preparing a
research grant proposal, please email Undergraduate Research Programs at
urp@vpue.stantord.edu.
