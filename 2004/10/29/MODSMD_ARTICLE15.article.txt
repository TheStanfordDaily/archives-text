# Public urination; illegal alcohol consumption
## 
### DEVON MAYLIE DESK EDITOR 
, Following Full Moon on the Quad, minors
in possession of alcohol and incidents of
drunken debauchery filled the pages of the
Stanford Police Department Public Bulletin.
Here are the crime highlights for Oct. 22-27,
as reported to the police department.
Friday, Oct. 22
It was reported today that an unidentified
person stole undergarments from an unat
tended dryer in the laundry room at Com
stock Circle two weeks ago.
In the CoHo, a student's Goodlink E-mail
device was stolen.

brary, a studier reported losing his cell phone.

Sunday, Oct. 24
At 11 p.m. in Larkin West, an unknown
person entered an unlocked room and took

the occupants wallet from his desk.
Monday, Oct. 25
It was discovered at 8 a.m. that the word
"CAL" had been written in pink and lavender
chalk on the wall of Hoover Tower.

Tuesday, Oct. 26
At 9:45 a.m. on Serra Street, a car struck

unknown driver.
Between 3:45 and 5 p.m. an unknown indi

In the afternoon, at 4:15 p.m., a non-injury

At 5 p.m. on Escondido Road, a witness re

an unknown person used a key to enter the

Wednesday, Oct. 27
On Campus Drive, an unidentified vehicle
struck and damaged a light pole and left the

In the Main Quad. 20 minutes later, a stu
