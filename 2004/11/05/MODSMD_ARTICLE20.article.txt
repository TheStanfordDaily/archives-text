# CoHo, campaign signs stolen; public masturbator caught
## 
### SAL UMBERTO BONACCORSO 
With Halloween and Election Day taking place in the
same week, campus crimes ranged from public masturba
tion and toilet-papering to stolen election campaign yard
signs and couches.
The following are the crime highlights for Oct. 29 to
Nov. 3 as recorded in the Stanford Police Department
Public Bulletin.
Friday, Oct. 29
An unknown person took a couch from the Narnia
House.
An unidentified person stole the registration papers,
the owner's manual, and a tire pressure gauge from the
glove box of an unlocked vehicle in the 28A Parking Lot.
Between 10:30 p.m. and 8:45 a.m. the following day,
five advertising banners and the home plate were stolen
from an athletic field.
Saturday, Oct. 30
At 12:45 a.m. at Mayfield, two males were found drag
ging a third who was under the influence of alcohol and
who later booked into jail.
At 6:15 a.m. on Palm Drive, an intoxicated driver was
found asleep at the wheel in a traffic lane and booked
into jail.
In Escondido Village, at 9:20 p.m., an unidentified
male was seen masturbating outside a female's apartment
window.
Sunday, Oct. 31
It was reported that an unidentified person took the
election campaign yard signs from a house on Alvarado
Row.

At 2:25 p.m., a person was discovered writing state
ments with chalk on the pavement at the entrance to the
Roble Parking Lot.
A resident reported that at 9:55 p.m. his house had
been decorated with toilet paper.
Monday, Nov. 1
Between 2:50 p.m. and 3:20 p.m., an unidentified per
son took an unsecured laptop from a fourth-floor desk at
the Center for Educational Research.
A locked bike was taken from a bike rack at Studio 5
in Escondido Village.
Tuesday, Nov. 2
At 10:47 a.m. on Campus Drive, a driver was cited and
released for driving without a license.
The CoHo sign was taken from the north wall of the
Coffee House.
Between 11 a.m. and 11:45 a.m., an unknown vehicle
hit a parked vehicle at Lambda Nu and then left the
scene.
At noon in the Stock Farm Lot, an unknown person
smashed the window of a vehicle and stole a stereo CD
player.
It was reported today that over the summer a laptop
was stolen from the storage room located in the Sterling
Quad Adams House.
At 9:40 p.m. on Bowdoin Street, a minor was cited and
released for being in possession of alcohol.
Wednesday, Nov. 3
A locked Haro bike was stolen from the bike rack out
side of the Ford Center.
