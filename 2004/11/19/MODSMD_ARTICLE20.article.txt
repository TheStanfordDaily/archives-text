# Bike tossers flee in golf cart; dog bites person
## 
### SAL UMBERTO BONACCORSO 
This week, four bicycle accidents occurred,
and a police official discovered a Cal bear
paw print stencil and spray paint inside of a
vehicle.
The following are the crime highlights for
Nov. 11-Nov. 17 as recorded in the Stanford
Police Department Public Bulletin.
Thursday, Nov. 11
At 11:40 a.m. on Salvatierra walk, a dog
owner was cited and released after his dog bit
a person.
At 12:45 p.m., a biker was transported to
Vaden Health Center after he fell and struck
his forehead on the pavement. He had been
trying to navigate a curb while holding an
umbrella.
An unlocked bike was taken from the
Cedro bike rack.
Friday, Nov. 12
At 4:20 a.m. on Panama Street, a driver
was cited and released for having a fraudulent
vehicle registration.
An unknown person broke into a vehicle
on Santa Teresa Street and stole the CD
stereo from inside.
Between 4:15 and 4:35 p.m. a person's bag
was stolen from the waiting area of the

Stanford Hair Salon while she was getting a
haircut.
Saturday, Nov. 13
At 1:30 a.m. at Mayfield, a minor was cited
and released for being in possession of a can
of beer.
Outside of Kimball Hall at 1:50 a.m. two
unidentified persons were seen throwing and
damaging bikes. They fled the scene in a golf
cart with three other unknown persons.
At 11:40 p.m. on Mayfield. another minor
was found in possession of a cup of beer and
was cited and released.
Sunday, Nov. 14
An unlocked garage and vehicle were ran
sacked on Salvatierra Walk, but nothing was
stolen.
At 7:25 p.m. at Panama Mall, a driver was
cited and released for driving without a valid
license.
Also reported today that an unknown per
son stole a locked Miyata bike from the rack
at Blackwelder Court.
Monday, Nov. 15
At 2 a.m. at Angel Field a Cal bear paw
print stencil and spray paint were found in a
Cal student's vehicle.

A checkbook was taken from a student's
desk drawer in her room in Mirrielies
Tuesday, Nov. 16
Between 2:50 and 3:25 p.m. at Building
160, an unattended laptop was stolen from
room 328.
At 9:15 p.m. two bikers were involved in a
collision at Blackwelder Court. One sus
tained injuries but refused treatment after
the Palo Alto Fire Department medics
responded.
Wednesday, Nov. 17
At Stern Hall at 12:30 a.m. a biker fell off
his bike and suffered lacerations to his face,
hands, and knees. Palo Alto Fire Department
medics transported him to Stanford
University Medical Center for treatment.
Later that day on Escondido Road at 1:05
p.m. a biker collided with another bicyclist
and was transported to Stanford University
Medical Center for treatment.
At 8:45 a.m. at the Hoover Pavilion South
Parking Lot, an unknown person smashed
the window of a vehicle and stole the 5-piece
drum set inside.
On Sweet Olive Way. a vehicle's window
was smashed open but nothing was taken
except for the DMV year tab on the rear
license plate.
