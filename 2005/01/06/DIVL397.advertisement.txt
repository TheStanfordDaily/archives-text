The Marjorie Lozoff Graduate
Essay Prize
Description: The Marjorie Lozoff Prize of $750 is awarded annually by the
Marjorie Lozoff Fund for Research on Women and Gender to promote scholar
ship in areas that further women's development.These include but are not
limited to such topics, particularly in a current context, as reproductive
choice, equal rights for women, women's participation in the professions and
other areas of work, women's role within the family, aging, and women and
the sciences.
Eligibility: The Marjorie Lozoff Graduate Essay Prize will be awarded to a
graduate student fora scholarly essay (of not more than 35 double-spaced
pages in length) that addresses some aspect of women's development as out
lined above. Contestants must be current registered students at Stanford
University. Graduate/professional students from any academic or professional
discipline are welcome to apply.
This prize honors the memory of Marjorie Morse Lozoff (September 30, 1916 -
April 18, 2002). In an extraordinarily productive life as wife, mother, sociolo
gist, social worker, teacher, researcher, and community leader, Marjorie Lozoff
served as a model for the idea that most deeply absorbed her: furthering
women's development for the benefit of women, men, children, and society.
Application Procedure: Submit two copies of the essay and of the entry
form to the office of the Institute for Research on Women and Gender at Serra
House, 556 Salvatierra Walk, Mail Code: 8640. Application forms can be found
at "Funding Opportunities" on the Institute web site at
http://www.stanford.edu/group/IRWG/, or contact Courtney Bennett at ben
nettc@stanford.edu.
