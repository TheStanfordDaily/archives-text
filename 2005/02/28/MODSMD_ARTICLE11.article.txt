# Calendar of events
## 
### 
Monday, February 28
"Conversations on Contemporary French Cinema,"
6 p.m.
This film screening will feature Arnaud Desplechin's
"Leo, en jouant dans la compagnie des Hommes" fol
lowed hy a discussion with the director. Cubberley Au
ditorium, 485 Lasuen Mall, Stanford Campus. This
event is free and open to the public. Contact: Conver
sations on Contemporary French Cinema, 650 KO4
4832, mireille@stanford.edu.
Author Candace Kushnell of "Sex and the City," 7:30
p.m.
Hear the author of "Sex and the City" — the real-life
Carrie Bradshaw — discuss her famous books and the
world they depict. Kresge Auditorium. Contact:
ASSU Speakers Bureau, 650-497-1739, jmeltz@stan
ford.edu.
Musical Performance: Flainc Ihornhurgh on Harpsi
chord, 8 p.m.
Tunes, Fantasias and Improvisations — works by
Byrd, Froberger, and Frescobaldi. Memorial Church

Chapel. Contact: Department of Music, (650) 723-
2720, baraka@stanford.edu.
"Just Jane" — A Cabaret, 8 p.m.
This cabaret show commemorates the death of Jane
Stanford on Feb. 28, 1905. Discover Jane's long and
loving life and her mysterious death. Join Jane and her
ghosts for this event, staged on the very centenary of
her death. Pigott Theater, Memorial Auditorium.
Contact: Drama Department, 723-2576, ishy@stan
ford.edu.
Tuesday, March 1
"Imperial Hubris: Why the West is Losing the War on
Terror," noon
Michael Scheuer, former chief of the Counterterrorist
Center's Bin Laden unit and author of "Imperial
Hubris: Why the West is Losing the War on Terror,"
will discuss the flawed logic of the 'War on Terror' and
sign copies of his best-selling book Stanford Book
store. Contact: Coaltion for Justice in the Middle East,
408-390-4802, oshakir@stanford.edu.
Performance: "Nice Jewish Girls Gone Bad," 8 p.m.
Hillel at Stanford presents "Nice Jewish Girls Gone
Bad," a night of comedy, music and burlesque, told by
the gals who learned to smoke at Hebrew School, got
drunk at their Bat-Mitzvahs and would rather have
more schtuppa than chupa. CoHo. Contact: Hillel at
Stanford, 724-2401, rittberg@stanford.edu.
More events can be found on the Events at Stanford
Web site, at http://events.stanford.edu. For information
about posting an event to Events at Stanford, contact
calendareditor@stanford. edu.
