### 
New Life for International Law?
The Role of International Courts and Tribunals in Promoting the
Rule of Law
In a series of panel discussions, eleven of the most prominent judges serving on the
most important international courts, including the International Court of Justice (the
"World Court"), the International Criminal Tribunals for Rwanda and the Former
Yugoslavia, the Permanent International Criminal Court, the European Court of
Justice, the European Court of Human Rights, and the Special Court for Sierra Leone,
will describe their experiences in trying to end human rights abuses and to promote
the rule of international law among states through the decisions of the courts on
which they sit.
Friday, March 18, 2005,
Room 290, Stanford Law School
9:15 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.
Free and open to the public.
For additional information about the participants and the precise schedule, please visit our
