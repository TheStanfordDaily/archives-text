# Synchro first at USCC
## Women end OSU dynasty with stellar performance 
### SAMUEL CHEN STAFF WRITER 
One way to put an end to a dynasty is to start
your own. This mindset was carried out to per
fection by Stanford's synchronized swimming
team, which took the reins of their fate and rode
to victory at the 2(X)5 United States Collegiate
Championships, held at the University of Florida
from March 24 to 26. The triumph marked the
first time in six years that Ohio State did not win
the title and the first time since 1999 that Stan
ford emerged from the competition at the top.
"We went into this meet focusing on what we
had control over — ourselves," Cardinal sopho
more Samantha Bongiovanni-Duclos said. "I
think our enthusiasm at the meet showed the
judges and other athletes how prepared we
were."
After a rough first day of preliminary compe
tition. Stanford was trailing Ohio State. Howev
er, beginning on day two, the Cardinal proceed
ed to sweep the top spot in every iinals competi
tion, taking at least two of the top three scores in
all but one event.
"This year, we didn't let jthe early deficit] get
to us." senior captain Katie Norris said. "We
knew we were the best and the meet wasn t over
until it was truly over."
On Friday, the Cardinal athletes kicked into
gear and took the top four spots in both the A el
ements and B figures events, with impressive
scores coming from all classes. Katie Norris took
the top spot in A elements, scoring an 82.4. Fol

lowing closely behind were sophomore Eliza
beth-Anne Markman, freshman Sara Lowe and
Bongiovanni-Duclos. who took the second, third
and fourth spots, respectively. Freshmen Courte
nay Stewart and Poppy Carlig also competed in
the event, finishing sixth and ninth in the finals,
respectively. In the B figures, freshman Kather
ine Norris led the field, trailed closely by team
mates freshman Hilary Homenko, sophomore
Mari Maekwa and freshman Hae-Rin Lee.
As Saturday competition kicked off, Stanford
showed no signs of losing any of the momentum
created by the previous day's performances, cap
turing the first and third spots in the trio finals.
The trio of Bongiovanni-Duclos, Stewart and
Lowe won me event with a score of 97.(MX). while
the team composed of Carlig. junior Cassidy Ra
mage and freshman Melissa Knight took home
the bronze medal. In the duet finals. Stewart and
Lowe combined to receive a score of 97.3(H),
good enough to edge Ohio State's top duo by
one-tenth of a point. Katie Norris and Bongio
vanni-Duclos finished a close third, scoring
97.(XX) points.
Norris was not yet done for the day. however,
as she captured the gold medal in her individual
swim in the solo finals, defeating second-place
finisher Becky Kim of Ohio State by over one
point. The team finals, the final event of the
meet, ensured Stanford the victory. The Cardinal
powerfully performed their final routine to earn
their highest score of the season. 99.200. The
Ohio State A and B teams came in second and
third, respectively.
In the final point tally. Stanford took the top
spot with 98.2, followed by Ohio State, which fin
ished with 90 points, significantly distancing the

two schools from the rest of the field.
The third-place finish went to the
University of the Incarnate World,
which finished with 67.5 points, and
the top five was rounded out with the
University of Alabama-Birmingham
and Canisius College.
With Katie Norris being the only
graduating senior on the young
Stanford team, the Cardinal said
they expect to maintain their top
spot and strive for even higher goals
in the future.
"Now that we have [completed a
sweep of the competition] once, there
is no reason why we shouldn't try to

do it again," Bongiovanni-Duclos
said.
Still, the event medals were not
the extent of the recognition impart
ed on the Cardinal athletes. Head
coach Heather Olson, a former
Stanford synchronized swimmer in
her third year at the helm, took
home Collegiate Coach of the Year
honors. Norris was named Colle
giate Athlete of the Year, while
Lowe was recognized as the High
point Athlete at the meet.
"Stanford is on the block, and now
the synchro world knows to look out
for our future victories," Norris said.
The Stanford synchronized
swimming team will strive to com
plete its undefeated season at its
iinal meet, the U.S. National Cham
pionships in Federal Way. Wash.,
from April 28 to 30.
