# Citations for public urination, drinking
## 
### 
This report covers a selection of crimes
from April 8 to April 13 as recorded in the
Stanford Police Department Public Bulletin.
Friday April 8
Sometime in the afternoon a vehicle on
Via Ortega was broken into and a HAM
radio, GPS receiver and duffle bag were re
ported missing.
A report stated that a disabled placard
was taken from a vehicle at Maples Pavilion
on the night April 2.
At 2:10 a.m. a student was cited and re
leased for urinating on the sidewalk in the
Tresidder Memorial Union parking lot.
At 2:20 a.m. a man was booked into San
Jose Main Jail for driving under the influ
ence of alcohol on Bowdoin Street.
Saturday April 9
At 6:20 p.m. it was reported that a male
touched a female's buttock twice while she
was studying in the East Wing of Green Li
brary.
At 9:15 p.m. a fire sprinkler broke in
Naranja, located in Lagunita, and flooded

a room.
At 9:35 p.m. a vehicle hit a light pole be
hind the Faculty Club, causing it to lean to
the side and expose electrical wiring.
At 1:40 a.m. three unknown suspects
threw beer bottles at a window in Faisan, lo
cated in Florence Moore Hall, causing the
window to crack.
Sunday April 10
At 3:30 p.m. a student's unattended purse
was taken from a table during Community
Day at Serra Mall and Main Quad.
Monday April 11
A golf cart was reported stolen and was
later found with minor damage.
At 10:00 a.m. a collision occurred on
Campus Drive between a vehicle and a stu
dent riding a bicycle.
Tuesday April 12
Sometime during the night an unknown
person tampered with the lock on the Tech
nical Communication Program office door
in Terman Engineering Center.

It was reported that a female received
three disturbing phone calls in Terman En
gineering Center from a male over the past
two days. At 7:28 p.m. the man was hooked
into the main jail for rthe possession of ille
gal weapons
Sometime between 3:15 p.m. and 7:00
p.m. a vehicle parked between Lasuen Mall
and Museum Way was broken into and the
car radio was stolen.
It was reported that an unknown person
entered an empty house and had a party on
the night of April 8.
At 3:30 a.m. a person was booked into the
San Jose Main Jail for being in possession of
a stolen bike.
Wednesday, April 13
Sometime between 6:00 a.m. and 12:00
p.m. an unknown suspect wrote a profanity
on the wall of an elevator in the McCullough
Building.
It was reported that there was black graf
fiti sprayed on the side of Wilbur Module A.
A female reported that she was pricked
by a laboratory syringe that was improperly
disposed of in the garbage can.
