What Matters to Me and Wnu
Mark Mancall
Professor of History, Emeritus
Wednesday, May 4, 2005
12:00 noon-1:00 P.M.
Side Chapel in Memorial Church.
Please feel free to bring your lunch.
Professor Mancall is an expert on the history, religions and cultures of Asia. Since coming to Stanford in
1%5, he has taught courses on Asian history and Buddhism. Professor Mancall directed the Overseas
Studies Program at Stanford from 1973 to 1985, and has led numerous Stanford Travel/Study journeys
to China, India, Nepal, Central Asia, Indonesia, Tibet and Bhutan.
What Mutters to Me and Why is a discussion and lecture series that encourages reflection within the Stanford
commwiity on mutters of personal values, beliefs, and motivations, in order to better understand the lives and
inspirations of those who shupe the University.
All are welcome!
For more information: 723-1762 or http://religiouslife.stanford.edu
