# Fight in Wilbur Dining; graffiti in Mirrielees
## 
### CAITLIN BORGER 
This report covers a selection
of crimes from April 28 to May 2
as recorded in the Stanford Police
Department Public Bulletin.
Thursday April 28
An unknown suspect damaged
a wall inside the Band Shack.
Friday, April 29
It was reported that a painting
was taken while it was on display at
the Bechtel International Center.
At 11:(M) a.m. an unknown sus
pect took two bags containing a
laptop and a video camcorder

from the rugby field.
At 12:35 p.m. a male student
was cited and released for display
ing a fraudulent disabled placard
at Lomita Court.
At !1:20 p.m. a male student
was cited and released for being a
minor in possession of alcohol on
Mayfield Avenue.
Saturday April 30
At 3:(X) a.m. an unknown sus
pect sprayed a fire extinguisher in
the dining room of Durand.
At 11:30 a.m. A person pushed
a male food service worker during
a verbal altercation that turned
physical at Wilbur Dining Hall.

The person was transported to the
Stanford University Medical Cen
ter fora finger injury.
At 9:15 p.m. a fire was started in
the courtyard of Crothers Hall
when a pile of papers was set
ablaze. No injuries or damage to
the structure were reported.
Sunday May 1
At 4:00 a.m. two students were
cited and released for having an
open container of alcohol in their
vehicle on Serra St.and El
Camino.
Sometime during the night an
unknown suspect spray-painted
graffiti on several doors in Mir

rielees.
It was reported that a master
key for the Rains Housing Com
plex was lost or misplaced.
Monday May 2
At 1:40 a.m. a dumpster out
side of Granada was on fire. The
Palo Alto Fire Department re
sponded and put out the fire.
Paintballs were fired on a vehi
cle parked in the Stanford Book
store loading dock and on the post
office window sometime over the
weekend.
An anonymous person report
ed that two females were assault
