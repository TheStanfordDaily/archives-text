The Center for Latin American Studies
invites you to a public lecture
"Two Complementary Visions of
Latin American Development"
by
Tinker Visiting Professor
Armando Di Filippo
Armando Di Filippo is a professor at the Universidad
Jesuita Alberto Hurtado de Chile and has taught for
Stanford in Santiago since winter 1999. He is an
economist devoted to the study of the economic
and social development of Latin America
and the author of two books.
Di Filippo has been a researcher and
adviser to the United Nations for
thirty years and written many reports
for the Economic Commission for
Latin America and the Caribbean.
In this talk, he will build a bridge between two
versions of institutional economics and discuss the
complementary nature of Douglass North and
Raul Prebisch's seminal works.
Wednesday, May 18
12:10 PM
Bolivar House, 582 Alvarado Row
