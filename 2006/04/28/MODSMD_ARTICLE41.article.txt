# POLICE BLOTTER
## Quad damaged, cacti left in a prickly situation 
### JENNIFER CHIN CONTRIBUTING WRITER 
This report covers a selection of crimes from
April 19 to April 25 as reported in the Stanford
Police Department Public Bulletin.
Wednesday, April 19
■ At 11:30 a.m. a construction employee dig
ging with a backhoe accidentally hit a natural
gas line at the intersection of Panama Street
and Via Ortega, breaking it.
■ Around 1:30 p.m. an unknown suspect took
a student's keys from outside their bedroom in
Lagunita's Eucalipto dorm.
Thursday, April 20
■ A male was arrested and booked at 6:15 p.m.
for trespassing and resisting/interfering with a
police officer. He was served with a second
stay-away letter.
Friday, April 21
■ Sometime between April 18 and April 21, a
vehicle window was vandalized while parked in
the Kappa Sigma lot. The victim suspects her
estranged husband was involved.
■ A suspicious, unattended briefcase was
found in Quarry Lot at 3:10 p.m. The owner
later went to the Stanford Police Station to
claim it.
■ At 3:45 p.m., a motor vehicle hit a bicyclist
near Campus Dr. and Santa Teresa. The bicy
clist went to Vaden Health Center for a medical
evaluation.
■ Police arrested three undergraduates partic
ipating in a demonstration against President

George W. Bush at
4:20 p.m. The students
were sitting in the
road, preventing a fire
engine from passing
through the intersec
tion of Serra St.and
Galvez.
■ Around 8:15 p.m.
an unknown suspect
stole a student's wal
let from a cubby in the
Arrillaga Family
Recreation Center.
Saturday, April 22
■ An unknown sus
pect damaged two
cacti in the Main
Quad during the
night.

■ At 1:20 p.m. a couple who have a child to
gether engaged in a verbal dispute. The argu
ment concerned the man's failure to return the
night before.
Sunday, April 23
■ During the night, an unknown suspect broke
the windshield and slit all four tires of a car
parked near Peter Coutts Hill.
■ Between 5 a.m. and 5:20 a.m. an unknown
suspect stole four sweaters from a male under
graduate in Florence Moore's Paloma lounge.
■ At 4:45 p.m. a female student lost her front
license plate in the Law School parking lot be
cause it was not secured properly.
■ At 5:45 p.m. a male was cited for driving with
a suspended license. He was booked into the
main jail for having two outstanding warrants
out of Newark.
Monday, April 24
■ An unknown suspect keyed a vehicle
parked near Stern Hall between 9 a.m. and 5
p.m.
■ Around 11 a.m. an individual with an active
stay-away letter from Stanford was found on
campus by Lyman Graduate Residence and
booked for trespassing.
