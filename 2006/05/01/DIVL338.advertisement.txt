Professor Emerita of English
Marjorie Perloff writes on contemporary and
avant-garde poetry and poetics, as well as on
intermedia and the visual arts. Her many books
include Poetic License: Essays on Modernist and
Postmodernist Lyric, The Poetics of Indeterminacy:
Rimbaud to Cage, The Futurist Moment Avant-Carde,
Avant-Guerre, and the Language of Rupture, and
Wittgenstein's Ladder. Professor Perloff has recently
published her cultural memoir The Vienna Paradox
which has been widely discussed. She has written
widely for periodicals, such as The Washington Post
mum an( j as we || as ma j or scholarly journals. She is
a member of the American Academy of Arts and
Sciences and is the current President of the
* Modern Language Association.
