# POLICE BLOTTER
## Small fires, suspicious cars and juvenile jubilee 
### ANDREA FULLER STAFF WRITER 
This report covers a selection of
crimes from May 26 to May 31 as
recorded in the Stanford Police
Department Public Bulletin.
Friday, May 26
■ At 11:40 p.m. an unidentified fe
male was reportedly put into a car
by several unidentified males. A
search was conducted throughout
the campus, but no vehicle match
ing the description was found.
■ At 9:00 p.m. a power cord ignit
ed after a laboratory pump was
plugged in at the Beckman Center
for Molecular and Genetic Medi
cine. A fire extinguisher was used
to put out the fire.
Saturday, May 27
■ At 1:00 a.m. A disturbance was
reported between Luce security

and another group during a party
at Kappa Sigma House.
■ At 4:35 p.m. a juvenile was cited
and released for stealing a bike.
Another was also cited and re
leased for misappropriation of
found property.
Sunday, May 28
■ At 11:20 p.m. six juveniles were
cited and released for trespassing
and for violating curfew. One party
was also cited for displaying an im
itation firearm.
Monday, May 29
■ At 10:40 a.m. a driver lost con
trol of his vehicle and hit two trees.
No injuries were reported.
Tuesday, May 30
■ Sometime during the day, a vehi
cle's rear tail-light lens and left front
blinker lens were stolen and tam
pered with near Parking Structure 1.

■ At 7:50 p.m. a pellet gun and
jacket were found on a walking
path near the Enchanted Broccoli
Forest.
Wednesday, May 31
■ Between 9:00 and 9:45 a.m. a
student's chemistry lab book was
taken from the inside basket of her
bike.
