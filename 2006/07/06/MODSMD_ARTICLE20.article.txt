# POLICE BULLETIN
## 
### DREW BYWATER 
Theft. Choking. Refusing to pay
cab fare. This report covers
selected crimes from June 6 to
July 2 as recorded in the
University Public Bulletin.
Monday, June 12
At 11:30 p.m., an empty,
abandoned beer keg was found
in the Quad. It had no indication
of ownership and was seized as
lost property.
Tuesday, June 13
At 10:35 p.m., a suspect steal
ing cans from a recycling bin was
found to have an active warrant,
and was later booked in the main
jail-
Wednesday, June 14
At 8:10 a.m., "X" reported
that he was followed by an older,
unidentifiable adult white
female.
Thursday, June 15
At 10:15 p.m., a victim's purse
was stolen in the Quad while
attending Senior Dinner.
Tuesday, June 20
At 3:00 p.m., an unknown

male asked to trade bikes with
another person. The accepting
party is now suspicious that the
bike is stolen property.
Thursday, June 22
At 7:50 a.m., a large tree limb
fell on an unoccupied vehicle.
Monday, June 26
At 8:51 a.m., person "P" was
choking on a pen cap that she
had swallowed. She was treated
by the Palo Alto Fire
Department, but declined fur
ther transportation and medical
attention.
At 6:00 a.m., a vehicle's front,
passenger-side window was
smashed, and items were stolen
from inside.
Tuesday, June 27
At 6:45 p.m., a victim report
ed that an unknown man entered
her room, threatened her with a
knife, and proceeded to kiss her
neck and face for a few minutes
before leaving.
Friday, June 30
At 4:00 a.m., an unknown
person refused to pay cab fare
upon arrival at the Sigma Alpha
Epsilon house.
