# ISRAEL:
|J Between Love
/112 v and Darkness
January 22, 2007 8:00 PM
k Kresge Auditorium
Stanford University
Professor Amos Oz is an award-winning author of many novels,
essays and short stories including A Tale of Love and Darkness,
The Same Sea, My Michael, and How to Cure a Fanatic. Originally
written in Hebrew, his work has been translated into 37 languages.
Oz is the winner of the Israel prize, the Prix Femina Etranger
(France's top literary award), England's Wingate Prize, and the
prestigious Goethe prize.
The lecture is free and open to the public.
For more information, please call The Taube Center for Jewish Studies
650-725-7589
