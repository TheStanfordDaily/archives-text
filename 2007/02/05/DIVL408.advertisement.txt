Fred Turner
Assistant Professor of Communication
K Fred Turner's research and teaching focus on digital media,
journalism and the intersection of media and American
cultural history. He is the author of two books: From
Counterculture to Cyberculture: Stewart Brand, the
Whole Earth Network and the Rise of Digital Utopianism
(2006) and Echoes of Combat:The Vietnam War in American
Memory (1996; Revised 2nd ed. 2001). His essays have largely
explored questions of media and cultural change and have
tackled topics ranging from the rise of reality crime television
to the countercultural roots of the idea of virtual community.
He also worked as a journalist for ten years. His news stories,
features and reviews have appeared in a variety of venues,
including the Pacific News Service, the Boston Phoenix and
the Boston Sunday Globe Magazine.
(Wednesday, February 7, 7 p.m.
