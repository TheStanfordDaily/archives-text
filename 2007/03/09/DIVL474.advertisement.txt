Teaching Opportunity
Offered spring quarter, E100— Teaching Public Speaking explores
the theory and practice of teaching public speaking and trains
students to serve as instructors in the Technical Communications
Program's public speaking courses, E100 offers a hands-on
introduction to designing an effective course, devising tutorial
techniques, and teaching delivery, organization, audience analysis,
visual aids, and style. Students deliver lectures, lead discussions,
critique each other's speeches, and take part in fieldwork.
If you have exceptional experience in debate or drama or public
speaking or teaching, take E100, become a speech instructor in
Stanford's premier public speaking course, and earn at least $14.00
an hour teaching students from all disciplines.
Five units. Meets M, 7:30-10:00 p.m. and Th, 12:15-1:05.
Admission by consent of instructor. Arrange for an interview, and
receive additional information from Dave Lougee in Terman 105
(723-2573 or dlougee@stanford.edu ).

Offered spring quarter, E103 —Public Speaking introduces students
to the full range of speaking activities, from impromptu talks to
carefully rehearsed, formal professional presentations. E103 teaches
students to
• combat nervousness
• organize and write speeches tor a variety of occasions
• analyze audiences
• create computer presentations using appropriate visual aids
• deliver informative and persuasive speeches effectively
Three units. Three sections: T, W, or Th, 7:30-10:00 pm. Meets in
Thornton 110.
Enrollment is limited. E103 is open to all students, but priority is
given to engineering students. Signups: End-quarter Period, Finals,
and April 2 in Terman 201.
For more information: http://soe.stanford.edu/tcp/ or talk to Dave
Lougee in Terman 105 (723-2573 or dlougee@stanford.edu ).
