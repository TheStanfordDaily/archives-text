Hilton Obenzinger
Associate Director of the Hume Writing
Center, Honors and Advanced Writing
Hilton Obenzinger has been hosting How I Write
conversations for the past five years. Now the time has come
to celebrate - and to turn the tables. First there will be a
reception celebrating five years of How I Write, and then
Kevin DiPirro, playwright and lecturer in the Program in
Writing and Rhetoric, will engage Hilton Obenzinger in
conversation on his own writing.
Hilton Obenzinger, Associate Director of the Hume Writing
Center, Honors and Advanced Writing, writes literary
criticism, history, fiction, and poetry, receiving the American
Book Award and other honors. His books include a*hole:a
novel, How I Survived the Holocaust as Told by Zosia Goldberg,
American Palestine: Melville, Twain and the Holy Land Mania,
Cannibal Eliot and the Lost Histories of San Francisco, New York
on Fire, and This Passover Or The Next I Will Never Be In
Jerusalem. Busy Dying, an autobiographical novel, will soon be
released.
