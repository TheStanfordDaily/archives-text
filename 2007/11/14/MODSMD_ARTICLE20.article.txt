# Strong start for Card wrestling squad
## Team dominant in dual meets and tournaments 
### DENIS GRIFFIN DESK EDITOR 
Since the wrestle-offs to deter
mine the squad's starting lineup.
Stanford wrestling has hit the ground
running to open its 2007-08 season
with strong showings at tournaments
and a dominating dual meet victory'
over Fort Hays State.
"I think it's going well — I think
our guys are getting better, 1 think the
attitude and the effort is high," third
year Stanford coach Kerry McCoy
said. "Some things we're doing really
well, some things we're not doing so
well, so we have to continue to work,
that's something I keep telling our
guys.
"I don't want to be saying it the
entire year, because we want to be
moving forward towards our goal,"
he continued. "But that's just the na

ture of our sport. Our guys have the
right attitude and are improving and
willing to work, and when you have
that then we're going to he success
ful."
On Nov. 3, the Cardinal sent a
bevy of young wrestlers to compete
locally in the Menlo Open for an
early season tune-up. Nine Stanford
athletes went undefeated at the
event, including five starters: redshirt
freshmen Justin Paulsen at 133
pounds and Lucas Espericueta at 149
pounds, sophomores Peter Miller at
165 pounds and Jake Johnson at 197
pounds and heavyweight senior Phil
Doerner. True freshmen Nick
Aniuchastegui. Max Rosefigura,
Murdoch Miller and Matt Winter
bourne also finished the day without
a loss.
Next up for the Cardinal was a trip
to Fort Hays State in Hays, Kansas,
where senior captain Tanner Gard
ner pinned his first opponent of the
year wrestling in his old high school
gymnasium at 125 pounds. The No. 5

The Stanford wrestling team has shown depth in its successes so far this season. At the Menlo Open on Nov. 3, nine Cardinal athletes went undefeated, and
seven of ten Stanford wrestlers won their matches in a dual meet at Fort Hays State. The Card next compete at the Fullerton Open this Saturday.
two-time Ail-American and Berry
ton, Kan. native downed the Tigers'
Kyle Patton at 5:42 to get his season
off to a rollicking start.
Fellow defending All-American
senior captain. No. 6 Josh Zupancic
also earned a win in his first action of
the season, shutting out his opponent
for a 4-0 victory. Seven of 10 Stanford
wrestlers won their matches on the
day. with Johnson and Miller defeat
ing opponents ranked fourth and
sixth, respectively, at the Division II
level, as the Cardinal rolled to a 26-9
opening win last Friday.
At the Central Missouri Open on
Sunday. Gardner and Zupancic con
tinued to dominate at the start of
their final season on the Farm. The
stellar pair both went undefeated in
their weight class to capture their
first tournament titles of the season.
Gardner pinned four more oppo
nents before shutting out Ben Ash
more of Oklahoma State in the title
match, earning Most Outstanding
Wrestler honors for the tournament.
Zupancic, meanwhile, went 5-0 on
the day to take home his second title
at the Open, after finishing first at
149 pounds in 2(X)5.
In the higher weight classes,a pair
of second year wrestlers, Johnson
and Zach Giesen at IS4 pounds, also
placed fourth and third for the Cardi
nal. respectively. Giesen. who quali
fied for the NCAA tournament as a
freshman last season, dominated in

three of his five matches, winning
once by pin, once by technical fall,
and once with an 8-1 major decision.
An 7-5 overtime loss to Jeremy Hud
son in his third match of the day put
the tournament championship just
out of reach.
Johnson, meanwhile, continued
his highly encouraging start to the
season with a 5-2 showing at the tour
nament. ITie sophomore looks to be
come a key part of Stanford's lineup
this season after moving up to the 197
pound weight class during the sum
mer.
"Jake's done a great job," McCoy
said. "He's been moving up. in
essence two weight classes, and he re
ally pushes everybody he's wrestling.
I don't care who it is. he's pushing
them to the limit. And he went
against a bunch of guys this weekend
and just broke 'em. mentally and
physically."
ITiis Saturday. Stanford wrestlers
will take part in the Fullerton Open
where they will get their first chance
to square off against Pac-10 confer
ence competition this season.
"It's going to be the first time we
get to see the majority of our confer
ence competition, and it's always
been a tough tournament," McCoy
said. "We haven't done as well as we
would have liked to in the past two
years since I've been here, so we're
iooking to improve our performance
in that.
"We'll have a good number of
guys competing and I'm looking for
ward to seeing everyone in action."
