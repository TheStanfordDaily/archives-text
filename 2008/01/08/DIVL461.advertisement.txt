I 1331 THE FREEMAN SPOCLI INSTITUTE FOR I
INTERNATIONAL STUDIES AT STANFORD UNIVERSITY I
I I The Freeman Spogli Institute for International Studies (FSI) is
Stanford University's primary forum for interdisciplinary
consideration of major international issues and challenges.
FSI consists of four major research centers:
° Center on Democracy, Development, and the Rule of Law
(CDDRL)
° Center for Health Policy/Center for Primary Care and
Outcomes Research (CHP/PCOR)
° Center for International Security and Cooperation (CISAC)
° Walter H. Shorenstein Asia-Pacific Research Center
(Shorenstein APARC)
FSI also has eight programs: the Forum on Contemporary Europe
; (FCE), the Initiative on Distance Learning (IDL), the Inter-
University Center for Japanese Language Studies (lUC), the
Program on Energy and Sustainable Development (PESD), the
Program on Food Security and the Environment (FSE), the
Program on Global justice (PGJ), the Stanford Center on
International Conflict and Negotiation (SCICN), and the Stanford j
Program on International and Cross-Cultural Education (SPICE). I
FSI does not grant degrees, but has active teaching programs for
graduate and undergraduate students. It has two honors
programs for undergraduates: the CISAC Interschool Honors
Program in International Security Studies and the CDDRL
Honors Program.
FSl's centers and programs hold weekly seminars and lectures on j
topical issues open to students, faculty, and staff. We welcome
your involvement. To subscribe to our weekly events listing,
please email Aysha Rajput at arajput@stanford.edu.
For more information, please visit our website:
http*y/fsi.stanford.edu
