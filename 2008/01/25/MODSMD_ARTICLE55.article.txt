BOOKS IN THE CDC LIBRARY:
Hollywood 101: The Film Industry, by Frederick Levy
• Inside stories and advice from key players. Covers job titles
such as producer, director, writer, talent agent, or any other
behind-the-camera professional.
Careers for Film Buffs & Other Hollywood Types by Jog Greenspon
• Careers in these movie company departments are covered:
camera and sound, grip and electric, set design, makeup
and costumes, animation, special effects, editing, and pro
duction. Specific jobs such as location scout, dance chore
ographer, property master, film journalist, and assistant
director are described.

Career Opportunities in the Film Industry, by Fred and Jan Yager
• Covers studio and independent producers, directors,
financing, legal issues, camera work, music, film editing,
special effects, sound editing, publicity and advertising.
Includes education and training resources, professional
unions and associations, fellowships and internships, and
film festivals and commissions.
Reel Directory: Northern California Resource Guide for Film, Video,
and Multi-Media, by Bonnie Carroll
• Directory of ad agencies, crew, post production, equip
ment, sets-props-costumes-special effects, animation,
music and sound, talent services, internet video & multime
dia, and more. Also see www.reeldirectory.com/listings/
