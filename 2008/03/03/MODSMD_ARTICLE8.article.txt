# World & Nation
## Calendar 
### 
The Global Climate and Energy Project (GCEP) — five-year anniversary
GCEP Director Lynn Orr will highlight research activities and re
view results from the first five years of this project. GCEP is a
$225-million effort that seeks new solutions to providing the ener
gy the world needs in a way that protects the environment. Orr's
talk will be followed by a discussion on future directions for GCEP
led by a panel of distinguished experts in the area of energy re
search.
3:30 p.m. ■ 5:30 p.m.— Hewlett Auditorium, Room 200
Just Say 'No' to Government Medicine
Deroy Murdock, a syndicated columnist, contributing editor to Na
tional Review Online, and a Hoover Media Fellow, will give a talk
entitled "Just Say 'No' to Government Medicine."
7:30 p.m. ■ 8:45 p.m. — Cypress Room, Tresidder Union
In Defense of Food: The Omnivore's Solution
Author and journalist Michael Pollan questions what we should
eat. Pollan traces the rise and triumph of "nutritionism" and the
Western Diet, before proposing an alternative approach to eating
that promises to improve both our health and the health of the en
vironment.
7:30 p.m. - 8:30 p.m. — Kresge Auditorium
Good Kurds, Bad Kurds
Join Pulitzer Prize nominated journalist Kevin McKiernan for the
presentation of his award winning film about the Kurds, who are
one of the largest ethnic groups in the world without their own
state.
7:00 p.m. - 8:30 p.m. — Oak West, Tresidder Union
