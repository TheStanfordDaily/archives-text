# Biology prof. awarded environmental achievement prize
## 
### THE DAILY NEWS STAFF 
Biology Prof. Harold Mooney was recently awarded
the 2(X)8 Tyler Prize for Environmental Achievement
due to his work on evolution theory.
The prize, which includes $200,000 and gold medals,
will he split with Environmental Engineering Professor
James Galloway of University of Virginia.
"Mooney provided key evidence for the theory of
convergent evolution." said Robert Simoni. chair of the
biology department, to the Palo Alto News. "[lt] holds
that different species in widely separated ecosystems,
but with similar climates, all tend to evolve toward simi
lar endpoints."
The late John and Alice established the award
in 1973.
