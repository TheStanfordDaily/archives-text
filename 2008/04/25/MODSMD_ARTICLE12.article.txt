# Police Blotter
## 
### SHELLEY GAO STAFF WRITER 
This report covers a selection
of crimes from April 17 to April
20 as recorded in the Stanford
Police Department Public
Bulletin.
THURSDAY, APRIL 17
■ Between 9:30 p.m. on
Wednesday, April 16 and 8
a.m. on Thursday. April 17, a
reporting party discovered
property damage: a cracked
window in Building 360. It may
have been caused accidentally
by frisbee golf players.
■ An instance of grand theft that
occurred between Feb. 25 and
Mar. 12 was reported on
Thursday, April 17. A new fiber
channel networking switch was
stolen from a storage room
before it was installed. There
are currently no suspects.
■ A sex offense occurred. The
arrestee was taken to the main
jail and booked for sexual
assault. No further details are
provided.
FRIDAY, APRIL 18
■ Between 5:12 p.m. on
Thursday, April 17 and 9:45
a.m. on Friday, April 18, a vio
lation of a restraining order
occurred at University Drive,
Menlo Park. The reporting
party and victim reported the
suspect violated a court order

by making contact with them
via e-mail.The suspect is still at
large.
■ Between 9 a.m. and 10 a.m.,
shoplifting occurred at the
Bookstore. ITie arrestee was
cited and released for petty
theft of textbooks.
SATURDAY, APRIL 19
■ At 8 p.m., an unknown male
approached the reporting
party and friend in Meyer
Library and asked about their
future job plans. The unknown
male engaged in suspicious
behavior and attempted to
obtain names and addresses
from them.
■ Between 8 p.m. on Friday,
April 18 and 1:20 a.m. on
Saturday, April 19, a burglary
occurred at Jerry. An unknown
suspect or suspects pried open
a screen of an open ground
floor window and stole the vic
tim's iPod from a desk. An
attempt was also made to steal
a laptop which was secured
with a cable lock. Footprints
on the window ledge were
taken.
SUNDAY, APRIL 20
■ A missing person report was
filed at 3 a.m. The Kimball resi
dent was subsequently located,
and the incident is unfounded.

stanford.edu.
