# Police Blotter
## 
### SHELLEY GAO STAFF WRITER 
This report covers a selection
of crimes from April 21 to April
27 as recorded in the Stanford
Police Department Public
Bulletin.
MONDAY, APRIL 21
■ At Terman Engineering
Center, a suspicious circulation
of email occurred. The report
ing party received a non
threatening, but nonetheless
disturbing, email at 7:03 p.m.
■ Between 9 a.m. and 10 a.m., an
unknown suspect or suspects
stole the victim's wallet from a
lecture hall at Building 420.
■ A deer was euthanized after
being hit by a vehicle between
noon and 5:13 p.m. at Stanford
Avenue.
TUESDAY, APRIL 22
■ Parking Structure 5 witnessed
two crimes. Between 8:30 a.m.
and 5:40 p.m., unknown sus
pects broke the driver's side
front window, unlocked the
door and stole the victim's car
radio.
■ At 7:30 p.m., an unknown sus
pect or suspects entered the
victim's locked vehicle by pry
ing the driver's side front win
dow. The suspect or suspects
stole the stereo and air condi
tioning unit from the console.
WEDNESDAY, APRIL 23
■ At 1:16 a.m., Stanford
Deputies assisted Palo Alto
Police Department units with
the identification and appre
hension of a felony hit and run
suspect.
THURSDAY, APRIL 24
■ Dormitory burglary occurred
between 12:01 a.m. and 1 a.m.
when the victim's laptop was
stolen from his unlocked room
at Kappa Alpha.
■ ABM custodians located and
recovered property from
Room 115 of Roble Gym.

There was no indication of
ownership. Ilie property was
brought to Stanford
Department of Public Safety
and turned over for destruc
tion.
FRIDAY, APRIL 25
■ Petty theft occurred at
Tressider between 11:10 a.m.
and 11:40 a.m. when items
from the victim's purse were
stolen by unknown suspect or
suspects.
■ Between 7 a.m. and 3:21 p.m.,
assault took place at 283
Campus West. The victim was
struck on the head by the sus
pect. No injury was sustained.
■ At 11:45 a.m. outside the Press
Building, the reporting party
observed a case of vehicle tem
pering. The suspect sat in the
driver's seat attempting to
operate a golf cart that did not
belong to him.
■ Misappropriation of property
occurred at 1:53 p.m. outside
Dinkelspiel Auditorium. Hie
suspect found the victim's
bike, appropriating it for his
personal use.
SATURDAY, APRIL 26
■ Between 6:15 p.m. and 7 p.m.,
the victim's wallet was stolen
at the counter of the Tree
House.
■At 8:05 p.m., the reporting
party received a suspicious
email in Building 420.
■ An unknown suspect broke a
large window at the Kappa
Alpha House at 6:45 p.m.
SUNDAY, APRIL 27
■In Parking Structure 2
between 4 p.m. and 7:05 p.m.,
the victim and companion
returned to their vehicle to
find the doors unlocked. An
iPod and a Macbook laptop
computer were missing. Latent
prints were found on the rear
hatch.

Contact Shelley Gao at sxgao@
stanford.edu.
