About Philip Taubman
Philip Taubman became associate editor for The New York Times in March 2007. In addition Mr. Taubman
has taken on the role of special correspondent for a reporting assignment in the area of national security.
Previously he served as Washington bureau chief of The New York Times since August 2003. Mr. Taubman
was deputy editor of the editorial page from 2002 and assistant editorial page editor from 1994 to 2002. He was
deputy national editor from 1993 to 1994 and deputy Washington editor from 1989 to 1992. Mr. Taubman was
based in Moscow from 1985 until the end of 1988, covering the first turbulent years of Mikhail Gorbachev's
tenure as Soviet leader. He served as Moscow bureau chief from 1986 to 1988. Mr. Taubman graduated with a
B.A. degree in history from Stanford in 1971 and was editor of the campus newspaper, The Stanford Daily. He
was a member of the University's Board of Trustees from 1978 to 1982.
