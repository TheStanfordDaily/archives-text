# Police Blotter
## 
### ERIC MESSINGER SENIOR STAFF WRITER 
This report covers a selection of crimes reported
from July 7 to July 22 as recorded in the Stanford
Police Department Public Bulletin.
A series of bike thefts occurred during this time.
JULY 8
■ An incident was reported involving Princeton
University in which an unknown suspect sent a
threatening letter through the U.S. Postal Service.
■ Between 12 p.m. on July 7 and 9:45 p.m. on July 8,
a juvenile received an annoying text message in
an incident somehow involving four people.
■ A victim who had borrowed a bike from another
person reported it stolen between 9 a.m. on July 7
and 6:30 p.m. on July 8.
JULY 10
■ At 1 p.m., a delivery vehicle backed into a parked,
unattended vehicle near Branner Hall.
■ Between 12 p.m. and 5 p.m., a victim's locked
bike was stolen from a bike rack left of the front
door of Hoover Tower, prompting the swift
release of the hounds.
■ Between 2 p.m. on July 9 and 1:55 p.m. on July 10,
a vehicle at Parking Structure 5 had its driver-side
window smashed and the stereo stolen.

■ At 4:50 p.m. at the Escondido II high-rise, a vic
tim received harassing emails from another per
son.

locked vehicle in order to steal a computer case.
■ At 1:45 p.m. a* the Row & Cluster on 672 Lomita
Drive, a theft of a department-rented golf cart
was reported.
■ Between 10 a.m. and 5:45 p.m. an unknown per
son used a fake $100 bill at the Stanford Store in
Tresidder Memorial Union.
JULY 12
■ At Lantana between 6 p.m. and 6:15 p.m., a victim

her key ring while she was showering.
■ Between 9 a.m. and 3 p.m. at 625 Serra, an
unknown suspect helped himself/herself to a lap
top from the backseat of a victim's unlocked,
unattended vehicle.
■ At 2:20 a.m., a patient sustained injuries when he
fell on the stairs at Meyer Library. The patient
walked it off and declined transport to the
Stanford Emergency Room.
JULY 13
■ At 7:47 a.m., an arrestee was cited and released
for not properly restraining his dog at Serra and
Campus Drive.

■ A stolen vehicle was recovered at the Hoover
Pavilion at 7:25 p.m.

p.m., a person enjoying a tasty beverage from
Jamba Juice had an allergic reaction to it, at
which point the patient was transported to the
Stanford Medical Center and had his/her parents
notified.
A person injured himself by falling over a bike rack
at Escondido Road and Arguello Mall at 12:30 p.m.
The patient was transported to Stanford Hospital,
and the bike rack remains at large.
JULY 15

between a bike and a golf cart, observed by two

JULY 19
