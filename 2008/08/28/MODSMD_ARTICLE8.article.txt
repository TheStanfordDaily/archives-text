of crimes reported from Aug. 16
to 26 as recorded in the Stanford

Bulletin.

during this time.
AUG. 15
■ At 3:20 p.m., a company con
tracted to remove wire from
the Serra buildings reported
that the wire was missing, pass
ing on the opportunity for an
easy work day.

summer camp students
climbed on top of Memorial

their information in case there
was damage to the roof.

Center, a suspect was observed
attempting to steal a bike. The
suspect was found to possess
paraphernalia and no subtlety,
and was arrested on outstand
ing warrants.

Blvd. and Stanford Ave., a

resulted in an injury, with no
word yet on the victor.
■ Between 1 and 1:45 a.m. at
Mirrielees, a cell phone was
stolen.
AUG. 17
■ At 12:40 a.m., a person was
cited for being a minor in pos
session of alcohol after falsely
identifying him/herself. Two
victims were reported, presum
ably traumatized after learning
their companion's identity.

Haus Mitteleuropa, 14 unse
cured laptops and an Xbox 360
were stolen, the criminal hon
oring traditionally high
German standards.

■ Between 4:10 and 5 p.m., a vic
tim's clothing was stolen from
an unsecured hallway closet in
Naranja.
■ Between 11 a.m. and 12:30
p.m., an unknown suspect
entered a restroom at Mudd
Chemistry building and caused
$400 worth of structural dam
age by an act of vandalism.
■At 12 a.m. at Lane L and
Governor's Ave., a non-injury,
hit-and-run, vehicle-versus
vehicle, hyphen-abusing colli
sion occurred.

AUG. 19
■ At 8:55 a.m. at Campus Drive
West and Searsville Rd., a
vehicle-versus-vehicle-versus
vehicle collision occurred, hut
no injuries were reported.
AUG. 20
■ Between 11:30 a.m. and 8:10
p.m. at Parking Structure 6. a
vehicle's window was smashed
and a cell phone was stolen.
■ Between June 21 and Aug. 20,
in an incident at Florence
Moore Hall, an unknown sus
pect placed a suspicious hand
written letter on the Paloma
door. The letter, oddly, was
described as being signed by
someone who was not the per
son who wrote it.
AUG. 21
■ At 3:10 p.m.. the window of a
vehicle at Parking Structure 5
was smashed. The victim was
out of town and could not ver
ify if any items had been
stolen.
■ At 7:37 a.m. at the Escondido
II Highrise. an unknown sus
pects) entered the basement's
equipment room and stole a

AUG. 22
■ Between 4:30 and 5 p.m. a per
son at Durand was arrested on
an outstanding warrant.
■ Between 2 and 2:30 p.m. at
Durand, an unknown suspect
stole six iMac monitors, possi
bly bringing attention to the
above.
AUG. 23
■ Between 10 p.m. on Aug. 22
and 9 a.m. on Aug. 23, an
unknown suspect stole a laptop
and keys from an unlocked
Rains apartment.
AUG. 24
■ At 4:45 p.m. at the De Guerre
Pools and Courts, a sports bag
containing personal belongings
was stolen by unknown sus
pects.
AUG. 25
■ A victim reported a threaten
ing phone call received

dumpster, leading to 20 min
utes of mutually confused
