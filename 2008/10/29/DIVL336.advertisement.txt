Fall 2008 Tanner Lectures
Origins of Human Cooperation
Michael Tomasello
(Max Planck Institute for Evolutionary Anthropology)
October 29 / 5:30 - 7:00 pm / Stanford Humanities Center
Lecture I: Ontogenetic Origins of Human Altruism
Tomasello examines one of the great debates in Western civilization: are humans
horn co-operative and helpful and society corrupts them, or are they horn selfish
and unhelpful and society teaches them to he better?
Discussion I: October 30 / 10:00 am - 12:00 pm / Landau Economics Bldg, SIEPR A
Respondents: Carol Dweck (Stanford Psychology) / Elizabeth Spelke (Harvard Psychology)
October 30 / 5:30 - 7:00 pm / Stanford Humanities Center
Lecture II: Phylogenetic Origins of Human Collaboration
Cooperation is evolutionarily "easy" if participants benefit and cheating is difficult
but humans collaborate in complex ways. Tomasello explains the psychological
processes that support our earliest forms of collaboration.
Discussion II: October 31 / 10:00 am - 12:00 pm / Landau Economics Bldg, SIEPR A
Respondents: Brian Skyrms (Stanford Philosophy) / Joan Silk (UCLA Anthropology)
