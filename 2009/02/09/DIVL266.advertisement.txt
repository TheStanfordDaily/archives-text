Terry Castle
Professor of English, Walter A. Haas Professor of the Humanities
Terry Castle specializes in the history of the novel, especially the works of Defoe,
Richardson, and Fielding, and in the study of eighteenth-century popular culture. She has
written seven books: Clarissa's Ciphers: Meaning and Disruption in Richardson's
'Clarissa' (1982); Masquerade and Civilization: The Camivalesque in Eighteenth-Century
English Culture and Fiction (1986); The Apparitional Lesbian: Female Homosexuality and
Modern Culture (1993); The Female Thermometer: Eighteenth-Century Culture and the
Invention of the I 'ncanny (1995), Noel Coward and Radcftffe Hall: Kindred Spirits
(1996): Boss Ladies, Watch Out! Essays on Women, Sex, and Writing (2002); and
Courage, Mm Amie (2002). She is the editor of The Literature of Lesbianism: A
Historical Anthology from Ariosto to Stonewall (2003). She has received a number of
awards and writes regularly for the London Review of Hooks, the New Republic; and
other magazines and journals.Terry Castle's book of autobiographical essays, The
Professor and Other Essays will be appearing this year. She also produces a visual art blog.
Fevered Brain Productions, at http://terry-castk-blog.blogspot.com
