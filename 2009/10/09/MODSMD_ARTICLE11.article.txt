# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### JANE LEPHAM CONTRIBUTING WRITER 
This report covers a selection of
crimes reported from Sept. 23 to Oct.
6 as recorded in the Stanford Police
Department Public Bulletin.
A series of bike thefts and alco
hol-related incidents occurred during
this time.
WEDNESDAY, SEPT. 23
■ Between 4:15 p.m. and 5:20 p.m.
outside of Bldg. 260, Victim #l's
locked bike was stolen from the
bike racks east of Bldg. 260. Victim
#2's lock was also cut, but the bike
was left behind.
■ Between 9:30 a.m. and 11 a.m. at
Narnia house, an unknown suspect
reached through an open window
of a dormitory room, but was
frightened away by the occupant
of the room.
THURSDAY, SEPT. 24
■ln an incident that occurred
between 7 a.m. and 9:45 p.m. on
the previous day, an unknown sus
pect stole the victim's "A" permit
after finding the vehicle key on the
ground next to the vehicle. The vic
tim had inadvertently dropped the
key.
SATURDAY, SEPT. 26
■At 12:22 a.m. outside of Casa
Italiana, an arrestee was cited and
released for being a public nui
sance. The arrestee was also caught
urinating in public giving new
meaning to the phrase "make it
rain."
■ At 8:40 p.m. at Arboretum Road at
Arboretum Grove, a large oak tree
fell in the grove area causing dam
age to nine parked vehicles during
the Stanford Football Game. The
tree had heroically aimed for visit
ing team vehicles.
SUNDAY, SEPT. 27
■ At 1:50 a.m. at Kappa Sigma, an
individual was cited and released
for taking the victim's television
"without consent."
■ln an incident that occurred
between 9 p.m. on Sept. 26 and 1
a.m. the next day, an unknown sus
pect spray-painted two walls inside
the Kappa Sigma house, causing
about $2OO in damage.
TUESDAY, SEPT. 29
■ Between 8:20 a.m. and 4:37 p.m. on
the 100 block of El Camino Real,

east of Embarcadero, an oak tree
fell because of high winds and
landed on a parked vehicle.
WEDNESDAY, SEPT. 30
■ In an incident that occurred on
Sept. 9 at the Old Union Surge
Trailer, an unknown suspect used a
University P-Card at three Wal-
Mart stores in West Virginia. The
purchases totaled approximately
$2,800.
THURSDAY, OCT. 1
■ In an incident that occurred on
Sept. 24 at the Enchanted Broccoli
Forest but reported on this day, a
routine background check uncov
ered an individual who had been
falsely using a victim's social secu
rity number.
■ At 2:45 p.m. a suspicious smolder
ing log outside of the Quarry
entrance gate on Old Page Mill
Road was extinguished.
■ At 9:10 p.m. in White Plaza, six stu
dents staged a fake robbery. No
one was hurt. The involved parties
were then located and asked ques
tions by real police officers.
FRIDAY, OCT. 2
■ In an incident that occurred
between 11:30 p.m. on Oct. 1 and
2:30 a.m. on this day at Sigma
Alpha Epsilon, a victim reported
his laptop missing, but later found
it misplaced in a friend's room.
Fraternity members celebrated
responsibly thereafter.
■ Between 5 p.m. on Oct.l and 8
a.m. on this morning, an exterior
window at the Language Center
was damaged by unknown means.
Although no entry was made into
the building, the Rosetta Stone
was reportedly found near the
scene.
■ At 2:45 p.m. a golf cart was stolen
from Memorial Church. It was
later recovered on Oct. 6 at Lane L
and Governors' Corner because
even with a golf cart, it takes four
days to get back to FroSoCo from
the Quad.
■ At 1 p.m. an unknown suspect
spray-painted letters on a wall
attached to Hoover Tower.
Stanford officials were notified to
repair damages.
■ At 6:25 p.m. an injured deer was
dispatched at the Stanford Golf
Course.
SATURDAY, OCT. 3
■ At 2:10 a.m. on Campus Drive at

Lane A, a victim was struck in the
face by a suspect. The suspect then
called the victim derogatory names
and then left the area.
■ Between 11:20 p.m. on Oct. 2 and
1:30 a.m. on this day, six separate
individuals were cited and
released for being minors in pos
session of alcohol at the corner of
Mayfield Ave. and Santa Ynez.
■ At 4:12 a.m. an individual was con
tacted at Meyer Library. He was
issued a Stanford University "stay
away" order. The other acknowl
edged the letter and departed cam
pus.
■ Between 1:30 p.m. on Oct. 1 and
9:30 a.m. on this day, an unknown
suspect(s) broke the driver's side
window and dented the door of the
victim's vehicle at the Varsity Lot.
Nothing was missing from the
vehicle.
MONDAY, OCT. 5
■ At 3 p.m., a large eucalyptus tree
fell onto two parked vehicles in the
parking lot at the southeast edge of
Studio 2 on 729 Escondido Road.
Both cars sustained minor dents
and scratches to their hoods and
roofs, the last skirmish in this
week's Attack of the Trees.
■ln an incident that occurred
between 9:02 p.m. and 9:15 p.m.
on Oct. 1, a suspect was seen by
the reporting party peeping into
windows of Narnia house. The
suspect fled on foot from the
scene.
■ Between 9 a.m. on Oct. 2 and 10:15
a.m. on this day, a vehicle was
parked illegally for several weeks
in the Galvez field. It was
processed as abandoned and
towed.
■ln an incident that occurred
between "Oct. 12,1991 and Oct. 4,
2009" at Stockfarm Lot 17, an
unknown suspect(s) entered the
victim's locked vehicle and stole a
stereo faceplate and an iPod auxil
iary jack.
TUESDAY, OCT. 6
■ Between 6:15 a.m. and 9 a.m. at
the corner of Nathan Abbott Way
and Lane A, a vehicle was cited
for multiple days in a row for
being parked in a red zone. The
vehicle had been reported stolen
from Palo Alto and the owner was
notified.
