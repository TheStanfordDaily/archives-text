# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### JANE LEPHAM STAFF WRITER 
This report covers a selection of crimes reported
from Nov. 9 to Nov. 17 as recorded in the Stanford Police
Department Public Bulletin.
A series of bike thefts occurred during this time.
MONDAY, NOV. 9
■ Between 8:30 a.m. and 5:45 p.m. at Parking Structure
5, an unknown suspect stole a victim's purse as well as
contents from the unlocked trunk of her car.
TUESDAY, NOV. 10
■ Between 5:15 p.m. and 10:15 p.m. on the corner of
Duena St. and Santa Teresa, an unknown suspect van
dalized the victim's vehicle by gouging the paint on
the left rear door with an unknown sharp object.
WEDNESDAY, NOV. 11
■ At 5 a.m., several suspects were seen climbing the
fence and exiting Sunken Diamond. Shortly after they
fled the area in a suspect's vehicle, and home plate
was discovered missing. Authorities struck out in their
attempts to figure out who stole home.
■ Between 4 p.m. and 5:30 p.m., an unknown suspect
stole a victim's unattended iPhone while he was play
ing basketball at the Arrillaga Recreation Center.
THURSDAY, NOV. 12
■ In an incident that occurred between Feb. 1 and this
day at the Landau Economics Center, an unknown
suspect used a victim's personal information to open
two bank accounts. Approximately $60,000 was used
between both accounts, or about a year-and-half's
worth of tuition.
■ At 3:28 p.m. in the Encina Hall Parking Lot, a non
injury vehicle-vs.-fixed object collision occurred.
FRIDAY, NOV. 13
■At 1:37 a.m. on the corner of Galvez St. and El
Camino Real, an arrestee was transported to the San
Jose Main Jail and booked for driving under the influ
ence.

SATURDAY, NOV. 14
■ Between 2 a.m. and 8:45 a.m., an unknown suspect
entered a victim's dorm room in Stern Hall and stole
his wallet. There were no signs of forced entry.
■ Between 7:30 p.m. and 9:30 p.m., an unknown suspect
smashed the rear window of a victim's vehicle, which
was parked in El Camino Grove.
SUNDAY, NOV. 15
■ Between 10 p.m. on Nov. 14 and 1 a.m. on this day, a
victim lost his iPhone while attending a party at the
Kappa Sigma house. The iPhone was then used by an
unknown suspect.
■ At 1:50 a.m. in the Roble Parking Lot, two suspects
jumped into the rear of the victim's vehicle, breaking
the spoiler on the trunk lid a perfect example of
what economists call the "free rider" problem.
■ At 2 a.m. at the corner of Palm Dr. and El Camino
Real, an arrestee was transported to the Main Jail and
booked for driving under the influence.
MONDAY, NOV. 16
■ Between 6:33 p.m. and 6:45 p.m. on Nov. 13, an
unknown suspect entered the telecommunications
room in the Studio 6 graduate student apartments and
stole two Cisco computer switches.
■ Between 7:35 p.m. and 8:20 p.m. on the 200 block of
Junipero Serra, an injured deer was dispatched using
a department-issued 12-gauge shotgun. Police main
tain that no other incidents occurred as a result of dis
patching the animal.
■At 8:30 p.m. at Branner Hall, a victim saw an
unknown suspect looking up at her from the bottom
of the shower stall when she was taking a shower. The
victim got a partial view of the suspect.
TUESDAY, NOV. 1/
■At 10:50 a.m. at Disk Loop Rd. at the Stanford
Observatory, a reporting party stated that a coyote
began to approach her son while they were walking
on the recreational path of the Stanford Foothills.

Contact Jane LePham at jlepham@stanford.edu.
