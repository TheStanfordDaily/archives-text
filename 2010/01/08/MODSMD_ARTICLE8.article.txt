# NEWS BRIEFS
## Bomb threat forces evacuation 
### THE DAILY NEWS STAFF 
At 10:05 a.m. Thursday morning, the Palo Alto
Police Department received a threat from an
anonymous man who claimed that a bomb had
been planted at Palo Alto City Council that would
detonate at 11:05 a.m. According to The San Jose
Mercury News, all 350 employees were evacuated
from the nine-story building at 10:40 a.m.
While there was no explosion at 11:05 a.m.,
bomb squads searched the building for about two
hours. Speaking to The Daily about the bomb
scare, Palo Alto Mayor Pat Burt stated, "There
were not any indications that it was a serious one
or a genuine one."
Burt went to explain that the police conducted
"a routine check to make sure that there was no
legitimacy to the threat."
The City Hall staff went back to work at 2 p.m.
after the four-hour evacuation.
