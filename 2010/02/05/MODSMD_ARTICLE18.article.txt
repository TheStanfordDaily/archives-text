# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### ELLEN HUET DESK EDITOR 
This report covers a selection of incidents from Jan.
27 to Feb. 2 as recorded in the Stanford Police
Department Public Bulletin.
Police received reports of bike thefts during this
period from the Gates Computer Science building,
Mirlo, Loro, the Mitchell Earth Sciences building,
the Band Shak and Enchanted Broccoli Forest. A
laptop was stolen from Mirrielees.
WEDNESDAY, JAN. 27
■ Between 1:30 a.m. and 4:55 a.m. an unknown sus
pect vandalized approximately 30 Marguerite
shuttle buses parked at 360 Oak Road by spray
painting a stenciled symbol on the back of the
buses.
THURSDAY, JAN. 28
■ Between 12 p.m. on Jan. 27 and 11:30 a.m. on this
day, an unknown suspect stole a "bansai" plant
from the victim's front yard at 1097 Carthcarat
Way.
■ On this day, Vaden Health Center staff reported
that the center had been receiving annoying and
harassing phone calls from an unknown suspect
for the past several months.
■ At 10:20 p.m. near the Terman Engineering
Center, two arrestees were transported to the
Main Jail and booked for vehicle theft. A juvenile
was cited for the same and released to the par
ents. Sometimes it's good to be the tag-along.
FRIDAY, JAN. 29
■ Between 12:58 a.m. and 2 a.m. an ABM custodian
made an unauthorized use of a University com
puter when she plugged in her phone to charge it
in the Terman Engineering Center.
■ Between 7 a.m. and 3:30 p.m. an unknown suspect
broke into a car in Parking Structure 5 near the
Sand Hill Athletic Fields, and stole a CD player,
an iPod, a GPS system and a parking permit.
■ Between 8:05 a.m. and 8:20 a.m., an unknown sus
pect slashed three tires on the victim's vehicle in
the Haas Center parking lot. The vehicle was
towed from the scene.
SATURDAY, JAN. 30
■ At 1 a.m., a student befriended a person at Rudy's
Pub at 117 University Ave. in Palo Alto. After the
student gave the person a ride, the person left
some suspicious property in the vehicle

according to a later University alert e-mail, hand
cuffs and lighter fluid.
■ Between 3 p.m. and 11:59 p.m., an unknown sus
pect stole an LCD television set from an
unlocked cabinet on the first floor of the Arroyo
lounge.
SUNDAY, JAN. 31
■ Between 10:15 p.m. on Jan. 30 and 2 a.m. on this
day, an unknown suspect caused more than $4OO
worth of damage to a motorcycle parked outside
of EAST.
■ At 12:35 a.m. on Mayfield Avenue, an arrestee
was cited and released for being a minor in pos
session of alcohol.
■ At 2:20 a.m. at El Camino and Page Mill, an
arrestee was transported to the San Jose Main Jail
and booked for driving under the influence.
■ At 11:40 p.m. on Mayfield Avenue, an arrestee
was cited and released for being a minor in pos
session of alcohol.
MONDAY, FEB. 1
■ Between 1 a.m. on Jan. 28 and 1 a.m. on this day,
an unknown suspect entered Herrin Hall and
attempted to take a projector from the ceiling. A
cable fastened to a wooden podium was also cut.
■ At 8:40 p.m., a stray dog was found in the area of
Mayfield Avenue and San Rafael Place. Witnesses
said they noticed another dog and a cat in the
area, and that the three of them seemed to be on
an incredible journey bound toward home.
■ Between 9:45 p.m. and 11 p.m., a wallet was stolen
from the Arrillaga Center for Sports and
Recreation while the owner was playing basket
ball.
TUESDAY, FEB. 2
■ Between 5:50 a.m. and 6:30 a.m., an injured deer
was dispatched on Junipero Serra Boulevard near
Santa Maria Avenue.
■ An injury vehicle-vs.-vehicle collision occurred at
9:55 a.m. at Palm Drive and Arboretum Road.
■ At 1:35 p.m. in the Varian physics labs, a person
was served a University-issued stay-away letter
prohibiting him from being on the Stanford cam
pus. The person agreed to comply.
■ Between 4:15 p.m. and 6:30 p.m. an unknown sus
pect entered a dormitory room in Roble Hall and
stole cash from the victim's wallet. The suspect
was seen leaving the room.
