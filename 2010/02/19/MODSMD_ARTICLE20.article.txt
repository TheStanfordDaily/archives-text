# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### ELLEN HUET STAFF WRITER 
This report covers a selection of incidents reported
from Feb. 10 to Feb. 16 as recorded in the Stanford
Police Department Public Bulletin.
During this period, two unlocked bikes were stolen
from the Law School quadrangle and from Larkin.
Three locked bikes were stolen from Kimball, Jerry
and Parking Structure No. 17 near Stock Farm Road.
WEDNESDAY, FEB. 10
■ Between 6:10 and 6:30 p.m., an unknown suspect
entered a dorm room at Enchanted Broccoli Forest
and stole a laptop computer. Two suspects were
seen prior to the theft.
THURSDAY, FEB. 11
■ At 2 p.m., a non-injury, hit-and-run, vehicle vs. vehi
cle collision occurred at the intersection of Museum
Way and Palm Drive,
FRIDAY, FEB. 12
■ At 3:15 a.m., two suspects in Crothers Hall were
involved in a domestic dispute with each other. Both
had visible injuries but denied any physical con
frontation.
■ Between 10 a.m. and 12 p.m., an unknown suspect
broke the window of a locked vehicle parked
behind Jordan Quad and stole the stereo faceplate.
■ Between 9:15 a.m. and 5:55 p.m., an unknown sus
pect broke a window of a vehicle parked at
Mayfield Avenue and Campus Drive and stole a
stereo.
■ At 8:20 p.m., a man at Maples Pavilion was observed
at the men's volleyball game unbuttoning his pants
and placing his jacket over his lap.
■ At 11:15 p.m., a student was cited and released on
Mayfield Avenue for being a minor in possession of
alcohol.
■ At 11:25 p.m., a student was arrested at the
Graduate Community Center for public intoxica
tion and was transported to the Main Jail.
SATURDAY, FEB. 13
■ Between 11:40 p.m. on Feb. 12 and 1:45 a.m. on Feb.
13, six students received citations: two for public
intoxication, two for being minors in possession of
alcohol and two for creating a public nuisance by
public urination. All six citations took place at Casa
Italiana. Now that's what we like to call efficiency.
■ Between 4:45 p.m. and 6 p.m., an unknown suspect
entered an unlocked dorm room in Otero and stole
several personal items.
■ At 8 p.m., a student found a clear plastic bag con
taining an unknown white powder under the cush

ions of a couch in the lower lounge of Kimball Hall.
SUNDAY, FEB. 14
■At 12:20 a.m., two students between Theta Delta
Chi and 808 were cited and released for being
minors in possession of alcohol, and criticized for
not picking a destination.
■ Between 1:30 a.m. and 12:20 p.m. an unknown sus
pect vandalized a vehicle parked outside Florence
Moore Hall by throwing a rock through the rear
window, cracking the windshield and denting the
trunk.
■At 2:30 a.m., an arrestee was transported from
Mayfield Drive and Campus Drive to the Main Jail
and booked for assault with force likely to cause
great bodily harm. The juvenile was cited and
released. There were five witnesses.
■ Between 6:50 a.m. and 8:35 a.m., an unknown sus
pect smashed the front passenger window of a vehi
cle parked outside Stanford Stadium and stole a
wallet and a dozen red roses. What a lucky lady.
■ Also in the same parking lot between 6:20 a.m. and
8:05 a.m., an unknown suspect forced entry into a
locked vehicle by breaking a window. The suspect
stole a fog machine.
■ Between 8 a.m. on Feb. 12 and 12:30 p.m. on Feb. 14,
an unknown suspect smashed the front passenger
window of a vehicle parked outside the Track
House and stole an iPod and charger.
MONDAY, FEB. 15
■ Between 6:30 p.m. on Feb. 14 and 7:30 a.m. on Feb. 15,
an unknown suspect threw raw eggs on several
parked vehicles in the open carport at Pearce Mitchell
Place. No cars were damaged.
■ East Palo Alto police officers recovered a laptop com
puter during a juvenile probation search. The laptop
had been taken from the University system support
office on Porter Drive.
TUESDAY, FEB. 16
■ At 12:05 a.m. in Escondido Village, two parties were
involved in a verbal altercation with each other. No
physical violence was reported.
■ Between 7:30 p.m. on Feb. 13 and 10 a.m. on Feb. 16,
an unknown suspect stole an unattended purse from
a locked room in Old Union.
■ A vehicle was stolen from the Stock Farm parking
lot near the intersection of Oak Road and Welch
Road at 4:40 p.m.
■ Between 4 p.m. on Feb. 15 and 11 a.m. on Feb. 16, an
unknown suspect tried unsuccessfully to steal a
LED television from an unlocked second-floor con
ference room in the sociology building.
