# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### ELLEN HUET STAFF WRITER 
This report covers a selection of incidents
from April 13 to April 21 as recorded in the Stan
ford Department of Public Safety bulletin.
During this time, six locked bikes were stolen
from Donner (two), De Guerre pools, Gates
Computer Science building, Whitman house and
Larkin. Three unlocked bikes were stolen from
Lucas Center, Lantana and Encina Commons.
TUESDAY, APRIL 13
■ Between 3 p.m. and 5 p.m. at Potter House, an
unknown suspect smeared dog feces over the
entry to the victim's residence in Robinson
House.
THURSDAY, APRIL 15
■ Between 12:52 p.m. on April 10 and 10:01 a.m.
on April 15, the reporting party received ha
rassing e-mails from another party, who was

contacted and asked to stop.
■ Between 5:30 p.m. on April 14 and 3:30 p.m. on
April 15 at McCullough Building, an unknown
suspect took scraps of copper sheeting from in
side room 27.
FRIDAY, APRIL 16
■ Between 3:45 p.m. and 4:04 p.m. in Munger
building 5, two candles left burning in a bath
room set off the fire alarm, and sprinklers
caused a flood that leaked to the storage room.
■ At 12:05 a.m. at Tresidder Memorial Union, an
arrestee was transported to San Jose main jail
and booked for public intoxication.
■ Between 7:25 p.m. and 7:30 p.m. at 63 Abrams
Ct., the victim and a suspect had a verbal and
physical alteration.
SATURDAY, APRIL 17
■ Between 3 p.m. on April 12 and 1:08 p.m. on
April 17 at the Park & Ride on Page Mill
Road, a resourceful victim located a stolen

bike on Craigslist and arranged to buy the bike
back. The seller was arrested for being in pos
session of stolen property.
■ Between 7:55 p.m. and 10 p.m. in Dinkelspiel
Auditorium, an unknown suspect stole the vic
tim's unattended iPhone from a concert.
■ Between 11:45 p.m. on April 16 and 1:45 a.m.
on April 17 atTresidder Memorial Union, a re
porting party reported missing $4O from his or
her wallet.
SUNDAY, APRIL 18
■ Between 12 a.m. and 1:25 a.m., a suspect used
force and fear to have sexual intercourse with
the victim without her consent.
■ Between 1:50 p.m. and 2:10 p.m. at Loro, an un
known suspected reached into the victim's
room through an open window and stole an
iPhone, iPod and wallet.
■ Between 4:15 p.m. and 6 p.m. in
Alondra, an unknown suspect en
tered the victim's dormitory and
stole a laptop and headphones.
MONDAY, APRIL 19
■At 12 a.m. at Panama Mall and
Galvez Mall, a non-injury, hit-and
run, vehicle-vs.-fixed object colli
sion occurred. The fire hydrant re
ported no serious injuries, just hurt
pride.
■ At 6:43 p.m. at 551 Serra Mall, an
arrestee was transported to the
San Jose main jail and booked for
resisting a peace officer and bat
tery on a peace officer.
TUESDAY, APRIL 20
■ Between 4 p.m. on April 19 and
10:09 a.m. on April 20, an un
known suspect vandalized a Stan
ford informational sign at May
field Avenue and Lagunita Drive
by writing on it with spray paint
and permanent marker.
■At 5:10 p.m. at Palm Drive and
Museum Way, an arrestee was
cited and released for driving unli
censed.
■ Between 10 a.m. and midnight ap
proximately six hundred people
reported not being arrested for
smoking an illegal substance.
WEDNESDAY, APRIL 21
■ Between 12 a.m. and 8:30 a.m. at
Palm Drive and El Camino Real,
an unknown suspect painted blue
letters on a store wall.
■ Between 6:30 p.m. and 6:45 p.m.,
an unknown suspect stole an
iPhone while the owner was play
ing basketball at the Arrillaga
Center for Sports and Recreation.

Contact Ellen Huet at ehuet@stan
ford.edu.
