# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### ELLEN HUET DESK EDITOR 
This report covers a selection
of incidents from April 29 to May
5 as recorded in the Stanford De
partment of Public Safety bul
letin.
During this time, four locked
bikes were reported stolen from
Sequoia Hall, Burbank Hall,
Abrams Court and the Graduate
School of Business. Four un
locked bikes were reported stolen
from the east wing of Green Li
brary, Escondido Village, Avery
Aquatics Center and the book
store.
THURSDAY, APRIL 29
■ At 4:30 p.m. at Campus Drive and
the Clark Center, an injury vehi
cle-vs.-bike collision occurred.
FRIDAY, APRIL 30
■ A man reported that between 6
p.m. on April 14 and 12 p.m. on
April 23, an unknown suspect
stole his motorcycle helmet
from his motorcycle, which was
parked in the Wilbur parking
structure.
■ At 10:05 a.m. at the Oval and
Serra Mall, a non-injury, hit
and-run, vehicle-vs.-vehicle, hy
phen-heavy collision occurred.
SATURDAY, MAY 1
■ A student became upset after
arguing with an unknown male
suspect at Muwekma-Tah-Ruk.
The student left his residence at
about 6 a.m., but called a friend
later to assure the friend he was
not missing.
■ At 7 p.m. at Lakeside Dining, a
person caused damage to the
dining hall wall and damaged
decorations for a party hosted
by Ujamaa. Charges were not
pressed. It was, however, a little

awkward.
■ Between 10:30 p.m. on April 30
and 9:30 a.m. on May 1 at 933
Valdez PI., an unknown suspect
smashed a vehicle window and
stole a laptop and charger.
SUNDAY, MAY 2
■At 1:38 a.m. at the Student
Flousing office at 565 Co well
Ln., an arrestee was transport
ed to the San Jose main jail and
booked for driving under the
influence.
TUESDAY, MAY 4
■ Someone reported that be
tween April 3 and April 28, an
unknown suspect made fraudu
lent charges on the debit card
of the reporting party, who said
the debit card was never out of
his or her possession.
■ At 7:55 p.m. at Stanford Avenue
and Raimundo Way, a person
was cited and released for driv
ing on a suspended license.
WEDNESDAY, MAY 5
■ At 2 a.m. at Lane Hall, a student
was cited and released for con
tributing to the delinquency of
a minor by providing alcoholic
beverages.
■At 12:00 p.m., a student was
transported to the San Jose
main jail and booked on an out
standing warrant from Santa
Clara County.
■At 7:23 p.m. at Bowdoin and
Campus Drive, a non-injury, ve
hicle-vs.-bike collision oc
curred.
■ Between 9:30 p.m. on May 5 and
1:30 a.m. on May 6, an unknown
suspected entered an unlocked
dorm room in Sigma Chi and
stole two laptop computers.

Contact Ellen Huet at ehuet@stan
ford.edu.
