# Haas Center appoints new faculty directors
## 
### DAILY NEWS STAFF 
Professors Larry Diamond '73
MA '7B Ph.D. 'BO and Julie Kennedy
Ph.D. '92 have been named the new
faculty directors for the Haas Center
for Public Service. They will assume
the roles in September. Diamond and
Kennedy succeed Gabriel Garcia,
professor of medicine, who is closing

out his fourth year in the position.
Diamond, a senior fellow at the
Hoover Institution and the Freeman
Spogli Institute for International
Studies, is also a professor by courtesy
of sociology and political science,
teaching courses in democratic devel
opment. In addition, he serves on the
Haas Center's faculty steering com
mittee and as chair of the FSI-Haas
International Public Service Advisory
Committee.
Kennedy joins Diamond on the
FSI-Haas International Public Ser
vice Advisory Committee. She is a
senior lecturer at the Woods Institute
for the Environment as well as in the
earth systems program at the School
of Earth Sciences.
Kennedy and Diamond won the
Dinkelspiel Award for outstanding
service to undergraduate education in
2002 and 2007, respectively.
