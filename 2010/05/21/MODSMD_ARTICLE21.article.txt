# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### JANE LEPHAM SENIOR STAFF WRITER 
This report covers a selection of incidents
from May 12 to May 18 as recorded in the
Stanford Department of Public Safety bul
letin.
A series of bike thefts and medical emer
gencies occurred during this period. Bike
thefts occurred at Stern Hall, the Terman En
gineering Lab, Meyer Library and the inter
section of Lasuen Mall and Serra Mall.
WEDNESDAY, MAY 12
■ Between 9:30 a.m. and 3:30 p.m., an un
known suspect stole the front unlocked
wheel of a victim's bike outside of Bldg.
200, increasing the use of unicycles on
campus.
■ At 3:30 p.m. at the Dept. of Public Safety, a
person was cited and released on an out
standing warrant from Santa Clara Coun-

■ Between 7 p.m. and 9:30 p.m., an unknown
suspect stole a victim's laptop from her

residence at the Kappa Alpha Theta
house.
THURSDAY, MAY 13
■ Someone reported that an unknown sus
pect painted white letters over the artwork
structure inside of the Shumway Fountain,
located at 557 Escondido Mall.
FRIDAY, MAY 14
■At 12 a.m. at Cypress Hall, an unknown
suspect stole a victim's unattended wallet.
The victim was not aware her wallet had
been stolen until an "unknown citizen"
contacted her.
■ At 7:20 a.m., a non-injury, vehicle-vs.-vehi
cle collision occurred on Roth Way, 48 feet
west of Lasuen Street.
■ At 8:25 a.m. outside of 680 Lomita Drive,
someone's vehicle was impounded for
having eight outstanding citations. The
owner's PWR professor, on the other
hand, was quite impressed.
■ At 2:55 p.m., a non-injury, bike-vs.-vehicle
collision occurred on Escondido Road,

174 feet west of Arguello Mall.
■ Between 8:30 p.m. and 11:20 p.m., an un
known suspect stole a victim's laptop from
the Stern dining hall.
■ At 11:20 p.m. atTheta Delta Chi, a person
falsely reported that he was physically at
tacked and forcibly robbed of his SUID.
He also provided false suspect informa
tion.
■ A bike stolen on Dec. 3 was recovered at
the Clubhouse on this day.
SATURDAY, MAY 15
■ At 1:05 a.m. on the corner of Mayfield Av
enue near Xanadu, a student was trans
ported to the San Jose main jail and
booked for public intoxication.
■ Between 9 p.m. and 9:30 p.m., an unknown
suspect stole a victim's unattended guitar
from White Plaza, creating discord.
SUNDAY, MAY 16
■ At 4:12 a.m. on the corner of Page Mill
and Coyote Hill, an arrestee was trans
ported to the San Jose main jail and

booked for driving under the influence.
■ Between 11:45 a.m. and 4 p.m. at the
Track House, an unknown suspect used a
tool to punch the door lock of a victim's
vehicle and stole his laptop computer.
■ At 10:02 p.m. at the Dept. of Public Safe
ty, an arrestee was transported to the
San Jose main jail and booked for public
intoxication.
MONDAY, MAY 17
■ In an incident that occurred between
Jan. 18 and May 17, an unknown suspect
used a motion-sensitive recording to
record two victims inside their private
bedrooms in Mirrielees. Police have
deemed this a "suspicious circum
stance."
■ In an incident that occurred between Oct.
25 and Nov. 7, 2009, an unknown suspect
made unauthorized purchases using a vic
tim's credit card.
■ Police investigated a report of grand theft
at 212 Rains House in Willis Court. The
case is in progress.

TUESDAY, MAY 18
■ At 12 a.m. at 630 Gerona Road, a person
reported that an unknown suspect had
made unauthorized purchases with his
credit card.
■At 10:30 a.m. on the corner of Dudley
Lane and Olmsted Road, a vehicle was
towed and stored for expired registration.
■ At 10:40 a.m. at 56 Dudley Lane, a second
vehicle was towed and stored for expired
registration.
■ At 11 a.m. at the Herbert Hoover Memor
ial Building, an unknown suspect was seen
handling two different bikes at a bike rack.
The suspect fled the scene.Three bikes and
one bike tire were later recovered.
■ At 3:15 p.m. at the Dept. of Public Safety, a
person was cited and released on an out
standing warrant from Santa Clara County.
■ At 3:33 p.m. in Parking Structure 1, a per
son was cited and released for displaying a
handicap placard not issued to him.
