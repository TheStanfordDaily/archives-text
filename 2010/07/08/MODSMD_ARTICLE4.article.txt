# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### AARON BRODER DAILY INTERN 
This report covers a selection of incidents from
June 28 to July 4, as recorded in the Stanford Depart
ment of Public Safety bulletin.
A series of bike thefts and medical emergencies oc
curred during this period. Bike thefts occurred at the
Rains Complex, Florence Moore Hall and Crothers
Memorial Hall.
MONDAY, JUNE 28
■ Between 8:00 a.m. on June 23 and 7 a.m. on June 28,
an unknown suspect entered the victim's dormitory
room at Slavianskii Dom and stole currency from
her wallet.
■ In an incident that occurred on at 12 a.m. on June 22,
but was reported on June 28, an unknown suspect
made fraudulent charges using the victim's debit
cards.
■ In an incident that occurred on June 24, but was re
ported on June 28, an unknown suspect stole the vic
tim's bike tool kit from her bike, which had been
cable-locked to the bike rack outside of Bldg. 380. In
a fit of mercy, however, the suspect left the bike
alone.
■ Between 7:30 a.m. on June 24 and 9 p.m. on June 28,
an unknown suspect entered the victim's room in
Escondido Village without force and stole a laptop
computer.
TUESDAY, JUNE 29
■ At 2:45 p.m. at the Li Ka Shing Center for Learning,
an individual was cited and released for misusing a
disability parking placard belonging to an unknown
person.
WEDNESDAY, JUNE 30
■ At 12 a.m., an unknown suspect entered the victim's
locked dormitory room in Branner Hall and stole
personal property, including a phone.
■ At 9:10 a.m. on the corner of Oak Road and Welch
Road, an individual was cited and released for dri
ving without a license.
■ At 4:14 p.m., an arrestee was transported to the San
Jose main jail and booked for dwelling "with no law

ful business" inside La Maison Francaise.
■ In an incident that occurred between June 23 and
June 25, but was reported on June 30, two victims
found their property spirited away by an unknown
suspect when they left it in a common area of the
Enchanted Broccoli Forest.
THURSDAY, JULY 1
■ In an incident that occurred between June 25 and
June 27, but was reported on July 1, an unknown
suspect stole a wheel from the victim's bike, which
he had U-locked to a bike rack on the east side of
Herrin Hall.
■ In an incident that occurred between May 5 and July
1 at Tresidder Memorial Union, the victim's ac
countant found discrepancies in the cash deposits.
The person with access to the cash was unable to
provide an explanation for the missing cash and in
consistencies.
FRIDAY, JULY 2
■ At 3:39 a.m. on the 600 block of Escondido Road, an
individual was cited and released for driving under
the influence.
■ Between 7:30 p.m. on July 1 and 11 a.m. on July 2, an
unknown suspect stole an art piece valued at around
$5OO from the Cantor Center for Visual Arts.
■ Between 12 p.m. on July 1 and 12 p.m. on July 2, a
suspicious letter with no return address was found in
the reporting party's mailbox.
SUNDAY, JULY 4
■ Between 10 p.m. on July 3 and 9:45 a.m. on July 4, an
unknown suspect broke the passenger window of
the victim's vehicle, which had been parked in Park
ing Structure 6, and stole multiple items from the in
terior compartment.
■ At 10:10 p.m., three suspects were observed riding
bikes while "guiding a riderless bike." When con
fronted, the suspects quickly rode off. The bikes
were later found abandoned in Palo Alto.
Jane LePham contributed to this report.
