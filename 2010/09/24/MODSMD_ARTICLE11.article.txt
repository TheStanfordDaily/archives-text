# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### IVY NGUYEN DESK EDITOR 
This report covers a selection of
incidents from Sept. 14 to Sept. 23 as
recorded in the Stanford Department
of Public Safety bulletin.
A series of bike thefts, car burgla
ries and drug citations occurred dur
ing this period.

TUESDAY, SEPT. 14
■ On Sept. 11 between 8:00 and 9:00
p.m. at Meyer Library, the report
ing party lost her key ring with her
Stanford I.D. attached to it. The key
ring was later returned without the
Stanford I.D.

WEDNESDAY, SEPT. 15
■ On Sept. 13 or 14, the victim's U

locked bike was stolen from the
bike racks on the Oval side of the
Graduate School of Business.
■ Between 11:20 a.m. and 1:20 p.m.,
the victim's unlocked bike was
stolen from the east side of the Mc-
Cullough Building.
THURSDAY, SEPT. 16
■ Between 12:30 a.m. and 8:30 a.m.,
the victim's U-locked bike was
stolen from the courtyard of Wilbur
Hall.

■ On Sept. 13 between 6:00 a.m. and
10:30 a.m., unknown suspects stole
several bicycle tire repair tools
from the seat bag of the victim's bi
cycle, parked at the bike racks out
side Mudd Chemistry Building.

FRIDAY, SEPT. 17
■ Between 8:00 a.m. and 12:00 p.m.,
the victim's cable-locked bike was
stolen from the bike rack on the
northwest corner of the Graduate
School of Business.
SATURDAY, SEPT. 18
■ Between the hours of 12 a.m. and
6:45 p.m., unknown suspects en
tered the Arbuckle Lounge in the
Graduate School of Business,
broke into the vending machine
and stole approximately $450 in
cash.
■ At 1:15 p.m. at the intersection of
Campus Drive and Lasuen Street,
the arrestee was cited and re
leased for driving on a suspended
license.
■At 2 p.m. a golf cart was stolen
from 436 Mayfield Ave.
■ As the Card stole all of Wake For
est's chances of bringing the foot
ball game to a tie, a rented genera
tor was stolen outside of Stanford
Stadium.
MONDAY, SEPT. 20
■At Adams House in Sterling
Quad, a victim reported that un
known suspects used his debit
card information to make several
fraudulent transactions in Fre
mont, Calif.
■ The victim's U-locked bike was
stolen from the east side of Otero
between 11:00 a.m. and 12:25
p.m., making that freshman's first
day of school the worst ever.
■ A victim at the Law School re
ported that another party had
been telephoning and leaving

rambling messages.
TUESDAY, SEPT. 21
■ At 12:00 p.m., the victim's un
locked bike was stolen from a stor
age facility at Donner.
■ Between Sept. 20 at 3 p.m. and
Sept. 21 at 9:30 a.m., the victim's
U-locked bike was stolen from the
east side of Casa Italiana.
■ Between Sept. 18 and Sept. 19, the
victim's U-locked bike was stolen
outside of Potter House.
WEDNESDAY, SEPT. 22
■ Between 4:30 a.m. and 6:30 a.m.,
unknown suspects stole a power
washer from a storage room in the
kitchen of Wilbur Hall.
■ Between 5:30 a.m. and 8:40 a.m.,
the victim's U-locked bike was
stolen outside Casa Italiana.
■ Between 6:00 p.m. and 9:00 p.m.,
the victim's U-locked bike was
stolen outside Durand.
Tyler Brown contributed to this re

port.
