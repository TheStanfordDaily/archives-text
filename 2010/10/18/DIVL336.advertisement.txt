In the tradition of such classic memoirs as Barack
Obama's Dreams from My Father and Henry
Louis Gates's Colored People, and written by one
of America's most admired women, this is a
poignant and instructive look at the author's
remarkable Birmingham, Alabama childhood, illu
minating her upbringing in the context of the
extraordinary efforts made by her parents and
other people in the community to raise children
against a backdrop of explosive racial tension.
ABOUT THE AUTHOR/ILLUSTRATOR:
Condoleezza Rice was the 66th U.S. secretary of
state and the first black woman to hold that
office. Prior to that, she was the first woman ever
to serve as national security advisor, was provost
of Stanford from 1993 to 1999, and served as the
Soviet and East European Affairs advisor during
the dissolution of the Soviet Union.
