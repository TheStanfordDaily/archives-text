# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### IVY NGUYEN DESK EDITOR 
This report covers a selection of
incidents from Jan. 13 to Jan. 20 as
recorded in the Stanford Depart
ment of Public Safety bulletin.
THURSDAY, JAN. 13
■At 1:35 a.m. at the intersection of
McFarland Court and Olmsted
Road, a man was transported to
the San Jose main jail and
booked for public intoxication.
FRIDAY, JAN. 14
■At 2 a.m., a man at Sigma Chi
was cited and released for being
a public nuisance by urinating in
public.
■At 12:30 p.m. at the intersection
of Via Ortega and Serra Street, a
man was transported to the San
Jose main jail for being in posses
sion of an imitation firearm and
for violating conditions of his pa
role.
■ Between 11:15 a.m. and 2:30
p.m., unknown suspects broke
into a car parked at Lomita
Drive and Museum Way, steal
ing two backpacks containing
clothing, a camera, a Kindle and
other electrical equipment.

■ Between 3:30 and 9:30 p.m.,

someone entered an unlocked
dorm room in Roble Hall and
stole a laptop.

■ At 8 p.m., a man was transported
to the San Jose main jail for
being drunk in public and prowl
ing between Trancos and Soto.

SATURDAY, JAN. 15
■ Between 8 p.m. on Jan. 14 and 2
a.m. on Jan. 15, unknown sus
pects entered an apartment at 71
Barnes Court, stealing a laptop
and other miscellaneous items.

■ At 6:15 p.m., a dog bit a visitor to
the Rodin Sculpture Garden.
The injured visitor was trans
ported to the hospital for med
ical attention. The hungry dog
reportedly mistook the visitor
for one of the six Burghers.

SUNDAY, JAN. 16
■ Between 8 p.m. on Jan. 15 and
3:30 a.m. on Jan. 16, an unknown
suspect entered an occupied
townhouse at 94 Thoburn
through an unlocked sliding
glass door, stealing a laptop and
money.

■At 8:45 p.m., an unlocked bike
was stolen from outside the Axe
and Palm.

■ Between 9 and 9:05 p.m., water

leaked from a sink in room 376
of the Keck Building, flooding
a hallway and adjacent rooms.
Water dripped down from the
ceiling onto various lab equip
ment and computers.
MONDAY, JAN. 17
■ Between 1:40 and 2 p.m., un
known suspects broke into a
car parked near the Track
House, stealing a backpack.
TUESDAY, JAN. 18
■ Between 9 and 10 a.m., a
locked bike was stolen from
the front of the Science and
Engineering Quad teaching
center.
■ At 3:30 p.m., a woman was ar
rested for petty theft at the
Stanford bookstore.
WEDNESDAY, JAN. 19
■ At 12 a.m. a resident of Mars re
ported receiving multiple un
wanted phone calls and text mes
sages containing obscene materi
al from an unknown suspect.
■ At 3:20 p.m., a man was cited and
released for vandalizing the Tan
ner Fountain by setting fire to a
collage using torch fuel.
