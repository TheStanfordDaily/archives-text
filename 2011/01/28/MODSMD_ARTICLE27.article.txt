# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### IVY NGUYEN DESK EDITOR 
This report covers a selection of incidents from
Jan. 21 to Jan. 27 as recorded in the Stanford Depart
ment of Public Safety bulletin.
FRIDAY, JAN. 21
■ At 1:10 a.m., a man was stopped at Sand Hill Road
and Pasteur Drive and cited for driving unli
censed.
■ A license plate was found at 5:49 a.m. in the
Maples Pavilion parking lot. The plate is currently
stored at SUDPS.
■ At 12:15 p.m., a man was cited and released near
campus on El Camino Real for driving with a sus
pended license. The vehicle was towed.
■At 5:05 p.m., the right wheel of a golf cart was
damaged when the driver hit a bollard. The bol
lard's condition is unknown.
■ At 11:50 p.m., a minor at Bob was cited and re
leased for possession of alcohol.
SATURDAY, JAN. 22
■ At 1:08 a.m., a man at Pi Beta Phi was transport
ed to the San Jose main jail and booked for pub
lic intoxication.
■ At 1:10 a.m., a person at 110 McFarland Ct. report
ed receiving obscene phone calls from a blocked
number.
■ At 8:45 a.m., three stolen golf carts were recov
ered at Arrillaga Sports Center.

■ A U-locked bike was stolen from the racks of
Sigma Nu between 8 and 9 p.m.
SUNDAY, JAN. 23
■ Between 2 and 2:50 p.m., a U-locked bike was
stolen from the bike racks outside of Arrillaga
Sports Center.
■ In the same period of time, an unlocked bike was
stolen from the Lasuen Mall side of Old Union.
MONDAY, JANUARY 24
■ At 4:40 p.m., a golf cart was stolen from the front
of Burnham Pavilion.
■ At 12:45 p.m., an unknown suspect approached a
person in Meyer Library with an open folding
knife in hand. The suspect did not speak to or
threaten the victim with the knife.
TUESDAY, JAN. 25
■ At 1:24 a.m., a car parked in the Rains parking lot
was towed after its alarm had gone off for 24
hours.
■ At 12 p.m., a fire alarm activated in Building 260
after water flooded the basement. Facilities oper
ations, EH&S and restoration management were
notified.
■ At 5 p.m., a pair of Oakley sunglasses were stolen
after they were left unattended on a bench in a
men's dressing room.
WEDNESDAY, JAN. 26
■ Between 12:30 and 1 p.m., an unlocked bike was
stolen from outside the post office.
