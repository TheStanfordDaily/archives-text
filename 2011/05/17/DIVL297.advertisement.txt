GEORGE PACKER
In Conversation with Tobias Wolff & Debra Satz
George Packer is a staff writer for The New Yorker and the author of
The Assassins' Gate: America in Iraq. The book traces America's entry
into the Iraq war and the troubled occupation. It was a finalist for the
2005 Pulitzer Prize, and was named by The New York Times as one of
the ten best books of 2005.
Packer will be in conversation with Tobias Wolff (Creative Writing,
Stanford) and Debra Satz (Philosophy, Stanford). Tobias Wolff has
written several books including Our Story Begins, The Barracks Thief,
Old School and This Boy's Life. Debra Satz is a Professor of Philoso
phy and the Director of the Center for Ethics in Society at Stanford.
