# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### IVY NGUYEN MANAGING EDITOR 
This report covers a selection of
incidents from Oct. 5 through Oct.
9 as recorded in the Stanford De
partment of Public Safety bulletin.
During this period, several vehicle
burglaries occurred throughout
campus.
WEDNESDAY, OCT. 5
■ At 1:55 a.m. a female student
walking along Mayfield Avenue
was cited and released for being a
minor in possession of alcohol.
■ At 3:25 p.m. a woman at Santa
Teresa Street and Lomita Drive
was cited and released for using an
altered disabled parking placard.
■ At 7 p.m., a man was cited and re
leased for shoplifting at the
Bookstore.
THURSDAY, OCT. 6
■ Between 11 p.m. the previous
day and 6:30 a.m. today at 658
Escondido Road and Campus
Drive someone stole several
items from an unlocked vehicle.
■ Between 3:30 p.m. the previous
day and 7:30 a.m. today someone
entered Suite 240 at the Arrillaga
Family Sports Center and stole
$l,OBO from a cash box in a
locked drawer.
■ Between 2:15 p.m. and 3:10 p.m.
someone stole an unattended
iPod from Jordan Hall.
FRIDAY, OCT. 7
■ Between 10 p.m. the previous day
and 10 a.m. today someone stole
a laundry basket from a vehicle
parked at 517 Co well Lane and
Campus Drive. There were no
signs of forced entry.
■ Between 10 a.m. and 11:40 a.m.
someone stole a V parking per
mit from a moped parked near
the Bookstore.
■ Between 11:10 a.m. and 3 p.m.
someone stole money from an un
attended purse located under the
counter at the Lagunita Dining
Hall.
■ Between 1:15 p.m. and 2:30 p.m.
someone stole an unlocked bike
outside of the Mudd Chemistry
Building.
■ Between 6 p.m. and 8:30 p.m.
someone vandalized a bike

parked at 85 Hulme Court at
Olmsted Road by pulling out the
valve stem. An unknown suspect
had also flattened the rear tire
days earlier.
■ At 11:15 p.m. a male student near
Casa Italiana was cited and re
leased for being a minor in pos
session of alcohol.
■ At the same time, a man at Cow
ell Lane and Campus Drive was
transported to the San Jose Main
Jail and booked for public intoxi
cation and vandalism.
■At 11:30 p.m. an RA reported a
student missing near the Eastside
West Bar at 3154 Fillmore Street
in San Francisco.The student was
later located at her dorm after
spending the night at a friend's
house.
■At 11:43 p.m. a male student at
586 Mayfield Avenue was cited
and released for being a minor in
possession of alcohol.
■ Between 11 p.m. and midnight
someone stole a locked bike near
Soto.
SATURDAY, OCT. 8
■ Between 1 a.m. and 9 a.m. a
woman lost her Swedish passport
somewhere on campus.
■ Between 3 p.m. the previous day
and 11 a.m. today, someone en
tered an unlocked vehicle parked
near Cowell Lane and Campus
Drive and stole some music CDs.
■At 3:37 p.m. a man at Stanford
Stadium was cited and released
on warrants out of Alameda
County.
■ At 4:02 p.m. another man at Stan
ford Stadium was transported to
the San Jose Main Jail and
booked for public intoxication.
■ At 4:25 p.m. a man at Stanford
Stadium was cited and released
on multiple warrants out of San
Francisco and Alameda coun
ties.
■At 6 p.m. a woman at Stanford
Stadium was transported to the
San Jose Main Jail and booked
for public intoxication.
SUNDAY, OCT. 9
■ At 1:21 p.m. a woman at the cor
ner of Page Mill Road and
Hanover Street was cited and re
leased for driving unlicensed.
