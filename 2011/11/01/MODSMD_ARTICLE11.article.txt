# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### ALICE PHILLIPS 
This report covers a selection of
incidents from Oct. 17 through Oct.
24 as recorded in the Stanford De
partment of Public Safety (SDUPS)
bulletin.
MONDAY, OCT. 17
■ Papers were stolen from the
glove box of an unlocked vehicle
on Pearce Mitchell Place be
tween 6 p.m. on Oct. 16 and 8 a.m.
on Oct. 17. Two possible sets of
prints were lifted at the scene.
■ Unknown suspect(s) entered an
unlocked vehicle parked in the
Pearce Mitchell Complex be
tween 9 p.m. on Oct. 16 and 11
a.m. on Oct. 17 and stole coins
and a power cable. A partial print
was recovered at the scene.
■ A U-locked bike was stolen from
a bike rack near the Carnegie In
stitution between noon on July 1
and noon on Oct. 17.
■ Between 8 p.m. on Oct. 16 and 4
p.m. on Oct. 17 unknown sus
pects) entered an unlocked vehi
cle and stole money and an audio
adapter. Two latent prints were
lifted at the scene.
■ At 1 p.m. an arrestee was trans
ported to San Jose Main Jail and
booked for driving under the in
fluence near the intersection of
Campus Drive and Jordan Way.
The vehicle was towed.
■ Between 4:15 p.m. and 4:25 p.m.
someone stole an unattended
iPhone on the 4th floor of Green
Library (Bing).
■ A cable-locked bike was stolen
from the bike racks outside of Ter
man Engineering Lab (Bldg. 500)
between 11 p.m. and midnight.
TUESDAY, OCT. 18
■ Between 4 p.m. on Oct. 17 and 9
a.m. on Oct. 18 a cable-locked
bike was stolen from the racks
outside of Escondido IV.
■ Someone stole a bike that was U
locked to itself near Castano be
tween 8 p.m. on Oct. 17 and 11
a.m. on Oct. 18.
■ A tennis umpire stand was stolen
from Taube Tennis South courts
between 9:15 p.m. on Oct. 17 and

11 a.m. on Oct. 18. A second stand
was found just outside of the
courts to the southeast.
■ A U-locked bike was stolen from
racks outside the Cummings Art
building between 11 a.m. and
12:15 p.m.
■ Between 12:15 and 1:05 p.m. an
iPhone was stolen from some
one's unattended carrying bag in
side Arrillaga Family Dining
Commons.
■ A woman was cited and arrested
for being in possession of a stolen
placard at the intersection of
Galvez Mall and Crothers Way at
4 p.m.
WEDNESDAY, OCT. 19
■At 4:45 p.m. an injury bike-vs.-
fixed-object collision occurred at
the intersection of Lasuen Mall
and Serra Mall.
THURSDAY, OCT. 20
■ A cable-locked bike was stolen
from a rack outside of Knight
Management Center between
8:30 a.m. and 6 p.m.
■ An unattended purse was stolen
from the McClelland building in
the Knight Management Center
between 6:15 p.m. and 10:30 p.m.
FRIDAY, OCT. 21
■ An abandoned keg was stored in
the SUDPS annex in Stern be
tween 11:25 p.m. and midnight.
The keg was found when SUDPS
was called out on a noise com
plaint.
SATURDAY, OCT. 22
■ A chain-locked bike was stolen
from a rack outside of Roble be
tween 10 p.m. on Oct. 20 and 7:30
a.m. on Oct. 22.
■ A cable-locked bike was stolen
from a rack near Rogers House
on Capistrano Way.
■ Someone stole a chain-locked
bike from the racks outside of Es
condido IV between 10:30 p.m.
on Oct. 21 and 1:30 p.m. on Oct.
22.
■ A vehicle was stolen from Park
ing Structure 6 between midnight
the previous night and 2:30 p.m.
■ An unattended laptop was stolen
at 2:30 p.m. from a coffee table in
Green Library (East).
■ At 2:45 p.m. a male was cited and
released on warrants out of San
Francisco near the intersection of
Campus Drive and Galvez Mall.
■ Someone stole an unattended
backpack from Lot 2 between
4:45 p.m. and 5 p.m.
■ Between 2:30 p.m. and 5:30 p.m.
three men were cited and re
leased at Stanford Stadium on
warrants out of San Francisco.
■ Someone entered a dormitory
room in Junipero through a
ground-floor window between 7
p.m. and 7:20 p.m. and stole a lap
top and headphones. The suspect
also attempted to enter other
rooms in the Wilbur Hall dormi
tory.
SUNDAY, OCT. 23
■ A male was cited and released for
being a minor in possession of al

cohol at 12:20 a.m. near Bob.
■ Two females were cited and re
leased for being minors in posses
sion of alcohol at 12:40 a.m. near
Theta Delta Chi.
■ Suspect(s) entered Kappa Alpha
house through an unlocked glass
door and stole a wallet contain
ing credit cards and cash between
10 p.m. the previous night and
6:05 a.m.
MONDAY, OCT. 24
■ A cable-locked bike was stolen
from a rack near Studio 1 in Es
condido Village between 11:15
a.m. on Oct. 21 and 9:50 a.m. on
Oct. 24.
■An unknown suspect used a
fraudulent check to pay for mer
chandise at the bookstore at
12:45 p.m.
■ A female was cited and released
for driving without a license at the
intersection of Stanford Avenue
and Raimundo Way at 4:03 p.m.
■ Somebody stole an unattended
iPhone from the CoHo between
7:15 p.m. and 7:20 p.m.
