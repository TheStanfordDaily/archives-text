What is a religion, and does Stanford qualify as one?
Stanford culture may seem very different from religions like Christian
ity or Buddhism. But a deeper look reveals that it has many qualities
of a religion - origin stories, rituals, taboos, and even some supernat
ural beings.
In RELIGST 2: Is Stanford a Religion? Professors Steven Weitzman and
Kathryn Gin Lum offer students the opportunity to learn and apply
some of the theories and methods of Religious Studies to the history
of Stanford and the Silicon Valley. Students will gain a better under
standing of what motivates people to be religious, how religion fos
ters group formation, and the similarities and differences between
religious and secular culture. Class meets Mondays and Wednesdays
from 2:15 pm - 3:30 pm in Bldg 540, Rm 108.
