### CALEB SMITH CONTRIBUTING WRITER 
This report covers a selection of
incidents reported between May 5
and May 12 as recorded in the Stan
ford Department of Public Safety
bulletin.
MONDAY, MAY 5
■ A female student reported that
she was sexually assaulted by a
non-student male acquaintance in
a car near the intersection of
Santa Teresa Street and Lomita
Drive.
TUESDAY, MAY 6
■ An unknown suspect or suspects
entered a Stanford employee's
unlocked vehicle and stole an iPod
Shuffle between 9:30 a.m. and
7:30 p.m. at Lasuen Mall at Ar
boretum Road.
WEDNESDAY, MAY 7
■ A female student reported suspi
cious circumstances that could be
indicative of a sexual assault.
THURSDAY, MAY 8
■ An incident of trespassing oc
curred at the Enchanted Broccoli
Forest between 1 a.m. and 1:05
a.m. An unknown suspect entered
the victim's unlocked dormitory
room and yelled at her, then left
without further incident.
FRIDAY, MAY 9
■ A non-injury, vehicle vs. vehicle,
hit-and-run collision occurred
around 12 a.m. at the Burnham
Pavilion East Parking Lot at Ar
guello Way.
■ A juvenile was cited and released
for violating curfew at 2:45 a.m. at
the corner of Lomita Street and
Lagunita Street.
SATURDAY, MAY 10
■ An incident of prowling occurred
at 3 a.m. at Building 8 of the Rains
Complex. An unknown suspect

opened the window to the victim's
apartment. When confronted, the
suspect apologized and said he
was mistaken. The suspect then
left the scene. An AlertSU was is
sued.
■ An unknown suspect or suspects
stole a wallet from a locked locker
in the Arrillaga Outdoor Educa
tion and Recreation Center while
the victim was working out be
tween 4 p.m. and 5 p.m. There was
no apparent force used to open
the locker.
■ An unlocked bike was stolen from
a patio near Escondido Village II-
Building 84.
SUNDAY, MAY 11
■ An unknown suspect or suspects
stole multiple wallets from sepa
rate lockers at the Arrillaga Out
door Education and Recreation
Center between 3:30 p.m. and 5:10
p.m. The lockers involved were re
portedly locked before they were
left unattended.
MONDAY, MAY 12
■An injury-causing bike accident
occurred when the bicyclist fell
off his bike as a result of a broken
steering tube due to excessive
rust at the corner of Palm Drive
and Campus Drive at 10:34 a.m.
■ A reportedly U-locked bike was
stolen from a bike rack outside of
Branner Hall between 1 p.m. and
4:05 p.m.
■ An unknown suspect or suspects
stole an unattended camera from
a hallway in Building 420 between
2:10 p.m. and 3:30 p.m.
■ The Palo Alto Fire Department
extinguished a fire between 4:08
p.m. and 4:15 p.m. on Roth Way be
tween Lasuen Street and Palm
Drive. A piece of artwork in the
form of a wall caught fire as a re
sult spontaneous combustion from
the effect of the sun on hot metal.
