# POLICE BLOTTER
## 
### CALEB SMITH CONTRIBUTING WRITER 
This report covers a selec
tion of incidents reported be
tween May 13 and May 21 as
recorded in the Stanford De
partment of Public Safety
(SUDPS) bulletin.
TUESDAY, MAY 13
■ A hit-and-run, non-injury,
vehicle vs. vehicle collision
occurred around midnight
at Burnham Pavilion.
■ A cable- and reportedly U
locked scooter was stolen
from the bike rack outside
the Avery Aquatic Center
between May 5 and May 13.
■ Suspicious circumstances
were reported at Paloma in
Florence Moore Hall be
tween May 12 and May 13.
An individual was reported
to have been engaging ran
dom females on campus in
conversation, which led to
an exchange of email infor
mation and persistent mes
sages afterwards.
WEDNESDAY, MAY 14
■ Six individuals were cited
and released for trespass
ing at the Avery Aquatic
Center around 11:30 p.m.
THURSDAY, MAY 15
■ The Palo Alto Fire Depart
ment and the SUDPS re
sponded to a call of a Mar
guerite bus on fire at Palm
Oval at the Marguerite Bus
Stop between 11:25 a.m. and
11:35 a.m. The driver and
the passenger on the bus
were safely evacuated. The
fire was extinguished and
the bus was put out of serv
ice.
■ The rear tire of a bike
locked to a rack was stolen
from near Dinkelspiel Audi
torium between 6 p.m. and
11 p.m.
■ An unknown suspect or sus
pects spray-painted the word
"stand" on the sandstone ex
terior of 419 Lasuen Mall be
tween May 14 and May 15.
The estimated cost of remov
ing the paint is $1,500.
FRIDAY, MAY 16
■ An incident of vandalism
occurred between May 11
and May 16 at the Hewlett
Teaching Center. An un
known suspect or suspects
used a permanent ink
marker to write the word
"stand" several times
across the top surface of a
granite wall.
■ AbikethatwasU-lockedto
itself was stolen from near

Tresidder Memorial Union
between May 14 and May 16.
SATURDAY, MAY 17
■ An individual was cited and
released for being a Minor
in Possession of alcohol
(MIP) at the 600 block of
Lomita Court at 12:35 a.m.
■ An individual was arrested
and transported to the San
Jose Main Jail for Driving
Under the Influence (DUI)
at Lane W and Mayfield
Avenue at 1:59 a.m.
■ An unknown suspect or sus
pects stole a wallet from an
unattended backpack at the
Burnham Pavilion between
9 a.m. and noon.
■ An unknown suspect or sus
pects stole an unattended
backpack, which contained
an iPhone, from Frost Am
phitheater around 8 p.m.
■ An individual was cited and

released for being a Minor
in Possession of alcohol
(MIP) at 11:15 p.m. at May
field Avenue.
■ An individual was arrested
and transported to the San
Jose Main Jail for Public In
toxication (DIP) at Sigma
Chi at 11:30 p.m.
SUNDAY, MAY 18
■ An individual was arrested
and transported to the San
Jose Main Jail for Public In
toxication (DIP) at Sigma
Chi at 12:35 a.m. The indi
vidual was subsequently
transported to the San Jose
Valley Medical Center.
MONDAY, MAY 19
■ An unknown suspect or sus
pects stole an envelope con
taining currency from the
inside front pocket of the
victim's unattended back

pack between 1:05 p.m. and
6 p.m. at Wilbur Dining.
■ An unlocked bike was stolen
from a bike rack at the
Blume Earthquake Engi
neering Center at Building
540 between May 16 and
May 19.
■ Between May 18 and May
19, an unknown suspect or
suspects spray-painted
symbols on the Lake Lagu
nita Fire Pit. Stanford Facil
ities cleaned up the area the
day of the occurrence.
■ A reportedly U-locked bike
was stolen near Escondido
Village 111 - Building 110 be
tween May 18 and May 19.
Two medical alcohol trans
ports were reported between
May 13 and May 21.
